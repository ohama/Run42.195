# Unit 71: Countable Nouns with a/an and some

## A. 단수 셀 수 있는 명사: a/an 필수

셀 수 있는 명사가 **단수**일 때는 반드시 **a/an**이나 다른 관사(the, my, this 등)가 필요합니다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I put on my new uniform."**
> 새 교복을 입었다.

- uniform = countable → a uniform / my uniform

> **"I found a spot near the back door."**
> 뒷문 근처에 자리를 찾았다.

- spot = countable → a spot

> **"It was a stupid idea."**
> 멍청한 생각이었다.

- idea = countable → a/an idea (NOT: was stupid idea)

---

## B. a vs an

- **a** + 자음 소리로 시작: a bus, a flower, a uniform
- **an** + 모음 소리로 시작: an alarm, an hour, an experience

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I set an alarm."**
> 알람을 맞췄다.

> **"An hour and ten minutes."**
> 한 시간 십 분.

> **"It was a choice."**
> 선택이었다.

**주의:** 발음 기준!
- a university (유-로 시작하므로 자음 소리)
- an hour (h가 묵음이므로 모음 소리)

---

## C. 복수 셀 수 있는 명사: a/an 없이 또는 some

복수 명사에는 a/an을 쓸 수 없습니다. 대신:
- 관사 없이: I saw flowers.
- some: I saw some flowers.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I saw rapeseed flowers along the road."**
> 길가에 유채꽃을 봤다.

- flowers (복수) → a/an 없음

> **"There were some cherry trees along the roadside."**
> 길가에 벚나무가 몇 그루 있었다.

- trees (복수) + some

> **"I heard birds singing."**
> 새들이 노래하는 소리가 들렸다.

- birds (복수) → a/an 없음

---

## D. some의 용법

**some**은 복수 명사와 셀 수 없는 명사 앞에 사용합니다.

### 복수 명사 + some:

> **"I still have some of the candy Minseo gave me."**
> 민서가 준 사탕이 아직 좀 남아 있다.

> **"Some kids looked at me."**
> 몇몇 애들이 나를 쳐다봤다.

### 셀 수 없는 명사 + some:

> **"I drank some water."**
> 물을 좀 마셨다.

> **"I need some advice."**
> 조언이 좀 필요하다.

---

## E. 일반적인 의미 vs 특정 대상

### 일반적인 의미 (관사 없음):
> **"I don't really like running."**
> 달리기를 별로 안 좋아한다.

- running = 일반적인 달리기

### 특정 대상 (a/an 또는 the):
> **"The first week is already over."**
> 첫째 주가 벌써 끝났다.

- the first week = 특정 주간

---

## F. 비교: a/an vs some vs nothing

| Singular Countable | Plural Countable | Uncountable |
|-------------------|------------------|-------------|
| **a/an** bus | buses / **some** buses | - |
| **an** idea | ideas / **some** ideas | - |
| - | flowers / **some** flowers | water / **some** water |
| **a** student | students / **some** students | information |

### From the Novel:

| Sentence | Analysis |
|----------|----------|
| "I had to catch **a** bus." | 단수 → a |
| "There were too many **buses**." | 복수 → 관사 없음 |
| "I drank **some** water." | 셀 수 없음 → some |
| "I saw **some** flowers." | 복수 + 약간의 → some |

---

## G. 직업, 신분을 나타낼 때: a/an 필수

> **"I'm a middle schooler now."**
> 나는 이제 중학생이다.

- a middle schooler (NOT: I'm middle schooler)

> **"He was a stranger."**
> 그는 낯선 사람이었다.

- a stranger (NOT: He was stranger)

## Exercises

### A. a, an, some 중 알맞은 것을 고르세요.

1. I need _______ advice.
2. She is _______ teacher.
3. I saw _______ birds this morning.
4. It was _______ good idea.
5. I drank _______ water.
6. He gave me _______ candy.
7. It takes _______ hour to walk.
8. I'm _______ student.

**Answers:**
1. some
2. a
3. some (또는 관사 없음)
4. a
5. some
6. some (또는 a)
7. an
8. a

### B. 틀린 부분을 고치세요.

1. I'm student. → _______
2. I need a advice. → _______
3. It was good experience. → _______
4. I saw a flowers. → _______
5. She is teacher. → _______

**Answers:**
1. I'm a student.
2. I need some advice.
3. It was a good experience.
4. I saw some flowers. / I saw flowers.
5. She is a teacher.

### C. 빈칸을 채우세요.

1. I put on _______ new uniform. (my/a)
2. I heard _______ bird singing. (a/some)
3. I need _______ information. (an/some)
4. It was _______ hour-long walk. (a/an)
5. There were _______ people on the bus. (a/some)

**Answers:**
1. my / a
2. a
3. some
4. an
5. some

## Common Mistakes (흔한 실수)

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| I'm student. | I'm **a** student. |
| I need advice. | I need **some** advice. |
| It was good idea. | It was **a** good idea. |
| I saw a flowers. | I saw **some** flowers. / I saw flowers. |
| He is teacher. | He is **a** teacher. |
