# Unit 27: Could (do) and could have (done)

## A. 형태 (Form)

### Could (do): 현재/미래의 가능성

| Subject | could | Verb | Example |
|---------|-------|------|---------|
| I/You/He/She/etc. | could | run | You could try running with me. |
| I/You/He/She/etc. | couldn't | run | I couldn't do that. |

### Could have (done): 과거의 가능성

| Subject | could have | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|------------|-----------------|---------|
| I/You/He/She/etc. | could have | run | I could have run faster. |
| I/You/He/She/etc. | couldn't have | imagined | I couldn't have imagined this. |

---

## B. Could (do): 현재/미래 가능성

### 용법 1: 가능성 (Possibility)

지금 또는 미래에 **가능한** 것:

> **"I could walk, but it would take too long."**
> 걸을 수도 있지만, 너무 오래 걸릴 거야.

### 용법 2: 제안 (Suggestion)

부드러운 제안:

> **"You could try running with me sometime."**
> 언젠가 나랑 같이 달려볼 수도 있어.

> **"We could meet at the park."**
> 공원에서 만날 수도 있어.

### From the Original Book:

> "A: What shall we do tonight?"
> "B: We **could** go to the cinema."
> 오늘 밤 뭐 할까? - 영화 보러 갈 수도 있어.

> "**Could** you pass me the salt, please?"
> 소금 좀 건네줄래요?

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"You could try running earlier."**
> 더 일찍 달려볼 수도 있어.

> **"You could join the track team."**
> 육상부에 들어갈 수도 있어.

---

## C. Could have (done): 과거 가능성

### 용법 1: 했지만 하지 않은 일 (Something possible but didn't happen)

> **"I could have taken the bus, but I chose to walk."**
> 버스를 탈 수도 있었는데, 걷기로 했어.

- 버스를 탈 수 **있었지만** 안 탔음

### 용법 2: 과거에 대한 후회 (Regret)

> **"I could have trained harder."**
> 더 열심히 훈련할 수 있었는데.

- 더 열심히 하지 **않은 것**에 대한 후회

### 용법 3: 과거에 대한 비난 (Criticism)

> **"You could have told me earlier!"**
> 더 일찍 말해줄 수도 있었잖아!

- 말해주지 않은 것에 대한 비난

### From the Original Book:

> "I **could have gone** to the party, but I decided to stay home."
> 파티에 갈 수도 있었는데 집에 있기로 했어.

> "You **could have been** hurt!"
> 다쳤을 수도 있었잖아!

> "Why didn't you tell me? You **could have told** me."
> 왜 안 말했어? 말해줄 수도 있었잖아.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I couldn't have imagined this a year ago."**
> 1년 전에는 이걸 상상도 못 했을 거야.

> **"I could have given up, but I didn't."**
> 포기할 수 있었지만, 안 했어.

---

## D. Couldn't have done: 과거 불가능

**과거에 불가능했던 것**을 표현합니다.

> **"I couldn't have finished without your help."**
> 네 도움 없이는 끝내지 못했을 거야.

> **"He couldn't have run that fast."**
> 그가 그렇게 빨리 달렸을 리가 없어.

### Couldn't have의 두 가지 의미:

| Meaning | Example | Korean |
|---------|---------|--------|
| 불가능했을 것 | I couldn't have done it without you. | 네 없이는 못 했을 거야. |
| 했을 리가 없다 (추측) | He couldn't have been there. | 그가 거기 있었을 리가 없어. |

---

## E. Could vs Could have done 비교

| Could (do) | Could have (done) |
|------------|-------------------|
| 현재/미래 가능성 | 과거 가능성 |
| 지금 할 수 있다 | 과거에 할 수 있었다 (안 했음) |
| You could walk. | You could have walked. |
| (지금 걸을 수 있어) | (과거에 걸을 수 있었는데 안 걸었어) |
| We could meet tomorrow. | We could have met yesterday. |
| (내일 만날 수 있어) | (어제 만날 수 있었는데 안 만났어) |

---

## F. Could have done의 다양한 용법

### 1. 하지 않은 과거 선택 (Unchosen option)

> **"I could have given up, but I didn't."**
> 포기할 수 있었지만, 안 했어.

### 2. 과거에 대한 추측 (Past possibility)

> **"He could have been tired."**
> 그가 피곤했을 수도 있어.

### 3. 과거에 대한 비난/후회 (Criticism/Regret)

> **"You could have told me earlier!"**
> 더 일찍 말해줄 수도 있었잖아!

> **"I could have trained harder."**
> 더 열심히 훈련할 수 있었는데.

---

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I could to run.~~ | I **could run**. | could + 동사원형 |
| ~~I could have ran.~~ | I **could have run**. | have + 과거분사 |
| ~~I could had done.~~ | I **could have done**. | could have + 과거분사 |
| ~~He could finished.~~ | He **could have finished**. | have 필요 |
| ~~I couldn't have imagine.~~ | I **couldn't have imagined**. | 과거분사 |
| ~~Could have you helped?~~ | **Could you have helped**? | 어순 주의 |

### 시제 선택 가이드:

| 상황 | 형태 | 예문 |
|------|------|------|
| 현재 가능 | could + 동사원형 | You could try running. |
| 과거 가능 (안 함) | could have + p.p. | You could have tried. |
| 현재 불가능 | couldn't + 동사원형 | I couldn't do it. |
| 과거 불가능 | couldn't have + p.p. | I couldn't have imagined. |

## Exercises

### A. Could vs Could have 선택 (Choose the correct form)

1. It's nice weather. We _______ go for a run. (지금 가능)
2. I _______ take the bus yesterday, but I walked instead. (과거)
3. You _______ tell me you were tired! (과거 - 비난)
4. He _______ be at the park now. (현재 가능성)
5. She _______ finish earlier if she had tried. (과거 가능성)
6. We _______ meet at the park tomorrow. (미래 제안)
7. I _______ run faster, but I was tired. (과거)
8. You _______ join the track team. (현재 제안)

**Answers:**
1. could
2. could have taken
3. could have told
4. could
5. could have finished
6. could
7. could have run
8. could

### B. 문장 완성 (Complete the sentences)

1. 1년 전에는 상상도 못 했을 거야.
   → I _______ this a year ago.

2. 버스 탈 수도 있었는데, 걷기로 했어.
   → I _______ the bus, but I chose to walk.

3. 더 열심히 훈련할 수 있었는데.
   → I _______ harder.

4. 포기할 수 있었지만, 안 했어.
   → I _______, but I didn't.

5. 도움 없이는 못 끝냈을 거야.
   → I _______ without your help.

**Answers:**
1. couldn't have imagined
2. could have taken
3. could have trained
4. could have given up
5. couldn't have finished

### C. 상황에 맞게 작성 (Write based on context)

1. (제안) 같이 달릴 수도 있어.
   → You _______ with me.

2. (후회) 더 일찍 떠났어야 했는데.
   → I _______ earlier.

3. (비난) 말해줄 수도 있었잖아!
   → You _______ me!

4. (과거 불가능) 네 도움 없이는 못 했을 거야.
   → I _______ without your help.

**Answers:**
1. could run
2. could have left
3. could have told
4. couldn't have done it

### D. 틀린 부분 고치기 (Error Correction)

1. I could have went to the party.
   → I could have **gone** to the party.

2. You could had told me.
   → You **could have told** me.

3. I could to run faster.
   → I **could run** faster.

4. He couldn't have imagine this.
   → He couldn't have **imagined** this.

5. Could have you helped me?
   → **Could you have** helped me?

| Usage | Example | Korean |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 과거 선택 (안 함) | I could have walked. | 걸을 수도 있었어. |
