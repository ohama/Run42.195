# Unit 121: At/on/in (time)

## A. At + 시각 / 특정 시점 (Form and Usage)

**At**은 **정확한 시각**이나 **특정 시점**에 사용합니다.

### 구조:
> at + 시각/특정 시점

### From the Original Book:
> "I'll meet you **at** 7:30."
> 7시 30분에 만나자.

> "The shops close **at** 5:00."
> 가게들은 5시에 닫는다.

### At 사용 상황:

| Usage | Examples | Korean |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 시각 | at 7:30, at noon, at midnight | ~시에 |
| 식사 시간 | at breakfast, at lunchtime | ~때에 |
| 특정 시점 | at the moment, at the same time | ~에 |
| 나이 | at the age of 14, at 14 | ~세에 |
| 연휴/축제 | at Christmas, at Easter | ~에 |
| 밤 | at night | 밤에 |
| 주말 | at the weekend (영국식) | 주말에 |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus comes at 7:30."**
> 버스는 7시 30분에 온다.

> **"School starts at 8:30."**
> 학교는 8시 30분에 시작한다.

> **"At the moment, I'm writing this diary."**
> 지금 이 순간, 일기를 쓰고 있다.

> **"At the age of 14, I ran my first marathon."**
> 14살에 첫 마라톤을 뛰었다.

---

## B. On + 요일 / 날짜 / 특정일

**On**은 **요일, 날짜, 특정한 날**에 사용합니다.

### 구조:
> on + 요일/날짜/특정일

### From the Original Book:
> "I'll see you **on** Friday."
> 금요일에 보자.

> "What did you do **on** Sunday?"
> 일요일에 뭐 했어?

### On 사용 상황:

| Usage | Examples | Korean |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 요일 | on Monday, on Fridays | ~요일에 |
| 날짜 | on March 4, on the 5th | ~일에 |
| 특정일 | on my birthday, on Christmas Day | ~날에 |
| 요일 + 시간대 | on Monday morning | ~요일 ~에 |
| 특별한 날 | on New Year's Day, on Independence Day | ~에 |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"On Monday, I missed the bus."**
> 월요일에 버스를 놓쳤다.

> **"On March 2nd, I ran the marathon."**
> 3월 2일에 마라톤을 달렸다.

> **"I run on Mondays and Thursdays."**
> 월요일과 목요일에 달린다.

> **"On race day, I woke up at 5 a.m."**
> 경기 날, 새벽 5시에 일어났다.

---

## C. In + 월 / 계절 / 년도 / 시간대

**In**은 **긴 기간** (월, 계절, 년도)이나 **하루 중 시간대**에 사용합니다.

### 구조:
> in + 월/계절/년도/시간대

### From the Original Book:
> "They got married **in** October."
> 그들은 10월에 결혼했다.

> "I'll call you **in** the morning."
> 아침에 전화할게.

### In 사용 상황:

| Usage | Examples | Korean |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 월 | in March, in October | ~월에 |
| 계절 | in spring, in summer | ~에 |
| 년도 | in 2024, in 2025 | ~년에 |
| 시간대 | in the morning, in the afternoon | ~에 |
| 기간 | in a week, in two hours | ~ 후에/안에 |
| 세기 | in the 21st century | ~세기에 |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I started running in March."**
> 3월에 달리기를 시작했다.

> **"In summer, I run at 5 a.m."**
> 여름에는 새벽 5시에 달린다.

> **"In 2024, I ran my first 10km race."**
> 2024년에 첫 10km 레이스를 뛰었다.

> **"I usually run in the morning."**
> 보통 아침에 달린다.

---

## D. At night / In the morning

**밤**에는 **at night**, 다른 시간대에는 **in the + 시간대**를 사용합니다.

| Expression | Example | Korean |
|------------|---------|--------|
| in the morning | I run in the morning. | 아침에 |
| in the afternoon | I rest in the afternoon. | 오후에 |
| in the evening | I stretch in the evening. | 저녁에 |
| at night | I sleep at night. | 밤에 |

### From the Original Book:
> "I can't sleep **at night**."
> 밤에 잠을 잘 수가 없어.

> "I'll see you **in the morning**."
> 아침에 보자.

### From the Novel:

> **"I couldn't sleep at night before the race."**
> 경기 전날 밤에 잠을 잘 수 없었다.

## E. 전치사 없이 쓰는 표현 (No Preposition)

**this, last, next, every** 앞에는 전치사를 쓰지 않습니다.

| Correct (O) | Incorrect (X) |
|-------------|---------------|
| I ran **this morning**. | ~~on this morning~~ |
| **Last Monday**, I missed the bus. | ~~On last Monday~~ |
| I'll run **next Saturday**. | ~~on next Saturday~~ |
| I run **every day**. | ~~on every day~~ |
| See you **tomorrow**. | ~~on tomorrow~~ |
| I went **yesterday**. | ~~on yesterday~~ |

### From the Novel:

> **"This morning, I ran 7km."** (not: on this morning)
> 오늘 아침에 7km를 달렸다.

> **"Last week, I ran every day."** (not: on last week)
> 지난주에 매일 달렸다.

---

## F. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~in 7 o'clock~~ | **at** 7 o'clock | 시각은 at |
| ~~at March~~ | **in** March | 월은 in |
| ~~in Monday~~ | **on** Monday | 요일은 on |
| ~~at the morning~~ | **in** the morning | 시간대는 in |
| ~~in night~~ | **at** night | 밤은 at |
| ~~on 2024~~ | **in** 2024 | 년도는 in |
| ~~on this week~~ | **this week** | this 앞에 전치사 없음 |
| ~~at the Christmas Day~~ | **on** Christmas Day | 특정 날은 on |

## Exercises

### A. at, on, in 중 알맞은 것을 고르세요.

1. The race starts _______ 8 o'clock.
2. I started running _______ March.
3. The marathon is _______ March 2nd.
4. I usually run _______ the morning.
5. I can't sleep _______ night before a race.
6. We met _______ Friday.
7. I was born _______ 2010.
8. She called me _______ noon.

**Answers:**
1. at
2. in
3. on
4. in
5. at
6. on
7. in
8. at

### B. 전치사가 필요하면 쓰고, 필요 없으면 X 표시하세요.

1. I'll run _______ next Monday.
2. I ran _______ yesterday.
3. The race is _______ October 5th.
4. I train _______ every morning.
5. See you _______ Friday.
6. I'll call you _______ this afternoon.
7. He arrived _______ last night.
8. We met _______ the weekend.

**Answers:**
1. X (next 앞에 전치사 없음)
2. X (yesterday 앞에 전치사 없음)
3. on
4. X (every 앞에 전치사 없음)
5. on
6. X (this 앞에 전치사 없음)
7. X (last 앞에 전치사 없음)
8. at / on (영국식 at, 미국식 on)

### C. 빈칸을 채워 문장을 완성하세요.

1. The bus comes _______ 7:30 _______ the morning.
2. I started running _______ March 2024.
3. _______ Mondays, I run to school.
4. _______ the age of 14, I completed a marathon.
5. The race is _______ May 15th.
6. I'll see you _______ noon.

**Answers:**
1. at, in
2. in
3. On
4. At
5. on
6. at

### D. 틀린 부분을 고치세요.

1. I wake up in 6 o'clock.
   → I wake up **at** 6 o'clock.

2. She was born in March 15th.
   → She was born **on** March 15th.

3. I'll see you on next week.
   → I'll see you **next week**.

4. We had a party at Christmas Day.
   → We had a party **on** Christmas Day.

5. I run at the morning.
   → I run **in** the morning.

## Summary: At/On/In (Time) 비교표

| Preposition | Usage | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **at** | 시각, 특정 시점, 밤 | at 7:30, at noon, at night, at the moment |
| **on** | 요일, 날짜, 특정일 | on Monday, on March 2nd, on my birthday |
| **in** | 월, 계절, 년도, 시간대 | in March, in summer, in 2024, in the morning |

---

## 전치사 선택 가이드

```
시각? (7:30, noon, midnight) → At
요일/날짜? (Monday, March 5th) → On
월/계절/년도? (March, spring, 2024) → In
시간대? (morning, afternoon, evening) → In
밤? → At night

this/last/next/every 앞? → 전치사 없음
```
