# Unit 70: Countable and Uncountable (2)

## A. 문맥에 따라 달라지는 명사 (Form)

일부 명사는 **의미에 따라** 셀 수 있기도 하고 셀 수 없기도 합니다.

| Word | Uncountable | Countable |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| experience | 경험/경력 (전체) | 하나의 경험 (특정) |
| time | 시간 (전체) | 횟수/한 번 |
| coffee | 커피 (물질) | 커피 한 잔 |
| hair | 머리카락 (전체) | 머리카락 한 올 |

### From the Original Book:

> "I don't have much **experience**." (경력 전체)
> "It was an interesting **experience**." (특정 경험)

---

## B. experience: 경험 vs 경험들 (Primary Usage)

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| experience (경험, 경력) | an experience (하나의 경험) |
| I have experience. | It was a good experience. |

### From the Original Book:

> "Do you have any **experience** of teaching?"
> 가르친 경험이 있으세요? (전체 경력)

> "I had a lot of interesting **experiences** while I was traveling."
> 여행하면서 재미있는 경험을 많이 했어. (개별 경험들)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I don't have much experience running."**
> 달리기 경험이 별로 없다. (uncountable: 전체 경험)

> **"Walking to school was a new experience."**
> 학교까지 걸어간 건 새로운 경험이었다. (countable: 특정 경험)

---

## C. time: 시간 vs 횟수 (Time)

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| time (시간 전체) | a time (한 번) / times (여러 번) |
| I don't have time. | Four times a day. |

### From the Original Book:

> "I don't have **time** to talk now."
> 지금 얘기할 시간이 없어. (시간 전체)

> "I've seen this movie three **times**."
> 이 영화를 세 번 봤어. (횟수)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"It takes more time."**
> 시간이 더 걸린다. (uncountable)

> **"In those four days, I felt nauseous four times."**
> 나흘 동안, 네 번 속이 메스꺼웠다. (countable: 횟수)

> **"This is my first time being late."**
> 처음으로 지각하는 거다. (countable: 한 번)

### time 관련 표현:

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| spend time | have a good time |
| waste time | three times |
| free time | next time |
| on time | the first time |

---

## D. space/room: 공간 vs 방 (Space)

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| space (공간) | a space (자리, 칸) |
| room (공간, 여유) | a room (방) |

### From the Original Book:

> "There isn't much **space** in this apartment."
> 이 아파트에 공간이 많지 않아. (공간 전체)

> "Is there **a space** for my car?"
> 내 차 댈 자리 있어? (특정 자리)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"There was no space to move."**
> 움직일 공간이 없었다. (uncountable)

> **"I found a space near the back door."**
> 뒷문 근처에 자리를 찾았다. (countable: 특정 자리)

### room의 두 가지 의미:

| Uncountable (공간) | Countable (방) |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Is there room for me? | There are three rooms. |
| There's no room to sit. | I need a room. |

---

## E. 음식/음료 관련 (Food & Drinks)

많은 음식/음료는 물질로는 uncountable이지만, 종류나 주문 단위로는 countable입니다.

| Uncountable (물질) | Countable (종류/주문) |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| coffee (커피) | a coffee (커피 한 잔) |
| tea (차) | two teas (차 두 잔) |
| cake (케이크) | a cake (케이크 한 판) |
| chicken (닭고기) | a chicken (닭 한 마리) |
| beer (맥주) | two beers (맥주 두 잔) |

### From the Original Book:

> "Would you like some **coffee**?" (물질)
> "Can I have **two coffees**, please?" (주문: 두 잔)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Seaweed soup sat on the table."**
> 미역국이 식탁에 있었다. (soup = uncountable: 물질)

> **"I ate a banana."**
> 바나나 하나를 먹었다. (banana = countable)

## F. 빛/소리/냄새 (Light/Sound/Smell)

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| light (빛) | a light (조명, 불빛) |
| sound (소리 전체) | a sound (소리 하나) |
| noise (소음) | a noise (소리 하나) |

### From the Original Book:

> "There isn't enough **light** to take a photo."
> 사진 찍기에 빛이 충분하지 않아. (빛 전체)

> "There was **a light** on in the building."
> 건물에 불이 켜져 있었어. (조명)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Morning light filtered through the leaves."**
> 아침 햇살이 나뭇잎 사이로 비쳤다. (light = uncountable)

> **"I heard a sound."**
> 소리 하나가 들렸다. (a sound = countable)

> **"Birdsong. Wind. A car passing in the distance."**
> 새소리. 바람. 멀리서 지나가는 차. (sounds = countable concepts)

---

## G. 머리카락 (Hair)

| Uncountable | Countable |
|-------------|-----------|
| hair (머리카락 전체) | a hair (머리카락 한 올) |
| I have black hair. | There's a hair in my soup. |

### From the Original Book:

> "She has long **hair**." (머리카락 전체)
> "There's **a hair** in my coffee!" (한 올)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"My hair was a mess."**
> 머리가 엉망이었다. (hair = uncountable: 머리카락 전체)

> **"My hair whipped around."**
> 머리카락이 휘날렸다. (hair = uncountable)

## H. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I don't have a time.~~ | I don't have **time**. | time(시간) = uncountable |
| ~~It was good experience.~~ | It was **a** good experience. | 특정 경험 = countable |
| ~~She has long hairs.~~ | She has long **hair**. | 머리 전체 = uncountable |
| ~~I drank a water.~~ | I drank **some water**. | water = uncountable |
| ~~There's no rooms.~~ | There's no **room**. | room(공간) = uncountable |
| ~~Four time.~~ | Four **times**. | 횟수 = countable |

## Exercises

### A. 올바른 형태를 고르세요.

1. I don't have (time / a time) to talk now.
2. It was (good experience / a good experience).
3. She has long (hair / hairs).
4. I drank (water / a water).
5. This is my first (time / times) here.
6. There's no (room / a room) to move.
7. I heard (sound / a sound) outside.
8. Would you like (coffee / a coffee)?

**Answers:**
1. time
2. a good experience
3. hair
4. water
5. time
6. room
7. a sound
8. coffee (물질) / a coffee (한 잔 제안)

### B. 각 문장에서 밑줄 친 단어가 countable(C)인지 uncountable(U)인지 표시하세요.

1. It takes more **time**. ___
2. I ran four **times** this week. ___
3. I need some **space**. ___
4. I found **a space** near the door. ___
5. Morning **light** filled the room. ___
6. There was **a light** on. ___
7. It was a wonderful **experience**. ___
8. She has no **experience**. ___

**Answers:**
1. U
2. C
3. U
4. C
5. U
6. C
7. C
8. U

### C. 빈칸을 채우세요.

1. Walking was a new _______ (experience) for me.
2. I don't have much _______ (experience) with running.
3. There were too many _______ (noise) on the bus.
4. The _______ (light) from the window was bright.
5. I had a cup of _______ (coffee).
6. Is there _______ (room) for one more person?
7. This is my third _______ (time) running a marathon.
8. There's _______ (a hair) in my soup!

**Answers:**
1. experience
2. experience
3. noises (각각의 소음들)
4. light
5. coffee
6. room
7. time
8. a hair

### D. 틀린 부분을 고치세요.

1. I had a great experiences.
   → _______

2. She has beautiful hairs.
   → _______

3. There's no rooms to sit.
   → _______

4. I ran four time this week.
   → _______

**Answers:**
1. I had **a great experience** / I had **great experiences**.
2. She has **beautiful hair**.
3. There's **no room** to sit.
4. I ran **four times** this week.

## Summary: 문맥에 따라 달라지는 명사

| Word | Uncountable | Countable |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **experience** | 경험, 경력 | 하나의 경험 |
| **time** | 시간 | 횟수, 한 번 |
| **space/room** | 공간 | 자리, 방 |
| **light** | 빛 | 조명, 불빛 |
| **sound/noise** | 소리 전체 | 소리 하나 |
| **hair** | 머리카락 전체 | 머리카락 한 올 |
| **paper** | 종이 | 서류, 신문 |
| **glass** | 유리 | 유리잔 |
| **coffee/tea** | 커피/차 (물질) | 커피/차 한 잔 |
| **chicken** | 닭고기 | 닭 한 마리 |

### Quick Check:
- 전체/물질/추상적? → **Uncountable**
- 특정/개별/셀 수 있는 단위? → **Countable**
