# Unit 69: Countable and Uncountable (1)

## A. 셀 수 있는 명사 vs 셀 수 없는 명사

영어 명사는 **셀 수 있는 명사(countable nouns)**와 **셀 수 없는 명사(uncountable nouns)**로 나뉩니다.

### Countable Nouns (셀 수 있는 명사)
- 하나, 둘, 셋... 개수를 셀 수 있음
- 단수/복수 형태가 있음: a bus → buses
- a/an을 붙일 수 있음

### Uncountable Nouns (셀 수 없는 명사)
- 개수를 셀 수 없음
- 복수형이 없음: water (NOT: waters)
- a/an을 붙일 수 없음

---

## B. 소설 속 셀 수 있는 명사

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I had to squeeze my way through the bodies."**
> 사람들 사이를 비집고 들어가야 했다.

- bodies = countable (body → bodies)

> **"Someone's bag pressed into my side."**
> 누군가의 가방이 내 옆구리를 눌렀다.

- bag = countable (a bag → bags)

> **"I saw the rapeseed flowers."**
> 유채꽃을 봤다.

- flowers = countable (a flower → flowers)

> **"I set three alarms."**
> 알람을 세 개 맞췄다.

- alarms = countable (숫자와 함께 사용)

---

## C. 소설 속 셀 수 없는 명사

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Cold air filled my lungs."**
> 차가운 공기가 폐를 채웠다.

- air = uncountable (NOT: an air, airs)

> **"I slurped down the soup."**
> 국을 후루룩 마셨다.

- soup = uncountable (NOT: a soup)

> **"Sweat, perfume, something damp."**
> 땀, 향수, 축축한 무언가.

- sweat = uncountable
- perfume = uncountable (향수 전체)

> **"The smell."**
> 그 냄새.

- smell = countable (a smell) 또는 uncountable (smell in general)

---

## D. 비교표: Countable vs Uncountable

| Countable | Uncountable |
|-----------|-------------|
| a bus, buses | traffic |
| a flower, flowers | nature |
| a step, steps | walking |
| a breath, breaths | air |
| a thought, thoughts | information |
| a word, words | advice |
| a bag, bags | luggage |
| a shoe, shoes | footwear |

## E. 흔히 헷갈리는 셀 수 없는 명사

다음은 한국어로는 "하나, 둘" 셀 수 있지만, 영어에서는 셀 수 없는 명사입니다:

| English | Korean | Note |
|---------|--------|------|
| advice | 조언 | NOT: an advice |
| information | 정보 | NOT: informations |
| news | 뉴스 | NOT: a news |
| luggage | 짐 | NOT: luggages |
| furniture | 가구 | NOT: furnitures |
| progress | 진전 | NOT: a progress |
| weather | 날씨 | NOT: a weather |
| traffic | 교통 | NOT: a traffic |
| homework | 숙제 | NOT: homeworks |
| equipment | 장비 | NOT: equipments |

### From the Novel:

> **"I made progress."**
> 진전이 있었다. (NOT: I made a progress)

> **"The weather was cold."**
> 날씨가 추웠다. (NOT: A weather was cold)

## F. 수량 표현과 함께

### Countable nouns:
- **a/an**: a bus, an alarm
- **숫자**: three alarms, seven kilometers
- **many/few**: many people, few students

### Uncountable nouns:
- **some**: some water, some air
- **much/little**: much time, little progress
- **a lot of**: a lot of traffic

### From the Novel:

> **"Too many people."**
> 사람이 너무 많아. (many + countable)

> **"I don't have much stamina."**
> 체력이 별로 없다. (much + uncountable)

### B. 알맞은 형태를 고르세요.

1. I need (an advice / some advice).
2. There were (many people / much people) on the bus.
3. I made (a progress / progress) in my running.
4. I heard (a bird / bird) singing.
5. There was too (much traffic / many traffic).

**Answers:**
1. some advice
2. many people
3. progress
4. a bird
5. much traffic

### C. 문장 완성하기

1. Cold _______ (air/airs) filled my lungs.
2. I saw many _______ (flower/flowers) along the road.
3. I need some _______ (information/informations) about the race.
4. There are too many _______ (bus/buses) in the morning.

**Answers:**
1. air
2. flowers
3. information
4. buses
