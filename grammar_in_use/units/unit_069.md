# Unit 69: Countable and Uncountable (1)

## A. 셀 수 있는 명사 vs 셀 수 없는 명사 (Form)

영어 명사는 **셀 수 있는 명사(countable nouns)**와 **셀 수 없는 명사(uncountable nouns)**로 나뉩니다.

| Countable Nouns | Uncountable Nouns |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| 하나, 둘, 셋... 셀 수 있음 | 개수를 셀 수 없음 |
| 단수/복수 형태 있음 | 복수형 없음 |
| a/an 붙일 수 있음 | a/an 붙일 수 없음 |
| a bus → buses | water (NOT: ~~waters~~) |

### From the Original Book:

> "I bought **a newspaper**." (countable: 신문 한 부)
> "I need **some paper**." (uncountable: 종이 좀)

---

## B. 소설 속 셀 수 있는 명사 (Countable Examples)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I had to squeeze my way through the bodies."**
> 사람들 사이를 비집고 들어가야 했다.

- bodies = countable (body → bodies)

> **"Someone's bag pressed into my side."**
> 누군가의 가방이 내 옆구리를 눌렀다.

- bag = countable (a bag → bags)

> **"I saw the rapeseed flowers."**
> 유채꽃을 봤다.

- flowers = countable (a flower → flowers)

> **"I set three alarms."**
> 알람을 세 개 맞췄다.

- alarms = countable (숫자와 함께 사용)

### 셀 수 있는 명사의 특징:

| Feature | Example |
|---------|---------|
| 단수에 a/an | **a** bus, **an** alarm |
| 복수형 | buses, flowers, bags |
| 숫자와 함께 | three alarms, two flowers |
| many/few | many people, few students |

---

## C. 소설 속 셀 수 없는 명사 (Uncountable Examples)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Cold air filled my lungs."**
> 차가운 공기가 폐를 채웠다.

- air = uncountable (NOT: ~~an air~~, ~~airs~~)

> **"I slurped down the soup."**
> 국을 후루룩 마셨다.

- soup = uncountable (NOT: ~~a soup~~)

> **"Sweat, perfume, something damp."**
> 땀, 향수, 축축한 무언가.

- sweat = uncountable
- perfume = uncountable (향수 전체)

> **"The smell."**
> 그 냄새.

- smell = countable (a smell) 또는 uncountable (smell in general)

### 셀 수 없는 명사의 특징:

| Feature | Example |
|---------|---------|
| a/an 불가 | ~~an air~~, ~~a water~~ |
| 복수형 없음 | ~~airs~~, ~~waters~~ |
| some과 함께 | some water, some air |
| much/little | much time, little progress |

## D. 비교표: Countable vs Uncountable (Comparison)

| Countable | Uncountable |
|-----------|-------------|
| a bus, buses | traffic |
| a flower, flowers | nature |
| a step, steps | walking |
| a breath, breaths | air |
| a thought, thoughts | information |
| a word, words | advice |
| a bag, bags | luggage |
| a shoe, shoes | footwear |
| a job, jobs | work |
| a coin, coins | money |

### 같은 개념, 다른 형태:

| Countable | Uncountable |
|-----------|-------------|
| a **suitcase** | **luggage** |
| a **tip** | **advice** |
| a **chair** | **furniture** |
| a **song** | **music** |
| a **job** | **work** |

## E. 흔히 헷갈리는 셀 수 없는 명사 (Common Uncountable Nouns)

다음은 한국어로는 "하나, 둘" 셀 수 있지만, 영어에서는 셀 수 없는 명사입니다:

| English | Korean | Wrong | Correct |
|---------|--------|-------|---------|
| advice | 조언 | ~~an advice~~ | some advice |
| information | 정보 | ~~informations~~ | some information |
| news | 뉴스 | ~~a news~~ | some news |
| luggage | 짐 | ~~luggages~~ | some luggage |
| furniture | 가구 | ~~furnitures~~ | some furniture |
| progress | 진전 | ~~a progress~~ | some progress |
| weather | 날씨 | ~~a weather~~ | the weather |
| traffic | 교통 | ~~a traffic~~ | heavy traffic |
| homework | 숙제 | ~~homeworks~~ | some homework |
| equipment | 장비 | ~~equipments~~ | some equipment |
| research | 연구 | ~~researches~~ | some research |
| knowledge | 지식 | ~~knowledges~~ | some knowledge |

### From the Novel:

> **"I made progress."**
> 진전이 있었다. (NOT: ~~I made a progress~~)

> **"The weather was cold."**
> 날씨가 추웠다. (NOT: ~~A weather was cold~~)

## F. 수량 표현과 함께 (Quantifiers)

### Countable nouns:
| Quantifier | Example |
|------------|---------|
| a/an | a bus, an alarm |
| 숫자 | three alarms, seven kilometers |
| many | many people |
| few | few students |
| a few | a few friends |
| several | several times |

### Uncountable nouns:
| Quantifier | Example |
|------------|---------|
| some | some water, some air |
| much | much time |
| little | little progress |
| a little | a little money |
| a lot of | a lot of traffic |
| plenty of | plenty of information |

### From the Novel:

> **"Too many people."**
> 사람이 너무 많아. (many + countable)

> **"I don't have much stamina."**
> 체력이 별로 없다. (much + uncountable)

---

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I need an advice.~~ | I need **some advice**. | advice = uncountable |
| ~~There were much people.~~ | There were **many people**. | many + countable |
| ~~I made a progress.~~ | I made **progress**. | progress = uncountable |
| ~~I heard a news.~~ | I heard **some news**. | news = uncountable |
| ~~I need informations.~~ | I need **some information**. | information = uncountable |
| ~~The traffics were bad.~~ | The **traffic was** bad. | traffic = uncountable |

## Summary: Countable vs Uncountable

| Category | Countable | Uncountable |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Article | a/an + 단수 | (no a/an) |
| Plural | 복수형 있음 | 복수형 없음 |
| Many/Much | many | much |
| Few/Little | few, a few | little, a little |
| Numbers | one, two, three... | (숫자 불가) |

### 항상 Uncountable인 명사들:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| 추상 | advice, information, progress, news |
| 물질 | water, air, money, traffic |
| 활동 | work, homework, research |
| 집합 | luggage, furniture, equipment |
