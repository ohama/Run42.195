# Unit 5: Past Simple (I did)

## A. 과거시제의 형태 (Form)

### Regular Verbs: verb + -ed
### Irregular Verbs: 각각 다른 형태 (memorize them!)

| Type | Base | Past | Example |
|------|------|------|---------|
| Regular | walk | walked | I walked to school. |
| Regular | start | started | I started running. |
| Regular | stop | stopped | The bus stopped. |
| Irregular | see | saw | I saw the school. |
| Irregular | go | went | I went home. |
| Irregular | get | got | I got off the bus. |
| Irregular | take | took | It took an hour. |
| Irregular | have | had | I had breakfast. |
| Irregular | come | came | He came late. |
| Irregular | run | ran | I ran to school. |

### Negative and Questions:

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Positive | subject + past form | I walked. / She went. |
| Negative | subject + didn't + verb | I didn't walk. / She didn't go. |
| Question | Did + subject + verb? | Did you walk? / Did she go? |

**주의:** did/didn't 뒤에는 동사원형!
- I walk**ed** → I **didn't walk** (NOT: didn't walked)
- She **went** → **Did** she **go**? (NOT: did went)

---

## B. 과거의 완료된 동작 (Completed actions in the past)

**"I did something" = I did it at a specific time in the past, and it's finished.**

과거시제는 **과거에 일어나서 끝난 일**을 표현합니다.

### From the Original Book:
> "Mozart **wrote** more than 600 pieces of music."
> "I **didn't sleep** well last night."
> "**Did** you **go** out last night?"

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I missed the bus today."**
> 오늘 버스를 놓쳤다.

> **"I jumped out of bed."**
> 침대에서 벌떡 일어났다.

> **"I started walking."**
> 걷기 시작했다.

> **"I slurped down the soup and grabbed my bag."**
> 국을 후루룩 마시고 가방을 집었다.

> **"The teacher looked at me."**
> 선생님이 나를 쳐다봤다.

---

## C. 과거의 연속된 동작 (Sequence of past events)

과거시제는 **연속으로 일어난 일들**을 나열할 때 사용합니다.

### From the Original Book:
> "I **got up** early, **had** a shower, **got dressed**, and **left** for work."

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I put on my new uniform and looked in the mirror."**
> 새 교복을 입고 거울을 봤다.

> **"The bus stopped. The doors opened. People poured off."**
> 버스가 멈췄다. 문이 열렸다. 사람들이 쏟아져 나왔다.

> **"I opened the map on my phone."**
> 폰에서 지도를 열었다.

> **"I sat on the bench and looked up at the school building."**
> 벤치에 앉아서 학교 건물을 올려다봤다.

---

## D. 부정문과 의문문 (Negatives and Questions)

### 부정문: did not (didn't) + 동사원형

| Full form | Contracted | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| did not walk | didn't walk | I didn't walk yesterday. |
| did not go | didn't go | She didn't go to school. |

> **"I didn't say anything."**
> 나는 아무 말도 안 했다.

> **"I didn't hear my alarm."**
> 알람 소리를 못 들었다.

### 의문문: Did + 주어 + 동사원형?

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Yes/No | Did + subject + verb? | Did you walk? |
| Wh- | Wh- + did + subject + verb? | Where did you go? |

> **"Did you catch the next one?"**
> 다음 버스 탔어?

> **"Did I see you this morning?"**
> 오늘 아침에 널 봤나?

> **"Miss the bus?"**
> 버스 놓쳤어? (Did you 생략 - 구어체)

## F. -ed 발음 규칙 (Pronunciation of -ed)

| Ending | Pronunciation | Examples |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| /t/ or /d/ | /ɪd/ | start**ed** /ɪd/, wait**ed** /ɪd/ |
| voiceless consonant | /t/ | walk**ed** /t/, stopp**ed** /t/, watch**ed** /t/ |
| voiced sound | /d/ | open**ed** /d/, arriv**ed** /d/, pull**ed** /d/ |

### From the Novel:
- start**ed** /ɪd/ - "I started walking"
- walk**ed** /t/ - "I walked all the way"
- open**ed** /d/ - "When I opened my eyes"
- stopp**ed** /t/ - "The bus stopped"

## G. 철자 규칙 (Spelling rules for -ed)

| Rule | Base | Past | More Examples |
|------|------|------|---------------|
| 대부분: +ed | walk | walked | work → worked, look → looked |
| -e로 끝남: +d | arrive | arrived | like → liked, live → lived |
| 자음+y: y → ied | study | studied | carry → carried, try → tried |
| 모음+y: +ed | play | played | stay → stayed, enjoy → enjoyed |
| 단모음+단자음: 자음 중복 | stop | stopped | plan → planned, drop → dropped |

## H. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I didn't went.~~ | I **didn't go**. | didn't + 동사원형 |
| ~~Did you went?~~ | **Did** you **go**? | did + 동사원형 |
| ~~I did walked.~~ | I **walked**. | 긍정문에 did 불필요 |
| ~~She didn't saw.~~ | She **didn't see**. | didn't + 동사원형 |
| ~~I runned fast.~~ | I **ran** fast. | 불규칙 동사 |
| ~~He goed home.~~ | He **went** home. | 불규칙 동사 |
| ~~Yesterday I walk.~~ | Yesterday I **walked**. | 과거 시제 필요 |

## Exercises

### A. 과거형으로 바꾸세요.

1. I (miss) _______ the bus.
2. The bus (stop) _______ at the station.
3. She (look) _______ at me.
4. We (start) _______ walking.
5. He (grab) _______ his bag.
6. I (open) _______ the door.
7. They (arrive) _______ on time.
8. She (study) _______ all night.

**Answers:**
1. missed
2. stopped
3. looked
4. started
5. grabbed
6. opened
7. arrived
8. studied

### D. 틀린 부분 고치기

Find and correct the errors:

   → She **ran** very fast.

   → **Did** you **see** the bus?

## Time Expressions with Past Simple

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| yesterday | I walked to school yesterday. |
| last week/month/year | The first week ended last Friday. |
| ago | Four days ago, I started middle school. |
| this morning | I missed the bus this morning. |
| when... | When I opened my eyes, it was 7:23. |
| in 2024 | I started running in 2024. |
| on Monday | I saw him on Monday. |

## Summary: When to Use Past Simple

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Completed past action | I walked to school yesterday. |
| Sequence of events | I woke up, got dressed, and left. |
| Specific past time | I started in March. |
| Past habits (with used to) | I used to take the bus. |
| Past states | I felt sick that day. |
