# Unit 92: Relative Clauses (1) - Clauses with who/that/which

## A. 관계대명사란? (What are relative clauses?)

관계절은 명사를 설명하거나 수식하는 절입니다. **who, that, which**는 관계대명사로, 앞의 명사(선행사)를 다시 받아서 추가 정보를 제공합니다.

A relative clause gives more information about a noun. We use **who/that** for people and **which/that** for things.

### Form (형태):

| 선행사 (Antecedent) | 관계대명사 | 예시 |
|---------------------|------------|------|
| 사람 (people) | who / that | the boy **who** runs |
| 사물 (things) | which / that | the bus **that** comes |
| 사람/사물 | that | (둘 다 가능) |

### Structure (구조):

```
명사(선행사) + 관계대명사 + 동사/주어+동사
The kid      who        hated exercise
The bus      that       comes at 7:30
```

---

## B. 사람에 대해 (For people: who/that)

**who** 또는 **that**을 사용합니다. 구어체에서는 **that**이 더 자연스럽습니다.

### From the Original Book:

> "The man **who** lives next door is very friendly."
> 옆집에 사는 남자는 매우 친절하다.

> "The girl **that** works in the bank is a friend of mine."
> 은행에서 일하는 여자는 내 친구다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The kid who hated exercise is running because he wants to."**
> 운동을 싫어하던 그 아이가 자기가 원해서 달리고 있다.

- "the kid" = 선행사 (antecedent)
- "who hated exercise" = 관계절 (relative clause)

> **"Minseo was the first friend that I made in middle school."**
> 민서는 내가 중학교에서 사귄 첫 친구였다.

> **"The teacher who noticed my improvement asked me to join the track team."**
> 내 발전을 눈여겨본 선생님이 육상부에 들어오라고 하셨다.

> **"The people that were on the bus looked exhausted."**
> 버스에 탄 사람들은 지쳐 보였다.

> **"The grandfather who works at the tangerine orchard encouraged me."**
> 귤밭에서 일하시는 할아버지가 나를 격려해 주셨다.

---

## C. 사물에 대해 (For things: which/that)

**which** 또는 **that**을 사용합니다.

### From the Original Book:

> "I don't like stories **that** have unhappy endings."
> 나는 불행한 결말의 이야기를 좋아하지 않는다.

> "Emily lives in a house **which** was built 100 years ago."
> 에밀리는 100년 전에 지어진 집에 산다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus that comes at 7:30 is always packed."**
> 7시 30분에 오는 버스는 항상 만원이다.

- "the bus" = 선행사
- "that comes at 7:30" = 관계절

> **"These were landscapes which I'd only ever glimpsed through the bus window."**
> 이것들은 내가 버스 창문으로만 언뜻 봤던 풍경들이었다.

> **"The running shoes that I bought this weekend are blue."**
> 이번 주말에 산 런닝화는 파란색이다.

> **"The medal which I got was heavier than I expected."**
> 내가 받은 메달은 예상보다 무거웠다.

> **"The candy which helped with motion sickness was from Minseo."**
> 멀미에 도움이 됐던 사탕은 민서가 준 거였다.

---

## D. 주어 역할의 관계대명사 (Subject relative pronouns)

관계대명사가 관계절 안에서 **주어** 역할을 할 때는 생략할 수 없습니다.

### Structure:

| Pattern | Example | 의미 |
|---------|---------|------|
| 사람 + who/that + 동사 | The teacher **who helped** me | 나를 도운 선생님 |
| 사물 + which/that + 동사 | The bus **that comes** at 7:30 | 7시 30분에 오는 버스 |

### How to identify subject relative pronouns:

```
관계대명사 + 바로 동사 → 주어 역할 → 생략 불가
The man who LIVES here...
         ↑    ↑
      관계대명사  동사
```

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The candy which helped with motion sickness was from Minseo."**
> 멀미에 도움이 됐던 사탕은 민서가 준 거였다.

> **"The grandfather who works at the tangerine orchard encouraged me."**
> 귤밭에서 일하시는 할아버지가 나를 격려해 주셨다.

---

## E. 목적어 역할의 관계대명사 (Object relative pronouns)

관계대명사가 관계절 안에서 **목적어** 역할을 할 때는 생략할 수 있습니다 (Unit 93에서 자세히).

### Structure:

| Pattern | Example | 의미 |
|---------|---------|------|
| 사람 + who(m)/that + 주어 + 동사 | The friend **who I met** | 내가 만난 친구 |
| 사물 + which/that + 주어 + 동사 | The medal **(that) I received** | 내가 받은 메달 |

### How to identify object relative pronouns:

```
관계대명사 + 주어 + 동사 → 목적어 역할 → 생략 가능
The man who I SAW...
         ↑   ↑  ↑
      관계대명사 주어 동사
```

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The route which I run every day passes through tangerine orchards."**
> 내가 매일 달리는 길은 귤밭을 지나간다.

> **"The coach that the school hired helped me a lot."**
> 학교에서 고용한 코치가 나를 많이 도와주었다.

## F. that vs who/which 사용 비교

### Comparison Table:

| 상황 | 선호하는 관계대명사 | 예시 |
|------|---------------------|------|
| 일반적 상황 | who/which 또는 that | The man who/that... |
| 구어체 (spoken) | that | The guy that helped me |
| 문어체/격식 (formal) | who/which | The person who helped me |
| 최상급/서수 뒤 | that (선호) | the best thing **that** I've ever done |
| everything/anything 뒤 | that (선호) | everything **that** happened |

### Formality Comparison:

| More formal | Less formal |
|-------------|-------------|
| The man **who** helped me | The man **that** helped me |
| The book **which** I read | The book **that** I read |
| The students **who** take the bus | The students **that** take the bus |

### From the Original Book:

> "We use **that** (not who/which) after: **everything, anything, something, nothing, all, none, any, only**"

> "Everything **that** happened was my fault."
> 일어난 모든 일이 내 잘못이었다.

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~The man which helped me~~ | The man **who/that** helped me | 사람에게는 who/that |
| ~~The bus who comes~~ | The bus **that/which** comes | 사물에게는 which/that |
| ~~The boy who he runs fast~~ | The boy **who** runs fast | 관계대명사 = 주어, he 불필요 |
| ~~The medal that I got it~~ | The medal **that** I got | 관계대명사 = 목적어, it 불필요 |
| ~~I have a friend runs fast~~ | I have a friend **who** runs fast | 주어 역할 관계대명사 필수 |
| ~~The bus that it comes~~ | The bus **that** comes | that이 이미 주어 역할 |

### 특히 주의할 점:

**1. 관계대명사 뒤에 대명사 반복 금지:**
- ~~The man **who** I met **him**~~ → The man **who** I met
- ~~The bus **that** **it** comes~~ → The bus **that** comes

**2. 사람/사물 구분:**
- People → who/that (NOT which)
- Things → which/that (NOT who)

## Exercises

### A. who, which, that 중 알맞은 것을 고르세요 (Fill in the blanks)

1. The bus _______ comes at 7:30 is always crowded.
2. Minseo is the friend _______ helped me on my first day.
3. The running shoes _______ I bought are very comfortable.
4. The teacher _______ noticed my improvement was impressed.
5. The tangerine orchard _______ I pass every day is beautiful.
6. The kid _______ used to hate exercise became a runner.
7. The candy _______ Minseo gave me helped with motion sickness.
8. The people _______ run in the morning look healthy.

**Answers:**
1. that/which
2. who/that
3. that/which
4. who/that
5. that/which
6. who/that
7. that/which
8. who/that

### B. 두 문장을 관계대명사를 사용하여 한 문장으로 만드세요 (Combine the sentences)

1. I have a friend. He takes the bus with me.
   → I have a friend **who takes the bus with me**.

2. This is the route. I run it every morning.
   → This is the route **that/which I run every morning**.

3. The medal is heavy. I received it at the race.
   → The medal **that/which I received at the race** is heavy.

4. The boy became a runner. He used to hate exercise.
   → The boy **who used to hate exercise** became a runner.

5. The shoes are blue. I bought them this weekend.
   → The shoes **that/which I bought this weekend** are blue.

6. The grandfather encouraged me. He works at the orchard.
   → The grandfather **who works at the orchard** encouraged me.

### C. 문장의 빈칸을 채우세요 (Complete with correct verb form)

1. The person who _______ (help) me most was my coach.
2. The shoes that I _______ (buy) last week are perfect.
3. Students who _______ (take) the bus look tired.
4. The race which _______ (start) at 8 AM was exciting.
5. The kid who _______ (hate) exercise became a marathoner.
6. The route that _______ (pass) the orchard is scenic.

**Answers:**
1. helped
2. bought
3. take
4. started
5. hated
6. passes

### D. 틀린 부분 고치기 (Error correction)

1. The man which helped me was very kind.
   → The man **who/that** helped me was very kind.

2. The bus who comes at 7:30 is always packed.
   → The bus **that/which** comes at 7:30 is always packed.

3. I have a friend who she runs with me.
   → I have a friend who runs with me. (she 삭제)

4. The medal that I received it was heavy.
   → The medal that I received was heavy. (it 삭제)

5. Everything who happened was unexpected.
   → Everything **that** happened was unexpected.

6. The shoes which I bought them are comfortable.
   → The shoes which I bought are comfortable. (them 삭제)

## Summary: who/which/that

### 선행사에 따른 관계대명사 선택:

| | 사람 (People) | 사물 (Things) |
|---|---|---|
| **Formal** | who | which |
| **Informal** | that | that |
| **Both OK** | who/that | which/that |

### 역할에 따른 생략 가능 여부:

| 역할 | 생략 | 예시 |
|------|------|------|
| 주어 | 불가 | The man **who** lives here (who 필수) |
| 목적어 | 가능 | The man (who) I met (who 생략 가능) |

### Quick Decision Guide:

```
1. 선행사가 사람인가? → who 또는 that
2. 선행사가 사물인가? → which 또는 that
3. 관계대명사 뒤에 바로 동사? → 주어 역할, 생략 불가
4. 관계대명사 뒤에 주어+동사? → 목적어 역할, 생략 가능
```

## Compare with Korean (한국어와 비교)

| English | Korean | 구조 차이 |
|---------|--------|----------|
| The bus **that comes** at 7:30 | 7시 30분에 **오는** 버스 | 영어: 명사 뒤 / 한국어: 명사 앞 |
| The friend **who helped** me | 나를 **도와준** 친구 | 영어: 명사 뒤 / 한국어: 명사 앞 |
| The medal **that I received** | 내가 **받은** 메달 | 영어: 명사 뒤 / 한국어: 명사 앞 |

한국어에서는 수식어가 명사 **앞에** 오지만, 영어에서는 관계절이 명사 **뒤에** 옵니다.
