# Unit 95: Relative Clauses (4) - Extra Information Clauses (1)

## A. 두 종류의 관계절 (Two types of relative clauses)

관계절에는 두 가지 종류가 있습니다:

### Comparison Table:

| 종류 | 영어명 | 설명 | 콤마 | 역할 |
|------|--------|------|------|------|
| **Defining** | Identifying | 필수 정보, 누구/무엇인지 명확히 함 | 없음 | 한정 |
| **Non-defining** | Extra information | 추가 정보, 없어도 의미 전달 가능 | 있음 | 보충 |

### From the Original Book:

> **Defining:** "The woman **who lives next door** is a doctor."
> (어떤 여자인지 특정함 - 옆집에 사는 여자)

> **Non-defining:** "My brother Jim**, who lives in London,** is a doctor."
> (짐이 누군지 이미 알고, 런던에 산다는 추가 정보)

---

## B. Defining clauses (한정적 관계절) - 콤마 없음

선행사가 어떤 것인지 **한정**하는 필수 정보입니다. 콤마를 사용하지 않습니다.

### Characteristics:

- 콤마 없음
- 필수 정보 (없으면 의미 불완전)
- that 사용 가능
- 목적어일 때 생략 가능

### From the Original Book:

> "Do you know anyone **who speaks French**?"
> 프랑스어를 하는 사람 아는 사람 있어?

> "Barbara works for a company **that makes furniture**."
> 바바라는 가구를 만드는 회사에서 일한다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus that comes at 7:30 is always packed."**
> 7시 30분에 오는 버스는 항상 만원이다.

- "that comes at 7:30" = 어떤 버스인지 한정함
- 이 정보가 없으면 어떤 버스인지 알 수 없음

> **"Kids who run in the morning look healthy."**
> 아침에 달리는 아이들은 건강해 보인다.

- "who run in the morning" = 어떤 아이들인지 한정함

> **"The medal that I received at the 10km race is on my wall."**
> 10km 대회에서 받은 메달이 내 벽에 걸려 있다.

> **"The route that passes through tangerine orchards is scenic."**
> 귤밭을 지나는 길은 경치가 좋다.

---

## C. Non-defining clauses (비한정적 관계절) - 콤마 있음

이미 특정된 선행사에 **추가 정보**를 제공합니다. 콤마로 구분합니다.

### Characteristics:

- 콤마 있음
- 추가 정보 (없어도 의미 전달 가능)
- **that 사용 불가**
- 관계대명사 생략 불가

### From the Original Book:

> "My brother Jim**, who lives in London,** is a doctor."
> 런던에 사는 내 형 짐은 의사다.

> "Colin told me about his new job**, which** he's enjoying a lot."
> 콜린이 새 직장에 대해 말해줬는데, 아주 즐기고 있대.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Minseo, who I met on the first day, became my best friend."**
> 첫날 만난 민서는 나의 가장 친한 친구가 되었다.

- Minseo는 이미 특정된 사람
- "who I met on the first day" = 추가 정보

> **"Grandfather Yeongcheol, who works at the tangerine orchard, always encourages me."**
> 귤밭에서 일하시는 영철 할아버지는 항상 나를 격려해 주신다.

> **"My coach, who made my training plan, believed in me."**
> 훈련 계획을 짜주신 코치님은 나를 믿어 주셨다.

> **"The 42.195km race, which I had been preparing for a year, was finally here."**
> 1년간 준비해 온 42.195km 대회가 드디어 다가왔다.

> **"Jeju, where I live, is a beautiful island."**
> 내가 사는 제주는 아름다운 섬이다.

## D. Defining vs Non-defining 비교

### Side-by-Side Comparison:

| | Defining (한정적) | Non-defining (비한정적) |
|---|-------------------|-------------------------|
| 콤마 | 없음 | 있음 |
| 역할 | 필수 정보 | 추가 정보 |
| that | 사용 가능 | **사용 불가** |
| 생략 | 목적어일 때 가능 | **생략 불가** |
| 선행사 | 일반명사 | 고유명사, 특정명사 |

### Meaning Difference Examples:

**Defining:**
> "The friend **who helped me** was Minseo."
> 나를 도와준 친구는 민서였다. (어떤 친구인지 한정)

**Non-defining:**
> "Minseo**, who helped me,** is my best friend."
> 나를 도와준 민서는 나의 가장 친한 친구다. (추가 정보)

### From the Original Book:

| Defining | Non-defining |
|----------|--------------|
| My sister **who lives in London** (여러 자매 중 런던에 사는 자매) | My sister**, who lives in London** (자매가 한 명이고, 런던에 산다는 추가 정보) |

---

## E. Non-defining 관계절의 규칙

### Rule 1: that 사용 불가

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| Minseo, **who** helped me | ~~Minseo, **that** helped me~~ |
| The race, **which** was hard | ~~The race, **that** was hard~~ |

### Rule 2: 관계대명사 생략 불가

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| Minseo, **who** I met | ~~Minseo, I met~~ |
| The medal, **which** I received | ~~The medal, I received~~ |

### Rule 3: 고유명사 뒤에 주로 사용

고유명사(사람 이름, 지명 등)는 이미 특정되어 있으므로 비한정적 관계절을 사용합니다.

> **"Jeju, where I live, is a beautiful island."**
> 내가 사는 제주는 아름다운 섬이다.

> **"March 2nd, which was race day, finally arrived."**
> 대회 날인 3월 2일이 드디어 왔다.

> **"Minseo, who I've known for a year, is my best friend."**
> 1년간 알고 지낸 민서는 내 가장 친한 친구다.

## F. 의미 차이 (Meaning Changes)

같은 문장이라도 콤마의 유무에 따라 의미가 달라질 수 있습니다.

### Examples:

| 문장 | 의미 |
|------|------|
| My brother **who** lives in Seoul called. | 서울에 사는 형이 전화했다. (형이 여러 명 중 서울에 사는 형) |
| My brother**, who** lives in Seoul**,** called. | 서울에 사는 우리 형이 전화했다. (형이 한 명이고 추가 정보) |

| 문장 | 의미 |
|------|------|
| Students **who** study hard pass exams. | 열심히 공부하는 학생들은 시험에 붙는다. (일부 학생들) |
| Students**, who** study hard**,** pass exams. | 학생들은, 열심히 공부해서, 시험에 붙는다. (모든 학생들) |

---

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~Minseo, that helped me~~ | Minseo, **who** helped me | Non-defining에서 that 불가 |
| ~~Jeju, that is an island~~ | Jeju, **which** is an island | Non-defining에서 that 불가 |
| ~~My coach, I respect~~ | My coach, **whom** I respect | Non-defining에서 생략 불가 |
| ~~The race which was hard~~ (for a specific race) | The race, **which** was hard | 특정 대회면 콤마 필요 |
| ~~Seoul, where I live at~~ | Seoul, **where** I live | where에 at 불필요 |

## Exercises

### A. Defining인지 Non-defining인지 구분하세요 (Identify the type)

1. The coach who trained me is very experienced.
   → **Defining** (콤마 없음)

2. My mom, who was watching from the sidelines, cried.
   → **Non-defining** (콤마 있음)

3. The race that I completed was 42km.
   → **Defining** (콤마 없음)

4. Grandpa Yeongcheol, who is 70 years old, still works every day.
   → **Non-defining** (콤마 있음)

5. Students who run regularly have better stamina.
   → **Defining** (콤마 없음)

6. March 2nd, which was race day, finally came.
   → **Non-defining** (콤마 있음)

7. The bus that comes at 7:30 is always crowded.
   → **Defining** (콤마 없음)

8. Jeju, where I was born, is famous for tangerines.
   → **Non-defining** (콤마 있음)

---

### B. 알맞은 형태로 문장을 완성하세요 (Complete with who/which/that)

1. Minseo, _______ (who/that) I met on the first day, is my friend.
   → **who** (non-defining, that 불가)

2. The bus _______ (which/that) comes at 7:30 is packed.
   → **which/that** (defining, 둘 다 가능)

3. March 2nd, _______ (which/that) was race day, finally came.
   → **which** (non-defining, that 불가)

4. The medal _______ (which/that) I received is heavy.
   → **which/that** (defining, 둘 다 가능)

5. My coach, _______ (who/that) believes in me, helped a lot.
   → **who** (non-defining, that 불가)

6. Kids _______ (who/that) run in the morning are healthy.
   → **who/that** (defining, 둘 다 가능)

---

### C. 콤마가 필요한 곳에 콤마를 넣으세요 (Add commas where needed)

1. My coach who believes in me made my training plan.
   → **My coach, who believes in me, made my training plan.**

2. The bus that comes at 7:30 is always crowded.
   → (콤마 불필요 - defining)

3. Jeju where I was born is famous for tangerines.
   → **Jeju, where I was born, is famous for tangerines.**

4. The shoes which I bought yesterday are comfortable.
   → (콤마 불필요 - defining)

5. Minseo who became my best friend helped me a lot.
   → **Minseo, who became my best friend, helped me a lot.**

6. March 2nd which was the marathon day finally arrived.
   → **March 2nd, which was the marathon day, finally arrived.**

---

### D. 틀린 부분 고치기 (Error correction)

1. Minseo, that I met on the first day, is my friend.
   → Minseo, **who** I met on the first day, is my friend.

2. My brother, I talked to, is a runner.
   → My brother, **whom/who** I talked to, is a runner.

3. The 42km race, that was exhausting, is over.
   → The 42km race, **which** was exhausting, is over.

4. Seoul, where I visited last year at, is beautiful.
   → Seoul, **which I visited last year**, is beautiful.

5. Grandfather Yeongcheol, that works at the orchard, waved at me.
   → Grandfather Yeongcheol, **who** works at the orchard, waved at me.

### Decision Table:

| | Defining | Non-defining |
|---|----------|--------------|

| 생략 | 목적어일 때 가능 | **불가** |
| 역할 | 한정 (필수) | 추가 정보 |
| 선행사 | 일반명사 | 고유명사, 특정명사 |

## Summary

### When to use which type:

| 상황 | 관계절 유형 | 예시 |
|------|-------------|------|
| 어떤 것인지 특정해야 할 때 | Defining | The bus that comes at 7:30 |
| 고유명사 뒤 | Non-defining | Minseo, who is my friend |
| 추가 정보 제공 | Non-defining | My coach, who trained me |
| 일반명사를 한정할 때 | Defining | Students who study hard |
