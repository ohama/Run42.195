# Unit 21: Will/Shall (1)

## A. 형태 (Form)

**Will**은 미래를 나타내는 가장 기본적인 조동사입니다.

### 긍정문 (Affirmative)

| Subject | Will | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|------|---------|
| I/You/He/She/It/We/They | will ('ll) | run | I'll run tomorrow. |

### 부정문 (Negative)

| Subject | Will not | Verb | Example |
|---------|----------|------|---------|
| I/You/He/She/It/We/They | will not (won't) | give up | I won't give up. |

### 의문문 (Questions)

| Will | Subject | Verb | Example |
|------|---------|------|---------|
| Will | you | help? | Will you help me? |
| Will | she | come? | Will she come? |

### 축약형 (Contractions)

| Full form | Contraction | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| I will | I'll | I'll be there. |
| You will | You'll | You'll do great. |
| He will | He'll | He'll finish. |
| She will | She'll | She'll run. |
| It will | It'll | It'll rain. |
| We will | We'll | We'll meet at 7. |
| They will | They'll | They'll come. |
| will not | won't | I won't stop. |

## B. 예측 (Predictions)

미래에 일어날 것으로 **생각하는** 일을 표현합니다.

### From the Original Book:

> "I **think** it **will** rain later."
> 나중에 비가 올 것 같아.

> "You **won't** pass the exam if you don't study."
> 공부 안 하면 시험 못 붙을 거야.

> "Do you think she **will** come to the party?"
> 그녀가 파티에 올 것 같아?

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I think I'll feel better soon."**
> 곧 나아질 것 같아.

- 미래에 대한 예측

> **"You'll do great."**
> 잘 할 거야.

- 친구의 격려/예측

> **"It'll be hard, but I'll manage."**
> 힘들겠지만 해낼 거야.

### Common Expressions with Predictions:

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| I think... | I **think** you**'ll** pass. |
| I'm sure... | I**'m sure** it**'ll** be fine. |
| I expect... | I **expect** he**'ll** finish. |
| I wonder... | I **wonder** if it**'ll** rain. |
| probably | He**'ll probably** be late. |

## C. 즉석 결정 (Spontaneous Decisions)

**말하는 순간 결정**하는 일을 표현합니다.

### From the Original Book:

> "A: There's no milk."
> "B: Oh, I didn't know. I**'ll** go and get some."
> 우유가 없네. - 오, 몰랐어. 가서 사올게.

> "A: The phone's ringing."
> "B: I**'ll** answer it."
> 전화 왔어. - 내가 받을게.

> "I'm tired. I think I**'ll** go to bed early tonight."
> 피곤해. 오늘 일찍 잘게.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I'll take these."** (running shoes)
> 이걸로 할게요.

- 가게에서 신발을 보고 그 자리에서 결정

> **"I'll try running to school tomorrow."**
> 내일 학교까지 달려볼게.

- 방금 결정한 것

### Key Point:
**Will**은 말하는 **순간** 결정할 때 사용합니다.
- "be going to"와의 차이: be going to는 **이미** 결정한 것

---

## D. 약속과 제안 (Promises and Offers)

### Promises (약속):

> **"I'll be waiting at the finish line."**
> 결승선에서 기다릴게.

> **"I won't tell anyone."**
> 아무에게도 말 안 할게.

> **"I'll call you tomorrow."**
> 내일 전화할게.

### Offers (제안):

> **"I'll make pasta for dinner."**
> 저녁으로 파스타 만들어 줄게.

> **"I'll help you with your stretching."**
> 스트레칭 도와줄게.

> **"I'll carry your bag."**
> 가방 들어줄게.

### Common Offer Expressions:

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| I'll... | I'll help you. |
| I'll get... | I'll get you some water. |
| Shall I...? | Shall I help? |
| I'll do... | I'll do it for you. |

---

## E. Shall의 용법 (Shall)

현대 영어에서 **shall**은 주로 다음 상황에서 사용됩니다.

### 제안 (Suggestions):

> "**Shall** I help you?"
> 도와줄까?

> "**Shall** I open the window?"
> 창문 열어줄까?

### 함께 하자고 할 때:

> "**Shall** we run together?"
> 같이 달릴까?

> "**Shall** we take a break?"
> 쉴까?

### Shall I vs Shall we:

| Shall I...? | Shall we...? |
|-------------|--------------|
| 내가 해줄까? | 우리 같이 할까? |
| Shall I carry that? | Shall we go? |
| Shall I get you water? | Shall we rest? |

---

## F. 미래의 확실한 사실 (Facts about the Future)

확실히 일어날 미래의 사실:

> **"Tomorrow will be Sunday."**
> 내일은 일요일이야.

> **"The race will be 42.195km."**
> 대회는 42.195km야.

> **"Next year, I'll be 15."**
> 내년에 15살이 돼.

---

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I will to help you.~~ | I**'ll help** you. | will + 동사원형 (to 없음) |
| ~~He wills run.~~ | He **will run**. | will은 변하지 않음 |
| ~~I'll going to run.~~ | I'll run. / I'm going to run. | will과 be going to 같이 안 씀 |
| ~~Do you will come?~~ | **Will** you come? | Will이 맨 앞으로 |
| ~~She will comes.~~ | She **will come**. | will + 동사원형 (s 없음) |
| ~~I amn't will.~~ | I **won't**. | 부정은 won't |

### Will vs Be going to 헷갈리는 상황:

| 상황 | Will | Be going to |
|------|------|-------------|
| 지금 결정 | I'll help you. | X |
| 이미 결정 | X | I'm going to help him. |
| 일반 예측 | I think it'll rain. | O (둘 다 가능) |
| 증거 기반 예측 | X | Look! It's going to rain. |
| 약속 | I'll be there. | X |

## Exercises

### A. 빈칸 채우기 (Fill in the blanks)

Use will ('ll) or won't.

1. Don't worry. You _______ (do) great.
2. "Which shoes?" - "I _______ (take) these."
3. I _______ (be) at the finish line.
4. I think it _______ (rain) tomorrow.
5. _______ I help you with that?
6. I promise I _______ (not/forget).
7. He _______ (probably/be) late.
8. I'm sure you _______ (pass) the exam.

**Answers:**
1. will do / 'll do
2. will take / 'll take
3. will be / 'll be
4. will rain / 'll rain
5. Shall
6. won't forget
7. will probably be / 'll probably be
8. will pass / 'll pass

### B. 문장 변환 (Sentence Transformation)

1. 긍정 → 부정: I'll run tomorrow.
   → I **won't run** tomorrow.

2. 긍정 → 의문: You will finish the race.
   → **Will you finish** the race?

3. 제안하기: help you
   → **Shall I help you?** / **I'll help you.**

4. 부정 → 긍정: She won't come.
   → She **will come**.

5. 예측 만들기: (rain / tomorrow / I think)
   → **I think it'll rain tomorrow.**

### C. Will vs Be going to 선택 (Choose the correct form)

1. "I've decided. I _______ (run) the marathon." - 이미 결정
   → **am going to run**

2. "Oh no, I forgot my water! I _______ (go) get it." - 지금 결정
   → **will go / 'll go**

3. "Look at those clouds! It _______ (rain)." - 증거 기반
   → **is going to rain**

4. "I think you _______ (do) great." - 일반 예측
   → **will do** (또는 are going to do)

5. "Don't worry, I _______ (help) you." - 약속/제안
   → **will help / 'll help**

### D. 상황에 맞는 응답 만들기 (Make appropriate responses)

1. "The phone's ringing."
   → I**'ll answer it**.

2. "I'm thirsty."
   → I**'ll get you some water**.

3. "It's cold in here."
   → I**'ll close the window**.

4. "I can't carry all these bags."
   → I**'ll help you**.

## Will vs Be going to vs Present Continuous

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Will | 즉석 결정, 예측, 약속 | I'll help you. |
| Be going to | 이미 결정한 계획, 증거 기반 예측 | I'm going to run. |
| Present Continuous | 확정된 약속 | I'm meeting him at 7. |
