# Unit 94: Relative Clauses (3) - whose/whom/where

## A. whose - 소유를 나타내는 관계대명사

**whose**는 "~의"라는 소유 관계를 나타냅니다. 사람과 사물 모두에 사용할 수 있습니다.

**whose** = of whom / of which (소유격)

| 선행사 | whose + 명사 |
|--------|--------------|
| 사람 | the runner **whose** shoes... |
| 사물 | the bus **whose** doors... |

---

## B. whose 예문 (Examples with whose)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Minseo, whose family came to cheer, was at the 10km mark."**
> 가족이 응원하러 온 민서가 10km 지점에 있었다.

- Minseo = 선행사
- whose family = 민서의 가족

> **"The grandfather whose tangerine orchard I pass every day always waves at me."**
> 내가 매일 지나가는 귤밭의 할아버지가 항상 손을 흔들어 주신다.

> **"The runner whose pace was steady finished strong."**
> 페이스가 일정했던 달리기 선수가 강하게 끝냈다.

> **"The school whose track team I joined is in Jeju City."**
> 내가 들어간 육상부가 있는 학교는 제주시에 있다.

### Pattern:
```
명사 + whose + 명사 + 동사
       (소유격)
```

---

## C. whom - 격식체 목적격 (Formal object form)

**whom**은 **who**의 목적격으로, 격식체에서 사용됩니다. 구어체에서는 who나 생략을 더 많이 씁니다.

| 격식 (Formal) | 비격식 (Informal) |
|---------------|-------------------|
| The friend **whom** I met | The friend **who** I met |
| The friend **whom** I met | The friend I met |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The coach, whom the school hired, helped me a lot."**
> 학교에서 고용한 코치가 나를 많이 도와주었다.

> **"Minseo, whom I met on the first day, became my best friend."**
> 첫날 만난 민서가 나의 가장 친한 친구가 되었다.

### 전치사 + whom:

전치사 뒤에는 반드시 **whom**을 사용합니다.

> **"The friend with whom I run every Saturday is Minseo."**
> 매주 토요일 함께 달리는 친구는 민서다.

> **"The teacher to whom I talked was very helpful."**
> 내가 이야기한 선생님은 매우 도움이 되셨다.

---

## D. where - 장소를 나타내는 관계부사

**where**는 장소를 나타내는 선행사 뒤에 사용됩니다.

where = in which / at which / to which (장소)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus stop where I wait every morning is near my house."**
> 내가 매일 아침 기다리는 버스 정류장은 집 근처에 있다.

- the bus stop = 선행사 (장소)
- where I wait = 관계절

> **"The tangerine orchard where Grandfather Yeongcheol works is on my route."**
> 영철 할아버지가 일하시는 귤밭은 내 통학길에 있다.

> **"The school where I became a runner is in Jeju City."**
> 내가 달리기 선수가 된 학교는 제주시에 있다.

> **"The finish line where I collapsed was the happiest place."**
> 내가 쓰러진 결승선이 가장 행복한 곳이었다.

### Pattern:
```
장소명사 + where + 주어 + 동사
```

---

## E. where vs which/that + 전치사

| where | which/that + 전치사 |
|-------|---------------------|
| the place **where** I ran | the place **which** I ran **at** |
| the school **where** I study | the school **that** I study **at** |

### Examples:

> **"The park where I run"** = **"The park which I run in"**
> 내가 달리는 공원

> **"The bench where I rest"** = **"The bench that I rest on"**
> 내가 쉬는 벤치

---

## F. 비교: whose vs who's

| whose | who's |
|-------|-------|
| 소유격 관계대명사 | who is / who has 줄임 |
| The runner **whose** shoes... | **Who's** running today? |
| ~의 | ~은/는 누구? |

## Exercises

### A. whose, whom, where 중 알맞은 것을 고르세요.

1. The runner _______ pace was steady won the race.
2. The bus stop _______ I wait is near the tangerine orchard.
3. The coach, _______ the school hired, is excellent.
4. The friend _______ family came to cheer was Minseo.
5. The school _______ I study has a good track team.

**Answers:**
1. whose
2. where
3. whom
4. whose
5. where

### B. 두 문장을 한 문장으로 만드세요.

1. I met a runner. His shoes were very professional.
   → I met a runner **whose shoes were very professional**.

2. This is the park. I run here every morning.
   → This is the park **where I run every morning**.

3. The teacher was helpful. I talked to her.
   → The teacher **to whom I talked** was helpful.
   → The teacher **(whom/who) I talked to** was helpful.

4. I passed the orchard. Grandfather works there.
   → I passed the orchard **where Grandfather works**.

5. Minseo is my friend. His family came to cheer.
   → Minseo, **whose family came to cheer**, is my friend.

### C. 올바른 문장을 고르세요.

1. a) The place where I ran was beautiful.
   b) The place whose I ran was beautiful.
   **Answer: a**

2. a) The runner who's shoes were red won.
   b) The runner whose shoes were red won.
   **Answer: b**

3. a) The friend whom I met was Minseo.
   b) The friend whose I met was Minseo.
   **Answer: a**
