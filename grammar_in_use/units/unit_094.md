# Unit 94: Relative Clauses (3) - whose/whom/where

## A. whose - 소유를 나타내는 관계대명사

**whose**는 "~의"라는 소유 관계를 나타냅니다. 사람과 사물 모두에 사용할 수 있습니다.

### Form (형태):

**whose** = of whom / of which (소유격)

| 선행사 | whose + 명사 | 의미 |
|--------|--------------|------|
| 사람 | the runner **whose** shoes... | 그 주자의 신발이... |
| 사물 | the bus **whose** doors... | 그 버스의 문이... |

### Structure:

```
명사 + whose + 명사 + 동사
Minseo, whose family came...
   ↑      ↑      ↑
선행사  소유격  소유물
```

### From the Original Book:

> "We saw some people **whose** car had broken down."
> 우리는 차가 고장난 사람들을 봤다.

> "What's the name of the man **whose** car you borrowed?"
> 네가 차를 빌린 그 남자 이름이 뭐야?

---

## B. whose 예문 (Examples with whose)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Minseo, whose family came to cheer, was at the 10km mark."**
> 가족이 응원하러 온 민서가 10km 지점에 있었다.

- Minseo = 선행사
- whose family = 민서의 가족

> **"The grandfather whose tangerine orchard I pass every day always waves at me."**
> 내가 매일 지나가는 귤밭의 할아버지가 항상 손을 흔들어 주신다.

> **"The runner whose pace was steady finished strong."**
> 페이스가 일정했던 달리기 선수가 강하게 끝냈다.

> **"The school whose track team I joined is in Jeju City."**
> 내가 들어간 육상부가 있는 학교는 제주시에 있다.

> **"The coach whose training plan I followed helped me improve."**
> 내가 따랐던 훈련 계획을 세운 코치가 나를 발전시켜 주셨다.

> **"The boy whose dream was to finish a marathon finally did it."**
> 마라톤 완주가 꿈이었던 소년이 드디어 해냈다.

---

## C. whom - 격식체 목적격 (Formal object form)

**whom**은 **who**의 목적격으로, 격식체에서 사용됩니다. 구어체에서는 who나 생략을 더 많이 씁니다.

### Comparison Table:

| 격식 (Formal) | 비격식 (Informal) | 구어체 (Spoken) |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| The friend **whom** I met | The friend **who** I met | The friend I met |
| The teacher **to whom** I spoke | The teacher **who** I spoke to | The teacher I spoke to |

### From the Original Book:

> "The woman with **whom** he fell in love left him." (formal)
> "The woman **who** he fell in love with left him." (informal)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The coach, whom the school hired, helped me a lot."**
> 학교에서 고용한 코치가 나를 많이 도와주었다.

> **"Minseo, whom I met on the first day, became my best friend."**
> 첫날 만난 민서가 나의 가장 친한 친구가 되었다.

> **"The grandfather, whom I see every morning, always encourages me."**
> 매일 아침 만나는 할아버지가 항상 나를 격려해 주신다.

---

## D. 전치사 + whom (Preposition + whom)

전치사 뒤에는 반드시 **whom**을 사용합니다 (격식체).

### Pattern Comparison:

| Formal (격식) | Informal (비격식) |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **with whom** I run | who I run **with** |
| **to whom** I talked | who I talked **to** |
| **for whom** I ran | who I ran **for** |

### From the Original Book:

> "The people **with whom** I work are very friendly." (formal)
> "The people **who** I work with are very friendly." (informal)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The friend with whom I run every Saturday is Minseo."**
> 매주 토요일 함께 달리는 친구는 민서다.

> **"The teacher to whom I talked was very helpful."**
> 내가 이야기한 선생님은 매우 도움이 되셨다.

> **"The coach for whom I have great respect trained me."**
> 내가 큰 존경심을 가진 코치님이 나를 훈련시켜 주셨다.

---

## E. where - 장소를 나타내는 관계부사

**where**는 장소를 나타내는 선행사 뒤에 사용됩니다.

### Form:

where = in which / at which / to which (장소)

### Structure:

```
장소명사 + where + 주어 + 동사
The park   where   I     run
```

### From the Original Book:

> "I recently went back to the town **where** I was born."
> 최근에 내가 태어난 도시로 돌아갔다.

> "I would like to live in a place **where** there is plenty of sunshine."
> 햇살이 풍부한 곳에서 살고 싶다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus stop where I wait every morning is near my house."**
> 내가 매일 아침 기다리는 버스 정류장은 집 근처에 있다.

- the bus stop = 선행사 (장소)
- where I wait = 관계절

> **"The tangerine orchard where Grandfather Yeongcheol works is on my route."**
> 영철 할아버지가 일하시는 귤밭은 내 통학길에 있다.

> **"The school where I became a runner is in Jeju City."**
> 내가 달리기 선수가 된 학교는 제주시에 있다.

> **"The finish line where I collapsed was the happiest place."**
> 내가 쓰러진 결승선이 가장 행복한 곳이었다.

> **"The bench where I always rest is near the gate."**
> 내가 항상 쉬는 벤치는 정문 근처에 있다.

---

## F. where vs which/that + 전치사

### Comparison:

| where | which/that + 전치사 |
|-------|---------------------|
| the place **where** I ran | the place **which** I ran **at** |
| the school **where** I study | the school **that** I study **at** |
| the park **where** I run | the park **(that)** I run **in** |

### Examples:

> **"The park where I run"** = **"The park which I run in"**
> 내가 달리는 공원

> **"The bench where I rest"** = **"The bench that I rest on"**
> 내가 쉬는 벤치

> **"The school where I study"** = **"The school (that) I study at"**
> 내가 공부하는 학교

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~The runner who's shoes~~ | The runner **whose** shoes | who's = who is (소유 아님) |
| ~~The place whose I ran~~ | The place **where** I ran | 장소에는 where 사용 |
| ~~The friend whose I met~~ | The friend **whom/who** I met | whose = 소유격, whom = 목적격 |
| ~~The school where I study at~~ | The school **where** I study | where에 at 불필요 |
| ~~The man whom helped me~~ | The man **who** helped me | 주어 역할에는 who |
| ~~To who I talked~~ | **To whom** I talked | 전치사 뒤에는 whom |

### whose vs who's 구분:

| whose | who's |
|-------|-------|
| 소유격 관계대명사 | who is / who has 줄임 |
| The runner **whose** shoes... | **Who's** running today? |
| ~의 | ~은/는 누구? / ~가 ...했다 |

## Exercises

### A. whose, whom, where 중 알맞은 것을 고르세요 (Fill in the blanks)

1. The runner _______ pace was steady won the race.
2. The bus stop _______ I wait is near the tangerine orchard.
3. The coach, _______ the school hired, is excellent.
4. The friend _______ family came to cheer was Minseo.
5. The school _______ I study has a good track team.
6. The boy _______ dream was to finish a marathon succeeded.
7. The teacher _______ I respect most encouraged me.
8. The park _______ I run every day is beautiful.

**Answers:**
1. whose
2. where
3. whom
4. whose
5. where
6. whose
7. whom (or who)
8. where

---

### B. 두 문장을 한 문장으로 만드세요 (Combine sentences)

1. I met a runner. His shoes were very professional.
   → I met a runner **whose shoes were very professional**.

2. This is the park. I run here every morning.
   → This is the park **where I run every morning**.

3. The teacher was helpful. I talked to her.
   → The teacher **to whom I talked** was helpful.
   → The teacher **(whom/who) I talked to** was helpful.

4. I passed the orchard. Grandfather works there.
   → I passed the orchard **where Grandfather works**.

5. Minseo is my friend. His family came to cheer.
   → Minseo, **whose family came to cheer**, is my friend.

6. The coach believed in me. I learned a lot from him.
   → The coach **from whom I learned a lot** believed in me.

7. The boy finished the marathon. His dream was to become a runner.
   → The boy **whose dream was to become a runner** finished the marathon.

---

### C. 올바른 문장을 고르세요 (Choose the correct sentence)

1. a) The place where I ran was beautiful.
   b) The place whose I ran was beautiful.
   **Answer: a** (장소에는 where)

2. a) The runner who's shoes were red won.
   b) The runner whose shoes were red won.
   **Answer: b** (소유격에는 whose)

3. a) The friend whom I met was Minseo.
   b) The friend whose I met was Minseo.
   **Answer: a** (목적격에는 whom/who)

4. a) The school where I study at is great.
   b) The school where I study is great.
   **Answer: b** (where에 at 불필요)

5. a) The man whom helped me was kind.
   b) The man who helped me was kind.
   **Answer: b** (주어 역할에는 who)

---

### D. 격식체와 비격식체로 바꾸세요 (Change formality)

**Formal → Informal:**
1. The friend with whom I run is Minseo.
   → The friend **who I run with** is Minseo.

2. The teacher to whom I spoke was helpful.
   → The teacher **who I spoke to** was helpful.

3. The coach from whom I learned a lot believed in me.
   → The coach **who I learned a lot from** believed in me.

**Informal → Formal:**
4. The person who I talked to was my coach.
   → The person **to whom I talked** was my coach.

5. The friend who I train with is from Jeju.
   → The friend **with whom I train** is from Jeju.

## Summary Table

### Quick Reference:

| 관계사 | 역할 | 선행사 | 예시 |
|--------|------|--------|------|
| **whose** | 소유 (~의) | 사람/사물 | the friend **whose** family... |
| **whom** | 목적격 (격식) | 사람 | the teacher **to whom** I talked |
| **where** | 장소 | 장소명사 | the place **where** I run |

### Formality Levels:

| 격식 수준 | whose | whom | where |
|-----------|-------|------|-------|
| Formal | ✓ | with whom, to whom | ✓ |
| Neutral | ✓ | who... with/to | ✓ |
| Informal | ✓ | 생략 가능 | 생략 가능 |

### whose vs who's vs whom Comparison:

| 단어 | 역할 | 예시 |
|------|------|------|
| whose | 소유격 (누구의) | The man **whose** car... |
| who's | who is / who has | **Who's** coming? |
| whom | 목적격 (누구를) | The man **whom** I met |
