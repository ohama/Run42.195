# Unit 11: How long have you (been)...?

## A. 문법 설명 (Grammar Explanation)

### English
To ask about **duration** (how long something has been happening), we use:
- **How long have you + past participle?** (Simple)
- **How long have you been + -ing?** (Continuous)

Both ask about the time from the past until now.

### Korean
**기간**을 묻는 질문 (얼마나 오래 계속되었는지):
- **How long have you + 과거분사?** (단순형)
- **How long have you been + -ing?** (진행형)

두 형태 모두 과거부터 현재까지의 시간을 묻습니다.

---

## B. 기본 구조 (Basic Structures)

### Present Perfect Simple:
> **"How long have you lived in Jeju?"**
> 제주에서 얼마나 오래 살았어?

### Present Perfect Continuous:
> **"How long have you been running?"**
> 얼마나 오래 달려왔어?

### 대답 방식:
- **For + 기간:** for three months, for a year
- **Since + 시점:** since March, since I started school

---

## C. 소설 속 예문 (Examples from the Novel)

### Example 1: 달리기 기간

**Question:**
> **"How long have you been running?"**
> 얼마나 오래 달려왔어?

**Answer:**
> **"I've been running since March."**
> 3월부터 달려왔어.

or

> **"I've been running for six months."**
> 6개월 동안 달려왔어.

### Example 2: 걷기 시작 시점

**Question:**
> **"How long have you been walking to school?"**
> 걸어서 학교 다닌 지 얼마나 됐어?

**Answer:**
> **"Since I missed the bus in March."**
> 3월에 버스 놓친 이후로.

### Example 3: 훈련 기간

**Question:**
> **"How long have you been training for the marathon?"**
> 마라톤 훈련한 지 얼마나 됐어?

**Answer:**
> **"For about a year."**
> 약 1년 됐어.

### Example 4: 상태의 기간

**Question:**
> **"How long have you known Minseo?"**
> 민서를 얼마나 오래 알았어?

**Answer:**
> **"Since the first day of middle school."**
> 중학교 첫날부터.

### Example 5: 대화에서

> **PE Teacher: "Have you been running a lot lately?"**
> **Doyun: "Yes, since March."**
> **Teacher: "So it's been three months."**

## D. For vs Since

| For | Since |
|-----|-------|
| + 기간 | + 시점 |
| **for** three months | **since** March |
| **for** a year | **since** last year |
| **for** two hours | **since** 8 o'clock |
| **for** a long time | **since** I was young |

### 소설 속 예문:

> **"I've been running for six months."** (기간)
> 6개월 동안 달려왔어.

> **"I've been running since March."** (시점)
> 3월부터 달려왔어.

---

## E. 대화 패턴 (Conversation Patterns)

### Pattern 1: How long + have been -ing?

```
A: How long have you been running?
B: For about six months.
A: Since when exactly?
B: Since mid-March.
```

### Pattern 2: How long + have + p.p.?

```
A: How long have you known each other?
B: Since the first day of school.
A: So, for about a year?
B: Yeah, about a year.
```

### Pattern 3: 소설 속 대화

```
Teacher: "Have you been practicing on your own?"
Doyun: "I walk to school... actually, I run."
Teacher: "Since when?"
Doyun: "I started walking in March, then started running in May."
Teacher: "So it's been three months."
```

## F. 시간 표현 총정리

### For + 기간:
- for a few days (며칠)
- for two weeks (2주)
- for three months (3개월)
- for a year (1년)
- for a long time (오랫동안)
- for ages (아주 오랫동안)

### Since + 시점:
- since yesterday (어제부터)
- since last week (지난주부터)
- since March (3월부터)
- since 2024 (2024년부터)
- since I was young (어렸을 때부터)
- since I started school (학교 다니기 시작한 이후로)

## Exercises

### A. For or Since?

1. I've been running _______ March.
2. I've been training _______ six months.
3. She's lived here _______ 2020.
4. He's known her _______ a long time.
5. I've been waiting _______ 8 o'clock.

**Answers:**
1. since (시점)
2. for (기간)
3. since (시점)
4. for (기간)
5. since (시점)

### B. 질문 만들기

Make "How long" questions:

1. I've been running. → How long _______?
2. She's lived in Jeju. → How long _______?
3. They've been waiting. → How long _______?
4. He's known about the race. → How long _______?

**Answers:**
1. have you been running
2. has she lived in Jeju
3. have they been waiting
4. has he known about the race

### C. 대화 완성하기

Complete the conversations:

1. A: How long have you been walking to school?
   B: _______ March. (시점)

2. A: How long have you known Grandpa?
   B: _______ about six months. (기간)

3. A: _______ have you been training?
   B: Since I decided to run the marathon.

**Answers:**
1. Since
2. For
3. How long

### D. 문장 변환

Change from "for" to "since" (or vice versa):

1. I've been running for six months.
   → I've been running _______ March. (if it's now September)

2. She's lived here since 2020.
   → She's lived here _______ four years. (if it's now 2024)

3. I've known him for a year.
   → I've known him _______ last year.

**Answers:**
1. since March
2. for four years
3. since last year

## Key Sentences (암기 문장)

| Question | Answer (Since) | Answer (For) |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| How long have you been running? | Since March. | For six months. |
| How long have you been walking to school? | Since I missed the bus. | For about a month. |
| How long have you known Minseo? | Since the first day. | For a year. |
| How long has it been raining? | Since morning. | For two hours. |
| How long have you lived in Jeju? | Since I was born. | For 14 years. |

## 주의사항 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| ~~How long are you running?~~ | How long have you been running? |
| ~~Since three months~~ | For three months |
| ~~For March~~ | Since March |
| ~~How long you have been waiting?~~ | How long have you been waiting? |
