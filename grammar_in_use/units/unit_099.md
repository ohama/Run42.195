# Unit 99: Adjectives - a nice new house, you look tired

## A. 형용사의 위치 (Position of adjectives)

영어에서 형용사는 두 가지 위치에 올 수 있습니다:

### Two Positions:

| 위치 | 영어 용어 | 설명 | 예시 |
|------|-----------|------|------|
| **명사 앞** | Attributive | 명사를 직접 수식 | a **new** uniform |
| **동사 뒤** | Predicative | be/look/feel 등 뒤에 | The uniform is **new**. |

### From the Original Book:

> "Tom lives in a **nice** house." (before noun)
> "Tom's house is **nice**." (after verb)

> "I met a **famous** actor." (before noun)
> "That actor is **famous**." (after verb)

---

## B. 명사 앞의 형용사 (Adjectives before nouns)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I put on my new uniform."**
> 새 교복을 입었다.

> **"Cold air filled my lungs."**
> 차가운 공기가 폐를 채웠다.

> **"The familiar route was comforting."**
> 익숙한 길이 위안이 됐다.

> **"I bought blue running shoes."**
> 파란색 런닝화를 샀다.

> **"It was a beautiful spring morning."**
> 아름다운 봄 아침이었다.

> **"The long distance seemed impossible."**
> 긴 거리가 불가능해 보였다.

---

## C. 동사 뒤의 형용사 (Adjectives after linking verbs)

**look, feel, seem, sound, smell, taste, appear, become, get** 같은 연결동사 뒤에 형용사가 옵니다.

### From the Original Book:

> "You **look** tired." (NOT: ~~tiredly~~)
> "Be careful!" "I **am** careful."
> "The soup **tastes** nice." (NOT: ~~nicely~~)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Hey, you look pale again."**
> 야, 너 또 창백해 보인다.

> **"The morning air felt fresh."**
> 아침 공기가 상쾌하게 느껴졌다.

> **"It seemed impossible at first."**
> 처음에는 불가능해 보였다.

> **"That sounds amazing."**
> 그거 대단하게 들린다.

> **"I felt good after the run."**
> 달리기 후에 기분이 좋았다.

> **"The seaweed soup smelled delicious."**
> 미역국이 맛있는 냄새가 났다.

### 주의: 부사가 아닌 형용사 사용!

| 올바름 | 틀림 | 설명 |
|--------|------|------|
| You look **tired**. | ~~You look tiredly.~~ | 연결동사 뒤는 형용사 |
| I feel **good**. | ~~I feel well.~~ (건강 의미 제외) | feel + 형용사 |
| It sounds **nice**. | ~~It sounds nicely.~~ | 연결동사 뒤는 형용사 |
| The food smells **good**. | ~~The food smells goodly.~~ | 연결동사 뒤는 형용사 |

---

## D. 형용사 순서 (Order of adjectives)

여러 형용사가 명사 앞에 올 때는 일반적인 순서가 있습니다:

### OSASCOMP Order:

**Opinion - Size - Age - Shape - Color - Origin - Material - Purpose + Noun**

| 순서 | 종류 | 예시 |
|------|------|------|
| 1 | 의견 (Opinion) | nice, beautiful, ugly, lovely |
| 2 | 크기 (Size) | big, small, tall, short |
| 3 | 나이 (Age) | new, old, young, ancient |
| 4 | 모양 (Shape) | round, flat, square, long |
| 5 | 색깔 (Color) | blue, red, yellow, green |
| 6 | 출신 (Origin) | Korean, Japanese, American |
| 7 | 재료 (Material) | leather, cotton, wooden |
| 8 | 목적 (Purpose) | running, sleeping, cooking |

### From the Original Book:

> "a **nice** **long** summer holiday" (opinion + age/length)
> "a **big** **black** dog" (size + color)
> "a **beautiful** **old** Italian clock" (opinion + age + origin)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I bought nice new blue running shoes."**
> 멋진 새 파란색 런닝화를 샀다.

- nice (의견) + new (나이) + blue (색깔) + running (목적) + shoes

> **"It was a beautiful clear spring morning."**
> 아름답고 맑은 봄 아침이었다.

> **"The old tangerine orchard was on my route."**
> 오래된 귤밭이 내 통학길에 있었다.

> **"I wore comfortable Korean cotton shorts."**
> 편한 한국산 면 반바지를 입었다.

---

## E. -ly로 끝나는 형용사 (Adjectives ending in -ly)

일부 형용사는 -ly로 끝납니다 (부사 아님!):

### Common -ly Adjectives:

| 형용사 | 의미 |
|--------|------|
| friendly | 친절한 |
| lonely | 외로운 |
| lovely | 사랑스러운 |
| lively | 활기찬 |
| elderly | 나이 든 |
| silly | 어리석은 |
| ugly | 못생긴 |
| likely | ~할 것 같은 |
| deadly | 치명적인 |
| costly | 비싼 |

### From the Original Book:

> "She has a **friendly** face."
> "It was a **lonely** place."

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The elderly grandfather always waved at me."**
> 나이 드신 할아버지가 항상 손을 흔들어 주셨다.

> **"It was a lovely morning for a run."**
> 달리기에 좋은 아침이었다.

> **"The lively atmosphere at the race was exciting."**
> 대회의 활기찬 분위기가 신났다.

> **"He gave me a friendly smile."**
> 그가 나에게 친절한 미소를 지어 주었다.

---

## F. 형용사만 쓰이는 위치 (Adjectives with restricted positions)

일부 형용사는 특정 위치에서만 사용됩니다:

### 명사 앞에서만 (Only attributive):

| 형용사 | 예시 |
|--------|------|
| main | the **main** reason |
| chief | the **chief** cause |
| only | the **only** way |
| elder | my **elder** brother |
| former | the **former** president |
| indoor/outdoor | **indoor** swimming |

> **"The main reason I run is for health."**
> 내가 달리는 주된 이유는 건강 때문이다.

> **"The only problem was the heat."**
> 유일한 문제는 더위였다.

### 동사 뒤에서만 (Only predicative):

| 형용사 | 예시 |
|--------|------|
| afraid | I was **afraid**. |
| alive | He is still **alive**. |
| alone | She was **alone**. |
| asleep | The baby is **asleep**. |
| awake | Are you **awake**? |
| ill | He felt **ill**. |
| well | I feel **well**. (건강) |

### From the Novel:

> **"I was afraid of failing."**
> 실패가 두려웠다.

> **"My sister was still asleep."**
> 여동생은 아직 자고 있었다.

> **"I was wide awake before the alarm."**
> 알람 전에 완전히 깨어 있었다.

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~He looks tiredly~~ | He looks **tired** | 연결동사 뒤는 형용사 |
| ~~I feel happily today~~ | I feel **happy** today | feel + 형용사 |
| ~~The food smells deliciously~~ | The food smells **delicious** | smell + 형용사 |
| ~~a new nice house~~ | a **nice new** house | 의견 → 나이 순서 |
| ~~a red big car~~ | a **big red** car | 크기 → 색깔 순서 |
| ~~an afraid boy~~ | a boy **who was afraid** | afraid는 명사 앞 불가 |
| ~~the asleep baby~~ | the **sleeping** baby | asleep은 명사 앞 불가 |

## Exercises

### A. 형용사의 위치가 올바른지 확인하세요 (Correct the errors)

1. He looks tiredly. → **tired** (연결동사 뒤 = 형용사)
2. I feel happily today. → **happy**
3. The food smells deliciously. → **delicious**
4. She seems nicely. → **nice**
5. It sounds interestingly. → **interesting**
6. The soup tastes wonderfully. → **wonderful**
7. You appear sadly today. → **sad**
8. The flower smells sweetly. → **sweet**

---

### B. 형용사를 올바른 순서로 배열하세요 (Arrange in correct order)

1. shoes (blue, new, running)
   → **new blue running** shoes

2. morning (spring, beautiful, clear)
   → **beautiful clear spring** morning

3. house (old, big, Korean)
   → **big old Korean** house

4. orchard (tangerine, small, lovely)
   → **lovely small tangerine** orchard

5. car (red, Italian, expensive)
   → **expensive red Italian** car

6. table (wooden, round, old)
   → **old round wooden** table

7. dress (silk, long, beautiful, white)
   → **beautiful long white silk** dress

8. bag (leather, small, black)
   → **small black leather** bag

---

### C. 빈칸에 알맞은 형용사를 넣으세요 (Fill in the blanks)

1. You look _______ (tire) today.
   → **tired**

2. The _______ (cold) air felt refreshing.
   → **cold**

3. I bought _______ (new) _______ (blue) running shoes.
   → **new blue**

4. The race seemed _______ (impossible) at first.
   → **impossible**

5. The _______ (elderly) grandfather waved at me.
   → **elderly**

6. The morning was _______ (lovely) for running.
   → **lovely**

7. I feel _______ (good) after running.
   → **good**

8. The scenery looked _______ (amazing).
   → **amazing**

---

### D. 틀린 부분을 고치세요 (Error correction)

1. The flowers smell beautifully.
   → The flowers smell **beautiful**.

2. I feel well after running. (기분이 좋다)
   → I feel **good** after running.

3. She looks happily today.
   → She looks **happy** today.

4. The only the reason I run is for health.
   → The **only** reason I run is for health.

5. I bought a red big car.
   → I bought a **big red** car.

6. The asleep baby is cute.
   → The **sleeping** baby is cute.

7. He is an afraid boy.
   → He is a boy **who is afraid**. / He is a **frightened** boy.

8. The food tastes nicely.
   → The food tastes **nice**.

## Summary

### Position Summary:

| 패턴 | 예시 |
|------|------|
| 형용사 + 명사 | a **new** uniform |
| be + 형용사 | The uniform is **new**. |
| look/feel/seem + 형용사 | You look **tired**. |
| 형용사 순서 | nice new blue running shoes |

### Adjective Order (OSASCOMP):

| Order | Category | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Opinion | nice, beautiful |
| 2 | Size | big, small |
| 3 | Age | new, old |
| 4 | Shape | round, long |
| 5 | Color | blue, red |
| 6 | Origin | Korean, Italian |
| 7 | Material | leather, cotton |
| 8 | Purpose | running, swimming |

### Linking Verbs + Adjectives:

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| be | She **is** happy. |
| look | You **look** tired. |
| feel | I **feel** good. |
| sound | It **sounds** nice. |
| smell | The food **smells** good. |
| taste | The soup **tastes** delicious. |
| seem | It **seems** impossible. |
| appear | He **appears** worried. |
| become | She **became** famous. |
| get | I **got** tired. |
