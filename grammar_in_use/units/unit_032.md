# Unit 32: Must, mustn't, needn't

## A. 핵심 개념 (Key Concepts)

| Expression | Meaning | Korean |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Must | ~해야 한다 | 의무 (강함) |
| Mustn't | ~하면 안 된다 | 금지 |
| Needn't | ~할 필요 없다 | 불필요 |

### 형태:

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| must + 동사원형 | You must rest. |
| mustn't + 동사원형 | You mustn't run. |
| needn't + 동사원형 | You needn't worry. |
| needn't have + p.p. | You needn't have worried. |

---

## B. Must: 의무/필요 (Obligation/Necessity)

**강한 의무** - "~해야 한다"

### From the Original Book:

> "You **must** keep it a secret. Don't tell anyone."
> 비밀로 해야 해. 아무에게도 말하지 마.

> "I **must** remember to phone Tom."
> 톰한테 전화해야지.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"You must rest before the race."**
> 대회 전에 꼭 쉬어야 해.

> **"I must finish this race."**
> 이 대회를 끝내야만 해.

---

## C. Mustn't: 금지 (Prohibition)

**하면 안 된다** - 금지의 의미

### From the Original Book:

> "You **mustn't** tell anyone what I said."
> 내가 말한 거 아무에게도 말하면 안 돼.

> "You **mustn't** be late."
> 늦으면 안 돼.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"You mustn't push too hard before the race."**
> 대회 전에 너무 무리하면 안 돼.

> **"You mustn't skip warm-ups."**
> 준비운동은 꼭 해야 해. (건너뛰면 안 돼)

> **"You mustn't give up."**
> 포기하면 안 돼.

---

## D. Needn't: 불필요 (Unnecessary)

**할 필요 없다** - 해도 되고 안 해도 됨

### From the Original Book:

> "You've got plenty of time. You **needn't** hurry."
> 시간 많아. 서두를 필요 없어.

> "You **needn't** come if you don't want to."
> 원하지 않으면 안 와도 돼.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"You needn't run every day. Rest is important too."**
> 매일 달릴 필요는 없어. 휴식도 중요해.

> **"You needn't wake up so early."**
> 그렇게 일찍 일어날 필요 없어.

---

## E. Mustn't vs Don't have to vs Needn't

### 핵심 차이:

| Expression | Meaning | Action |
|------------|---------|--------|
| mustn't | 금지 | 하면 안 됨 |
| don't have to | 불필요 | 안 해도 됨 |
| needn't | 불필요 | 안 해도 됨 |

**don't have to = needn't** (같은 의미)

### Comparison:

| Mustn't (금지) | Needn't / Don't have to (불필요) |
|----------------|----------------------------------|
| You **mustn't** run. | You **needn't** run. |
| (달리면 안 돼) | (안 달려도 돼) |
| You **mustn't** give up. | You **needn't** worry. |
| (포기하면 안 돼) | (걱정할 필요 없어) |

### From the Original Book:

> "You **mustn't** be late. The train leaves at 9."
> 늦으면 안 돼. 기차가 9시에 출발해.

> "You **needn't** be there at 9. The meeting doesn't start until 10."
> 9시에 있을 필요 없어. 회의가 10시에 시작하거든.

---

## F. Needn't have done: 했지만 필요 없었음

**과거에 했지만, 나중에 보니 필요 없었던 일**

### From the Original Book:

> "I **needn't have bought** so much food. Only a few people came."
> 음식을 그렇게 많이 살 필요 없었어. 몇 명밖에 안 왔거든.

> "She **needn't have worried**. Everything was fine."
> 걱정할 필요 없었어. 다 괜찮았거든.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I needn't have worried. The race went well."**
> 걱정할 필요 없었어. 대회 잘 됐으니까.

> **"I needn't have brought my umbrella. It didn't rain."**
> 우산 안 가져와도 됐었어. 비 안 왔거든.

---

## G. Didn't need to vs Needn't have

| Didn't need to | Needn't have |
|----------------|--------------|
| 필요 없어서 안 함 | 했는데 필요 없었음 |
| I didn't need to run. (안 달림) | I needn't have run. (달렸는데 필요 없었음) |

### From the Original Book:

> "I **didn't need to** get up early, so I stayed in bed."
> 일찍 일어날 필요 없어서 침대에 있었어. (안 일어남)

> "I got up early, but I **needn't have** done."
> 일찍 일어났는데, 그럴 필요 없었어. (일어났음)

---

## H. 질문에 대한 응답 (Answering Questions)

### Q: "Must I...?"

| Answer | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| "Yes, you must." | 응, 해야 해. |
| "No, you needn't." | 아니, 할 필요 없어. |
| "No, you don't have to." | 아니, 안 해도 돼. |

**주의**: "No, you mustn't."는 **"하면 안 돼"**라는 금지의 의미!

### Example:

> **Q:** "Must I run today?"
> **A:** "No, you **needn't**." (필요 없어)
> **A:** "No, you **don't have to**." (안 해도 돼)
> **A:** "No, you **mustn't**. You have an injury." (하면 안 돼 - 부상)

## I. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~You mustn't to run.~~ | You **mustn't run**. | to 없음 |
| ~~You needn't to worry.~~ | You **needn't worry**. | to 없음 |
| ~~He mustn't runs.~~ | He **mustn't run**. | 동사원형 |
| ~~I needn't had worried.~~ | I **needn't have worried**. | needn't have + p.p. |
| ~~You don't must go.~~ | You **mustn't go**. / You **don't have to go**. | 형태 주의 |
| ~~You haven't to go.~~ | You **don't have to go**. | 형태 주의 |

### Mustn't vs Needn't 구분 팁:

| 상황 | 선택 |
|------|------|
| 하면 위험하거나 문제가 됨 | mustn't |
| 그냥 필요 없음 | needn't / don't have to |
| 규칙 위반 | mustn't |
| 선택 가능 | needn't / don't have to |

## Exercises

### A. Must, Mustn't, Needn't 선택 (Choose the correct form)

1. You _______ rest before the marathon. It's important. (의무)
2. You _______ run if your legs hurt. (금지)
3. You _______ buy expensive shoes. Cheap ones work too. (불필요)
4. You _______ skip breakfast on race day. (금지)
5. You _______ wake up at 4 a.m. 5 a.m. is fine. (불필요)
6. I _______ finish this. I promised. (의무)
7. You _______ worry. Everything will be fine. (불필요)
8. Students _______ cheat on exams. (금지)

**Answers:**
1. must
2. mustn't
3. needn't / don't have to
4. mustn't
5. needn't / don't have to
6. must
7. needn't
8. mustn't

### B. 응답 완성 (Complete the responses)

1. "Must I run today?"
   "No, you _______." (필요 없어)

2. "Can I skip the warm-up?"
   "No, you _______." (하면 안 돼)

3. "Should I bring water?"
   "Yes, you _______." (가져와야 해)

4. "Do I have to come early?"
   "No, you _______." (필요 없어)

5. "Must I wear running shoes?"
   "Yes, you _______." (신어야 해)

**Answers:**
1. needn't / don't have to
2. mustn't
3. must
4. needn't / don't have to
5. must

### C. Needn't have 문장 만들기 (Form sentences with needn't have)

1. 우산 가져올 필요 없었어. (비 안 옴)
   → I _______ my umbrella.

2. 걱정할 필요 없었어. (잘 됨)
   → I _______.

3. 그렇게 일찍 올 필요 없었어. (회의 늦게 시작)
   → You _______ so early.

4. 음식 그렇게 많이 살 필요 없었어.
   → We _______ so much food.

**Answers:**
1. needn't have brought
2. needn't have worried
3. needn't have come
4. needn't have bought

### D. Didn't need to vs Needn't have (Choose and explain)

1. I _______ get up early, so I stayed in bed. (안 일어남)
   → **didn't need to**

2. I got up early, but I _______ . It was a holiday. (일어났는데 필요 없었음)
   → **needn't have done** / **needn't have got up**

3. She _______ take the exam because she already passed. (안 봄)
   → **didn't need to**

4. He bought a gift, but he _______ . There was one already. (샀는데 필요 없었음)
   → **needn't have bought one**
