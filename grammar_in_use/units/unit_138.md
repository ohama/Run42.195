# Unit 138: Phrasal Verbs (2) - in/out

## A. 개념 설명 (Grammar Explanation)

### English
The particles **in** and **out** are opposites. **In** generally means "entering" or "inside," while **out** means "exiting" or "outside." However, when combined with verbs, they often create new, figurative meanings beyond physical movement.

### Korean
**in**과 **out**은 반대 의미의 입자입니다. **in**은 일반적으로 "안으로/내부"를, **out**은 "밖으로/외부"를 의미합니다. 그러나 동사와 결합하면 물리적 이동을 넘어 새로운 비유적 의미를 만들어냅니다.

---

## B. IN의 주요 의미 (Main Meanings of IN)

### 1. 물리적으로 안으로 (Physically entering)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| come in | 들어오다 |
| go in | 들어가다 |
| get in | 들어가다/타다 (차) |
| let in | 들여보내다 |

> **"Cool air came in."**
> 시원한 공기가 들어왔다.

> **"I got in the car."** (작은 차량)
> 차에 탔다.

### 2. 제출/도착 (Submission/Arrival)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| hand in | 제출하다 |
| come in | 도착하다 |
| send in | 보내다 |

> **"I handed in my application."**
> 지원서를 제출했다.

### 3. 포함/참여 (Inclusion/Participation)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| join in | 참여하다 |
| fill in | 채우다/기입하다 |
| fit in | 어울리다 |

> **"I want to join in."**
> 참여하고 싶다.

---

## C. OUT의 주요 의미 (Main Meanings of OUT)

### 1. 물리적으로 밖으로 (Physically exiting)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| go out | 나가다 |
| come out | 나오다 |
| get out | 나가다/내리다 |
| step out | 나가다 |

> **"I stepped out the door."**
> 문을 나섰다.

> **"Should I just go out and run?"**
> 그냥 밖에 나가서 뛰어야겠다.

### 2. 나타남/발생 (Appearing/Emerging)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| break out | (땀, 발진 등이) 나다 |
| come out | 나오다, 발표되다 |
| turn out | ~으로 드러나다 |

> **"Cold sweat broke out on my forehead."**
> 이마에 식은땀이 났다.

> **"The sun came out."**
> 해가 나왔다.

### 3. 소멸/소진 (Disappearing/Exhaustion)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| run out (of) | 다 떨어지다 |
| wear out | 닳다/지치다 |
| burn out | 소진되다 |

> **"I ran out of water."**
> 물이 다 떨어졌다.

### 4. 발견/해결 (Discovery/Solution)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| find out | 알아내다 |
| work out | 해결하다/운동하다 |
| figure out | 알아내다/이해하다 |

> **"I'll find out when I do it."**
> 해보면 알게 될 거야.

---

## D. 소설 속 예문 (Examples from the Novel)

### Example 1: come in / come out

> **"Cool air came in. The bus still swayed, but sitting made it easier."**
> 시원한 공기가 들어왔다. 버스는 여전히 흔들렸지만, 앉으니 나았다.

> **"The sun came out."**
> 해가 나왔다.

### Example 2: break out

> **"That thought made it worse. Cold sweat broke out on my forehead."**
> 그 생각이 상황을 더 나쁘게 만들었다. 이마에 식은땀이 났다.

### Example 3: find out

> **"Who knows. I'll find out when I do it."**
> 누가 알겠어. 해보면 알게 될 거야.

### Example 4: run out

> **"I have to eat breakfast too. Otherwise I'll run out of energy halfway."**
> 아침도 먹어야 한다. 안 그러면 중간에 에너지가 바닥날 거다.

### Example 5: work out

> **"I worked out a training plan with my teacher."**
> 선생님과 훈련 계획을 세웠다.

## E. IN vs OUT 비교 (Comparison)

| IN (안으로/포함) | OUT (밖으로/소멸) |
|-----------------|------------------|
| come in (들어오다) | come out (나오다) |
| get in (들어가다/타다) | get out (나가다/내리다) |
| check in (체크인하다) | check out (체크아웃하다) |
| fill in (채우다) | run out (다 떨어지다) |
| let in (들여보내다) | let out (내보내다) |
| break in (침입하다) | break out (발생하다) |
| hand in (제출하다) | hand out (나눠주다) |

## F. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~I run out water.~~ | I **ran out of** water. | run out **of** + 명사 |
| ~~The sun came in.~~ (해가 나옴) | The sun **came out**. | 나타남은 out |
| ~~find up~~ | **find out** | 알아내다는 find out |
| ~~I worked up a plan.~~ | I **worked out** a plan. | 해결하다는 work out |
| ~~Sweat broke in.~~ | Sweat **broke out**. | 발생은 break out |

### 주의: get in vs get on

| get in | get on |
|--------|--------|
| 작은 차량 (car, taxi) | 큰 대중교통 (bus, train, plane) |
| I got **in** the taxi. | I got **on** the bus. |

### B. 알맞은 구동사 선택하기

1. 시원한 공기가 들어왔다.
   - a) came out  b) came in  c) went out

2. 물이 다 떨어졌다.
   - a) ran in  b) ran out  c) broke out

3. 이마에 식은땀이 났다.
   - a) came out  b) worked out  c) broke out

4. 해가 나왔다.
   - a) came out  b) came in  c) went in

5. 훈련 계획을 세웠다.
   - a) found out  b) worked out  c) ran out

**Answers:**
1. b) came in
2. b) ran out
3. c) broke out
4. a) came out
5. b) worked out

### C. 틀린 부분 고치기 (Error Correction)

1. I run out water during the race.
2. Sweat broke in on my forehead.
3. The sun came in after the rain.
4. I find up the answer yesterday.
5. She worked up a solution.

**Answers:**
1. I **ran out of** water during the race.
2. Sweat **broke out** on my forehead.
3. The sun **came out** after the rain.
4. I **found out** the answer yesterday.
5. She **worked out** a solution.

### E. 문장 변환 (Sentence Transformation)

Use the phrasal verb to rewrite:

1. Sweat appeared on my forehead. (break out)
   → Sweat _______ on my forehead.

2. The water is finished. (run out)
   → I've _______ water.

3. Fresh air entered the room. (come in)
   → Fresh air _______.

4. I discovered the truth. (find out)
   → I _______ the truth.

5. She created a plan. (work out)
   → She _______ a plan.

**Answers:**
1. broke out
2. run out of
3. came in
4. found out
5. worked out

## IN vs OUT 요약표 (Summary)

| Meaning | IN | OUT |
|---------|-----|-----|
| 물리적 이동 | come in, get in | come out, get out |
| 제출/도착 | hand in, check in | hand out, check out |
| 포함/참여 | join in, fill in | - |
| 나타남/발생 | - | break out, come out |
| 소멸/소진 | - | run out, wear out |
| 발견/해결 | - | find out, work out |

## 추가 IN/OUT 구동사 정리

### More IN phrasal verbs:
| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| check in | 체크인하다 | We checked in at the hotel. |
| fill in | 기입하다 | Fill in the blanks. |

| fit in | 어울리다 | She fits in well with the team. |
| give in | 굴복하다 | Don't give in! |

### More OUT phrasal verbs:
| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| check out | 확인하다/체크아웃 | Check out this video! |
| hand out | 나눠주다 | They handed out water. |
| work out | 운동하다 | I work out every day. |
| turn out | ~으로 드러나다 | It turned out well. |
| pass out | 기절하다 | He passed out from the heat. |
