# Unit 98: Adjectives Ending in -ing and -ed

## A. -ing vs -ed 형용사의 차이 (The Difference)

같은 동사에서 파생된 -ing와 -ed 형용사는 의미가 다릅니다.

### Core Concept:

| -ing | -ed |
|------|-----|
| ~하게 만드는 (원인) | ~한 감정을 느끼는 (결과) |
| Describes the **thing/person** causing the feeling | Describes the **person** feeling the emotion |
| The race was **exciting**. | I was **excited**. |
| 대회가 흥미진진했다. (원인) | 나는 흥분했다. (결과) |

### From the Original Book:

> "The film was **boring**." (The film caused boredom)
> "I was **bored**." (I felt bored because of the film)

> "It was quite **surprising** that he passed the exam."
> "Everyone was **surprised** that he passed the exam."

---

## B. -ing 형용사 (원인/특성)

**-ing 형용사**는 어떤 감정을 **일으키는** 것을 설명합니다.

### Common -ing Adjectives:

| -ing | 의미 |
|------|------|
| exciting | 흥미진진한, 신나게 하는 |
| boring | 지루하게 하는 |
| tiring | 피곤하게 하는 |
| interesting | 흥미롭게 하는 |
| surprising | 놀라게 하는 |
| amazing | 놀랍게 하는 |
| exhausting | 지치게 하는 |
| embarrassing | 당황하게 하는 |
| confusing | 혼란스럽게 하는 |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The race was exciting."**
> 대회는 흥미진진했다.

- 대회가 흥분을 일으킴

> **"The bus ride was tiring."**
> 버스 타기가 피곤했다.

- 버스가 피로를 일으킴

> **"Walking was more interesting than taking the bus."**
> 걷는 것이 버스 타는 것보다 더 재미있었다.

> **"The crowded bus was suffocating."**
> 만원 버스는 숨 막혔다.

> **"The marathon was exhausting but satisfying."**
> 마라톤은 지치게 했지만 만족스러웠다.

> **"The scenery was amazing."**
> 경치가 놀라웠다.

## C. -ed 형용사 (감정/상태)

**-ed 형용사**는 감정을 **느끼는** 사람의 상태를 설명합니다.

### Common -ed Adjectives:

| -ed | 의미 |
|-----|------|
| excited | 흥분한 |
| bored | 지루해하는 |
| tired | 피곤한 |
| interested | 흥미를 느끼는 |
| surprised | 놀란 |
| amazed | 감탄한 |
| exhausted | 지친 |
| embarrassed | 당황한 |
| confused | 혼란스러워하는 |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I was tired after the run."**
> 달리기 후에 피곤했다.

- 내가 피로를 느낌

> **"I was excited about the race."**
> 대회가 기대됐다.

- 내가 흥분을 느낌

> **"I felt exhausted after 30 kilometers."**
> 30킬로미터 후에 지쳤다.

> **"Minseo looked surprised when I told him."**
> 내가 말했을 때 민서는 놀란 것 같았다.

> **"I was embarrassed but also happy."**
> 부끄러웠지만 또한 기뻤다.

> **"My mom looked worried before the race."**
> 대회 전에 엄마가 걱정하는 것 같았다.

## D. 같은 상황, 다른 표현 (Same situation, different focus)

| -ing (원인) | -ed (감정) |
|-------------|-----------|
| The race was **exciting**. | I was **excited** about the race. |
| The news was **surprising**. | I was **surprised** by the news. |
| The bus was **tiring**. | I felt **tired** after the bus ride. |
| The view was **amazing**. | I was **amazed** by the view. |
| The story was **boring**. | I felt **bored** during the story. |

### From the Novel:

> **"The view of rapeseed flowers was amazing."** (경치가 놀라움을 줌)
> 유채꽃 경치가 놀라웠다.

> **"I was amazed by the rapeseed flowers."** (내가 놀람을 느낌)
> 나는 유채꽃에 감탄했다.

### From the Original Book:

> "The movie was **disappointing**. We expected it to be much better."
> "We were **disappointed** with the movie. We expected it to be better."

## F. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| 틀린 표현 | 올바른 표현 | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| I am ~~interesting~~ in running. | I am **interested** in running. | 내가 느끼는 것 → -ed |
| The race was ~~excited~~. | The race was **exciting**. | 대회가 주는 것 → -ing |
| I feel ~~boring~~. | I feel **bored**. | 내가 느끼는 것 → -ed |
| The news was ~~surprised~~. | The news was **surprising**. | 뉴스가 주는 것 → -ing |
| I am ~~confusing~~. | I am **confused**. | 내가 느끼는 것 → -ed |
| The explanation was ~~confused~~. | The explanation was **confusing**. | 설명이 주는 것 → -ing |

### 기억 방법 (Memory Tips):

```
사람이 주어 + 감정을 느낀다 → -ed
  I was tired/excited/surprised

사물/상황이 주어 + 감정을 일으킨다 → -ing
  The race was tiring/exciting/surprising
```

### Visual Guide:

```
[원인: -ing]              [결과: -ed]
The movie (boring) ───→ I feel (bored)
The news (surprising) ──→ I am (surprised)
The race (exciting) ────→ I was (excited)
```

## G. 특별한 경우 (Special Cases)

### -ed + by/with/about:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| excited **about** | I'm excited **about** the race. |
| surprised **by/at** | I was surprised **by** the news. |
| interested **in** | I'm interested **in** running. |
| bored **with** | I was bored **with** the lecture. |
| satisfied **with** | I'm satisfied **with** the result. |
| disappointed **with/in** | I was disappointed **with** my time. |
| amazed **at/by** | I was amazed **at** the view. |

### Things can feel emotions too:

동물이나 사람을 설명할 때 -ing와 -ed 모두 가능합니다:

> "He's a **boring** person." (그가 지루함을 유발)
> "He's a **bored** person." (그가 지루해 함)

## Quick Rule (빠른 규칙)

### Subject-Based Decision:

| 주어가... | 사용하는 형용사 | 예시 |
|-----------|-----------------|------|
| **사람** (감정을 느끼는) | -ed | I was **tired**. |
| **사물/상황** (감정을 일으키는) | -ing | The race was **tiring**. |

### Simple Formula:

```
감정을 느끼는 주체 → -ed
  I am excited/tired/surprised

감정을 일으키는 원인 → -ing
  The movie is exciting/tiring/surprising
```

### Memory Trick:

```
-ED = I (사람) fEEl this
-ING = The thING causes this
```
