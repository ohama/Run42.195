# Unit 113: Although / though / even though / In spite of / despite

## A. Although / Though / Even though의 형태와 용법 (Form and Usage)

**Although, though, even though**는 모두 "~에도 불구하고, ~이지만"의 의미로, **양보**를 나타냅니다. 뒤에 **절(주어 + 동사)**이 옵니다.

### 구조:
> Although/Though/Even though + 주어 + 동사, 주절

| Expression | Meaning | Strength | Formality |
|------------|---------|----------|-----------|
| although | ~이지만, ~에도 불구하고 | 중립적 | 격식/중립 |
| though | ~이지만 (although보다 구어적) | 약함 | 구어체 |
| even though | ~임에도 불구하고 | 강조 | 중립 |

### From the Original Book:
> "**Although** it was cold, we went swimming."
> 추웠지만, 우리는 수영을 갔다.

> "**Even though** I was very tired, I couldn't sleep."
> 매우 피곤했음에도 불구하고, 잠을 잘 수 없었다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Even though it was hard, I kept running."**
> 힘들었지만, 계속 달렸다.

> **"I ran to school though my legs were tired."**
> 다리가 피곤했지만 학교까지 달렸다.

> **"Although I was nervous, I started running."**
> 긴장되었지만, 달리기 시작했다.

---

## B. In spite of / Despite의 형태와 용법

**In spite of**와 **despite**는 같은 의미로 "~에도 불구하고"를 나타냅니다.
뒤에 **명사(구)** 또는 **동명사(-ing)**가 옵니다.

### 구조:
> In spite of / Despite + 명사(구) / 동명사

| Expression | Structure | Example |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| in spite of | + 명사/동명사 | in spite of the rain |
| despite | + 명사/동명사 | despite feeling tired |

### From the Original Book:
> "**In spite of** the rain, we went out."
> 비에도 불구하고, 우리는 나갔다.

> "**Despite** having no money, she went on holiday."
> 돈이 없음에도 불구하고, 그녀는 휴가를 갔다.

### 주의: "despite of"는 틀린 표현입니다!

| Correct (O) | Wrong (X) |
|-------------|-----------|
| **despite** the rain | ~~despite of~~ the rain |
| **in spite of** the rain | (올바름) |

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I kept running despite the heat."**
> 더위에도 불구하고 계속 달렸다.

> **"In spite of the pain in my legs, I didn't stop."**
> 다리의 통증에도 불구하고 멈추지 않았다.

> **"Despite feeling exhausted, I crossed the finish line."**
> 지쳐있었지만 결승선을 통과했다.

## C. Although vs In spite of / Despite 비교

| Although + 절 | In spite of / Despite + 명사(구) |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| Although **it was raining**, I ran. | In spite of **the rain**, I ran. |
| Although **I was tired**, I kept going. | Despite **my tiredness**, I kept going. |
| Although **he was nervous**, he ran well. | In spite of **his nervousness**, he ran well. |

### 변환 방법:

```
Although + 주어 + be + 형용사
→ Despite/In spite of + 소유격 + 명사

Although I was tired → Despite my tiredness
Although he was nervous → In spite of his nervousness
Although it rained → Despite the rain
```

### From the Novel - Same meaning, different structure:

> **"Although my stomach was churning, I didn't throw up."**
> = **"In spite of my churning stomach, I didn't throw up."**
> 속이 울렁거렸지만 토하지 않았다.

> **"Even though I was near the bottom in elementary school, I improved."**
> = **"Despite being near the bottom in elementary school, I improved."**
> 초등학교 때 거의 꼴찌였지만, 나는 나아졌다.

## D. Though의 특별한 용법 (Though at the End)

**Though**는 문장 끝에 올 수 있습니다 (although는 불가).

### From the Original Book:
> "The house isn't very nice. I like the garden, **though**."
> 집은 별로 좋지 않아. 그래도 정원은 마음에 들어.

> "I see him every day. I've never spoken to him, **though**."
> 매일 그를 봐. 하지만 말을 걸어본 적은 없어.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"It was hard, though."**
> 그래도 힘들긴 했다.

> **"I arrived on time. I was exhausted, though."**
> 제시간에 도착했다. 그래도 지치긴 했다.

> **"The run felt good. My legs hurt, though."**
> 달리기가 기분 좋았다. 그래도 다리가 아프긴 했다.

### Though의 위치 비교:

| 문장 앞 | 문장 끝 |
|---------|---------|
| **Though** it was hard, I finished. | It was hard. I finished, **though**. |
| 힘들었지만, 끝냈다. | 힘들었다. 그래도 끝냈다. |

---

## E. Even though vs Even if

| Even though | Even if |
|-------------|---------|
| 사실에 대해 말할 때 | 가정에 대해 말할 때 |
| "~임에도 불구하고" (실제 상황) | "설령 ~하더라도" (가정 상황) |
| 과거/현재의 실제 사실 | 미래/가정의 상황 |

### From the Original Book:
> "**Even though** I was very tired, I couldn't sleep." (사실)
> 매우 피곤했는데도, 잠을 잘 수 없었다.

> "**Even if** I were very tired, I wouldn't be able to sleep." (가정)
> 매우 피곤하더라도, 잠을 잘 수 없을 것이다.

### Examples:

> **"Even though I was tired, I ran."** (실제로 피곤했음)
> 피곤했지만 달렸다.

> **"Even if I were tired, I would run."** (가정)
> 설령 피곤하더라도 달릴 것이다.

## F. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~Despite of the cold weather~~ | **Despite** the cold weather | despite 뒤에 of 없음 |
| ~~Although the heat, I kept running.~~ | **Although it was hot** / **In spite of the heat** | although 뒤에 절 필요 |
| ~~In spite of I was tired~~ | **In spite of being tired** / **Although I was tired** | in spite of 뒤에 명사/동명사 |
| ~~Though very cold, but I went out.~~ | **Though it was very cold**, I went out. | but 중복 사용 금지 |
| ~~Although tired~~ | **Although I was tired** | 절 완성 필요 (주어+동사) |
| ~~Despite I was sick~~ | **Despite being sick** / **Although I was sick** | despite 뒤에 절 불가 |

### But 중복 주의:

> ~~**Although** it rained, **but** I went out.~~ (X)
> **Although** it rained, I went out. (O)
> It rained, **but** I went out. (O)

## Exercises

### A. 빈칸에 although, though, even though, in spite of, despite 중 알맞은 것을 쓰세요.

1. _______ the bus was crowded, I got a seat.
2. I kept walking _______ feeling sick.
3. The run was good, _______ it was hard at the end.
4. _______ my tiredness, I finished the race.
5. _______ I hated running in elementary school, I love it now.
6. I'll go to school _______ the rain.
7. It was a great race. I was nervous, _______.
8. _______ working hard, she didn't pass the exam.

**Answers:**
1. Although / Even though
2. despite / in spite of
3. though
4. Despite / In spite of
5. Although / Even though
6. despite / in spite of
7. though
8. Despite / In spite of

### B. 두 문장을 한 문장으로 연결하세요.

1. It was raining. I went running.
   → _______ (although)
   → _______ (in spite of)

2. My legs were sore. I didn't stop.
   → _______ (even though)
   → _______ (despite)

3. He was sick. He went to work.
   → _______ (though)
   → _______ (in spite of)

**Answers:**
1. Although it was raining, I went running. / In spite of the rain, I went running.
2. Even though my legs were sore, I didn't stop. / Despite my sore legs, I didn't stop.
3. Though he was sick, he went to work. / In spite of his sickness, he went to work.

### C. 틀린 부분을 고치세요.

1. Despite of the cold weather, I ran outside.
   → **Despite the cold weather**, I ran outside.

2. Although the heat, I kept running.
   → **Although it was hot** / **In spite of the heat**, I kept running.

3. In spite of I was tired, I finished the race.
   → **In spite of being tired** / **Although I was tired**, I finished the race.

4. Even though it was hard, but I continued.
   → **Even though it was hard, I continued.** (but 삭제)

5. Despite having no energy, but he finished.
   → **Despite having no energy, he finished.** (but 삭제)

### D. although/though를 사용한 문장을 despite/in spite of로 바꾸세요.

1. Although it was cold, we went outside.
   → **Despite the cold** / **In spite of the cold**, we went outside.

2. Even though I was tired, I kept running.
   → **Despite being tired** / **In spite of my tiredness**, I kept running.

3. Although the traffic was heavy, we arrived on time.
   → **Despite the heavy traffic** / **In spite of the heavy traffic**, we arrived on time.

## Summary: 양보 표현 비교표

| 표현 | 구조 | 격식 | 예시 |
|------|------|------|------|
| although | + 절 | 격식/중립 | Although it rained, ... |
| though | + 절 / 문장 끝 | 구어체 | Though it rained, ... / ..., though. |
| even though | + 절 | 중립 (강조) | Even though it rained, ... |
| despite | + 명사/동명사 | 격식 | Despite the rain, ... |
| in spite of | + 명사/동명사 | 중립 | In spite of the rain, ... |

## 변환 규칙 요약

| Although + 절 | → | Despite/In spite of + 명사 |
|---------------|---|---------------------------|
| Although it rained | → | Despite the rain |
| Although I was tired | → | Despite my tiredness |
| Although he worked hard | → | Despite his hard work |
| Although feeling sick | → | Despite feeling sick |

## Compare with Korean

| English | Korean |
|---------|--------|
| although / even though + 절 | ~지만, ~임에도 불구하고 |
| in spite of / despite + 명사 | ~에도 불구하고 |
| though (문장 끝) | 그래도, 하지만 |

한국어에서는 "~지만", "~에도 불구하고", "~임에도"로 양보를 표현합니다.
