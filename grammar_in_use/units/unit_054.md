# Unit 54: Verb + to... (decide to... / forget to..., etc.)

## A. 동사 + to부정사

특정 동사 뒤에는 **to부정사**가 옵니다. 이런 동사들은 -ing가 아닌 to + 동사원형을 목적어로 취합니다.

**주요 동사들:**
- decide / choose / plan / hope / want
- need / expect / promise / agree / refuse
- learn / offer / manage / fail / afford
- forget / remember (의미 차이 있음 - Unit 56 참조)

---

## B. decide / choose / plan + to

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I decided to run to school."**
> 학교까지 달려가기로 결심했다.

- **decide + to**: ~하기로 결정하다

> **"I planned to run the whole way."**
> 전 구간을 달릴 계획이었다.

- **plan + to**: ~할 계획이다

> **"Today, I planned to run."**
> 오늘은 달리기로 했다.

---

## C. want / need / hope + to

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I wanted to run."**
> 달리고 싶었다.

- **want + to**: ~하고 싶다

> **"I want to hear the birds again."**
> 새소리를 다시 듣고 싶다.

> **"I need to sleep early."**
> 일찍 자야 해.

- **need + to**: ~해야 하다

> **"I hope to finish the race."**
> 레이스를 완주하고 싶다.

- **hope + to**: ~하기를 바라다

---

## D. learn / manage / fail + to

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I learned to pace myself."**
> 페이스 조절하는 법을 배웠다.

- **learn + to**: ~하는 법을 배우다

> **"I managed to finish 10km."**
> 어떻게든 10km를 완주했다.

- **manage + to**: 어렵지만 ~해내다

> **"I failed to wake up on time."**
> 제때 일어나지 못했다.

- **fail + to**: ~하지 못하다

---

## E. promise / agree / refuse + to

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I promised to run with Minseo on Saturday."**
> 토요일에 민서랑 달리기로 약속했다.

- **promise + to**: ~하기로 약속하다

> **"Minseo agreed to run with me."**
> 민서가 같이 달리기로 했다.

- **agree + to**: ~하기로 동의하다

> **"I refused to give up."**
> 포기하지 않았다.

- **refuse + to**: ~하기를 거부하다

---

## F. 주요 동사 비교표

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| decide | 결정하다 | I decided to walk to school. |
| want | 원하다 | I wanted to run. |
| need | 필요하다 | I need to rest. |
| plan | 계획하다 | I planned to leave at 5 AM. |
| hope | 바라다 | I hope to finish the marathon. |
| learn | 배우다 | I learned to run properly. |
| promise | 약속하다 | I promised to train hard. |
| agree | 동의하다 | She agreed to come cheer. |
| refuse | 거부하다 | I refused to quit. |
| manage | 해내다 | I managed to finish. |
| fail | 실패하다 | I failed to wake up early. |
| afford | 여유가 되다 | I can't afford to skip training. |

---

## G. would like to vs want to

| Expression | Politeness | Example |
|------------|------------|---------|
| want to | 직접적 | I **want to** run tomorrow. |
| would like to | 공손함 | I **would like to** join the race. |

### From the Novel:

> **"What do you want for dinner?"**
> 저녁에 뭐 먹고 싶어?

- 가족 간의 대화에서는 want to 사용

## Exercises

### A. 올바른 형태를 고르세요.

1. I decided (run / to run) every morning.
2. She hopes (finish / to finish) the race.
3. He wants (join / to join) the track team.
4. I need (rest / to rest) before the race.
5. They agreed (meet / to meet) at the park.

**Answers:**
1. to run
2. to finish
3. to join
4. to rest
5. to meet

### B. 문장을 완성하세요.

1. I learned _______ (pace) myself during long runs.
2. She promised _______ (come) to the race.
3. He managed _______ (finish) despite the pain.
4. I failed _______ (hear) my alarm this morning.
5. They planned _______ (leave) at 6 AM.

**Answers:**
1. to pace
2. to come
3. to finish
4. to hear
5. to leave

### C. 문장 완성하기

1. I want _______ a marathon someday. (run)
2. She decided _______ with me. (train)
3. He hopes _______ his personal record. (break)
4. I need _______ more water. (drink)

**Answers:**
1. to run
2. to train
3. to break
4. to drink
