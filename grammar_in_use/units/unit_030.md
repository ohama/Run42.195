# Unit 30: May and might (2)

## A. 형태 (Form)

### May/Might have done: 과거에 대한 추측

과거에 일어났을 수도 있는 일을 추측할 때 사용합니다.

| Subject | may/might have | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| He | may have | missed | He may have missed the bus. |
| She | might have | been | She might have been tired. |
| They | may not have | heard | They may not have heard. |
| I | might have | forgotten | I might have forgotten. |

---

## B. 과거에 대한 추측 (Past Possibility)

### From the Original Book:

> "**A:** I wonder why Kate didn't answer the phone."
> "**B:** She **might have been** asleep."
> 케이트가 왜 전화 안 받았을까? - 자고 있었을 수도 있어.

> "I can't find my bag anywhere. I **may have left** it on the train."
> 가방을 어디서도 못 찾겠어. 기차에 두고 왔을 수도 있어.

> "Why didn't she say hello? She **might not have seen** me."
> 왜 인사 안 했을까? 못 봤을 수도 있어.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"He might have forgotten about the race."**
> 그가 대회를 잊었을 수도 있어.

- 과거에 대한 불확실한 추측

> **"They may not have heard me."**
> 그들이 내 말을 못 들었을 수도 있어.

- 과거에 대한 부정적 추측

> **"He might have been tired after the run."**
> 달리기 후에 피곤했을 수도 있어.

- 과거 상태에 대한 추측

---

## C. Might (but didn't): 할 수 있었는데 안 함

과거에 일어날 수 있었지만 **실제로 일어나지 않은** 일:

### From the Original Book:

> "You **might have been killed**!" (but you weren't)
> 죽었을 수도 있었잖아! (하지만 안 죽음)

> "I was so tired! I **might have fallen** asleep during the exam."
> 너무 피곤했어! 시험 중에 잠들 뻔했어.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I might have given up at 30km."**
> 30km에서 포기할 수도 있었어.

- 일어날 뻔했지만 안 일어난 일 (포기 안 함)

> **"I might have been hurt, but I was lucky."**
> 다칠 수도 있었는데, 운이 좋았어.

---

## D. May/Might have vs Could have 비교

| May/Might have done | Could have done |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| ~했을 수도 있다 (추측) | ~할 수 있었다 (능력/선택) |
| He might have missed the bus. | He could have taken the bus. |
| (버스를 놓쳤을 수도 있어) | (버스를 탈 수 있었는데 안 탔어) |

### Key Difference:

- **May/might have**: 과거에 대한 **추측** (무엇이 일어났을 수도)
- **Could have**: 과거에 **가능했던 선택** (하지 않은 것)

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I could have walked, but I might have been late."**
> 걸을 수도 있었는데, 늦었을 수도 있어.

- could have: 가능한 선택 (걷기)
- might have: 결과의 가능성 (늦을 수도)

---

## E. 과거 추측 비교 (Past Deduction Comparison)

| 표현 | 확신도 | 예문 | Korean |
|------|--------|------|--------|
| must have | 90% | He **must have** trained hard. | 열심히 훈련했을 게 틀림없어. |
| may/might have | 50% | He **may/might have** trained hard. | 열심히 훈련했을 수도 있어. |
| could have | 50% | He **could have** trained hard. | 열심히 훈련할 수 있었어. |
| can't have | 0% | He **can't have** trained hard. | 열심히 훈련했을 리가 없어. |

---

## F. Might vs May (과거 추측에서)

과거 추측에서 **might**가 더 자주 사용됩니다:

> "He **might have** been sick." (더 일반적)
> "He **may have** been sick." (가능하지만 덜 흔함)

### 둘 다 올바름:

| May have | Might have |
|----------|------------|
| He may have forgotten. | He might have forgotten. |
| She may have been tired. | She might have been tired. |
| They may not have heard. | They might not have heard. |

---

## G. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ~~He may has forgotten.~~ | He **may have** forgotten. | may + have + p.p. |
| ~~She might had been.~~ | She **might have been**. | might + have + p.p. |
| ~~They may have not heard.~~ | They **may not have** heard. | not 위치 |
| ~~I may have went.~~ | I **may have gone**. | 과거분사 |
| ~~He might been tired.~~ | He **might have been** tired. | have 필요 |
| ~~May he have forgotten?~~ | **Do you think** he might have forgotten? | 의문문 형태 |

### 현재 vs 과거 추측:

| 현재 | 과거 |
|------|------|
| He may be tired. | He may have been tired. |
| (지금 피곤할 수도) | (그때 피곤했을 수도) |
| She might be at home. | She might have been at home. |
| (지금 집에 있을 수도) | (그때 집에 있었을 수도) |

## Exercises

### A. May/Might have done 완성 (Complete with may/might have done)

1. 그가 버스를 놓쳤을 수도 있어.
   → He _______ the bus.

2. 그녀가 피곤했을 수도 있어.
   → She _______ tired.

3. 그들이 못 들었을 수도 있어.
   → They _______ me.

4. 나 열쇠를 집에 두고 왔을 수도 있어.
   → I _______ my keys at home.

5. 그가 대회를 잊었을 수도 있어.
   → He _______ about the race.

**Answers:**
1. might have missed / may have missed
2. might have been / may have been
3. may not have heard / might not have heard
4. might have left / may have left
5. might have forgotten / may have forgotten

### B. Must have vs Might have 선택 (Choose the correct form)

1. He finished 42km. He _______ trained for months. (확실히)
2. I don't know why he's late. He _______ missed the bus. (불확실)
3. She looks exhausted. She _______ run a long distance. (확실히)
4. Maybe he forgot. He _______ not remembered the date. (불확실)
5. The door is open. Someone _______ left it open. (확실히)
6. I'm not sure what happened. He _______ been sick. (불확실)
7. She's smiling. She _______ passed the exam. (확실히)
8. Perhaps they got lost. They _______ taken the wrong road. (불확실)

**Answers:**
1. must have
2. might have
3. must have
4. might have
5. must have
6. might have
7. must have
8. might have

### C. 문장 변환 (Sentence Transformation)

Transform using may/might have.

1. Perhaps he was tired. → He _______ tired.
2. Maybe they didn't see me. → They _______ me.
3. It's possible that I made a mistake. → I _______ a mistake.
4. Perhaps she forgot the meeting. → She _______ the meeting.
5. Maybe it was cold yesterday. → It _______ cold yesterday.

**Answers:**
1. might have been / may have been
2. might not have seen / may not have seen
3. might have made / may have made
4. might have forgotten / may have forgotten
5. might have been / may have been

### D. 상황에 맞게 작성 (Write based on context)

1. (일어날 뻔한 일) 포기할 수도 있었어.
   → I _______, but I didn't.

2. (과거 추측) 그가 늦었을 수도 있어.
   → He _______ late.

3. (반사실) 다쳤을 수도 있었잖아!
   → You _______ hurt!

4. (과거 가능성) 그녀가 바빴을 수도 있어.
   → She _______ busy.

**Answers:**
1. might have given up
2. might have been / may have been
3. might have been
4. might have been / may have been
