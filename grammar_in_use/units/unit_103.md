# Unit 103: Enough and Too

## A. enough - 충분한/충분히 (The Basics)

**enough**는 "충분한"의 의미로, 형용사/부사 **뒤에**, 명사 **앞에** 옵니다.

### Position Rules:

| 위치 | 구조 | 예시 |
|------|------|------|
| 형용사/부사 **뒤** | 형용사 + enough | strong **enough** |
| 명사 **앞** | enough + 명사 | **enough** time |

### From the Original Book:

> "I've got **enough** money." (before noun)
> "Is your coffee hot **enough**?" (after adjective)

---

## B. 형용사/부사 + enough (Adjective/Adverb + enough)

### Pattern:

```
형용사/부사 + enough + to부정사
I was strong enough to finish.
```

### From the Original Book:

> "She's old **enough** to get married."
> 그녀는 결혼할 나이가 됐다.

> "I didn't run fast **enough** to catch the bus."
> 버스를 잡을 만큼 빨리 달리지 못했다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"Am I strong enough to finish?"**
> 내가 완주할 만큼 강한가?

> **"I wasn't fast enough to catch the bus."**
> 버스를 잡을 만큼 빠르지 않았다.

> **"I trained hard enough to complete the race."**
> 대회를 완주할 만큼 충분히 훈련했다.

> **"The weather was warm enough to run in shorts."**
> 반바지 입고 달릴 만큼 따뜻했다.

> **"I woke up early enough to run before school."**
> 학교 가기 전에 달릴 만큼 일찍 일어났다.

> **"I felt good enough to continue."**
> 계속할 만큼 기분이 좋았다.

---

## C. enough + 명사 (enough + noun)

### Pattern:

```
enough + 명사 + to부정사
I have enough stamina to run 10km.
```

### From the Original Book:

> "We've got **enough** food."
> 음식이 충분해.

> "I haven't got **enough** time to talk now."
> 지금 이야기할 시간이 없어.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"I didn't have enough time to rest."**
> 쉴 시간이 충분하지 않았다.

> **"I brought enough water for the run."**
> 달리기에 충분한 물을 가져왔다.

> **"I had enough energy to finish."**
> 완주할 에너지가 충분했다.

> **"There wasn't enough space on the bus."**
> 버스에 공간이 충분하지 않았다.

> **"I had enough stamina to complete the marathon."**
> 마라톤을 완주할 체력이 충분했다.

> **"There was enough daylight to run safely."**
> 안전하게 달릴 만큼 충분한 햇빛이 있었다.

---

## D. too - 너무 (부정적)

**too**는 "너무"라는 의미로, **문제나 어려움**을 나타냅니다. 형용사/부사 **앞에** 옵니다.

### Pattern:

```
too + 형용사/부사 + to부정사
It was too hot to run.
```

### From the Original Book:

> "The box was **too** heavy to carry."
> 상자가 너무 무거워서 들 수 없었다.

> "I was **too** surprised to say anything."
> 너무 놀라서 아무 말도 할 수 없었다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The bus was too crowded."**
> 버스가 너무 붐볐다.

> **"It was too hot to run in the afternoon."**
> 오후에 달리기엔 너무 더웠다.

> **"The distance was too long."**
> 거리가 너무 멀었다.

> **"I was too tired to move."**
> 너무 피곤해서 움직일 수 없었다.

> **"I woke up too late and missed the bus."**
> 너무 늦게 일어나서 버스를 놓쳤다.

> **"The hill was too steep to run up."**
> 언덕이 너무 가팔라서 달려 올라갈 수 없었다.

---

## E. too vs very (중요한 차이!)

**too**와 **very**는 다릅니다!

### Key Difference:

| too | very |
|-----|------|
| 부정적/문제 있음 | 단순 강조 |
| 너무 (못할 정도로) | 매우 |
| 결과: 불가능 | 결과: 없거나 긍정 |

### From the Original Book:

> "I'm **very** tired, but I have to keep working."
> (피곤하지만 계속 일해야 한다)

> "I'm **too** tired to work."
> (너무 피곤해서 일할 수 없다)

### Comparison Examples:

| very (매우) | too (너무 - 문제) |
|-------------|-------------------|
| The race was **very** hard. (힘들었지만 완주) | The race was **too** hard. (힘들어서 문제) |
| It was **very** hot, but I ran. (더웠지만 달림) | It was **too** hot to run. (더워서 못 달림) |
| I was **very** tired, but I continued. | I was **too** tired to continue. |

### From the Novel:

> **"It was very hot, but I ran anyway."** (더웠지만 달림)
> 매우 더웠지만 어쨌든 달렸다.

> **"It was too hot to run."** (더워서 달릴 수 없음)
> 너무 더워서 달릴 수 없었다.

---

## F. enough vs too 비교 (Comparison)

### Opposite Meanings:

| enough (충분한) | too (너무) |
|-----------------|-----------|
| 긍정적/가능 | 부정적/불가능 |
| strong enough to finish | too tired to finish |
| warm enough to run | too hot to run |
| fast enough to catch | too slow to catch |

### Examples:

> **"I was strong enough to finish."** (완주 가능)
> 완주할 만큼 충분히 강했다.

> **"I was too weak to finish."** (완주 불가능)
> 너무 약해서 완주할 수 없었다.

> **"I had enough energy to continue."** (가능)
> 계속할 에너지가 충분했다.

> **"I was too exhausted to continue."** (불가능)
> 너무 지쳐서 계속할 수 없었다.

---

## G. too + for + 사람 (too...for)

**too...for**는 "~에게 너무"라는 의미입니다.

### Pattern:

```
too + 형용사 + for + 사람 + to부정사
The distance was too long for a beginner to run.
```

### From the Original Book:

> "This shirt is **too** big **for** me."
> 이 셔츠는 나에게 너무 크다.

> "The puzzle was **too** difficult **for** children."
> 그 퍼즐은 아이들에게 너무 어려웠다.

### Examples from the Novel:

> **"The distance was too long for a beginner."**
> 그 거리는 초보자에게 너무 멀었다.

> **"The race was too hard for me at first."**
> 처음에는 대회가 나에게 너무 힘들었다.

> **"The morning was too cold for running."**
> 아침이 달리기에 너무 추웠다.

> **"The bus was too crowded for comfort."**
> 버스가 편안하기엔 너무 붐볐다.

## H. 흔한 실수 (Common Mistakes)

| Wrong (X) | Correct (O) | 설명 |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| ~~enough strong~~ | strong **enough** | 형용사 뒤에 enough |
| ~~too much tired~~ | **too** tired | too + 형용사 |
| ~~I have time enough~~ | I have **enough** time | 명사 앞에 enough |
| ~~enough old~~ | old **enough** | 형용사 뒤에 enough |
| ~~I was very tired to continue~~ | I was **too** tired to continue | 불가능 → too |
| ~~The race was too hard but I finished~~ | The race was **very** hard but I finished | 완주 가능 → very |

## Summary

### Position Rules:

| | enough | too |
|---|--------|-----|
| 의미 | 충분한 | 너무 (문제) |
| 위치 | 형용사 뒤, 명사 앞 | 형용사/부사 앞 |
| 결과 | 가능 | 불가능 |
| 예시 | strong enough to | too weak to |

### too vs very:

| too | very |
|-----|------|
| 부정적 결과 | 중립적 강조 |
| too hot to run (못 달림) | very hot but I ran (달림) |

### Pattern Summary:

```
enough:
형용사 + enough + to부정사: I'm old enough to drive.
enough + 명사 + to부정사: I have enough time to rest.

too:
too + 형용사 + to부정사: I'm too tired to work.
too + 형용사 + for + 사람: It's too hard for me.
```

### Quick Decision:

```
결과가 가능한가?

  ┌─┴─┐
  ↓   ↓
 예   아니오
  │   │
  ↓   ↓
enough/very  too
```
