# Unit 17: Have and Have got

## A. 문법 설명 (Grammar Explanation)

### English
**Have** and **Have got** both express **possession**, but they have different forms and uses:
- **Have** is used in all contexts (formal, informal, American, British)
- **Have got** is common in British English, mainly present tense, informal

### Korean
**Have**와 **Have got** 모두 **소유**를 표현하지만 형태와 용법이 다릅니다:
- **Have**: 모든 상황에서 사용 (공식, 비공식, 미국식, 영국식)
- **Have got**: 주로 영국식 영어, 현재시제, 비공식적 상황에서

---

## B. 형태 비교 (Form Comparison)

### 긍정문 (Affirmative)

| Have | Have got |
|------|----------|
| I have a bag. | I've got a bag. |
| She has a medal. | She's got a medal. |

### 부정문 (Negative)

| Have | Have got |
|------|----------|
| I don't have a bag. | I haven't got a bag. |
| She doesn't have time. | She hasn't got time. |

### 의문문 (Questions)

| Have | Have got |
|------|----------|
| Do you have a bag? | Have you got a bag? |
| Does she have time? | Has she got time? |

---

## C. 소설 속 예문 (Examples from the Novel)

### Example 1: 소유 (Have)

> **"I don't have much stamina."**
> 나는 체력이 별로 없다.

- **don't have** = 소유 부정
- "I haven't got much stamina." 도 가능

### Example 2: 소유 (Have got)

> **"I've got some candy from Minseo."**
> 민서한테 받은 사탕이 있다.

- **I've got** = I have got (축약형)
- 소유를 나타냄

### Example 3: 시간/여유

> **"I don't have time to rest."**
> 쉴 시간이 없다.

> **"Have you got time to talk?"**
> 얘기할 시간 있어?

### Example 4: 신체적 특성

> **"I've got sore legs."**
> 다리가 아프다.

> **"I have muscle pain in my calves."**
> 종아리에 근육통이 있다.

### Example 5: 소지품

> **"I've got everything I need for the race."**
> 대회에 필요한 건 다 있다.

> **"Do you have your water bottle?"**
> 물병 가지고 있어?

## D. Have의 다른 용법 (Have = 행위)

### Have = 먹다, 마시다 (Eat/Drink)

> **"I have breakfast at 6:30."**
> 6시 30분에 아침을 먹는다.

> **"I had some soup before leaving."**
> 떠나기 전에 국을 먹었다.

**주의:** 이 경우 **have got** 사용 불가!
- ~~"I've got breakfast at 6:30."~~ (X)

### Have = 경험하다 (Experience)

> **"Have a good time!"**
> 좋은 시간 보내!

> **"I had fun at the race."**
> 대회에서 재미있었다.

### Have = 샤워, 휴식 등

> **"I'll have a shower after running."**
> 달리고 나서 샤워할 거야.

> **"Let's have a break."**
> 쉬자.

---

## E. Have to (의무) - Have got to

### Have to = ~해야 한다

> **"I have to wake up at 6:20."**
> 6시 20분에 일어나야 한다.

> **"I have to take the bus tomorrow."**
> 내일은 버스를 타야 한다.

### Have got to = ~해야 한다 (구어체)

> **"I've got to go now."**
> 지금 가야 해.

> **"You've got to try running!"**
> 달리기 해봐야 해!

**축약형:** I've gotta go. (매우 구어체)

---

## F. Have vs Have got 사용 가이드

### Have를 주로 쓰는 경우:
- 과거시제 (had)
- 미래시제 (will have)
- have to (의무)
- 행위 표현 (have breakfast, have fun)
- 미국 영어

### Have got을 쓰는 경우:
- 현재 소유
- 현재 상태
- 비공식적 대화
- 영국 영어

---

## G. 과거형 (Past Tense)

### Had (have의 과거형):

> **"I had my water bottle with me."**
> 물병을 가지고 있었다.

> **"I didn't have my lunch."**
> 점심이 없었다.

**주의:** **Had got**은 잘 사용하지 않습니다.
- "I had a bag." (O)
- "I'd got a bag." (드물게 사용)

## Exercises

### A. Have 또는 Have got으로 바꾸기

Change to the other form:

1. I have a new uniform.
   → I _______ a new uniform.

2. She's got three medals.
   → She _______ three medals.

3. Do you have time?
   → _______ you _______ time?

4. I haven't got my phone.
   → I _______ my phone.

**Answers:**
1. 've got
2. has
3. Have ... got
4. don't have

### B. 올바른 형태 선택

1. I (have / 've got) breakfast at 7 every day.
2. She (has / 's got) a beautiful running watch.
3. We (have to / 've got to) leave early tomorrow.
4. Do you (have / got) your race bib?
5. I (had / 'd got) a great time at the race.

**Answers:**
1. have (breakfast = 행위)
2. has 또는 's got (소유 - 둘 다 가능)
3. have to 또는 've got to (의무 - 둘 다 가능)
4. have (Do you have...?)
5. had (had a great time = 행위)

### C. 부정문 만들기

Make negative sentences:

1. I have much time. → I _______ much time.
2. She's got a car. → She _______ a car.
3. They have their bags. → They _______ their bags.
4. I've got my phone. → I _______ my phone.

**Answers:**
1. don't have
2. hasn't got
3. don't have
4. haven't got

### D. 의문문 만들기

Make questions:

1. You have a water bottle.
   → _______ a water bottle?

2. She's got time.
   → _______ time?

3. They have their medals.
   → _______ their medals?

**Answers:**
1. Do you have
2. Has she got
3. Do they have
