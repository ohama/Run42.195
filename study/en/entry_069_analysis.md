# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Training Expressions

### "Starting today, I'm not increasing distance anymore."

**Original Korean:** "오늘부터는 거리를 더 늘리지 않는다."

**Grammar Point:** "Starting [time], I'm + gerund..." = 지금부터 ~할 거다

**Structure:**
```
Starting [time/date], I'm + gerund/present continuous
```

**More Examples:**
| Time | Activity | Full Sentence |
|------|----------|---------------|
| today | not eating sweets | "Starting today, I'm not eating sweets." |
| next week | coming in early | "Starting next week, I'm coming in early." |
| tomorrow | going to bed earlier | "Starting tomorrow, I'm going to bed earlier." |
| this month | exercising daily | "Starting this month, I'm exercising daily." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "From now on, I won't increase distance."
- "I've decided not to increase distance anymore."
- "From this point, no more distance increases."

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "Starting today, I not increase distance."
✅ "Starting today, I'm not increasing distance."

---

### "Time to fine-tune my condition."

**Original Korean:** "컨디션을 다듬을 시간이다"

**"Time to" Pattern:** 이제 ~할 때다 (행동 시작의 필요성)

**Structure:** Time to + verb (infinitive)

**Examples:**
| Situation | Action | Full Sentence |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| 준비 끝남 | rest | "Time to rest before the race." |
| 날씨 좋음 | go outside | "Time to go outside and enjoy the weather." |
| 저녁 됨 | eat dinner | "Time to eat dinner." |
| 마감 임박 | finish the project | "Time to finish the project." |
| 집 떠남 | leave for work | "Time to leave for work." |

**"Time for" vs. "Time to":**
```
Time for + noun: "Time for dinner." (명사)
Time to + verb: "Time to eat dinner." (동사)
```

**Variations:**
- "It's time to fine-tune my condition." (더 격식)
- "I need to fine-tune my condition now." (더 직설적)
- "Now I'm fine-tuning my condition." (진행 중)

### "6-minute pace"

**Original Korean:** "6분 페이스"

**Pace Vocabulary in Running:**

**What is "pace"?**
- 시간당 거리 아님 (속도)
- 거리당 시간 (분:초/km)
- 예: "6-minute pace" = 1km에 6분 걸린다

**Common Paces:**
| Pace | Description | Speed (km/h) |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 4-minute pace | 매우 빠름 (sprint) | ~15 |
| 5-minute pace | 빠름 (5K race) | ~12 |
| 6-minute pace | 중간 (10K race) | ~10 |
| 7-minute pace | 편함 (long run) | ~8.5 |
| 8-minute pace | 아주 편함 (recovery) | ~7.5 |

**Speaking About Pace:**
- "What pace are you running?" (무슨 페이스로?)
- "I ran at a 6-minute pace." (6분 페이스로 뛰었어)
- "Can you keep up this pace?" (이 속도 유지할 수 있어?)

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"This morning I kept a 6-minute pace."
"For the long run, I stay at a 7-minute pace."
"My race pace is around 5:30 per kilometer."
```

## 1.2 School & Social Interactions

**Original Korean:** "레이스 준비는 잘 되고 있어?"

**Structure:** How's + [noun] + going?

**Examples:**

|-------|---------------|--------|

| "Could be better." | 낫지 않음 | ★★☆☆☆ |

## 1.3 Health & Recovery Advice

### "Don't push yourself too hard."

**Original Korean:** "자신을 너무 밀어붙이지 마."

**"Push yourself" Meaning:** 자신의 한계를 밀다, 무리하다

**Intensity Spectrum of "Push":**
| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "Take it easy." | 편하게 해 | 무리 금지 |
| "Don't overdo it." | 과하게 하지 말아 | 주의 |
| "Don't push yourself too hard." | 너무 밀지 마 | 경고 |
| "Push your limits." | 한계 밀다 | 도전 권유 |
| "Go all out." | 전부 다 하다 | 최대 노력 |

**Health Advice Phrases:**
- "Get good sleep." (충분히 자다)
- "Eat well." (잘 먹다)
- "Stay hydrated." (수분 유지하다)
- "Take rest days." (휴식일 가지다)
- "Don't skip meals." (식사 거르지 말다)

---

### "Get good sleep, eat well."

**Original Korean:** "잘 자고, 잘 먹어."

**Fragment Pattern:** 명령문 + 쉼표 + 명령문 = 여러 조언

**Why This Works:**
- 여러 조언을 동등한 중요도로 제시
- 리스트 형태로 강조
- 영어의 자연스러운 조언 방식

**Health Advice Combinations:**
```
"Get good sleep, eat well."
"Stay hydrated, stretch regularly."
"Listen to your body, don't overdo it."
"Warm up properly, cool down afterward."
```

**Dialogue:**
```
Doctor: "You need to get better."
Patient: "What should I do?"
Doctor: "Get good sleep, eat well, and drink water."
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING ATHLETIC PROGRESS & TIMELINE (운동 진행과 시간 표현)

## 3.1 Countdown & Timeline Expressions

### "T-20" / "Twenty days left"

**Original Korean:** "T-20" / "20일 남았다"

**Countdown Vocabulary:**

**"T-time" Meaning:** 발사 시간(T)까지 남은 시간
- 과학/우주 용어에서 기원
- 스포츠, 이벤트에서도 사용

**Examples:**
| T-time | Context | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| T-60 | 경주 60초 전 | 경주 시작까지 60초 |
| T-20 | 마라톤 20일 전 | 경주까지 20일 |
| T-0 | 경주 시작 | 지금 |
| T+3 | 경주 후 3일 | 경주 끝난 후 3일 |

**Alternative Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "Twenty days left." | 20일 남았다 |
| "Three weeks to go." | 3주 남았다 |
| "The race is in 20 days." | 경주가 20일 남았다 |
| "20 days until race day." | 경주까지 20일 |

**Dialogue:**
```
A: "How many days until the marathon?"
B: "T-20. Twenty days left."
A: "Are you nervous?"
B: "A little, but excited mostly."
```

---

### "Twenty days from now. 21km."

**Original Korean:** "지금부터 20일 후. 21km."

**Fragment Timeline Expressions:**

**Why These Are Fragments:**
- 시간 표현 강조
- 거리 강조
- 문장을 끊어서 긴장감 표현
- 일기 스타일

**Future Timeline Expressions:**
| Time | Korean | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| 20 days from now | 지금부터 20일 후 | "20 days from now, I'll finish." |
| two weeks from today | 오늘로부터 2주 후 | "Two weeks from today is race day." |
| in three weeks | 3주 후 | "In three weeks, it'll all be over." |
| by next month | 다음 달까지 | "By next month, I should be recovered." |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Starting [time], I'm [action]"

**Structure:** Starting + [time/date] + [subject] + present continuous

**From Text:** "Starting today, I'm not increasing distance anymore."

| Time | Action | Full Sentence |
|------|--------|---------------|
| today | not eating sugar | "Starting today, I'm not eating sugar." |
| tomorrow | waking up earlier | "Starting tomorrow, I'm waking up earlier." |
| next week | going to the gym | "Starting next week, I'm going to the gym." |
| next month | working harder | "Starting next month, I'm working harder." |

**Variations:**
- "From now on, I'm not increasing distance."
- "I'm done increasing distance."
- "As of today, no more distance increases."

---

## Pattern 2: "Right now, [noun] is what matters most."

**Structure:** Right now + [noun] + is what matters most

**From Text:** "Right now, recovery is what matters most."

| Situation | What Matters | Full Sentence |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| 경주 1주일 전 | recovery | "Right now, recovery is what matters most." |
| 시험 기간 | studying | "Right now, studying is what matters most." |
| 아기 있을 때 | sleep | "Right now, sleep is what matters most." |
| 건강 위험 | staying safe | "Right now, staying safe is what matters most." |

---

## Pattern 3: "[Action] doesn't require [action]"

**Structure:** [Gerund] + doesn't require [noun/gerund]

**Understanding the Negation:**
- "Marathon prep isn't just about running."
- "Recovery matters just as much."

**Meaning:** 마라톤은 뛰기만이 아니고, 회복도 중요하다

**Related Patterns:**
```
"Success doesn't just come from hard work."
"Health isn't only about exercise."
"Friendship doesn't depend on proximity."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "*Affirmation. Statement. Instruction.*"

**Structure:** Italicized thought + Statement + Instruction

**From Text:**
```
*No more pushing hard now.* I'll keep running,
but I'm cutting back on distance and speed.
Sleep enough, eat well, let my body rest.
```

**This Pattern Shows:**
- 내적 결심 (*italic = 생각*)
- 행동 설명 (무엇을 할 거다)
- 구체적 지시사항 (어떻게 해야 한다)

---

## Pattern 5: "Twenty days from now. [Distance]. *I can do this.*"

**Structure:** Timeline + Result + Self-encouragement

**From Text:**

```

**This Pattern Shows:**

- Progressive intensity
- Final affirmation in italics

**Similar Patterns:**
```
"The race is next month. 42km. I'm ready."

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Checking Training Progress

```
Friend: "How's your marathon training going?"
You: "Pretty well. I'm in the taper phase now."
Friend: "What's that?"
You: "It's when you cut back on distance and speed before the race."
Friend: "That sounds nice. Less running?"
You: "Kind of. I'm still running, but keeping it easy."
Friend: "Smart. When's the race?"
You: "Twenty days from now. I'm getting nervous."
Friend: "You'll do great. You've trained hard."
You: "Thanks. I hope so."
```

### Scenario 2: Teacher-Student Conversation

```
Teacher: "I heard about your marathon plans. How's the prep going?"
Student: "Good, thanks for asking. I'm on track."
Teacher: "Right now, recovery is what matters most, you know."
Student: "I know. I've been stretching and getting good sleep."
Teacher: "Excellent. Don't push yourself too hard."
Student: "I won't. Just twenty days left."
Teacher: "You've got this. Keep training smart."
Student: "Thank you. I really appreciate your support."
```

### Scenario 3: Recovering After a Training Run

```
A: "You look exhausted. How was the run?"
B: "Good. 7 kilometers at an easy pace."
A: "That's impressive. What's next?"
B: "Recovery. I'm rolling out my legs and stretching."
A: "For how long?"
B: "About thirty minutes, then I'll ice my knees."
A: "That's a lot of work."
B: "Yeah, but recovery matters just as much as the running."
A: "I guess that makes sense for a marathon."
B: "Exactly. You can't just run hard. You need balance."
```
