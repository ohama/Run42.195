# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Greeting & Planning

### "Hey, want to try going further than usual?"

**Original Korean:** "평소보다 더 멀리 달려볼까?"

**Grammar Point:** Informal question with "want to" + action

**Structure:** Casual verb + want to + infinitive?

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Context | Tone |
|------------|---------|------|
| "Want to try going further?" | 활동 제안 | 친근함 |
| "Feel like pushing yourself?" | 도전 제안 | 약간 도전적 |
| "Ready to go the distance?" | 완주 도전 | 긍정적 |
| "Wanna see what you can do?" | 능력 시험 | 동기 부여 |
| "Up for a challenge?" | 도전 제안 | 재미있음 |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "Do you want to try going further?"
- "Want to push it a little?"
- "How about we go a bit further?"
- "You ready to tackle 5km?"

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: Hey, want to try going further than usual?
B: How much further?
A: Maybe 5km. Celebrate your milestone.
B: 5km? That's ambitious.
A: Come on, you can do it.
```

**Cultural Note:**
친구 관계에서는 very casual하게 말할 수 있음. 격식 있는 상황에서는 "Would you like to..." 사용.

---

### "You good with that?"

**Original Korean:** "괜찮아?"

**Nuance:** 동의 여부 확인, 비공식적 확인

**Formality Spectrum:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "You good with that?" | "Are you okay with that?" | "Does that work for you?" |
| "Cool?" | "Is that alright?" | "Would that be acceptable?" |
| "Sound good?" | "Does that sound good?" | "Does that suit you?" |

**Pronunciation Tips:**
- "You good" = /ju ɡʊd/ (g를 약하게, d는 거의 안 들림)
- 캐주얼하게 말할 때는 "You good?" (질문 톤)

**Similar Check-in Phrases:**
- "Does that work?"
- "Are you comfortable with that?"
- "That okay?"
- "Is that cool?"

**Physical Context:**
이 문맥에서는 5km 목표에 대해 확인하는 것.

## 1.2 During the Run

**Original Korean:** "천천히 가. 서두를 필요 없어."

**Structure:** Imperative + reassurance

**Key Elements:**
- "Go slow" = 천천히 (명령)
- "No rush" = 급할 필요 없어 (안심)

**Variations:**

| Expression | Meaning | Tone |
|------------|---------|------|
| "Go slow." | 천천히 가 | 중립 |
| "Take your time." | 천천히 해 | 친절 |

## 1.3 Post-Run Reflections

### "That's runner's high."

**Original Korean:** "그게 러너스 하이야"

**Scientific Term:** Brain releases endorphins during exercise

**"High" Multiple Meanings:**

| Context | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 약물/알콜 | euphoric state from substances |
| 운동 | endorphin-induced happiness |
| 감정 | temporary emotional peak |
| 높이 | physical elevation |

**Related Experience Terms:**
- "endorphin rush" = 엔도르핀 러시
- "getting a natural high" = 자연스러운 쾌감
- "the runner's euphoria" = 러너의 황홀감
- "exercise-induced happiness" = 운동으로 인한 행복감

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How are you feeling?
B: It's hard, but... I feel good.
A: That's runner's high. Your brain releases good chemicals.
B: Really? Is that what this is?
A: Yeah. That's why runners are addicted.
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING RUNNING & PARTNERSHIP (달리기와 우정 묘사)

## 3.1 Running Together vs. Alone

### "It was different from running alone."

**Original Korean:** "혼자 달리는 것과는 달랐다"

**Comparison Structure:** It was different from...

**Full Pattern:**
```
It + was + different from + noun/gerund
```

**Examples:**

| Comparison | Sentence |
|------------|----------|
| 혼자 달리기 vs 함께 | "It was different from running alone." |
| 예상 vs 현실 | "It was different from what I expected." |
| 작년 vs 올해 | "This year is different from last year." |
| 이 도시 vs 저 도시 | "Seoul is different from Busan." |

**Emotional Nuance:**
The difference is positive - partnership provides something that soloing doesn't.

---

**Original Korean:** "누군가 옆에 있고. 우리가 같은 방향으로 가고 있다."

**Internal Reflection:** Thoughts presented as fragments

**Why This Works:**

- Reader feels the character's realization
- Broken sentences show emotional truth

**Partnership Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "beside you" | 곁에 있다 |
| "by your side" | 당신 곁에 |

**Dialogue about Support:**
```

**Why Minseo's Statement Matters:**

---

## Pattern 2: "Because you're there, [consequence]"

**Structure:** Because + you + [action], [impact on speaker]

**Cause-Effect in Relationships:**

| Because | Result | Full Sentence |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| 너 때문 | 멈추지 않음 | "Because you're there, I could keep running." |
| 너 때문 | 겁이 안 남 | "Because you're there, I'm not scared." |

| 너 때문 | 가능 | "Because you're there, I can do it." |

**Emotional Vulnerability:**
These statements show deep gratitude and interdependence.

---

## Pattern 3: "If I'd been alone, [negative], But because [help], [positive]"

**Structure:** Hypothetical without [support] vs. with [support]

**Full Pattern:**
```
"If I'd been alone, [would have quit].
But because you were there, [kept going]."
```

**Examples:**

| Alone | With Support |
|-------|--------------|
| "I would've walked at 3km." | "I kept running." |
| "I would've given up." | "I finished." |
| "I'd be sitting at home." | "I'm here with you." |
| "I'd have failed." | "I succeeded." |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: What would you have done without me?
B: Probably quit at 3km and walked home.
A: And now?
B: I finished 5km. And I feel amazing.
```

```

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|

| 문장 반복 | "It matters. It matters so much." |
| 변형 반복 | "Hard. Really hard. Almost impossible." |
| 강조 반복 | "No. No way. Absolutely not." |

## 7.4 Ellipsis & Pause

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Proposing a Running Date

```
A: Hey, want to try going further than usual?
B: How much further?
A: Maybe 5km. We could celebrate your milestone.
B: 5km is a lot for me. You sure?
A: Absolutely. I believe in you.
B: You're good with that? Me taking it slow?
A: You good with that? Me slowing down for you?
B: We'll figure it out together.
```

---

### Scenario 2: During the Difficult Middle

```
A: You breathing hard. How you holding up?
B: It's... it's tough. Are we halfway there?
A: Almost. Just a little more.
B: I want to quit. My legs are killing me.
A: I know. But you've got this. Keep going.
B: What if I can't?
A: Then we walk. But I know you can.
B: [breathing heavily] Okay. Okay. Let's keep going.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Post-Run Conversation

```
A: We did it! We actually did it!
B: I still can't believe it.
A: You ran 5km, Minseo. 5 kilometers.
B: If I'd been alone, I would've quit at 3.
A: But you weren't alone.
B: That's why I made it. Because you were there.
A: You did the running though. That was all you.
B: But having you there made it possible.
```

---

### Scenario 4: Reflection on Companionship

```
A: Hey, what were you thinking about back there?
B: Just... how different this was from running alone.
A: Different how?
B: When you run alone, it's just you. Your pace, your struggle.
A: And now?
B: There's someone beside you. Someone pushing too.
A: Is that better?
B: So much better. It matters. Having someone there.
```
