# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Expressions of Doubt & Questioning

### "A strange thought hit me today."

**Original Korean:** "오늘 이상한 생각이 들었다"

**Analysis:**
- "Hit" = 갑자기 떠오르다, 생각나다
- 주로 부정적이거나 예상 밖의 생각에 사용
- 감정적 영향을 강조할 때 좋음

**Variations by Intensity:**

| Expression | Intensity | Nuance |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "A thought crossed my mind." | ★☆☆ | 가볍게 떠오르다 |
| "A thought occurred to me." | ★★☆ | 좀 더 의도적 |
| "A thought hit me." | ★★★ | 갑작스럽고 영향 있음 |
| "A disturbing thought struck me." | ★★★★ | 불안감 강조 |
| "It suddenly dawned on me." | ★★★★ | 깨달음의 순간 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's wrong? You look distracted.
B: A strange thought hit me earlier.
A: What do you mean?
B: I was just wondering if I'm actually happy doing what I do.
A: That's pretty deep.
```

---

### "Why do I run?"

**Original Korean:** "왜 달리지?"

**Analysis:**
- 가장 단순한 질문 형식이 가장 강력
- 이 문맥에서는 자기 자신에게 던지는 본질적 질문

**Self-Questioning Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example | Depth |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Simple why | "Why do I run?" | 표면적 질문 |
| Challenging | "Why am I even doing this?" | 의심 |
| Searching | "What's the real reason?" | 더 깊은 탐색 |
| Desperate | "What's the point anymore?" | 절망감 |

**Related Questions:**
- "What's the point?" (무슨 소용이야?)
- "What's it all for?" (뭐가 목적이야?)
- "Does any of this matter?" (이게 중요해?)
- "Am I crazy?" (내가 미쳤나?)

---

### "Taking the bus would be so much easier."

**Original Korean:** "버스를 타는 게 훨씬 쉬울 텐데"

**Grammar Point:** Conditional comparison with "would be"

**Structure:** [Action] would be so much + adjective

**Comparison Patterns:**

| Sentence | Contrast | Implication |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| "Running is hard. Taking the bus would be easier." | 어려움 vs. 쉬움 | 포기하고 싶음 |
| "Writing takes time. Recording would be faster." | 방식 비교 | 효율성 추구 |
| "Driving is expensive. Walking would be cheaper." | 비용 비교 | 대안 모색 |

**Intensifier "So Much":**
- Makes the comparison feel dramatic
- Emphasizes the temptation
- Creates internal conflict

**Example Sentences:**
```
"It would be so much easier to just quit."
"Making food would be so much faster than cooking from scratch."
"Saying no would be so much simpler."
```

---

### "Maybe I'll just take the bus today."

**Original Korean:** "오늘은 버스나 탈까"

**"Maybe" + Resignation Pattern:**

| Pattern | Meaning | Commitment Level |
|---------|---------|------------------|
| "I'll run." | 확정 | ★★★★★ |
| "I should run." | 의무감 | ★★★☆☆ |
| "I might run." | 불확실 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| "Maybe I'll run." | 약한 의도 | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| "I guess I could run." | 심한 의심 | ☆☆☆☆☆ |

**Resignation Expressions:**
- "Maybe I'll just..." (그냥... 할까)
- "I could always..." (그냥... 할 수 있지)
- "Perhaps I should..." (어쩌면 ... 해야 하나)
- "I suppose..." (그렇겠지만...)

## 1.2 Responding to Internal Conflict

### "But my heart wasn't in it."

**Original Korean:** "그런데 마음이 없었다"

**Idiom Analysis:** "Heart's not in it" = 진심이 없다, 마음이 없다

**Similar Idiomatic Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "My heart's not in it." | 진심이 없어 | 마지못해 하는 것 |
| "I'm not feeling it." | 기분이 아니야 | 몸과 마음의 불일치 |
| "I'm just going through the motions." | 형식적으로 하고 있어 | 의욕 없음 |
| "I'm half-hearted about it." | 반쪽짜리야 | 충분한 노력 안 함 |
| "My mind's elsewhere." | 마음이 다른 곳에 | 집중 못 함 |

**Physical Manifestations:**
- 움직임이 느릴 수 있음
- 열정 부족 보임
- 노력이 덜함
- 결과가 좋지 않음

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How was practice today?
B: I went, but my heart wasn't in it.
A: What do you mean?
B: I was just tired. Didn't feel motivated.
A: Everyone has those days.
B: Yeah, but it happens too often now.
```

---

### "I don't know."

**Original Korean:** "모르겠다"

**Nuance:** 확실한 답이 없는 상태

**Levels of "I don't know":**

| Response | Certainty | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| "I know the answer." | 100% | 확실함 |
| "I think I know." | 70% | 대체로 확신 |
| "I'm not sure." | 40% | 불확실 |
| "I don't know." | 10% | 거의 모름 |
| "I have no idea." | 0% | 전혀 모름 |

**"I don't know" in Philosophy:**
- "I don't know why I run." (본질적 질문)
- "I don't know the answer." (사실 불명)
- "I don't know what to do." (행동 결정 못함)

---

### "Out of breath, sure, but..."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 찬 건 맞지만..."

**Grammar Point:** Concession + contrast with "but"

**Structure:** [Concede point], but [real reason]

**Concession Patterns:**

| Concession | Real Point | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| 피곤함 | 더 큰 문제 | "I'm tired, but my motivation died." |
| 두통 | 더 큰 이유 | "I have a headache, but it's not that." |
| 비용 | 더 큰 문제 | "The cost is high, but that's not the issue." |

**Using "Sure":**
- "Sure" = 인정하다
- "Sure, I'm tired, but..." (피곤한 건 맞지만)
- Very useful for showing internal reasoning

# PART 2: INTERNAL STATES & MOTIVATION (내적 상태와 동기)

## 2.1 Loss of Motivation

### "My motivation just died."

**Original Korean:** "동기가 사라져 버렸다"

**Verb Choices - "Died" vs. Alternatives:**

| Verb | Intensity | Suddenness |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| "Faded" | ★★☆ | 천천히 사라짐 |
| "Disappeared" | ★★★ | 갑자기 없어짐 |
| "Died" | ★★★★ | 확실히 끝남 |
| "Evaporated" | ★★★★ | 흔적 없이 사라짐 |
| "Vanished" | ★★★★★ | 신비롭게 없어짐 |

**Related Motivation Expressions:**

| Expression | Stage | Meaning |
|------------|-------|---------|
| "I'm losing motivation." | 초기 | 동기가 약해지는 중 |
| "My motivation is gone." | 진행 | 더 이상 없음 |
| "I've lost all motivation." | 극단 | 완전히 끝남 |
| "Motivation just died." | 최악 | 확실한 끝 |

**Example Contexts:**
```
"I was excited about the project, but my motivation just died after the setback."
"He was training hard, then his motivation just died when he got injured."
"The first week was great, but by week two, my motivation just died."
```

---

### "At first, it was because of motion sickness."

**Original Korean:** "처음엔 차멀미 때문이었어"

**"At first...but now" Pattern:** Change over time

**Structure:** At first, [original reason]. But now, [situation changed].

**Progress Narratives:**

| Time | Reason | Why Change |
|------|--------|-----------|
| At first | 멀미 때문 | 강제적 (건강 문제) |
| In May | 늦잠 때문 | 우발적 (실수) |
| Summer | 습관 형성 | 자동적 (루틴) |
| Now | ??? | 불명 (의심 시작) |

**Dialogue:**
```
A: Why did you start running?
B: At first, it was because of motion sickness on the bus.
A: And now?
B: Now... I'm not really sure. That's the problem.
A: You've lost interest?
B: Not exactly. But I've lost the reason.
```

---

### "It's summer break."

**Original Korean:** "지금 여름 방학이야"

**Context Shift:**
- Season/schedule change removes original constraint
- External pressure gone = needs new internal motivation

**Similar Contextual Changes:**

| Change | Lost Reason | New Challenge |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| "It's summer break." | 통학 압박 제거 | 자발적 동기 필요 |
| "I got a new job." | 이전 루틴 | 새로운 습관 |
| "We moved." | 이전 환경 | 새로운 적응 |
| "I'm retired now." | 일의 구조 | 목표 재설정 |

## 2.2 Searching for Meaning

### "Is that reason enough?"

**Original Korean:** "그게 충분한 이유가 될까?"

**Questioning Adequacy:**

| Question | Level of Doubt |
|----------|--------|
| "That makes sense." | ★☆☆ (만족) |
| "I suppose." | ★★☆ (약간 의심) |
| "Is that enough?" | ★★★ (진심 의문) |
| "Is that reason enough?" | ★★★★ (본질 의문) |
| "Does anything matter?" | ★★★★★ (절망) |

**Sufficiency Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "That's good enough." | 충분해 |
| "That's barely enough." | 간신히 충분해 |
| "Is that enough?" | 충분할까? |
| "That's not nearly enough." | 전혀 부족해 |

---

### "Wouldn't it be easier to just live comfortably?"

**Original Korean:** "그냥 편하게 살면 안 될까?"

**Rhetorical Question Structure:**
- Suggests attractive alternative
- Implies current path is harder
- Questions value of difficulty

**Wouldn't it be easier...?** Pattern:

| Question | Hidden Assumption |
|----------|------------------|
| "Wouldn't it be easier to quit?" | 계속하기가 어렵다 |
| "Wouldn't it be easier to say no?" | 동의하기가 편하다 |
| "Wouldn't it be easier to give up?" | 노력이 힘들다 |

**Temptation Expressions:**
- "That would be the easy way out."
- "Why make it harder than it has to be?"
- "I could just..."
- "It would be so much simpler to..."

---

### "Wouldn't that make me happier?"

**Original Korean:** "그게 나를 더 행복하게 해 주지 않을까?"

**Questioning Happiness:**

This is the deepest question - not just comfort, but actual happiness.

**Happiness Questions Spectrum:**

| Question | Assumption |
|----------|-----------|
| "Wouldn't that be easier?" | 쉬움 = 좋음 |
| "Wouldn't that be more fun?" | 재미 = 행복 |
| "Wouldn't that make me happier?" | 쾌락 = 행복 |
| "Wouldn't that be the right choice?" | 도덕적 선택 |

**Related Reflections:**
- "What really makes me happy?"
- "Is comfort the same as happiness?"
- "Does doing hard things matter?"
- "What's the point of suffering?"

# PART 3: DESCRIBING MOTIVATION & EMOTIONAL STATES (감정 상태 묘사)

## 3.1 Emotional Weight

### "Something felt heavy inside."

**Original Korean:** "뭔가 무거운 게 있었다"

**"Heavy" as Emotional Metaphor:**

| "Heavy" Usage | Meaning |
|---------------|---------|
| Physical | 무거운 (literal) |
| Emotional | 마음이 무겁다 |
| Atmospheric | 분위기가 무겁다 |
| Burden | 짐 같은 책임 |

**Emotional Weight Vocabulary:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "Something felt off." | ★★☆ | 이상함 |
| "Something felt wrong." | ★★★ | 틀렸음 |
| "Something felt heavy." | ★★★★ | 무거움 |
| "Everything felt hollow." | ★★★★★ | 공허함 |

**Dialogue:**
```
A: How was the lunch with your friend?
B: Good, I guess. But something felt heavy inside.
A: What do you mean?
B: I didn't want to worry him. So I pretended everything was fine.
A: That must have been exhausting.
B: Yeah, it was.
```

### "I played along."

**Original Korean:** "나는 따라갔다"

**Phrasal Verb Analysis:** "Play along" = 함께 맞춰주다, 연기하다

**"Play Along" Contexts:**

| Situation | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Friend excited | 상대 기분 맞춰주기 |
| Joke | 농담에 웃어주기 |
| Pretense | 거짓 감정 표현 |
| Performance | 연기하기 |

**Related Expressions:**
- "I went along with it." (함께했다)
- "I played the part." (역할을 했다)
- "I put on a smile." (웃음을 지었다)
- "I pretended." (척했다)
- "I didn't let on." (들키지 않게 했다)

**Why People "Play Along":**
- Don't want to hurt someone's feelings
- Don't want to be a burden
- Protecting their own image
- Avoiding conflict

## 3.2 Fatigue & Exhaustion

### "I was tired this morning."

**Original Korean:** "이 아침 피곤했어"

**Tiredness Spectrum:**

| Expression | Level | Recovery Time |
|------------|-------|---------------|
| "I'm a little tired." | ★☆☆ | 짧은 휴식 |
| "I'm tired." | ★★☆ | 한 번의 수면 |
| "I'm exhausted." | ★★★ | 여러 번의 수면 |
| "I'm dead tired." | ★★★★ | 하루 쉬어야 함 |
| "I can barely keep my eyes open." | ★★★★★ | 즉시 수면 필요 |

**Cause vs. Effect:**
```
"I stayed up late last night." (원인)
"I'm tired this morning." (결과)
```

**English Usage Note:**
- Prefer specific time reference (morning, today, right now)
- Shows cause-and-effect thinking
- More relatable than general tiredness

---

### "My body felt heavy."

**Original Korean:** "몸이 무거웠다"

**Physical Manifestations of Fatigue:**

| Expression | Physical Sign |
|------------|--------------|
| "Body felt heavy." | 움직임이 둔함 |
| "I felt weak." | 힘이 없음 |
| "My limbs ached." | 팔다리가 아픔 |
| "I could barely move." | 거의 움직일 수 없음 |
| "Everything hurt." | 전신 통증 |

**"Felt" vs "Was":**
- "My body felt heavy." = 주관적 느낌 (더 자연스러움)
- "My body was heavy." = 객관적 서술 (덜 자연스러움)

---

### "I'm tired."

**Original Korean:** "피곤했어"

**Why Repetition Matters:**
- Character emphasizes this feeling
- Simple statement = powerful
- Shows deep exhaustion

**Building on Tiredness:**

| Progression | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| "I was tired." | 상태 설명 |
| "I stayed up late." + "I was tired." | 원인 + 결과 |
| "I didn't want to run." + "I was tired." | 동기 제거 |

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS & ACTION VERBS (구동사와 행동 동사)

## 4.1 Motivation & Decision-Making

### "debated whether to go run or not"

**"Debate" Nuance:** 내부에서 양쪽을 놓고 고민하다

**Debate vs. Consider vs. Decide:**

| Verb | Process | Certainty |
|------|---------|-----------|
| consider | 생각만 함 | 불명 |
| debate | 양쪽 논거 | 불명 |
| decide | 결정함 | 확실 |
| choose | 선택함 | 확실 |

**Related Thinking Verbs:**
- "I was torn between..." (양쪽 사이에 갈등)
- "I weighed the options." (저울질하다)
- "I went back and forth." (앞뒤로 오락가락)
- "I couldn't decide." (결정 못함)

**Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you run this morning?
B: I debated whether to go or not.
A: And?
B: I went anyway, even though I didn't feel like it.
A: That took willpower.
B: Not really. I just felt obligated.
```

---

### "kept thinking, Why am I running?"

**"Keep + -ing" Pattern:** 계속 반복되는 행동/생각

**Structure:** kept + [verb-ing]

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "kept thinking" | 계속 생각함 |
| "kept asking myself" | 계속 자신에게 묻다 |
| "kept doubting" | 계속 의심함 |
| "kept hoping" | 계속 바라다 |

**Intrusive Thought Pattern:**
- Unwanted thoughts that return
- Not easily dismissible
- Shows mental struggle

**Related Expressions:**
- "I couldn't stop thinking about..."
- "The thought kept coming back to..."
- "I couldn't shake the feeling that..."

---

### "I stopped."

**Original Korean:** "멈췄다"

**Multiple Meanings in Context:**

1. **Physical:** Stopped running
2. **Mental:** Stopped pushing
3. **Emotional:** Gave up

**The Power of One-Word Sentences:**
- Creates dramatic pause
- Shows finality
- Indicates resignation

**Stopped + Different Objects:**

| Object | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| "I stopped." | 멈췄다 (모든 게) |
| "I stopped running." | 달리기 멈췄다 |
| "I stopped trying." | 노력 포기했다 |
| "I stopped believing." | 신념 포기했다 |

## 4.2 Physical Movement

### "walked the rest of the way"

**Original Korean:** "나머지를 걸어갔다"

**"Walk" vs "Run" - Symbolic Shift:**

This is important: switching from running to walking shows mental/emotional change.

| Mode | Effort | Symbolism |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Running | 높음 | 의지, 목표 추구 |
| Walking | 중간 | 계속 진행 |
| Standing | 낮음 | 멈춤 |
| Sitting | 없음 | 완전 포기 |

**"The rest of the way" Idiom:**
- means remaining distance
- but also = remaining struggle, remaining life

---

### "I thought about it as I walked."

**Original Korean:** "걸으면서 생각했다"

**Thinking While Moving:**
- Common in English narratives
- Shows internal monologue during action
- Creates narrative flow

**Paired Activities:**

| Activity Pair | Meaning |
|---------------|---------|
| "Walked and thought" | 신체 활동 중 명상 |
| "Drove and listened" | 수동적 활동 중 수용 |
| "Sat and watched" | 관찰하며 생각 |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Repeating Questions for Emphasis

**Structure:** 같은 질문을 반복하되 맥락을 달리함

**From Text:**
```
"Why do I run?"
"Why do I keep going?"
"Why am I still running?"
"Why do I do something that's so hard?"
"Why do I run?" (다시)
```

**Why Repetition Works:**
1. Shows obsessive thinking
2. Emphasizes confusion
3. Builds emotional intensity
4. Shows deepening doubt

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: You look stressed.
B: I am. I keep asking myself "Why am I doing this?"
A: Doing what?
B: This job, this routine, everything. And I can't answer the question.
A: Maybe that's okay. Not everything needs an answer.
```

---

## Pattern 2: "Because X? True. But Y..."

**Structure:** 한 이유를 인정하되 더 큰 이유가 있음을 시사

**From Text:**
```
"Because I like running? True. I do like it. But on days like today, it's just hard."
"Because it's making me healthier? That's true too. But is that reason enough?"
```

**Analysis & Practice:**

| Reason | Concession | Counter |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| 재미 있음 | True | But not every day |
| 건강 개선 | That's true | But is it enough? |
| 친구 있음 | I suppose | But that's not really it |

**Similar Patterns:**
- "I want to, but..."
- "It makes sense, but..."
- "That's logical, but..."

---

## Pattern 3: "What if...? What if...?"

**Structure:** 여러 가정을 나열하며 고민

**From Text:**
```
"What if I took the bus? I could sleep thirty more minutes."
"What if I feel like this tomorrow too?"
```

**Escalating Worry Pattern:**

| Level | Question | Anxiety |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | "What if I feel this tomorrow?" | 중간 |
| 2 | "What if it continues?" | 높음 |
| 3 | "What if it never stops?" | 매우 높음 |

**"What if" Conversation:**
```
A: I'm worried about this slump.
B: What specifically worries you?
A: What if I feel like this tomorrow? And the next day?
B: Let's focus on today first.
A: But what if it gets worse?
```

---

## Pattern 4: Self-Diagnosis Pattern

**Structure:** "Maybe that's what's happening to me."

**From Text:**
```
"When you're exercising and suddenly lose all motivation."
"When you start asking yourself, 'Why am I even doing this?'"
"Maybe that's what's happening to me."
```

**Pattern Structure:**
1. Learn/Hear about phenomenon
2. Recognize parallel to own experience
3. Self-diagnose

**Similar Self-Diagnosis Patterns:**
- "I've heard about that. That's probably what's happening."
- "I think I might have... [condition]"
- "That sounds like what I'm going through."

**Dialogue:**
```
A: I've heard of something called "runner's burnout."
B: What is it?
A: When you lose motivation even though you love the activity.
B: Oh. I think that might be me.
```

---

## Pattern 5: The Resigned Conclusion

**Structure:** 분석 → 인정 → 수용

**From Text:**
```
"I think I'm in a slump."
"Why do I do this? What's the point of making myself suffer?"
"I don't know. What about tomorrow?"
"For now, I should just sleep."
```

**Logic Flow:**
1. Identify problem (슬럼프)
2. Question purpose (의미 있나?)
3. Accept uncertainty (모르겠다)
4. Take small action (일단 자자)

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

| Fragment | Effect |

- Creates emphasis
- Shows emotional intensity

## 7.2 Repetition for Emphasis

**From Text:**
- Multiple "Why do I..." questions
- Multiple "I don't know" responses
- Repeated "But..." to show conflicting thoughts

**Stylistic Effect:**
- Shows circular thinking
- Indicates obsession
- Builds emotional weight

## 7.3 Hedging & Uncertainty

| Hedger | Example |
|--------|---------|
| "kind of" | "It's kind of pointless." |
| "sort of" | "I sort of gave up." |
| "I guess" | "I guess I'm in a slump." |
| "maybe" | "Maybe I should quit." |

**Why Teens Use Hedging:**
- Less confident/experienced
- Avoid absolute statements
- Soften potentially critical views
- Show humility

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Talking to a Friend About Losing Motivation

```
A: You don't seem excited about running anymore.
B: I'm not. I don't know what happened.
A: When did it change?
B: I can't pinpoint it. At first, I ran because of bus sickness. Then summer came and...
A: And what?
B: The reason disappeared. Now I'm just running out of habit.
A: Have you thought about stopping?
B: Every day. But I feel like I should keep going.
A: For what? You need a reason.
B: I know. That's what kills me.
```

### Scenario 2: Talking to Someone About Pretending

```
A: You seemed off at lunch yesterday.
B: Yeah, I wasn't really feeling it.
A: But you seemed fine.
B: I was playing along. Minseo was so excited about his running shoes.
A: You didn't want to hurt his feelings?
B: Exactly. But something felt heavy inside.
A: That must be exhausting.
B: It is. I'm tired of pretending.
```

### Scenario 3: Describing a Slump

```
A: What's wrong? You look down.
B: I think I'm in a slump.
A: With running?
B: With everything. I keep asking myself why I even bother.
A: That's concerning.
B: It is. It's like I've lost all motivation overnight.
A: Have you talked to anyone about it?
B: Not yet. For now, I'm just trying to sleep more.
```
