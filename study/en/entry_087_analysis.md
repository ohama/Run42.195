# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Time & Transition Expressions

### "Break is almost over."

**Original Text:** "Break is almost over."

**Korean Equivalent:** 방학이 거의 끝났다

**Analysis:**
- 간결하고 직접적인 표현
- "almost"로 임박한 상황 표현
- 학교생활에서 매우 자주 쓰이는 표현

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Break's almost over" | "Break is almost over" | "The break is nearly concluded" |
| "Break's ending soon" | "The vacation is almost finished" | "The holiday period is drawing to a close" |

**Nuance Spectrum:**

| Expression | Nuance | Example |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "almost over" | 거의 끝남 (중립) | "Break is almost over." |
| "nearly over" | 거의 끝남 (약간 격식) | "The semester is nearly over." |
| "coming to an end" | 끝나가는 중 | "Winter break is coming to an end." |
| "winding down" | 서서히 마무리되는 | "The vacation is winding down." |
| "just about done" | 거의 다 됨 (캐주얼) | "Break's just about done." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: When does school start?
B: Next Monday. Break is almost over.
A: Already? That went by fast.
B: I know. Time flies during vacation.
A: Are you ready to go back?
B: Kind of. I'm actually looking forward to it.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "The break is almost finishing." (진행형 오류)
- ✅ "Break is almost over."
- ❌ "Break almost over." (be동사 누락)
- ✅ "Break is almost over."

**Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "Winter break is ending" | 겨울방학이 끝나간다 | "Winter break is ending soon." |
| "Back to school" | 개학 | "It's back to school time." |
| "The countdown begins" | 카운트다운 시작 | "The countdown to school begins." |

### "I'll be in tenth grade soon."

**Original Text:** "I'll be in tenth grade soon."

**Korean Equivalent:** 곧 2학년이 된다

**Grammar Point:** Future with "will be" + time marker "soon"

**Structure Analysis:**
```
I'll be + [status/grade] + soon
Subject + will + be + noun phrase + time adverb
```

**Grade/Year Expressions:**

| Korean School System | US Equivalent | Expression |
|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| 중학교 1학년 | 7th grade (or 9th) | "I'll be in ninth grade" |
| 중학교 2학년 | 8th grade (or 10th) | "I'll be in tenth grade" |
| 중학교 3학년 | 9th grade (or 11th) | "I'll be in eleventh grade" |

**Note:** In this translation, Korean middle school years are rendered as 9th-11th grade (US high school system).

**Similar Future Expressions:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "I'll be a sophomore soon" | 곧 2학년이 될 거야 |
| "I'm becoming a sophomore" | 2학년이 되고 있어 |
| "I'm going into tenth grade" | 10학년에 진학해 |
| "I'll be starting tenth grade" | 10학년을 시작할 거야 |

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"I'll be in tenth grade soon. Time really flies."
"Next semester, I'll be in eleventh grade."
"In just a few months, I'll be a high schooler."
```

### "When does school start?"

**Original Text:** "When does school start?"

**Korean Equivalent:** 학교 언제 시작해?

**Analysis:**
- 가장 일반적인 개학 시기 질문
- "does"는 일정/스케줄에 대한 현재형 질문
- 친구간 대화에서 흔히 사용

**Question Patterns About School:**

| Question | Korean | Context |
|----------|--------|---------|
| "When does school start?" | 학교 언제 시작해? | 일반적 질문 |
| "What time does school start?" | 등교 시간이 몇 시야? | 시간 질문 |
| "When do classes begin?" | 수업 언제 시작해? | 약간 격식 |
| "When does the semester start?" | 학기 언제 시작해? | 대학생 느낌 |

**Response Patterns:**

| Response | Korean |
|----------|--------|
| "Next Monday" | 다음 주 월요일 |
| "On the 13th" | 13일에 |
| "In a week" | 일주일 뒤에 |
| "After the holidays" | 연휴 끝나고 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: When does school start?
B: Next Monday.
A: Already?
B: Yeah. Tenth grade.
A: Hope we're in the same class.
B: Me too.
```

| Expression | Meaning |

### "Hope we're in the same class."

**Original Text:** "Hope we're in the same class."

**Korean Equivalent:** 같은 반이면 좋겠다

**Grammar Point:** "Hope" + present tense (희망을 나타내는 표현)

**Structure:**
```
Hope + subject + verb (present tense)
"Hope we're in the same class."
"Hope you have a good day."
```

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Hope we're in the same class" | "I hope we're in the same class" | "I sincerely hope we'll be placed in the same class" |

**Similar Hope Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "Hope we're in the same class" | 같은 반이면 좋겠다 | 친구간 |
| "Hope we get the same teacher" | 같은 선생님이면 좋겠다 | 학교 |
| "Hope we have classes together" | 같이 수업 들으면 좋겠다 | 대학 |
| "Hope we're on the same team" | 같은 팀이면 좋겠다 | 스포츠/프로젝트 |

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "Hope we will be in the same class." (will 불필요)
- ✅ "Hope we're in the same class."
- ❌ "I hope we are being in the same class." (진행형 오류)
- ✅ "I hope we're in the same class."

## 1.3 Weather & Time Expressions

### "Cloudy. Cold."

**Original Text:** "Cloudy. Cold."

**Korean Equivalent:** 흐림. 추움.

**Analysis:**
- 일기 형식의 간결한 날씨 묘사
- 완전한 문장 없이 형용사만으로 표현
- 일기/다이어리 특유의 스타일

**Weather Description Patterns:**

| Fragment Style | Full Sentence Style |
|----------------|---------------------|
| "Cloudy. Cold." | "It was cloudy and cold." |
| "Clear. Warm." | "The sky was clear and it was warm." |
| "Rainy. Windy." | "It was rainy and windy." |

**Winter Weather Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean | Usage |
|---------|--------|-------|
| "cloudy" | 흐린 | "Cloudy skies all day" |
| "cold" | 추운 | "It's cold outside" |
| "chilly" | 쌀쌀한 | "A bit chilly this morning" |
| "freezing" | 얼어붙는/매우 추운 | "It's freezing today" |
| "overcast" | 잔뜩 흐린 | "Completely overcast" |

## 2.1 Running & Recovery

**Original Text:** "Did an easy 10km run."

**Korean Equivalent:** 가벼운 10km 달리기를 했다

**Running Intensity Vocabulary:**

| Intensity | Expression | Korean | Purpose |
|-----------|------------|--------|---------|
| ★☆☆☆☆ | "easy run" | 가벼운 달리기 | 회복/유지 |
| ★★☆☆☆ | "light jog" | 가벼운 조깅 | 워밍업 |
| ★★★☆☆ | "steady run" | 일정한 페이스 달리기 | 지구력 |
| ★★★★☆ | "tempo run" | 템포런 | 속도 훈련 |
| ★★★★★ | "sprint" / "all-out" | 전력 질주 | 스피드 |

**"Did + [activity]" Pattern:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "Did an easy 10km run" | 가벼운 10km를 뛰었다 |
| "Did some stretching" | 스트레칭을 했다 |
| "Did a workout" | 운동을 했다 |


### "A recovery run."

**Original Text:** "A recovery run."

**Korean Equivalent:** 회복 러닝

**Running Terminology:**

| Term | Korean | Definition |
|------|--------|------------|
| "recovery run" | 회복 러닝 | 힘든 훈련 후 가벼운 달리기 |
| "long run" | 장거리 달리기 | 긴 거리 달리기 (지구력 훈련) |
| "interval training" | 인터벌 훈련 | 빠르게/느리게 반복 |
| "tempo run" | 템포런 | 일정한 빠른 페이스 유지 |
| "fartlek" | 파틀렉 | 속도 변화가 있는 달리기 |

**Recovery Run Characteristics:**

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Pace | Slow and comfortable (편한 페이스) |
| Distance | Shorter than usual (평소보다 짧게) |
| Purpose | Help muscles recover (근육 회복) |
| Timing | After hard workouts (힘든 훈련 후) |

**Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I kept it short" | 짧게 했다 | 거리 조절 |
| "Taking it easy" | 가볍게 하는 중 | 강도 조절 |
| "Rest day tomorrow" | 내일은 휴식일 | 훈련 계획 |
| "Active recovery" | 적극적 회복 | 가벼운 활동으로 회복 |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "This time last year..."

**Original:** "This time last year. 'It was winter break after the second semester.'"

**Structure:** This time last year + [past situation/feeling]

**Uses:** 과거와 현재를 비교할 때

**Practice Examples:**

| Time Reference | Past Situation | Full Sentence |
|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| 작년 이맘때 | 학교 가기 싫었다 | "This time last year, I dreaded school." |
| 1년 전 | 달리기 못했다 | "A year ago, I couldn't run at all." |
| 과거 | 버스만 탔다 | "Back then, I only took the bus." |

**Variations:**
- "Same time last year, I was..."
- "A year ago today, I..."

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Discussing the New School Year

```
A: Can you believe break is almost over?
B: I know. We start next Monday.
A: Already? Time flew by.
B: Yeah. I'll be in tenth grade soon.
A: Same here. Hope we're in the same class.
B: Me too. That would be great.
A: Are you nervous about going back?
B: Not really. I'm actually looking forward to it.
A: Really? You've changed.
B: I guess I have. A lot can happen in a year.
```

### Scenario 2: Talking About Running

```
A: How was your run this morning?
B: Good. Did an easy 10km.
A: That's still pretty far for "easy."
B: Well, I ran 20km last Thursday, so today was a recovery run.
A: Makes sense. How long until your race?
B: 55 days.
A: Are you nervous?
B: A little. But I'm more excited than scared.
A: You've come a long way.
B: Thanks. This time last year, I couldn't run at all.
```

### Scenario 3: Reflecting on Change

```
A: What were you like a year ago?
B: Totally different. I hated going to school.
A: Because of the bus ride, right?
B: Yeah. Motion sickness was terrible.
A: And now?
B: I'm actually looking forward to school.
A: Why the change?
B: Because I can run there now. No more bus.
A: That's amazing. You really grew.
B: I guess so. Things are different now.
```

7. **"Will anything change?"** What makes us ask this question about new chapters in life?

8. **How important is it to have friends in the same class?** Have you ever hoped for this?
