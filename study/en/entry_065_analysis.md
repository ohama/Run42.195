# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Achievement Expressions

### "Take off."

**Original Korean:** "출발했다"

**Analysis:**
- 간단하고 직설적인 표현
- 러닝 시작할 때 자주 사용
- 더 정식한 표현: "I started my run." / "I began running."

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Take off." | "I took off." | "I began my journey." |
| "Let's go." | "I started running." | "I commenced the run." |
| "Ready? Let's do this!" | "I set off." | "I initiated the route." |

**Running Contexts:**
| Expression | Usage |
|------------|-------|
| "Take off" | 달리기 시작할 때 (격식 없음) |
| "Head out" | 출발하다/나가다 |
| "Set off" | 출발하다 (좀 더 정식) |
| "Start the run" | 달리기 시작하다 |
| "Get going" | 가기 시작하다 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: You ready?
B: Yeah, let's take off.
A: What route are you doing?
B: 15km today—out to the coast and back.
```

---

### "Going beyond 10km for the first time."

**Original Korean:** "처음 10km를 넘어서다"

**Grammar Point:** Gerund for describing the achievement

**Structure:** Going + beyond + distance + for the first time

**Achievement Milestones:**
| Milestone | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| 첫 달성 | "Going beyond 10km for the first time." |
| 개인 기록 | "Breaking my personal best." |
| 새로운 거리 | "Tackling a new distance." |
| 목표 달성 | "Hitting the 15km mark." |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "I'm pushing past 10km today."
- "This is the furthest I've ever run."
- "Breaking new ground at 15km."
- "I'm venturing into uncharted territory."

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I'm going beyond 10km the first time."
✅ "I'm going beyond 10km for the first time."

---

### "Out 7.5km, back 7.5km."

**Original Korean:** "왕복 7.5km씩"

**Analysis:** 간결한 달리기 계획 표현

**Running Route Descriptions:**
| Format | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Out/back 체계 | "Out 7.5km, back 7.5km." |
| 원형 경로 | "A 10km loop." |
| 왕복 경로 | "5km out and back = 10km total." |
| 거리 합산 | "15km total." |

**More Natural Phrasing:**
- "I'm doing an out-and-back route: 7.5km each way."
- "7.5km to the turning point, then back."
- "Double out-and-back: two 7.5km legs."

---

### "That's 15km total."

**Original Korean:** "총 15km다"

**Pattern:** Stating the total distance clearly

**Distance Confirmation Phrases:**
```
"That's 15km total."
"Which adds up to 15km."
"For a total of 15km."
"15km altogether."
"All in, that's 15km."
```

---

### "I did it."

**Original Korean:** "해냈다"

**Nuance:** 성취감, 자부심, 안도감이 담긴 표현

**Achievement Expressions Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I made it." | ★★☆ | 버텼다/해냈다 |
| "I did it." | ★★★ | 해냈다 (자부심) |
| "I nailed it." | ★★★★ | 완벽히 했다 |
| "I crushed it." | ★★★★ | 잘해냈다 (격식 없음) |
| "I pulled it off." | ★★★☆ | 어떻게든 해냈다 |
| "I conquered it." | ★★★★★ | 정복했다 (위대함) |

**Context in Running:**
- After finishing: "I did it!" (자부심)
- Barely finishing: "I made it." (안도감)
- Exceeding expectations: "I crushed it." (성취감)

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How did the 15km go?
B: I did it! 1 hour 28 minutes.
A: That's amazing! How did you feel?
B: Exhausted, but so proud.
```

## 1.2 Progress & Goal Expressions

### "First time I've gone beyond 10km."

**Original Korean:** "처음으로 10km를 넘었다"

**Grammar Point:** Present Perfect for lifetime achievements

**Structure:** First time I've + past participle

**Personal Achievement Statements:**
| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| "First time I've gone beyond 10km." | 처음으로 10km를 넘음 |
| "First time I've run 15km." | 처음으로 15km를 달렸음 |
| "First time I've broken an hour." | 처음으로 1시간 미만 |
| "First time I've felt strong at this distance." | 처음으로 이 거리에서 강함 |

**Milestone Language:**
- "This is the furthest I've ever gone."
- "I've never pushed this far before."
- "This is new territory for me."
- "I'm officially a 15km runner now."

---

### "Next week I'm going for 17km."

**Original Korean:** "다음주에는 17km에 도전할 거야"

**Pattern:** Planning the next goal

**Goal-Setting Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning |

| "I'm aiming for 20km." | 20km를 목표로 |

```
Week 1: 10km

**Countdown Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS DURING RUNNING (러닝 중 신체 감각)

## 2.1 Exhaustion & Effort Spectrum

### "My legs are starting to feel heavy."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 무거워지기 시작했다"

**Vocabulary:**
- Heavy: 무거운 (피로/무리로 인한)
- Legs: 다리

**Fatigue Expressions Spectrum:**

| Expression | Distance | Intensity |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "My legs feel fresh." | 0-3km | ★☆☆ |
| "My legs feel a little heavy." | 3-5km | ★★☆ |
| "My legs are getting heavy." | 5-10km | ★★★ |
| "My legs feel like lead." | 10-15km | ★★★★ |
| "My legs are completely shot." | 15km+ | ★★★★★ |

**Related Body Fatigue:**

| Body Part | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| 다리 | "My legs feel heavy." |
| 종아리 | "My calves are burning." |
| 허벅지 | "My quads are on fire." |
| 발 | "My feet are killing me." |
| 허리 | "My back is screaming." |
| 전신 | "I feel like lead." |

**Cause & Effect:**
```
Cause: Pushing beyond 10km
Effect: Legs get heavier
Response: Keep going (endurance mental game)
```

**Original Korean:** "숨이 가빠졌다"

**Breathing in Running:**

| Stage | Expression |
|-------|------------|

| 조금 거칠어짐 | "My breathing is getting heavier." |
| 거칠어짐 | "My breathing gets rougher." |
| 매우 거칠어짐 | "I'm gasping for air." |
| 극도로 거칠어짐 | "I can barely catch my breath." |

**Running Breathing Vocabulary:**

| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| rough breathing | 거친 호흡 | 힘들어하는 |
| shallow breathing | 얕은 호흡 | 충분하지 않음 |
| labored breathing | 힘든 호흡 | 고생하는 |
| catch your breath | 숨 고르다 | 회복 중 |
| be out of breath | 숨이 참 | 매우 피곤 |

**Breathing Management Tips:**
```
"Control your breathing."

"Steady your breathing."
"Focus on your breath."
```

### "It's hard. Really hard."

**Original Korean:** "힘들었다. 정말 힘들었다"

**Repetition for Emphasis:**

**Why Repetition Works:**
- 강조 효과
- 진정성 전달
- 감정의 크기 표현
- 독자 공감 유도

**Similar Emphatic Repetitions:**
| Repeated Expression | Impact |
|-------------------|--------|
| "It's hard. Really hard." | 힘든 정도 강조 |
| "It hurt. So much." | 고통의 정도 |
| "I was tired. So tired." | 피로의 정도 |
| "It was slow. Really slow." | 속도의 느림 |
| "I wanted to quit. Badly." | 포기하고 싶은 정도 |

**Intensifying Words with "Really":**
```
"Really hard"
"Really brutal"
"Really long"
"Really tough"
"Really painful"
```

---

### "That was brutal."

**Original Korean:** "정말 힘들었다"

**Nuance:** 고통스럽고 강렬한 경험에 대한 표현

**"Brutal" Context:**

| Expression | Situation |
|-----------|-----------|
| "That was brutal." | 힘든 경험을 마친 후 |
| "This is brutal." | 현재 겪고 있는 고통 |
| "It gets brutal after 11km." | 특정 지점부터 힘들어짐 |

**Similar Harsh Experience Words:**
- harsh
- intense
- punishing
- grueling
- exhausting
- relentless

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How was the 15km?
B: Brutal. After 11km, it got really brutal.
A: Did you finish?
B: Yeah, but barely. I wanted to quit so many times.
```

## 2.2 Pain & Discomfort

### "After 11km, it got really brutal."

**Original Korean:** "11km 이후로 정말 힘들어졌다"

**Stage Analysis:**
- 0-10km: Familiar territory (manageable)
- 11-15km: New territory (brutal)

**Distance-Based Difficulty:**

| Distance | Level | How You Feel |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| 0-5km | Warm-up | Good |
| 5-10km | Familiar | Okay |
| 10-11km | Threshold | Getting tough |
| 11-15km | Breakthrough zone | Brutal |
| 15-21km | Deep water | Extremely brutal |

**Breaking Point Analysis:**
```
"Once I got past 11km..."
"After 11km, everything changed."
"11km was my breaking point."
"The pain really hit at 11km."
```

**Running Strategy:**
- Pace management (속도 조절)
- Energy conservation (에너지 절약)
- Building into the run (천천히 준비하기)

**Pacing Vocabulary:**

| Speed | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| 매우 느림 | "I started really slow." |
| 느림 | "Started slow." |
| 중간 | "At a steady pace." |
| 빠름 | "I picked up the pace." |
| 매우 빠름 | "I sprinted." |

**Why Start Slow:**
```
"Let your body warm up."
"Build into the run gradually."
"Save energy for later."
"Find your rhythm."
"Get into a groove."
```

**Original Korean:** "거리 달리기는 속도가 아니라 거리가 중요하다"

**Running Philosophy:**

**Types of Running:**

| Type | Focus | Philosophy |
|------|-------|-----------|

**Pace Acceptance:**
```

```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "How many km?" / Response

**Structure:** Direct distance question and answer

**Exchange:**
```
A: How many km?
B: 15km.
A: 15km?! That's insane.
B: It was brutal. Once I got past 11km, it was really hard.
```

**Distance Question Patterns:**
| Question | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "How many km?" | "15km." |
| "How far did you go?" | "15km." |
| "What's your distance today?" | "15km." |
| "How long?" | "1 hour 28 minutes." |

---

## Pattern 2: "It was brutal. Once I got past [distance], it was really hard."

**Structure:** General statement + specific turning point

**Application:**
```
"The run was brutal. Once I got past 10km, it was really hard."
"The workout was brutal. Once I got past the halfway point, it was really hard."
"The hike was brutal. Once I got past the first peak, it was really hard."
```

**Turning Points in Physical Activities:**
- "Once I got past 5km, my legs started failing."
- "After 30 minutes, the difficulty increased."
- "At the 10km mark, everything changed."
- "Once fatigue set in, it got harder."

---

## Pattern 3: "First time [achievement]. [Number/Time]."

**Structure:** Milestone + metric

**Examples:**
```
"First time I've gone beyond 10km. 15km total."
