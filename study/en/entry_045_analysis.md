# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Achievement & Goals

### "I challenged myself to run 10km."

**Original Korean:** "10km를 달리기로 도전했다"

**Analysis:**
- 자신에게 도전장을 내리다는 표현
- 더 일반적인 표현: "I set myself a goal" / "I decided to run 10km"
- 스포츠 맥락에서 매우 자연스러운 표현

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Nuance | Formality |
|------------|--------|-----------|
| "I challenged myself to run 10km." | 스스로 도전 | Casual |
| "I set myself a 10km goal." | 목표 설정 | Standard |
| "I decided to push myself and run 10km." | 자신을 밀어붙이다 | Casual |
| "My goal was to run 10km." | 객관적 진술 | Formal |
| "I was aiming for 10km." | 목표 지향 | Casual |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's your running goal?
B: I challenged myself to run 10km this month.
A: That's ambitious! How far can you run now?
B: About 8km comfortably. But I think I can make it.
```

### "And I did it."

**Original Korean:** "그리고 해냈다"

**Nuance:** 짧지만 강력한 성취 선언

**Why This Works:**
- 불필요한 장식 없음
- 확실한 자신감 표현
- 독자가 기분 좋게 느껴짐

**Similar Achievement Statements:**

| Expression | Intensity | Tone |
|------------|-----------|------|
| "And I did it." | ★★★★★ | 확신 있고 간단함 |
| "I made it." | ★★★★ | 다소 놀라움 |
| "I actually made it." | ★★★★ | 의심했던 톤 |
| "I accomplished my goal." | ★★★★ | 격식체 |
| "I pulled it off." | ★★★★ | 캐주얼, 자랑스러움 |
| "I succeeded." | ★★★ | 객관적 |

## 1.2 Describing Process & Effort

### "I mapped out the route yesterday."

**Original Korean:** "어제 경로를 정했다"

**Vocabulary:** "Map out" = 자세히 계획하다, 경로를 정하다

**"Map out" Usage:**

| Object | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| route | "map out a route" | 경로 정하기 |
| plan | "map out a plan" | 계획 세우기 |
| strategy | "map out a strategy" | 전략 계획 |
| future | "map out your future" | 미래 설계 |
| itinerary | "map out an itinerary" | 여행일정 정하기 |

**Alternative Expressions:**
- "I planned out the route."
- "I figured out the best route."
- "I decided on a route."

---

### "My usual 8km loop, plus one extra lap."

**Original Korean:** "항상 다니는 8km 루프에 추가 한 바퀴"

**"Loop" Vocabulary:**
- Loop (noun): 한 바퀴, 도는 길
- Lap (noun): 한 바퀴 (경기장)

**Distinguishing Loop vs. Lap:**

| Term | Usage | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| loop | 일반적인 도는 길 | "I run the same loop every day." |
| lap | 경기장이나 정식 코스 | "20 laps around the track." |
| circuit | 폐곡선 경로 | "a cycling circuit" |
| route | 경로 (도는 길 아님) | "a 5km route" |

---

### "That should be about 10km."

**Original Korean:** "그러면 10km가 될 거다"

**Grammar Point:** "That should be + [amount]"

**Structure:** Estimating/predicting a result

**Examples:**

| Sentence | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "That should be about 10km." | 거리 추정 |
| "That should be enough time." | 시간 추정 |
| "That should cost about $50." | 비용 추정 |
| "That should take two hours." | 소요시간 추정 |

**Similar Estimating Phrases:**
- "That should come to about 10km."
- "That'll probably be around 10km."
- "If I'm right, that's about 10km."

## 1.3 Time & Preparation

### "I woke up at 5 a.m."

**Original Korean:** "새벽 5시에 일어났다"

**Time Expressions for Early Morning:**

| Time | Expression | Usage |
|------|------------|-------|
| 4am-5am | "I woke up at dawn." | 해 떠기 직전 |
| 5am-6am | "I woke up early." | 이른 아침 |
| 6am-7am | "I woke up in the morning." | 아침 시간 |

**"Woke up" vs. "Got up":**
- "Woke up" (일어났다/깼다) - The moment you open eyes
- "Got up" (일어났다/침대에서 나왔다) - Getting out of bed

**Example:**
```
"I woke up at 5, but I didn't get up until 5:15."
(5시에 깼지만 5:15까지 침대에 있었다)
```

---

### "I drank plenty of water."

**Original Korean:** "물을 많이 마셨다"

**"Plenty of" Meaning:** 충분한 양의, 많은

**Quantity Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "plenty of water" | 충분한 양 | "I drank plenty of water before running." |
| "a lot of water" | 많은 양 | "I drank a lot of water." |
| "some water" | 적당한 양 | "I drank some water." |
| "a little water" | 조금 | "I drank a little water." |
| "no water" | 전혀 안 | "I didn't drink any water." |

---

### "I took off at 5:15."

**Original Korean:** "5시 15분에 출발했다"

**"Take off" Meanings:**

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 출발하다 | leave, depart | "I took off at 5:15." |
| 벗다 | remove (clothing) | "Take off your shoes." |
| 날다 | fly (airplane) | "The plane took off." |
| 성공하다 | become successful | "Her career really took off." |

**Alternative Running Departure Phrases:**
- "I headed out at 5:15."
- "I started running at 5:15."
- "I left at 5:15."

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running & Fatigue

### "My legs started feeling heavier."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 무거워지기 시작했다"

**Fatigue Progression Spectrum:**

| Stage | Expression | Intensity |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| Early | "My legs feel a bit tired." | ★☆☆ |
| Middle | "My legs are getting heavier." | ★★☆ |
| Advanced | "My legs felt stiff." | ★★★ |
| Severe | "My legs were screaming." | ★★★★ |
| Critical | "My legs felt like jelly." | ★★★★★ |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "My legs felt weary."
- "My legs were getting tired."
- "I could feel the fatigue setting in."
- "My legs grew heavy."

**Pronunciation:**
- "heavier" = /ˈhevɪər/

---

### "My breathing got rough."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 가빠졌다"

**"Rough" for Breathing:**
- Rough (형용사): 거칠다, 거친
- Getting rough: 거칠어지고 있다

**Breathing Quality Spectrum:**

| Stage | Expression | Meaning |
|-------|------------|---------|
| Normal | "I was breathing easily." | 쉬운 호흡 |
| Elevated | "My breathing got heavy." | 무거운 호흡 |
| Strained | "My breathing got rough." | 거친 호흡 |
| Labored | "I was gasping for breath." | 헐떡거리는 호흡 |
| Critical | "I could barely breathe." | 거의 숨을 못 쉬는 |

**Complete Breathing Sequence:**
```
My breathing got heavy → My breathing got rough →
I was gasping for breath → I could barely breathe.
```

---

### "My breath came up to my throat."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 목까지 차올랐다"

**Anatomical Breathing Language:**

| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "I'm out of breath." | 숨이 차다 | ★★★ |
| "My breath came up to my throat." | 목까지 차올랐다 | ★★★★ |
| "I felt like I couldn't breathe." | 숨을 못 쉴 것 같았다 | ★★★★ |
| "I was suffocating." | 질식할 것 같았다 | ★★★★★ |

**Vivid Physical Descriptions:**
These expressions make the reader *feel* the physical struggle.

---

### "My legs were shaking."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 떨렸다"

**Trembling/Shaking Vocabulary:**

| Body Part | Expression | Cause |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| legs | "My legs were shaking." | 피로/공포 |
| hands | "My hands were trembling." | 신경/추위 |
| voice | "My voice was shaking." | 감정 |
| knees | "My knees were weak." | 공포/약함 |
| body | "My body was shaking." | 추위/두려움 |

**Intensity Levels:**
- shake (흔들리다)
- tremble (떨리다)
- quiver (떨리다)
- convulse (경련하다)

---

### "My legs screamed."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 비명을 지르는 듯했다"

**Personification: Body Parts as Active Agents**

When bodies communicate through pain/sensations, we use vivid language:

| Expression | Literal Meaning | What It Means |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "My legs screamed." | 다리가 비명을 지르다 | 심한 근육통 |
| "My muscles were on fire." | 근육이 불타다 | 타는 듯한 통증 |
| "My lungs were burning." | 폐가 타다 | 산소 부족 감각 |
| "My body was rebelling." | 몸이 반항하다 | 신체 거부반응 |

**Why Writers Use This:**
- 독자가 고통을 더 생생하게 느낌
- 수동적 진술보다 더 강력
- 14세 소년의 과장된 표현이 진정성 있음

## 2.2 Exhaustion & Recovery

### "I was gasping for breath."

**Original Korean:** "헐떡거렸다"

**"Gasp" Vocabulary:**

| Word | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| gasp (noun) | 헐떡임 | "a gasp of shock" |
| gasp (verb) | 헐떡거리다 | "I was gasping for breath." |
| panting | 헐떡거리는 | "panting heavily" |
| wheezing | 쌕쌕거리는 | "breathing difficulty" |

**"Gasp for air" vs. "Gasp for breath":**
- Both mean 숨을 몰아쉬다
- "Gasp for breath" (더 일반적)
- "Gasp for air" (더 극적)

---

### "I could barely breathe."

**Original Korean:** "거의 숨을 못 쉴 지경이었다"

**"Barely" Intensity Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I could breathe." | ★☆☆ | 정상 |
| "I could kind of breathe." | ★★☆ | 약간 힘들다 |
| "I could barely breathe." | ★★★★ | 거의 못 쉰다 |
| "I couldn't breathe." | ★★★★★ | 완전히 못 쉰다 |

---

### "My feet were heavy."

**Original Korean:** "발이 무거웠다"

**Body Part + Adjective for Fatigue:**

| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "My legs are tired." | 다리가 피곤하다 | ★★ |
| "My legs are heavy." | 다리가 무겁다 | ★★★ |
| "My legs are dead." | 다리가 죽었다 | ★★★★ |
| "My legs are killing me." | 다리가 죽겠다 | ★★★★★ |

**Regional Note:**
- "My feet were heavy" = 발이 무거웠다 (미국식)

### "I slowed down. More of a fast walk at this point."

**Original Korean:** "속도를 줄였다. 거의 빠르게 걷는 거랑 비슷했다"

**Running Pace Terminology:**

| Pace | Speed | Effort | Description |
|------|-------|--------|-------------|
| sprint | 빠름 | ★★★★★ | very fast running |
| running pace | 보통 | ★★★ | normal running |
| jogging pace | 느림 | ★★ | slow running |
| fast walk | 걷기 | ★★ | 빠른 보행 |
| regular walk | 걷기 | ★ | 일반 보행 |

**Honest Admission in Text:**
> "I slowed down. More of a fast walk at this point."

This shows:
- Reality, not heroic fantasy
- Honesty about physical limits
- Teenage voice (자조적)

---

### "Sweat poured off me like rain."

**Original Korean:** "비처럼 흘렀다"

**Simile for Sweat:**

| Simile | Intensity | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| "a little sweat" | ☆☆☆ | 약간의 땀 |
| "sweat dripped down" | ★★☆ | 땀이 떨어진다 |
| "sweat poured down" | ★★★ | 땀이 흐른다 |
| "sweat poured like rain" | ★★★★ | 땀이 비처럼 흐른다 |
| "I was drenched in sweat" | ★★★★★ | 완전히 젖었다 |

**Why Similes Work:**
- "Like rain" makes reader visualize the amount
- More vivid than "a lot of sweat"
- Teenager's natural speech pattern

# PART 3: GROWTH & PROGRESS (성장 표현)

## 3.1 Comparing Past to Present

### "In March, even 1km felt impossible."

**Original Korean:** "3월에는 1km도 불가능해 보였다"

**Grammar Point:** "Even + [object] + felt + adjective"

**Structure:** Emphasizing how extreme something was

**Examples:**

| Expression | Context |
|------------|---------|
| "Even 1km felt impossible." | 1km도 어려웠다 |
| "Even walking felt hard." | 걷기도 힘들었다 |
| "Even thinking about it scared me." | 생각하는 것도 무서웠다 |
| "Even breathing felt difficult." | 숨 쉬는 것도 어려웠다 |

**This Shows Progress:**
- January→March: couldn't run
- March→May: 7km achieved
- August: 10km achieved
- Timeline creates narrative tension

---

### "In May, I thought I'd die after 7km."

**Original Korean:** "5월엔 7km 후에 죽을 것 같았다"

**Hyperbolic Teenage Expression:**

| Statement | Reality | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| "I thought I'd die." | 죽지 않음 | 과장으로 피로 표현 |
| "I felt like dying." | 살아있음 | 극심한 고통 |
| "I was dying." | 멀쩡함 | 캐주얼 과장 |

**When These Expressions Are Appropriate:**
- First-person retrospective (회상)
- Teenage voice (14세의 목소리)
- Emotional intensity needed (강조)
- Not factual reporting

---

### "Now I've run 10km."

**Original Korean:** "이제 10km를 달렸다"

**Present Perfect: Lifetime Achievement**

**Structure:** "Now I've + past participle"

**Examples:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "Now I've run 10km." | 이제 10km를 달렸다 |
| "Now I've graduated." | 이제 졸업했다 |
| "Now I've achieved my goal." | 이제 목표를 달성했다 |
| "Now I've proven myself." | 이제 자신을 증명했다 |

**Why "Now":**
- 과거와의 대비
- 성장의 순간 강조
- 변화의 중심점

---

### "Five months ago, I couldn't have imagined it."

**Original Korean:** "5개월 전엔 상상도 못했다"

**Past Perfect Counterfactual:**

| Time | Expression | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 5 months ago | "I couldn't have imagined it." | 상상도 못했다 |
| Back then | "I'd never thought it possible." | 가능하다고 생각도 못했다 |
| When I started | "I never would have believed this." | 절대 믿지 않았을 것 |

**"Couldn't have imagined" Usage:**
- Expressing past inability to imagine future
- Shows growth/change
- More sophisticated than "I didn't think..."

## 3.2 Achievement Reflection

### "That's not even a quarter of a marathon."

**Original Korean:** "그래도 풀 마라톤의 1/4도 안 된다"

**Fraction/Proportion Language:**

| Fraction | Expression | 42.195km 기준 |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| 1/4 | "a quarter" | ~10.5km |
| 1/3 | "a third" | ~14km |
| 1/2 | "half" | ~21km |
| 2/3 | "two-thirds" | ~28km |
| 3/4 | "three-quarters" | ~32km |

**Examples:**
```
"10km is just a quarter of the marathon distance."
"A quarter of the way there."
"Almost a third of the distance."
```

---

### "Just 10 out of 42.195km."

**Original Korean:** "42.195km 중 10km일 뿐이다"

**Mathematics Language:**

| Format | Example | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "X out of Y" | "10 out of 42.195" | 비율 표현 |
| "X of the total" | "10km of the total" | 전체 비율 |
| "only X" | "only 10km" | 축소 표현 |

**Why This Expression Works:**
- 수치화된 성취 표현
- 겸손함 + 자부심 동시 표현
- 마라톤 목표를 항상 상기

---

### "But for me, it's huge."

**Original Korean:** "그래도 내게는 엄청 크다"

**Grammar Point:** Positioning "for me"

**Structure:** "It's + adjective + for me"

**Examples:**

| Expression | Context |
|------------|---------|
| "It's huge for me." | 나에겐 크다 |
| "It's a big deal for me." | 나에겐 중요하다 |
| "It's meaningful for me." | 나에겐 의미 있다 |
| "It's a milestone for me." | 나에겐 이정표다 |

**Subjectivity:**
- Acknowledging different values for different people
- Personal achievement worth = not about absolute numbers
- Teenage emotional intelligence

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

**Original Korean:** "들어갔다/안으로 들어갔다"

**"Head [direction]" Pattern:**

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|

| head out | 나가다 | "I headed out early." |

**Casual Nature of "Head":**

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Progress Through Time

**Structure:** "From [time period] on, things got [harder/better]."

**From Text:** "From 6km on, things got harder."

**Examples:**

| Time | Change | Full Sentence |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 6km on | harder | "From 6km on, things got harder." |
| mile 5 on | tougher | "From mile 5 on, things got tougher." |
| day 3 on | better | "From day 3 on, things got better." |
| week 2 on | more interesting | "From week 2 on, it got more interesting." |

**Use Case:**
- Describing turning points
- Narrative progression
- Physical/emotional challenges

## Pattern 2: Retrospective Comparison

**Structure:** "I remembered [past event], when I [did something]. It was similar now."

**From Text:** "I remembered May, when I ran 7km for the first time. How I thought I was going to die at 4km. It's similar now."

**Examples:**

| Past | Present | Full Pattern |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Failed test | Difficult exam | "I remembered the failed test from last year. It was similar now." |
| First time nervous | New situation | "I remembered my first day. I felt the same way now." |
| Doubt from before | Current doubt | "I remembered having doubts then. It was similar now." |

**Psychological Purpose:**
- Reduces fear through comparison
- Shows patterns of overcoming
- Builds confidence

## Pattern 3: Mental Self-Talk During Challenge

**Structure:** "Thought/realization in italics: *[internal dialogue]*"

**From Text:**
- *Five kilometers. Halfway there.*
- *Let's do this.*
- *But I made it through that too, didn't I?*
- *Just 1km. One more kilometer.*
- *I did it.*

**Patterns in Self-Talk:**

| Type | Example | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| Milestone | *Five kilometers. Halfway there.* | 진행 상황 확인 |
| Motivation | *Let's do this.* | 스스로 격려 |
| Retrospective | *But I made it through that too.* | 자신감 상기 |
| Focus | *Just 1km.* | 거대한 목표를 축소 |
| Victory | *I did it.* | 성취 선언 |

**Dialogue Translation:**
```
Internal thought → What you'd say aloud if someone was listening
*Let's do this.* → "Let's do this!"
*I did it.* → "Yes! I did it!"
```

## Pattern 4: "I didn't stop."

**Structure:** Negative statement for emphasis

**From Text:** "But my feet kept moving in a running motion. I didn't stop."

**Why This Works:**
- "I didn't stop" stronger than "I continued"
- Shows willpower/refusal to quit
- Defiant tone

**Similar Emphasis Patterns:**

| Negative | Positive | Nuance Difference |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| "I didn't stop." | "I continued." | Defiance vs. statement |
| "I didn't give up." | "I persevered." | Personal choice vs. fact |
| "I didn't quit." | "I finished." | Refusal vs. completion |

## Pattern 5: Announcement + Realization

**From Text:**

I stopped.

**Why Fragments Work:**

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Sharing Running Achievement

```
A: Hey, you look exhausted. What happened?
B: I just finished a 10km run!
A: Wait, 10km? I thought you could only do 8km.
B: I know! It was way harder than I expected.
A: How long did it take?
B: 58 minutes. I was completely wiped out.
A: That's amazing! You should be proud.
B: Thanks. I have to admit, I'm pretty proud of myself right now.
A: You should be. That's a huge achievement.
B: You know what? For me, it really is huge.
```

---

### Scenario 2: Describing the Physical Challenge

```
A: Tell me about the run. Was it hard?
B: Hard doesn't even cover it. Around 6km, things got really tough.

B: My legs started feeling heavier. My breathing got rough.

B: Yeah, but I reminded myself I'd done 7km before, even when I thought I was dying.
A: So you pushed through?
B: Exactly. From 9km on, I was more of a fast walk than a run, but I didn't stop.
A: That takes guts.
B: Thanks. By the end, I was gasping for breath and covered in sweat, but smiling.
```
