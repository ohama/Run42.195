## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **❌ "The air felt differently"** → **✓ "The air felt different"** (felt = linking verb, use adjective not adverb)
2. **❌ "Way cooler than"** (too informal for formal writing) → **✓ "Much cooler than"** (formal contexts)
3. **❌ "You're dripping sweat"** (missing "of") → **✓ "Dripping with sweat"** (more natural)
4. **❌ "Smells like you exercised"** (incomplete in formal English) → **✓ "It smells like you've exercised"** (formal)

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

## Phrasal Verbs in the Text

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Korean | Example from Text | Literal vs. Figurative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **leave at** | Depart at specific time | ~시에 나가다 | "I left the house at 5:30." | Literal |
| **hit (+ distance)** | Reach a specific point | (거리)에 도달하다 | "By the time I hit 3km..." | Literal (running jargon) |
| **pour from** | Flow heavily from | ~에서 쏟아지다 | "sweat was pouring from everywhere" | Literal |
| **stream down** | Flow in steady line down | ~을 타고 흘러내리다 | "Sweat streamed down my skin." | Literal |
| **wipe away** | Remove by rubbing | 닦아내다 | "I wiped away the sweat." | Literal |
| **get home** | Arrive at home | 집에 도착하다 | "When I got home, I showered." | Literal |
| **showered** | Take a shower (not phrasal, but compound) | 샤워했다 | "When I got home, I showered." | Literal |

## Phrasal Verbs Similar to Text Expressions

| Expression Type | Similar Options | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Flow/Movement of liquids** | pour / stream / drip / gush | "Sweat poured down" (heavy) vs. "dripped" (light) |
| **To stop/end action** | wipe away / dry off / wash away | "wipe away" (quick action) vs. "wash away" (more thorough) |
| **To arrive** | get home / get to school / arrive at | "got home" (casual) vs. "arrived home" (formal) |

## Comparison: Similar Verbs

### POUR vs. STREAM vs. DRIP

```
INTENSITY:  DRIP (minimal) → STREAM (moderate) → POUR (heavy)
TIME:       DRIP (continuous drops) → STREAM (steady flow) → POUR (rapid flow)

"My sweat dripped onto the ground." (한두 방울씩)
"Sweat streamed down my face." (지속적인 흐름)
"Sweat poured from everywhere." (엄청난 량)
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Dialogue Patterns from the Text

### Pattern 1: Seeking Explanation/Clarification

**Structure:** "Why [action]?" / "What [action]?" / "Do [subject/verb]?"

```
Minseo: "Why so early?"
Doyun: "To beat the heat. It's cooler at dawn."

Doyun: "Do I [smell]?"
Minseo: "You're fine. Just smells like sweat."
```

**Usage:**
- Direct question about motivation or state
- Response provides reason or assessment
- Formality: Casual, friend-to-friend

**Alternatives:**
- "What's the reason for...?" (More formal)
- "How come?" (More casual)
- "Is there something...?" (More indirect)

### Pattern 2: Surprise/Realization

**Structure:** "Oh..." / "You're [adjective]." / "That's [noun]."

```
Doyun: "So this is what sweat means."
Doyun: "This feels good. Sweating feels good."
Doyun: "Sweat wasn't a bad thing after all."
Doyun: "It felt like some kind of recognition."
```

**Usage:**
- Internal reflection or realization
- Emotional recognition of change
- Formality: Internal monologue (introspective)

**Alternatives:**
- "I never realized that..."
- "It turns out..."
- "I understand now..."

### Pattern 3: Contrast/Change Recognition

**Structure:** "[Past state] / But now [Current state]."

```
"I used to hate sweating."
"But now it's different."

"I used to just want to shower right away."
"But now... [I think differently]"

"It's the same sweat."
"Have I changed? Or is the sweat different?"
```

**Usage:**
- Shows personal growth or shift in perspective
- Creates emotional resonance
- Formality: Reflective, personal

**Examples from learner perspective:**
- "I used to hate early mornings. But now I love them."
- "Public speaking used to terrify me. But after presentations, it's different."

### Pattern 4: Direct Assertion/Statement

**Structure:** "Sweat [verb/description]." / "[Experience] [verb] proof."

```
"Sweat wasn't a bad thing after all."
"Sweating feels good."
"Sweat is proof that you worked hard."
"The meaning of sweat has changed for me."
```

**Usage:**
- Declarative statements of belief/understanding
- Emphasizes transformation
- Formality: Personal reflection

## 5+ Example Sentences Using These Patterns

1. **Seeking Clarification Pattern:**
   - "What time did you leave?" (asking about timing)
   - "Why would you go so early?" (asking about reason)
   - "Does it hurt?" (asking about sensation)

2. **Surprise Pattern:**
   - "So this is what hard work feels like."
   - "I never knew that could feel good."
   - "That's a kind of achievement."

3. **Contrast Pattern:**
   - "I used to avoid exercise. Now I crave it."
   - "Public speaking used to make me nervous. But now I feel confident."
   - "That used to embarrass me. But now I'm proud of it."

4. **Assertion Pattern:**
   - "Effort shows in results."
   - "Pain is temporary, pride is forever."
   - "The journey changes you."

## Alternative Structures

**For Pattern 1 (Seeking Info):**
```
Direct:    "Why so early?"
Softened:  "I was wondering why you'd leave so early?"
Polite:    "Could you explain why you left at 5:30?"
Indirect:  "That's quite early. What made you decide to do that?"
```

## When to Use / When NOT to Use

| Pattern | WHEN TO USE | WHEN NOT TO USE |
|---|---|---|
| Seeking Clarification | Genuine question, casual setting, friend context | Formal interviews, don't know the person well, hierarchical settings |
| Surprise/Realization | Personal reflection, emotional moments, diary writing | Academic writing, formal reports, professional settings |
| Contrast | Showing growth, personal essay, narrative | Objective descriptions, factual reporting |
| Direct Assertion | Personal belief, conclusion, manifesto | Tentative opinions, uncertain situations |

## Practice Exercise 1: Dialogue Practice (Scenario 1)

**Scenario: Talking with a friend about your first successful early morning run**

```
Friend: "I heard you ran at 5:30 this morning. That's incredibly early!"

You: "Yeah, I did. My grandfather suggested I try running at dawn because the air is cooler."

Friend: "Did it help?"

You: "Absolutely. The temperature was perfect—around 22 degrees. I wasn't gasping
      as much as I usually do."

Friend: "How far did you go?"

You: "7 kilometers in 42 minutes. For me, that's a really good time."

Friend: "Wow, and you finished the whole way without stopping?"

You: "Yes, I ran the whole way. When I got to school, I was sweating like rain, but
      it felt amazing."

Friend: "You look really happy about it."

You: "I am. I used to hate sweating, but now I understand it's proof that I worked hard.
      It feels refreshing."

Friend: "That's an interesting perspective. So you've changed how you think about exercise?"

You: "Definitely. When I got home and looked in the mirror, my face was flushed and I
      looked healthy. It felt like some kind of recognition."
```

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What made the protagonist decide to run at dawn?
2. What was the perfect temperature for running?
3. How long did the 7km run take?
4. How did the protagonist feel about sweating at the end?

