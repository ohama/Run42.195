# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Planning & Preparation Expressions

### "I checked everything I need."

**Original Korean:** "필요한 것들을 다 확인했다"

**Analysis:**
- 준비 과정을 나타내는 일상적 표현
- 시제: 과거완료형 (체크리스트 완성 후 현재)
- 더 일반적 변형: "I went through my checklist" / "I made sure I had everything"

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Nuance | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| "I checked everything." | 중립적, 단순 | "I packed my bag and checked everything." |
| "I double-checked." | 두 번 확인 | "I double-checked the address." |
| "I made sure..." | 확실히 하다 | "I made sure I had my passport." |
| "I went through..." | 하나하나 확인 | "I went through my packing list." |
| "I verified..." | 공식적 확인 | "I verified all the details." |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: Are you ready for tomorrow?
B: I checked everything I need.
A: Did you remember your shoes?
B: Yeah, I even cleaned them.
A: That's thorough.
B: I want to make sure nothing goes wrong.
```

### "All set."

**Original Korean:** "다 준비됐다"

**Meaning:** 준비가 완료되었다 (격식 없고 긍정적)

**Pronunciation Tip:**
- "All set" = /ɔːl sɛt/ (모두 준비됨)
- 한 문구처럼 빠르게 발음

**When to Use "All Set":**

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| 짐 준비 완료 | "My luggage is packed. All set!" |
| 회의 준비 | "The projector is ready. We're all set to start." |
| 주문 | "Three coffees. All set to go!" |
| 출발 준비 | "Everyone's here and we have everything. All set!" |

**Similar Expressions:**

| Expression | Formality | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "All set." | Casual | 다 됐다 |
| "I'm ready." | Standard | 준비됐다 |
| "Everything's in order." | Formal | 모든 게 갖춰졌다 |
| "Good to go." | Casual | 출발해도 된다 |
| "We're good." | Very casual | 괜찮아 |

**Related Expressions:**
- "We're all set for the weekend."
- "Ready when you are."
- "Just give me five minutes and I'll be all set."

### "I need to sleep early."

**Original Korean:** "일찍 자야 한다"

**Grammar Point:** "Need to" + 동사원형 (should를 더 강하게)

**Structure:** I need to + 동사원형

**Examples with "Need to":**

| Expression | Meaning | Urgency |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| "I need to leave." | 떠나야 한다 | Very strong |
| "I should leave." | 떠나야 한다 | Strong |
| "I ought to leave." | 떠나야 한다 | Moderate |
| "I might leave." | 떠날 수도 있다 | Weak |

**Health-Related "Need to":**
- "I need to sleep more." (더 자야 한다)
- "I need to eat something." (뭔가 먹어야 한다)
- "I need to drink water." (물을 마셔야 한다)
- "I need to rest." (쉬어야 한다)

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: Want to watch a movie?
B: I can't tonight. I need to sleep early.
A: Why?
B: I have a big day tomorrow.
A: Oh right, good luck!
```

## 1.3 Pre-Race Mindset Talk

### "I can do this. I can."

**Original Korean:** "할 수 있다. 할 수 있어."

**Analysis:**
- 자기 격려 (self-affirmation/pep talk)
- 반복으로 강조 효과
- 불안감을 극복하려는 심리적 노력

**Self-Affirmation Patterns:**

| Pattern | Effect | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| "I can do this." | 전체 능력 강조 | 큰 목표 |
| "I can do this. I can." | 반복 강조 | 결정 강화 |
| "I've got this." | 자신감 표현 | 캐주얼한 상황 |
| "If I can do this, I can do anything." | 일반화 | 어려운 과제 |

**Building Courage Talk:**
- "You've trained for this."
- "You're ready."
- "Just take it one step at a time."
- "Don't think too far ahead."
- "One foot in front of the other."

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: I'm so nervous about tomorrow.
B: You've done the training. You can do this.
A: What if I can't finish?
B: You will. I believe in you.
A: Thanks. I needed to hear that.
```

## 3.2 Determination & Motivation

### "I want to finish."

**Original Korean:** "결승선을 통과하고 싶다"

**"Want to" vs. "Need to" vs. "Have to":**

| Modal | Meaning | Strength |
|-------|---------|----------|
| want to | 원한다 | Desire (약함) |
| would like to | 좋아하고 싶다 | Polite desire |
| need to | 필요하다 | Necessity |
| have to | 해야 한다 | Obligation |
| must | 반드시 | Very strong obligation |

**Expressions of Strong Desire:**
- "I really want to finish."
- "More than anything, I want to cross that line."
- "I'm determined to finish."
- "I will finish."
- "Nothing's going to stop me."

**Goal-Setting Language:**
- "My goal is to finish."
- "I'm aiming for..." (목표로 삼다)
- "I set a target of..." (목표를 정하다)
- "I'm pushing for..." (목표로 밀어붙이다)

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "You worked hard from X until now."

**Structure:** You + worked hard + from + time + until now / to the present

**From Text:** "You worked hard. From March until now."

| Example | Time Period | Effect |
|---------|------------|--------|
| "You worked hard from March until now." | 6개월 | 장기적 노력 인정 |
| "You've been training for months." | 긴 시간 | 일관된 노력 |
| "Look at the progress you've made." | 과정 강조 | 성장 확인 |

**Acknowledging Effort:**
- "I've watched your progress."
- "I've seen how hard you've worked."
- "You've given it your all."
- "You've stayed committed."

---

## Pattern 2: "Whether you finish or not, I'm proud of you."

**Structure:** Whether + 동사/결과 + or not, [감정 표현]

**Meaning:** 결과에 관계없이

**From Text:** "Whether you finish or not, I'm proud of you."

| Condition | Statement |
|-----------|-----------|
| finish or not | "I'm proud of you." |
| win or lose | "I'm happy you tried." |
| succeed or fail | "I love you anyway." |
| get it right or wrong | "You did your best." |

**Parent/Supporter Expressions:**
- "I'm proud of you no matter what."
- "The result doesn't matter. You showed up."
- "I'm just glad you're healthy."
- "Your effort is what counts."

---

## Pattern 3: "That meant a lot."

**Structure:** [Thing/Words] + meant + a lot / meant everything

**Meaning:** 그게 정말 중요했다 / 고마워

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| "That meant a lot." | 그게 정말 많이 의미했어 |
| "That meant everything." | 정말 중요했어 |
| "It meant the world to me." | 정말 소중했어 |
| "I really needed to hear that." | 그 말이 필요했어 |

**Grateful Responses:**
- "Thank you for saying that."
- "I really appreciate that."
- "That means so much to me."
- "I won't forget this."

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Night Before a Big Event (형제자매 버전)

```
Older Sibling: You seem quiet tonight.
Younger Sibling: Yeah, I'm nervous about the race.
OS: You've trained hard though.
YS: I know, but what if I can't finish?
OS: You'll finish. And even if you don't, that's okay.
YS: But I really want to.
OS: Then you will. Get some rest, okay?
YS: I'll try. Can't sleep though.
OS: That's normal before big events. Your body will take over tomorrow.
```

### Scenario 2: Pre-Race Conversation with Parent

```
Parent: Have you thought about what you'll eat tomorrow?
Child: Maybe pasta?
Parent: Good choice. Carbs for energy.
Child: I've checked everything I need. My shoes, my watch, everything.
Parent: That's good. Sounds like you're ready.
Child: I'm nervous though.
Parent: Being nervous is normal. You've done the training.
Child: Yeah. I really want to finish.
Parent: I know you do. And I believe you will.
Child: Thanks for saying that.
Parent: Get some sleep now. You need rest.
```

### Scenario 3: Talking to a Friend About Tomorrow

```
Friend: Big day tomorrow, huh?
You: Yeah. 10km. I'm freaking out.
Friend: You've been training for months!
You: I know, but that's different from actually running it.
Friend: You'll be fine. What's the course like?
You: Mostly flat. That's good at least.
Friend: See? You've got this.
You: I hope so. I really want to finish.
Friend: You will. I'm gonna cheer for you!
You: Seriously? Thanks.
```
