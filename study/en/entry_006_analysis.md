# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Greeting & Meeting Strangers

### "Oh, student."

**Original Text:** "Oh, student."

**Korean Equivalent:** 아, 학생

**Analysis:**
- 나이 든 분이 젊은 사람을 부르는 전형적인 방식
- 한국식 호칭 - 이름 대신 사회적 역할로 부르기
- 친근하지만 존중을 담은 호칭

**Addressing Strangers by Role:**

| Role | Address Term | Context |
|------|--------------|---------|
| 학생 | "Student" | 나이 든 분이 젊은이에게 |
| 아가씨/아주머니 | "Miss" / "Ma'am" | 여성에게 |
| 아저씨 | "Sir" / "Mister" | 남성에게 |
| 젊은이 | "Young man/woman" | 청년에게 |

**Variations:**

| Expression | Tone | Context |
|------------|------|---------|
| "Oh, student" | 놀람 + 친근함 | 예상치 못한 만남 |
| "Hey, student" | 캐주얼 | 친근한 부름 |
| "Excuse me, student" | 정중 | 공손한 부름 |
| "Young man" | 중립/격식 | 일반적 부름 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Old man: Oh, student.
Student: ...Yes?
Old man: Where are you headed?
Student: To school.
Old man: At this hour? Walking?
Student: Yes, sir.
```

**Cultural Note:**
- 한국에서 "학생"이라고 부르는 것은 영어권에서는 다소 특이하게 느껴질 수 있음
- 영어에서는 보통 "young man," "excuse me," 또는 이름을 직접 물음

### "Where are you going?"

**Original Text:** "Where are you going?"

**Korean Equivalent:** 어디 가?

**Analysis:**
- 간단하지만 관심을 표현하는 질문
- 한국에서는 인사처럼 자주 쓰이는 표현
- 직역하면 "어디 가?"

**Greetings That Are Questions:**

| Expression | Real Meaning | Korean |
|------------|--------------|--------|
| "Where are you going?" | 관심 표현/인사 | 어디 가? |
| "Where have you been?" | 반가움 + 궁금함 | 어디 갔다 왔어? |
| "What are you doing?" | 인사/안부 | 뭐 해? |
| "How are you?" | 인사 | 어떻게 지내? |

**Asking About Destination:**

| Expression | Formality | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "Where are you going?" | ★★★ | 중립 |
| "Where you headed?" | ★★☆ | 캐주얼 |
| "Where are you off to?" | ★★☆ | 친근함 |
| "May I ask where you're going?" | ★★★★ | 매우 정중 |

### "That's quite a ways from here."

**Original Text:** "That place? That's quite a ways from here."

**Korean Equivalent:** 거기? 여기서 꽤 먼데.

**"Quite a ways" Analysis:**
- 구어체 표현으로 "상당히 먼 거리"를 의미
- "ways"는 "way"의 비표준 복수형이지만 구어에서 자연스러움
- 미국 영어에서 흔히 사용

**Distance Expressions:**

| Expression | Distance | Korean |
|------------|----------|--------|
| "just around the corner" | 매우 가까움 | 바로 코앞 |
| "not far" | 가까움 | 멀지 않아 |
| "a bit of a walk" | 걸을 만함 | 좀 걸어야 해 |
| "quite a ways" | 상당히 멀음 | 꽤 먼데 |
| "pretty far" | 멀음 | 꽤 멀어 |
| "a long way" | 매우 멀음 | 먼 길이야 |

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "quite a ways" | "quite far" | "a considerable distance" |
| "a good bit away" | "rather far" | "somewhat distant" |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Where's your school?
B: Jeju Middle School.
A: That place? That's quite a ways from here.
B: About an hour and ten minutes.
A: And you're walking there?
B: Yes.
```

### "What did he say?"

**Original Text:** "What did he say?"

**Korean Equivalent:** 뭐라고 했어?

**Follow-up Questions in Conversations:**

| Question | Purpose | Korean |
|----------|---------|--------|
| "What did he say?" | 구체적 내용 | 뭐라고 했어? |
| "Then what?" | 다음 내용 | 그래서? |
| "And?" | 계속 | 그래서? |
| "What happened next?" | 다음 상황 | 다음에 뭐가 됐어? |
| "How did you respond?" | 반응 | 넌 뭐라고 했어? |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: I met an old man at the tangerine orchard today.
B: An old man? Who?
A: I don't know. I think he's the owner of the orchard.
B: What did he say?
A: He asked where I was going. When I said school, he was surprised.
B: Then what?
A: He said "gyeong hae." Go ahead and do that.
B: Sounds like he was cheering you on.
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING SPACES/SITUATIONS (공간/상황 묘사)

## 3.1 Describing People

### "An old man."

**Original Text:** "Someone was standing up ahead. An old man."

**Korean Equivalent:** 누군가 앞에 서 있었다. 할아버지였다.

**Fragment Style for Impact:**
- 짧은 문장으로 긴장감/호기심 유발
- "Someone was standing." + "An old man." = 점진적 정보 공개

**Describing Unknown People:**

| Order | Example | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 1 | "Someone was there" | 존재 인식 |
| 2 | "An old man" | 대략적 정보 |
| 3 | "Wearing a worn jacket" | 외모 묘사 |
| 4 | "In his seventies" | 구체적 추정 |

## Pattern 2: "A simple [noun]. But somehow... it felt [adjective]."

**Original:** "A simple exchange. But somehow... it felt warm."

**Korean:** 간단한 대화였다. 하지만 왠지... 따뜻하게 느껴졌다.

**Structure:** [Simplicity statement] + "But somehow... it felt [emotion]."

**Practice Examples:**

|--------------|---------|---------------|

**"Somehow" Function:**
- 설명하기 어려운 느낌 표현
- "왠지," "어쩐지"의 뉘앙스
- 감정의 신비로움/깊이 암시

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Meeting a Stranger

```
You: [Walking past a garden]
Stranger: Oh, young man.
You: ...Yes?
Stranger: Where are you headed?
You: To the library.
Stranger: The library? Walking?
You: Yes. It takes about thirty minutes.
Stranger: That's quite a walk. Good for you.
You: Thank you. Have a nice day.
Stranger: You too. Take care.
```

### Scenario 2: Telling a Friend About an Encounter

```
Friend: Anything interesting happen today?
You: Actually, yeah. I met an old man at the park.
Friend: An old man? Who?
You: No idea. I think he was feeding the birds.
Friend: What did he say?
You: He asked where I was going. When I said I was walking to school, he seemed impressed.
Friend: That's nice. What else?
You: He just said "Keep it up." Simple, but it felt good.
Friend: Sounds like he was cheering you on.
You: Yeah, I think so.
```

### Scenario 3: Reflecting on a Meaningful Interaction

```
Mom: How was your walk today?
You: It was good. I met someone interesting.
Mom: Oh? Who?
You: An old man at the tangerine orchard. I think he's the owner.
Mom: What did he say?
You: He was surprised I was walking to school. But he didn't think it was weird.
Mom: What did he say exactly?
You: "Gyeong hae." Like, "go ahead and do that."
Mom: That's nice. Sounds like he was supporting you.
You: Yeah. I can't stop thinking about it. It felt like... being acknowledged.
Mom: That's what happens when someone sees your effort.
You: I want to see him again.
```

## 10.5 Discussion Questions

1. **Have you ever had a brief encounter with a stranger that stayed with you?** What made it memorable? Why do simple interactions sometimes mean more than long conversations?

2. **The old man said "gyeong hae" (go ahead/do that) instead of questioning or criticizing.** How does unconditional acceptance feel different from advice or judgment?

3. **Doyun says "It felt like being acknowledged."** What does it mean to feel acknowledged? Why is it important, especially when doing something different from the norm?

4. **The narrator hesitated to greet the stranger but the old man spoke first.** How does it change an interaction when someone else initiates? Have you experienced this?

5. **Why do you think Doyun wants to see the old man again?** They only exchanged a few words. What makes us want to reconnect with certain people?

6. **Minseo says "He probably thought you were impressive."** Do you think walking to school for over an hour is impressive? What makes an action impressive?

7. **Doyun plans to greet the old man next time: "Good morning, I'm heading to school."** Why is this significant? How does planning a greeting show character growth?
