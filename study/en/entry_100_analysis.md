### Phrasal Verbs in Entry 100

| Phrasal Verb | Korean | Structure | Example from Text | Meaning |
|--------------|--------|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| "take it easy" | "가볍게/편하게 하다" | take + it + easy | "Take it easy this week." | Move/live without stress or excessive effort |

### Detailed Analysis: "Take It Easy"

```
LITERAL: take (grasp) + it (unspecified object) + easy (without difficulty)

ACTUAL MEANING: Live/proceed/exist without stress or maximum effort

COACH'S USAGE IN ENTRY 100:
"Take it easy this week."
→ Reduce training intensity
→ Allow body recovery
→ Maintain mental calm

SIMILAR EXPRESSIONS:
- "Go easy" (더 부드럽게)
- "Take things slowly" (천천히)
- "Chill out" (긴장 풀다)
- "Relax" (편하게 있다)

VARIATIONS IN CONTEXT:
- Doctor: "Take it easy on that leg." (부상 부위)
- Friend: "Take it easy, man!" (스트레스 풀어)
- Coach: "Take it easy this week." (경기력 회복)
```

### Phrasal Verb Comparisons

| Phrasal Verb | Similar Verb | Difference | Example |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| "pack... up" | "pack" | "pack up" includes organization/finality | "I packed up my gear." vs. "I packed my gear." |
| "take... easy" | "rest" | "take it easy" includes mental aspect | "I rested." vs. "I took it easy." |
| "give... rest" | "rest" | "give rest" emphasizes allowing/permitting | "Your body needs rest." vs. "Give your body a rest." |

### Expressing Mixed Emotions in English

**Pattern: "Yeah. But [contrasting emotion]"**

```
Structure:
Affirmative to first question + But + contradicting/additional emotion

Examples:
- "Are you nervous?" "Yeah. But I'm excited too."
- "Are you ready?" "Yeah. But I'm still a bit scared."
- "Do you like the pressure?" "Yeah. But it's stressful too."

Why this works:
- The "Yeah" shows honesty about first feeling
- The "But" shows it's not the whole picture
- Creates realistic, nuanced emotional portrait
- Especially authentic for teen speech patterns
```

## PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

### Characteristics of Doyun's Teen Voice

#### Feature 1: Sentence Fragments

| Feature | Example | Why It's Natural |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Incomplete sentences | "Feeling good." | Teen speech drops subjects |
| One-word answers | "Yeah." "Okay." "Got it." | Quick conversational rhythm |
| Subject omission | "Just 5km, nice and easy." | Korean-influenced directness |
| Predicate focus only | "Clear skies, warm weather." | Efficiency in diary writing |

#### Feature 2: Parallel Structures

```
COACH'S RAPID-FIRE INSTRUCTIONS:
"Sleep early. Don't eat too much. Don't stress."

WHY THIS IS CASUAL:
- Avoids connecting words (and, so, therefore)
- Uses simple imperative form
- Creates rhythmic, memorable pattern
- Sounds like real coach speech

FORMAL VERSION:
"You should sleep early, avoid eating too much, and try not to stress."
(Loses the casual punch and authenticity)
```

#### Feature 3: Contrastive "But"

```
"Yeah. But I'm excited too."

WHAT THIS DOES:
1. Affirms first statement (nervous)
2. Immediately qualifies it (but also excited)
3. Shows emotional complexity without over-explaining
```

### Quick Reference Card 8: Comparison of Similar Expressions

**Card Title: Choose the Right Expression**

```
FOR READINESS:
"I'm ready." (strongest) ✓ Use for final declaration
"I feel ready." (moderate) ✓ Use for middle discussion
"I'm kind of ready." (weaker) ✓ Use only if truly uncertain

FOR INSTRUCTIONS:
"Take it easy." (casual) ✓ Coach to athlete
"You should take it easy." (formal) ✓ Parent to child
"Take it easy, if you would." (very formal) ✗ Wrong for this context

FOR PACKING:
"I packed it." (simple) = basic completion
"I packed everything." (emphatic) = thorough completion
"I packed everything already." (emphatic + timing) ✓ Shows advance readiness

FOR EMOTIONS:
"I'm nervous." (single emotion) ✗ Incomplete picture
"Yeah. But I'm excited too." (balanced) ✓ Realistic complexity
"I'm nervous and excited." (both listed) = Acceptable but less natural

FOR MOTIVATION:
"I think I can do this." (uncertain) ✗ Reduces power
"I can do this." (assured) ✓ Strong, authentic
"I will do this." (determined) = Even stronger, slightly different
```
