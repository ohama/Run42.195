# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Expressing Plans & Commitments

### "I'm gonna come cheer for you at the race."

**Original Korean:** "경기에 응원하러 갈 거야."

**Grammar Point:** Future tense with "gonna" (informal)

**Pronunciation Tip:**
- "gonna" = /ɡɑnə/ (going to의 축약)
- 공식 문서에선 "going to", 대화에선 "gonna" 많이 사용

**Variations by Formality:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "I'm gonna come cheer." | "I'm going to come cheer." | "I will come to cheer you on." |
| "I'll be there!" | "I'll be there to support you." | "I shall attend the event." |
| "Count me in!" | "I'll definitely go." | "You can count on my attendance." |

**Commitment Expressions:**

| Expression | Strength | Example |
|------------|----------|---------|
| "I might come." | 약한 약속 | "I might come if I'm free." |
| "I'll try to come." | 시도 | "I'll try to come, but no promises." |
| "I'm gonna come." | 강한 약속 | "I'm gonna come cheer for you." |
| "I'll definitely be there." | 확실한 약속 | "I'll definitely be there." |
| "Nothing could stop me." | 매우 강한 약속 | "Nothing could stop me from being there." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Will you come to my soccer game?
B: I'm gonna come cheer for you!
A: Really? When is it?
B: This Saturday at 2pm.
A: Perfect, I'll be there!
```

**Family Participation Phrases:**


## 1.2 Asking for Information

### "Where's the race course?"

**Original Korean:** "경기 코스가 어디야?"

**Question Word Usage:**

| Word | Subject | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Where | 장소 | "Where's the race course?" |
| What | 것 | "What time does it start?" |
| When | 시간 | "When is the race?" |
| How long | 거리/시간 | "How long is the race?" |
| Which | 선택 | "Which course is easiest?" |

**Race-Related Questions:**

```
"Where do runners start?"
"How long does it take to finish?"
"Are there water stations?"
"Will there be medical staff?"
"What's the weather forecast?"
```

---

### "So I can see you somewhere in the middle?"

**Original Korean:** "그럼 중간쯤에 나 볼 수 있어?"

**Grammar Point:** "So" for connecting cause and effect

**Structure:** So [purpose/result]?

**Examples:**

| Context | Purpose | Full Sentence |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| 경기 코스 설명 후 | 볼 수 있나 | "So I can see you somewhere in the middle?" |
| 시간 물었을 때 | 약속 가능한가 | "So you can definitely make it?" |
| 거리 알았을 때 | 도움될 수 있는가 | "So you'll need water on the way?" |

**Logical Connection Phrases:**

```
"That means you'll pass by here?"
"In other words, you'll run along the coast?"
"So the finish line is back at the starting point?"
"That means we could cheer twice?"
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS & EMOTIONAL STATES (신체 감각 및 감정 표현)

## 2.1 Fear & Anxiety

### "I'm scared. 21km is my first time."

**Original Korean:** "무서워. 21km는 처음이야."

**"Scared" vs "Nervous" vs "Afraid":**

| Expression | Context | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "I'm nervous." | 불안한 상태 | ★★☆ |
| "I'm anxious." | 계속되는 불안 | ★★★ |
| "I'm scared." | 두려움 | ★★★★ |
| "I'm terrified." | 극심한 두려움 | ★★★★★ |

**Fear Expressions About New Challenges:**

```
"I'm scared of running that distance."
"I'm worried I won't finish."
"What if I hit the wall at 15km?"
"I'm not sure I'm ready for this."
"The distance intimidates me."
```

**Reassurance Responses:**

| Statement | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm scared." | "That's normal. You'll do great." |
| "I'm nervous." | "You've trained for this. Trust yourself." |
| "What if I fail?" | "You won't fail. Just finish—that's the goal." |

---

### "You were scared before the 10km race too, right?"

**Original Korean:** "10km 경기 전에도 무서워했잖아."

**Grammar Point:** "Before" as time reference

**"Before" Usage:**

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Before + noun | 시간 이전 | "Before the race, I trained hard." |
| Before + -ing | 행동 이전 | "Before running, I stretch." |
| Have + before | 과거 경험 | "Have you run a marathon before?" |
| Not + before | 처음 경험 | "I've never been this nervous before." |

**Pattern: "You [did this] before"**

```
"You were scared before, and you made it."
"You doubted yourself last time, but you succeeded."
"You've handled difficult situations before."
"Remember when you thought you couldn't? You did it."
```

---

### "But you did great then. You'll do great this time too."

**Original Korean:** "그런데 잘했잖아. 이번에도 잘할 거야."

**Reassurance & Encouragement:**

| Type | Expression | Effect |
|------|------------|--------|
| 과거 성공 언급 | "You did great last time." | 자신감 회복 |
| 능력 인정 | "You're capable." | 자존감 상승 |
| 미래 긍정 | "You'll do great this time." | 희망 제공 |
| 논리 제시 | "You trained hard. You're ready." | 합리적 위로 |
| 감정 공유 | "I believe in you." | 지지 표현 |

**Encouragement Patterns:**

```
"You did it before, you can do it again."
"This time will be even better than last time."
"Look how far you've come. You're strong."
"I've seen what you're capable of. Trust yourself."
"Remember how you felt after finishing? You'll feel that again."
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING RACE PREPARATION & TRAINING (경기 준비 및 훈련 묘사)

## 3.1 Training Language

### "I'm only doing 5 to 7km these days."

**Original Korean:** "요즘은 5~7km만 하고 있어."

**Describing Training Volume:**

| Expression | Distance | Meaning |
|------------|----------|---------|
| "I'm doing short runs." | 3-5km | 가벼운 훈련 |
| "I'm doing medium runs." | 5-10km | 중간 정도 훈련 |
| "I'm doing long runs." | 10km+ | 장거리 훈련 |

**Training Phases:**

```
"Building up for the race"
"Increasing my mileage"
"Maintaining my fitness"
"Tapering down before the race"
"Easy runs to recover"
"Speed work on Tuesdays"
```

---

### "You shouldn't push hard right now."

**Original Korean:** "지금은 과하게 하면 안 된다."

**"Shouldn't" for Advice/Warnings:**

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| should | 일반적 조언 | "You should train regularly." |
| shouldn't | 피해야 할 것 | "You shouldn't overdo it." |
| must | 필수 | "You must stretch after running." |
| mustn't | 절대 피해야 할 것 | "You mustn't run injured." |
| had better | 강한 권고 | "You'd better rest now." |

**Coach-like Advice:**

```
"Don't push yourself too hard."
"You need to ease up on the training."
"Now's the time to conserve energy."
"Rest is part of training."
"Pushing now could lead to injury."
```

---

### "Cut it down even more. Maybe 3 to 5km."

**Original Korean:** "더 줄여. 아마 3~5km?"

**Tapering Language (경기 전 훈련 감소):**

| Week | Volume | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| 4 weeks out | 75% | "Keep regular training" |
| 2 weeks out | 50% | "Start tapering" |
| 1 week out | 25% | "Just easy runs" |
| Race week | 10% | "Minimal running" |

**Tapering Instructions:**

```
"Reduce your mileage by half."
"Run shorter distances."
"Focus on recovery this week."
"Light jogging only—nothing intense."
"Your body needs rest before the big day."
```

## 3.2 Race Logistics

### "It starts at Jeju Sports Complex, goes out along the coastal road, then comes back to the complex."

**Original Korean:** "제주 스포츠 컴플렉스에서 시작해서, 해안도로로 나갔다가 다시 돌아와."

**Describing a Route:**

| Part | Korean | English |
|------|--------|---------|
| 시작점 | "출발점" | "Starting point" |
| 방향 | "해안도로를 따라" | "Along the coastal road" |
| 방식 | "나갔다가 돌아와" | "Out and back" |
| 종점 | "돌아와서 끝" | "Returns to start" |

**Route Description Pattern:**

```
"We start at [location]..."
"...then we head [direction]..."
"...the route takes us [along/through]..."
"...finally we return to [location]..."
```

**Route Types:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Out and back | 나갔다가 돌아오는 코스 |
| Loop | 한 바퀴 도는 코스 |
| Point-to-point | 시작점과 다른 곳에서 끝남 |
| Multiple loops | 같은 구간 여러 번 |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Where's [place]?"

**Structure:** Where's + location/thing?

**Examples in Context:**

```
"Where's the race course?"
"Where's the starting point?"
"Where's the finish line?"
"Where's the 10km mark?"
```

**Variations:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Where's the finish?" | "Where is the finish line?" | "Where is the designated finish line?" |
| "Where at?" | "Where is it?" | "Where might that be?" |

---

## Pattern 2: "Can you [verb] [object] somewhere?"

**Structure:** Can you [verb] me somewhere (in the race)?

**Examples:**

```
"Can you see me somewhere in the middle?"
"Can you cheer for me around the 10km mark?"
"Can you find a good spot to watch?"
"Can you wait somewhere along the route?"
```

**Permission Variations:**

| Question | Implication |
|----------|-------------|
| "Can you see me?" | 가능한가? |
| "Will you be able to see me?" | 가능할까? |
| "Could you see me?" | 예의 있는 요청 |
| "Do you think you'll see me?" | 불확실성 |

---

## Pattern 3: "You [did X before], you'll do [good outcome] this time too."

**Structure:** You + past perfect + you'll + future

**From Text:**
```
"You were scared before the 10km race too, right?"
"But you did great then. You'll do great this time too."
```

**Formula for Encouragement:**

```
You [doubted yourself] before, and you [succeeded].
You can [do it] again.

You [were nervous] before, and you [did well].
This time will be [even better].

You [overcame challenges] before.
You'll [overcome this one] too.
```

**Practice:**

```
"You thought you couldn't finish 10km, but you did. This time will be even easier."
"You were scared to try a new distance. You did great. You'll do great again."
"You didn't think you could run that far. Look at yourself now."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "Talked with [person] about [topic]"

**Structure:** Talked with + person + about + topic

**Examples:**

```
"Talked with my family about the race."
"Talked with Coach about my training."
"Talked with Minseo about his cheering plan."
```

**Alternative Structures:**

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Talked to | "I talked to my parents." |
| Discussed | "We discussed the race plan." |
| Had a conversation | "I had a conversation with Coach." |
| Brought it up | "I brought up the race with my family." |

---

## Pattern 5: "[Person] said [statement]" / "[Person] asked [question]"

**Structure:** Direct reporting of dialogue

**From Text:**

```
"Dad said, 'I'll wait at the finish line.'"
"Mom asked, 'Can you see me somewhere in the middle?'"
"My little sister said, 'I want to go to the 10km mark!'"
```

**Variation with "said/asked":**

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Person + said | "My sister said she wanted to come." |
| Person + asked | "Coach asked how I was feeling." |
| Person + wondered | "I wondered if I could make it." |
| Person + mentioned | "Dad mentioned the finish line plan." |

---

## Pattern 6: "So many [noun]"

**Structure:** So many + plural noun

**From Text:**
```
"So many people cheering me on."
```

**Emphasis Pattern:**

| Expression | Meaning |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Discussing Race Preparation

```
A: Hey, how's your training going?
B: Pretty good. I'm doing 5 to 7km these days.
A: Are you doing anything special to prepare?
B: Yeah, I'm tapering this week. Coach said to cut back.
A: Smart. How many kilometers for the race?
B: 21 kilometers. It's my first time at that distance.
A: That's a big jump! Are you nervous?
B: Honestly? Yeah, a bit. But I trained hard, so I'm ready.
A: You'll do great! When is it?
B: In seventeen days. I'm really grateful everyone wants to come.
```

### Scenario 2: Planning Where to Cheer

```
A: I'm coming to cheer for you at the race!
B: Really? That's awesome!
A: Yeah! My whole family wants to come too.
B: That's so great. The course starts at the Sports Complex and goes out along the coastal road.
A: So where can I see you?
B: Around the 10km mark, probably. That's the halfway point of the coastal section.
A: Got it. I'll be there waiting for you.
B: Thanks so much. Having you there will mean a lot.
A: We can cheer you on at the 10km mark, and my dad will wait at the finish line.
B: This is making me feel so much better. I was nervous, but now I feel strong.
```

### Scenario 3: Giving Encouragement

```
A: Hey, are you nervous about the race?
B: Yeah, kind of. 21km is my first time. What if I can't finish?
A: You'll finish. Look, you were nervous before the 10km race too, right?
B: Yeah, I was.
A: But you did great then. You'll do great this time too.
B: I guess you're right. I did finish that one.
A: Exactly. You've got this.
B: Thanks. I really appreciate the encouragement.
A: That's what friends are for. Let me know if you need anything.
```
