# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Daily Routine & Habit Formation

### "Running every day has become a habit at this point."

**Original Korean:** "매일 뛰는 것이 이제 습관이 됐다"

**Analysis:**
- 행동이 자동적으로 반복되는 상태 표현
- "at this point"는 "지금쯤이면/이제는"의 의미

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "It's a habit now." | "It's become a habit at this point." | "It has become habitual." |
| "I do it every day." | "I run every day automatically." | "I engage in daily running practice." |
| "It's just what I do." | "It's just routine now." | "It is now part of my routine." |

**Grammar Point:** Present Perfect for Completed Actions with Present Relevance
```
It + has become + noun
Subject + has been + verb-ing + for time period
```

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Do you still hate running?
B: Not anymore. Running every day has become a habit at this point.
A: When did that change?
B: Gradually. I didn't notice at first, but now I just... do it.
```

---

### "Left at 5:15 AM."

**Original Korean:** "새벽 5시 15분에 나갔다"

**Grammar Point:** Elliptical sentence (주어/목적어 생략)

**Full form:** "I left at 5:15 AM."

**Departure Expressions:**

| Time | Expression | Nuance |
|------|------------|--------|
| 아침 일찍 | "Left early morning." | 자연스러운 설명 |
| 정확한 시간 | "Left at 5:15 AM." | 구체적 시간 |
| 무심코 | "Took off at dawn." | 약간 캐주얼 |
| 서두르며 | "Rushed out at 5 AM." | 바쁜 느낌 |

**Diary Writing Style:**
In diary/journal writing, subjects are often omitted for brevity:
```
"Left at 5:15 AM. 10km route."
"Finished in 56 minutes."
"Had lunch with Dad."
```

---

### "Can I keep running while I'm going to school?"

**Original Korean:** "학교에 다니면서 계속 뛸 수 있을까?"

**"While/While -ing" Pattern:**

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| while I'm + verb-ing | 동시에 진행 | "Keep running while I'm at school" |
| while I + verb | 시간 동안 | "What can I do while I wait?" |
| while I'm doing X | 특정 기간 | "Can I work while I'm studying?" |

**Variations:**
- "Can I manage running and school at the same time?"
- "Is it possible to keep running once school starts?"
- "Will I be able to run while attending school?"

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "Can I keep running when I'm going to school?" (의미 다름 - 출발할 때를 의미)
✅ "Can I keep running while I'm going to school?" (다니는 동안)

## 1.2 Personal Reflection & Planning

### "I need to figure that out."

**Original Korean:** "그걸 어떻게 할지 생각해봐야겠다"

**"Figure out" Meanings:**

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 알아내다 | discover | "I figured out the answer." |
| 계획을 세우다 | plan | "We need to figure out the details." |
| 이해하다 | understand | "Can you help me figure this out?" |
| 해결책을 찾다 | solve | "How will I figure out the transportation?" |

**Similar Problem-Solving Expressions:**

| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "I need to figure it out." | 스스로 해결해야 함 |
| "I need to work it out." | 좀 더 천천히 풀어야 함 |
| "I need to sort it out." | 정리해야 함 |
| "I need to deal with it." | 대응해야 함 |
| "I need to handle it." | 처리해야 함 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: What are you worried about?
B: School starts soon, and I'm not sure how I'll keep up with running.
A: Have you thought about waking up earlier?
B: Yeah, I need to figure that out. Maybe early morning runs before school?
```

### "I'm actually looking forward to it."

**Original Korean:** "진짜 기대돼."

**"Look forward to" Pattern:**

| Structure | Tense | Meaning |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| look forward to + noun | present | 미래를 기대하다 |
| am looking forward to | present progressive | 지금 기대하고 있다 |
| was looking forward to | past | 기대했었다 |
| wouldn't look forward to | conditional | 기대하지 않을 것 |

**Enthusiasm Spectrum:**

| Expression | Level | Meaning |
|------------|-------|---------|
| "I'm excited about it." | ★★ | 조금 기대 |
| "I'm interested in it." | ★★ | 관심 있음 |
| "I'm looking forward to it." | ★★★ | 기대 중 |
| "I'm really looking forward to it." | ★★★★ | 매우 기대 |
| "I can't wait!" | ★★★★★ | 매우 들뜸 |

**Pronunciation & Intonation:**
- "I'm actually looking forward to it." (강조 - 예상과 다른 감정 강조)
- "I'm looking forward to it." (평탄 - 자연스러운 기대)

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running & Athletic Experience

**Original Korean:** "해바라기들이 시들어가고 있었다"

**Vocabulary:**
- Droop: 고개를 숙이다, 처지다
- Wilt: (식물이) 시들다
- Sag: 축 처지다

**Flower/Plant Life Stages:**

| Stage | Expression | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 피어남 | "blooming" | "The cherry blossoms are blooming." |
| 만개 | "in full bloom" | "The roses are in full bloom." |
| 지기 시작 | "starting to fade" | "The flowers are fading." |
| 시듦 | "drooping" | "The sunflowers were drooping." |
| 떨어짐 | "petals falling" | "Petals were falling everywhere." |
| 시듦 | "wilting" | "The roses are wilting." |

**Seasonal Imagery in Writing:**
```
"The sunflowers drooped as summer ended."
(여름이 끝나감을 상징)

"Their heads bowed down. Petals falling."
(슬픔/피로의 은유)
```

### "Their heads bowed down."

**Original Korean:** "그들의 머리가 숙어 있었다"

**Metaphorical Language:**
- Flowers have "heads" that can "bow"
- This personification creates emotional connection

**Similar Personification:**
- "The trees swayed gently." (나무가 흔들렸다)
- "The grass bent in the wind." (풀이 구부러졌다)
- "Branches reached toward the sky." (가지가 뻗어 올랐다)

**Practice - Write your own seasonal personification:**
```
"The ___ was ___ing."
Examples:
"The leaves were falling softly."
"The wind was howling loudly."
"The rain was pounding the roof."
```

---

### "Petals falling."

**Original Korean:** "꽃잎이 떨어지고 있었다"

**Fragment for Sensory Effect:**
- Complete form: "I noticed petals falling."
- Fragment: "Petals falling." (더 시적이고 임팩트 있음)

**Why fragments work:**
- 독자의 주의력 강화
- 시간 흐름의 가속화 효과
- 슬픔/변화의 감정 전달

### "Even the cicada sounds seemed quieter than before."

**Original Korean:** "매미 소리도 전보다 조용해 보였다"

**Grammar Point:** "Seemed" for Observation and Speculation

| Structure | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|

| Verb | What You Perceive |

# PART 3: DESCRIBING SEASONAL CHANGE (계절 변화 묘사)

## 3.1 Seasonal Observations

### "Running gave me time to feel the seasons changing."

**Original Korean:** "달리기는 계절 변화를 느낄 수 있는 시간을 줬다"

**Grammar Point:** "gave me time to [verb]" 구조

| Structure | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "give + person + time to + verb" | ~할 시간을 주다 |
| "allow + person + to + verb" | ~하도록 허락하다 |
| "provide + person + with + opportunity to + verb" | 기회를 제공하다 |

**Synonyms:**
- "Running allowed me to feel the seasonal changes."
- "Through running, I was able to feel the seasons changing."
- "Running provided time for me to experience the seasons."

**Similar Life Lesson Expressions:**
```
"[Activity] gave me time to appreciate..."
"[Activity] allowed me to understand..."
"[Activity] taught me to notice..."
```

---

### "When I first started back in March, it was chilly."

**Original Korean:** "3월에 처음 시작했을 때는 쌀쌀했다"

**Temporal Reference Phrases:**

| Phrase | Meaning | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "When I first started" | 처음 시작했을 때 | Past event |
| "back in [month]" | ~전에, 예전에 | Nostalgia |
| "it was [adjective]" | 그때는 [상태]였다 | Past observation |

**Timeline Narrative Structure:**
```
"When I first started back in March, it was chilly."
"April came with cherry blossoms."
"May had mandarin blossoms."
"June brought hydrangeas and the rainy season."
```
(한 년의 흐름을 계절별로 표현)

### "April came with cherry blossoms."

**Original Korean:** "4월이 되고 벚꽃이 피었다"

**Poetic Language - "Month + came with":**

| Expression | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| "April came with cherry blossoms." | 시적, 자연스러운 흐름 |
| "Cherry blossoms arrived in April." | 더 직설적 |
| "April brought cherry blossoms." | 중간 톤 |
| "The cherry blossoms came in April." | 일상적 |

**Why this language works:**
- Personifies months as bearers of seasonal change
- Creates poetic, flowing narrative
- Evokes nostalgia and beauty

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "How are [things] doing?"

**Structure:** How + be verb + [noun] + doing?

**From Text:** "How are your mandarins doing, Grandfather?"

| Question | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "How are your mandarins doing?" | 과일/작물 |
| "How are the kids doing?" | 가족 |
| "How are things doing at work?" | 직장 |
| "How's the business doing?" | 사업 |

**Variations:**
- "How are your mandarins coming along?"
- "How's the mandarin crop progressing?"
- "Tell me about your mandarins."

---

## Pattern 2: "Once [condition], they'll [consequence]."

**Structure:** Once + clause, + subject + will + verb

**From Text:** "Once fall gets here, they'll start changing color."

| Example | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| "Once fall gets here, they'll start changing color." | 색이 변한다 |
| "Once school starts, I'll have less time." | 시간이 줄어든다 |
| "Once winter comes, they'll be dormant." | 휴면한다 |

**Similar Temporal Clauses:**

| Conjunction | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| once | "Once it starts, it won't stop." |
| when | "When it starts, it won't stop." |
| as soon as | "As soon as it starts, it stops raining." |
| by the time | "By the time we arrive, it'll be done." |

---

## Pattern 3: "Keep [verb-ing]."

**Structure:** Keep + verb-ing

**From Text:** "Keep working hard. Running in fall is the best."

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "Keep working hard." | 계속 열심히 해 |
| "Keep running." | 계속 뛰어 |
| "Keep trying." | 계속 노력해 |
| "Keep moving." | 계속 나아가 |

**Variations:**
- "Don't stop working hard."
- "Continue working hard."
- "Stay committed to hard work."

---

## Pattern 4: "I'll bring you [object] [time]."

**Structure:** I'll bring you + object + when/by + time

**From Text:** "I'll bring you some then."

**Variations:**

| Example | Time Reference |
|---------|-----------------|
| "I'll bring you some then." | 그때 |
| "I'll bring you some next week." | 다음주 |
| "I'll bring you some when they're ready." | 준비되면 |
| "I'll bring you some by November." | 11월까지 |

---

## Pattern 5: "[Time] passed. [Event happened]."

**Structure:** Time reference + past tense

**From Text:**
```
"Finished the 10km in 56 minutes.
Two minutes faster than last time."
```

**This pattern is common in:**
- Diary entries
- Sports/fitness logs
- Brief news reporting
- Quick summaries

---

## Pattern 6: "What happens when [condition]?"

**Structure:** What + happens + when + [future scenario]?

**From Text:** "What happens when summer break ends?"

| Question | Worry |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Grandfather & Grandchild About Farming

```
Doyun: Grandfather, how are your mandarins doing?
Grandfather: They're starting to grow now. Once fall gets here, they'll change color.
Doyun: When do you harvest them?
Grandfather: Around November. I'll bring you some then.
Doyun: Thank you! They must be hard to grow.
Grandfather: They are, but it's worth it. Keep working hard at your running.
Doyun: I will. I love running in the fall.
Grandfather: Me too. The mornings are so crisp.
```

### Scenario 2: Father Notices Son's Progress

```
Dad: So you're running these days?
Doyun: Yeah, I've been running every day.
Dad: How much are you running?
Doyun: 10 kilometers.
Dad: 10km? That's impressive!
Doyun: I'm two minutes faster than last week.
Dad: You're doing great. I'm proud of you.
Doyun: Really? You actually noticed?
Dad: Of course. You're getting stronger every day.
```

### Scenario 3: Talking About Seasonal Change

```
Friend: What have you been up to?
Doyun: Just running mostly. It gave me time to feel the seasons changing.
Friend: Like how?
Doyun: Well, back in March it was chilly, then cherry blossoms came in April.
Friend: And now?
Doyun: Now the mornings are getting cooler. Summer's almost over.
Friend: Are you sad?
Doyun: No, actually. I'm looking forward to running in the fall. That's the best season.
Friend: I should start running too.
```
