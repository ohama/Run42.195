## PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

### Essential Daily Phrases from the Text

| Expression | Korean | Context | Formality |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **I ran on purpose.** | 목적을 가지고 뛰었다 | Intentional action, choice | Casual |
| **I wanted to run.** | 뛰고 싶었다 | Expressing desire | Casual |
| **I didn't oversleep.** | 늦잠을 자지 않았다 | Waking up on time | Casual |
| **I picked up the pace.** | 속도를 올렸다 | Gradually increasing speed | Casual |
| **Things fly by.** | 물건들이 날아간다 | Visual sensation of speed | Colloquial |
| **I was gasping for breath.** | 숨을 헐떡거렸다 | Physical exertion | Casual |
| **Huff, huff.** | 헉헉 (의성어) | Breathing sound effect | Casual |
| **I ran into Grandpa.** | 할아버지를 만났다 | Chance meeting | Casual |
| **His eyes went wide.** | 그의 눈이 커졌다 | Expression of surprise | Casual |
| **You're sweating a lot.** | 땀을 많이 흘리고 있네 | Physical observation | Casual |

### Formality Spectrum & Variations

**Basic Form → Progressive → Stronger Emphasis**

1. **"I wanted to run."**
   - ★☆☆☆☆ Casual: I wanted to run.
   - ★★☆☆☆ Slightly Formal: I felt like running.
   - ★★★☆☆ More Emphatic: I really wanted to run.
   - ★★★★☆ Strong Desire: I desperately wanted to run.

2. **"I didn't oversleep."**
   - ★☆☆☆☆ Casual: I didn't oversleep.
   - ★★☆☆☆ Slightly Formal: I woke up on time.
   - ★★★☆☆ Informative: I managed to wake up at the usual time.
   - ★★★★☆ Proud: For once, I didn't oversleep.

3. **"I picked up the pace."**
   - ★☆☆☆☆ Casual: I picked up the pace.
   - ★★☆☆☆ Natural: I started running faster.
   - ★★★☆☆ Descriptive: I gradually increased my speed.
   - ★★★★☆ Technical: I accelerated my running pace.

### Similar/Related Expressions

**Expressing Intention:**
- I ran on purpose. (=I ran intentionally)
- I meant to run. (deliberate action)
- I ran because I wanted to. (motivation-focused)
- I chose to run. (emphasis on agency)

**Describing Physical Sensations:**
- I was gasping for breath. (heavy breathing)
- I was panting. (lighter breathing)
- I was out of breath. (very tired)
- I could barely catch my breath. (extreme exhaustion)

### Practice Dialogue 1: Talking About Intentions

```
A: Why did you come in late today?
B: I didn't oversleep, if that's what you're wondering.
A: Oh? So you came late on purpose?
B: Sort of. I had to take care of something first.
A: I see. Well, glad you're here now.
```

### Practice Dialogue 2: Reacting to Effort

```
A: You look exhausted. What happened?
B: I went for a run this morning.
A: A run? I thought you hated exercise?
B: I did. But now I actually want to. It feels good.
A: Really? How so?
B: Hard to explain. You just feel energized afterward.
```

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "I ran on the purpose." | I ran on purpose. | "on purpose" is fixed idiom, no article needed |
| "I picked up a pace." | I picked up the pace. | "pick up the pace" is specific phrase |
| "His eyes went widely." | His eyes went wide. | "go wide" uses adjective, not adverb |
| "I was sweating very much." | I was sweating a lot. | Native speakers prefer "a lot" in casual contexts |
| "I ran into him accidently." | I ran into him. | "ran into" means coincidentally met, so "accidently" is redundant |

## PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

### Running-Related Physical Sensations

| Sensation | Expression | Korean | Intensity |
|-----------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Breathing difficulty | Gasping for breath | 숨을 헐떡거리다 | ★★★★★ |
| Heavy breathing | Huff, huff | 헉헉 | ★★★☆☆ |
| Racing heart | Heart pounded | 심장이 쿵쿵거렸다 | ★★★☆☆ |
| Wind sensation | Wind brushed my face | 바람이 얼굴을 스쳤다 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| Hair movement | Hair whipped around | 머리가 흔들렸다 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| Perspiration | Sweating a lot | 땀을 많이 흘리다 | ★★★☆☆ |
| Refreshment | Felt refreshing | 상큼했다 | ★★★☆☆ |
| Energy loss | Something drained out | 뭔가 빠져나갔다 | ★★☆☆☆ |

### Creating Physical Sensation Descriptions

**The Formula:**
[Body part] + [Physical verb] + [Manner/Intensity]

**Examples from the text:**
- "My **heart** **pounded**" (심장이 쿵쿵 뛰었다)
- "My **hair** **whipped around**" (머리가 휘날렸다)
- "Wind **brushed** my **face**" (바람이 얼굴을 스쳤다)

### Intensity Spectrum of Breathing Difficulty

```
Light Exercise:    (normal) → breathing faster
Moderate Exercise: breathing faster → panting
Hard Exercise:     panting → gasping for breath
Extreme Effort:    gasping → can barely catch breath → nearly fainting
```

**Vocabulary:**
- Breathing harder
- Panting (헉헉대다)
- Gasping for breath (숨을 헐떡거리다)
- Short of breath (숨이 찬)
- Out of breath (숨이 차다)
- Breathless (숨도 못 쉴 정도로)

### The Runner's Body: Before and After

**During Running:**
- Heart pounding (심장이 쿵쿵 뛰다)
- Gasping for breath (숨을 헐떡거리다)
- Body heating up (몸이 뜨거워지다)
- Muscles burning (근육이 타는 듯한 느낌)

**After Running:**
- Sweating heavily (땀을 많이 흘리다)
- Breathing gradually slowing (숨이 천천히 가라앉다)
- Feeling refreshed (상큼한 느낌)
- Energy drained (에너지가 빠져나간 듯한 느낌)

### Practice Dialogue 3: Describing Physical Experience

```
A: How did your run go?
B: Exhausting! I was gasping for breath the whole time.
A: Did you push yourself too hard?
B: Maybe a little. My heart was pounding really fast.
A: You should take it easier next time.
B: I know. But I felt so refreshed afterward.
A: That's the runner's high, I guess.
B: Exactly! All that discomfort is worth it.
```

### Common Mistakes with Physical Descriptions

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "My heart pounded fast." | My heart pounded. | "pounded" already implies fast/strong beating |
| "I was sweating very." | I was sweating a lot. | "very" doesn't go with "sweating"; use "a lot" or "heavily" |
| "Breathing was hard." | I was gasping for breath. | More descriptive and natural in narrative |
| "Wind hit my face." | Wind brushed my face. | "brushed" is gentler, more literary than "hit" |

## PART 3: DESCRIBING SPACES/SITUATIONS (공간/상황 묘사)

### Environmental Descriptions

| Element | Expression | Korean | Visual Quality |
|---------|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Weather | Clear skies. The wind was cool. | 맑은 하늘. 바람이 시원했다. | Vivid |
| Speed perception | The scenery passes slowly. | 풍경이 천천히 지나간다. | Comparative |
| Movement | Things fly by in front of my eyes. | 물건들이 눈 앞에서 날아간다. | Dynamic |
| Location | The tangerine orchard | 귤밭 | Specific place |
| Time | 6:20 a.m., 7:42, Over an hour | 시간 표현 | Precise |

### Descriptive Framework: Contrasting Views

**Key Comparison in Entry 020:**

Walking vs. Running perspectives:
```
Walking:        Scenery passes slowly → Leisurely pace → Time dilates
Running:        Scenery passes fast → Energetic pace → Time compresses
```

**Sentence structures used:**
- "When I walk, [statement]. But when I run, [contrasting statement]."
- This structure emphasizes the difference in experience

### Physical Space: The Route

```
Starting point: Village boundary
↓ (Running 1 km)
Middle point: Tangerine orchard (meets Grandpa)
↓ (Continue running 1 km)
Running total: 2 km
Walking: Remaining 5 km to school
↓
Endpoint: School (arrive at 7:42)
```

### Temporal Descriptors

| Time Reference | Meaning | Korean |
|----------------|---------|--------|
| "The usual time" | 6:20 a.m. (습관적 시간) | 보통 시간 |
| "Same time as always" | Daily routine marker | 항상 같은 시간 |
| "Eight minutes earlier than usual" | Time saved by running | 평소보다 8분 빨리 |
| "At 6:40" | When he left house | 6시 40분 |
| "By 7:42" | School arrival | 7시 42분 |

### Creating Situational Context

**The entry establishes:**
1. **Initial situation:** Woke up on time (not overslept) - choice, not accident
2. **Transition:** Left at usual time but with different intention
3. **Action:** Gradually increased pace
4. **Peak moment:** Runner's high, meeting grandpa
5. **Resolution:** Arrived early, felt refreshed, convinced of running's value

### Practice Dialogue 4: Discussing Locations and Routines

```
A: So you run every morning?
B: Not every morning. But I did this morning.
A: How far do you go?
B: To school, basically. That's about 7 kilometers.
A: And you run the whole way?
B: Not yet. I only ran 2 kilometers. I walked the rest.
A: Where did you run through?
B: Started at the village boundary, ran past the tangerine orchard.
A: Nice area for a run.
B: Yeah. Plus I ran into my grandfather there.
```

### Mistakes in Spatial/Temporal Description

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "I left the house at 6:40 as usually." | I left at the usual time. | Subject verb agreement; "usual" is adjective |
| "By running, the scenery was fast." | When I run, scenery passes fast. | Clearer cause-effect relationship |
| "I arrived eight minutes earlier than usually." | I arrived eight minutes earlier than usual. | "usual" is the correct form here |
| "The tangerine orchard which I met him there." | I ran into him at the tangerine orchard. | Avoid redundant location markers |

## PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

### Essential Phrasal Verbs in Entry 020

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Korean | Example from Text |
|-------------|---------|--------|------------------|
| **pick up** (pace/speed) | 속도를 높이다 | 加速する | "I gradually picked up the pace." |
| **run into** | 우연히 만나다 | 偶然に会う | "I ran into Grandpa Yeongcheol." |
| **fly by** | 빠르게 지나가다 | 素早く通り過ぎる | "Things fly by in front of my eyes." |
| **brush by/against** | 스쳐 지나가다 | かすめて通る | "The wind brushed my face." |

### Detailed Analysis: "Pick Up"

**Different meanings depending on object:**
- Pick up the pace = 속도를 높이다 (increase speed)
- Pick up the phone = 전화를 받다 (answer call)
- Pick up groceries = 장을 보다 (fetch/buy)
- Pick up on = ~를 눈치채다 (notice)

**Gradient of speed:**
```
"started jogging" → "gradually picked up the pace" → running fast
천천히 시작      →    서서히 속도를 높임      →    빠르게 뛰기
```

### Detailed Analysis: "Run Into"

**Two distinct meanings:**

1. **Literal collision:** To bump into someone physically
   - "I ran into the wall." (벽에 부딪혔다)

2. **Chance meeting:** To meet someone unexpectedly
   - "I ran into Grandpa Yeongcheol at the orchard." (우연히 할아버지를 만났다)
   - Similar: bump into, come across, encounter

**Context matters:**
- "I ran into an old friend." (우연히 만났다 - positive)
- "I ran into a problem." (문제에 부딪혔다 - negative)

### Detailed Analysis: "Fly By"

**Visual/temporal imagery:**

- Literal: "The birds flew by." (새들이 날아갔다)
- Figurative: "The scenery flies by when running." (풍경이 빨리 지나간다)
- Time passing: "The afternoon flew by." (오후가 금방 지나갔다)

**Contrast in entry:**
```
"When I walk, the scenery passes slowly.
But when I run, it's fast. Things fly by in front of my eyes."
```

### Similar Verbs to "Fly By"

| Verb | Korean | Intensity | Context |
|------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Fly by | 날아가다 | ★★★★★ | Very fast, dramatic |
| Zoom past | 쌩하고 지나가다 | ★★★★☆ | Fast, mechanical |
| Rush by | 서두르면서 지나가다 | ★★★☆☆ | Quick but less dramatic |
| Pass by | 지나가다 | ★★☆☆☆ | Neutral speed |
| Drift by | 천천히 지나가다 | ★☆☆☆☆ | Slow, dreamy |

### Practice Dialogue 5: Using Phrasal Verbs in Context

```
A: Tell me about your run this morning.
B: Well, I started slowly and then picked up the pace gradually.
A: How far did you end up running?
B: Two kilometers before I had to walk.
A: That's good progress. Anything interesting happen?
B: Actually, I ran into my grandfather at the orchard.
A: What did he say?
B: He told me to keep running while I'm young. Said when you get old, you can't anymore.
A: That's wise. Everything else okay on the route?
B: Yeah, the scenery was beautiful. When you're running fast, everything flies by though.
A: You'll get better at taking in the sights as you build endurance.
```

### Common Phrasal Verb Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "I picked up the pace at." | I picked up the pace. | "at" is unnecessary with this verb |
| "I ran into him on the orchard." | I ran into him at the orchard. | Use "at" for specific locations |
| "The scenery flew by quickly." | The scenery flew by. | "flew by" already implies speed |
| "I brushed against by the wind." | The wind brushed my face. | Correct agent and patient roles |

## PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

### Pattern 1: Expressing Unexpected Change

**Structure:** "But [time marker], it's different. [New behavior]."

**Formula:**
```
[Past negative experience]
But [now/then], [it's/things are] different.
[Positive new experience]
```

**Examples from text:**
- "In elementary school, I hated running during PE. But now it's different. I run because I want to."
- "At first, I walked because I missed the bus. But I kept doing it because I liked it."

**Variations:**
1. "I used to hate it, but now I love it."
2. "It wasn't like this before, but things have changed."
3. "I never thought I'd enjoy this, but I do."

**Practice sentences:**
- I used to dread the commute, but now I actually enjoy the run to school.
- At first, exercise felt like punishment, but now it's something I choose.
- Exercise used to be mandatory, but wanting to do it changes everything.

---

### Pattern 2: Cause-and-Effect with "Because"

**Simple Structure:** "I do X because [reason]."

**Examples from text:**
- "I run because I want to."
- "It was hard because I ran."
- "Grandpa smiled. I smiled too."

**Variations:**
1. **Affirmative:** "I ran again because I wanted to."
2. **Contrast:** "Not because I had to, but because I wanted to."
3. **Emphasis:** "I ran purely because I wanted to."

**Why this matters:** In Korean culture, agency and choice are emphasized. This pattern shows personal growth.

**Practice dialogue:**
```
A: Why did you start running?
B: Because I wanted to feel that runner's high again.
A: So it's not because you need to get to school?
B: Right. I could take the bus if I had to. I choose to run.
A: That's a big difference.
B: Exactly. Forced exercise and voluntary exercise feel completely different.
```

---

### Pattern 3: Comparative Reflection

**Structure:** "When [condition 1], [result 1]. But when [condition 2], [contrasting result 2]."

**Example from text:**
```
"When I walk, the scenery passes slowly.
But when I run, it's fast.
Things fly by in front of my eyes."
```

**Variations:**
1. **Time perspective:** "When I was younger, I couldn't understand running. But now that I'm older, I see its value."
2. **Action-based:** "When people force you to exercise, it feels like punishment. When you choose it, it feels rewarding."
3. **Intensity-based:** "At first, running was painful. But after 2 kilometers, the pain turned into pleasure."

**Why use this pattern:**
- Shows growth/development
- Illustrates cause-effect
- Creates vivid contrast
- Engages the listener

**Extended example:**
```
A: So has your attitude toward running changed?
B: Completely. When I was forced to run in PE class, I hated it. But when I choose to run on my own, it's amazing.
A: What's the difference?
B: Control, I think. When it's forced, it feels like punishment. When you choose it, it becomes freedom.
```

---

### Pattern 4: Reflection with Rhetorical Questions

**Structure:** "*[Question]* I really had no idea."

**Example from text:**
```
*Running was this good?*
I really had no idea.
```

**Variations:**
1. "*Could I actually enjoy running?* I never would have thought so."
2. "*Was it really worth the effort?* I had no idea until I tried it."
3. "*Why didn't I discover this sooner?* The answer surprised me."

**Emotional weight:**
- Creates introspection
- Engages readers/listeners
- Shows honest surprise
- Emphasizes personal discovery

**Practice dialogue:**
```
A: You seem really surprised about your own enjoyment of running.
B: I am. I mean, *running was this good?* I had no idea.
A: What surprised you most?
B: That it felt good despite being hard. The pain actually made it better somehow.
A: That's the runner's high working.
B: Exactly. *Why didn't anyone explain this to me before?* I might have tried it sooner.
```

---

### Pattern 5: Advice and Wisdom Exchange

**Structure:** "[Person] told me. [Advice]. I think [he/she]'s right."

**Example from text:**
```
"Grandpa Yeongcheol told me.
Run a lot while you're young.
I think he's right.
I should run while I still can."
```

**Variations:**
1. "My coach told me to trust the process. I think he's right."
2. "My friend said finding what you love is key. I think she's right."
3. "An old runner told me that consistency matters most. I think he's right."

**Cultural significance:**
- Shows respect for elders
- Validates wisdom through reflection
- Demonstrates personal agreement/buy-in
- Common in Korean communication

**Extended dialogue:**
```
A: Did anyone encourage your running?
B: Actually, yes. My grandfather. He told me to run while I'm young.
A: What do you think about that?
B: I think he's right. When you get old, you want to run but you can't. So now's the time.
A: That's wise thinking.
B: I realized that advice applies to lots of things, not just running.
```

---

### Pattern 6: Building Evidence ("Even" vs. "But")

**Structure:** "It was hard, *but* it felt good. Just like Monday."

**Example from text:**
```
"I was gasping for breath...
It was hard, but it felt good. Just like Monday."
```

**Key insight:** "But" contradicts expectation. Usually: hard = bad. But here: hard = good (because of the runner's high).

**Variations:**
1. "The pain was intense, but the aftermath felt incredible."
2. "Running every day is challenging, but it's become my favorite part of the day."
3. "It's difficult to wake up early, but I've never regretted doing it."

**Practice context:**
```
A: Isn't running hard?
B: Yeah, definitely. It's hard, but it feels good.
A: How can something hard feel good?
B: That runner's high I mentioned. Your body releases chemicals or something.
A: So the difficulty makes it better?
B: Kind of. The effort is part of what makes it rewarding.
```

## PART 6: EXPRESSING EMOTIONS (감정 표현)

### Emotion Spectrum and Intensity

| Emotion | Mild | Moderate | Strong | Text Evidence |
|---------|------|----------|--------|---------------|
| **Joy/Satisfaction** | Good | Great | Excellent/Amazing | "It felt good" |
| **Enthusiasm** | Interested | Excited | Passionate | "I wanted to run" |
| **Surprise** | Interesting | Surprising | Shocking | "His eyes went wide" |
| **Wonder** | Curious | Amazed | Astounded | "*Running was this good?*" |
| **Confidence** | Sure | Confident | Convinced | "I think he's right" |

### Joy and Satisfaction Expressions

**From the text:**

1. **"It felt good."**
   - Mild intensity
   - Korean: "기분이 좋았다"
   - Context: After running and walking
   - Why understated: Authentic teenage voice

2. **"Just like Monday."**
   - Reinforces positive memory
   - Korean: "지난 월요일처럼"
   - Establishes pattern

3. **"Felt refreshing."**
   - Physical + emotional relief
   - Korean: "상큼했다"
   - Sensory quality of the emotion

4. **"*Running was this good?*"**
   - Combination of wonder and joy
   - Korean: "뛰기가 이렇게까지 좋았나?"
   - Shows genuine discovery

5. **"That's right for you."**
   - Grandfather's approval brings joy
   - Korean: "너한테 딱 맞네"
   - Validation from elder

### Building a Positive Case

**The emotional arc of the entry:**

```
Curiosity (wanting runner's high again)
    ↓
Courage (choosing to run on purpose)
    ↓
Effort (running 2 km)
    ↓
Physical challenge (gasping for breath)
    ↓
Validation (meeting grandfather, friend interest)
    ↓
Realization (running IS this good)
    ↓
Commitment (I'll run again, even farther)
```

### Expressing Surprise at One's Own Enjoyment

**Key phrases:**
- "I never thought I'd enjoy this."
- "I had no idea it was so [good/enjoyable/rewarding]."
- "I didn't expect to love it."
- "It surprised me how much I liked it."

**Why the surprise matters:**
- Shows contrast with past self
- Makes the transformation more credible
- Engages listener's empathy
- Creates relatable moment

**Example dialogue:**
```
A: You seem amazed by your own enjoyment of running.
B: I am! I mean, *running was this good?* I had no idea.
A: Why are you so surprised?
B: Because I used to hate running during PE class.
A: People change their minds about things.
B: I guess. But this feels different. I'm choosing it now.
A: That's the difference choice makes.
```

### Physical Manifestations of Emotion

| Emotion | Physical Sign | Korean | Example |
|---------|--------------|--------|---------|
| Surprise | Eyes go wide | 눈이 크게 떠진다 | "His eyes went wide." |
| Happiness | Smile | 웃음 | "I smiled too." |
| Exhaustion from joy | Breathing hard (good way) | 숨을 헐떡거리다 | "I was gasping for breath. It felt good." |
| Uncertainty | Pause/hesitation | 멈칫하다 | "Minseo paused." |
| Realization | Head shake | 고개를 흔들다 | "Minseo shook his head" |

### Practice Dialogue 6: Discussing New Discoveries

```
A: So you've discovered you love running?
B: Yeah, kind of surprising to me too.
A: What made the difference?
B: Doing it because I wanted to, not because I had to.
A: That makes sense. Are you happy about it?
B: Really happy. I feel like I've unlocked something.
A: Will you keep running?
B: Definitely. Even farther than today if I can.
A: That's the spirit.
B: I used to dread exercise. Now I'm excited about it. Weird, right?
A: Not weird at all. You found something you're passionate about.
```

### Common Emotion Expression Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "I was feeling good." | It felt good. | Simpler, more natural in narrative |
| "I was so amazing surprised." | I was amazed. / That amazed me. | Avoid double emphasis; cleaner structure |
| "The wind felt cold but I liked." | The wind was cool, but I enjoyed it. | Complete the thought; include object of "liked" |
| "I was happy much." | I was really happy. | Use "really" or "very," not "much" after adjective |

## PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

### Characteristic 1: Sentence Fragments (불완전한 문장)

**Pattern:** Incomplete sentences that suggest emotion or emphasis.

**Examples from text:**

1. "*That feeling.*"
   - Korean: "그런 느낌이야"
   - Why fragment: Emphasis, wonder
   - Complete version: "That was the same feeling I had on Monday."
   - Fragment effect: More immediate, intimate

2. "*This feeling.*"
   - Korean: "이 느낌 말이야"
   - Same emphasis technique
   - Creates a "call-out" moment

3. "*So this really is runner's high.*"
   - Korean: "그래서 이게 진짜 러너스 하이구나"
   - Realization moment
   - Fragment creates sudden understanding

4. "*Running has a lot of perks.*"
   - Korean: "뛰기는 장점이 많네"
   - Conclusion moment
   - Could be complete but fragment keeps it casual

### Characteristic 2: Rhetorical Questions (수사적 질문)

**Pattern:** Questions that aren't meant to be answered, but create reflection.

**Examples:**

1. "*Running was this good?*"
   - Korean: "뛰기가 이렇게까지 좋았나?"
   - Effect: Wonder, amazement
   - Not expecting answer; self-directed

2. "*Isn't it hard?*"
   - Korean: "힘들지 않아?"
   - Function: Grandfather's checking-in question
   - Answer expected here, unlike the others

3. "*What I'd read online was true.*"
   - Korean: "온라인에서 본 글이 참이었나"
   - Reflection: Confirming research with experience

### Characteristic 3: Sound Effects/Onomatopoeia (의성어)

**Pattern:** Using sounds to convey physical experience.

**Examples from text:**

1. "Huff, huff."
   - Korean: "헉헉" or "헐떡헐떡"
   - Represents heavy breathing
   - More vivid than "I was panting"
   - Very teen/casual

2. "*Heart pounded*"
   - Not strictly onomatopoeia, but sound-suggesting verb
   - Korean: "두두득두두득" or "쿵쿵거렸다"
   - Creates rhythm in writing

### Characteristic 4: Casual Conversational Tags (상황 표현)

**Pattern:** Adding dialogue indicators that show tone rather than just quoting.

**Examples:**

- "Grandpa nodded. I smiled too." (actions with dialogue)
- "Minseo paused." (shows hesitation)
- "Minseo shook his head." (shows amazement)
- "Minseo thought for a moment, then asked." (shows process)

**Why teens use this:** Shows they're thinking about relationships and reactions, not just words.

### Characteristic 5: Simple Vocabulary + Complex Ideas

**Pattern:** Casual words expressing deep concepts.

**Example:**
```
"Being forced and wanting to are completely different."
(간단한 단어들로 깊은 개념 표현)
```

**Other examples:**
- "Making my own choice changes everything." (자신의 선택이 모든 것을 바꾼다)
- "When you get old, you want to run but you can't." (나이 들면 뛰고 싶어도 못 뛴다)

### Characteristic 6: Short, Punchy Sentences (짧은 문장 연속)

**Pattern:** Series of short statements building momentum.

**Example from text:**
```
"I didn't oversleep. I woke up at the usual time. 6:20 a.m.
But I wanted to run."
```

**Effect:**
- Creates pace
- Emphasizes each point
- Feels youthful and energetic
- More engaging than complex sentences

**Another example:**
```
"Grandpa nodded.
I smiled too.
'That's good. You should run a lot while you're young.'"
```

### Characteristic 7: Repetition for Emphasis (반복를 통한 강조)

**Pattern:** Repeating words or phrases to stress importance.

**Examples:**

1. "*Running was this good?* I really had no idea."
   - "Running" - repeated to emphasize focus
   - Korean: "뛰기가"를 두 번 강조

2. "At first, I walked because I missed the bus. But I kept doing it because I liked it.
   Running is the same. At first, I ran because I overslept. But I'm running again because I like it."
   - Structure parallelism creates emphasis
   - Pattern: "At first... because... But I... because..."

3. "Being forced and wanting to are completely different."
   - "forced" and "wanting" - opposing concepts emphasized through balance

### Practice Dialogue 7: Casual Teen Speech


### Common Teen Speech Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correction | Style Note |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Over-explaining every feeling | Let fragments breathe | Trust the reader |
| Too many sound effects (onomatopoeia) | Use sparingly for impact | Can feel forced if overused |
| All simple sentences, no variety | Mix short and complex | Reads flat if all the same |
| Trying too hard to be casual | Be naturally conversational | Don't force slang |

## PART 8: SENTENCE STARTERS (문장 시작 표현)

### Category 1: Narrative Transitions (이야기 진행)

| Starter | Korean | When to Use | Example |
|---------|--------|-------------|---------|
| Today... | 오늘... | Setting scene/context | Today I ran on purpose. |
| I left the house at... | ...시에 집을 나왔다 | Establishing timeline | I left the house at 6:40. |
| I ran about... | 약 ...를 뛰었다 | Describing action/distance | I ran about 2 km to school. |
| I sat on a bench and... | 벤치에 앉아서... | Describing aftermath actions | I sat on a bench and drank some water. |

**How to use:** These ground your narrative in specific time and action.

---

### Category 2: Causation/Explanation (원인/설명)

| Starter | Korean | Effect | Example |
|---------|--------|--------|---------|
| Because... | ~때문에 / ~라서 | Shows motivation | I wanted to run because I wanted to feel that runner's high. |
| That's because... | 그건 ...때문이다 | Explains preceding statement | "The wind was cool. That's because it was early morning." |
| It was... | 그것은 ...였다 | Describes quality/state | It was hard, but it felt good. |

**Structure pattern:**
```
[Action/Observation]
That's because [Explanation]
```

**Dialogue integration:**
```
A: Why did you run today?
B: Because I wanted to feel that runner's high again.
A: How did it go?
B: Great! It was hard but felt good. That's because of the endorphins.
```

---

### Category 3: Contrast/Unexpected Turns (대조/반전)

| Starter | Korean | Tone | Example |
|---------|--------|------|---------|
| But today... | 그런데 오늘... | Signals change | But today, I planned to run. |
| But I wanted to... | 하지만 난 ...하고 싶었어 | Contradiction | But I wanted to run. |
| But now it's different. | 하지만 이제는 달라 | Major shift | But now it's different. I run because I want to. |
| But it felt... | 하지만 기분이... | Surprising feeling | But it felt good. |

**Impact:** Creates drama and shows character growth.

---

### Category 4: Reflection/Realization (성찰/깨달음)

| Starter | Korean | Function | Example |
|---------|--------|----------|---------|
| *[Rhetorical question]* I had no idea. | 나는 몰랐다 | Shows surprise | *Running was this good?* I really had no idea. |
| I think... | 난 생각한다 | Personal conviction | I think he's right. |
| I realized... | 나는 깨달았다 | Major insight | I realized that choice changes everything. |
| What I'd read... was true. | ~가 참이었다 | Confirms knowledge | What I'd read online was true. |

**Effect:** Creates introspection, invites reader reflection.

### Category 5: Dialogue Markers (대화 표시)

| Starter | Korean | Tone | Example |
|---------|--------|------|---------|

| [Person] told me. | ~가 나에게 말했다 | Receiving wisdom | Grandpa told me. |

### Category 6: Conclusion/Summary (결론/요약)

| Starter | Korean | Context | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|---------|
| Making my own choice... | 내 선택을... | Final thought | Making my own choice changes everything. |
| I think he's right. | 나는 그가 맞다고 생각한다 | Agreement | I think he's right. |
| Next [time]... | 다음 [시간] | Forward-looking | Next Monday, I'll run again. |

### Practice: Building a Paragraph Using Multiple Starters

**Example using various starters:**

```
Today I ran on purpose. [Narrative]
I didn't oversleep, which surprised me. [Explanation]
But I wanted to run. [Contrast]
That's because I wanted to feel that runner's high again. [Causation]
I left the house at 6:40 and started jogging once I passed the village. [Timeline]
Running felt different than walking. [Reflection]
When I walk, the scenery passes slowly. But when I run, things fly by. [Contrast]
It was hard—I was gasping for breath after 1 km. [Physical description]
But it felt good. [Unexpected turn]
I think I understand what everyone means by runner's high. [Realization]
```

### Common Starter Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Starting too many sentences with "I" | Use varied starters | Maintains reader interest |
| Starting multiple sentences with "The" | Mix subjects and perspectives | Sounds more natural |
| Using starters that don't connect logically | Ensure "but," "because," "that's why" make sense | Confuses reader otherwise |
| Overusing rhetorical questions | Use sparingly | Loses power if overused |

   - What was the transition like?
   - Physical sensations or emotional moments?
   - Who influenced you (like Grandpa in the text)?

3. **Realization (3-4 sentences):**
   - What did you learn?
   - How is this different from before?

4. **Conclusion (2-3 sentences):**
   - What's your new perspective?
   - How will you move forward?

**Model opening:**
"Like Doyun, I discovered that [activity/experience] was much better than I expected. I used to think [old belief], but now I understand [new perspective]. This realization came when..."
