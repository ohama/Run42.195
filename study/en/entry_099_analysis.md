### Common Mistakes (흔한 실수)

1. **"in full bloom" vs "full bloom"**
   - Correct: "The flowers are **in full bloom**"
   - Wrong: "The flowers are **full bloom**"
   - Note: Must use "in" as a preposition

2. **"Ran into" - past tense specific**
   - Correct: "I **ran into** my teacher"
   - Wrong: "I **run into** my teacher" (if talking about past)
   - Note: This is past tense irregular

3. **"Two weeks left" vs "Two weeks from now"**
   - "Two weeks left" = We're counting time already passing
   - "Two weeks from now" = Looking forward to future
   - Context matters!

4. **"Catch" in this context**
   - Correct: "Can you **catch me** at the 20km mark?" (intercept, meet)
   - Wrong: "Can you **catch me**?" (implies running away)
   - Note: Needs location context

## PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

### Phrasal Verbs in Entry 099

| Phrasal Verb | Korean | Literal Meaning | Actual Usage | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **come to** | (행동으로) 옮기다, 해내다 | To arrive at | To go to a location to support | "I'm going to **come** [to the race]" (implied) |

| **come through** | 성공하다, 도와주다 | To penetrate | To succeed or deliver | "I'll **come through** with a good race" (implied meaning) |
| **wait (for)** | 기다리다 | Stay in place anticipating | Same | "I'll **wait** there" |
| **come back** | 돌아오다, 돌아가다 | Return to previous location | Return home | "I **came back** to my room" |

### Similar Verb Combinations - Comparative Analysis

**"Run into" vs Other Encounter Verbs:**

```
RUN INTO:    Unexpected, casual, unplanned
             "I ran into my teacher"

BUMP INTO:   More casual/informal, stronger collision sense
             "I bumped into my friend at the store"

MEET UP:     Planned, intentional, deliberate
             "We met up for coffee"

ENCOUNTER:   Formal, can be positive or negative
             "I encountered some difficulties"

COME ACROSS: Discovering something, less personal
             "I came across an old photo"
```

### "CATCH" Verb Usage in Different Contexts

```
CATCH (Intercept/Meet):
"Can you catch me at the 20km mark?"
Meaning: Be at a location to meet/see me

CATCH (Capture - sports):
"The goalkeeper caught the ball"
Meaning: Got control of flying object

CATCH (Understand):
"I didn't catch what you said"
Meaning: Didn't understand/didn't hear clearly

CATCH (Disease):
"I caught a cold"
Meaning: Contracted, became infected

CONTEXT MATTERS for different meanings!
```

## PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

### Pattern 1: ANNOUNCING SUPPORT + CONFIRMING LOGISTICS

**Pattern Structure:**
```
A: [Event happening soon]
B: [Commitment to support]
A: [Practical question about logistics]
B: [Specific agreement with location/time]
```

**From Text Example:**
```
A: "I'm going to come cheer you on at the race."
B: "Really?" [Confirms commitment]
A: "Yeah. Will I be able to see you somewhere in the middle?"
B: "You should be able to catch me at the 20km mark."
```

**Practice Variations:**

```
Example 1:
A: "I'm going to come see your concert."
B: "Seriously? That would be amazing."
A: "Yeah. Will you have time before you go on stage?"
B: "I'll try to find you about 30 minutes before."

Example 2:
A: "I'm coming to watch your debate competition."
B: "Wait, really? I can't believe it."
A: "Of course. But I might be late. When do you go on?"
B: "3 p.m. But doors open at 2 p.m. Come then."

B: "For real? That would mean so much."

```

### Pattern 2: EXPRESSING MIXED EMOTIONS (Fear + Excitement)

**Pattern Structure:**
```
A: [Direct emotion 1] + [Contrast word] + [Emotion 2]
Emotional Spectrum: Negative ↔ Positive
```

**From Text Example:**
```

```

**Extended Examples:**

```

"Terrified. But optimistic."

**Full Dialogue Implementation:**

```

-- The teacher VALIDATES the mixed emotion state --
-- Shows that fear + excitement = healthy preparation --

### Common Patterns to Remember

1. **The "But" Pattern (대조 패턴)**
   - Emotion 1 + But + Emotion 2
   - "Scary. But exciting."
   - "Nervous. But ready."
   - Shows emotional maturity

2. **The "So" Intensifier (강조 표현)**
   - "So beautiful" (not just beautiful)
   - "So many people" (emphasizes quantity)
   - Common in teen/casual speech

3. **The Support Network (지지 시스템)**
   - Friend: practical, specific location
   - Family: emotional, multiple voices
   - Teacher: validation, confidence building

4. **The Countdown (카운트다운)**
   - D-13: creates urgency
   - "Two weeks left": repetition for emphasis
   - Both forward and backward references

5. **The Gratitude Reflection (감사 성찰)**
   - Moves from activity log → emotional synthesis
   - Lists support → expresses gratitude → commits to action
   - Personal philosophy emerges

### Cultural Notes (문화적 배경)

**D-13 Notation:**
- Military-style countdown (한국 군대 문화)
- "D-day" = specific important date
- Creates sense of urgency and structure
- Common in Korean planning culture

**Family Support Emphasis:**
- Strong collectivist values (가족주의)
- Multiple family members showing specific support
- Not individualistic "I did it alone" narrative
- Gratitude explicitly stated (감사 표현)

**Teacher-Student Relationship:**
- Respectful but warm
- Teacher validates emotions rather than dismiss them
- Educational support extends beyond academics
- Common in Korean school culture

**Natural Observation:**
- Rapeseed flowers = seasonal marker (유채꽃은 봄의 상징)
- Runner notices nature while training
- Moment of beauty amid anxiety
- Connects personal journey to natural cycles
