# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Training Expressions

### "Strapped on my running watch and headed out."

**Original Korean:** "러닝 워치를 찼고 나갔다"

**Analysis:**
- "Strap on" = 꽉 채우다/꼭 끼우다 (특히 시간이 촉박할 때)
- "Headed out" = 나가다/출발하다 (행선지는 명확하지만 명시 안 함)

**Variations by Activity:**

| Activity | Expression |
|----------|------------|
| 시계 | "Strapped on my watch" |
| 신발 | "Strapped on my shoes" |
| 헬멧 | "Strapped on my helmet" |
| 배낭 | "Strapped on my backpack" |
| 장비 | "Strapped on my gear" |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "Put on my running watch" (더 일반적)
- "Grabbed my watch and headed out" (더 캐주얼)
- "Set out with my watch" (더 격식)

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: You're up early. Where are you going?
B: For a run. I strapped on my watch and headed out.
A: Before breakfast?
B: Yeah, want to come?
A: Maybe next time.
```

### "Take it slow at the start."

**Original Korean:** "처음엔 천천히 가자"

**Grammar Point:** Imperative + 목적 부사구

**Structure:** Take it + 속도/방식 + 상황

**Examples with "Take it":**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "Take it slow." | 천천히 가다 | 운동, 관계, 일 |
| "Take it easy." | 쉽게 가다/힘 빼다 | 휴식, 조언 |
| "Take it fast." | 빨리 가다 | 레이스, 긴급 상황 |
| "Take it steady." | 꾸준하게 | 마라톤 같은 장거리 |

**Running Context:**
```
Coach: For the first 3km, take it slow. Save your energy.
Runner: Got it. Warm up first, kick at the end.
Coach: Exactly. You've got this.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "Take slowly" (불가능)
✅ "Take it slow"
✅ "Go slowly"

### "Watched the watch while running helps me control my speed."

**Original Korean:** "시계를 보면서 달리는 게 속도 조절에 도움이 된다"

**Key Phrase:** "Watching the watch helps me..."

**Grammar Pattern:** Gerund subject (동명사 주어) + helps me + infinitive (원형)

**Structure:** [Gerund] + helps + [person] + [infinitive]

**Examples:**

| Activity | Benefit | Full Sentence |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| 음악 듣기 | 집중하다 | "Listening to music helps me focus." |
| 시계 보기 | 속도 조절 | "Watching the watch helps me control my speed." |
| 깊게 숨쉬기 | 진정하다 | "Taking deep breaths helps me calm down." |
| 계획 세우기 | 준비하다 | "Making a plan helps me prepare." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "It helps me control my speed when I watch the watch."
- "Watching the watch is helpful for controlling my speed."
- "The watch helps me maintain my pace."

### "Gotta remember this feeling."

**Original Korean:** "이 느낌을 기억해야겠다"

**Casual Contraction:** "Gotta" = Got to (해야 해)

**Using "Gotta":**

| Meaning | Casual | Standard | Formal |
|---------|--------|----------|--------|
| 해야 해 | "Gotta go." | "I have to go." | "I must depart." |
| 찾아야 해 | "Gotta find it." | "I need to find it." | "I must locate it." |
| 기억해야 해 | "Gotta remember." | "I need to remember." | "I must retain this." |
| 해봐야 해 | "Gotta try." | "I should try." | "One must attempt." |

**"Remember This Feeling" Pattern:**
- "Gotta remember this moment."
- "Remember this feeling when it gets hard."
- "I need to lock this feeling in my memory."

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
Runner: I just broke 5-minute pace!
Coach: How does it feel?
Runner: Smooth. Natural. Gotta remember this feeling.
Coach: When the race gets tough, come back to this.
Runner: Right. Got it.
```

**Original Korean:** "다리가 비명을 지르고 있다. 폐가 터질 것 같다"

**Dramatic Fatigue Language - Figurative:**

These are hyperbolic expressions common in sports narrative:

| Expression | Literal | Figurative |
|------------|---------|-----------|

**Using These Expressions Naturally:**

- Push through discomfort
- Access reserve energy

- "Time to dig deep."

- "That's where you dig deep."
