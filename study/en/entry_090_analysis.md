## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **"Rest day" vs "Resting day"**
   - CORRECT: "I'm taking a rest day" (정해진 휴식일)
   - INCORRECT: "I'm taking a resting day" (부자연스러움)

2. **"No carsickness" with weak negation**
   - CORRECT: "No carsickness. Not even a hint of it." (강한 부정, 완전 없음)
   - INCORRECT: "Not much carsickness" (약한 부정, 약간 있다는 뉘앙스)

3. **"Stuck with me" meaning**
   - CORRECT: "That comment stuck with me" = 기억에 남았다 (긍정적/중요함)
   - INCORRECT: "Stuck with me" ≠ 나한테 들러붙었다 (물리적 의미)

4. **Using "better" vs "best"**
   - CORRECT: "A little better" (조금 개선)
   - INCORRECT: "A little best" (문법 오류)

## Phrasal Verb Usage Patterns

### "Stick with (me)" - Emotional Impact
- **Pattern:** Something + stick with + person
- **Meaning:** Make a strong impression
- **Formality:** Casual
- **Example from entry:** "Coach said something that stuck with me"
- **Similar expressions:**
  - "That stayed with me" (같은 의미)
  - "That resonated with me" (더 깊은 의미)
  - "I'll never forget that" (명시적으로)

### "Look out" - Window Observation
- **Pattern:** look out (of/from) + location
- **Meaning:** Peer from a window or opening
- **Formality:** Casual

  - "I stared out the window" (집중)
  - "I peered out the window" (호기심)

### "Fly past" - Quick Passage
- **Pattern:** (Things/Everything) + fly past + rapidly
- **Meaning:** Pass very quickly
- **Formality:** Casual (somewhat poetic)

  - "Everything whizzed by" (의성어적)

- **Pattern:** Keep (it/things) + light/easy

## Common Phrasal Verb Mistakes

### Mistake 1: "Stick to" vs "Stick with"
- **CORRECT:** "That comment stuck with me" (기억/영향)
- **INCORRECT:** "That comment stuck to me" (물리적)
- **Note:** "Stick to" = 고수하다 (decisions, plans); "Stick with" = 기억나다, 지속하다 (memories, people)

### Mistake 2: "Look out" vs "Look at"
- **CORRECT:** "I looked out the bus window" (창밖 바라보기)
- **INCORRECT:** "I looked the bus window" (syntax 오류)
- **INCORRECT:** "I looked at the bus window" (창 자체를 봄, 풍경이 아님)

### Mistake 3: "Keep it light" Positioning
- **CORRECT:** "Keep it light" or "Keep things light" (명령)
- **INCORRECT:** "You keep it light" (의심스러운 형식)
- **Use in coaching:** "Keep the pace light" / "Keep your effort light"

### Pattern 3: Contrast Expressions

**Structure:**
```
Observation A: [Initial statement]
Contrast word: "but" / "yet" / "though"
Observation B: [Different perspective]
```

**Example from Entry:**
"Same landscape I see while running, but so different."

**Analysis:**
- First part: acknowledges similarity
- Conjunction: signals contrast
- Second part: emphasizes difference
- Creates TENSION between expectation and reality

**Similar Expressions:**
```
It's the same place, yet it feels completely different.
Same route, but the experience is nothing alike.
The landscape looks familiar, but from the bus it's all wrong.
```

**Why This Matters:**
- Shows MATURE perspective (understanding multiple viewpoints)
- Common in teenage writing (confusion, realization)
- DEVELOPS the running theme (running = more meaningful than just transportation)
