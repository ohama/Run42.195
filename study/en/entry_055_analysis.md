# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Dinner Conversation Openers

### "Doyun, you're running in a marathon?"

**Original Korean:** "도윤아, 마라톤 하니?"

**Analysis:**
- 놀라움과 함께 관심을 나타내는 질문
- 아버지가 처음 아들의 활동에 대해 물어보는 순간

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "You're running a marathon?" | "You're running in a marathon?" | "I understand you're training for a marathon?" |
| "So you run?" | "You run races?" | "You participate in marathon events?" |
| "Running now?" | "When did you start running?" | "Might I inquire about your running pursuits?" |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Father: Doyun, you're running in a marathon?
Son: Yes, a 10 km race in October.
Father: That's impressive. When did you start?
Son: Back in March. It's been almost six months now.
```

---

### "10 km? How long does that take?"

**Original Korean:** "10킬로미터? 얼마나 걸려?"

**Grammar Point:** Clarifying question + time inquiry

**Structure:** [Distance/Quantity] + "How long does that take?"

**Examples:**
| Distance | Question | Answer |
|----------|----------|--------|
| "10 km" | "How long does that take?" | "About 55 minutes." |
| "Marathon" | "How long does that take?" | "About 4 hours." |
| "5 km" | "How long does that take?" | "Around 25 minutes." |
| "20 km" | "How long does that take?" | "About 2 hours." |

**Alternative Phrasing:**
- "How much time does that take?"
- "How long would that take you?"
- "What's your time for 10 km?"
- "How fast are you?"

---

### "About 55 minutes right now. My goal is to get under 50 minutes."

**Original Korean:** "지금은 55분 정도. 목표는 50분 안에 하는 거야."

**Grammar Point:** Current status + goal statement

**Pattern:** "Right now [current reality]. My goal is [aspirational target]."

**Examples:**
| Current | Goal | Full Sentence |
|---------|------|---------------|
| 55 minutes | under 50 | "About 55 minutes right now. My goal is to get under 50." |
| $50,000 salary | $70,000 | "I make $50,000 right now. My goal is $70,000." |
| B+ student | A student | "I'm a B+ right now. My goal is to be an A student." |
| 5 km runs | 10 km runs | "I run 5 km right now. My goal is 10 km." |

**Similar Structures:**
- "I'm currently at [X], but I'm aiming for [Y]."
- "Right now I can [X], but I want to [Y]."
- "My personal best is [X], and I'm working toward [Y]."

## 1.2 Acknowledging Time Passage

### "Six months? It's been that long?"

**Original Korean:** "6개월? 그동안?"

**Pattern:** Time expression + surprised follow-up

**Nuance:** 시간이 빨리 지나간 것에 대한 놀라움

**Similar Surprised Questions:**
| Expression | Context | Emotional Tone |
|------------|---------|----------------|
| "Six months? Already?" | 빠른 시간 경과 | 놀라움 |
| "That long?" | 긴 시간 확인 | 의심 |
| "Has it really been that long?" | 정중한 놀라움 | 부드러움 |
| "Time flies!" | 시간이 빨리 감 | 긍정적 |
| "Where does the time go?" | 시간 어디로 | 감상적 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
Dad: "It's been almost six months?"
Mom: "Yes, he started in March."
Dad: "March? That long already?"
Mom: "Time flies when you're busy with work."
```

---

### "I've been busy... with work. There's been a lot of business trips lately."

**Original Korean:** "일이 많아서... 요즘 출장도 많고."

**Grammar Point:** Present Perfect + explanation with hesitation

**Structure:** I've been [verb-ing] + explanation with "because"/"due to"

**Hesitation Markers:**
- "I've been..." (trailing off = 말을 아리송하게 끝냄)
- Three dots (...) = 설명이 어색함을 표현

**Examples:**
| Excuse | Full Sentence |
|--------|---------------|
| busy with work | "I've been busy... with work." |
| dealing with family stuff | "I've been... dealing with family stuff lately." |
| overwhelmed with projects | "I've been overwhelmed... with new projects." |
| occupied with meetings | "I've been occupied... with back-to-back meetings." |

**Why the Hesitation Matters:**
- 자신의 부재를 인정하려는 어색함
- 아들의 활동을 못 본 것에 대한 약간의 죄책감
- 합리화하려는 시도

### "Doesn't it hurt?"

**Original Korean:** "고생하지 않니?"

**Nuance:** 신체적 힘듦에 대한 관심 있는 질문

**Pain/Physical Struggle Questions:**
| Question | Context | Concern |
|----------|---------|---------|
| "Doesn't it hurt?" | 신체적 고통 | 걱정 |
| "Isn't it exhausting?" | 피로도 | 관심 |
| "Don't your legs ache?" | 근육통 | 구체적 |
| "That must be tough." | 동감 | 위로 |
| "How do you manage?" | 극복 방법 | 존경 |

**Response Pattern:**
```
Parent: Doesn't it hurt?
Child: It was hard at first, but it's okay now.
Parent: That's good to hear.
```

---

### "It was hard at first, but it's okay now."

**Original Korean:** "처음에는 힘들었는데, 이제는 괜찮아."

**Grammar Point:** Past vs. Present contrast with "but"

**Structure:** It was [difficult] at first, but it's [easier] now.

**Emphasis on Progress:**
| Past State | But | Present State | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-----|---------------|---------------|
| hard | but | okay now | "It was hard at first, but it's okay now." |
| scary | but | not so bad | "It was scary at first, but it's not so bad now." |
| confusing | but | clearer | "It was confusing at first, but it's clearer now." |
| exhausting | but | manageable | "It was exhausting, but it's manageable now." |

**Variations:**
- "At first it was difficult, but now I'm used to it."
- "In the beginning it was tough, but I've adapted."
- "Initially challenging, but I've gotten stronger."

# PART 2: FAMILY RELATIONSHIPS & EMOTIONS (가족관계 표현)

## 2.1 Realizations About Family

### "Dad is always busy with work."

**Original Korean:** "아버지는 늘 일이 많다."

**Grammar Point:** Habitual state with "always"

**Pattern:** Subject + is/are + always + [characteristic]

**Examples:**
| Subject | Characteristic | Full Sentence |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Dad | busy with work | "Dad is always busy with work." |
| Mom | worried | "Mom is always worried." |
| Brother | late | "Brother is always late." |
| Sister | energetic | "Sister is always energetic." |
| Teacher | strict | "The teacher is always strict." |

**Tone Variations:**
- Neutral: "Dad is always busy." (사실 진술)
- Slightly resentful: "Dad is ALWAYS busy." (항상 바쁨에 대한 불만)
- Understanding: "Dad is always busy, I understand." (이해함)
- Resigned: "Dad is always busy, as usual." (늘 그렇지)

---

### "I guess that's why he didn't know much about my running."

**Original Korean:** "그래서 내 달리기를 잘 모르는구나."

**Grammar Point:** Cause-and-effect reasoning with "that's why"

**Structure:** I guess that's why + [consequence]

**Cause & Effect Examples:**
| Cause | "That's why" | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| He's always working | that's why | he didn't know |
| She's been ill | that's why | she wasn't there |
| They're busy | that's why | they forgot |
| It's complicated | that's why | nobody talks about it |

**Alternative Phrasings:**
- "I suppose that explains why..."
- "I guess that's the reason..."
- "That would explain why..."
- "So that's why..."

**Tone & Function:**
- Understanding/realization tone (깨달음의 톤)
- Justifying/excusing tone (변명/정당화)
- Forgiving tone (용서하는 톤)

---

### "My chest felt tight."

**Original Korean:** "가슴이 철렁했다."

**Physical Manifestation of Emotion:**
- 목이 메임 (throat tightening)
- 눈물이 나옴 (tears forming)
- 가슴이 철렁함 (sudden tightness/flutter)

**Chest Tightness Contexts:**
| Emotion | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| 감동/감정 | "My chest felt tight." |
| 두려움 | "My chest felt tight with fear." |
| 기쁨 | "My chest swelled with joy." |
| 불안 | "My chest tightened with anxiety." |
| 슬픔 | "My heart ached." |

**Similar Physical Reactions:**
- "A lump formed in my throat." (목이 메였다)
- "Tears welled up in my eyes." (눈물이 나왔다)
- "My heart swelled." (심장이 부풀어올랐다)
- "Goosebumps covered my arms." (소름이 돋았다)

**Dialogue Context:**
```
Father: "Can I come cheer you on at the race?"
Son: "Huh?" [My chest felt tight.]
Son: "Yes, please come."
```

### "I thought he didn't care."

**Original Korean:** "아버지는 신경도 안 썼을 거라고 생각했어."

**Grammar Point:** Past tense assumption

**Structure:** I thought + subject + didn't + verb

**Misunderstandings & Realization:**
| Assumption | Reality | Sentence |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| 신경 안 씀 | 몰랐을 뿐 | "I thought he didn't care. It's not that he didn't care. He just didn't know." |
| 관심 없음 | 바빴을 뿐 | "I assumed he wasn't interested. He was just busy." |
| 싫어함 | 모를 뿐 | "I thought he hated it. He just didn't understand." |

**Common Misunderstandings in Families:**
```
"I thought you didn't love me."
"I assumed you didn't care."
"I believed you forgot about me."
"I figured you weren't interested."
```

---

### "But today he asked."

**Original Korean:** "하지만 오늘 물었다."

**Turning Point in Relationship:**

**"But" for Contrast/Hope:**
- 전의 가정을 깨뜨리는 전환
- 긍정적인 변화 암시
- 희망의 신호

**Before/After Structure:**
```
[Assumption]: "I thought he didn't care."
[Change]: "But today he asked."
[Realization]: "Now he knows."
```

---

- 현재의 변화된 상황을 인정

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "It's not that [negative]. He just [positive explanation]."

**Structure:** It's not that + [negative assumption] + period. + [subject] just + [explanation]

**From Text:**
```
"I thought he didn't care.
But today I realized something.
It's not that he didn't care.
He just didn't know."
```

**Reframing Negative Assumptions:**
| Assumption | Explanation | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| didn't care | didn't know | "It's not that he didn't care. He just didn't know." |
| wasn't interested | was too busy | "It's not that he wasn't interested. He was just too busy." |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Parent Asking About Child's Activities

```
Parent: I heard you've been running?
Child: Yes, for about six months now.
Parent: Six months? That long? Why did you start?
Child: I had motion sickness on the bus, so I walked instead. Then I tried running and liked it.
Parent: That's interesting. How far do you run?
Child: About 10 km on long run days. My goal is to compete in a race.
Parent: A race? That's impressive! Can I come watch?
Child: Really? You'd come?
Parent: Of course. I want to support you.
Child: That means a lot to me.
```

### Scenario 2: Discussing Family Support

```
Child: My mom has been supporting me from the beginning.
Friend: That's nice. What about your dad?
Child: I thought he didn't care because he's always busy with work.
Friend: Has that changed?
Child: Yes, actually. The other day he asked about my running and said he'd come to my race.
Friend: That must have meant a lot.
Child: It did. I realized he just didn't know before. He cares, he's just busy.
Friend: That's wonderful. You have his support now.
```

### Scenario 3: Family Dinner Conversation

```
Dad: So you're running a 10 km race?
Son: Yes, it's in October.
Dad: How long will it take you?
Son: About 55 minutes right now, but my goal is under 50.
Sister: Oppa is so fast these days!
Dad: You've really changed. I'm proud of you.
Son: Thanks, Dad. It means a lot that you're interested.
Dad: Of course I'm interested. Can I come watch the race?
Son: Yes, please. October 5th.
Dad: I'll mark it down. I won't miss it.
```
