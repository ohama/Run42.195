# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Seasonal Greetings & Observations

### "Autumn has come."

**Original Korean:** "가을이 왔다"

**Analysis:**
- 계절의 도래를 선언하는 표현
- Simple yet powerful statement about seasonal change
- Often used in reflective, diary-like contexts

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Literary |
|--------|----------|----------|
| "Autumn's here." | "Autumn has come." | "Autumn has arrived." |
| "Fall's starting." | "Fall has begun." | "The season has turned." |
| "It's getting cold." | "The weather's changing." | "The transition is underway." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Can you feel it?
B: Feel what?
A: Autumn has come. The air's different.
B: You're right. It's cooler than before.
A: Perfect weather for running.
```

---

### "The air is different."

**Original Korean:** "공기가 달라졌다"

**Nuance:** 감각적 변화에 대한 관찰

**Sensory Awareness Expressions:**

| Expression | Sense | Example |
|------------|-------|---------|
| "The air is different." | 냄새/온도/습도 | 가을 공기의 변화 |
| "The sky looks different." | 시각 | 가을 하늘의 맑음 |
| "The light is different." | 빛 | 계절별 광선 각도 |
| "The ground feels different." | 촉각 | 습도 차이 |
| "The wind feels different." | 촉각 | 공기의 성질 변화 |

**Deeper Analysis:**
```
"The air is different. Not that humid, heavy summer air anymore."
→ 구체적으로 무엇이 다른지 설명해서 더 효과적

Enhanced version:
"The air felt different—lighter, less humid, more refreshing."
```

---

### "Light, cool, refreshing."

**Original Korean:** "가볍고, 시원하고, 상큼하다"

**Grammar Point:** Fragment listing for sensory impact

**Adjective Trilogy:**
- **Light** (가볍다): 무겁지 않은 느낌
- **Cool** (시원하다): 따뜻하지도 춥지도 않은
- **Refreshing** (상큼하다/활기차다): 신선한, 활기 있는

**Similar Sensory Trios:**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "Warm, soft, comfortable." | 아늑한 | 겨울 방 설명 |
| "Bright, colorful, vibrant." | 활발한 | 봄 풍경 |
| "Hot, humid, oppressive." | 답답한 | 여름 날씨 |
| "Cold, crisp, invigorating." | 정신 차리는 | 가을/겨울 아침 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: How do you describe fall air?
B: Light, cool, refreshing—the total opposite of summer.
A: What makes it different?
B: Summer's humid and heavy. Fall air is clean and crisp.
```

## 1.2 Training & Running Expressions

### "According to my training plan..."

**Original Korean:** "훈련 계획에 따르면..."

**Grammar Point:** Reporting facts with authority

**"According to" Structure:**
```
According to [source/plan], [information]
```

**Examples:**

| Source | Example |
|--------|---------|
| my training plan | "According to my training plan, I run 10km today." |
| the coach | "According to the coach, rest is important." |
| the schedule | "According to the schedule, we're done at 6pm." |
| my doctor | "According to my doctor, I need more sleep." |
| the weather forecast | "According to the forecast, it'll rain tomorrow." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "My training plan says..." (더 casual)
- "The plan calls for..." (격식)
- "I'm supposed to..." (의무감)

**Original Korean:** "편안한 속도로 10km를 뛴다"

| Term | Definition | Korean |
|------|-----------|--------|

| **pace** | 속도/리듬 | 분당 몇 km |

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running Sensations

### "The moment my feet hit the ground, I knew."

**Original Korean:** "발이 땅에 닿는 순간 알았다."

**Grammar Point:** "The moment [event], [result]" = "[일이] 순간적으로 일어나다"

**Structure:** The moment + clause + immediate result

**Examples:**

| Event | Immediate Result | Full Sentence |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| I stepped outside | I felt the cold | "The moment I stepped outside, I felt the cold." |
| I started running | I knew it'd be good | "The moment I started, I knew it'd be good." |
| I saw the crowd | I froze | "The moment I saw the crowd, I froze." |
| I heard the news | My heart sank | "The moment I heard, my heart sank." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "As soon as my feet hit the ground, I knew."
- "Right when my feet touched the ground, I knew."
- "The second my feet hit the ground, I knew."

---

### "My sweat evaporates."

**Original Korean:** "땀이 증발한다."

**Vocabulary:**
- **Sweat** (땀): 운동할 때 나오는 물질
- **Evaporate** (증발하다): 액체가 기체로 변함

**Physical Comfort Progression:**

| Weather | Sweat Behavior | Feeling |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| Hot, humid summer | "Sweat pours down like rain." | 답답하고 불편 |
| Cool fall | "My sweat evaporates." | 시원하고 쾌적 |
| Windy day | "The breeze dries my sweat." | 쾌적 |
| Cold winter | "I barely sweat." | 따뜻함 |

**Environmental Comfort Expressions:**

```
Summer humidity: "Moving humid air around" = 답답한 느낌
Fall dryness: "My sweat evaporates" = 상큼함
```

**Body Temperature Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "feel overheated" | 몸이 너무 더우면 |
| "cool down" | 식히다/진정하다 |
| "dry off" | 말리다 |
| "shiver" | 떨다 |
| "feel chilled" | 추위를 느끼다 |

## 2.2 Weather Sensations

### "A breeze came. So cool."

**Original Korean:** "바람이 났다. 너무 시원하다."

**"Breeze" Definition:**
- 약한 바람, 산들바람
- 대비: "wind" (강한 바람), "gust" (돌풍)

**Wind Expressions Spectrum:**

| Expression | Strength | Korean |
|------------|----------|--------|
| "a gentle breeze" | 약함 | 산들바람 |
| "a cool breeze" | 약함 (시원함) | 시원한 바람 |
| "a strong wind" | 중간 | 센 바람 |
| "a gusty wind" | 불규칙함 | 돌풍 |
| "the wind howled" | 매우 강함 | 윙윙 부는 바람 |

**Seasonal Wind Descriptions:**

```
Summer: "The wind blew, but it was hot. Just moving humid air around."
→ 열풍, 불쾌감

Fall: "When the wind blows, it's actually cool. My sweat evaporates."
→ 상큼함, 쾌감
```

**Temperature + Wind Combinations:**

| Combination | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Cool breeze" | 상큼하고 시원운 |
| "Cold wind" | 춥고 날카로운 |
| "Warm breeze" | 따뜻하고 부드러운 |
| "Hot wind" | 불쾌하고 따뜻한 |

---

### "In summer, even when the wind blew, it was hot."

**Original Korean:** "여름에는 바람이 불어도 덥다."

**Comparative Seasonal Analysis:**

| Season | Wind Effect | Overall Feel |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Summer | "even when the wind blew, it was hot" | 답답함 |
| Fall | "When the wind blows, it's actually cool" | 상큼함 |
| Winter | Wind makes it colder | 쌀쌀함 |

**"Even when" Structure:**
```
"Even when [something expected], [unexpected result]"
→ 기대와 달리, 예상 밖의 일

Examples:
"Even when I studied hard, I failed." (공부했는데도 떨어짐)
"Even when it rained, I went running." (비 와도 뛰었어)
"Even when I tried, I couldn't succeed." (노력했는데도 안 됨)
```

## 2.3 Environmental Sensations

### "The mandarins are still green. When autumn gets deeper, they'll turn orange."

**Original Korean:** "귤이 아직 초록색이다. 가을이 깊어지면 주황색이 될 거다."

**Seasonal Color Progression:**

| Stage | Color | What Happens |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| Summer | green | 열매 성장 중 |
| Early fall | green-ish | 변화 시작 |
| Mid fall | orange | 익음 |
| Late fall | orange-red | 완전히 익음 |

**Plant/Food Ripeness Vocabulary:**

| State | Expression | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| 미성숙 | "still green" | "The mandarins are still green." |
| 성숙 중 | "ripening" | "The rice is ripening." |
| 익음 | "ripe" | "The fruit is ripe." |
| 너무 익음 | "overripe" | "The banana is overripe." |

**"When autumn gets deeper":**
- "깊어지다" = 진행되다, 심해지다
- 계절이 진행되는 과정 표현

**Time Progression Expressions:**

```
"When autumn gets deeper" = 가을이 진행되면서
"As winter approaches" = 겨울이 가까워지면서
"When spring arrives" = 봄이 오면
"As summer ends" = 여름이 끝나가면서
```

## 3.2 Temporal Transitions

| Month | What You See | Korean |
|-------|-------------|--------|

| September | "rice ripening" | 벼가 익어감 |

**Time Reference Expressions:**

| Expression | Type | Example |
|------------|------|---------|

| "now" | 현재 | "Now the rice is ripening" |

**Progression Markers in Narrative:**


## 4.2 Running Completion & Recovery

**Original Korean:** "정리 운동을 하고 세면대에서 씻었다"

**"Cool down":**
- 운동 후 천천히 운동 강도 줄임
- 근육 회복, 심박수 정상화

**Post-run Activities Sequence:**

| Activity | Purpose | Expression |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Cool down | 심박수 정상화 | "cooled down" |
| Stretch | 근육 이완 | "stretched" |
| Wash up | 씻기 | "washed up" |
| Eat | 영양 보충 | "ate" |
| Rest | 회복 | "rested" |

**"Wash up" Meanings:**

| Context | Meaning |

**Uses:** 진전/발전 표현, 계절 변화, 능력 향상

| Past | Present | Implication |
