# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Expressions of Observation and Recognition

### "Have you been running a lot lately?"

**Original Korean:** "요즘 많이 달렸어?"

**Analysis:**
- 관찰에 기반한 질문
- "lately"는 "요즘, 최근에" 의미
- 누군가의 행동 변화에 대한 직접적인 질문

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Nuance | When to Use |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| "Have you been running a lot lately?" | 관찰 기반, 중립적 | 변화를 발견했을 때 |
| "I've noticed you've been running more." | 직접적 관찰 표현 | 더 명확한 인정 |
| "You seem to be running more these days." | 추측 섞인 | 확실하지 않을 때 |
| "You've really gotten into running." | 취미/열정 강조 | 긍정적 평가 |
| "Your running has improved a lot." | 결과 기반 평가 | 성과 강조 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Teacher: Have you been running a lot lately?
Student: Yes, I run to school a few times a week.
Teacher: How far is that?
Student: About 7 kilometers.
Teacher: That's impressive for a student.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "Have you been running a lot in lately?" (전치사 중복)
✅ "Have you been running a lot lately?"

---

### "Your running has improved a lot."

**Original Korean:** "너의 달리기가 많이 좋아졌어."

**Analysis:**
- 성과 평가의 직접적 표현
- "running"을 추상 명사로 사용 (달리기 능력)
- "has improved" = 현재완료형 (과거부터 현재까지의 변화)

**Improvement Expression Patterns:**

| Expression | Focus | Tone |
|------------|-------|------|
| "Your running has improved a lot." | 능력 향상 | 정중, 평가적 |
| "You've gotten much faster." | 구체적 속도 | 직접적 |
| "You run with better form now." | 기술 개선 | 기술적 |
| "Your stamina is so much better." | 지구력 | 놀람 |
| "I can really see the difference." | 비교 강조 | 감탄적 |

## 1.2 Expressions of Investigation and Questions

### "Where do you live?"

**Original Korean:** "어디에 살아?"

**Analysis:**
- 기본적이고 직접적인 질문
- 교사-학생 대화에서 일반적
- 맥락 설명을 위한 정보 수집

**Variations in Different Contexts:**

| Expression | Formality | Context |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "Where do you live?" | Standard | 일반적 질문 |
| "Where are you from?" | Standard | 출신지 묻기 |
| "What area do you live in?" | More formal | 지역 구체화 |
| "How far from school is your home?" | Specific | 거리 관련 |

---

### "How far is that?"

**Original Korean:** "거리가 얼마나 돼?"

**Analysis:**
- 거리 정보를 요청하는 간단한 질문
- "How far"은 거리 측정을 위한 표준 표현
- 실용적인 정보 수집

**Distance-Asking Questions:**

| Expression | Nuance | Answer Type |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| "How far is that?" | 구체적 거리 | "7 kilometers." |
| "How far away is it?" | 더 강한 거리 강조 | "About 7km away." |
| "What's the distance?" | 중립적 | "7 kilometers." |
| "Is it far from school?" | 상대적 거리 | "Yes, fairly far." |

## 1.3 Expressions of Explanation and Clarification

### "I walk to school... actually, I run."

**Original Korean:** "학교에 걸어간다... 사실 뛴다."

**Analysis:**
- 자기 수정 (self-correction) 표현
- 처음 보수적으로 대답한 후 진실 공개
- 주저함과 그 다음의 정직함을 보여줌

**Self-Correction Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example | Implication |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| "I walk... actually, I run." | 처음 보수, 그 다음 진실 | 겸손에서 정직으로 |
| "It's about 6km. Well, 7 kilometers." | 수정과 정확화 | 정확성 강조 |
| "I go a few times... maybe more." | 처음 보수, 그 다음 보정 | 빈도 재평가 |
| "I started walking, then running." | 시간순 설명 | 진전 과정 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Teacher: Do you walk to school?
Student: I walk... actually, I run. To get more exercise.
Teacher: How often?
Student: About two or three times a week.
Teacher: That's serious training!
```

---

### "About two or three times a week."

**Original Korean:** "일주일에 두세 번."

**Analysis:**
- 빈도 표현
- "about"은 정확하지 않은 수를 나타낼 때 사용
- 실용적인 일상 활동 빈도

**Frequency Expressions:**

| Expression | Frequency | Precision |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| "Two or three times a week." | 주 2-3회 | 정확한 범위 |
| "About two times a week." | 주 약 2회 | 근사값 |
| "Several times a week." | 주 여러 번 | 비구체적 |
| "A few times a month." | 월 몇 번 | 덜 자주 |
| "Almost every day." | 거의 매일 | 매우 자주 |

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Emotional Reactions to Recognition

### "My heart skipped a beat."

**Original Korean:** "가슴이 철렁 내려앉았다."

**Analysis:**
- 예상치 못한 상황에 대한 신체적 반응
- "skip a beat" = 심장이 한 박자 뛰지 않은 것처럼 느껴짐
- 놀람, 긴장, 또는 두려움을 나타냄

**"Skip a Beat" Definition:**
- /skɪp ə biːt/ - 심장이 한 박자 건너뛰다
- 갑작스러운 감정적 충격의 신체 반응
- 긍정적 또는 부정적 상황 모두에서 사용 가능

**Heartbeat-Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "My heart skipped a beat." | ★★★★☆ | 놀람/긴장 |
| "My heart sank." | ★★★★☆ | 실망/두려움 |
| "My heart was racing." | ★★★★★ | 극심한 흥분 |
| "My heart was pounding." | ★★★★☆ | 강한 흥분/두려움 |
| "I felt a flutter in my chest." | ★★★☆☆ | 약간의 긴장 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Friend: Did you get nervous when the teacher called you?
You: Yeah, my heart skipped a beat. I thought I was in trouble.
Friend: But it was good news!
You: I know, but my body didn't know that yet.
```

## 3.1 Describing Locations and Distances

### "I live in the Jocheon area."

**Original Korean:** "조천 지역에 살아."

**Analysis:**
- 제주 지역의 구체적 위치 명시
- 문화적 배경 제공
- 거리 설명을 위한 문맥 설정

**Location Description Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example | Context |
|---------|---------|---------|
| "I live in [area] area." | "I live in the Jocheon area." | 지역 명시 |
| "I'm from [neighborhood]." | "I'm from the northern part." | 더 광범위한 지역 |
| "My house is near [landmark]." | "My house is near the mountains." | 랜드마크 기준 |

| "A fair distance." | 중거리 | 긍정적 평가 |

### "That explains why your stamina has improved so much."

**Original Korean:** "그래서 체력이 그렇게 늘었구나."

**Analysis:**
- 원인과 결과의 연결
- "That explains why"는 이해와 수용의 표현
- 교사의 전문적 판단과 이해

**Cause-Effect Explanation Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| "That explains why..." | "That explains why you improved." | 원인 찾기 |

| "No wonder..." | "No wonder you're exhausted after school." | 당연한 결과 |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## 5.1 Recognition and Compliment Pattern

### Pattern: Direct Observation → Specific Evidence → Encouragement

**Structure:**
```
"I've noticed [observation]."
+ "Specifically, [concrete example]."
+ "You're doing [positive evaluation]."
= Complete recognition
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"I've noticed in PE class—your running has improved a lot. You've gotten faster in the 100 meters, and you're doing well in the endurance runs too."
```

**More Examples:**

| Observation | Evidence | Encouragement |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| "Your English has improved." | "You speak much more fluently now." | "You should be proud." |
| "Your work quality is better." | "Your recent projects are detailed." | "Keep it up." |
| "You're more confident." | "You speak up in class more." | "It's great to see." |

---

## 5.2 Explanation Pattern

### Pattern: Problem Stated → Clarification → Context Provided

**Structure:**
```
[Initial unclear statement]
+ [Self-correction or clarification]
+ [Additional context to explain]
= Understanding achieved
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"I walk to school... actually, I run."
+ "7 kilometers."
+ "About two or three times a week."
= Complete picture of their training
```

**More Examples:**

| Initial | Clarification | Context |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| "I study a lot." | "Actually, I study every day." | "About three hours per day." |
| "I exercise." | "Well, I run, specifically." | "5km distance." |
| "I work." | "Part-time work, I mean." | "At a café, weekends." |

---

## 5.3 Invitation and Proposal Pattern

### Pattern: Suggestion → Non-Force Clause → Open Invitation

**Structure:**
```
"Have you thought about [opportunity]?"
+ "[Specific interest statement]."
+ "I'm not forcing you. Just let me know if interested."
= Respectful proposal
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"Have you ever thought about joining the track team?"
+ "We need long-distance runners."
+ "I know. I'm not forcing you. Just let me know if you're interested."
```

**More Examples:**

| Opportunity | Interest | Non-Force |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| "Have you considered getting a tutor?" | "Your math grade could improve." | "It's entirely up to you." |
| "Want to try the debate club?" | "You'd be great at it." | "No pressure, just a suggestion." |
| "Ever thought about leadership roles?" | "You show real potential." | "Just think about it." |

---

## 5.4 Sharing News Pattern

### Pattern: Announcement → Reaction Captured → Personal Response

**Structure:**
```
"I tell [person] [news]."
+ [Person's reaction captured]
+ "I respond with [my reaction]."
= Natural dialogue flow
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"At lunch, I told Minseo, 'Hey, the PE teacher asked me to join the track team.'"
+ "Minseo's eyes went wide. 'What? Seriously?'"
+ "'Yeah. They said they need long-distance runners.'"
```

# PART 6: EXPRESSING EMOTIONS (감정 표현)

## 6.1 Embarrassment and Self-Consciousness

### "I felt embarrassed, though I'm not sure why."

**Original Korean:** "부끄러웠다. 왜 그런지는 모르겠지만."

**Analysis:**
- 감정의 명확한 표현과 그 원인의 불명확성
- 칭찬받았을 때의 일반적인 반응
- 청자의 공감을 유도

**Embarrassment Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Cause |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| "I felt embarrassed." | ★★★☆☆ | 일반적 부끄러움 |
| "I was deeply embarrassed." | ★★★★☆ | 심한 부끄러움 |
| "I couldn't look him in the eye." | ★★★★★ | 극심한 부끄러움 |
| "I felt a bit shy." | ★★☆☆☆ | 약한 부끄러움 |
| "I was self-conscious." | ★★★☆☆ | 자의식 많음 |

**Similar Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| "I felt embarrassed." | "부끄러웠다" | 칭찬받을 때 |
| "I was flustered." | "당황했다" | 예상 밖 상황 |
| "I didn't know what to say." | "뭐라 말해야 할 지 몰랐다" | 말문이 막혔을 때 |
| "I wanted to hide." | "숨고 싶었다" | 극심한 부끄러움 |

## 6.2 Hesitation and Uncertainty About Change

### "I just run as a hobby."

**Original Korean:** "나는 그냥 취미로 달린다."

**Analysis:**
- 현재 상태에 대한 설명
- "just"는 강조와 제한을 함께 표현
- 팀에 참가하기 전의 개인적 성격 강조

**Hobby vs. Professional Expressions:**

| Expression | Level | Commitment |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| "I run as a hobby." | Amateur | Low |
| "I'm serious about running." | Semi-professional | Medium |
| "I train regularly." | Professional | High |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Teacher Recognizing Student Achievement

```
Teacher: Kim Doyun, have you been running a lot lately?
Student: Yes, I... actually, I run to school a few times a week.
Teacher: To school? Where do you live?
Student: In the Jocheon area.
Teacher: From there? How far is that?
Student: About 7 kilometers.
Teacher: You run 7 kilometers? (eyes wide) That's quite a distance!
Student: Yes, about two or three times a week.
Teacher: That explains why your stamina has improved so much. You're doing great.
Student: Thank you... (feeling embarrassed)
Teacher: Have you ever thought about joining the track team? We need long-distance runners.
Student: The track team? I... I'm not sure. I'll think about it.
Teacher: I'm not forcing you. Just let me know if you're interested.
```

**Key Phrases:**
- "I run to school" = 학교에 달려 간다
- "How far is that?" = 거리가 얼마나 돼?
- "That explains why" = 그래서 그런 거군요
- "I'm not forcing you" = 강요하지 않습니다

---

### Scenario 2: Sharing News with a Friend

```
Minseo: Hey, how was school?
You: Good. Something weird happened though.
Minseo: What?
You: The PE teacher asked me to join the track team.
Minseo: What? Seriously? (eyes wide)
You: Yeah. He said they need long-distance runners.
Minseo: Dude, that's huge. You got scouted!
You: I wouldn't call it scouted...
Minseo: Isn't it the same thing? The teacher came to you first.
You: I guess. But I'm not sure if I should do it.
Minseo: Why not?
You: I like running on my own. I'm not sure about competition.
Minseo: You should think about it. You're fast!
You: Maybe. I need to talk to my mom first.
```

**Key Phrases:**
- "Something weird happened" = 이상한 일이 생겼어
- "You got scouted?" = 스카웃된 거야?
- "I wouldn't call it..." = 그렇게까지는 아니고...
- "That's huge" = 완전 대박이네

---

### Scenario 3: Talking to Parent About Decision

```
Mom: How was school?
You: Good. Oh, the PE teacher asked me to join the track team.
Mom: The track team? You?
You: Yeah. He said my running has improved.
Mom: (smiling) Wow. The boy who used to hate exercise...
You: I know. I'm not sure if I'll do it though.
Mom: Why?
You: If I join, everything changes. There's daily training. Competitions. I'd get nervous.
Mom: That's true. But you love running, right?
You: Yeah, but I run for fun. I'm not sure about doing it for the team.
Mom: Take your time thinking about it. Do it if you really want to.
You: Okay. Thanks, Mom.
```

**Key Phrases:**
- "Join the track team" = 육상팀에 들어가다
- "My running has improved" = 달리기가 좋아졌어
- "Everything changes" = 모든 게 바뀐다
- "Take your time thinking" = 천천히 생각해

## 10.2 Writing Prompts

- Did it change how you see yourself?

- The opportunity presented
- What attracted you to it
- What worried you about it
- How you made your decision
- The result
