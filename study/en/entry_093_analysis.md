### Expression Analysis with Context

**"second nature to me now"** - Indicates mastery through repetition
- **Original context:** "Running in the rain is second nature to me now."
- **Similar expressions:**
  - "I'm good at it" (더 직접적)
  - "I'm a pro at it" (더 캐주얼)
  - "It comes naturally to me" (더 formal)
- **Formality spectrum:**
  - ★☆☆☆☆ "I can do it in my sleep"
  - ★★☆☆☆ "second nature to me"
  - ★★★☆☆ "It has become an ingrained habit"
  - ★★★★☆ "My proficiency is now automatic"

**Practice Dialogue 1:**

```
A: "I can't believe you're training in this weather!"
B: "Yeah, I know. But honestly, running in the rain is second
   nature to me now. I've been doing it for months."
A: "How are you not exhausted?"
B: "I guess your body just adapts. What seems impossible at first
   becomes totally normal."
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "It's second nature for me" (slightly awkward)
- ✓ "It's second nature to me" (correct)
- ❌ "I do it second nature" (wrong structure)
- ✓ "It's second nature to me" (correct)

---

**"I ran into Coach"** - Phrasal verb meaning unplanned encounter
- **Original context:** "Lunch. I ran into Coach."
- **Pronunciation:** /ræn ɪntu/
- **Distinction from "run into" (collision):**
  - "I ran into the fence" = accidentally collided with
  - "I ran into Coach" = happened to meet by chance
- **Variations:**
  - "I bumped into Coach"
  - "I came across Coach"
  - "I ran across Coach"
  - "I happened to see Coach"

**Practice Dialogue 2:**

```
A: "Did you talk to Coach about the training plan?"
B: "Actually, I ran into him at lunch today."
A: "Oh great! What did he say?"
B: "He told me we're starting to taper next week. Cutting back
   the distance."
A: "That makes sense with only 34 days left."
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "I ran in Coach" (wrong preposition)
- ✓ "I ran into Coach" (correct)
- ❌ "I ran at Coach" (sounds aggressive)
- ✓ "I ran into Coach" (correct)

---

**"Can't tell if that's sweat or rain water"** - Expression of confusion/uncertainty
- **Original context:** "Got to school. Can't tell if that's sweat or rain water."
- **Pattern:** "Can't tell if... or..."
- **Variations:**
  - "I can't tell if... or..." (more formal)
  - "I don't know if it's... or..." (clearer)
  - "It's hard to tell if... or..." (more polite)
  - "I'm not sure if... or..." (more tentative)

**Practice Dialogue 3:**

```
A: "You look soaked! Are you okay?"
B: "Yeah, I just ran 7km in the rain. I can't tell if this is
   sweat or rainwater."
A: "Maybe both? That sounds intense."
B: "It was, but honestly, I'm getting used to it."
```

| "ran into" | ~을 우연히 만나다 | Encountered by chance | "I ran into Coach" |

| "start reducing" | 줄이기 시작하다 | Begin process of reduction | "start reducing distance" |

### Phrasal Verb Deep Dive

**"RAN INTO" vs. "RUN INTO"**

- **Past tense:** "I ran into Coach"
- **Present tense:** "I run into Coach"
- **Present perfect:** "I have run into Coach several times"

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Collision** | 물리적으로 부딪히다 | "I ran into the wall" |
| **Coincidental meeting** | 우연히 만나다 | "I ran into Coach at lunch" |
| **Problem encounter** | 문제에 부딪히다 | "I ran into some difficulties" |

**Compare similar expressions:**

```
"ran into" = 우연히 만남 (완전히 예상하지 못함)
"bumped into" = 우연히 만남 (ran into와 같은 의미, 더 캐주얼)
"came across" = 만남 (더 formal, 또는 발견함)
"happened to see" = 마침 봄 (매우 우연적)
"encountered" = 만남 (formal, often implies difficulty)
```

**"SCALE BACK" - Reducing Size/Quantity**

- **Pattern:** Scale back + noun
- **Examples:**
  - "Scale back the volume" (양을 줄이다)
  - "Scale back training" (훈련량을 줄이다)
  - "Scale back expenses" (비용을 줄이다)
  - "Scale back the project" (프로젝트를 축소하다)

| Verb | Korean | Intensity | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| "reduce" | 줄이다 | Neutral | General purpose |
| "cut back" | 줄이다, 감소시키다 | More decisive | Health/spending |
| "scale back" | 규모를 줄이다 | Systematic | Large projects/training |
| "trim" | 다듬다, 삭제하다 | Specific areas | Detailed cuts |
| "pare down" | 필수만 남기다 | Minimal approach | Essential elements only |

**"KEEP GETTING"**

- **Pattern:** Keep + verb+ing (continuous action)
- **Meaning:** Progressive change; ongoing trend
- **Example:** "It keeps getting shorter" (계속해서 더 짧아진다)

```
"It keeps getting shorter"
↓
Progressive reduction = Intentional training strategy
↓
Coach's tapering plan = Deliberately reducing distance
```

**Pattern Structure:**
```
A: "What's [time period + noun]?"
B: "[Date/Time], [number + unit]. [Additional info]."
```

**Original from Text:**
```
"Coach, what's the plan for this week?"
"Tomorrow, 25km. Thursday off. Then we start reducing distance
next week."
```

```

in March."

### Pattern 4: Coach-Athlete Communication

**Pattern Structure:**
```
Athlete: [Seeks information/clarification]
Coach: [Gives instruction with explanation]
Athlete: [Shows understanding/asks follow-up]
```

**Original from Text:**
```

"Tomorrow, 25km. Thursday off. Then we start reducing distance

"Huh?"
"Tapering. You have to cut back the distance before the race."
"Oh, like the 21km week."
"Right. But we'll scale back the volume for longer—about two
weeks."
```

**Practice Examples:**

```
Example 1:
A: "Coach, should I run this Saturday?"
B: "Light 8km. We're starting to taper."

B: "Exactly. Your body needs recovery time before the race."

A: "Is 25km too much this week?"
B: "No, it's part of the plan. After that, we reduce."
A: "How much will we reduce?"
B: "Week by week. By race day, you'll be well-rested."

A: "So less training equals better performance?"
B: "Yes. Your body recovers, and you'll be fresh and strong."

Example 4:
A: "Coach, what does tapering mean?"
B: "It means reducing your training volume as you approach
the race."
A: "For how long?"
B: "Usually two to three weeks. Depends on your base."

Example 5:
A: "How many kilometers next week?"
B: "We're starting the taper. Expect 20km total."
A: "That's quite a drop from today's 25km."
B: "Right. We're giving your body time to adapt and recover."

## PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

### Dialogue Practice 1: Coach Conversation (Advanced)

**Context:** Student athlete discussing training plan with coach one month before race

```
A: "Coach, I wanted to ask about the January schedule."
B: "Sure. Next week is heavy—25km on Monday and Wednesday."
A: "And what about Thursday? I'm feeling a bit tired."
B: "Thursday is off. You need recovery."
A: "How long before we start the taper?"
B: "After this week. You'll reduce distance gradually over two weeks."
A: "So by early March, I'll be running less?"
B: "Exactly. Your body needs to be fresh for the race."
A: "Won't I lose fitness if I run less?"
B: "No. You've built a strong base. Now it's about being ready,
   not about building more strength."
A: "That makes sense. I'm nervous about race day."
B: "You should be prepared, not nervous. Trust your training."
```

**Vocabulary Focus:**
- "heavy" (힘든, 강한)
- "recovery" (회복)
- "taper" (거리 줄이기)
- "gradually" (점진적으로)
- "fresh" (신선한, 충분히 쉼을 얻은)
- "fitness" (체력)
- "base" (기초 체력)
- "Trust your training" (자신의 훈련을 믿다)

### Common Mistakes & Corrections

**Mistake 1: Confusing similar phrasal verbs**

❌ "I ran past Coach" (의미: 코치를 지나쳤다)
✓ "I ran into Coach" (의미: 코치를 만났다 - 우연한 만남)

---

**Mistake 2: Incorrect article usage**

❌ "Running in rains is second nature" (rain은 uncountable)
✓ "Running in the rain is second nature"

---

**Mistake 3: Confusing tense with habitual action**

❌ "I run 7km yesterday" (과거이므로 run이 아님)
✓ "I ran 7km yesterday"

But: "I run 7km every day" (습관적 행동 = 현재형)

---

**Mistake 4: Word order in informal diary**

❌ "I bathroom washed up in and headed to class" (단어 순서 혼동)
✓ "I washed up in the bathroom and headed to class"

Or (더 캐주얼): "Washed up in the bathroom, headed to class"

---

**Mistake 5: Misusing "scale back" vs. "cut back"**

- **"Scale back"** - 큰 규모에서의 체계적 감소
  ✓ "We scaled back the entire training program" (전체 프로그램을 체계적으로)

- **"Cut back"** - 더 직접적인 감소
  ✓ "I cut back on sweets for my health" (직접적 감소)

Both work in training context, but "scale back" is slightly more formal.

---

**Mistake 6: Confusing "peak" vs. "peak condition"**

❌ "I want to reach peak" (불완전)
✓ "I want to be in peak condition" OR "I want to reach my peak"

---

**Mistake 7: Assuming all "-ing" forms are progressive**

❌ "I am running 7km tomorrow" (시간 표현이 미래이므로 이상함)
✓ "I'm running 7km tomorrow" (계획된 미래)
✓ "I run 7km tomorrow" (확정된 일정)

Both are acceptable, but "am running" implies more certainty.
