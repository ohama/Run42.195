# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Morning Routine & Hesitation

### "I took the bus today. On purpose."

**Original Korean:** "오늘 버스를 탔어. 일부러."

**Analysis:**
- Short, emphatic sentences for impact
- "On purpose" signals intentional choice despite known difficulty
- 일반적 표현: "I deliberately took the bus." / "I took the bus on purpose."

**Variations by Emphasis:**
| Casual | Standard | Emphatic |
|--------|----------|----------|
| "I took the bus." | "I took the bus today." | "I took the bus today. On purpose." |
| "Just riding the bus." | "I rode the bus." | "I deliberately took the bus." |
| "I chose to take the bus." | "I decided to take the bus." | "I consciously took the bus." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Why did you take the bus?
B: I took the bus today. On purpose.
A: Really? But you've been running every day.
B: Yeah, I just wanted to try something different.
```

---

### "When the alarm went off, I thought: Let's just take the bus today."

**Original Korean:** "알람이 울렸을 때, 생각했다: 그냥 버스를 타자."

**Grammar Point:** Colon introduces direct thought/decision

**Structure:**
```
When [action], I thought: Let's [decision].
```

**Similar Thought Patterns:**
| Situation | Thought |
|-----------|---------|
| alarm | "Let's just take the bus." |
| tired | "Let's just skip it." |
| cold morning | "Let's just stay in bed." |
| hard task | "Let's just give up." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "My first thought was to take the bus."
- "The moment the alarm went off, I thought about taking the bus."
- "I woke up thinking: maybe I'll take the bus today."

---

### "I didn't want to run. Didn't want to get up."

**Original Korean:** "뛰고 싶지 않았다. 일어나고 싶지 않았다."

**Grammar Point:** Parallel negative statements for emphasis

**Structure:**
```
Didn't want to [verb]. Didn't want to [verb].
```

**Parallel Construction Examples:**
| Negative Parallel | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| "I didn't want to run. Didn't want to get up." | 체념 두 가지 |
| "I'm not smart. Not talented. Not special." | 자조 |
| "He didn't apologize. Didn't even look at me." | 실망 강조 |

**Emotional Context:**
This structure shows resignation and apathy. Both actions were ones the character usually did, but motivation disappeared.

## 1.2 Rationalizations & Justifications

### "The bus is easier, right? I can just sit and ride."

**Original Korean:** "버스가 편하지, 않나? 그냥 앉아서 타면 되지."

**Analysis:**
- "right?" seeks agreement/validation
- 자신을 납득시키려는 논리

**Seeking Confirmation Patterns:**
| Expression | Korean | Tone |
|------------|--------|------|
| "right?" | "맞지?" | Casual |
| "isn't it?" | "아니야?" | Casual |
| "you know?" | "알지?" | Seeking understanding |
| "don't you think?" | "안 그래?" | Requesting opinion |

**Rationalization Examples:**
```
"The bus is faster, right?"
"Sitting down is more comfortable, isn't it?"
"I deserve a break, don't I?"
"This makes sense, right?"
```

---

### "Motion sickness? It's summer break, so there'll be fewer people."

**Original Korean:** "차멀미? 여름방학이니까 사람이 적을 거야."

**Grammar Point:** Question + explanation (dismissing concern)

**Structure:**
```
[Concern]? [Logical reason why concern doesn't matter].
```

**Dismissing Worries Pattern:**
| Worry | Rationalization |
|-------|-----------------|
| "Motion sickness?" | "It's summer, so fewer people." |
| "It'll be boring?" | "I can read on the way." |
| "I'll be tired?" | "I'll sleep in tomorrow." |
| "What if I fail?" | "I can always try again." |

**Analysis:** The character tries to convince himself his concerns aren't valid. Rhetorical structure shows internal debate.

## 1.3 Emotional Assessment

### "Things have been weird since Thursday."

**Original Korean:** "목요일부터 이상했어."

**"Weird" in Context:**
- Vague description of emotional/physical state
- Shows narrator doesn't fully understand his own feelings
- 감정을 정확히 말하기 어려움

**"Weird/Off/Strange" Spectrum:**
| Expression | Specificity | Feeling |
|------------|------------|---------|
| "Things have been off." | ★☆☆ | 뭔가 이상함 |
| "I feel weird." | ★☆☆ | 이상한 기분 |
| "Something feels wrong." | ★★☆ | 뭔가 안 맞음 |
| "I'm in a slump." | ★★★ | 불황/저기분 |
| "I'm feeling burnt out." | ★★★★ | 탈진 |

### "Friday. I ran, but it just felt hard."

**Original Korean:** "금요일. 뛰었는데, 그냥 힘들었어."

**"Just" for Minimization:**
- "just felt hard" = 단순히, 그냥 힘들었어
- Not from external factors but internal state
- 이유 없이 힘들었던 것

**"Just" Usage Examples:**
| Usage | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| "It just felt wrong." | 그냥 이상했어 (이유 없이) |
| "It just didn't work." | 그냥 안 됐어 |
| "I just felt tired." | 그냥 피곤했어 |
| "It just felt off." | 그냥 이상했어 |

## 1.4 Short Narrative Statements

### "Saturday. I was supposed to run with Minseo. But I didn't want to go. I went anyway."

**Original Korean:** "토요일. 민서와 함께 뛰기로 했는데, 가고 싶지 않았다. 그런데도 갔어."

**Analysis:** Fragment sentences create diary-like pacing. Contrasts intention vs. action.

**Internal Conflict Pattern:**
| Situation | Internal Conflict | Action |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| "I didn't want to go. But I went anyway." | 가기 싫음 vs. 친구 | Went |
| "I was tired. But I had to." | 피곤함 vs. 책임감 | Did it anyway |
| "I was scared. But I had to try." | 두려움 vs. 용기 | Tried it |

---

### "Because Minseo showed up in new running shoes."

**Original Korean:** "민서가 새 러닝화를 신고 나타났기 때문에."

**"Showed up" Nuance:**
- Sudden appearance
- 예기치 않은 발견
- 감정적 영향

**"Showed up" vs. "Arrived":**
| Expression | Nuance |
|-----------|--------|
| "appeared" | 뜻밖에 나타남 |
| "showed up" | 캐주얼하게 나타남 |
| "arrived" | 계획대로 도착 |
| "came by" | 방문함 (친근함) |

**Impact:** Small detail (new shoes) becomes reason to participate. Shows how others' actions motivate us.

## 2.2 Breathing & Suffocation

### "I felt suffocated. ... Like I couldn't breathe."

**Original Korean:** "질식할 것 같았다. ... 숨을 못 쉴 것 같았다."

**Suffocation Metaphors:**
- 신체적 질식 (physical choking)
- 감정적 질식 (emotional constraint)
- 심리적 답답함 (psychological heaviness)

**Related Expressions:**
| Expression | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| "I felt trapped." | 갇혀 있는 기분 |
| "I couldn't breathe." | 질식 (심리/신체) |
| "I felt stifled." | 억압된 기분 |
| "I felt claustrophobic." | 폐쇄공포증 |
| "It was suffocating." | 숨 막히는 상황 |

**Physical vs. Psychological:**
```
"I couldn't breathe." - could mean:
1. 사람 많아서 숨이 막혀 (physical)
2. 불안해서 숨이 막혀 (emotional)
In this context: both!
```

---

### "I was comfortable. But something felt missing."

**Original Korean:** "편했어. 그런데 뭔가 부족했어."

**Comfort vs. Satisfaction Contrast:**
| State | Definition | Feeling |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Comfortable | 신체적으로 편함 | 좋음 |
| Satisfied | 욕구 충족 | 더 좋음 |
| Fulfilled | 영혼/정신 만족 | 최고 |
| Empty | 무언가 부족함 | 나쁨 |

**The Core Realization:**
```
Comfort ≠ Good
Good involves satisfaction beyond physical ease
```

**Post-Exercise Sensations:**
| Sensation | English | Korean |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| 상큼함 | refreshed, invigorated | 상큼해 |
| 깨끗함 | clean, purified | 깨끗해 |

| 뿌듯함 | fulfilled, accomplished | 뿌듯해 |
| 가벼움 | light, unburdened | 가벼워 |

**Why These Matter:**
- Not just physical sensations
- Emotional/psychological benefits of exertion
- Sense of accomplishment

## 2.3 Sensory Details

### "I looked out the window. Familiar scenery rushed past. The tangerine orchards. The road I run every day."

**Original Korean:** "창밖을 봤다. 익숙한 풍경이 빠르게 지나갔다. 귤 과수원. 매일 뛰는 길."

**Fragment Descriptions for Vivid Imagery:**
- Each noun stands alone
- Creates stopping effect
- Reader "sees" what character sees

**This Technique:**
- 명사 중심 서술 (noun-focused)
- 마치 사진처럼 (like photographs)
- 강렬한 이미지 (intense imagery)

**Practice Creating Fragments:**
```
"The city passed by. The streets. The familiar corners. The life I was leaving behind."
"She entered the room. The silence. The cold air. The dust particles."
"I saw my hometown. The mountains. The river. Home."
```

---

### "Wonder if the old man's there."

**Original Korean:** "할아버지가 있나 싶었다."

**"Wonder if" (궁금해하다):**
| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "I wonder if..." | ~인지 궁금해 |
| "I wondered if..." | ~인지 궁금했어 (과거) |

**The "old man" Reference:**
- Character they see on the running route
- Symbol of their daily running practice
- Absence from bus means disconnect from this life


## 3.2 Realization & Insight

### "I think I understood why I run."

**Original Korean:** "왜 내가 뛰는지 이해한 것 같았다."

**"Think I understood" (semi-confidence):**
- Not fully certain yet
- Growing realization
- Still processing

**Certainty Spectrum:**
| Expression | Certainty |
|-----------|-----------|
| "I realized..." | 확실히 깨달았다 |
| "I think I understood..." | 이해하는 것 같다 |
| "I'm starting to see..." | 보이기 시작해 |
| "Maybe I get it now..." | 이제 알 것 같아 |

---

### "I don't run to be comfortable. ... Comfortable and good are different things."

**Original Korean:** "편하려고 뛰는 게 아니야. ... 편함과 좋음은 달라."

**Philosophical Binary:**
```
COMFORTABLE: Easy, effortless, no struggle
VS.
GOOD: Meaningful, fulfilling, produces growth

Comfort ≠ Good
Good often requires discomfort
```

**Examples of This Distinction:**
| Situation | Comfortable | Good |
|-----------|------------|------|
| School | Skip class | Attend, engage |
| Food | Fast food | Healthy meal |
| Relationships | Avoid conflict | Address issues |
| Exercise | Rest | Run despite fatigue |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "I took the bus today. On purpose."

**Structure:** Announcement + emphasis of intentionality

**Uses:**
- When doing something unexpected
- Showing deliberate choice despite hesitation

**Examples:**
```
"I quit my job today. On purpose."
"I deleted their number. On purpose."
"I forgave them. On purpose."
```

---

## Pattern 2: "Things have been weird since [time]."

**Structure:** State change from specific point

**Uses:**
- Describing slumps
- Marking turning points
- Expressing confusing periods

**Examples:**
```
"Things have been weird since that day."
"Everything changed since she left."
"It's been different since last week."
```

---

## Pattern 3: "[Verb], but it just felt [adjective]."

**Structure:** Action + contradiction + feeling

**From Text:** "I ran, but it just felt hard."

**Examples:**
```
"I smiled, but it just felt fake."
"I tried, but it just felt hopeless."
"I went, but it just felt wrong."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "I was supposed to [action]. But I didn't want to [action]. I [action] anyway."

**Structure:** Internal conflict revealed

**From Text:** "I was supposed to run with Minseo. But I didn't want to go. I went anyway."

**Examples:**
```
"I was supposed to focus. But I couldn't. I tried anyway."
"I was supposed to be happy. But I felt sad. I pretended anyway."
"I was supposed to sleep. But I was restless. I stayed in bed anyway."
```

---

## Pattern 5: "The bus is [adjective], right? I can [action]."

**Structure:** Rhetorical question + rationalization

**Uses:**
- Trying to convince yourself
- Self-dialogue
- Rationalizing decisions

**Examples:**
```
"The couch is comfortable, right? I can just rest here."
"This is easy, right? I can finish it quickly."
"They won't notice, right? I can just sneak out."
```

---

## Pattern 6: "[Negative Concern]? [Positive Reason why it doesn't matter]."

**Structure:** Dismissing worries with logic

**From Text:** "Motion sickness? It's summer break, so there'll be fewer people."

**Examples:**
```
"I'll be tired? I can sleep in tomorrow."
"It might rain? I have an umbrella."
"What if I fail? I can always try again."
```

---

## Pattern 7: "[Experience]. And then [action]."

**Structure:** Quick sequence showing cause-effect

**From Text:** "Friday. I ran, but it just felt hard. Saturday. I was supposed to run with Minseo... Sunday. Did nothing. And then today."

**Effect:** Shows passage of time, mounting pressure

---

## Pattern 8: "I think I got it. ... I think I understood..."

**Structure:** Progressive realization

**Uses:**
- Moment of insight
- Gradual understanding
- Tentative conclusions

**Examples:**
```
"I think I see the problem now."
"I think I finally understand why you left."
"I think I'm starting to get it."
```

---

## Pattern 9: "[Comfortable thing]. But [missing feeling]."

**Structure:** Acknowledging trade-off

**From Text:** "I was comfortable. But something felt missing."

**Examples:**
```
"It was easy. But it felt hollow."
"I had everything. But I felt empty."
"It was safe. But it felt like dying."
```

---

## Pattern 10: "Comfortable and [state] are different things."

**Structure:** Philosophical distinction

**From Text:** "Comfortable and good are different things."

**Examples:**
```
"Happy and satisfied are different things."
"Easy and right are different things."
"Popular and loved are different things."
"Success and fulfillment are different things."
```

---

## 6.2 Internal Discomfort Despite Ease

### "I felt suffocated. ... Like I couldn't breathe."

**Anxiety Despite Security:**
- On a comfortable bus
- With air conditioning
- With a seat
- Yet feeling trapped

**The Paradox:**
```
Everything is "fine" externally
But internally: suffocation, unease
This is depression/anxiety in a nutshell
```

**Expressions for This State:**
```
"I felt trapped even though I was free."
"I felt empty even though I had everything."
"I felt dead inside even though everything was okay."
"I felt alone even though I was surrounded."
```

### "I think my slump is lifting a bit."

**Recovery Process:**
| Stage | Expression |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Confessing the Bus Experiment

```
A: Hey Doyun, you didn't run this morning?
B: No, I took the bus.
A: Really? Why?
B: I don't know. Things have been weird since Thursday.
   I just didn't feel like running.
A: And? How was the bus?
B: Comfortable. Really comfortable, actually.
A: So you'll take it again?
B: No way. Something felt off. I think I need to run.
A: Good. The bus is fine, but running is... different.
B: Yeah. Good different.
```

### Scenario 2: Discussing Comfort vs. Good

```
A: Why do you run if it's so hard?
B: Because I like it.
A: But the bus is easier.
B: Yeah, but it's not the same.
A: How so?
B: The bus is comfortable. But running feels good.
   They're completely different things.
A: I don't follow.
B: Comfort is easy. Good is... I don't know... real?
A: That's deep.
B: I'm still figuring it out.
```

### Scenario 3: Explaining the Realization

```
A: So what did you learn from taking the bus?
B: That I don't actually want comfort. I want something good.
A: You found that out from one bus ride?
B: Yeah. I was expecting to feel better on the bus,
   but I felt empty. That's when I realized.
A: Realized what?
B: That the hard stuff isn't really hard.
   It's actually what I need. Does that make sense?
A: Kind of. Like, you need the challenge.
B: Exactly. The challenge makes it good.
```
