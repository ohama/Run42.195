# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Pre-Race Expressions & Preparations

### "I didn't get much sleep, but somehow I wasn't tired."

**Original Korean:** "잠을 잘 못 잤는데도 피곤하지 않았다"

**Analysis:**
- 감정적 흥분이 육체적 피로를 극복하는 상황
- "somehow" = 어떻게 해서인지, 이유는 모르지만
- Conjunction: 예상과 다른 결과를 나타냄

**Variations by Cause:**

| Expression | Reason | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I didn't get much sleep, but I wasn't tired." | 감정적 흥분 (이벤트) | 특별한 날 |
| "I didn't sleep well, but adrenaline kicked in." | 신체적 반응 | 위험 상황 |
| "I barely slept, yet I felt energized." | 긍정적 에너지 | 기대감 |
| "I got no sleep, but the excitement kept me going." | 순수한 흥분 | 극적 상황 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you sleep before the race?
B: Not much, but somehow I wasn't tired.
A: How's that possible?
B: Just adrenaline, I guess. I was too excited.
A: That's amazing. That's the athlete's high.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I didn't sleep much, but I wasn't tiring." (피곤한 것은 형용사)
✅ "I didn't sleep much, but I wasn't tired."

### "Must be adrenaline."

**Original Korean:** "아드레날린 때문인 거 같다"

**Analysis:**
- "Must be" = 분명 ~일 거다 (추론하는 톤)
- 자신의 상태를 짧게 설명하는 자연스러운 표현
- 정확하지는 않지만 그럴 듯한 설명

**Adrenaline-Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Nuance | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "It must be adrenaline." | 추론 | 확실하지 않은 설명 |
| "Adrenaline's pumping." | 현재 진행 | 지금 느껴지는 것 |
| "My adrenaline is spiking." | 과학적 | 신체 반응 설명 |
| "I'm running on adrenaline." | 일반적 | 에너지원 설명 |
| "Pure adrenaline." | 절대적 | 다른 요인 없음 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: You look wide awake. How much sleep did you get?
B: Three hours maybe.
A: Must be adrenaline then.
B: Yeah, I've been thinking about the race all night.
```

### "I showered, put on my running gear, and ate a banana."

**Original Korean:** "샤워를 하고 러닝복을 입고 바나나를 먹었다"

**Grammar Point:** Simple past series with "and"

**Structure:** [Action 1] + and + [Action 2] + and + [Action 3]

**Pre-Event Preparation Sequences:**

| Sequence | Type | Timing |
|----------|------|--------|
| Shower → Get dressed → Eat breakfast | Physical prep | 30 mins before |
| Check gear → Stretch → Hydrate | Athletic warm-up | 15 mins before |
| Review strategy → Take deep breath → Get focused | Mental prep | 5 mins before |

**Why Banana Before Running:**
- High potassium = prevents muscle cramps
- Quick carbs = energy boost
- Easily digestible = won't cause stomach issues
- Common pre-race food

**Variations:**
- "I got up, took a quick shower, and had a banana."
- "I showered, changed into my gear, and grabbed a banana."
- "After showering and dressing, I ate a banana."

### "You'll be fine. Just enjoy it."

**Original Korean:** "괜찮을 거야. 즐겨"

**Analysis:**
- 두 가지 다른 조언을 섞은 표현
- 첫 번째: 성공할 거다 (안심)
- 두 번째: 과정을 즐겨라 (마음가짐)

**Encouragement Patterns:**

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Reassurance + instruction | "You'll be [good]. Just [action]." | "You'll be fine. Just enjoy it." |
| Confidence + focus | "You've got this. Just [focus]." | "You've got this. Just stay calm." |
| Belief + mindset | "I believe in you. [Do this]." | "I believe in you. Run your race." |

**Conversation Context:**
```
Dad: You'll be fine. Just enjoy it.
Doyun: Yeah, but what if I can't finish?
Dad: You've trained for this. Trust yourself.
Mom: And remember, this is your first race. Just have fun.
```

## 2.1 Pre-Start Nervousness

### "My heart was pounding like crazy."

**Original Korean:** "심장이 미친 듯이 뛰었다"

**Analysis:**
- "Pounding" = 심장이 빠르게 뛰다 (육체적 감각)
- "Like crazy" = 미친 듯이 (강도 극대화)
- 매우 구체적이고 감정적인 표현

**Heart Racing Expressions (강도별):**

| Expression | Intensity | Physical Sign |
|------------|-----------|---------------|
| "My heart was racing." | ★★★ | 빠른 맥박 |
| "My heart was pounding." | ★★★★ | 큰 맥박 |
| "My heart was pounding like crazy." | ★★★★★ | 극도의 긴장 |
| "My heart was about to burst." | ★★★★★ | 절정 |
| "My heart was in my throat." | ★★★★ | 목까지 올라옴 |

**Physical Manifestations of Nervousness:**

| Symptom | Expression | Context |
|---------|------------|---------|
| 빠른 심장박동 | "My heart was racing." | 일반적 |
| 손 떨림 | "My hands were shaking." | 통제 불능 |
| 호흡 곤란 | "I was breathing hard." | 과호흡 |
| 배에 불안감 | "I had butterflies in my stomach." | 신경 |
| 목 건조 | "My throat was dry." | 탈진 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How did you feel at the starting line?
B: Nervous. My heart was pounding like crazy.
A: Did you think about backing out?
B: For a second, yeah. But then the gun went off.
```

## 2.2 During the Race - Pace Management

### "I checked my watch. 5:18. Right on pace."

**Original Korean:** "시계를 봤다. 5:18. 딱 맞는 속도다."

**Analysis:**
- Running watch = 시간/거리/속도를 추적
- 5:18 = 5분 18초 per km (페이스)
- "Right on pace" = 계획한 속도대로 진행 중

**Running Pace Vocabulary:**

| Term | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| Pace | 킬로미터당 시간 | "My pace is 5 minutes per km." |
| Split | 구간별 시간 | "My 1km split was 5:18." |
| Negative split | 후반부 더 빠른 시간 | "I ran a negative split in the race." |
| Even pace | 일정한 속도 | "Run an even pace for consistency." |
| Slow start | 처음 천천히 | "Always do a slow start to warm up." |

**Pace-Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Situation |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "Right on pace." | 계획대로 | 잘 진행 중 |
| "Ahead of pace." | 더 빠름 | 계획 초과 |
| "Behind pace." | 더 느림 | 계획 미달 |
| "I lost my pace." | 리듬 상실 | 곤란한 상황 |
| "I found my rhythm." | 리듬 회복 | 좋은 상황 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How's the race going?
B: Good so far. I'm right on pace.
A: What's your target time?
B: About 50 minutes for the 10km.
A: You've got this!
```

## 2.3 The Mental Game During Racing

### "I picked up the pace. 5:02. My breathing got a bit harder. But still manageable."

**Original Korean:** "페이스를 올렸다. 5:02. 숨이 조금 거칠어졌다. 하지만 아직 견딜 만했다."

**Analysis:**
- 속도 증가의 결과 (호흡 변화)
- "Manageable" = 통제 가능한 범위 내
- 신체 신호를 읽고 해석하기

**Breathing Pattern Descriptions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Aerobic State |
|------------|-----------|---------------|
| "I was breathing normally." | ★☆☆ | Conversational pace |
| "My breathing got harder." | ★★☆ | Tempo pace |
| "I was breathing heavily." | ★★★ | Hard effort |
| "I couldn't catch my breath." | ★★★★ | Anaerobic threshold |
| "I was gasping for air." | ★★★★★ | Maximum effort |

**Effort Level Expressions:**

| Expression | Difficulty | Control |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "Easy." | ★☆☆ | 완전 통제 |
| "Manageable." | ★★☆ | 통제 가능 |
| "Getting hard." | ★★★ | 어려워짐 |
| "Really hard." | ★★★★ | 통제 어려움 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How was km 3?

```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Race Morning Preparation

**Context:** 아침 6시, 아버지가 도윤을 차에 데려감

```
Dad: You ready? Let's go.
Doyun: Yeah. My stomach's a bit nervous.
Dad: That's normal. You've trained hard.
Doyun: What if I can't finish?
Dad: You will. Just take it steady at first.
Doyun: Okay.
Dad: And enjoy it. Don't just race—enjoy the moment.
Doyun: Got it.
```

**Variations:**
- Change the family member (mom, sister)
- Add concerns about specific distances
- Include advice about pacing

**Context:** 도윤이 8km 지점에서 매우 힘들어함

```
Doyun's thought: This is so hard. My legs are screaming.

Doyun: I've come this far. Can't quit now.
Doyun: *breathing hard* One foot in front of the other.
Doyun: The finish line... it's close.
```

**Variations:**
- Different km markers

- Different motivational thoughts
