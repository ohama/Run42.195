### Practice Dialogue 1: Telling a Friend Your New Goal

**Student A (You):**
- Context: Sharing your new goal with a friend at school

```
A: Hey, guess what? I've set a new goal.
B: What is it?
A: I'm going to do a half marathon. 21km.
B: What?? That's like double your last one!
A: Yeah. I'm scared, honestly. But I'm going to prepare gradually.
B: If you say you're doing it, I believe you.
A: Thanks.
```

**Useful phrases to use:**
- "I've decided to..." = (결정했어)
- "I'm honestly scared, but..." = (솔직히 무서워 하지만)
- "I'm going to prepare gradually" = (천천히 준비할 거야)

### Practice Dialogue 2: Discussing Concerns with Family

**Context: Evening conversation at the dinner table**

```
Parent: Isn't that too much for you?
Student: No, my teacher made a training plan for me. I'm going to prepare gradually.
Parent: Well, if you're prepared and have a plan, then you've got this.
Student: Yeah. I used to think 10km was impossible too.
Parent: Exactly. You've already accomplished so much.
```

**Key expressions:**
- "Isn't that too much?" = (그건 너한테 너무 많지 않아?)
- "I'm going to prepare gradually" = (천천히 준비할 거야)
- "You've got this" = (넌 할 수 있어)

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "I set a goal" (one time past) | "I've set a new goal" (recently completed, present relevance) | Use present perfect for recent decisions that matter now |
| "I am scary" | "I'm scared" | Scared = feeling fear; Scary = inspiring fear |
| "the last 2km was hard" | "the last 2km was brutal" | "Brutal" shows intensity; more natural in casual context |
| "I looked worried" | "I looked worried" (observing self vs others) | "Mom looked worried" (observing others' expressions) |
| "That's crazy" | "That's insane" | Both work; "insane" more colloquial for shock/disbelief |

## PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

### Athletic and Physical Expressions

| Expression | Korean | Physical Meaning | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I'm scared" | 무섭다/두렵다 | Fear sensation; nervous energy in body | Mental/emotional response to challenge |
| "the last 2km was brutal" | (마지막 2km가 정말 힘들었다) | Physical exhaustion, pain, struggle | Describing the difficulty of running |
| "you risk injury" | 부상 위험이 있다 | Potential bodily harm | Training caution |
| "let your body rest" | 몸을 쉬게 하다 | Physical recovery; muscles need recovery time | Training principle |
| "That's insane" | 미쳤어/대박 | Shock response in the body (wide eyes, disbelief) | Observing something extreme |
| "I laughed" | 웃었다 | Physical response to humor/relief | Emotional release |

### Physical Training Vocabulary

| Term | Korean | Definition | Example from text |
|------|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Long run | 장거리 달리기 | Extended distance running session | "long run once a week" |
| Distance | 거리 | Measured length of run | "jumping from 10km to 21km" |
| Training plan | 훈련 계획 | Structured running schedule | "worked out a training plan" |

### Sensory Details Practice

Describe your own physical sensation:

```
Question: How did your body feel the last 2km of your 10km run?
Possible answers:
- "My legs felt heavy and tired"
- "I was breathing really hard"
- "My whole body was burning"
- "I felt exhausted but determined"
- "Everything felt numb, but I kept going"
```

### Common Mistakes in Physical Expression

| Mistake | Correct | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "I was hard" | "It was hard" or "I was struggling" | "Hard" describes difficulty, not the person |
| "The pain was insane" | "The pain was intense/brutal" | "Insane" for shock; "brutal" for difficulty |
| "My legs were dead" | "My legs felt dead" or "I was exhausted" | Poetic but needs context; safer option |
| "I felt scared of my pain" | "I was in pain and getting nervous" | Clearer separation of sensations |

## PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

### Key Phrasal Verbs in Entry 063

| Phrasal Verb | Korean | Original Meaning | Usage in Context | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Work out** | 계획을 세우다 / 운동하다 | 1) Create a plan; 2) Exercise | Plan creation | "I worked out a training plan with my teacher" |
| **Set up** | 준비하다/세우다 | Prepare or arrange | Similar to "work out" for planning | "He set up the training schedule" |
| **Jump from...to...** | ...에서 ...로 뛰어올라가다 | Make a big leap/increase | Dramatic change | "If you're jumping from 10km to 21km, you need to increase gradually" |
| **Add to** | 더하다/추가하다 | Include additional | Building incrementally | "Add to your long run once a week" |
| **Cut back** | 줄이다/감소시키다 | Reduce amount/intensity | Training principle | "The week before the race, cut back the distance" |
| **Push too hard** | 너무 무리하다 | Exceed safe limits | Warning/caution | "You push too hard, you risk injury" |
| **Told my family** | 가족에게 말하다 | Share information | Communication | "I told my family. I'm going to do a half marathon" |

### Phrasal Verb Comparison Table

| Phrasal Verb | Similar Option | Difference | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Work out a plan" | "Make a plan" / "Create a plan" | More conversational; suggests negotiation/collaboration | Teacher and student together |
| "Jump from 10km to 21km" | "Go from 10km to 21km" | "Jump" emphasizes the big leap; dramatic | Highlighting the challenge |
| "Cut back the distance" | "Reduce the distance" / "Lower the distance" | "Cut back" more colloquial, common in athletic context | Training advice |
| "Add to your long run" | "Increase your long run" | "Add to" is more specific about incremental growth | Weekly training progression |

### Practice Sentences with Phrasal Verbs

1. **Work out:**
   - "We worked out a training plan that fits my schedule."
   - "I need to work out the details before the race."
   - "Have you worked out your running route yet?"

2. **Jump:**
   - "Don't jump from 10km straight to 21km without preparation."
   - "He jumped into the competition without proper training."
   - "You're jumping to conclusions; let's see what happens."

3. **Cut back:**
   - "A week before the race, you need to cut back your training."
   - "Cut back on processed food if you want to run better."
   - "The team cut back their schedule due to injuries."

4. **Add to:**
   - "Add to your running distance gradually each week."
   - "Can we add to the training plan?"
   - "I'm going to add to my morning routine."

### Common Mistakes with Phrasal Verbs

| Mistake | Correct | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "I worked out in the gym a plan" | "I worked out a training plan at the gym" | Phrasal verb stays together |
| "Jump to from 10 to 21" | "Jump from 10km to 21km" | Correct preposition sequence |
| "Cut back the distance training" | "Cut back the distance" or "Cut back on training" | Choose one object |
| "Add to distance every week" | "Add to your long run distance every week" | Needs clearer noun |

## PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

### Pattern 1: Announcing a Significant Decision

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[Statement of decision] + [Additional context]
"I've set a new goal." + "Half marathon. 21km."
```

**Variations:**
- "I've decided to run a half marathon."
- "I'm going to do a half marathon."
- "My new goal is to complete a half marathon."
- "I'm attempting a 21km run in November."

**Usage examples from dialogue:**
```
1. "I've set a new goal. Half marathon. 21km."
2. "I'm going to do a half marathon. 21km."
3. "I'm going to do a half marathon."
4. "My new goal is a half marathon."
```

**When to use each:**
- "I've set a goal" = Decision already made, announcing it now
- "I'm going to do..." = Future intention, already decided
- "I'm attempting..." = More formal, emphasizing the challenge
- "My goal is..." = Most formal, stated clearly

---

### Pattern 2: Seeking or Offering Advice

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[Context] + [Concern/Question] → [Advice/Reassurance]
"Isn't that too much for you?" → "No. My teacher made a plan. I'm going to prepare gradually."
```

**Variations:**
- "Isn't that too much for you?" (parent concern)
- "You push too hard, you risk injury" (cautionary advice)
- "Don't rush it, no matter what" (strong advice)
- "We did that with the 10km race too" (reassuring comparison)

**Practice examples:**
```
A: How will you handle the fatigue?
B: I'll follow the training plan. My teacher made sure it's realistic.

A: Are you worried about getting injured?
B: Yeah, but my teacher said not to push too hard. So I'm taking it slowly.

A: What if you can't finish?
B: I finished 10km, didn't I? I just need to trust the process.
```

---

### Pattern 3: Expressing Fear or Doubt (but Determination)

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[Honest admission of fear] + [Reason for attempt] + [Evidence of capability]
"I'm scared. Honestly." + "But I want to try." + "Back in March, even walking 7km was tough. But now I can run 10km in under fifty minutes."
```

**Variations:**
- "I'm scared. Honestly. But I want to try."
- "Even with 10km, the last 2km was brutal. What about 21km?"
- "I'm scared. But I want to try."
- "I'm worried, but I'm going to prepare gradually."

**Practice examples:**
```
1. "I'm scared about the distance, but I want to try."
2. "Honestly? I'm nervous. But my teacher has a plan."
3. "The challenge seems huge, but I've overcome challenges before."
4. "I'm scared of failing, but I'm more scared of not trying."
```

---

### Pattern 4: Making Comparisons to Show Growth

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[Past difficulty] + Contrast + [Current achievement] = [Future possibility]
"Back in March, even walking 7km was tough." + "But now I can run 10km in under fifty minutes." = "So shouldn't I be able to do 21km too?"
```

**Variations:**
- "10km was already hard, and now 21km? But I finished 10km."
- "I used to think the same thing. Now look at me."
- "Back in March, 7km seemed impossible. Now 10km is done."
- "Even with 10km, the last 2km was brutal. But I did it."

**Full conversation example:**
```
Student: What if I can't finish 21km?
Parent: You couldn't finish 10km at first either, right?
Student: Yeah... that's true.
Parent: So you've already proven you can grow. You can do this too.
Student: Thanks. I guess I just need to trust myself.
```

### Pattern 5: Acknowledging Time/Distance Progression

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[Timeframe] + [Specific distances] + [Progression logic]
"You've got about a month until the November race." + "This week 12km, next week 14km, the week after 16km..."
```

**Variations:**
- "Four weeks. Slowly building up the distance."
- "This week 12km, next week 14km, the week after 16km."
- "Add to your long run once a week."
- "The week before the race, cut back the distance."

**Practice:**
```
Coach: Let's look at your preparation timeline.
Student: How long do I have?
Coach: About a month until November. We'll increase distance gradually.
Student: Like how much each week?
Coach: Start with 12km, then 14km, then 16km, then pull back to 12km the final week.
Student: That makes sense.
```

### Pattern 6: Reacting to Surprising Information

**Pattern Structure:**
```
[New information] → [Reaction] + [Clarifying question]
"21km." → "What's that?" + "Half of a full marathon?"
```

**Variations:**
- "A half? What's that?" (family member, confused)
- "21km? That's like double 10km." (surprised realization)
- "You're running for two hours straight?" (shock)
- "That's insane..." (disbelief)
- "Whoa. That's insane..." (strong reaction)

**Practice:**
```
Friend: I'm doing a half marathon.
You: A half? What's that exactly?
Friend: 21km. Half of a full 42km marathon.
You: Wow, that's way more than your last race.
Friend: Yeah, it's scary, but I'm going to try.
You: That's amazing. When's the race?
```

## PART 6: EXPRESSING EMOTIONS (감정 표현)

### Emotion Spectrum: Fear and Determination

| Intensity | Expression | Korean | Context | Physical Signs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low anxiety | "I'm a bit nervous" | 좀 불안해 | Mild concern | Slight tension |
| Moderate | "I'm worried" | 걱정돼 | Genuine concern | Thinking, planning |
| High | "I'm scared" | 무서워/두려워 | Significant fear | Hesitation, doubt |
| Very high | "I'm terrified" | 정말 무서워/공포스러워 | Extreme fear | Avoidance, panic |

**From the text:**
- "Honestly? I'm scared." (moderate-to-high fear, but vulnerable honesty)
- "I'm scared. Honestly." (emphasis on honesty; reiterating the feeling)

**Initial emotion (teacher consultation):**
- Professional tone, taking notes
- Focused, accepting guidance
- Emotion: **Cautious optimism** + some concern

**After contemplation (bus ride):**

**Sharing with family:**
- Mom: worried ("Isn't that too much for you?")

### Practice Dialogue: Expressing Emotional Vulnerability

**Scenario: Talking with a trusted friend about your fears**

```
Friend: You seem nervous about the half marathon.
You: Yeah, I'm scared, honestly. 10km was already hard at the end.
Friend: But you finished it, right?
You: Yeah, but 21km is way more. What if I can't?
Friend: What's the worst that could happen?
You: I don't finish. I get injured. I look stupid.
Friend: Have you failed before?
You: Not at running, no.
Friend: So you've never proven you can't do hard things. Only that you can.
You: That's... actually really helpful. Thanks.
```

### Common Emotional Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "I'm scared to run" | "I'm scared of the distance" or "I'm scared about the race" | Be specific about what you fear |
| "I'm emotional" | "I'm nervous" or "I'm scared" | State the specific emotion |
| "I feel bad" | "I'm scared" or "I'm worried" or "I feel discouraged" | "Bad" is too vague |
| "I was thinking afraid" | "I was thinking about it" or "I was scared" | Can't combine thinking and emotion this way |

## PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

### Incomplete Sentences and Fragments

**From the text:**
```
"Half marathon. 21km."
[Complete sentence would be: "I'm going to run a half marathon, which is 21km."]

"After school."
[Complete sentence would be: "The meeting was after school."]

"On the bus ride home."
[Complete sentence would be: "I was thinking about it on the bus ride home."]

"Evening."
[Complete sentence would be: "In the evening, I told my family."]

"Cloudy with occasional sun."
[Complete sentence would be: "It was a cloudy day with occasional sun."]
```

**Why this works in teen speech:**
- Diary format allows fragments (natural, conversational)
- Creates rhythm and emphasis
- Shows how a teen mind jumps between ideas
- Each fragment can stand alone as a "beat" in the narrative

### Filler Words and Hedging

**Actual usage from text:**
- "Yeah." (agreement, used 3 times in dialogue)
- "Like" - not used explicitly, but implied in "like double 10km"
- "Honestly?" (hedging, admits following statement is genuine feeling)
- "Actually, I'm going to try" (slight correction, afterthought)

**Comparison:**
| Filler | Korean | Usage | Example |
|--------|--------|-------|---------|
| Yeah | 응 | Agreement, acknowledgment | "Yeah." / "Yeah, there's a race in November." |
| Like | 어떻게 말하자면 | Comparison, approximation | "That's like double 10km" |
| Actually | 실제로/사실은 | Correction, addition | "Actually, I'm going to try" |
| Honestly | 솔직히 | Admitting truth, emphasis | "Honestly? I'm scared." |
| You know | 알지 | Seeking agreement, emphasizing | Less used in this entry |

### Intensifiers

| Intensifier | Korean | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| totally | 완전히 | ★★★☆☆ | "That's totally different now" |
| really | 정말 | ★★★☆☆ | "That's really hard" |
| so | 정말 | ★★★☆☆ | "You're so tired" |
| insane | 미쳤어/대박 | ★★★★☆ | "That's insane..." |
| brutal | 정말 힘들다 | ★★★★☆ | "the last 2km was brutal" |
| literally | 말 그대로 | ★★☆☆☆ | (not in text, but would work) |

**From the text:**
- "the last 2km was brutal" - shows intensity of difficulty
- "That's insane..." - shows surprise and shock
- Used sparingly, which makes them more effective

### Conversational Contractions

| Full Form | Contraction | Casual Use |
|---|---|---|
| "I have set" | "I've set" | "I've set a new goal" |
| "You have got" | "You've got" | "You've got about a month" |
| "That is" | "That's" | "That's insane" |
| "Do not" | "Don't" | "Don't rush it" |
| "Will not" | "Won't" | (not in text, would be "won't make it") |

### Sentence Starters in Teen Speech

**From the text:**
- "Yeah." - agreement opener
- "Honestly?" - vulnerability opener
- "Actually..." - correction opener
- "I used to think..." - reflection opener
- "So shouldn't I...?" - logical conclusion opener

**Practice mixing these:**
```
"Yeah, I know it's scary. But honestly? I'm more afraid of not trying.
Actually, my teacher made a realistic plan. So shouldn't I be able to do it?"
```

### Practice: Rewriting Formal to Casual

**Formal version:**
"I have decided to participate in a half marathon race scheduled for November. I acknowledge that this represents a significant increase in distance compared to my previous 10-kilometer run."

**Casual teen version:**
"I've set a new goal. Half marathon. 21km. That's like double my last race. Honestly? I'm scared. But I want to try."

**Why the casual version works:**
- Fragments match diary format
- "Honestly?" shows vulnerability
- Shorter, punchier sentences
- Shows genuine emotion rather than formal declaration

## PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

### Dialogue Practice 1: Consulting with Your Coach/Teacher

**Scenario:** You want to train for a half marathon and need advice.

**Student role:** You're nervous but determined. You want specific guidance on how to prepare safely.

```
Student: I want to run a half marathon. 21km. But I'm scared.
Teacher: That's a big jump from 10km. What makes you scared?
Student: What if I get injured? What if the last part is too hard?
Teacher: Those are valid concerns. Here's what we'll do. You need a
         training plan. Four weeks, gradual increase.
Student: How much should I run each week?
Teacher: Start with 12km, then 14km, then 16km. The final week,
         cut back to 12km to let your body recover.
Student: That makes sense. No pushing too hard?
Teacher: Exactly. Consistency beats intensity. You've got this.
Student: Thanks. I feel better about it now.
```

**Key expressions used:**
- "I want to..." = 하고 싶어
- "What makes you scared?" = 뭐가 무섭니?
- "Big jump" = 큰 도약
- "Valid concerns" = 타당한 걱정
- "Gradual increase" = 천천한 증가
- "Let your body recover" = 몸을 회복시키다
- "Consistency beats intensity" = 꾸준함이 강도보다 중요하다

**Variations to practice:**
- Replace "scared" with "nervous" or "worried"
- Replace "jump" with "leap" or "increase"
- Add your own questions about nutrition, sleep, or pacing
