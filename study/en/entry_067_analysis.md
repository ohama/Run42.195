# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Registration & Sign-Up Expressions

### "I signed up for the half marathon."

**Original Text:** "I signed up for the half marathon."

**Korean Equivalent:** 하프마라톤에 등록했다

**Analysis:**
- 핵심 표현: 공식적으로 참가 신청을 완료했다는 의미
- "sign up for" = 등록하다, 신청하다
- 스포츠 이벤트, 수업, 서비스 등에 널리 사용

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "signed up for" | "registered for" | "enrolled in" |
| "I'm in" | "I've registered" | "I have completed registration" |
| "I entered" | "I applied for" | "I submitted my entry" |

**Nuance Spectrum:**

| Expression | Nuance | Example |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "signed up" | 일반적/캐주얼 | "I signed up for the race." |
| "registered" | 약간 더 격식 | "I registered for the marathon." |
| "enrolled" | 수업/프로그램 | "I enrolled in the class." |
| "entered" | 대회/경쟁 | "I entered the competition." |
| "put my name down" | 매우 캐주얼 | "I put my name down for the event." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Are you running the Jeju Half Marathon?
B: Yeah, I signed up for it yesterday.
A: Really? That's awesome!
B: Thanks. I'm nervous, but excited.
A: When is it?
B: November 24th.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "I signed up to the half marathon." (to 대신 for 사용)
- ✅ "I signed up for the half marathon."
- ❌ "I signed the half marathon." (up 누락)
- ✅ "I signed up for the half marathon."

**Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "register online" | 온라인으로 등록하다 | "I registered online." |
| "complete registration" | 등록을 완료하다 | "Registration complete!" |
| "pay the entry fee" | 참가비를 내다 | "I paid the entry fee." |

## 1.2 Reporting & Telling Others

### "I told Minseo."

**Original Text:** "I told Minseo."

**Korean Equivalent:** 민서에게 말했다

**Grammar Point:** Simple past tense for sharing news

**Variations for Sharing News:**

| Expression | Nuance | Korean |
|------------|--------|--------|
| "I told" | 단순 전달 | ~에게 말했다 |
| "I informed" | 격식/공식적 | ~에게 알렸다 |
| "I let know" | 알려주다 | ~에게 알려줬다 |
| "I shared with" | 나누다 | ~와 공유했다 |
| "I broke the news" | 중요한 소식 | 소식을 전했다 |


# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Nervousness & Physical Reactions

### "My hands were shaking."

**Original Text:** "My hands were shaking."

**Korean Equivalent:** 손이 떨렸다

**Analysis:**
- 긴장, 흥분, 두려움 등의 감정을 신체적으로 표현
- 중요한 순간의 떨림을 묘사

**Physical Signs of Nervousness:**

| Expression | Korean | Intensity |
|------------|--------|-----------|
| "My hands were shaking" | 손이 떨렸다 | ★★★ |
| "My heart was racing" | 심장이 빠르게 뛰었다 | ★★★ |
| "I had butterflies" | 긴장됐다 | ★★☆ |
| "My palms were sweaty" | 손바닥에 땀이 났다 | ★★★ |
| "I was trembling" | 몸이 떨렸다 | ★★★★ |
| "I felt jittery" | 안절부절못했다 | ★★☆ |

**"Shake" in Different Contexts:**

| Context | Expression | Meaning |
|---------|------------|---------|
| 긴장 | "My hands were shaking" | 손이 떨렸다 |
| 추위 | "I was shaking from the cold" | 추워서 떨렸다 |
| 분노 | "He was shaking with anger" | 화가 나서 떨렸다 |
| 흥분 | "I was shaking with excitement" | 흥분해서 떨렸다 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Were you nervous when you signed up?
B: Yeah, my hands were shaking.
A: Really? That nervous?
B: I know it sounds silly, but 21km is a big deal.
A: It's not silly at all. It's a huge commitment.
B: Thanks for understanding.
```

## 10.5 Discussion Questions

1. **Have you ever signed up for something that scared you?** What was it? How did you feel when you registered?
