# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Expressing Restlessness & Physical Urges

### "My body feels restless."

**Original Korean:** "몸이 좀 불안하다"

**Analysis:**
- 신체적으로 뭔가를 해야 할 것 같은 불편한 감정
- 정신적 불안감과 다르게 신체적 불편함 표현

**Variations by Intensity:**
| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I feel a little antsy." | ★☆☆ | 약간 안달이 나다 |
| "My body feels restless." | ★★☆ | 몸이 불안하다 |
| "I'm going crazy not [doing something]." | ★★★ | 미치겠다 |
| "I'm climbing the walls." | ★★★★ | 벽을 타고 올라갈 정도로 초조하다 |

**Related Expressions:**
| English | Korean | Context |
|---------|--------|---------|
| "I feel antsy." | 안절부절못하다 | 움직이고 싶은 기분 |
| "I'm restless." | 불안정하다 | 뭔가를 해야 할 것 같다 |
| "I can't sit still." | 가만히 앉아있을 수 없다 | 자꾸만 움직이고 싶다 |
| "I'm fidgety." | 안절부절못하다 | 자꾸 만지작거린다 |
| "I'm going stir-crazy." | 갇혀있어서 미친다 | 실내에만 있을 때 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: You look antsy. What's wrong?
B: I've been stuck inside for three days. My body feels restless.
A: Why don't you go for a run?
B: I can't. The typhoon is still passing through.
A: That must be driving you crazy.
B: Completely. I need to move.
```

### "My legs are dying to run."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 뛰고 싶어서 안달이 난다"

**Grammar Point:** "be dying to" = 매우 ~하고 싶다

**Structure:** Subject + be dying to + verb (present tense)

**Intensity Spectrum:**
| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I want to run." | ★☆☆ | 기본 표현 |
| "I really want to run." | ★★☆ | 강한 욕망 |
| "I'm dying to run." | ★★★ | 매우 간절한 욕망 |
| "I'm desperate to run." | ★★★★ | 절실한 욕망 |
| "I can't not run." | ★★★★★ | 강박적 욕망 |

**Similar "Dying to" Expressions:**
| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| "dying to" | "I'm dying to see the movie." (영화 보고 싶어서 죽겠어) |
| "itching to" | "I'm itching to leave." (떠나고 싶어서 견딜 수 없다) |
| "longing to" | "I'm longing to go home." (집에 가고 싶다) |
| "eager to" | "I'm eager to start." (시작하고 싶다) |
| "craving" | "I'm craving coffee." (커피 마시고 싶다) |

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "My legs are dying to running." (동사원형)
✅ "My legs are dying to run."

## 1.2 Weather-Related Expressions

### "Rain. Typhoon passing through."

**Original Korean:** "비. 태풍이 지나가는 중이다"

**Analysis:** Fragment structure for dramatic effect

**Weather Events Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean | Severity |
|---------|--------|----------|
| rain | 비 | ★☆☆ |
| heavy rain | 많은 비 | ★★☆ |
| downpour | 호우 | ★★★ |
| storm | 폭풍 | ★★★ |
| typhoon | 태풍 | ★★★★ |
| hurricane | 허리케인 | ★★★★ |

**Why This Opening Works:**
- 두 단어로 상황을 완벽히 설정
- 일기의 즉각적이고 솔직한 톤
- 불편함과 제약을 즉시 전달

### "The wind could pick up again out of nowhere."

**Original Korean:** "바람이 갑자기 다시 세질 수 있다"

**Grammar Point:** "could + verb" for unexpected possibilities

**Structure:** Subject + could + verb + out of nowhere / at any moment

**"Out of Nowhere" Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Formality |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "out of nowhere" | 갑자기, 아무데서나 | Casual |
| "out of the blue" | 예상 못했게 | Casual/Neutral |
| "all of a sudden" | 갑자기 | Neutral |
| "without warning" | 경고 없이 | More formal |
| "unexpectedly" | 예상 외로 | Formal |

**Unpredictable Weather Expressions:**
```
"The weather could change at any moment."
"The storm might get worse without warning."
"The wind could pick up again out of nowhere."
"Rain could start again at any time."
"Conditions could deteriorate suddenly."
```

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Can we go outside yet?
B: The forecast says the worst is over.
A: Are you sure?
B: Well, the wind could pick up again, so we should be careful.
A: That's what Mom said.
B: Smart woman. Better safe than sorry.
```

**Original Korean:** "한번 중독되면 계속 하고 싶어진다"

| "I'm addicted." | 중독됐다 | 더 강한 표현 |
| "I can't get enough." | 포만할 줄 모른다 | 욕망 강조 |
| "I keep wanting more." | 계속 더 원한다 | 반복적 욕망 |

**Addiction Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean | Formality |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| "hooked" | 중독되다 | Casual |
| "addicted" | 중독된 | Neutral |
| "obsessed" | 집착하는 | Strong |
| "dependent on" | 의존하는 | Formal |
| "can't live without" | 없이는 살 수 없다 | Emotional |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```

```

## 3.2 Comparison & Inadequacy

### "But it wasn't the same as running."

**Original Korean:** "하지만 진짜 뛰는 것과는 달랐다"

**Grammar Point:** "It wasn't the same as [noun/gerund]"

**"Same" vs "Different" Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "It was the same." | 같았다 |
| "It wasn't the same." | 같지 않았다 |
| "It was different." | 달랐다 |
| "It wasn't quite the same." | 거의 같지만 약간 다르다 |
| "Nothing compared to..." | ~와는 비교가 안 된다 |
| "Nowhere near as good as..." | ~만큼 좋지 않다 |

**Disappointment Expressions:**

| Expression | Tone |
|------------|------|
| "It wasn't the same." | 객관적 |
| "It wasn't good enough." | 불만족 |
| "It left me wanting more." | 아쉬움 |
| "It didn't cut it." | 만족하지 못함 |
| "It didn't live up to my expectations." | 기대 미달 |
