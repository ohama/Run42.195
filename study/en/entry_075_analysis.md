# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Starting the Day - Race Preparation

### "My eyes opened. Before the alarm even went off."

**Original Korean:** "눈을 떴다. 알람이 울리기도 전에."

**Analysis:**
- 두 개의 짧은 문장으로 긴장과 기대감 표현
- "before the alarm went off" = 알람이 울리기도 전에 (미리 깬 것 강조)

**Similar Morning Awakening Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Enthusiasm Level |
|------------|---------|------------------|
| "I woke up naturally." | 자연스럽게 깬 거 | ★★☆ |
| "My eyes opened." | 눈을 떴어 | ★★☆ |
| "I woke up before my alarm." | 알람 전에 깼어 | ★★★ |
| "I couldn't sleep. I was awake all night." | 잠 못 잤어 | ★★★★★ |

**Race Day Preparation Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean | Context |
|---------|--------|---------|
| wake up naturally | 자연스럽게 깨어나다 | 충분히 휴식한 경우 |
| set alarm | 알람을 맞추다 | 일반적 |
| sleep through the night | 밤새 자다 | 깊은 수면 |
| toss and turn | 뒹굴다 | 잠을 설친다 |

---

**Original Korean:** "가볍게 먹었다"

**Grammar Point:** Elliptical expression (주어와 동사 생략)

**Full form:** "I kept my breakfast light." → "Kept it light."

**Food Amount Expressions for Race Day:**

| Expression | Meaning | Amount |

| "Ate nothing." | 아무것도 안 먹음 | ★☆☆ |
| "Kept it light." | 가볍게 먹음 | ★★☆ |
| "Had a normal breakfast." | 평소처럼 먹음 | ★★★ |
| "Ate a lot." | 많이 먹음 | ★★★★ |

**Pre-Race Nutrition Advice:**

```

```

**Original Korean:** "긴장되지?"

**Question + Statement Pattern:** 확인과 공감을 동시에

**Father's Reassurance Phrases:**

**Original Korean:** "민서가 있었다"

**Simple Statement - Big Emotional Impact**

이 짧은 문장이 얼마나 중요한 순간인지 보여줌. 친구의 존재 = 정서적 지지

## 1.4 Support & Encouragement

### "You got this!"

**Original Korean:** "넌 할 수 있어!"

**Casual Encouragement - Very Common**

| Expression | Intensity | Formality |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| "You got this." | ★★★ | Very casual |
| "You can do it." | ★★★ | Casual |
| "You're doing great." | ★★★ | Encouraging |
| "Keep going!" | ★★★ | Urgent |
| "You're almost there!" | ★★★★ | Near finish |

**"Oppa" Cultural Reference:**

**Original Korean:** "오빠, 화이팅!" (from Minseo's sister)

- "Oppa" = older brother (누나가 오빠 부를 때 친밀한 표현)
- "화이팅" = "Fighting!" (Korean English, 화파이팅 = fighting, go for it!)

**English Equivalents:**
- "You got this!"
- "Go, oppa!"
- "Come on, you can do it!"

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS - RUNNING & EXERTION (신체 감각 - 달리기 표현)

## 2.1 Heart & Breathing During Race

### "My heart was pounding."

**Original Korean:** "심장이 철렁 내려앉으면서 쿵쿵거렸다"

**Vocabulary Deep Dive:**

**Pound /paʊnd/**
- 원래 의미: 두드리다, 내려치다
- 운동 때: 심장이 크게 뛰다

**Heart Rate Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "My heart was beating." | ★☆☆ | 정상 |
| "My heart was racing." | ★★★ | 불안/흥분 |
| "My heart was pounding." | ★★★★ | 극도의 흥분/두려움 |
| "My heart was about to explode." | ★★★★★ | 매우 극적 |

**Related Physical Symptoms:**

| Symptom | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| 심장 뛰기 | "heart pounding" |
| 호흡 가쁘기 | "breathing hard" |
| 손 떨기 | "hands shaking" |
| 냉땀 나기 | "cold sweat" |

---

### "My legs are heavy. My breathing's ragged."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 무겁다. 호흡이 가쁘다."

**Fatigue Descriptions - Fragment Listing**

두 개의 독립적 증상을 나열해 힘든 상태를 강조

**Leg Fatigue Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "My legs feel tired." | ★★☆ | 약간의 피로 |
| "My legs feel heavy." | ★★★ | 상당한 피로 |
| "My legs feel like lead." | ★★★★ | 극도의 피로 |
| "My legs aren't listening anymore." | ★★★★★ | 지쳐서 반응 없음 |

**Breathing During Exertion:**

| Expression | Intensity | Status |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| "breathing normally" | ★☆☆ | 편함 |
| "breathing heavily" | ★★☆ | 운동 중 |
| "breathing hard" | ★★★ | 힘든 운동 |
| "my breathing's ragged" | ★★★★ | 매우 피곤 |
| "gasping for air" | ★★★★★ | 거의 한계 |

---

### "Gasping for breath."

**Original Korean:** "숨을 헐떡거리며"

**Onomatopoeia - Sound of Effort**

"Gasp" = 갑자기 숨을 쉬는 소리 (반사적 반응)

**After Crossing Finish Line:**

```
"I stopped.
Gasping for breath.
My legs were shaking.
But I was smiling."
```

이 연속적 단문장이 완주의 혼합된 감정을 잘 표현

## 2.2 Physical Struggle During Race

**Original Korean:** "다리가 말을 안 듣는다"

**Personification - Legs as Separate Entity**

신체가 마음의 지시를 따르지 않음을 표현 (의인화)

**Extreme Fatigue Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "My legs weren't listening." | 다리가 말을 안 듣는다 |
| "My body was shutting down." | 몸이 멈춰가고 있었다 |
| "I couldn't feel my legs." | 다리가 안 느껴졌다 |
| "Every step hurt." | 매 발걸음이 고통스러웠다 |
| "I was running on empty." | 기력이 다 떨어졌다 |

---

### "My legs were shaking."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 떨리고 있었다"

**Exhaustion vs Emotion:**

| Context | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| During intense exercise | 근육 피로 (lactic acid buildup) |
| After finishing | 정서적 방출 (emotional release) |
| From cold | 추위 반응 |
| From fear | 공포 반응 |

**In this story:** 마라톤 완주 직후는 신체 피로 + 정서적 혼합

## 2.3 Tempo & Pace Control

### "6:30 pace."

**Original Korean:** "분당 6분 30초"

**Running Terminology:**

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| pace | 분당 시간 (시간/거리) | "6:30 pace" |
| tempo | 달리기 속도 | "steady tempo" |

| cadence | 스트라이드 빈도 (분당 발걸음 수) | "170 cadence" |

**Pace Progression in Story:**

| Distance | Time | Pace | Status |
|----------|------|------|--------|
| 5km | 33 min | 6:30/km | On schedule |
| 10km | 1:05 | 6:30/km | Same as 10km race |
| 15km | 1:37 | Slowing | Unknown territory |
| 17km | 1:58 | Slower | Previous limit |
| 20km | 2:18 | 7:00/km | One more km |
| 21.0975km | 2:24:38 | - | **Finished** |

### "Getting excited and running too fast—that's how you blow up."

**Original Korean:** "흥분해서 빨리 달리면 그렇게 터진다"

**"Blow up" Phrasal Verb:**

**Structure:** that's how you + verb (원인-결과 설명)

**Race Strategy Vocabulary:**

| Expression | Meaning | Result |
|------------|---------|--------|
| "blow up" | 번아웃되다/체력 다 떨어지다 | 후반부 힘 없음 |

| "burn out" | 타버리다 | 체력 소진 |
| "fade" | 체력 떨어지다 | 점진적 약해짐 |

**Coach's Wisdom:**

```
"First 5km nice and easy."
"Like Coach said."
"Getting excited and running too fast—
that's how you blow up."
```

전문적 조언을 정리한 대사

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "I was [emotion]. After [distance], [realization]."

**From Text:** "My legs were heavy. After 17km, my legs weren't working right."

**Structure:** I was + emotion. After + distance + comma + consequence.

| Example | Progress |
|---------|----------|
| "I was confident. After 15km, I struggled." | 심리 변화 |
| "I was tired. After 20km, I was exhausted." | 피로 누적 |
| "I was strong. After 18km, I hit the wall." | 갑작스런 변화 |

---

## Pattern 2: "One step. One step. Keep going."

**From Text:** (19km에서)

**Repetition for Determination - Mantra Style**

같은 단어 반복으로 꾸준함과 결연함 표현

**Inner Monologue Mantras:**

| Mantra | Context |
|--------|---------|
| "One step. One step." | 앞으로 나아가기 |
| "Just keep moving." | 계속 진행 |
| "Don't stop. Don't stop." | 멈추지 말기 |
| "Almost there. Almost there." | 끝에 가까움 |
| "You can. You can." | 자기 격려 |

---

## Pattern 3: "At [distance]km, [thought]. [But response]."

**From Text:** "At 18km, 'Should I just stop?' The thought flashed through my head. [But] No. I came this far."

**Structure:** Crisis moment → Recovery moment

| Km | Thought | Response |
|----|---------|----------|
| 18km | "Should I just stop?" | "No. I came this far." |
| 20km | "One more kilometer" | "Just one more." |
| 결승 | (암묵적 극복) | (달리기 완주) |

**Internal Conflict Resolution:**

```
Temptation: "Maybe I should quit."
Reason: "Eight months of training."
Motivation: "I can't give up now."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "The [part of body] that [past reference]."

**From Text:** "The me from March who got carsick on the bus. The me who couldn't even walk 7km."

**Structure:** The [noun] that [past detail] + verb (comparison)

| Past Self | Current Achievement |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "got carsick on the bus" | ran 21km |
| "couldn't even walk 7km" | completed marathon |

**Self-Transformation Narrative:**

```
"The me from March who got carsick on the bus.
The me who couldn't even walk 7km.
That me just ran 21km."
```

극적인 변화를 강조하는 평행 구조

---

## Pattern 5: "[Time period]. [Challenges]. But [determination]."

**From Text:** "Eight months. A lot happened. Ran in the rain. Ran in the heat. Hit a slump. But I didn't give up."

**Structure:** Duration + list of obstacles + final resolution

**Challenge Summary:**

```
"Eight months.
A lot happened.
Ran in the rain.
Ran in the heat.
Hit a slump.
But I didn't give up.
That's how I got here."
```

과정의 어려움과 인내를 병렬로 나열

---

## Pattern 6: "The [noun] feels [adjective]. [Explanation]."

**From Text:** "The finisher's medal feels heavy. Good heavy."

**Structure:** Emotional paradox - 부정적 용어 + 긍정적 의미

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "feels heavy" (물리적) | weighs a lot |
| "feels heavy" (정서적) | 의미 있다/중요하다 |
| "Good heavy" | 좋은 의미의 무거움 |

**Weight as Metaphor:**

```
"The finisher's medal feels heavy.
Good heavy."
```

This paradox captures the complexity of achievement - physical weight = emotional significance

## 6.2 Relief & Accomplishment

### "I did it."

**Original Korean:** "해냈다"

**Simple but Profound**

세 단어로 8개월의 여정을 압축

**Achievement Expressions - Intensity Progression:**

| Expression | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| "I finished." | 객관적 완주 |
| "I did it." | 개인적 성취감 |
| "I made it." | 힘든 과정 극복 |
| "I conquered it." | 강한 의지 |

**Repeated Affirmation:**

```
"*I did it.*" (첫 번째 - 순간의 기쁨)
"I did it." (마지막 - 반성적 확인)
"I did it." (맨 끝 - 최종 확인)
```

## 6.4 Reflection & Integration

### "It was hard."

**Original Korean:** "힘들었다"

**Honest Acknowledgment**

부정하지 않고 인정하는 성숙함

**Difficulty Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "It was hard." | 힘들었다 |
| "It was brutal." | 극도로 힘들었다 |
| "It was tougher than expected." | 예상보다 힘들었다 |
| "But I did it anyway." | 그렇지만 해냈다 |

### "Good heavy."

**Original Korean:** "좋은 무거움"

**Paradoxical Emotion - Complexity**

무거움이 긍정적 의미 (책임, 성취, 의미)

**Weights of Life:**

```
"The finisher's medal feels heavy.
Good heavy." = 8개월의 무게
```

물리적 무게가 정서적/정신적 무게를 대표

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

## 7.1 Fragmentary Writing Style

**Why Fragments?**
- 강조 효과
- 생각의 빠른 흐름
- 감정의 직접 표현
- 피로의 느낌 전달

| Fragment | Full Sentence | Effect |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| "Clear. Cold." | "The weather was clear and cold." | 직접성 |
| "My heart was going crazy." | "I was extremely nervous." | 감정 전달 |
| "Five a.m." | "It was five a.m." | 시간 강조 |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Pre-Race Confidence Building

```
Dad: Nervous?
Doyun: Yeah.
Dad: You got this. I'll be waiting at the finish line.
Doyun: Thanks, Dad.
Mom: (later) You did great. I'm so proud.
Doyun: Thank you. It was hard.
Mom: But you did it.
Doyun: I did.
```

---

### Scenario 2: During the Race - Internal Dialogue

```
(15km mark)
"I'm in unknown territory. I've trained up to 17km, but never past that."

(18km mark)
*Should I just stop?*
"No. I came this far. Eight months of training. I can't give up now."

(20km mark)
*One more kilometer. Just one more.*
"I can do this. The finish line is close."
```

### Scenario 3: After Finishing - Friends & Family

```
Friend: Yo! You finished!

Doyun: Yeah. After 18km, it got really hard.

Doyun: It was. The hardest thing I've ever done.
Friend: You should be proud.
Doyun: I am.
```
