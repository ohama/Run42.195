## Extended Practice Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Confirming Plans
```
Context: A parent checking in about their child's marathon preparation

Parent: "When did you say the race was again?"
Child: "March 2nd, Mom. Less than a month left."
Parent: "That's exciting. Do you feel ready?"
Child: "Yeah, I'm preparing hard. My body feels great."

```

### Dialogue 2: School Conversation
```
Context: Friends discussing athletic training

Friend: "You seem less busy these days. Everything okay?"
Doyun: "Yeah, totally. I'm doing something called tapering."
Friend: "Tapering? What's that?"
Doyun: "You reduce your distance before the race. You need to rest your body so you can run well on race day."

```

### Dialogue 3: Weekly Check-in
```
Context: Grandfather and grandson discussing the upcoming marathon

Grandfather: "How's your running going, son?"
Doyun: "I'm in the final month now. February has begun, and the race is just 27 days away."
Grandfather: "Are you managing okay?"

```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correction | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| "February is beginning" | "February has begun" | We use "has begun" to show the process is already underway |
| "My body feels well" | "My body feels great" | "Well" is for adverbs of manner; "great" is for states of being |
| "I'm preparing hardly" | "I'm preparing hard" | "Hardly" means "scarcely"; "hard" means "with effort" |
| "The race is less a month away" | "Less than a month left" | Requires "than" in comparative construction |
| "I'm root for you" | "I'm rooting for you" | Verb form needs gerund (-ing form) after "am" |

## Detailed Analysis - Root For

```
PHRASAL VERB: "root for"
Literal meaning: To cheer with roots (metaphorical)
Usage context: Supporting someone's effort
Intensity spectrum:
  ★☆☆☆☆ "I like what you're doing"
  ★★☆☆☆ "I'm supporting you"
  ★★★☆☆ "I'm cheering for you" / "I'm rooting for you"
  ★★★★☆ "I'm incredibly invested in your success"
  ★★★★★ "I'm completely backing you all the way"

Similar verbs:
- "cheer for" - similar meaning, more literal reference to cheering
- "support" - broader, less enthusiastic
- "back" - more formal, business-like
- "encourage" - focuses on giving courage

Example sentences:
1. "My grandpa is rooting for me in the marathon."
2. "Everyone at school is rooting for Doyun to finish the race."
3. "I'm rooting for you to do well!"
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Confirming Information

### Structure
```
Q: "When did you say [topic] was again?"
A: "[Time reference]"
Q: "[Comment about time/distance]"
A: "Yeah, [additional context]"
```

### Full Example from Text
```
Grandfather: "When did you say the race was again?"
Doyun: "March 2nd!"
Grandfather: "Less than a month left."
Doyun: "Yeah. I'm preparing hard."
```

### Pattern Variations
| Pattern Variation | Example |
|---|---|
| Neutral confirmation | "When is the race again?" |
| Expressing surprise | "Already? When did you say it was?" |
| Casual confirmation | "So when's the race again?" |
| Respectful inquiry | "If you don't mind, when did you say the race was?" |

### 5+ Practice Examples
1. **Parent to Child**: "When did you say the test was again?" / "Friday." / "That's soon!" / "Yeah, I'm studying hard."
2. **Coach to Athlete**: "When did you say the competition was again?" / "Next month." / "That's great timing!" / "Yeah, I'm training hard."
3. **Friend to Friend**: "When did you say your birthday was again?" / "Next week." / "Oh exciting!" / "Yeah, I'm looking forward to it."
4. **Coworker to Coworker**: "When did you say the deadline was again?" / "Friday." / "That's pretty close." / "Yeah, I'm finishing up soon."
5. **Student to Classmate**: "When did you say the project was due again?" / "Next Wednesday." / "That's soon." / "Yeah, we're almost done."

## Pattern 2: Explaining Concepts

### Structure
```
Q: "What is [unfamiliar concept]?"
A: "[Definition] + [Explanation of purpose]"
```

### Full Example from Text
```
Minseo: "Tapering?"
Doyun: "You reduce your distance before the race."
Minseo: (implied understanding)
Doyun: "You have to rest your body so you can run well on race day."
```

### Pattern Analysis
| Step | Component | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge confusion | "Tapering?" |
| 2 | Simple definition | "You reduce your distance before the race" |
| 3 | Purpose explanation | "So you can run well on race day" |
| 4 | Full reason chain | "You have to rest your body..." |

### 5+ Practice Examples

1. **About Interval Training**:
   - Q: "What's interval training?"
   - A: "It's when you alternate between fast and slow running."
   - Follow-up: "You do this to build speed and endurance."

2. **About Cross-Training**:
   - Q: "Cross-training?"
   - A: "It means doing different types of exercise besides your main sport."
   - Follow-up: "This helps prevent injuries and build overall fitness."

3. **About Carbo-Loading**:
   - Q: "Why are you eating so much pasta?"
   - A: "It's called carbo-loading. You eat extra carbs before a big race."
   - Follow-up: "This gives your muscles fuel for the long distance."

4. **About Recovery Days**:
   - Q: "Why aren't you running today?"
   - A: "Today is a recovery day. I rest between hard workouts."
   - Follow-up: "Your muscles need time to repair and get stronger."

5. **About Peak Performance**:
   - Q: "What do you mean by 'peaking'?"
   - A: "Peaking means reaching your best condition at exactly the right time."
   - Follow-up: "Athletes time their training so they're strongest on race day."
