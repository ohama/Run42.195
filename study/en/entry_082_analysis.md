# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Weather & Seasonal Descriptions

### "Overcast. Snow in the forecast."

**Original Korean:** "흐림. 날씨 예보에 눈이 난다."

**Grammar Point:** Fragment/telegram style opening (짧고 사실적인 서술)

**Analysis:**
- Complete sentences 대신 fragment 사용
- 날씨 보도문처럼 간결한 표현
- 시간의 흐름과 계절감 설정

**Weather Forecast Vocabulary:**

| Expression | Meaning | Formality |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "It's overcast." | 흐려요 | Neutral |
| "Snow in the forecast." | 눈이 올 예정 | Casual/written |
| "Clear skies." | 맑은 하늘 | Descriptive |
| "Rain expected." | 비 올 예정 | Neutral |
| "Sunny and mild." | 맑고 따뜻해 | Descriptive |

**Variations:**
- "The forecast calls for snow." (정식)
- "They're predicting snow." (일상)
- "Snow is coming." (간결)

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's the weather like tomorrow?
B: Overcast. Snow in the forecast.
A: How cold?
B: Minus 5 degrees.
A: I'll need to bundle up.
```

### "It was cold. Minus 5 degrees."

**Original Korean:** "추웠다. 영하 5도."

**Temperature Expressions:**

| Temperature | Expression | Feeling |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| Below -10°C | "Bone-chilling" | 뼛속까지 추움 |
| -5 to -10°C | "Freezing" | 엄청 추움 |
| 0 to -5°C | "Very cold" | 매우 추움 |
| 5 to 10°C | "Cold" | 춥다 |
| 10 to 15°C | "Chilly/Cool" | 쌀쌀하다/시원하다 |

**How to Say Temperature:**
- "It was minus 5 degrees." (영하 5도였다)
- "It's 5 degrees below zero." (영하 5도다)
- "Negative 5." (영하 5도 - very casual)

**Running in Cold Weather:**
```
"7km run to school. It was cold. Minus 5 degrees."
= 학교까지 7km 달렸는데 엄청 추웠다. 영하 5도였다.
```

## 1.2 Year-End Phrases

### "The year's ending soon."

**Original Korean:** "한 해가 끝나간다."

**Time Reference Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|

| New Year | Year-End |

## 1.3 Gratitude & Thank You Expressions

### "Sir, thank you for everything this year."

**Original Korean:** "선생님, 올해 감사했습니다."

**Formal Gratitude in English:**

| Expression | Context | Formality |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "Thank you." | 감사해 | Basic |
| "Thank you very much." | 많이 감사해 | More formal |
| "Thank you for everything." | 모든 것 감사해 | Warm |
| "I appreciate everything you've done." | 당신이 한 모든 게 고마워 | Genuine |
| "Thank you for everything this year." | 올해 모든 것 감사해 | Seasonal |

**To Teachers/Authority Figures:**

| English | Korean | Situation |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| "Thank you, sir." | "선생님, 감사합니다." | Formal/respectful |
| "I appreciate your guidance." | "당신의 지도가 고마워요." | Professional |
| "You've helped me so much." | "정말 많이 도와줬어요." | Warm/informal |
| "I couldn't have done it without you." | "당신이 없었으면 못 했을 거 같아요." | Sincere |

**Response to Gratitude:**
- "What are you talking about? You did great."
- "It was my pleasure."
- "You did the work."
- "You're very welcome."

**Natural Dialogue:**
```
Student: Sir, thank you for everything this year.
Teacher: What are you talking about? You did great.
Student: Because you helped me.
Teacher: Let's prepare together next year too.
Student: Yes, sir!
```

**Cultural Note:** 한국식 존댓말을 영어로 표현할 때, "sir"는 남성 교사에게, "ma'am"는 여성 교사에게 사용. 하지만 친구 같은 관계면 이름으로 부르거나 "Mr./Ms. [성]"이라고 부름.

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running & Athletic Expressions

### "7km run to school"

**Original Korean:** "학교까지 7km를 달렸다"

**Distance + Running:**

| Expression | Distance | Speed | Meaning |
|------------|----------|-------|---------|
| "a quick jog" | 1-3km | Slow | 가벼운 조깅 |
| "a short run" | 3-5km | Medium | 짧은 달리기 |
| "a 7km run" | 7km | Medium | 7km 달리기 |
| "a half marathon" | 21.1km | Fast | 하프 마라톤 |
| "a full marathon" | 42.195km | Sustained | 풀 마라톤 |

**Ways to Say It:**
- "I ran 7km to school." (I ran TO school)
- "My run to school was 7km." (The run WAS 7km)
- "7km run to school." (Fragment - casual)
- "I covered 7km getting to school." (Formal)

**Running Routine Vocabulary:**

| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| "morning run" | 아침 달리기 | 운동 시간 |
| "route" | 노선 | 어느 길로 달릴지 |
| "pace" | 속도 | 빠르기 |
| "distance" | 거리 | 얼마나 멀리 |
| "endurance" | 지구력 | 오래 달리는 능력 |

### "While I was running, I thought about the year."

**Original Korean:** "달리면서 한 해를 생각했다"

**"While + -ing" Pattern:**

**Structure:** While + -ing, [main action]

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| "While I was running, I thought about the year." | 달리면서 한 해를 생각했다 |
| "While eating, I made a plan." | 먹으면서 계획을 세웠다 |
| "While walking home, I realized something." | 집에 걸어가면서 뭔가 깨달았다 |
| "While studying, I fell asleep." | 공부하다가 잠들었다 |

**Variations:**
- "As I was running, I thought about the year."
- "I ran while thinking about the year."
- "Running gave me time to think about the year."

**Reflective Activities Pattern:**
```
While + [activity], I [thought/realized/remembered]
```

**Examples:**
- "While running, I thought about my friends."
- "While showering, I realized the truth."
- "While waiting, I remembered something."

### "The kid who used to ride the bus became the kid who runs 21km."

**Original Korean:** "버스를 타던 아이가 21km를 달리는 아이가 되었다"

**Transformation Pattern:**

**Structure:** The kid who [used to + verb] became the kid who [now + verb]

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "The kid who used to X became the kid who now Y." | X했던 아이가 Y하는 아이가 되었다 |

**Examples:**
```
"The girl who used to be shy became the girl who speaks confidently."
= 수줍던 여자가 자신감 있게 말하는 여자가 되었다

"The man who used to hate running became the man who runs marathons."
= 달리기 싫어하던 남자가 마라톤을 달리는 남자가 되었다

"The student who used to fail became the student who excels."
= 떨어지던 학생이 잘하는 학생이 되었다
```

**"Used to" Grammar:**

| Verb Form | Meaning | Tense |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| "used to ride the bus" | 예전에 타곤 했다 | Past habit |
| "used to be afraid" | 원래 두려워했다 | Past state |
| "used to hate running" | 예전에 싫어했다 | Past feeling |

**Contrast Structure:**
- "The bus kid → the running kid"
- "The anxious boy → the confident boy"
- "The sedentary teen → the athlete"

# PART 3: DESCRIBING PERSONAL JOURNEYS (개인적 여정 묘사)

## 3.1 Timeline & Progress Expressions

### "March. It started with bus sickness."

**Original Korean:** "3월. 버스 멀미로 시작했다."

**Chronological Narrative:**

| Structure | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Month + Fragment | "March. It started..." | Simple, clear |
| Month + Complete sentence | "In March, it started..." | More flowing |
| "By March" | "By March, I had..." | Passage of time |
| "Since March" | "Since March, everything changed." | Duration |

**Why Fragment Style Works:**
- Creates rhythm in storytelling
- Emphasizes each milestone
- Feels like a diary entry
- Builds momentum

**Progress Markers - Year Divided by Months:**

| Month | Event | Progress |
|-------|-------|----------|
| March | "bus sickness" | Problem identified |
| April | "Walking became a habit" | Solution found |
| May | "I started running" | Major change |
| June | "I bought running shoes" | Commitment |
| July | "A slump hit" | Setback |
| August | "Signed up for 10km race" | Goal set |
| Sept-Oct | "Completed my first race" | Goal achieved |
| November | "Finished a half marathon" | Progress |
| December | "Decided on a full marathon" | Next goal |

**Narrative Pattern:**
```
Problem (March) → Solution (April-May) → Challenge (July) →
Success (Sept-Oct) → Growth (Nov) → New Goal (Dec)
```

---

### "In nine months."

**Original Korean:** "9개월 만에."

**Time Period Expressions:**

| Time | Expression | Emphasis |
|------|------------|----------|
| Short (weeks) | "In just a few weeks" | 짧은 시간 강조 |
| Medium (months) | "In nine months" | 구체적 기간 |
| Longer (year) | "Over the course of a year" | 긴 과정 |
| Specific | "From March to December" | 정확한 기간 |

**"In + time period" for Achievement:**
```
"In nine months, I ran a marathon."
= 9개월 동안 마라톤을 뛸 수 있게 되었다
```

**Variations:**
- "In just nine months" (짧은 시간 강조)
- "Over nine months" (긴 과정)
- "Within nine months" (기간 내)
- "Within the span of nine months" (문학적)

| Verb | Meaning | Nuance |
|------|---------|--------|

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "What's your best memory from this year?"

**Original Korean:** "올해 최고의 추억이 뭐예요?"

**Memory/Reflection Questions:**

| Question | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "What's your best memory from this year?" | 가장 좋은 추억 |
| "What stands out most to you?" | 가장 기억에 남는 것 |
| "What was the highlight?" | 최고의 순간 |
| "Do you have any regrets?" | 후회는 있어? |
| "What changed the most?" | 가장 많이 바뀐 게 뭐? |

**Answer Patterns:**

| Answer | Why | Nuance |
|--------|-----|--------|
| "Running." | 활동으로 답함 | Direct/simple |
| "Meeting you." | 사람으로 답함 | Heartfelt |
| "Completing the race." | 성취로 답함 | Proud |
| "Getting stronger." | 개인 성장 | Reflective |

```
A: What's your best memory from this year?

```

## Pattern 3: "Figure." / "Thanks."

**Original Korean:** "그럼 당연하지." / "고마워."

**One-Word Responses:**

| Response | Meaning | Context |
|----------|---------|---------|
| "Figure." | 당연해 (that makes sense) | Answer explanation |
| "Thanks." | 고마워 | Appreciation |
| "Sure." | 응/좋아 | Agreement |
| "Yeah." | 응 | Confirmation |
| "Right." | 맞아 | Agreement |

**Extended Dialogue:**
```
A: "Running."
B: "Figure." [Of course running makes sense for a runner]
A: "What about you?"
B: "Running with you."
A: "Thanks."
```

**Why Short Answers Work:**
- Reflects natural friendship speech
- Shows comfort/no need for formality
- Moves conversation forward
- Shows understanding without explaining

## 6.1 Pride & Achievement

### "You did great."

**Original Korean:** "너 정말 잘했어."

**Achievement Recognition:**

| Expression | Strength | Formality |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "Good job." | ★☆☆ | Casual |

| "You did great." | ★★★ | Warm |
| "You did an amazing job." | ★★★★ | Enthusiastic |
| "I'm proud of you." | ★★★★★ | Most personal |

**Different Contexts:**

| Situation | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| Quick praise | "Good job." |
| Actual achievement | "You did great." |
| Overcoming difficulty | "I'm proud of you." |
| Surprising success | "You did an amazing job." |

**Dialogue:**
```
Student: I worked hard this year.
Teacher: You did great. I'm proud of you.
Student: Thank you, sir.
```

**Original Korean:** "많이 바뀌었어."

**Personal Transformation Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

## 7.1 Diary Entry Style (일기체 특징)

### Fragment Opening: "Overcast. Snow in the forecast."

**Why Fragments Matter:**
- 일기는 완전한 문장 필요 없음
- 감정과 상황을 빠르게 전달
- 독자가 함께 발견하는 느낌
- 시간의 흐름을 간결하게

**Diary-Style Fragments:**

| Fragment | Full Sentence | Effect |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| "Overcast." | "It was overcast." | 사실만 기록 |
| "7km run." | "I ran 7km." | 객관적 기록 |
| "Cold." | "It was very cold." | 감정 강조 |
| "A slump hit." | "I experienced a slump." | 극적 표현 |

---

### Fragment Lists: "March. It started with bus sickness. April. Walking became a habit."

**Year-End Summary Style:**

| Structure | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Month + Event | Timeline 설정 | "March. Bus sickness." |
| Month + Change | 진행 표시 | "May. I started running." |
| Month + Achievement | 성공 기록 | "October. Completed my first race." |

**Bullet-Point Narrative:**
```
March - problem identified
April - solution attempted
May - major commitment
June - investment in gear
July - reality check (slump)
August - new goal (race)
September-October - first success
November - bigger goal
December - ultimate goal
```

## 7.3 Short, Punchy Statements

### "It started with running. It will end with running."

**Circular Structure (순환 구조):**

| Pattern | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| "It started with X. It will end with X." | 완성감 + 깨달음 |
| "From nothing to everything." | 극적 변화 |
| "Beginning to end." | 순환 |

**Thematic Bookending:**

```
Beginning: "It started with bus sickness."
Middle: "Walking became a habit... I started running..."
End: "It will end with running."

The theme returns, but transformed.
Problem → Solution → Passion
```

**Power of Repetition:**
- "It started with..."
- "It will end with..."
- Creates poetic closure
- Shows how running changed everything
