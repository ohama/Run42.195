# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Movement Expressions

### "I ran to school and the fog was thick."

**Original Korean:** "뛰어서 등교하는데 안개가 끼어 있었다."

**Analysis:**
- 일상적인 행동 표현
- 주어진 상황을 자연스럽게 설정
- Running (달리기)는 이미 일과

**Related Transportation/Movement Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I ran to school" | 뛰어서 등교했다 | 달려서 학교에 감 |
| "I rode the bus home" | 버스 타고 집에 갔다 | 버스로 돌아감 |
| "I got to school" | 학교 도착했다 | 도착 완료 |
| "I headed out" | 나가다 | 떠남 |
| "I made my way through" | 지나가다 | 통과함 |

**Variations:**
- "I jogged to school this morning."
- "I took a run to school."
- "I headed to school on my run."

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: How do you get to school usually?
B: I run. It takes about 20 minutes.
A: Every day? That's impressive!
B: Yeah, I kind of enjoy it now.
```

### "Couldn't see more than ten meters ahead."

**Original Korean:** "앞이 잘 안 보였다. 10미터 앞이 뿌옇다."

**Vocabulary:**
- **Ten meters:** 10미터 (거리 표현)
- **Blurry:** 뿌연, 희미한

**Visibility Spectrum:**

| Expression | Distance | Condition |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "Crystal clear" | 무한 | 완전히 선명 |
| "Can see clearly" | 수백 미터 | 좋은 시야 |
| "Can see about 50 meters" | ~50m | 양호 |
| "Can barely see 20 meters" | ~20m | 악조건 |
| "Everything's blurry" | <10m | 매우 불명확 |
| "Can't see an inch ahead" | <1m | 최악 |

**Examples:**
```
"In the fog, I couldn't see more than ten meters ahead."
"The rain made everything blurry."
"I could barely see the road in front of me."
"Clear skies meant I could see for miles."
```

### "So I just ran slower."

**Original Korean:** "천천히 뛰었다."

**Pattern: So I [verb] [manner]**

**Structure:** So + I + simple past + adverb

**Examples:**

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Fog was thick | "So I just ran slower." |
| It was late | "So I just went to bed." |
| I was tired | "So I just took a break." |
| They weren't listening | "So I just stopped talking." |
| It was crowded | "So I just waited." |

**Casual Tone:**
- "I just ran slower" (matter-of-fact, practical)
- vs. "I decided to slow down" (more deliberate)
- vs. "I had to slow down" (forced)

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Didn't you get scared in the fog?
B: A little, but the roads were empty so I just ran slower.
A: That was smart. Safety first.
B: Yeah, no cars to worry about at 5:30 a.m.
```

**Structure:** No, seriously, + adjective/statement

**Examples:**

| Context | Expression |
|---------|------------|

| 장난이 아님을 강조 | "No, seriously, I mean it." |
| 진실을 주장 | "No, seriously, that's what happened." |
| 부인 후 재주장 | "No, seriously, it works." |

**Variations:**
- "Seriously, it's really beautiful."
- "I'm being serious—it's beautiful."
- "For real, it's gorgeous."
- "Honestly, it's stunning."

**Intensifiers with "seriously":**
- "Seriously, it's actually amazing."
- "No joke, it's beautiful."
- "I'm not kidding, it's gorgeous."

### "k" / "okay"

**Original Korean:** "ㅇㅋ"

**Minimal Acknowledgment:**

| Text | Full Word | Tone | When to Use |
|------|-----------|------|-------------|
| "k" | okay | Very casual | 친한 친구 |
| "ok" | okay | Casual | 일반적 |
| "okay" | okay | Standard | 중립적 |
| "sure" | - | Friendly | 동의 |
| "yup" | - | Casual | 빠른 동의 |
| "sounds good" | - | Warm | 긍정 |

**Tone Consideration:**
- "k" - Can seem dismissive if not context-appropriate
- "ok" - Neutral, safe
- "Sure" - Friendly and warm
- "Sounds good" - Most enthusiastic

**Practice:**
```
A: Let's go Saturday.
B: k
vs.
A: Let's go Saturday.
B: Sounds good! Can't wait.
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Sharing a Discovery

```
A: Hey, I found this amazing place on my route.
B: Really? Where?
A: There's a silver grass field at the top of a hill.
B: Wait, where I've been passing for months?
A: Exactly! You can't see it from the bus though.
B: No way. That sounds incredible.
A: It is. The light in the morning was perfect.
B: You have to show me. When can we go?
A: How about Saturday?
B: Definitely. I want to see it.
```

### Scenario 2: Taking a Photo & Sharing

```
A: [takes photo] This came out really nice.
B: What is it?
A: The silver grass in the fog. I'm going to send it to Minseo.
B: That's beautiful. Send me one too?
A: Sure, I'll take another shot.
B: The light in the photo is amazing.
A: Yeah, the early morning light just before sunrise.
B: You've really become a photographer.
A: [laughs] No, I just see stuff when I'm running.
B: Well, you have a good eye for it.
```

### Scenario 3: Discussing the Beauty of Direct Experience

```
A: How was your trip to the field?
B: Better than I expected. Seeing it in person is so different.
A: Different how?
B: Photos don't capture it. The air, the smell, the feeling.
A: I know what you mean. You have to be there.
B: Exactly. It's something you experience, not just see.
A: Running through it would be even better.
B: Right? It's completely different from the bus.
A: That's why I love what Doyun's doing.
B: Me too. He's experiencing the world differently.
```

### Scenario 4: Friendship & Shared Moments

```
A: Thanks for coming with me today.
B: Of course! I'm glad we did this together.
A: It's better seeing it together, you know?
B: Definitely. It makes it more special.
A: That's what I thought. Moments like this...
B: Are better with friends. I get it.
A: Yeah. This is why I wanted to show you.
B: I appreciate that. Now I understand why you love running.
A: Right? You see things most people miss.
B: We should do this again next season.
```

## 10.3 Fill-in-the-Blank Exercises

### Exercise 1: Nature & Sensory Vocabulary

```
1. I couldn't see _____ than ten meters ahead in the fog.
   (a) more  (b) less  (c) longer  (d) further

2. The grass _____ like it was dancing in the wind.
   (a) moved  (b) swayed  (c) drifted  (d) turned

3. The silver grass flowers looked almost _____ in the mist.
   (a) unreal  (b) dreamlike  (c) surreal  (d) magical

4. I ran _____ the mandarin groves and up the hill.
   (a) through  (b) past  (c) along  (d) across

5. Cherry blossoms were _____ down like snow.
   (a) drifting  (b) falling  (c) floating  (d) scattering

Answers: 1.(d) further  2.(b) swayed  3.(b) dreamlike  4.(b) past  5.(a) drifting
```

### Exercise 2: Photography & Sharing Vocabulary

```
1. I _____ _____ my phone and took a photo.
   (두 단어: 핸드폰을 꺼내다)
   Answer: pulled out

2. I _____ the photo to Minseo right away.
   (보냈다)
   Answer: sent

3. "That photo is _____ beautiful," she said.
   (진짜로, 정말로)
   Answer: seriously

4. The light just _____ the sun rises is the best.
   (해가 뜨기 직전)
   Answer: before

5. I should _____ this with someone who understands.
   (공유하다, 나누다)
   Answer: share
```

### Exercise 3: Conversational Responses

```
1. "You've become a photographer."
   "_____, I just _____ stuff when I'm running."
   Answer: Nah, see

2. "There's a place like that near you?"
   "You can't _____ it from the _____."
   Answer: see, bus

3. "It's just a photo."
   "But the _____ doesn't _____ all of it."
   Answer: camera, catch

4. "You should show me that place."
   "_____ _____ Saturday?"
   Answer: Let's go, on Saturday (또는 Let's, go)

5. "Is it as beautiful as the photo?"
   "It's _____ _____ when you're there in _____."
   Answer: better, even/so, person
```

### Exercise 4: Verb Forms & Tenses

```
1. I _____ to school and the fog was thick. (Past tense of run)
   Answer: ran

2. After _____ the hill, I saw cosmos flowers. (Coming down)
   Answer: coming down

3. The _____ grass field was _____ in the fog. (Verb forms)
   Answer: silver, blooming

4. I _____ _____ my phone and took a photo. (Two verbs: 꺼내다)
   Answer: pulled out

5. It's _____ different from _____ on a bus. (Two verbs: 앉다)
   Answer: completely, sitting
```
