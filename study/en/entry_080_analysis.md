# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Asking for and Giving Instructions

### "In December, we're building your base fitness."

**Original Korean:** "12월에는 기초체력을 쌓는 거야."

**Analysis:**
- 지시/설명하는 표현
- 현재진행형으로 미래 계획 설명
- 원인/목표를 자연스럽게 제시

**Variations by Context:**

| Full Sentence | Purpose | Tone |
|---------------|---------|------|
| "In December, we're building your base fitness." | 첫 단계 설명 | 신중함 |
| "We'll gradually increase distance each week." | 진행 방식 설명 | 체계적 |
| "You need to do 30km or more." | 필수 요구사항 | 명확함 |
| "If your body hurts, rest." | 조건부 지시 | 배려 |

**Key Phrase: "Building base fitness"**
- Base fitness: 기초체력 (foundation for harder training)
- Build: 구축하다, 쌓다
- Similar: "build strength," "build endurance," "build stamina"

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Coach: In December, we're building your base fitness.
Athlete: How much should I run each day?
Coach: Gradually increase your long run distance each week.
Athlete: And then we move to distance training in January?
Coach: Exactly. That's when we seriously push it.
```

### "Gradually increase your long run distance each week."

**Original Korean:** "1주일에 한 번은 길게 뛰는데, 점차 거리를 늘려나가."

**Structure:** Adverb + Verb + Object + Time expression

**"Gradually" 패턴:**

| Expression | Meaning | Timeline |
|------------|---------|----------|
| "gradually increase" | 천천히 늘리다 | 몇 주 |
| "slowly build up" | 천천히 쌓다 | 점진적 |
| "progressively increase" | 단계별로 늘리다 | 계획적 |
| "step by step" | 한 번씩 | 매우 점진적 |

**Similar Training Instructions:**
- "We'll ramp up the intensity gradually."
- "Progress at your own pace."
- "Don't jump ahead—stick to the schedule."

**Important Context:**
운동 지도에서 "gradually"는 부상 방지를 위한 필수 원칙

## 1.2 Making Observations

### "If your body hurts, rest."

**Original Korean:** "몸이 아프면 쉬어."

**Conditional Structure:** If + condition, + imperative

**Simple Health Rules:**

| Condition | Action | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| If your body hurts | rest | recovery is important |
| If you feel dizzy | sit down | prevent fainting |
| If you can't breathe | stop | prevent injury |
| If you have pain | see a doctor | get professional help |

**Stronger Expressions:**
- "If anything hurts, you stop immediately."
- "At the first sign of pain, rest."
- "Pain is your body's warning signal—listen to it."

**Important Nuance:**
한국식 "무시하고 계속해" 문화와 달리, 영어권에서는 몸의 신호를 듣는 것이 강조됨

---

### "If you get injured, you'll have to skip the race."

**Original Korean:** "상처라도 입으면 대회를 못 가게 돼."

**Consequence Structure:** If + condition, + will/have to + consequence

**Cause & Effect Patterns:**

| If Clause | Result Clause | Severity |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| "If you get injured," | "you'll skip the race." | Very serious |
| "If you overtrain," | "you'll burn out." | Serious |
| "If you don't rest," | "you can't recover." | Serious |
| "If you push through pain," | "you'll make it worse." | Critical |

**Vocabulary: "Skip" vs "Miss" vs "Sit out"**

| Verb | Usage | Nuance |
|------|-------|--------|
| skip | 의도적으로/강제로 빠지다 | deliberate |
| miss | 참석하지 못하다 | unintentional |
| sit out | 참여 안 함 | neutral |

# PART 3: EXPRESSING EMOTIONS ABOUT GOALS (목표에 대한 감정)

## 3.1 Fear and Excitement Mixed

### "It's scary but also exciting."

**Original Korean:** "무섭지만 또 설렌다"

**"But" for Contrasting Emotions:**

| Emotion Pair | Intensity | Example |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| scared + excited | ★★★★ | "It's scary but also exciting." |
| nervous + confident | ★★★ | "I'm nervous but confident." |
| worried + hopeful | ★★★ | "I'm worried but hopeful." |
| tired + determined | ★★★ | "I'm tired but determined." |

**Emotion Spectrum - Fear Side:**

| Expression | Intensity | Nuance |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "It's concerning." | ★☆☆ | mildly worrying |
| "It's daunting." | ★★☆ | overwhelming but manageable |
| "It's scary." | ★★★ | frightening |
| "It's terrifying." | ★★★★ | very frightening |

**Emotion Spectrum - Excitement Side:**

| Expression | Intensity | Nuance |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "It's interesting." | ★☆☆ | mild curiosity |
| "It's exciting." | ★★☆ | anticipation |
| "It's thrilling." | ★★★ | intense anticipation |
| "It's exhilarating." | ★★★★ | euphoric excitement |

**"But also" Pattern:**
- 두 감정이 동시에 존재
- 상충되는 감정을 자연스럽게 표현
- "also"는 두 번째 감정을 추가로 강조

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Friend: Are you nervous about the marathon?
You: It's scary but also exciting.
Friend: How so?
You: I'm scared I won't finish, but I'm excited to try.
Friend: That's normal. You've got this.
```

## 3.2 Moving from Fear to Determination

### "I just have to follow it."

**Original Korean:** "그냥 따라가면 돼."

**Simplification through Trust:**

| Expression | Meaning | Nuance |
|------------|---------|--------|
| "I just have to follow it." | 따라가면 된다 | trust in the system |
| "I just need to stick to the plan." | 계획을 지키면 된다 | discipline |
| "I just have to show up." | 나타나면 된다 | commitment |
| "I just need to do the work." | 일하면 된다 | responsibility |

**"Just" for Minimization:**
- "Just" = 간단하게, 그냥
- 복잡한 상황을 단순하게 표현
- 심리적으로 부담을 덜어줌

**Motivation Pattern:**
1. Recognize the goal (42.195km)
2. Trust the system (follow the plan)
3. Break it down (one week at a time)
4. Execute (one step at a time)

---

### "One week at a time."

**Original Korean:** "일주일 단위로"

**Breaking Down Goals - Chunking Strategy:**

| Timeframe | Strategy | Benefit |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| "one week at a time" | 주간 단위 | manageable |
| "one day at a time" | 일일 단위 | very immediate |
| "one month at a time" | 월간 단위 | broader view |
| "one race at a time" | 레이스 단위 | focused energy |

**Related Expressions:**
- "I'm not thinking about the whole marathon—just this week."
- "Focus on the immediate training, not the final distance."
- "Take it step by step."
- "Break it into smaller chunks."

**Psychology Behind This:**
마라톤 42.195km는 압도적이므로, 일주일씩 나누면 관리 가능해짐

---

### "One step at a time."

**Original Korean:** "한 발씩"

**Metaphorical Language for Progress:**

| Expression | Literal | Figurative |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "one step at a time" | 한 발씩 | 천천히 진행 |
| "one mile at a time" | 1마일씩 | 일정 거리씩 |
| "one breath at a time" | 숨 하나씩 | 현재 집중 |
| "keep moving forward" | 앞으로 계속 | 진행 유지 |

**Running Metaphors in English:**
- "One mile at a time"—distance에 집중
- "One breath at a time"—호흡/페이스 집중
- "One stride at a time"—움직임 집중

**Emotional Benefit:**

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Coach's Instruction Delivery

**Structure:** Coach + verb (give/explain) + what + why

**From Text:**
```
"In December, we're building your base fitness. Gradually increase
your long run distance each week."

"From January, we'll seriously push the distance. Up to 30 kilometers."

"The key thing is not to overdo it. If your body hurts, rest."
```

**Analysis:**
- 단계적 설명 (December → January → February)
- 구체적 수치 제시 (10-30km 명확히)
- 주의사항 강조 (don't overdo, rest if hurt)
- 이유 제시 (if you get injured, you'll skip the race)

**Your Turn - Create Coach Instructions:**
```
"In [time period], we're [goal]. [Action] each [time unit].

The key thing is [most important point].
If [condition], [consequence]."
```

---

## Pattern 2: Personal Goal Reflection

**Structure:** Large goal + contradiction/complexity + breakdown + perspective

**From Text:**
```
"42 kilometers requires 12 weeks.

12 weeks. That's long.

It's scary but also exciting.

Because my coach made the plan. I just have to follow it.
One week at a time. One step at a time."
```

**Breakdown:**
1. State the challenge (42km, 12 weeks)
2. Express feeling (It's long)
3. Acknowledge complexity (scary but exciting)
4. Provide reason for confidence (coach's plan)
5. Simplify approach (one week, one step)

**Practice Creating This Pattern:**
```
[Large goal] requires [effort].

[Time needed]. That's [reaction].

[Emotional response].

Because [reason]. I just [action].
[Smaller unit] at a time."
```

---

## Pattern 3: Fear-to-Confidence Transition

**Structure:** Initial fear → source of confidence → simplification → focus

| Element | Example | Function |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Initial statement | "It's scary" | acknowledge difficulty |
| Contradiction | "but also exciting" | add nuance |
| Reason | "because my coach made the plan" | explain confidence |
| Simplification | "I just have to follow it" | reduce complexity |
| Action focus | "one week at a time" | break into chunks |

---

## Pattern 4: Comparison with Past Achievement

**From Text:**
```
"But I trained for 4 weeks before the 21km.

42 kilometers requires 12 weeks."
```

**Structure:** [Past achievement] + [amount of effort] + [New challenge] + [increased effort]

**Why This Works:**
- 과거 성공 경험 → 확신 구축
- 수치 비교 → 객관적 이해
- 마라톤이 도달 가능함을 시사

**Similar Patterns:**
```
"I did X before. Now Y requires Z, which is [comparison]."
"Last time I trained for [period]. This time I need [longer period]."
"The half marathon took [time]. A full marathon takes [longer time]."
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Receiving a Training Plan

```
Coach: Doyun, here's your 12-week marathon training plan.

You: Okay. When do we start with longer distances?

Coach: In December, we're building your base fitness.
       Then from January, we'll seriously push the distance.

You: Up to how much?

Coach: 30 kilometers. If you want to run a full marathon,
       you need to have done 30km or more.

You: That's quite a jump from the half marathon.

Coach: It is, but we'll build gradually. The key thing is not to overdo it.

You: What should I do if something hurts?

Coach: Rest. If you get injured, you'll have to skip the race.

You: Understood. I'll follow the plan.
```
