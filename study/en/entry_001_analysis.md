# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Morning Routine Expressions

### "Breakfast!"

**Original Korean:** "도윤아, 밥!"

**Analysis:**
- 영어에서도 한 단어로 부를 수 있음
- 더 일반적인 표현: "Breakfast is ready!" / "Time for breakfast!"
- 미국식: "Come eat!" / "Food's ready!"

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Food!" | "Breakfast is ready!" | "Breakfast is served." |
| "Come eat!" | "Time to eat!" | "Please come to the table." |
| "Breakfast!" | "Come get your breakfast!" | "Your breakfast is waiting." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Mom: Breakfast!
You: Coming!
Mom: Hurry up, it's getting cold.
You: Just a minute, I'm getting dressed.
```

### "It's your first day, so leave early."

**Original Korean:** "오늘 첫날이니까 일찍 가."

**Grammar Point:** "It's [situation], so [advice]" 패턴

**Structure:**
```
It's + [상황 명사/형용사], so + [명령문/조언]
```

**More Examples:**
| Situation | Advice | Full Sentence |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| your first day | leave early | "It's your first day, so leave early." |
| raining | take an umbrella | "It's raining, so take an umbrella." |
| cold outside | wear a jacket | "It's cold outside, so wear a jacket." |
| a long drive | pack some snacks | "It's a long drive, so pack some snacks." |
| Monday | traffic will be bad | "It's Monday, so traffic will be bad." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "Since it's your first day, you should leave early."
- "You'd better leave early since it's your first day."
- "Leave early—it's your first day, after all."

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "It's your first day, so you leave early." (명령문에 you 불필요)
✅ "It's your first day, so leave early."

### "Don't miss the bus."

**Original Korean:** "버스 놓치면 안 돼."

**Grammar Point:** Negative imperative (부정 명령문)

**Structure:** Don't + 동사원형

**Similar Warning Expressions:**
| Expression | Situation | Tone |
|------------|-----------|------|
| "Don't miss the bus." | 교통 | 중립 |
| "Don't be late." | 시간 | 중립 |
| "Don't forget your lunch." | 소지품 | 다정함 |
| "Don't stay up too late." | 건강 | 걱정 |
| "Don't worry about it." | 위로 | 따뜻함 |

**Stronger Versions:**
- "Make sure you don't miss the bus." (더 강조)
- "Whatever you do, don't miss the bus." (매우 강조)
- "You'd better not miss the bus." (경고 뉘앙스)

**Softer Versions:**
- "Try not to miss the bus."
- "Be careful not to miss the bus."

### "Anything's fine."

**Original Korean:** "아무거나요."

**Nuance:** 무관심하거나 결정을 상대에게 맡기는 표현

**Pronunciation Tip:**
- "Anything's" = /ˈeniθɪŋz/
- 's를 확실히 발음 (is의 축약)

**Variations by Nuance:**

| Expression | Nuance | When to Use |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| "Anything's fine." | 중립, 약간 무관심 | 진짜 상관없을 때 |
| "Whatever you want." | 상대 존중 | 상대 의견 따르고 싶을 때 |
| "I don't mind." | 편안함 | 어느 쪽이든 괜찮을 때 |
| "I'm easy." | 매우 캐주얼 | 친구 사이 |
| "You decide." | 결정 위임 | 고르기 귀찮을 때 |
| "I could go either way." | 중립 | 두 가지 선택지 중 |
| "Surprise me." | 재미있게 | 기대하는 뉘앙스 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: What do you want for dinner?
B: Anything's fine.
A: Come on, just pick something.
B: Really, I'm not picky. Whatever you feel like making.
A: How about pasta?
B: Sounds good to me.
```

**Cultural Note:**
한국어의 "아무거나"처럼, "Anything's fine"도 때로 상대방을 답답하게 할 수 있음.
더 협조적인 대답: "I'm in the mood for something light" / "Maybe something warm?"

## 1.2 Casual Greetings & Short Responses

### "Nervous? First day and all?"

**Original Korean:** "첫날인데 긴장되지?"

**Grammar Point:** Fragment question + "and all"

**"And all" 설명:**
- "~이기도 하고" / "그런 거 다 포함해서"
- 추가 이유를 암시하는 캐주얼한 표현

**Examples with "and all":**
| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| "First day and all" | 첫날이기도 하고 (여러 이유 중 하나) |
| "It's your birthday and all" | 생일이기도 하고 |
| "You're new here and all" | 여기 새로 왔으니까 |
| "With the rain and all" | 비도 오고 해서 |
| "Being Monday and all" | 월요일이기도 하고 |

**Full Sentence Versions:**
- "Are you nervous? It's your first day and all."
- "You must be nervous, what with it being your first day and all."

**Similar Check-in Questions:**
| Question | Situation |
|----------|-----------|
| "Nervous?" | 긴장 체크 |
| "Excited?" | 기대감 체크 |
| "Ready?" | 준비 상태 체크 |
| "Tired?" | 피로 체크 |
| "Okay?" | 일반적 안부 |
| "Hungry?" | 배고픔 체크 |

### "Not really."

**Original Korean:** "별로요."

**Nuance Spectrum:**

| Response | Meaning | Enthusiasm Level |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| "Yes, very!" | 매우 그렇다 | ★★★★★ |
| "Yeah, kind of." | 좀 그렇다 | ★★★☆☆ |
| "A little bit." | 조금 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| "Not really." | 별로 | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| "Not at all." | 전혀 | ☆☆☆☆☆ |

**Pronunciation & Intonation:**
- 무관심: "Not really." (평탄하게)
- 방어적: "Not really..." (끝을 늘이며)
- 확신: "Not really." (짧고 단호하게)

**Alternative Expressions:**
- "Nah." (very casual)
- "I guess not."
- "Not particularly."
- "Meh." (slang, 무관심)

**Important Context from Story:**
> "Not really." I was, actually. But saying so felt embarrassing.

이 문맥에서 "Not really"는 사실을 숨기는 표현. 실제로는 긴장했지만 인정하기 부끄러워서 한 대답.

### "Huh? What?"

**Original Korean:** "응? 뭘?"

**Usage:** 못 알아들었거나 예상 못한 질문을 받았을 때

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Huh?" | "Sorry?" | "I beg your pardon?" |
| "What?" | "What's that?" | "Could you repeat that?" |
| "Hmm?" | "Pardon?" | "I didn't quite catch that." |
| "Say again?" | "What did you say?" | "Would you mind repeating?" |

**Pronunciation:**
- "Huh?" = /hʌ/ (입을 약간 벌리고 짧게)
- "What?" = /wʌt/ 또는 /wɒt/

**Body Language:**
- 눈썹을 살짝 올리거나
- 고개를 약간 기울이거나
- 귀를 가까이 대는 제스처

### "No way, same as me!"

**Original Korean:** "와, 나랑 같네!"

**"No way" 의 다양한 의미:**

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 놀라움 (긍정) | 대박/진짜? | "No way, same as me!" |
| 놀라움 (부정) | 말도 안 돼 | "No way! That's impossible." |
| 거절 | 절대 안 해 | "No way am I doing that." |
| 감탄 | 와, 대단해 | "No way! You finished already?" |

**Similar Surprise Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Formality |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| "Really?" | ★★☆ | Neutral |
| "Seriously?" | ★★★ | Casual |
| "No way!" | ★★★★ | Casual |
| "Are you kidding?" | ★★★★ | Casual |
| "You're joking!" | ★★★★ | Casual |
| "Get out of here!" | ★★★★★ | Very casual |
| "Shut up!" | ★★★★★ | Very casual (친한 사이) |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: I take the 201 bus.
B: No way, same as me!
A: Really? I've never seen you.
B: I know, right? It's always so packed.
A: Total hell.
B: Tell me about it.
```

### "Uh... sure."

**Original Korean:** "어... 그래."

**Analysis:** 망설임 + 동의

**What the Hesitation Conveys:**
- 약간의 불확실함
- 처음 만난 사람에 대한 경계
- 예상 못한 제안에 대한 반응
- 내성적인 성격 표현

**Variations of Hesitant Agreement:**

| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "Uh... sure." | 망설이지만 동의 |
| "I guess so." | 확신 없지만 동의 |
| "Yeah, okay." | 약간 마지못해 |
| "Sure, why not." | 별 이유 없이 동의 |
| "I suppose." | 격식체 망설임 |
| "If you say so." | 상대방 말을 따름 |

**Confident Agreement (비교):**

| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "Sure!" | 확실한 동의 |
| "Absolutely!" | 강한 동의 |
| "Of course!" | 당연하다는 뉘앙스 |
| "Definitely!" | 확실히 |
| "I'd love to!" | 기꺼이 |

## 2.1 Nausea & Motion Sickness

## 3.2 Sensory Descriptions

### "Sweat, perfume, something damp."

**Original Korean:** "땀 냄새, 향수 냄새, 뭔가 축축한 냄새"

**Sentence Structure:** Fragment listing for impact

**Why This Works:**
- 완전한 문장 대신 명사 나열
- 독자가 직접 냄새를 느끼는 것 같은 효과
- 불쾌함을 더 강하게 전달

**Smell Vocabulary:**

| Type | Positive | Negative |
|------|----------|----------|
| 향수 | fragrance, perfume | overpowering perfume |
| 음식 | aroma, appetizing smell | greasy smell |
| 땀 | - | body odor (B.O.), sweat |
| 습기 | fresh, clean | musty, damp, moldy |
| 담배 | - | cigarette smoke, stale smoke |

**Describing Bad Smells:**

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "I don't look/feel all that different from..."

**Structure:** Subject + don't + verb + all that + adjective + from + comparison

**"All that"의 역할:** "그렇게까지", "그다지"

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "I don't look all that different." | 그렇게 달라 보이지 않아 |
| "It's not all that hard." | 그렇게 어렵지 않아 |
| "I'm not all that hungry." | 그렇게 배고프진 않아 |
| "The movie wasn't all that good." | 영화가 그렇게 좋진 않았어 |

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"This restaurant isn't all that expensive."
"The test wasn't all that difficult."
"I don't feel all that tired."
"The weather isn't all that bad today."
```

---

## Pattern 2: "It didn't feel real."

**Structure:** It + didn't feel + adjective

**Uses:** 비현실적 경험, 예상과 다른 감정

| Situation | Sentence |
|-----------|----------|
| 합격 통지 | "When I got in, it didn't feel real." |
| 졸업 | "Graduating didn't feel real at first." |
| 결혼 | "Even after the wedding, it didn't feel real." |
| 나쁜 소식 | "Hearing the news, it didn't feel real." |

**Variations:**
- "It felt surreal." (초현실적이었다)
- "It felt like a dream." (꿈 같았다)
- "I couldn't believe it was happening." (일어나는 게 믿기지 않았다)
- "It took a while to sink in." (실감하는 데 시간이 걸렸다)

---

## Pattern 3: "But saying so felt embarrassing."

**Structure:** But + gerund + felt + emotion

**Expressing Hidden Feelings:**

| Hidden Feeling | Expression |
|----------------|------------|
| 긴장됨 인정 | "But admitting I was nervous felt embarrassing." |
| 도움 요청 | "But asking for help felt embarrassing." |
| 모른다고 말함 | "But saying I didn't know felt embarrassing." |
| 울고 싶음 | "But crying in front of them felt embarrassing." |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "I was nervous, but I didn't want to admit it."
- "I couldn't bring myself to say I was nervous."
- "It felt weird to admit I was nervous."

---

## Pattern 4: "Just like that, ~"

**Meaning:** 간단하게, 그렇게 해서 (일이 쉽게/빠르게 일어남)

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| "Just like that, I made my first friend." | 친구 사귐 |
| "Just like that, the meeting was over." | 회의 끝남 |
| "Just like that, he quit his job." | 직장 그만둠 |
| "Just like that, they broke up." | 헤어짐 |
| "Just like that, I became a millionaire." | 부자 됨 |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "And that was that." (그렇게 끝났다)
- "Before I knew it..." (어느새)
- "In the blink of an eye..." (눈 깜짝할 새)
- "Out of nowhere..." (갑자기)

---

## Pattern 5: "The thought is overwhelming."

**Structure:** The thought/idea + is + overwhelming/terrifying/exciting

| Thought | Emotion | Full Sentence |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| 3년 더 | overwhelming | "Three more years. The thought is overwhelming." |
| 혼자 여행 | terrifying | "Traveling alone. The thought is terrifying." |
| 새 집 | exciting | "A new house. The thought is exciting." |
| 다 끝남 | depressing | "It's all over. The thought is depressing." |

**Variations:**
- "I can't even think about it."
- "The mere thought of it makes me anxious."
- "Just thinking about it stresses me out."
- "I don't even want to think about it."

---

## Pattern 6: "I was more worried about X than Y."

**Structure:** I was more + emotion + about X than (about) Y

**From Text:** "I was more worried about the bus than any of that."

| Example | X | Y |
|---------|---|---|
| "I was more worried about the presentation than the exam." | 발표 | 시험 |
| "I was more excited about the food than the party." | 음식 | 파티 |
| "I'm more concerned about the cost than the time." | 비용 | 시간 |

---

## Pattern 7: "If someone asked..., honestly, all I can think about is..."

**Structure:** Hypothetical + honest reflection

**Uses:** 하루/경험 회상할 때

**Full Pattern:**
```
If someone asked [question], honestly, all I can think about is [main memory].
```

**Examples:**
```
"If someone asked about my vacation, honestly, all I can think about is the food."

```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Asking About Someone's Commute

```
A: Hey, how do you get to school?
B: I take the 201 bus. You?

```

B: Uh... sure. That'd be good.
