# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Talking About Possessions & Experiences

### "It's been a week since I got my running shoes."

**Original Korean:** "러닝화를 산 지 일주일 됐다"

**Grammar Point:** "It's been [time period] since + past event"

**Structure:**
```
It's been + [time duration] + since + past event
```

**More Examples:**

| Time | Event | Full Sentence |
|------|-------|---------------|
| a week | got my shoes | "It's been a week since I got my running shoes." |
| a month | started jogging | "It's been a month since I started jogging." |
| three days | finished the project | "It's been three days since I finished the project." |
| a year | moved here | "It's been a year since I moved here." |
| two weeks | saw you last | "It's been two weeks since I saw you last." |

**Variations:**

- "I got my running shoes a week ago."
- "I've had my running shoes for a week."
- "A week has passed since I got my running shoes."

**Common Mistakes:**

❌ "It's been a week I got my running shoes." (since 필수)
✅ "It's been a week since I got my running shoes."

---

### "Four runs in the past week."

**Original Korean:** "지난 한 주에 네 번 뛰었다"

**Fragment Expression:** 명사 구문으로 빠른 설명

**Why Writers Use This Pattern:**
- 직접적이고 통계적 느낌
- 성취감 표현
- 일기 특유의 간결함

**Converting Fragments to Full Sentences:**

| Fragment | Full Sentence |
|----------|---------------|
| "Four runs in the past week." | "I went for four runs in the past week." |
| "Monday, Thursday, Saturday, Sunday." | "I ran on Monday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday." |
| "Eight minutes faster." | "This is eight minutes faster than last time." |

**Similar Tracking Language:**

```
"Three workouts this week."
"Five miles total."
"Best time yet: 42 minutes."
"Personal record."
```

---

### "I even ran on the weekend."

**Original Korean:** "주말에도 뛰었다"

**"Even" for Emphasis:** 예상 밖으로 할 정도로 (극단적/특별함)

**"Even" Examples:**

| Example | Nuance |
|---------|--------|
| "I even ran on the weekend." | 주말에까지 뛸 정도 |
| "She even helped with the dishes." | 그래도 설거지를 도울 정도로 |
| "I even cried." | 울 정도로 (신체 반응) |
| "He even admitted he was wrong." | 잘못을 인정할 정도로 |

**Pronunciation Tip:**
- Even = /ˈiːvən/ (강조할 때 높게)

**Related Emphasis Expressions:**
- "I ran on the weekend too."
- "I was running so much I even went on weekends."
- "Not just weekdays—I even ran on weekends."

## 1.2 Casual Conversation Responses

### "Because I love my new shoes that much."

**Original Korean:** "그 정도로 새 신발을 좋아해서"

**"That much" for Intensity:** 그 정도로, 그렇게까지

**"That much" Pattern:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "I love it that much." | 그 정도로 좋아해 | "The shoes are great—I love them that much." |
| "I was tired that much." | 그렇게까지 피곤했어 | "I fell asleep immediately, I was that tired." |
| "He cares that much." | 그 정도로 신경 써 | "He stayed late—he cares that much." |

**Intensity Spectrum:**

| Expression | Level | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| "I like it." | ★☆☆ | mild interest |
| "I like it a lot." | ★★☆ | strong interest |
| "I love it." | ★★★ | very strong |
| "I love it that much." | ★★★★ | extreme/obsessive |

---

### "Of course, I can't run this fast every time."

**Original Korean:** "물론 매번 이렇게 빠르게 뛸 수는 없지"

**"Of course" for Hedging:** 당연히 (한계 인정)

**When to Use "Of course":**

| Context | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Agreement | "Of course! I agree." |
| Obvious fact | "Of course, it rains sometimes." |
| Acknowledging limitation | "Of course, I can't be perfect." |
| Sarcasm | "Of course! Said no one ever." |

**Similar Hedge Expressions:**

```
"I mean, obviously..."
"Sure, but obviously..."
"Naturally, I can't..."
"I can't always..."
```

---

### "It depends on how I'm feeling."

**Original Korean:** "그날 컨디션에 따라 다르다"

**"Depends on" Pattern:** ~에 따라 다르다

**Structure:** It depends on + [noun/gerund]

**Examples:**

| Depends on | Full Sentence |
|----------|---------------|
| the weather | "It depends on the weather." |
| your mood | "It depends on how you're feeling." |
| the time | "It depends on what time it is." |
| energy level | "It depends on my energy level." |
| traffic | "It depends on the traffic." |

**Variations:**
- "That depends on..."
- "It all depends on..."
- "Everything depends on..."

## 1.3 Noticing & Observing

### "I glanced at him while running."

**Original Korean:** "뛰다가 할아버지를 슬쩍 봤다"

**"Glance" vs. "Look":**

| Verb | Duration | Attention | Example |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|
| glance | 아주 짧음 | 낮음 | "I glanced at him." |
| look | 짧음 | 중간 | "I looked at him." |
| stare | 오래 | 매우 높음 | "I stared at him." |
| gaze | 오래/낭만 | 깊음 | "I gazed at the stars." |
| watch | 지속 | 중간-높음 | "I watched him work." |

**Other "Quick Look" Verbs:**

```
"I peeped over my shoulder."
"I caught a glimpse of him."
"I snuck a look."
"I stole a glance."
```

---

### "He was doing something in the field."

**Original Korean:** "할아버지가 귤나무 사이를 움직이며 뭔가 하고 있었다"

**Vague Description:** "doing something" = 정확히 뭔지 모를 때

**Why Use Vague Descriptions:**
- 정확하지 않은 거리에서 봤음
- 시간이 없어서 자세히 못 봤음
- 중요하지 않은 세부사항

**Similar Vague Phrases:**

```
"He was doing something."
"I saw something on the ground."
"There was some kind of activity."
"He seemed to be working on something."
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING TIME & MEASUREMENT (시간과 측정 표현)

**Original Korean:** "7km를 42분에 달렸다"

**Time Duration Format:** "[distance] in [time]"

**Running Times & Paces:**

| Distance | Time | Pace/km | Assessment |
|----------|------|---------|-----------|

| 10 km | 60 min | 6:00 | steady pace |
| 5 km | 25 min | 5:00 | good pace |

**How to Express Athletic Performance:**

```

"My time was 42 minutes for 7 km."
"I covered 7 km in just 42 minutes."
"42 minutes for the 7 km route."
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "The same distance that took me X minutes last week."

**Structure:** Comparison describing previous effort

**Uses:** Improvement tracking, sharing progress

**Full Examples:**

```
"The same distance that took me 50 minutes last week."
"The same route that used to exhaust me."
"The same time slot that felt impossible before."
"The same pace I could never maintain."
```

**Variations:**
- "What took me 50 minutes last week..."
- "A distance I used to do in 50 minutes..."
- "I'm now doing in less time what took me 50 minutes before."

---

## Pattern 2: "It's been [time] since [major event]"

**Structure:** Reflection on time passage

**Uses:** Marking milestones, showing change

| Time | Event | Full Sentence |
|------|-------|---------------|
| a week | got shoes | "It's been a week since I got my running shoes." |
| a month | started running | "It's been a month since I started running seriously." |
| 2 months | ran my first 10k | "It's been 2 months since I completed my first 10k." |

---

## Pattern 3: "Grandpa works every day too. I run every day. Somehow, it feels similar."

**Structure:** Observation → Observation → Comparison

**Effect:** Finding unexpected connections

**Similar Patterns:**

```
"He comes to the field daily.
I run every morning.
Something about that resonates with me."
```

**How to Use This Pattern:**

```
"[Subject 1] does [action] regularly.
I [do action] regularly.
There's something [emotion/observation] about that."
```

---


# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Talking About Athletic Progress

```
A: Hey, how's the running training going?
B: Really well! It's been a week since I got new running shoes.
A: New shoes? Do they help?
B: Yeah, massively. I just ran 7 km in 42 minutes.
A: That's pretty fast! Is that your personal best?
B: Close! Last week it took me 50 minutes for the same distance.
A: Wait, 8 minutes faster? That's a big improvement!
B: I know, right? I wasn't sure good shoes would make such a difference.
A: That's great! Are you running every day?
B: Almost. Four times this week—Monday, Thursday, Saturday, Sunday.
A: No rest days?
B: Maybe tomorrow. We'll see how I'm feeling.
```

### Scenario 2: Sharing Athletic Motivation

```
A: Doyun, I noticed you run past the tangerine orchard every morning.
B: Yeah, I do. I see Grandpa Yeongcheol working there.
A: Does he know you run by?
B: I wave at him sometimes, and he waves back.
A: That's nice. Do you know what he's doing?
B: I think he's tending to the trees. He's there every day.
A: Just like you run every day?
B: Exactly. We both show up and do our work. Something about that feels... I dunno, connected?
A: That's a sweet observation.
B: Yeah, I've learned that consistency matters. Whether you're running or farming.
```

### Scenario 3: Discussing Gear & Equipment

```
A: Your new shoes look nice!
B: Thanks! They've made a huge difference in my running.
A: How so?
B: Before, my feet would hurt after a run. Especially my knees.
A: And now?
B: Now it feels like running on clouds. The cushioning absorbs all the shock.
A: They're worth it, then?
B: Totally. Mom helped me pay for half, so I could afford them.
A: That was nice of her.
B: Yeah, I'm really grateful. She helped me realize this is important to me.
A: Have your times improved?
B: Eight minutes faster than last week for the same distance!
A: That's impressive! The shoes or better fitness?
B: Probably both, to be honest. But I definitely feel more confident in them.
```
