# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Panic & Urgency Expressions

### "What?!"

**Original Korean:** "뭐?!"

**Analysis:**
- 놀람과 패닉을 표현하는 가장 기본적인 감탄사
- 발음: 짧고 날카롭게 /wʌt/
- 억양: 급격히 올라감

**Similar Shock Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "What?" | ★★☆ | 뭐? (일반적 놀람) |
| "What?!" | ★★★★ | 뭐?! (강한 놀람) |
| "What the—" | ★★★★ | 뭐야— (중단된 욕설) |
| "Are you kidding me?!" | ★★★★ | 장난해?! |
| "No way!" | ★★★★ | 말도 안 돼! |
| "Oh no!" | ★★★ | 안 돼! |
| "Oh my God!" | ★★★★★ | 세상에! |

**Waking Up Late Reactions:**
```
"What?! It's already 7:30?!"
"No, no, no, no..."
"This can't be happening!"
"I'm so dead."
```

### "I jumped out of bed."

**Original Korean:** "벌떡 일어났다"

**"Jump" for Sudden Movement:**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "jump out of bed" | 벌떡 일어나다 | 급하게 일어날 때 |
| "jump up" | 벌떡 일어나다 | 앉다가 일어날 때 |
| "jump to one's feet" | 벌떡 일어서다 | 서 있는 상태로 |
| "spring out of bed" | 벌떡 일어나다 | 활기차게 |

**Getting Out of Bed Spectrum:**

| Expression | Speed/Energy |
|------------|--------------|
| "crawl out of bed" | ★☆☆☆☆ (느리게, 힘들게) |
| "drag myself out of bed" | ★★☆☆☆ (억지로) |
| "get out of bed" | ★★★☆☆ (중립) |
| "hop out of bed" | ★★★★☆ (가볍게) |
| "jump out of bed" | ★★★★★ (급하게) |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Why are you so out of breath?
B: I overslept. I jumped out of bed and ran here.
A: Did you even eat breakfast?
B: No time. I barely had time to brush my teeth.
```

## 1.2 Time Pressure Expressions

### "Seven minutes."

**Original Korean:** "7분."

**Fragment for Emphasis:**
- 완전한 문장 없이 숫자만 사용
- 긴박감과 압박감 전달
- 독자의 집중을 유도

**Similar Time Pressure Fragments:**
```
"Ten seconds."
"Five minutes left."
"Three days."
"Two hours until the deadline."
```

**Time Countdowns in Context:**
```
"Seven minutes. That's all I had."
"Three stops left. I might make it."
"Five minutes until class starts."
```

---

### "I had to wash up, get dressed, and make it to the bus stop in seven minutes."

**Original Korean:** "7분 안에 씻고, 옷 입고, 정류장까지 가야 한다"

**"In [time]" for Deadlines:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "in seven minutes" | 7분 안에 |
| "in an hour" | 한 시간 안에 |
| "in two days" | 이틀 안에 |
| "within the hour" | 한 시간 이내에 |

**Listing Tasks Under Pressure:**

**Structure:** I had to + verb₁ + verb₂ + and + verb₃ + in [time]

```
"I had to shower, eat, and leave in fifteen minutes."
"She had to pack, check out, and catch her flight in an hour."
"We have to finish, review, and submit by midnight."
```

**Morning Rush Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean |
|---------|--------|
| wash up | 씻다 |
| get dressed | 옷 입다 |
| grab breakfast | 아침 챙기다 |
| brush teeth | 양치하다 |
| make it to (place) | ~에 도착하다 |

### "Impossible."

**One-Word Verdicts:**

| Word | Meaning | Tone |
|------|---------|------|

**But Then:**
```
"Impossible. But I tried anyway."

"It seemed crazy. But what did I have to lose?"
```

### "But I tried anyway."

**Original Korean:** "그래도 해봤다"

**"Anyway" for Determination:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "But I tried anyway." | 그래도 해봤다 |
| "I did it anyway." | 어쨌든 했다 |
| "I went anyway." | 어쨌든 갔다 |
| "Let's try anyway." | 어쨌든 해보자 |

**Concession + Action Pattern:**

**Structure:** It seemed [negative]. But [positive action] anyway.

```
"It seemed pointless. But I tried anyway."
"I knew I'd be late. But I ran anyway."
"It looked impossible. But I gave it a shot anyway."
```

## 1.4 Missing Transportation

### "I could see the bus too. In the distance. Already pulling away."

**Original Korean:** "버스도 보였다. 멀리서. 이미 정류장을 떠나고 있었다."

**Fragment Style for Visual Impact:**
- 짧은 문장들이 순간을 분절
- 절망이 서서히 다가오는 느낌
- "In the distance" - 손 닿지 않는 거리 강조

**"Pull away" = Leave (vehicles):**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "The bus was pulling away." | 버스가 떠나고 있었다 |
| "The train pulled away from the station." | 기차가 역을 떠났다 |
| "I watched the taxi pull away." | 택시가 떠나는 걸 봤다 |

**Watching Something Leave:**
```
"I got there just in time to see the bus pull away."
"The elevator doors closed right in front of me."
"I missed it by seconds."
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Running Late

```
A: You look like you just ran a marathon. What happened?
B: I overslept. My alarm didn't go off.
A: Did you catch the bus?
B: No, I missed it. I saw it pulling away as I got to the stop.
A: So you waited for the next one?
B: Actually, I walked.
A: Walked?! How far is that?
B: Seven kilometers. Took me about an hour and ten minutes.
A: That's insane!
B: I know. But either way I was going to be late, so...
A: So what's the difference, right?
B: Exactly.
```

### Scenario 2: Discovering Something New

```
A: How was your walk to school?
B: Honestly? Kind of amazing.
A: Really? Wasn't it tiring?
B: Sure, my legs hurt. But the air was so different.
A: Different how?
B: Fresh. Clean. Not like that stuffy bus.
A: I never thought about it that way.
B: Neither did I. And I saw rapeseed flowers blooming.
A: There are rapeseed flowers on the way?
B: Right? I never knew either. I'd only ever seen them through the bus window.
A: Maybe I should try walking sometime.
B: It's pretty far. But... not bad.
```

### Scenario 3: Reflecting with a Friend

```
A: You seem... different today. In a good way.
B: Really?
A: Yeah, you're not as pale as usual.
B: I walked to school this morning.
A: On purpose?
B: No, I missed the bus. But walking felt better than I expected.
A: Better how?
B: I don't know. My legs hurt, but my stomach didn't.
A: No motion sickness?
B: None. Just physical tiredness.
A: So are you going to walk every day now?
B: No, an hour is too long. But... the bus might not be the only answer.
A: That's deep, man.
B: [laughs] I don't even know what I mean. I just feel good today.
```
