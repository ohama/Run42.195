# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Race Preparation & Planning

### "Race week has started."

**Original Korean:** "대회주간이 시작했다."

**Grammar Point:** Present Perfect - marking the beginning of a significant period

**Structure:** [Time period] + has + past participle

**Context Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "Race week has started." | 대회주간이 시작됐다 | Entry point |
| "Training season has begun." | 훈련 시즌이 시작됐다 | Broader scope |
| "The countdown has started." | 카운트다운이 시작됐다 | Anticipatory |
| "The final stretch has started." | 마지막 단계가 시작됐다 | Emphasis |

**Similar Significant Events:**
| Event | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| 시험주간 | "Exam week has started." |
| 이사 준비 | "Moving day has arrived." |
| 휴가 시작 | "Vacation has begun." |
| 중요한 회의 | "The meeting season has begun." |

**Variations:**
- "Race week is here." (더 캐주얼)
- "It's race week." (간단함)
- "The race is just around the corner." (다가옴)

---

### "Six days left."

**Original Korean:** "나흘 남았다."

**Counting Down:** Fragment for immediacy and impact

**Countdown Expressions:**
| Days | Expression | Intensity |
|------|------------|-----------|
| 30 | "A month away." | ★☆☆ |
| 7 | "A week away." | ★★☆ |
| 3-4 | "Just a few days left." | ★★★ |
| 2 | "Two days left." | ★★★ |
| 1 | "Tomorrow's the day." | ★★★★ |

**Full vs. Fragment:**
- Fragment: "Six days left." (강조, 감정적)
- Full: "There are six days left." (중립적)
- Casual: "Just six more days." (친근함)

**Time Pressure Expressions:**
| Expression | Context |
|------------|---------|
| "Countdown to [event]" | 기대/두려움 섞임 |
| "[X] days to go" | 진행 중 알림 |
| "The clock is ticking." | 시간이 없다 (긴급) |
| "Time's running out." | 더 강한 긴박감 |

### "I'm just taking it easy, working on my conditioning."

**Original Korean:** "지금은 충분히 쉬면서 컨디션을 다지는 중이다."

**"Take it easy" Pattern:** 속도를 낮추다, 부담 줄이다

**Different Meanings of "Take it easy":**
| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 속도/강도 낮추기 | 천천히/가볍게 | "Take it easy with your training." |
| 휴식하기 | 쉬다 | "Take it easy for a while." |
| 걱정 말기 | 너무 걱정 말 | "Take it easy, it'll be fine." |
| 인사 | 잘 있어 | "Take it easy!" (작별 인사) |

**"Working on [skill]":**
| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| working on + noun | "Working on my conditioning." |
| working on + gerund | "Working on improving my speed." |
| working on + [improvement area] | "Working on my endurance." |

**Related Training Vocabulary:**
```
"I'm training hard." (강하게 훈련 중)
"I'm building endurance." (지구력 키우는 중)
"I'm focusing on pace." (속도에 집중 중)
"I'm doing maintenance runs." (유지 달리기 중)
"I'm tapering down." (운동량 줄이는 중)
```

**Original Korean:** "음식을 잘 먹고 잘 자야 한다."

**Why This Works:**
- 두 가지 중요한 요소를 동등하게 강조
- 간단하면서도 모든 기초를 언급

**Health/Training Advice Patterns:**
| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "You need to [verb] well." | "You need to eat well." |
| "Make sure you [verb]." | "Make sure you rest." |
| "Don't forget to [verb]." | "Don't forget to hydrate." |
| "[Verb] regularly." | "Sleep regularly." |

**Recovery Vocabulary:**
```
"Get enough sleep." (충분한 수면)
"Eat balanced meals." (균형잡힌 식사)
"Stay hydrated." (수분 섭취)
"Stretch regularly." (정기적 스트레칭)

## 1.4 Race Instructions & Logistics

### "This week just do about 3km. Keep it slow."

**Original Korean:** "이번 주는 3km 정도만. 천천히."

**Coach's Directive - Casual but Clear:**

**Training Instruction Patterns:**
| Pattern | Example | Emphasis |
|---------|---------|----------|
| "do about [distance]" | "do about 5km" | 정확성 |
| "keep it [adverb]" | "keep it slow" | 방식 강조 |
| "[distance], then [next step]" | "5km, then rest" | 순차적 |

**"About" for Approximation:**
```
"about 3km" = 대략 3km (정확하지 않아도 됨)
"exactly 3km" = 정확히 3km (정확해야 함)
"3km or so" = 3km 정도 (매우 캐주얼)
```

**Pace Instructions:**
| Instruction | Meaning | Context |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| "Keep it slow." | 천천히 | 회복 주간 |
| "Easy pace." | 편한 속도 | 일반 운동 |
| "Speed work." | 빠른 속도 | 인터벌 훈련 |
| "Race pace." | 경주 속도 | 경주 연습 |

---

### "Thursday do about 5km, then rest on Friday and Saturday."

**Original Korean:** "목요일은 5km 정도, 그 다음 금토는 쉬어."

**Multi-day Training Schedule - Fragment Format:**

**Weekly Schedule Pattern:**
| Day | Activity | Format |
|-----|----------|--------|

| Friday-Saturday | "rest" | 휴식 |
| Sunday | "[race]" | 목표 |

```
"Heavy training" → "Light training" → "Rest" → "Race"

"It's time to rest." (휴식 시간)
```

### "I'm nervous, but I'm not afraid."

**Original Korean:** "긴장되지만 두렵지 않다."

**Distinguishing Between Two Similar Emotions:**

**Nervous vs. Afraid:**
| Nervous | Afraid |
|---------|--------|
| 긴장한 (심장 두근거림) | 두려운 (공포감) |
| 준비된 상태 | 도망치고 싶은 상태 |
| 행동 가능 | 행동 불가능 |
| 경주 전: 정상 | 경주 전: 문제 |

**Emotional Distinctions in English:**
| Emotion | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| nervous | excited anticipation + worry | "Nervous about the presentation." |
| anxious | excessive worry | "Anxious about the future." |
| scared | fear of something specific | "Scared of the dark." |
| afraid | fear/terror | "Afraid I'll fail." |
| worried | concern | "Worried about the weather." |

**Pattern: "Emotional state, but not [other state]":**
```
"Tired, but not giving up."
"Disappointed, but not defeated."
"Angry, but not violent."
"Frustrated, but still trying."
```

**"Nice and Easy" Intensifier Phrase:**

**Why "Nice and [adj]":**
- 두 형용사로 긍정적 느낌 배가

- 캐주얼하고 친근한 톤

"effortless stride"
```

### "Felt incredibly short."

**Original Korean:** "엄청 짧게 느껴졌다."

**Distance Perception - Positive Surprise:**

**"Felt [adjective]" for Subjective Experience:**
| Felt... | Meaning | Tone |
|---------|---------|------|
| "short" | 금방 지남 | 긍정 |
| "long" | 오래 걸림 | 부정 |
| "easy" | 무리 없음 | 긍정 |
| "hard" | 힘듦 | 부정 |
