# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Expressions of Achievement and Accomplishment

### "Today I ran 5km without stopping for the first time."

**Original Korean:** "오늘 처음으로 5km를 쉬지 않고 달렸다."

**Analysis:**
- 성취를 알릴 때의 기본 표현
- "For the first time"은 과거에 처음 한 일을 말할 때 사용
- 진행 중인 일의 완료를 강조

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Nuance | When to Use |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| "I ran 5km without stopping for the first time." | 정중, 보고적 | 일기, 누군가에게 설명 |
| "I finally ran 5km non-stop." | 개인적 성취감 | 친근한 상황 |
| "I managed to run 5km non-stop." | 노력 강조 | 어려움을 극복함 |
| "I just ran 5km straight!" | 흥분, 즉각적 | 방금 달성했을 때 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Friend: How was your run today?
You: Amazing! I ran 5km without stopping for the first time.
Friend: Really? That's impressive! How long did it take?
You: About 28 minutes. I was shocked I could do it.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I ran 5km without stopping for first time." (article 누락)
✅ "I ran 5km without stopping for the first time."

---

### "Non-stop. 5km."

**Original Korean:** "쉬지 않고. 5km."

**Analysis:**
- Fragment sentences (불완전한 문장) 사용 - 매우 캐주얼하고 드라마틱
- 한국어처럼 영어에서도 완전한 문장 없이 강조 가능
- 감정적 임팩트를 위해 의도적으로 사용

**Variations:**
- "No stopping. 5km." (원문과 동일한 뉘앙스)
- "Without a break. 5km straight." (더 설명적)
- "The whole 5km. Non-stop." (어떤 부분도 걷지 않음을 강조)

**Fragment Sentence Examples in Running Context:**
| Fragment | Full Sentence | Impact |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| "Non-stop. 5km." | "I ran 5km without stopping." | 강조, 놀람 |
| "28 minutes. Straight." | "I ran for 28 minutes straight." | 몸으로 표현하는 느낌 |
| "My limit. Broken." | "I broke my limit." | 극적 |
| "First time. Ever." | "It was my first time ever." | 강렬한 감정 |

---

### "It was my first time. My chest swelled with pride."

**Original Korean:** "처음이었다. 가슴이 뭉클했다."

**Analysis:**
- 두 개의 짧은 문장으로 감정 표현
- 신체 감각을 통한 감정 전달
- 한국식 감정 표현을 영어로 자연스럽게 변환

**Grammar Point:** "My chest swelled with [emotion]"
- 신체 부위 + swelled + with + 감정
- "내 마음이 ~로 가득 찼다" 느낌

**Similar Expressions:**
| Expression | Korean | Emotion |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "My chest swelled with pride." | "가슴이 뭉클했다" | 자부심 |
| "My heart swelled with joy." | "마음이 뭉클했다" | 기쁨 |
| "My eyes filled with tears." | "눈물이 났다" | 감동 |
| "My hands were shaking." | "손이 떨렸다" | 흥분/긴장 |
| "I felt butterflies in my stomach." | "배가 철렁 내려앉았다" | 긴장/설렘 |

## 1.2 Casual Affirmations & Short Responses

### "Yeah."

**Original Korean:** "응."

**Analysis:**
- 매우 캐주얼한 단음절 답변
- 친한 친구와의 대화에서 자주 사용
- 길게 설명할 필요 없을 때

**Formality Levels:**
| Level | Expression | Tone |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Casual | "Yeah." | 친근함, 단호함 |
| Standard | "Yes." | 중립적 |
| Emphatic | "Yeah, totally." | 강한 동의 |
| Emphatic | "Hell yeah." | 흥분 (좀 더 강함) |
| Emphatic | "You bet." | 확신 있는 동의 |

---

### "Whoa. Seriously?"

**Original Korean:** "와, 진짜?"

**Analysis:**
- Interjection ("Whoa") + question으로 놀람 표현
- 반복된 감정 표현 - 청자의 주의를 끈다
- 캐주얼하고 자연스러운 대화 스타일

**Similar Expressions for Surprise:**
| Expression | Intensity | Formality |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| "Whoa." | High | Casual ★★★★★ |
| "Seriously?" | High | Casual ★★★★☆ |
| "Wait, really?" | High | Casual ★★★★☆ |
| "No way!" | Very High | Casual ★★★★★ |
| "You're kidding!" | Very High | Casual ★★★★★ |
| "That's amazing!" | Medium | Standard ★★★☆☆ |

## 1.3 Expressions of Limitation & Possibility

### "That's crazy. I can barely do 1km."

**Original Korean:** "미쳤다. 난 1km도 겨우."

**Analysis:**
- "barely"는 "겨우, 간신히" 의미
- 상대방의 성취와 자신의 현주소를 대비시키는 표현
- 친구에게 칭찬하면서 자신의 한계를 인정하는 겸양

**"Barely" 용법:**

| Sentence | Meaning | Nuance |
|----------|---------|--------|
| "I can barely do 1km." | "1km도 겨우 할 수 있다" | 자신 없음 |
| "We barely made it on time." | "간신히 시간에 도착했다" | 위험함 |
| "I barely remember." | "거의 기억 안 난다" | 희미함 |
| "He barely speaks English." | "거의 영어를 못 한다" | 제한적 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
You: I ran 5km non-stop today.
Friend: That's crazy. I can barely do 1km.
You: You will eventually. It just takes practice.
Friend: When did you even get this good?
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Breathing and Physical Exertion

### "My breathing was ragged. My heart was pounding. My legs were shaking."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 가쁜 상태였다. 심장이 뛰었다. 다리가 떨렸다."

**Analysis:**
- 연속된 신체 감각 묘사로 강렬한 상태 표현
- 현재분사가 아닌 과거형으로 "상태"를 묘사
- 세 가지 신체 부위의 반응을 동시에 묘사

**"Ragged" Definition:**
- /ˈræɡɪd/ - 거친, 불규칙한 (숨, 목소리에 사용)
- 깔끔하지 않은, 고르지 않은 상태
- "ragged breathing" = 가빠지고 불규칙한 호흡

**Physical Sensations Spectrum (강도별):**

| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "My heart was racing." | ★★★★★ | 매우 심한 운동/흥분 |
| "My heart was pounding." | ★★★★☆ | 강한 운동/감정 |
| "My heart was beating fast." | ★★★☆☆ | 보통 운동 |
| "My heart was beating normally." | ★★☆☆☆ | 휴식 중 |

**Similar Physical Sensation Expressions:**

| Sensation | Expression | Korean |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 호흡 (Breathing) | "My breathing was ragged." | "숨이 거칠었다" |
| 호흡 (Breathing) | "I was gasping for air." | "숨을 헐떡거렸다" |
| 심박수 (Heartbeat) | "My heart was pounding." | "심장이 터질 것 같았다" |
| 심박수 (Heartbeat) | "My heart was racing." | "심장이 빠르게 뛰었다" |
| 근육 (Muscles) | "My legs were shaking." | "다리가 떨렸다" |
| 근육 (Muscles) | "My whole body was trembling." | "온몸이 떨렸다" |

### "I wasn't as out of breath."

**Original Korean:** "숨도 덜 찼다."

**Analysis:**
- "out of breath" = 숨 차다, 헐떡거리다
- "as out of breath" = "~만큼 숨 차지 않다" (비교 표현)
- 긍정적인 진전을 나타냄

**Breath-Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "I was out of breath." | "숨이 찼다" | ★★★★★ |
| "I was getting out of breath." | "숨이 차기 시작했다" | ★★★★☆ |
| "I was catching my breath." | "숨을 고르고 있었다" | ★★★☆☆ |
| "My breathing was normal." | "숨이 정상적이었다" | ★★☆☆☆ |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Trainer: How did you feel during the run?
You: At the beginning, I was out of breath pretty quickly.
Trainer: And later?
You: After a while, I wasn't as out of breath. My body adapted.
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING SPACES/SITUATIONS (공간/상황 묘사)

## 3.1 Describing Running Progress and Milestones

### "1km. Fine. / 2km. Still fine. / 3km. A little tough, but manageable."

**Original Korean:** "1km. 괜찮다. / 2km. 여전히 괜찮다. / 3km. 좀 힘들지만 할 만하다."

**Analysis:**
- Fragment sentences로 진행 상황을 단계별로 묘사
- 각 거리마다의 신체 상태를 간결하게 표현
- Progression을 독자가 따라갈 수 있도록 구조화

**Progression Structure:**
```
[Distance]. [Status].
[Distance]. [Escalation].
[Distance]. [Challenge + Assessment].
```

**Running Progress Vocabulary:**

| Distance | Condition | Expression |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 1km | 좋음 | "Fine." / "Good." / "No problem." |
| 2km | 유지 | "Still fine." / "Still okay." / "Holding up." |
| 3km | 어려워짐 | "A little tough." / "Getting harder." / "Feeling it now." |
| 4km | 한계접근 | "Really pushing it." / "Getting tired." / "This is hard." |
| 5km | 극복/성공 | "Made it!" / "Did it!" / "Finally!" |

---

### "4km was my limit."

**Original Korean:** "4km가 내 한계였다."

**Analysis:**
- 과거의 개인적 한계를 객관적으로 진술
- "limit"은 능력의 경계를 뜻함
- 이전 상태와 현재 상태의 비교를 위한 기준점

**"Limit" Usage:**

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| "4km was my limit." | "4km가 내 최대였다" | 과거의 한계 |
| "I hit my limit." | "내 한계에 도달했다" | 현재 진행 중 |
| "I've reached my limit." | "더 이상 못 한다" | 현재 상태 |
| "I pushed past my limit." | "내 한계를 넘었다" | 성취 |
| "The limit is 5km." | "한계는 5km다" | 객관적 기준 |

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

## 4.1 Phrasal Verbs from Entry 029

### "Keep going" / "Keep running"

**Original Korean:** "계속 달리다" / "계속하다"

**Analysis:**
- "Keep + [현재분사]" = 계속해서 ~하다
- 행동을 유지, 지속한다는 의미
- 매우 일반적인 표현

**Structure:**
```
Keep + 현재분사 (verb-ing)
= Continue to + 동사원형
```

**Variations:**

| Expression | Meaning | Strength |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| "Keep running." | "계속 달려" | 중립 |
| "Keep pushing." | "계속 밀고 나가" | 격려적 |
| "Keep it up." | "그 조정으로 가" | 격려적 |
| "Don't stop." | "멈추지 마" | 직접적 |
| "Press on." | "계속 앞으로" | formal |

**Practice Sentences from Text:**
- "My legs were moving smoothly. I wasn't as out of breath. **I kept running.**"
- "At 4km, I could have walked, but I **kept going.**"

---

### "Start walking" / "Start running"

**Original Korean:** "걷기 시작하다" / "달리기 시작하다"

**Analysis:**
- "Start + [현재분사]" = ~하기 시작하다
- 행동의 시작점을 표현
- 전환점을 나타낼 때 사용

**Structure:**
```
Start + 현재분사 (verb-ing)
= Begin to + 동사원형
= Begin + 현재분사
```

**Context Examples:**

| Expression | Context | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| "I started running." | 처음 달리기 | 처음 시작함 |
| "I started running faster." | 속도 증가 | 더 빠르게 함 |
| "I started feeling tired." | 상태 변화 | 피로감 시작 |
| "I started seeing improvement." | 진전 | 개선 시작 |

**Sentence from Text:**
- "Normally this is where I **start walking.** 4km was my limit."

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## 5.1 Achievement Celebration Pattern

### Pattern: Share Achievement → Express Detail → Show Emotion

**Structure:**
```
[Major Achievement Statement]
+ [Specific Detail]
+ [Emotional Response]
= Complete achievement celebration
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"Hey, I ran 5km non-stop today."
"About 5 and a half minutes per kilometer. Took me 28 minutes."
"My chest swelled with pride."
```

**More Examples:**

| Achievement | Detail | Emotion |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I finished the marathon!" | "In 4 hours and 30 minutes." | "I was so proud." |
| "I got the promotion!" | "Starting next month." | "I couldn't believe it." |
| "I passed my exam!" | "Score of 92%." | "I was relieved." |
| "I finally fixed it!" | "Only took three hours." | "So satisfied." |

---

### Pattern: Positive Reaction → Confirmation → Follow-up Detail

**Structure:**
```
[Positive Reaction]
"[Confirmation/Denial]"
[Additional Detail/Explanation]
```

**Example from Text:**
```
Friend: "Whoa. Seriously?"
You: "Yeah."
You: "I ran for 28 minutes straight without resting."
```

**More Examples:**

| Reaction | Confirmation | Detail |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| "You got first place?" | "Yeah!" | "Beat everyone by 10 seconds." |
| "You're moving to Seoul?" | "Yeah, for work." | "Starting in March." |
| "You learned French?" | "Yeah, just basics." | "But I'm getting better." |

---

## 5.2 Expressing Doubt and Counter-Boasting

### Pattern: Challenge Achievement → Minimize It → Assert Importance

**Structure:**
```
[Challenge to achievement's importance]
+ [Minimization of achievement]
+ [Personal assertion of importance]
= Humble but confident tone
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"Maybe it's not that impressive. Marathon runners do 42km."
"But for me, it's huge."
```

**More Examples:**

| Challenge | Minimization | Assertion |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| "5km is short." | "It's nothing compared to marathons." | "But it's my personal win." |
| "I made the team." | "Loads of people make it." | "But I trained hard for it." |
| "Got a B grade." | "It's not an A." | "But it's progress for me." |

---

### Pattern: Expressing Personal Growth Through Timeline

**Structure:**
```
[Time period 1] + [Limited ability]
+ [Time period 2] + [Improved ability]
+ [Time period 3] + [Major achievement]
+ [Reflection on growth]
```

**Example from Text:**
```
"When I first walked in March, even 1km was hard.
When I first tried running in April, I was gasping after 500 meters.
When I attempted the full route in May, I had to walk at 4km.
But now I run 5km non-stop."
```

**Timeline Structure:**
```
Three months ago → barely 1km
Two months ago → gasped at 500m
One month ago → walked at 4km
Today → 5km non-stop
```

**Practice Creating Your Own Timeline:**

Try this pattern with your own achievement:
```
[Time 1]: I could barely [action].
[Time 2]: I managed [better action].
[Time 3]: I achieved [best action].
Now: I'm [current state].
```

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

## 7.1 Fragment Sentences (불완전한 문장)

### "Non-stop. 5km. Fine. Still fine. A little tough, but manageable."

**Original Korean:** "쉬지 않고. 5km. 괜찮다. 여전히 괜찮다. 좀 힘들지만 할 만하다."

**Analysis:**
- 완전한 문장(주어+동사)이 아닌 단어나 구만 사용
- 일기와 캐주얼한 대화에서 흔함
- 즉각적인 반응과 속도감을 전달

**Fragment Types:**

| Type | Example | Full Sentence |
|------|---------|---------------|
| 단어만 | "Non-stop." | "I ran non-stop." |
| 동사구 | "Caught my breath." | "I caught my breath." |
| 형용사 | "Fine." | "Everything is fine." |
| 과거분사 | "Shaking." | "My legs were shaking." |
| 명사 + 형용사 | "Heart pounding." | "My heart was pounding." |

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I ran non-stop 5km." (fragment와 complete 섞임)
✅ "Non-stop. 5km." (consistent fragments)
✅ "I ran non-stop for 5km." (complete sentence)

## 7.2 Filler Words and Hedging

### "I don't know why."

**Original Korean:** "왜 그런지 모르겠다."

- 명확한 이유가 없는 상황에서의 정직한 표현
- 특히 신체 감각에 대해 설명할 수 없을 때 사용
- 청자의 공감과 이해를 이끄는 표현

**Filler/Hedging Words in Running Context:**

|-----------|---------|---------|

| "kind of" | 완화 | "It was kind of hard." |
| "sort of" | 완화 | "I was sort of tired." |
| "maybe" | 불확실성 | "Maybe I slept well." |
| "somehow" | 설명 불가능 | "Somehow I just felt ready." |
| "I guess" | 추측 | "I guess I was in good shape." |

# PART 8: SENTENCE STARTERS (문장 시작 표현)

## 8.1 Personal Reflection Starters

### "But today was different."

**Original Korean:** "하지만 오늘은 달랐다."

**Analysis:**
- Contrast 표현으로 새로운 방향 제시
- "different" = 예상과 다른, 특별한
- 이야기의 전환점을 표현

**Contrast Starters:**

| Starter | Nuance | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| "But today was different." | 긍정적 전환 | "Usually I struggle. But today was different." |
| "But this time was different." | 이번이 특별함 | "I've tried many times. But this time was different." |
| "However, this time..." | formal | "Previous attempts failed. However, this time..." |
| "Yet today..." | literary | "I doubted myself. Yet today I succeeded." |
| "This time, though..." | casual | "Last time was hard. This time, though, I was ready." |

---

### "Anyway, my legs were moving smoothly."

**Original Korean:** "어쨌든 다리가 잘 움직였다."

- 설명의 근거나 원인 제시를 생략하고 결과로 이동

- 자연스러운 일상적 표현

**"Anyway" Usage:**

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| 이유 생략 | "I don't know why. Anyway, I felt great." |
| 화제 전환 | "That was frustrating. Anyway, let's talk about today's run." |
| 요점 강조 | "Details don't matter. Anyway, I did it." |
| 결론으로 이동 | "Whatever the reason. Anyway, I succeeded." |

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Telling a Friend About Your Achievement

```
Friend: Hey, how was your run?
You: Amazing! I ran 5km without stopping for the first time.
Friend: No way! That's impressive!
You: I know, right? I'm shocked. When I started running in April, I could barely do 500 meters.
Friend: So what was different today?
You: Honestly, I don't know. My body just felt light. My legs were responding well. And I decided to just keep pushing.
Friend: What was your time?
You: 28 minutes. About 5 and a half minutes per kilometer.
Friend: That's actually a decent pace!
You: Thanks! I want to either run 7km next or get faster at 5km.
```

---

### Scenario 2: Discussing with Your Running Mentor

```
Mentor: I heard you had a good run?
You: Yeah, it was incredible. I ran the whole 5km without walking.
Mentor: How did you feel during it?
You: At 1km, I felt fine. At 2km, still okay. By 3km, it was getting tough. But at 4km, instead of walking like I usually do, I decided to keep running.
Mentor: And you made it?
You: I did. My heart was pounding, my breathing was ragged, but I pushed through.
Mentor: That's real progress. You should be proud.
You: I am. Three months ago, even 1km was hard.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Having Self-Doubt Despite Achievement

```
Friend: You just ran 5km non-stop! Why do you look worried?
You: Well, marathon runners do 42km. 5km isn't that much.
Friend: Are you kidding? That's amazing!
You: Maybe, but for a real runner, it probably seems easy.
Friend: You're not a "real runner" yet. You're becoming one. There's a difference. And you should celebrate that.
You: Yeah, I guess you're right. I just... I want to keep improving.
Friend: And you will. But give yourself credit for today.
You: Okay. Today was actually incredible.
```

## 10.2 Writing Prompts

### Prompt 1: Your Personal Milestone

Write about a time when you achieved something you thought was impossible. Use the structure:
- When I first tried [activity], I could barely...
- [Time period] later, I...
- Today, I...
- This shows me that...

**Example Opening:**
"When I first started learning English, I could barely understand a sentence. Six months later, I could have simple conversations. Today, I can watch movies without subtitles..."
