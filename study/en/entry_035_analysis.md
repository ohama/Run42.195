# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Encouragement & Motivation

### "Just a little more."

**Original Korean:** "조금만 더."

**Analysis:**
- Simple but powerful motivational phrase
- Often used as self-talk or encouragement
- Present tense suggests immediate action/decision

**Similar Encouragement Phrases:**

| Expression | Intensity | Formality | Context |
|------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "Just a little more." | ★★★ | Casual | 자기 자신/친구 격려 |
| "You can do it." | ★★★ | Casual | 일반 응원 |
| "Almost there." | ★★★ | Casual | 곧 끝난다는 뜻 |
| "Keep going." | ★★★ | Casual | 계속하라는 뜻 |
| "Don't give up." | ★★★★ | Casual | 포기하지 말라 |
| "One more mile." | ★★★★ | Casual | 마일/거리 중심 |
| "You've got this." | ★★★★ | Very casual | 미국식, 자신감 표현 |

**Internal Monologue vs. Out Loud:**
```
(Internal) Just a little more. (속으로 중얼거리며)
(Out loud) Just a little more! I can make it! (큰 목소리로)
```

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
Runner A: I'm exhausted. I can't go further.
Runner B: Come on, just a little more. We're almost there.
Runner A: I don't know if I can...
Runner B: Yes you can. Just a little more and we can rest.
```

## 1.2 Practical Advice & Suggestions

### "Drink lots of water. Rest when it gets too hot."

**Original Korean:** "물 많이 마셔. 더우면 쉬어."

**Grammar Point:** Imperative + compound instruction

**Structure:** 명령문 + 명령문 (나열식 조언)

**These are direct commands typical in:**
- Parents advising children
- Coaches instructing athletes
- Elders giving practical wisdom

**Advice Patterns - Formality Spectrum:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Drink lots of water." | "You should drink plenty of water." | "It is advisable to drink plenty of water." |
| "Rest when it's hot." | "You should rest when it gets hot." | "One should rest during high temperatures." |
| "Don't push yourself." | "You shouldn't push yourself too hard." | "It is not recommended to exert yourself excessively." |

**Health & Exercise Advice Phrases:**
```
"Hydrate regularly."
"Take breaks when needed."
"Don't overdo it."
"Pace yourself."
"Listen to your body."
"Know your limits."
```

### "Once the sun rises, it gets hot fast."

**Original Korean:** "해가 나면 더워지는 게 빨라."

**Grammar Point:** Adverbial clause (시간 표현) + Verb

**Structure:** Once + 시간 표현, 결과

**"Once" Meaning:**
- 일단 ~하면
- 어느 순간부터는

**Other Time Adverbial Clauses:**

| Starter | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Once | "Once you start, it's hard to stop." | 일단 시작하면 |
| When | "When the sun comes up, it gets hot." | 언제 |
| As soon as | "As soon as the sun rose, heat hit." | 바로 |
| The moment | "The moment the sun appeared, temperature shot up." | 바로 그 순간 |
| After | "After sunrise, we couldn't run anymore." | ~한 후 |

**Natural Variations:**
- "Once the sun rises, the heat is overwhelming."
- "It gets hot fast once the sun comes up."
- "The moment the sun rises, the temperature shoots up."

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
Grandpa: Once the sun rises, it gets hot fast.
Boy: That's why I'm running so early.
Grandpa: Smart. The early bird gets the cooler morning.
Boy: Will it get hotter today?
Grandpa: Probably. Summer's just started.
```

### "Still gonna be over 30 degrees."

**Original Korean:** "그래도 30도 이상이긴 할 거야."

**"Still gonna be" Pattern:**
- Acknowledges progress but notes continuing challenge
- "Gonna" = going to (very casual, conversational)

**Realistic Expectation Setting:**

| Acknowledgment | Reality Check | Full Exchange |
|---|---|---|
| "Should cool down" | "Still gonna be over 30" | "It should cool down once the sun sets." / "Still gonna be over 30 degrees." |
| "It'll get better" | "Still rough though" | "The afternoon will be better." / "Still rough compared to morning." |
| "At least it's something" | "Not much help really" | "At least we tried." / "Not much help, but okay." |

**Pronunciation Notes:**
- "gonna" = /ɡɑnə/ (casual, written as "going to" in formal writing)
- "over 30" = /ˈoʊvər ˈθɜrti/ (초과)

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "What time did you leave?"

**Question asking for specific time information**

**Structure:** What + auxiliary verb + subject + verb?

**Time-Asking Patterns:**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "What time did you wake up?" | "5 AM." / "At 5 o'clock." |
| "What time did you leave?" | "5:15." / "At a quarter past five." |
| "What time did you arrive?" | "6:40." / "Forty minutes later." |
| "When do you usually run?" | "In the morning." / "At dawn." |

**Time Expression Responses:**
- Specific hour: "5 o'clock"
- Specific minute: "5:15" / "5 fifteen"
- General: "in the morning" / "at dawn"
- Duration: "40 minutes"

---

## Pattern 2: "I can barely/just manage to [verb]"

**Expression of difficulty with accomplishment**

**Structure:** I + can + barely/just + infinitive

**Examples with Effort:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "can barely" | "I can barely keep up." |
| "can just" | "I can just make it." |
| "can hardly" | "I can hardly manage." |
| "can scarcely" | "I can scarcely breathe." (formal) |
| "can manage" | "I can manage it." |

**Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you finish the 5 km run?
B: I can barely manage it every time.
A: It gets easier with practice.
B: I hope so. Right now it's exhausting.
```

---

## Pattern 3: "Thanks to [reason], [result]"

**Attribution of positive outcome to specific cause**

**Structure:** Thanks to + noun phrase, + result

**From Text:** "Thanks to leaving earlier."

| Reason | Result | Full Sentence |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| 일찍 나감 | 5km 달림 | "Thanks to leaving earlier, I ran 5 km." |

```

Minseo: "5:15."

```
