# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Making Deliberate Choices

### "on purpose"

**Original Text:** "Today I walked to school on purpose."

**Korean Equivalent:** 일부러, 의도적으로

**Analysis:**
- 핵심 표현: 우연이 아닌 의도적 행동임을 강조
- 반대말: by accident, accidentally (실수로)
- 문맥에 따라 긍정적(목표 달성) 또는 부정적(고의적 잘못) 뉘앙스

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "on purpose" | "intentionally" | "deliberately" |
| "I meant to" | "It was deliberate" | "It was a conscious decision" |
| "I did it on purpose" | "I did it intentionally" | "I acted with intention" |

**Nuance Spectrum:**

| Expression | Nuance | Example |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "on purpose" | 일부러 (중립/캐주얼) | "I walked on purpose." |
| "intentionally" | 의도적으로 (격식) | "I intentionally left early." |
| "deliberately" | 의도적으로/신중히 (격식) | "She deliberately chose this path." |
| "by design" | 계획적으로 | "It happened by design, not by chance." |
| "I meant to" | ~하려고 했어 | "I meant to call you." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you miss the bus again?
B: No, I walked on purpose.
A: On purpose? Why would you do that?
B: I actually like walking now. It's my choice.
A: That's different. You used to hate walking.
B: I know. Things change.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ "I did it on the purpose." (the 불필요)
- ✅ "I did it on purpose."
- ❌ "I on purpose walked to school." (어순 오류)
- ✅ "I walked to school on purpose."

**Related Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "It wasn't an accident" | 사고가 아니었어 | "It wasn't an accident—I planned it." |
| "I chose to" | ~하기로 선택했어 | "I chose to walk." |
| "It was a choice" | 선택이었어 | "It was a choice, not a necessity." |

### "It was a choice. My choice."

**Original Text:** "I didn't miss the bus. It was a choice. My choice to walk."

**Korean Equivalent:** 선택이었다. 내 선택.

**Grammar Point:** Emphasis through repetition and short sentences

**Structure Analysis:**
```
[Negation of assumption] + [Declaration] + [Ownership]
"I didn't miss the bus." + "It was a choice." + "My choice to walk."
```

**Why This Structure Works:**
- 짧은 문장으로 강조 효과
- "My choice"로 주체성 강조
- 독자/청자의 오해를 바로잡음

**Similar Patterns for Emphasizing Ownership:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "It was my decision" | "It was my decision to quit." |
| "I decided for myself" | "I decided for myself." |
| "Nobody made me" | "Nobody made me do it." |
| "It was entirely my choice" | "It was entirely my choice." |
| "I own this decision" | "I own this decision." |

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"I didn't fail. I chose to stop. My choice."
"It wasn't forced. It was a choice. My choice to stay."
"They didn't fire me. I quit. It was my choice."
```

**Korean Equivalent:** 알람을 세 개나 맞춰놨어 / 세 개 설정했어

```
B: So much better. I saw the cherry blossoms starting to bloom.

B: Exactly. And the morning air was so refreshing.
A: Sounds nice.
B: It was. I felt strangely peaceful.
A: Are you going to keep walking?
B: I think so. Maybe twice a week for now.
A: That's great. You've really changed.
B: Maybe I have.
```

## 10.5 Discussion Questions

1. **Have you ever done something "on purpose" that surprised others?** What was it? Why did you choose to do it?

2. **Do you prefer walking or taking transportation?** What are the advantages and disadvantages of each?

3. **Describe a time when you noticed nature because you were moving slowly.** What did you see that you might have missed otherwise?

4. **The narrator says "My mind was at ease" when walking by choice vs. walking because they missed the bus.** Do you agree that the same activity can feel different based on whether it's a choice or necessity?

5. **What does "taking it slow" mean to you?** In what areas of life do you prefer to take it slow?

6. **The narrator says walking took the same time but "felt different."** Can you think of other examples where mindset changes how you experience something?

7. **Would you walk for over an hour to avoid something you dislike (like motion sickness)?** Why or why not?
