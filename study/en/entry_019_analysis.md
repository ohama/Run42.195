# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Oversleeping & Morning Rush

### "I overslept today."

**Original Korean:** "오늘 늦잠을 잤다"

**Analysis:**
- Simple statement of a common problem
- Sets up the entire chain of events
- Past tense for completed action

**Similar Morning Problem Statements:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "I overslept today." | 오늘 늦잠을 잤다 |
| "I slept in." | 늦게까지 잤다 |
| "I slept through my alarm." | 알람도 못 들었다 |
| "I woke up late." | 늦게 깼다 |

**Morning Excuse Patterns:**
```
"I overslept today. Maybe because I stayed up late."
"I didn't hear my alarm. By the time I woke up, it was already 6:50."
"I slept in and completely lost track of time."
```

---

### "Maybe because I stayed up late last night for the Children's Day holiday."

**Original Korean:** "어제 어린이날이라고 밤새 늦게까지 놀았나."

**"Stay up late" + Reason:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "stayed up late for the holiday" | 명절이라고 밤새 있었다 |
| "stayed up late watching TV" | TV 보느라 늦게까지 있었다 |
| "stayed up late playing games" | 게임하느라 밤을 샜다 |

**Cause and Effect in Telling Time:**
```
"I stayed up late last night. That's probably why I didn't hear my alarm."
"We had the holiday, so I was up late. This morning I overslept."
"I couldn't sleep at a normal time because of the festivities."
```

---

### "Thirty minutes later than usual."

**Original Korean:** "평소보다 30분이나 늦었다"

**Time Delay Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "thirty minutes late" | 30분 늦었다 |
| "half an hour behind schedule" | 30분 늦었다 |
| "thirty minutes off schedule" | 일정상 30분 밀렸다 |
| "I lost thirty minutes" | 30분을 낭비했다 |

**Calculating Time Pressure:**
```
"Thirty minutes later than usual. I need to leave at 6:40."
"I'm thirty minutes down. Can I still make it?"
"Losing thirty minutes means losing my margin for error."
```

---

### "If I leave now, I'll arrive at 8:20."

**Original Korean:** "지금 가면 8시 20분에 도착할 것 같다"

**Time Calculation Pattern:**

**Structure:** If I [verb] now, I'll [arrive/get there] at [time].

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| "If I leave now, I'll arrive at 8:20." | 지금 가면 8시 20분에 도착한다 |
| "If I run, I can make the 8:00 bus." | 뛰면 8시 버스를 탈 수 있다 |
| "If I hurry, I'll be there by 8:30." | 서두르면 8시 30분에 도착한다 |

## 1.2 Decision-Making Under Pressure

### "Walking won't cut it."

**Original Korean:** "걸어서는 안 된다"

**"[Option] won't cut it" = Not sufficient:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "Walking won't cut it." | 걸어서는 부족하다 |
| "That won't cut it." | 그건 충분하지 않다 |
| "This approach won't cut it." | 이 방식으로는 안 된다 |
| "Waiting won't cut it." | 기다려서는 안 된다 |

**What Are My Options Pattern:**
```
"Walking won't cut it. What do I do?"
"Waiting thirty-five minutes won't cut it either."
"The bus is too slow. It won't cut it."
"I need something faster."
```

---

### "What do I do?"

**Original Korean:** "어떡하지?"

**Internal Panic Questions:**

| Expression | Type | Korean |
|------------|------|--------|
| "What do I do?" | question | 어떡하지? |
| "What am I supposed to do?" | helplessness | 어떻게 해야 하지? |
| "Now what?" | frustration | 이제 뭐? |
| "Should I try running?" | brainstorm | 달려봐야 할까? |

**Rhetorical vs Real Question:**
- "What do I do?" (with ?) - seeking actual solutions
- "What do I do." (with .) - rhetorical, expressing panic

---

### "Bus? If I head to the bus stop now, I can catch the 7:20 bus."

**Original Korean:** "버스? 지금 정류장으로 가면 7시 20분 버스를 탈 수 있을 것 같다"

**Considering Options Style:**

**Structure:** [Option]? If [condition], [result].

```
"Bus? If I run to the stop, I might catch the 7:20."
"Walk? If I leave now, I'd get there by 8:40."
"Run? I've never done that before."
```

**Option Evaluation:**
```
"The 7:20 bus would get me there around 8:00."
"That gives me thirty minutes of buffer."
"But what if I miss it?"
```

---

### "Or."

**Original Korean:** "아니면."

**Transition to Next Idea:**
- Single word indicating a new option is coming
- Creates suspense and signals a decision point
- Common in diary/internal monologue style

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Starting to Run

### "I started running."

**Original Korean:** "달리기를 시작했다"

**Simple Action Statement:**
- Marks a major turning point
- Short, direct, no hesitation
- Past tense shows commitment

**Running vs Walking Comparison:**

| Activity | Expression |
|----------|------------|
| Walking | "I started walking" |
| Running | "I started running" |
| Jogging | "I started jogging" |
| Sprinting | "I started sprinting" |

---

### "The stretch out of the village. About 1km. Usually takes ten minutes on foot."

**Original Korean:** "마을 밖 구간. 1km 정도. 보통 걸어서 10분 정도."

**Describing Distance & Time:**

**Structure:** [Location]. [Distance]. Normally takes [time].

```
"The steep hill. About 2km. Usually takes fifteen minutes."
"The bridge. About 500 meters. Takes five minutes."
"The main road. About 3km. Normally takes twenty minutes."
```

**"on foot" = by walking:**
```
"Usually takes ten minutes on foot."
"Five kilometers on foot takes about an hour."
"I've done this route many times on foot."
```

---

### "I moved my feet fast. Jogged. Ran."

**Original Korean:** "다리를 재빨리 움직였다. 조깅했다. 뛰었다"

**Progression of Pace:**

| Expression | Pace | Korean |
|------------|------|--------|
| "moved my feet fast" | building speed | 다리를 빠르게 움직였다 |
| "jogged" | moderate running | 조깅했다 |
| "ran" | full running | 뛰었다 |

**Building Momentum:**
```
"I started moving faster, then jogging, then full running."
"My pace increased gradually—fast walk, jog, then run."
"From walking to jogging to actually running."
```

---

### "My heart started pounding."

**Original Korean:** "심장이 철렁철렁 뛰기 시작했다"

**Heart Activity Vocabulary:**

| Expression | Korean | Intensity |
|------------|--------|-----------|
| "My heart started racing." | 심장이 빨라졌다 | ★★★★ |
| "My heart started pounding." | 심장이 철렁철렁 뛰었다 | ★★★★ |
| "My heartbeat quickened." | 맥박이 빨라졌다 | ★★★ |
| "I could feel my pulse." | 맥박을 느낄 수 있었다 | ★★★ |

**Physical Response to Exertion:**
```
"My heart started pounding the moment I began running."
"I could feel my heart racing in my chest."
"It was pounding so hard I thought it might burst."
```

---

### "My breathing grew heavy."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 거칠어졌다"

**Breathing During Exercise:**

| Expression | Korean | Intensity |
|------------|--------|-----------|
| "breathing a little fast" | 숨이 좀 빨라졌다 | ★★☆ |
| "breathing grew heavy" | 숨이 거칠어졌다 | ★★★ |
| "breathing hard" | 헉헉거리다 | ★★★★ |
| "gasping for air" | 숨을 헐떡이다 | ★★★★★ |

**Progressive Breathing Descriptions:**
```
"My breathing quickened, then grew heavy, then I was gasping."
"What started as heavy breathing became full-on panting."
"I could hear myself breathing louder and louder."
```

---

### "*This is hard.*"

**Original Korean:** "이건 힘들다"

**Immediate Physical Feedback:**
- Italics = internal monologue
- Present tense = experiencing it now
- Simple statement = no room for embellishment

**Internal Reactions to Difficulty:**
```
"*This is hard.*"
"*I'm dying.*"
"*Can't keep this up.*"
"*Why did I think I could do this?*"
```

## 2.2 The Struggle Point

### "By the 500m mark, I was already struggling."

**Original Korean:** "500m쯤에서 이미 힘들어졌다"

**Distance Marker Pattern:**

**Structure:** By/At the [distance] mark, [physical state].

| Example | Korean |
|---------|--------|
| "By the 500m mark, I was struggling." | 500m쯤에서 힘들어졌다 |
| "At the 1km point, my legs started hurting." | 1km 지점에서 다리가 아프기 시작했다 |
| "By the halfway point, I was exhausted." | 중간 지점에서 지쳤다 |

---

### "My legs ached. I was out of breath."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 아팠다. 숨이 찼다"

**Listing Physical Complaints:**

**Structure:** Short sentences describing separate problems.

```
"My legs ached. My lungs burned. My head felt light."
"Legs hurt. Out of breath. Wanting to quit."
"My muscles screamed. I couldn't breathe. I wanted to stop."
```

**"Out of breath" = unable to breathe properly:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "I was out of breath." | 숨이 찼다 |
| "I caught my breath." | 숨을 고르다 |
| "I was gasping for breath." | 숨을 헐떡이다 |
| "I'm breathless." | 숨이 차다 |

---

### "But I didn't stop."

**Original Korean:** "하지만 멈추지 않았다"

**Determination Despite Difficulty:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "But I didn't stop." | 하지만 멈추지 않았다 |
| "But I kept going." | 하지만 계속 갔다 |
| "But I pushed through." | 하지만 밀어붙였다 |
| "But I forced myself to continue." | 하지만 자신을 억지로 계속 갔다 |

**Three-Part Structure:**
1. Problem statement (My legs ached...)
2. Consequence (I was out of breath...)
3. Resolution (But I didn't stop.)

## 2.4 The Unexplained Good Feeling

### "I felt good."

**Original Korean:** "기분이 좋았다"

**Simple Statement of Unexpected Joy:**
- No explanation
- Contrast with the physical difficulty
- Sets up the mystery to be solved

**Variations:**
```
"I felt good. Really good."
"Something felt... good?"
"Somehow, I felt good."
"I couldn't believe it, but I felt good."
```

---

### "It was exhausting, I was out of breath, so why did I feel good?"

**Original Korean:** "힘들었는데, 숨이 찼는데, 왜 기분이 좋은가?"

**Paradox Expression:**

**Structure:** [Negative]. [Negative]. So why [positive]?

```
"My muscles hurt. I was exhausted. So why did I feel energized?"
"I was tired. I was sweating. So how was I smiling?"
"It was difficult. I was struggling. Yet I felt exhilarated?"
```

**The Contradiction:**
```
"Everything about it should have felt bad."
"But nothing about it felt bad."
"The physical challenge contradicted the mental feeling."
```

---

### "Something that came after running. Refreshed? A rush? I don't know what to call it."

**Original Korean:** "달린 후에 오는 뭔가. 상쾌한? 짜릿한? 뭐라고 불러야 할지 모르겠다"

**Describing Unfamiliar Sensations:**

| Possible Feeling | Korean |
|-----------------|--------|
| "refreshed" | 상쾌한 |
| "a rush" | 짜릿한 느낌 |
| "exhilarated" | 興奮된 |
| "energized" | 활기찬 |
| "high" | 우쩌한 느낌 |

**"I don't know what to call it" Pattern:**
```
"It's like... joy? Accomplishment? I don't have a word for it."
"A feeling I've never had before. I can't describe it."
"It was good. Really good. But I don't know the name for it."
```

---

### "But I definitely felt good."

**Original Korean:** "하지만 분명히 기분이 좋았다"

**Affirmation Despite Confusion:**

| Expression | Korean |
|------------|--------|
| "But I definitely felt good." | 하지만 분명히 좋았다 |
| "One thing was clear: I felt great." | 한 가지는 확실했다: 기분이 좋았다 |
| "Of that, I was certain." | 그것만은 확실했다 |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "*Should I try [action]?*"

**From Text:** "*Should I try running?*"

**Structure:** *Should I try [challenging action]?* (internal monologue)

**Examples:**
```
"*Should I try running?*"
"*Should I attempt it?*"
"*What if I did it?*"
"*Could I even do that?*"
```

**How It Works:**
- Italics indicate internal thought
- Shows decision-making moment
- Sets up the turning point

---

## Pattern 2: "[Difficulty]. [Difficulty]. But I didn't stop."

**From Text:** "My legs ached. I was out of breath. But I didn't stop."

**Structure:** [Complaint]. [Complaint]. But [determination].

**Examples:**
```
"My lungs burned. My legs screamed. But I kept going."
"Pain shot through me. I wanted to quit. But I pushed on."
"Every step hurt. Every breath was hard. But I continued."
```

---

## Pattern 3: "[Time]. Much faster than usual."

**From Text:** "Seven minutes. Three minutes faster than usual."

**Structure:** [Marker]. [Comparison with expectation].

**Examples:**
```
"7:12. Seven minutes had passed. Three minutes faster than my usual pace."
"My time was 45 minutes. That's 15 minutes faster than expected."
"I finished in record time. 20% quicker than before."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "*[Question]*. [Contradiction]."

**From Text:** "*Why did I feel good?* It was exhausting."

**Structure:** *[Why question]*. [But it was still positive].

**Examples:**
```
"*How was I smiling when I was so tired?*"
"*Why did everything feel right when it should feel wrong?*"
"*Where did this energy come from?*"
```

---

## Pattern 5: "[Sensation]. [Sensation]. I don't know what to call it."

**From Text:** "Refreshed? A rush? I don't know what to call it."

**Structure:** [Feeling]? [Feeling]? I don't know what to call it.

**Examples:**
```
"Energized? Alive? I couldn't name it."
"Joy? Accomplishment? It was something beyond words."
"Euphoria? Contentment? I lacked the vocabulary."
```

---

## Pattern 6: "Still [difficulty]. But something felt [change]."

**From Text:** "Still breathing hard. But something felt strange."

**Structure:** Still [negative condition]. But something felt [positive shift].

**Examples:**
```
"Still exhausted. But something inside felt lighter."
"Still struggling. But somehow everything seemed clearer."
"Still tired. But my heart felt full."
```

---

## Pattern 7: "This time it was [easier]. Compared to [reference point]."

**From Text:** "This time it was a bit easier. Compared to before."

**Structure:** This time [improvement]. Compared to [previous experience].

**Examples:**
```
"This time I felt stronger. Compared to the first run."
"This attempt felt better. Compared to my initial effort."
"The second try was smoother. Compared to the first attempt."
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: First Running Experience

```
A: Why are you so out of breath?
B: I just ran. For the first time.
A: Ran? Like, actually ran?
B: Yeah. About 1.5 kilometers.
A: That's incredible! How was it?
B: Hard. Really hard. My legs ached and I was gasping for air.
A: So why did you do it?
B: I overslept and had to make up time.
A: And? Did you regret it?
B: Weirdly, no. I felt... I don't know. Good?
A: Good? Even though it was so hard?
B: Yeah. It's strange. I was exhausted, but something felt amazing.
A: That sounds like runner's high!
B: Is that what it's called?
```

---

### Scenario 2: Comparing Before and After

```
A: You look different today.
B: Really?
A: Yeah. More... alive?
B: I ran to school this morning. And I think something's changed.
A: Running changed you?
B: Not just the running. What I discovered while running.
A: What do you mean?
B: I've been taking the bus every day. Same route. But I never really saw anything.
A: And now?
B: Now I see. The tangerine orchards. The flowers. Even the birds singing.
A: You never noticed those before?
B: Never. I was always stuck in that stuffy bus.
A: So running did more than give you exercise?
B: Way more. It opened my eyes.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Understanding Runner's High

```
A: So you actually ran?
B: Yeah, about 1.5 kilometers.
A: How was it?
B: Hard. Out of breath. My legs hurt.
A: Sounds terrible.
B: That's what I thought too. But then... something happened.
A: What?
B: After I ran, I felt amazing. Like nothing I've ever felt before.
A: You felt amazing after something hard?
B: I know, it doesn't make sense.
A: Actually, it does. That's endorphins.
B: Endorphins?
A: Yeah. When you run, your brain releases chemicals that make you feel good.
B: So I wasn't imagining it?
A: Not at all. It's real. And now that you've felt it, you'll want to do it again.
B: Maybe I will.
```
