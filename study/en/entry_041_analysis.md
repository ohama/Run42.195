# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Morning Routine & Running Expressions

### "I should run."

**Original Korean:** "뛰어야겠다" / "뛸까"

**Analysis:**
- Simple statement of intention
- Shows shift from hesitation to determination
- Used as internal monologue (private thought)

**Variations by Certainty:**

| Expression | Certainty | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I might run." | ★☆☆ | Considering the option |
| "I should run." | ★★☆ | Ought to, but not certain |
| "I'll run." | ★★★ | Decision made |
| "I'm going to run." | ★★★★ | Definite plan |
| "I have to run." | ★★★★★ | No choice, obligation |

**Contrast with Monday:**
> "Not like Monday, when I thought maybe I'll take the bus."

The grammar shift matters:
- "maybe I'll take the bus" = uncertain, hesitant
- "I should run" = more committed

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you go running this morning?
B: Yeah, I actually did. I woke up and thought, "I should run."
A: Even though you were tired?
B: Especially because I was tired. I needed to break out of my slump.
```

---

### "I was in a bit of a slump."

**Original Korean:** "좀 슬럼프였어"

**"Slump" Definition:**
- A period of poor performance or motivation
- Business slump = recession
- Athletic slump = poor performance period
- Can also mean emotional downturn

**Slump Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Usage |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| "I'm a bit off." | ★☆☆ | Slightly unmotivated |
| "I'm in a slump." | ★★☆ | Standard way |
| "I'm in a real slump." | ★★★ | More serious |
| "I've hit a wall." | ★★★ | Lost all motivation |
| "I've completely lost it." | ★★★★★ | Severe downturn |

**Related Phrases:**
- "I was in a funk." (더 캐주얼)
- "I was in a rut." (같은 패턴의 반복)
- "I lost my mojo." (흥미를 잃었어 - 매우 캐주얼)
- "I couldn't get motivated." (동기부여가 안 됐어)

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's been going on lately?
B: Honestly, I was in a bit of a slump.
A: About what?
B: Running. I just didn't feel like doing it.
A: How long did it last?
B: About a week. But I'm out of it now.
```

---

### "Didn't want to run."

**Original Korean:** "뛸 마음이 안 났어"

**"Want to" vs. "Feel like" (비교):**

| Expression | Nuance | Example |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I want to run." | Strong desire | 적극적으로 원함 |
| "I feel like running." | Spontaneous urge | 하고 싶은 기분 |
| "I don't want to run." | Rejection | 거부감 |
| "I didn't feel like running." | Lack of motivation | 마음이 없음 |
| "I have to run." | Obligation | 해야 할 의무 |

**Context Matters:**
> "Didn't want to run. Why am I even doing this?"

The question reveals self-doubt, not just laziness.

## 1.2 Casual Conversation Responses

### "Oh, really? How?"

**Original Korean:** "어? 어떻게?"

**Follow-up Question Pattern:**
- Shows interest
- Invites explanation
- Casual tone

**Variations:**

| Expression | Tone | Situation |
|------------|------|-----------|
| "Oh, really?" | Neutral interest | Acknowledging news |
| "How?" | Direct | Asking for method |
| "How'd you do that?" | Friendly | Impressed/curious |
| "How'd you manage that?" | Appreciative | Impressed |
| "What did you do?" | Curious | Wanting details |

---

### "Taking the bus showed me."

**Original Korean:** "(버스 탄 것이) 깨달음을 줬어"

**"Show" meaning "teach/reveal":**

| Usage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| "X showed me [lesson]" | X taught me/revealed | "The bus showed me I prefer running." |
| "X showed me [truth]" | X revealed | "Failure showed me what matters." |
| "X showed me how to [verb]" | X taught method | "My friend showed me how to run better." |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "Taking the bus made me realize..."
- "The bus ride made me understand..."
- "I discovered something taking the bus..."

---

### "Running's still better."

**Original Korean:** "역시 뛰는 게 낫지"

**"Still" meanings in this context:**

| Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|
| 여전히 | "It's still raining." |
| 그래도 (양보) | "He's difficult, but still my friend." |
| 결국/역시 | "Running's still better." (결과가 같음/여전히 같은 결론) |

**Pattern: "X is still better."**
- Shows comparison after reconsideration
- After testing alternative, returning to original choice

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Did you really take the bus on Monday?
B: Yeah, I was tired of running.
A: And?
B: Well, running's still better. The bus was crowded and uncomfortable.
```

---

### "By a lot."

**Original Korean:** "훨씬"

**Emphasis Adverbs:**

| Expression | Intensity | Example |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "Better." | ★☆☆ | Slight improvement |
| "Much better." | ★★☆ | Significant improvement |
| "Way better." | ★★★ | Much more better (very casual) |
| "Better by a lot." | ★★★ | Clear, significant difference |
| "So much better." | ★★★ | Very casual, emotional |

# PART 3: RUNNING ACHIEVEMENT & MOTIVATION (달리기 성취 표현)

## 3.1 Distance & Performance

### "I ran the full 7 km."

**Original Korean:** "온전히 7km를 뛰었다"

**"Full" meanings:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "ran the full 7 km" | 7km 전체를 | Complete distance |
| "ran the entire 7 km" | 처음부터 끝까지 | Whole distance |
| "completed 7 km" | 7km를 마쳤다 | Finished distance |

**Running Achievement Language:**
- Emphasizes completion
- Shows persistence
- Contrasts with "didn't walk once"

---

### "Didn't walk once. Ran the whole way."

**Original Korean:** "한 번도 안 걸었어. 내내 뛰었어"

**Two-Statement Emphasis:**
- First statement: What DIDN'T happen
- Second statement: What DID happen
- Creates contrast/strength

- "Didn't stop once. Kept going the whole way."
- "Didn't give up. Finished strong."
- "Never looked back. Just kept running."

---

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

## 4.1 Breaking Through Difficulty

### "break out of the slump"

**Original Korean:** "슬럼프에서 벗어나다"

**"Break out of" meanings:**

| Usage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| "break out of prison" | 감옥에서 탈출 | Escape from confinement |
| "break out of a slump" | 슬럼프에서 벗어남 | Escape difficulty |
| "break out of routine" | 루틴에서 벗어남 | Change daily pattern |
| "break out of habit" | 습관에서 벗어남 | Stop repeating pattern |

**"Break out" vs. "Get out of":**

| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "break out of" | Active effort, forceful |
| "get out of" | More neutral, passive |
| "escape from" | Desperate, sudden |

**Related Phrasal Verbs:**
- "come out of" (벗어나다 - 자연스럽게)
- "pull out of" (벗어나다 - 노력해서)
- "snap out of" (정신차리다 - 갑자기)

---

**Original Korean:** "이겨냈다"

**"Get through" meanings:**

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Difficulty | 견디어내다 | "I got through the slump." |
| Conversation | 통화를 하다 | "I finally got through to her." |
| Thick material | 관통하다 | "The bullet got through the wall." |
| Understanding | 이해시키다 | "Can you help me get this through to him?" |

**Related Phrases:**
- "I made it through." (겨우 버텼다)
- "I survived it." (살아남았다)
- "I pulled through." (극복했다)

---

### "back to it" (다시 시작하다)

**"Back to" Construction:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "I escaped the slump."

**Structure:** Subject + verb (past tense) + specific difficulty

**Uses:** Achievement announcement
- Sharing good news
- Brief update
- Showing progress

**Full Context:**
```
"Hey, I escaped the slump."
"Oh, really? How?"
"Taking the bus made me realize. Running's still better."
```

**Similar Achievement Announcements:**
- "I got through it."
- "I made it past the hard part."
- "I'm over the worst."
- "I finally feel better."

---

## Pattern 2: "Why did X happen?"

**Structure:** Why + past tense + question about cause

**From Text:**
> "Why did the slump happen? Tired? The heat? Lost motivation? Probably all of the above."

**Rhetorical Questions:**
- Speaker asks but doesn't expect answer
- Lists possible causes
- Concludes with summary

**Similar Patterns:**
- "What went wrong? Lack of sleep? Too much stress? Both?"
- "Why did I quit? Boredom? Frustration? Everything?"
- "What made me change? Time? Experience? Just maturity?"

---

## Pattern 3: "But the important thing is..."

**Structure:** But + importance statement + [lesson learned]

**Shifting Focus:**
- From causes to solutions
- From problems to wisdom
- From questions to answers

**From Text:**
> "But the important thing is I got through it."

**Similar Constructions:**
- "The thing is, I learned something valuable."
- "What matters is that I kept going."
- "The key is accepting that it happened."
- "The real lesson is not giving up."

---

## Pattern 4: "Taking the bus showed me. I need to run."

**Structure:** [Experience] + showed me + [realization]

**Two-Sentence Format:**
- First: What happened
- Second: What it revealed

**Uses:**
- Comparing alternatives
- Testing choice
- Confirming preference

**Full Insight Process:**
1. Experiment (taking bus)
2. Realization (I prefer running)
3. Confirmation (running is better)

---

## Pattern 5: "I feel X sometimes too."

**Structure:** "I + feel + emotion + sometimes + too" = showing commonality

**Empathy Expression:**
- Shows speaker also experiences this
- Reduces isolation of listener
- Creates connection

**Similar Supportive Responses:**
- "I get lazy sometimes too."
- "I struggle with that sometimes too."
- "I understand. It happens to me too."
- "I know what you mean. Me too."

---

## Pattern 6: "On Saturdays when I go to practice."

**Structure:** Time period + when + specific event

**Provides Context:**
- Explains when feeling occurs
- Makes experience relatable
- Shows understanding of specific situations

**Similar Examples:**
- "On Mondays when I'm tired."
- "When I wake up early."
- "During difficult projects."
- "When it's hot outside."

---

## Pattern 7: "What matters is not quitting completely."

**Structure:** "What matters is" + [key understanding]

**Wisdom Statement:**
- Identifies principle
- Generalizes from experience
- Offers advice

**Similar Wisdom Statements:**
- "What matters is trying again."
- "What matters is persistence."
- "What matters is not giving up for good."
- "The key is acceptance."

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Friend Notices Your Change

```
A: You seem different today. Better mood?
B: Yeah, actually. I broke out of my slump.
A: What slump? I didn't even know.
B: Running. I just didn't feel like doing it for a week.
A: So what changed?
B: I took the bus Monday and realized how much I prefer running.
A: Wait, you took the bus? By choice?
B: Yeah. I was so unmotivated. But the bus was awful.
A: And that helped?
B: Totally. It made me realize what I was missing.
A: So you're back to running daily?
B: Yeah. With summer break, I can run every day now.
```

### Scenario 2: Friend Shares Their Own Struggle

```
A: I feel lazy sometimes too. On practice days especially.
B: Really? You always seem so motivated.
A: Not really. But I feel great after running, you know?
B: That must be it then. Thinking about how good it feels.
A: Exactly. That gets me going.
B: I'm going to remember that. When I don't feel like running.
A: It helps. Just think about that post-run feeling.
B: Yeah. I'll try that tomorrow morning.
```

### Scenario 3: Discussing Slumps with an Adult (Old Man Yeongcheol-style)

```
A: I've been in a bit of a slump with my running.
B: That happens to everyone. Whatever you do, there'll be times you don't want to.
A: Right? It's like, why am I even doing this?
B: That's normal. The important thing is getting back to it.
A: That's what I did. I felt better immediately.
B: Good for you. Most people just give up.
A: I almost did. But I realized running is better than the alternative.
B: There you go. That's wisdom.
```

### Scenario 4: Motivation Check-In

```
A: How are things with running now?
B: Really good. I escaped the slump.
A: What got you through it?
B: Honestly? Taking a break. I took the bus instead.
A: Wait, that helped? How?
B: I realized how much I missed it. And how good it feels.
A: By a lot better?
B: Yeah, by a lot. Summer break started, so I'm training hard.
A: That's awesome. What's your goal?
B: Just run every day and give it my all for now.
```

## 10.2 Writing Prompts

### Prompt 1: Your Own "Slump" Experience

Write about a time you lost motivation for something you normally enjoy.

```
Include:
- What activity or hobby it was
- How long the slump lasted
```
