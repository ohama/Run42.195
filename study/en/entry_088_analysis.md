# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running-Related Everyday Phrases

### "Taking on 25km for the first time."

**Original Korean:** "처음으로 25km에 도전한다"

**Analysis:**
- "Take on"은 도전의 뉘앙스를 포함한 더 적극적인 표현
- "Try"보다 더 의지적이고 강함

**Variations by Nuance:**

| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| "I'm trying 25km." | 시도한다 | ★★☆ |
| "I'm running 25km today." | 오늘 25km를 뛴다 | ★★☆ |
| "I'm taking on 25km." | 25km에 도전한다 | ★★★ |
| "I'm attempting 25km." | 25km를 시도해본다 | ★★★ |
| "I'm tackling 25km." | 25km에 맞서다 | ★★★★ |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's your goal for today?
B: Taking on 25km for the first time.
A: That's ambitious! Are you ready?
B: I hope so. I've been training for this.
A: You've got this!
```

### "My body's warmed up now."

**Original Korean:** "몸이 풀렸다"

**Grammar Point:** Present perfect to describe a physical state change

**Structure:** My body's + warmed up / loosened up / adjusted

**Running Warm-up Terminology:**

| Stage | Expression | Korean |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Before run | "I need to warm up." | 몸을 풀어야 해 |
| Warming up | "I'm getting warmed up." | 몸이 풀리고 있어 |
| Warmed up | "My body's warmed up now." | 몸이 풀렸다 |
| Fully ready | "I'm ready to push harder." | 이제 속도를 올릴 준비됐어 |

**Similar Physical State Changes:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "My legs are warmed up." | 다리가 풀렸다 |
| "I'm finally finding my rhythm." | 드디어 리듬을 찾았다 |
| "My breathing is settling." | 호흡이 정상화됐다 |
| "I've hit my stride." | 제 페이스를 찾았다 |

### "This part I know."

**Original Korean:** "이 구간은 아는 곳이다"

**Nuance:** 익숙함, 자신감

**Context Shift During Long Run:**
- 초반: "This part I know." = 이미 몇 번 뛴 구간이라 안심
- 중반: "This part is new." = 처음 가는 구간, 미지의 영역
- 후반: "I don't know this part yet." = 아직 경험 못한 거리

**Similar Expressions:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "I've done this before." | 전에 해봤어 |
| "I know this route." | 이 길을 알아 |
| "I'm in familiar territory." | 익숙한 영역이야 |
| "This is new to me." | 처음이야 |
| "I've never gone this far." | 여기까지 와본 적 없어 |

## 1.2 Race/Distance Terminology

### "The sun came up."

**Original Korean:** "햇빛이 떠올랐다"

**Context:** 5:00am 출발했으므로, 10km 즈음에 해가 뜬다는 의미

**Time-Based Running Expressions:**

| Time of Day | Expression | Context |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| Very early | "It was still dark." | 아직 어두웠다 |
| Dawn | "The sun came up." | 해가 떠올랐다 |
| Morning | "The light was getting brighter." | 점점 밝아졌다 |
| Midday | "The sun was beating down." | 햇빛이 내리쬐었다 |
| Afternoon | "The light was fading." | 해가 질 준비 중 |
| Evening | "It was getting dark." | 어두워지고 있었다 |

**Sensory Details of Different Times:**

```
Early morning: cold, quiet, stars visible
Dawn: sky lightening, air still cool
Morning: warming up, other runners appearing
Afternoon: heat building, shadows shortening
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Leg & Fatigue Sensations

### "My legs started getting heavy."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 무거워지기 시작했다"

**Leg Fatigue Spectrum During Long Run:**

| Kilometer | Sensation | Expression |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 5-10km | Fresh, responsive | "My legs feel light." |
| 10-15km | Loosening up | "My legs are warming up." |
| 15-20km | Slight fatigue | "My legs are getting a bit heavy." |
| 20-25km | Noticeable weight | "My legs started getting heavy." |
| 25-30km | Significant fatigue | "My legs were incredibly heavy." |
| 30km+ | Extreme fatigue | "My legs barely respond." |

**Related Heavy/Tired Leg Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "My legs feel tired." | ★★☆ | 피곤한 느낌 |
| "My legs are getting heavy." | ★★★ | 무거워지는 느낌 |
| "My legs are screaming." | ★★★★ | 극심한 통증 |
| "I can barely move my legs." | ★★★★★ | 거의 움직일 수 없음 |

**Training Vocabulary:**
- "Hit a wall" - 벽에 부딪히다 (에너지 떨어짐)
- "Bonk" - 극도로 피로해지다
- "Runner's high" - 러너스 하이 (운동 중 쾌감)
- "Recovery" - 회복 (휴식 후 체력 회복)

**Physical Breakdown Expressions:**

| Stage | Expression |

## 2.2 Breathing & Cardiovascular

**Original Korean:** "숨이 차다"

**Context:** 22km 이후, 신체 극한 상태

**Breathing During Long Distance Running:**

| Distance | Breathing Pattern | Expression |
|----------|------------------|------------|
| 5km | Normal, controlled | "Easy breathing" |
| 10km | Steady rhythm | "Finding my rhythm" |
| 15km | Noticeable effort | "Breathing harder" |
| 20km | Heavy breathing | "Breathing heavily" |
| 22km+ | Labored breathing | "Barely able to breathe" |

**Breathing Difficulty Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Description |
|------------|-----------|-------------|
| "I'm out of breath." | ★★☆ | 숨이 차다 |
| "I'm breathing hard." | ★★★ | 거칠게 숨 쉬다 |
| "I'm barely able to breathe." | ★★★★ | 거의 숨을 못 쉬다 |
| "I'm gasping for air." | ★★★★★ | 허덕이며 숨을 쉬다 |

**Running Breathing Techniques:**

```
Easy pace: "Breathe in through your nose, out through your mouth."
Hard pace: "Control your breathing - in for two, out for two."
Extreme effort: "Just breathe. Keep moving."
```

## 2.3 Temperature & Time Awareness

### "Clear skies, below freezing."

**Original Korean:** "맑고 영하다"

**Weather Impact on Running:**

| Condition | Effect | Challenge |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Clear, cold | Refreshing at start | Stiff muscles, slower warmup |
| Overcast, cold | Dreary | Harder motivation |
| Clear, warm | Pleasant | Heat management |
| Cloudy, humid | Oppressive | Heat + difficulty breathing |

**Early Morning Running Temperature Awareness:**

```
5:00 AM - January: below freezing, very cold
After warmup (5km): body heating up despite cold air
After 10km: significant warmth generated
After 15km: sweating despite cold weather
```

**Temperature Expressions in Running:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "The cold bit at my face." | 추위가 얼굴을 물다 |
| "The air was crisp." | 공기가 선명하고 차다 |
| "I could see my breath." | 김이 나왔다 |
| "Despite the cold, I was sweating." | 추위에도 땀이 났다 |
| "The wind cut through my layers." | 바람이 옷을 뚫고 들어왔다 |

## 2.4 Leg Recovery & Post-Run

### "My legs were shaking."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 떨렸다"

**Post-Run Physical State:**

| Time After Run | Physical Condition | Expression |
|----------------|--------------------|------------|
| Immediately | Extreme fatigue | "I could barely walk." |
| 5 minutes | Shaking, unsteady | "My legs were shaking." |
| 15 minutes | Still weak | "I walked slowly." |
| 30 minutes | Recovering | "My legs are getting steadier." |
| 1+ hours | Mostly recovered | "I feel better." |

**Synonyms for Leg Shaking:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "My legs were shaking." | 다리가 떨렸다 |
| "My legs were trembling." | 다리가 떨렸다 (더 formal) |
| "My legs were wobbly." | 다리가 불안정했다 |
| "I was unsteady on my feet." | 발이 불안정했다 |
| "My legs gave out beneath me." | 다리가 주저앉았다 |

**Why Legs Shake After Extreme Effort:**
- Muscle glycogen depletion (근육 글리코겐 고갈)
- Lactic acid accumulation (젖산 축적)
- Nervous system fatigue (신경 계통 피로)
- Electrolyte imbalance (전해질 불균형)

# PART 3: DESCRIBING RUNNING EXPERIENCE (달리기 경험 묘사)

## 3.1 Distance & Progression

### "Turn around at 12.5km."

**Original Korean:** "12.5km에서 돌아온다"

**Running Route Types:**

| Route Type | Description | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Out-and-back | 절반 거리까지 가서 돌아옴 | 12.5km × 2 = 25km |
| Loop | 같은 루트를 반복 | 5km loop × 5 = 25km |
| Point-to-point | 시작과 끝이 다름 | Start A, End B = 25km |
| Trail | 산/자연길 | 25km mixed terrain |

**Distance Checkpoint Language:**

| Distance | Expression | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| 5km | "I've completed the first segment." | 첫 구간 완주 |
| 10km | "I'm at the halfway warmup point." | 절반 나왔다 |
| 12.5km | "I'm at the turnaround point." | 돌아서는 지점 |
| 20km | "I can see the finish line." | 끝이 보인다 |
| 25km | "Done." | 끝 |

**Useful Running Phrases:**

```
"I'm pacing myself at 6:30 per km."
"My split time for the first 10km was [time]."
"I need to maintain this pace until the turnaround."
"The return route seems harder."
```

### "This is new territory."

**Original Korean:** "이곳은 새로운 영역이다"

**Psychological Landmarks in Distance Running:**

| Distance Range | Psychological State | Expression |
|----------------|--------------------|------------|
| 0-10km | Confident, familiar | "This I know." |
| 10-21km | Getting harder, familiar distance | "I've been here." |
| 21-25km | New territory, pushing limits | "This is new territory." |
| 25km+ | Unknown, beyond experience | "I've never gone this far." |

**Mindset Shifts in Long Distance:**

```
Confidence → Uncertainty → Determination → Breakthrough
"I know this." → "I don't know this." → "I can figure this out." → "I did it!"
```

**Mental Language:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "I'm in uncharted territory." | 미지의 영역이다 |
| "I'm pushing past my limits." | 한계를 넘다 |
| "I'm going further than ever before." | 전에 못 가본 데까지 가다 |

## 3.2 Pace & Energy Management

### "My pace dropped."

**Original Korean:** "페이스가 떨어졌다"

**Pace Management Terminology:**

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| pace | 속도/km당 걸리는 시간 | "I'm running at a 6:30 pace." |
| drop (pace) | 속도가 떨어지다 | "My pace dropped to 7:00." |

**Pace Changes Throughout Run:**

| Time | Pace | Status |
|------|------|--------|
| 5-10km | 6:30/km | Strong, fast |
| 10-20km | 6:40/km | Steady |
| 20-22km | 6:50/km | Starting to slow |
| 22-25km | 7:15/km | Dropping significantly |

**Energy Management Language:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|

| "I need to dig deep." | 깊숙이 파고 들어야 한다 (더 열심히) |

## 3.3 Mental Determination

### "It's okay. Just keep moving forward."

**Original Korean:** "괜찮아. 그냥 앞으로 나아가자"

**Self-Talk During Difficult Moments:**

| Situation | Self-Talk | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 피로 극도 | "Just keep moving forward." | 동기 부여 |
| 포기 욕구 | "You can do this." | 자신감 |
| 고통 관리 | "It's only temporary." | 심리적 거리 |
| 페이스 조절 | "Slow is fine." | 현실적 목표 |
| 마지막 구간 | "Almost there." | 희망 |

**Encouraging Self-Talk Phrases:**

| Expression | When to Use | Effect |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| "Just keep moving forward." | 일반적 피로 | 계속 진행 |
| "It's okay to slow down." | 속도 저하 | 현실 수용 |
| "You've trained for this." | 자신감 필요 | 능력 상기 |
| "Only X km left." | 마지막 구간 | 거리 축소 느낌 |
| "This is temporary." | 극심한 고통 | 심리적 거리 |

**Coach vs. Self-Talk:**

```
Coach's Role: "You got over it today. You can get over it next time too."
Self-Talk: "Just keep moving forward."
→ Both reinforce capability and persistence
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Distance Milestone Reporting

**Structure:** [Subject] + [action] + [distance] + [time/achievement]

**From Text:** "Coach, I ran 25km." / "25km complete." / "2 hours 42 minutes."

**Variations:**

```
"I ran 25km today."
"I just finished 25km."
"I completed 25km in 2:42."
"Today's run: 25km, 2 hours 42 minutes."
```

**Coach Response Pattern:**

```
Coach: "Nice work. How was it?"
Athlete: "[Difficulty assessment] After [checkpoint km]."
Coach: [Encouragement] + [Future perspective]
```

---

## Pattern 2: "That's the wall."

**Structure:** [Phenomenon name] + explanation + [context]

**From Text:**
```
Athlete: "It was tough. After 22km especially."
Coach: "That's the wall. In a full marathon, there's a bigger wall past 30km."
```

**Wall Analogy in Running:**

| Distance | The Wall | Explanation |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| 22km | First wall (half-marathon+) | Energy depletion begins |
| 30km | Second wall (marathon) | Severe glycogen depletion |
| 35km+ | The real wall | Extreme mental/physical challenge |

**Teaching Pattern:**

```
Athlete experiences: [difficulty at X distance]
Coach identifies: "That's the [phenomenon]"
Coach explains: "[Scientific/experiential reason]"
Coach encourages: "[You can overcome it]"
```

---

## Pattern 3: Growth Mindset - Encouraging Progression

**Structure:** [Past success] → [Future challenge] → [Capability]

**From Text:**
```
"But you got over it today. You can get over it next time too."
```

**Growth Mindset Language:**

| Past | Present | Future |
|------|---------|--------|
| "You did it." | "That was hard." | "You can do it again." |
| "You got through." | "That challenged you." | "You'll handle it better." |
| "You persevered." | "You pushed hard." | "You'll be stronger." |

**Encouraging Progression Phrases:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| [Past] → [Future] | "You handled 25km today. 30km is next." |
| [Difficulty] → [Capability] | "That wall was tough. You have what it takes to break through." |
| [Effort] → [Result] | "You trained for this. The result shows it." |

### Scenario 1: Reporting Marathon Training Progress

```
Coach: "You look tired. How did the run go?"

Athlete B: "You'll get there. We all do."
```
