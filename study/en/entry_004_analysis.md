# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Reflecting on Past Experiences

### "I'm still thinking about walking to school on Monday."

**Original Korean:** "월요일에 걸어서 등교한 일을 아직도 생각하고 있다."

**Grammar Point:** Present continuous for ongoing thoughts

**Structure:** I'm still + verb-ing + about + noun/gerund

**Similar Expressions:**

| Expression | Context | Nuance |
|------------|---------|--------|
| "I'm still thinking about it." | 아직도 그것에 대해 생각 중 | 결정 못 내림/계속 마음에 남음 |
| "I can't stop thinking about it." | 생각을 멈출 수가 없어 | 더 강한 집착 |
| "It keeps coming back to me." | 자꾸 떠올라 | 무의식적으로 |
| "It's been on my mind." | 머릿속에 계속 맴돌아 | 지속적인 생각 |
| "I keep replaying it in my head." | 머릿속에서 자꾸 재생돼 | 장면 회상 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: You seem distracted. What's up?
B: I'm still thinking about that interview yesterday.
A: It went well, didn't it?
B: Yeah, but I keep wondering if I said the right things.
A: You're overthinking it. Just wait for the results.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- "I still think about walking." (현재 습관처럼 들림)
- "I'm still thinking about walking." (지금 계속 생각 중)

### "Back to normal."

**Original Korean:** "다시 일상으로."

**Analysis:**
- 짧은 문장으로 상황 요약
- 이전 상태로 돌아감을 의미

**Full Forms:**
- "Things are back to normal."
- "Everything's back to normal."
- "I'm back to my normal routine."

**Variations:**

| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "Back to normal." | 중립적 |
| "Back to the grind." | 힘든 일상으로 (약간 부정적) |
| "Back to reality." | 현실로 돌아옴 (꿈에서 깨듯) |
| "Back to square one." | 처음으로 돌아감 (실패 후) |
| "Back to the same old." | 똑같은 일상으로 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: How was your vacation?
B: Amazing! But now I'm back to normal.
A: Back to the grind, huh?
B: Yeah, it's a shock to the system.
```

### "Something's strange."

**Original Korean:** "뭔가 이상하다."

**Analysis:** 변화/차이를 느낄 때 사용

**Variations by Certainty:**

| Expression | Certainty Level | Korean |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| "Something seems off." | ★★☆☆☆ | 뭔가 이상한 것 같아 |
| "Something's strange." | ★★★☆☆ | 뭔가 이상해 |
| "Something's wrong." | ★★★★☆ | 뭔가 잘못됐어 |
| "Something's definitely off." | ★★★★★ | 확실히 뭔가 이상해 |

**Similar Expressions:**
- "Something feels different."
- "Something's not right."
- "Something's changed."
- "I can't put my finger on it, but something's different."

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Something's strange.
B: What do you mean?
A: I don't know. The bus used to be fine, but now it bothers me.
B: Maybe because you know there's another option now.
A: That might be it.
```

**Original Korean:** "사흘간 버스를 탔다."

**Grammar Point:** Number + days/weeks/months + of + gerund

---

### "Packed bus, motion sickness, churning stomach."

**Original Korean:** "꽉 찬 버스, 멀미, 울렁거리는 속."

**Analysis:** 명사구 나열로 상황 요약 (Fragment listing)

**Why This Works:**
- 간결하고 강렬한 전달
- 일기체/내면 독백에 적합
- 리듬감 있는 표현

**More Examples of Fragment Listing:**

| Situation | Fragment List |
|-----------|---------------|
| 피곤한 하루 | "Long day. Tired feet. Empty stomach." |
| 좋은 하루 | "Sunny weather. Good coffee. Nice people." |
| 스트레스 | "Deadlines. Meetings. Emails." |
| 추억 | "Summer nights. Old friends. Cold beer." |

## 1.3 Asking Permission

### "Mom, can I walk to school sometimes?"

**Original Korean:** "엄마, 가끔 걸어서 등교해도 돼요?"

**Grammar Point:** Can I + verb + adverb of frequency?

**Permission Request Spectrum:**

| Expression | Formality | Nuance |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "Can I...?" | ★★☆☆☆ | 캐주얼, 가족/친구 |
| "Could I...?" | ★★★☆☆ | 약간 더 정중 |
| "May I...?" | ★★★★☆ | 정중한, 공식적 |
| "Would it be okay if I...?" | ★★★★★ | 매우 정중, 조심스러움 |
| "Do you mind if I...?" | ★★★★☆ | 상대방 배려 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Child: Mom, can I walk to school sometimes?
Mom: Walk? All the way there?
Child: Yeah, the bus makes me sick.
Mom: Isn't it too far?
Child: It's about an hour, but I feel better when I walk.
Mom: Well... if it's just sometimes, okay.
```

### "Sure, go ahead."

**Original Korean:** "그래, 해봐."

**Analysis:** 허락 + 격려

**Permission Responses Spectrum:**

| Response | Enthusiasm | Nuance |
|----------|------------|--------|
| "Absolutely!" | ★★★★★ | 완전 지지 |
| "Of course!" | ★★★★☆ | 당연하다는 뉘앙스 |
| "Sure, go ahead." | ★★★☆☆ | 괜찮다, 해봐 |
| "I guess so." | ★★☆☆☆ | 마지못해 |
| "If you must." | ★☆☆☆☆ | 내키지 않음 |
| "I'd rather you didn't." | ☆☆☆☆☆ | 거절 |

**Variations of "Go ahead":**
- "Go for it." (더 격려하는)
- "Sure thing." (캐주얼)
- "Be my guest." (정중/유머)
- "Knock yourself out." (매우 캐주얼)

---

### "But don't push yourself."

**Original Korean:** "근데 무리하지 마."

**Grammar Point:** But + don't + verb

**Parental Warning Patterns:**

| Warning | Context |
|---------|---------|
| "Don't push yourself." | 무리하지 마 |
| "Don't overdo it." | 너무 과하게 하지 마 |
| "Take it easy." | 무리하지 말고 천천히 |
| "Don't strain yourself." | 몸 상하게 하지 마 |
| "Listen to your body." | 몸이 하는 말 들어 |

**Full Dialogue Example:**
```
Mom: Sure, go ahead. But don't push yourself.
Son: I won't.
Mom: If it gets hard, just take the bus.
Son: Okay.
Mom: And you have to eat breakfast before you go.
Son: Got it.
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Nausea & Discomfort

**Original Korean:** "오늘 아침에도 버스에서 속이 울렁거렸다."

**Vocabulary Deep Dive:**

**Churn /tʃɜːrn/**
- 원래 의미: 버터 만들 때 휘젓다
- 비유적 의미: 속이 뒤틀리다, 울렁거리다

**Stomach Churning Triggers:**

| Cause | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| 멀미 | "Motion sickness made my stomach churn." |
| 긴장 | "Anxiety made my stomach churn." |
| 역겨움 | "The sight made my stomach churn." |
| 불안 | "Waiting for results, my stomach was churning." |

**Related Expressions:**

| English | Korean | Intensity |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| "My stomach felt uneasy." | 속이 좀 불편했다 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| "My stomach started churning." | 속이 울렁거리기 시작했다 | ★★★☆☆ |
| "My stomach was doing flips." | 속이 뒤집어졌다 | ★★★★☆ |
| "I felt like I was going to throw up." | 토할 것 같았다 | ★★★★★ |

**Original Korean:** "숨이 막히는 것 같았다."

**Analysis:** 공기 부족/답답함

**Suffocation Expressions:**

| Expression | Context |
|------------|---------|
| "I felt suffocated." | 숨 막힘 (물리적/감정적) |

| "I needed air." | 공기가 필요했어 |

**Usage in Different Contexts:**

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|

| 감정적 | "His controlling behavior made me feel suffocated." |

### "My calves were killing me."

**Original Korean:** "종아리가 아파 죽겠었다."

**Grammar Point:** [Body part] + was/were + killing me

**"Killing me" = 너무 아프다 (과장)**

**Body Part Pain Expressions:**

| Body Part | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| calves | "My calves were killing me." |
| feet | "My feet are killing me." |
| back | "My back is killing me." |
| head | "My head is killing me." (두통) |
| legs | "My legs were killing me after the hike." |

**Pain Intensity Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity |
|------------|-----------|
| "My calves ached." | ★★☆☆☆ |
| "My calves were sore." | ★★★☆☆ |
| "My calves were really hurting." | ★★★★☆ |
| "My calves were killing me." | ★★★★★ |

**Alternative Expressions:**
- "My calves were on fire."
- "I could barely walk, my calves hurt so much."
- "My calves were screaming."

### "My legs were shaking going down the stairs."

**Original Korean:** "계단 내려가는데 다리가 후들거렸다."

**Physical Weakness Expressions:**

## 2.3 Energy & Stamina

### "I'd run out of energy halfway."

**Original Korean:** "중간에 힘이 빠질 거야."

**Grammar Point:** Run out of + noun

**"Run out of" Expressions:**

| Noun | Example | Korean |
|------|---------|--------|

### "Otherwise you'll collapse."

**Original Korean:** "안 그러면 쓰러져."

**Grammar Point:** Otherwise + future consequence

**Warning Structure:** [Advice]. Otherwise, [bad consequence].

| Advice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

## 4.1 Thinking & Realizing

### "The thought crossed my mind."

**Original Korean:** "그런 생각이 스쳤다."

**"Cross one's mind" = 생각이 스치다, 떠오르다**

**Thinking Phrasal Verbs:**

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| cross my mind | 생각이 스치다 | "The thought crossed my mind." |
| come to me | 생각이 떠오르다 | "It just came to me." |
| hit me | 깨닫다 (갑자기) | "It suddenly hit me that I was wrong." |
| dawn on me | 깨닫다 (서서히) | "It finally dawned on me." |
| occur to me | 생각나다 | "It didn't occur to me until later." |

**Usage Examples:**
```
"The idea of walking just crossed my mind."
"It never crossed my mind that it could work."
"Walking in the morning? The thought crossed my mind."
```

## 4.2 Physical Actions

### "Pulled out one of the peppermints"

**Original Korean:** "민서가 준 페퍼민트 사탕 하나를 꺼냈다."

**"Pull out" = 꺼내다**

**"Pull" Phrasal Verbs:**

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| pull out | 꺼내다 | "I pulled out a candy." |
| pull over | (차를) 세우다 | "The driver pulled over." |
| pull through | 극복하다 | "She pulled through the illness." |
| pull off | 해내다 | "I can't believe we pulled it off." |

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Before, I just accepted it as the way things were."

**Structure:** Before, I [past habit]. Now [change].

**Realization Pattern:**

| Before | Now |
|--------|-----|
| "accepted it" | "it feels harder" |
| "didn't question it" | "I wonder why" |
| "never thought about it" | "it bothers me" |
| "just did it" | "I want alternatives" |

**Practice Examples:**
```

"Before, I never exercised. Now I can't imagine not running."
"Before, I ate whatever. Now I'm more careful about food."
```

## Pattern 2: "Maybe because I know what [X] felt like."

**Structure:** Maybe because I know what + [noun/gerund] + felt like.

**Comparative Awareness:**

| Experience | Full Sentence |
|------------|---------------|

**Variations:**

## Pattern 3: "So why do you look like you want to do it again?"

| Observation | Question |
|-------------|----------|

| 뭔가 숨기는 것 같음 | "Why do you sound like you're hiding something?" |

**Defensive Responses:**

## Pattern 4: "It was hard, though."

**Structure:** It was [positive/negative], though.

**Concession Pattern:**

| Main Point | Concession |
|------------|------------|

**Position Options:**
- Beginning: "Though it was hard, it was worth it."
- Middle: "It was nice, though hard."
- End: "It was nice. Hard, though."

## Pattern 5: "But still... it was better than [alternative]."

**Structure:** But still... it was better than + [noun/gerund].

**Weighing Options:**

| Challenge | Alternative | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|

**Similar Expressions:**
- "All things considered, it was better."
- "Even so, I preferred it."
- "Despite everything, it was the better option."

## Pattern 6: "If I spend [X] extra minutes, I don't have to [Y]."

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Discussing Your Commute Options

```
A: How do you usually get to school?
B: I take the bus. It's about 40 minutes.
A: That's not bad.
B: Yeah, but I get motion sickness. My stomach churns the whole way.
A: Have you tried walking?
B: Once. It took an hour and ten minutes.
A: Wow, that's long. But did you feel better?
B: Actually, yeah. My stomach didn't churn at all.
A: So what are you going to do?
B: Maybe I'll walk sometimes. Like once a week.
A: That might be worth it.
```

### Scenario 2: Asking Permission from a Parent

```
Child: Mom, can I walk to school sometimes?
Mom: Walk? All the way there?
Child: Yeah. The bus makes me sick.
Mom: How long does it take?
Child: About an hour and ten minutes.
Mom: What? That's a long walk!
Child: I know, but when I walk, I don't feel nauseous.
Mom: Hmm. If it's just sometimes, I guess it's okay.
Child: Thanks, Mom.
Mom: But don't push yourself. And eat breakfast first!
Child: Got it.
```

### Scenario 3: A Friend Noticing Your Feelings

```
A: Hey, wasn't walking on Monday tough?
B: It was. My legs hurt.
A: So why do you look like you want to do it again?
B: [pauses] What are you talking about?
A: You made a face when we talked about the bus. But when you mentioned walking, your expression totally changed.
B: You're so observant. Kind of scary.
A: So? Am I wrong?
B: No... I guess I do want to walk again.
A: Even though it takes over an hour?
B: I don't know. I just want to.
A: That's interesting. Maybe I should try it too.
```
