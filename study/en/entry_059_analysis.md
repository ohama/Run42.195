# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Race Week Mentality

### "Don't push it this week."

**Original Korean:** "이번 주는 무리하지 마"

**Analysis:**
- Coach's instruction to reduce intensity
- 부상 방지와 에너지 보존
- Wise coaching wisdom

**Variations by Formality:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Don't push it." | "Don't overdo it." | "Avoid excessive strain this week." |
| "Take it easy." | "Keep it light." | "Maintain reduced intensity." |
| "Go easy." | "Don't do too much." | "Preserve energy for race day." |

**Coach's Reasoning Spectrum:**

| Concern | Expression | Effect |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Injury risk | "You might get hurt." | Protective |
| Fatigue | "You'll be too tired." | Preventive |
| Recovery | "Your body needs rest." | Scientific |
| Performance | "You won't have anything left." | Motivational |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: I want to do a long run today.
B: Don't push it this week.
A: Why not?
B: Because if your body gets too tired, you won't have anything left for race day.
A: Oh, you're right. I'll take it easy.
B: Good thinking.
```

## 1.2 Mental Preparation for Race Day

### "I'm nervous though."

**Original Korean:** "나 긴장돼"

**Nervousness in Athletic Context:**

| Expression | Level | Context |
|------------|-------|---------|
| "A little nervous" | Mild | Normal pre-race jitters |
| "Nervous" | Moderate | Expected for big event |
| "Very nervous" | High | Some anxiety |
| "Terrified" | Extreme | Overwhelming fear |

**Pre-Race Nervousness Types:**

| Type | Expression | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| Performance anxiety | "What if I can't finish?" | 완주 가능성 불안 |
| Social nervousness | "I'm nervous about running in front of everyone." | 관중 앞에서 달리기 |
| Unknown nervousness | "It's my first race." | 경험 부족 |
| Physical nervousness | "My legs feel shaky." | 신체적 떨림 |

**Coach's Perspective:**

```
Coach: "Being nervous is normal."
Doyun: "Good thing?"
Coach: "Nervous means you're focused. If you're too comfortable, that's when you mess up."
```

**Translation of Coach's Wisdom:**
- 긴장함 = 집중 상태 (nervousness = focused state)
- 편함 = 방심 위험 (comfort = risk of carelessness)

### "I don't want to let them down."

**Original Korean:** "그들을 실망시키고 싶지 않아"

**Motivation Through Others:**

| Stage | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| Self-oriented | "I want to finish." |
| Group-oriented | "I don't want to let them down." |
| Extended reach | "They believe in me." |
| Deeper meaning | "I don't want to let *myself* down." |

**Pressure vs. Motivation:**

```
Negative pressure: "I have to do this or they'll be disappointed."
Healthy motivation: "Their support gives me strength."
Self-directed: "I'm doing this for myself first."
```

---

### "Actually, I don't want to let *myself* down."

**Original Korean:** "사실은, 나 자신을 실망시키고 싶지 않아"

**Self-Accountability Language:**

| Focus | Expression | Korean |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Others | "I don't want to let them down." | 그들을 실망 |
| Combination | "I don't want to let anyone down." | 누구든 실망 |
| Self | "I don't want to let myself down." | 자신을 실망 |

**This Shift is Important:**
- From external motivation → internal motivation
- From seeking approval → self-respect
- From obligation → commitment

**Deepening Self-Accountability:**

```
Level 1: "I promised to try."
Level 2: "I promised my family."
Level 3: "I promised myself."
Level 4: "I don't want to let myself down."
Level 5: "I'm doing this for me."
```

**Dialogue Showing This Growth:**
```
A: Why are you really doing this race?
B: My family is coming to cheer.
A: And?
B: And... I don't want to let myself down. I want to prove something to myself.
A: That's powerful.
```

## 3.2 Race Strategy

**Original Korean:** "시작에서 너무 빨리 나가지 마"

**Pacing Strategy Language:**

| Strategy | Expression | Danger |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Go out too fast | "Don't go out too fast" | 앞에서 너무 빨리 |
| Negative split | "Run the second half faster" | 뒤에서 더 빠르게 |
| Consistent pace | "Keep steady pace throughout" | 일정한 페이스 유지 |
| Kick at the end | "Save energy for the end" | 마지막을 위해 에너지 비축 |

**Why Pacing Matters:**

```
Problem: Going out too fast
Result: Hit the wall at kilometer 7
Solution: Negative split (start slow, finish fast)
```

**Pacing Conversations:**

```
Coach: "Don't go out too fast at the start."
Doyun: "When should I kick?"
Coach: "Save your kick for the final kilometer."
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Coach told me: 'Don't push it this week.'"

**Structure:** Attribution + direct quote from authority

**Meaning:** 권위자의 직접 조언 (비유적, 중요한 충고)

**Pattern in Context:**

```
[Situation] + [Authority] + [Quote]
"This week" + "Coach told me" + "Don't push it."
```

**Similar Patterns:**

| Pattern | Effect | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|

## Key Learning Points from Entry 059

1. **Pre-Race Mental State:** Nervousness is normal and actually helps focus; frame it positively.

6. **Family Support:** Their presence matters but isn't the final motivation; doing it for yourself is most powerful.

7. **Physical Awareness:** Recognize how your body changes at each kilometer marker; this is expected and manageable.

8. **D-5 Meaning:** Five days before race day; time for light activity, heavy mental preparation, family coordination.
