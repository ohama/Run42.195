## PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

### 5.1 Pattern: Asking About Emotional State

**Pattern Structure:**
```
Q: "Are you [emotion adjective]?"
A: "[Intensity] [acknowledgment]"
```

**Examples from Text:**
1. Q: "Are you nervous?" A: "A little."
2. Q: "How are you feeling?" A: "Good. Light."
3. Q: "How's your condition?" A: "Good. Light."

**5+ Extended Practice Examples:**
- Q: "Are you anxious?" A: "A bit. But I'm handling it."
- Q: "Are you confident?" A: "Mostly. I've trained hard."
- Q: "Are you ready?" A: "I think so. Still a little nervous."
- Q: "How's your preparation?" A: "Complete. I'm well-prepared."
- Q: "Are you scared?" A: "Not really. Just nervous."

**Alternative Structures:**
- "How are you feeling about the marathon?"
- "What's your mental state right now?"
- "Are you in good shape?"
- "How's your training going?"

**When to Use / When NOT to Use:**
- USE: Before major events, with coaches/mentors, expressing vulnerability
- DON'T USE: In formal interviews, with strangers, in written formal documents

---

### 5.2 Pattern: Coach Giving Instructions (Imperative)

**Pattern Structure:**
```
Coach: "[Action/Guideline]"
Student: "[Agreement/Acknowledgment]"
Coach: "[Explanation/Rationale]"
Student: "[Confirmation]"
```

**Examples from Text:**
1. "First 10km, take it slow. Don't get excited." → "Yes."
2. "The 20-30km section is crucial. Maintain your pace." → "Yes."
3. "After 30km, the wall hits. Don't give up then." → "I understand."

**Extended Practice Examples:**
1. Coach: "Hydrate every 5km."
   Student: "Got it."
   Coach: "Dehydration will slow you down."
   Student: "I'll remember."

2. Coach: "Don't start too fast."
   Student: "Yes."
   Coach: "You'll burn out before the finish."
   Student: "I understand."

3. Coach: "Trust your training."
   Student: "I will."
   Coach: "You've prepared for this all year."
   Student: "You're right."

4. Coach: "Listen to your body."
   Student: "Okay."
   Coach: "Pain is information."
   Student: "I got it."

5. Coach: "Stay focused."
   Student: "Yes, coach."
   Coach: "Many runners lose focus at 35km."
   Student: "I'll stay sharp."

**Alternative Structures:**
- "Make sure to [action]."
- "Don't forget to [action]."
- "Remember to [action]."
- "Keep [action]ing throughout."

### 5.4 Pattern: Personal Reflection (Stream of Consciousness)

**Pattern Structure:**
```
"I'm [emotional state]"
"But I'm [counter-emotion]"
"I've [past action]"
"[Current reason/fact]"
"I'm [commitment statement]"
```

**Examples from Text:**
- "I'm nervous. But I'm ready. I've spent a whole year preparing for this."
- "I know. I'm not going to give up then."

**Extended Examples:**
1. "I'm tired. But I'm motivated. I've come too far to quit now. This is my moment. I'm going to finish strong."

2. "I'm doubtful. But I'm prepared. I've trained every single day. My coach believes in me. I'm going to prove myself."

3. "I'm anxious. But I'm determined. I've overcome obstacles before. Today is different. I'm going to succeed."

---

### 5.5 Pattern: Comparison and Scale

**Pattern Structure:**
```
"How's it compare to [previous reference]?"
"[Subject] is [comparison] [degree modifier]"
```

**Examples from Text:**
- Q: "More than for the 21km?"
- A: "Yeah. 42km is twice as far."

**Extended Practice Examples:**
- "This marathon is two times harder than the 21km run."
- "42km will take roughly twice as long as my last race."
- "The effort required is significantly greater than before."
- "This is the biggest challenge I've faced so far."

## PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

### 7.1 Short, Clipped Sentences

**Characteristic:** Young speakers use fragments and minimal sentences

| Pattern | Korean Example | Full Version |
|---|---|---|
| One-word answer | "A little." | "I am a little nervous." |
| Noun-only response | "Lunch time." | "It is now lunch time." |
| Fragment statement | "Morning." | "It is morning." |
| Short acknowledgment | "Yeah." | "Yes, I agree." |

**Why Used:** Speed, directness, emotional honesty, journalistic style (diary format)

### 7.2 Imperative/Command Tone (Coach Instructions)

**Pattern:** Direct instructions without softening language

| Feature | Example | Korean | Formality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bare imperative | "Take it slow." | "천천히 가." | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| Negation imperative | "Don't get excited." | "흥분하지 마." | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| Directive statement | "Maintain your pace." | "속도를 유지해." | ★★☆☆☆ |

### 7.3 Casual Discourse Markers and Hesitation

| Feature | Usage | Example in Text | Korean |
|---|---|---|---|
| Filler words (implied) | Thinking time | Not present, but typical | "Like", "you know" |
| Hedging (soft certainty) | Expressing doubt | "A little" instead of "very" | "조금" 대신 "아주" |
| Intensifiers | Strong emphasis | Not in this entry | "Totally", "literally" |

### 7.4 Dialogue Vs. Narration Switches

**Dialogue (Direct speech):**
- Lower formality
- Shorter sentences
- Question-answer pairs
- Immediate, reactive

**Narration (Diary reflection):**
- Slightly higher formality
- Longer, complete sentences
- Reflective tone
- Thoughtful, processed

**Example from Entry 098:**
```
DIALOGUE:
"Are you nervous?"
"A little."

NARRATION:
"I'm back in my room writing this diary.
I'm nervous.
But I'm ready.
I've spent a whole year preparing for this."
```

### 7.5 Repetition for Emphasis (14-year-old pattern)

| Technique | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|

| Statement-negation | "I'm nervous. But I'm ready." | Emotional balance |
