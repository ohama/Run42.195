# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Winter Training Expressions

### "First day of sub-zero temperatures."

**Original Korean:** "영하의 날씨 첫날"

**"Sub-zero" Meaning:** 영하의 (0도 보다 낮은)

**Structure:** sub-zero + noun (temperatures/weather)

**Temperature Vocabulary:**
| Temperature | English | Korean | Context |
|------------|---------|--------|---------|
| -2°C | minus two | 영하 2도 | 매우 추움 |
| sub-zero | 영하의 | 영하의 | 0도 이하 |
| below freezing | 빙점 아래 | 빙점 이하 | 과학적 표현 |
| freezing cold | 몹시 추운 | 얼 듯이 추운 | 일상 표현 |
| chilly | 약간 추운 | 약간 찬 | 약한 추위 |

**Example Sentences:**
```
"It's the first day of sub-zero temperatures."
"Sub-zero temperatures make running challenging."
"We had sub-zero weather all week."
"The forecast shows sub-zero nights."
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "It's sub-zero degree temperature."
✅ "It's sub-zero temperatures." / "It's minus two."

### "The cold air bit at my face."

**Original Korean:** "차가운 공기가 얼굴을 물었다"

**Metaphorical Expression:** "Bit" = 깨물다 (신체적 감각 표현)

**Structure:** [Cold element] + bit at + [body part]

**Similar Metaphorical Weather Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning | Korean |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| "Cold air bit at my face" | 차가운 공기가 따갑게 느껴짐 | 차가운 공기가 얼굴을 물었다 |
| "Wind lashed my cheeks" | 바람이 뺨을 치다 | 바람이 뺨을 때렸다 |
| "Frost nipped my ears" | 서리가 귀를 따끔거리게 함 | 서리가 귀를 따끔거리게 했다 |
| "Icy rain stung my eyes" | 얼음처럼 찬 빗방울이 눈을 쏘다 | 찬 빗방울이 눈을 자극했다 |

**Usage Context:**
- Winter running 설명
- 자연 현상을 의인화
- 감각적 표현 강조

**Alternative Expressions:**
- "The cold air felt sharp against my face."
- "My face stung from the cold."
- "The cold was biting."

### "Every breath came out as white clouds of mist."

**Original Korean:** "숨이 하얀 연기로 나왔다"

**Visual Description Pattern:** [Action] + came out as + [description]

**Examples:**
| Situation | What Comes Out | Full Sentence |
|-----------|---|---------------|
| Winter breath | white clouds of mist | "Every breath came out as white clouds of mist." |
| Smoke | thick white fog | "The smoke came out as thick white fog." |
| Steam | visible vapor | "Steam came out as visible vapor." |
| Exhaust | black smoke | "The exhaust came out as black smoke." |

**Scientific Term:**
- "Breath condensation" (숨의 응축)
- "Water vapor" (수증기)
- "Visible exhale" (보이는 숨)

**Describing Visible Breath:**
```
"In the cold, every breath becomes visible."
"My breath formed white clouds in the frigid air."
"I could see my breath with each exhale."
"The mist from my breathing created a small cloud."
```

### "It was freezing, but running kept me warm."

**Original Korean:** "얼을 정도로 추웠지만, 달리기가 나를 따뜻하게 해줬다"

**Contrast Pattern:** [Negative] + but + [positive action]

**Structure:** It was [adjective], but [action] + kept me + [state]

**Examples:**
| Weather | But Activity | Full Sentence |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| freezing | running | "It was freezing, but running kept me warm." |
| pouring rain | cycling | "It was pouring rain, but cycling kept me warm." |
| windy | hiking | "It was windy, but hiking kept me warm." |
| snowing | swimming | "It was snowing, but swimming kept me warm." |

**"Keep me [warm/motivated/focused]" Pattern:**
- "Running keeps me warm."
- "Exercise keeps me energized."
- "Practice keeps me focused."
- "Support keeps me motivated."

## 1.2 Achievement & Accomplishment Language

### "I finished 21km!"

**Original Korean:** "21km를 완주했어!"

**"Finish" vs "Complete":**

| Verb | Meaning | Korean | Context |
|------|---------|--------|---------|
| finish | 끝내다 | 끝냈다 | 경주, 작업 완료 |
| complete | 완수하다 | 완수했다 | 과제, 목표 달성 |
| accomplish | 성취하다 | 성취했다 | 목표 달성 |
| do | 하다 | 했다 | 일상 표현 |
| run | 뛰다 | 뛰었다 | 거리 주시 |

**Achievement Intensity Spectrum:**
| Expression | Confidence | Korean |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| "I did 21km." | ★★☆ | 21km 했어 |
| "I completed 21km." | ★★★ | 21km 완주했어 |
| "I finished 21km!" | ★★★★ | 21km 완주했어! |
| "I crushed 21km!" | ★★★★★ | 21km 제압했어! |
| "I conquered 21km!" | ★★★★★ | 21km 정복했어! |

**Full Achievement Context:**
```
"Grandpa! Hi!"
"You're running even in this cold? I heard you did great in the race."
"Yeah! I finished 21km!"
```

**Original Korean:** "3개월 전 3월에는 7km를 걷고 있었어"

**Structure:** [Time] ago + [subject] + was + [gerund]

**Examples:**
| Time | Action | Full Sentence |

### "Why couldn't I do 42.195km?"

**Original Korean:** "왜 42.195km를 할 수 없을까?"

**Rhetorical Question Pattern:** Why couldn't I [do big thing]?

**Meaning:**
- Not actually asking "why not?"
- Expressing confidence that it IS possible
- Building determination
- Rhetorical assertion rather than real question

**Examples:**
```
"Why couldn't I do 42.195km?" (가능할 것 같은데)
"Why couldn't I learn this language?" (배울 수 있잖아)
"Why couldn't I finish this project?" (끝낼 수 있잖아)
```

**Growth Mindset Language:**
```
LIMITING: "I can't do a full marathon."
CONFIDENT: "Why couldn't I do 42.195km?"
```
