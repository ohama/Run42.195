# Entry 079 - Comprehensive English Conversation Study Guide

## PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

### Essential Daily Expressions

| Expression | Korean | Usage/Nuance | Formality |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| "I've decided." | "나는 결심했다." | Simple statement of commitment; direct and powerful | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "What?!" | "뭐?!" | Exclamation expressing shock or surprise | ★☆☆☆☆ Very casual |
| "Yeah." | "응/맞아." | Affirmative response; teenage casual speech | ★☆☆☆☆ Very casual |
| "I know." | "알아." | Acknowledgment; shows understanding | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "So why?" | "그럼 왜?" | Question asking for reason/motivation | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "Because I want to try it." | "하고 싶어서." | Explanation of motivation | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "You're actually crazy." | "너 정말 미쳤다." | Friendly teasing; implies admiration | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "But it's cool." | "근데 멋있어." | Praise/acknowledgment of boldness | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "Thanks." | "고마워/감사해." | Simple gratitude | ★★☆☆☆ Casual |
| "You've made up your mind?" | "결심했어?" | Confirmation question about decision | ★★☆☆☆ Semi-formal |
| "Good. Let's prepare together." | "좋아. 함께 준비하자." | Encouragement and support | ★★☆☆☆ Semi-formal |

### Expression Variations and Nuances

#### "I've decided" variations:
- "I've decided." (final, strong commitment)
- "I've made up my mind." (slightly more formal)
- "I'm going to do it." (active, forward-looking)
- "I'm committed." (shows dedication)
- "I'm all in." (completely dedicated - very American)

**When to use each:**
- "I've decided" = personal, diary-like, reflective
- "I've made up my mind" = more formal, responding to doubt
- "I'm going to do it" = action-oriented, excited
- "I'm committed" = professional, serious tone

#### "What?!" variations:
- "What?!" (shocked surprise)
- "No way!" (disbelief)
- "Really?!" (double-checking)
- "Are you serious?" (questioning if joking)
- "You're kidding!" (assumes joking)

**Cultural note:** "What?!" can sound rude in formal contexts, but between friends it's normal and friendly.

### Practice Dialogue 1: Breaking Big News

```
A: "I'm going to run a full marathon."
B: "What?! That's crazy!"
A: "I know it sounds insane, but I've decided."
B: "But you said the half marathon was hard?"
A: "It was. But I want to try it."
B: "...You're actually crazy. But it's cool."
```

### Practice Dialogue 2: Responding to Doubt

```
A: "I'm running a full marathon in March."
B: "A full one? 42km? Are you sure?"
A: "Yeah, I'm sure. I've made up my mind."
B: "You struggled with the half marathon though..."
A: "I know. But I really want to do this."
B: "If that's what you want, I'll support you."
```

### Practice Dialogue 3: With Authority Figure

```
A: "Sir, I'm going to run a full marathon."
B: "You've made up your mind?"
A: "Yes. The Jeju Full Marathon on March 2nd."
B: "Good. Let's prepare together."
```

### Common Mistakes

❌ **Incorrect:** "I've decided to run a full marathon yesterday."
✅ **Correct:** "I've decided to run a full marathon." (no time marker with "have decided")

❌ **Incorrect:** "What?! 42 kilometers is very hard!"
✅ **Correct:** "What?! 42 kilometers is too hard!" or "What?! 42 kilometers is incredibly hard!"

❌ **Incorrect:** "You're crazy, but it's a cool thing."
✅ **Correct:** "You're crazy, but it's cool." (natural, concise)

## PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

### Expressing Physical and Emotional States

| Expression | Korean | Body Language/Context | Intensity |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|-----------|
| "His eyes went wide." | "그의 눈이 커졌다." | Surprise reaction; visual description | ★★★☆☆ Moderate |
| "I'm scared." | "나는 무섭다/겁난다." | Honest emotional/physical response | ★★★☆☆ Moderate |
| "Mom looked worried." | "엄마가 걱정스러운 표정이었다." | Facial expression showing concern | ★★☆☆☆ Light |
| "You struggled so much" | "너는 그렇게 힘들어했다." | Visible difficulty; physical exhaustion | ★★★★☆ Strong |

### Intensity Spectrum for Fear/Concern

```
★☆☆☆☆ Mildly concerned: "a bit worried," "slightly nervous," "a little concerned"
★★☆☆☆ Moderately concerned: "worried," "nervous," "concerned"
★★★☆☆ Quite concerned: "very worried," "quite nervous," "really concerned"
★★★★☆ Seriously concerned: "deeply worried," "extremely nervous," "very concerned"
★★★★★ Extremely concerned: "terrified," "panicked," "extremely frightened"
```

### Physical Manifestations in the Text

**Eyes:**
- "His eyes went wide." = Surprise, shock
- Physical response: pupils dilate, eyebrows raise, eyes open larger

**Facial expressions:**
- "Mom looked worried." = Concern, tension
- Physical response: forehead wrinkles, eyes narrow slightly, corners of mouth turn down

**Overall body sensations:**
- "I'm scared." = Internal anxiety
- Physical response: heart rate increases, breathing becomes shallow, muscles tense

### Describing Your Own Physical State

```
"I feel anxious about..."
"My heart is racing when..."
"I'm getting nervous about..."
"My stomach is tight because..."
"I feel weak/tired/exhausted from..."
```

### Practice Dialogue: Responding to Physical Reactions

```
A: "I told him I'm running a full marathon."
B: "What was his reaction?"
A: "His eyes went wide. He was shocked."
B: "Are you scared?"
A: "Yeah, honestly. But excited too."
```

### Common Mistakes

❌ **Incorrect:** "He went his eyes wide."
✅ **Correct:** "His eyes went wide."

❌ **Incorrect:** "I'm being scared."
✅ **Correct:** "I'm scared." (no progressive form with stative verbs)

❌ **Incorrect:** "Mom looked worriedly."
✅ **Correct:** "Mom looked worried." (adjective, not adverb after "looked")

## PART 3: DESCRIBING SPACES/SITUATIONS (공간/상황 묘사)

### Time and Place Markers

| Expression | Korean | Context | Function |
|-----------|--------|---------|----------|
| "This morning." | "이번 아침에." | While running; thinking time | Setting scene |
| "At school." | "학교에서." | Conversation with Minseo | Location marker |
| "After school." | "방과후에." | Conversation with teacher | Time marker |
| "Evening." | "저녁에." | Family conversation | Time marker |
| "In my room now, writing this." | "지금 내 방에서 쓰고 있다." | Present moment reflection | Location + action |
| "Cloudy and cold." | "흐리고 춥다." | Weather; sets mood | Atmospheric |

### Building Situational Context

**Three-part structure for setting a scene:**
1. **Where** (location)
2. **When** (time)
3. **What** (activity/event)

**Example from text:**
```
Location: "At school"
Time: "[morning conversation]"
Activity: "I told Minseo"
```

### Describing Weather and Atmosphere

```
"Cloudy and cold." - Simple, direct, sets mood
"It was cloudy and cold." - Adding verb for narrative flow
"The weather was cloudy and cold." - More formal/descriptive
```

### Scene-Setting Dialogue

```
A: "When did you tell him?"
B: "This morning, while running."
A: "What did he say?"
B: "He was shocked. His eyes went wide."
```

### Practice: Reconstructing the Timeline

**From the entry:**
- Morning: Running, thinking about the marathon
- School: Telling Minseo
- After school: Telling the teacher
- Evening: Telling family
- Night: Writing in room

**Dialogue practice:**
```
A: "Tell me about your day."
B: "Well, this morning while running, I decided something. At school, I told Minseo. After school, I told my teacher. In the evening, I told my family."
A: "What did they all say?"
```

### Common Mistakes

❌ **Incorrect:** "Cloudy and cold was the weather."
✅ **Correct:** "Cloudy and cold." or "It was cloudy and cold."

❌ **Incorrect:** "At the evening I told my family."
✅ **Correct:** "In the evening I told my family." or "That evening I told my family."

❌ **Incorrect:** "This morning, I was thinking in running."
✅ **Correct:** "This morning while running, I was thinking."

## PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS (구동사)

### Key Phrasal Verbs in the Text

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Korean | Example | Difficulty |
|-------------|---------|--------|---------|------------|
| **go wide** | Open very large (eyes) | (눈이) 커지다 | "His eyes went wide." | ★★☆☆☆ |
| **go up** | Increase | 올라가다 | "The distance is going up." (implied) | ★★☆☆☆ |
| **build up** | Gradually increase | 늘리다/증진하다 | "We need to build your base fitness up over winter." | ★★★☆☆ |
| **increase** | Go higher | 증가하다 | Not a phrasal verb, but "increase the distance" is important | ★★☆☆☆ |
| **let down** | Disappoint | 실망시키다 | Implied: "I don't want to let you down" | ★★★☆☆ |

### Understanding "go" + Adjective

The verb "go" can be used with adjectives to describe sudden changes:

```
- "His eyes went wide" = sudden opening of eyes
- "He went crazy" = became crazy
- "I went pale" = became pale
- "She went silent" = became silent
- "The situation went wrong" = situation became wrong
```

**Pattern:**
- Subject + "go" + adjective = sudden change of state
- Often used to describe emotions, expressions, or conditions

### Phrasal Verb: "build up"

**Structure:** build + object + up
**Alternative:** build up + object
**Meaning:** gradually increase or strengthen

```
"We need to build your base fitness over winter."
= We need to gradually increase your fitness foundation during winter.

Variations:
- "Build up your stamina" = gradually increase endurance
- "Build up your confidence" = gradually increase self-confidence
- "Build up your base" = create strong foundation
```

### Practice with Phrasal Verbs

```
A: "How will we prepare for the marathon?"
B: "We'll build your fitness up gradually over winter."
A: "Then what?"
B: "Starting in January, we'll really increase the distance."
```

### Common Mistakes with "go"

❌ **Incorrect:** "His eyes went to wide."
✅ **Correct:** "His eyes went wide."

❌ **Incorrect:** "He go crazy about the marathon."
✅ **Correct:** "He went crazy about the marathon." (past tense)

❌ **Incorrect:** "Build up your fitness" (as advice)
✅ **Correct:** "Build up your fitness" or "Build your fitness up" (both correct)

## PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

### Pattern 1: Announcement + Reaction

**Structure:**
```
A: "I'm going to [do something difficult]."
B: "What?!" / "Really?!" / "Are you sure?"
A: "[Confirms decision]"
B: "[Responds with emotion]"
```

**Examples from text:**
```
A: "I'm going to run a full marathon."
B: "What?! 42km?!"
A: "Yeah."
B: "But you said the half marathon was hard?"
```

**Practice variations:**
- "I'm going to run a marathon." / "What?! You hate running!"
- "I've decided to change jobs." / "Really?! When?!"
- "I'm going back to school." / "What?! At your age?!"

### Pattern 2: Expressing Doubt → Overcoming Doubt

**Structure:**
```
A: [Makes statement of doubt]
B: "I know. But [reason for doing it anyway]"
A: [Shows support]
```

**Examples:**
```
"But you said the half marathon was hard?"
"I know. But I want to try it."

"You struggled so much with the half marathon..."
"I know. But I want to try it."

"Going from 21km to 42km takes time."
"I know."
```

**Key phrase pattern:** "I know. But..."
- Shows you understand the concern
- Transitions to your reasoning
- Demonstrates mature thinking

### Pattern 3: Confirming a Decision

**Structure:**
```
A: "You've made up your mind?"
B: "Yes. [Specific detail]."
A: "[Shows support/agreement]"
```

**Examples:**
```
Teacher: "You've made up your mind?"
Doyun: "Yes. The Jeju Full Marathon on March 2nd."
Teacher: "Good. Let's prepare together."

Dad: "A full one? 42km?"
Doyun: "Yes."
Dad: "If that's what you want to do, Doyun, I'll support you."
```

### Pattern 4: Building Support Through Conversation

**Structure:**
```
1. Announce decision to different people
2. Each person responds differently
3. Each person eventually shows support
4. Build momentum through multiple confirmations
```

**The "three conversations" pattern:**
- Friend (Minseo): Expresses shock, then admits it's cool
- Authority (Teacher): Shows confidence, offers help
- Family: Shows concern, then offers support

**Why this works:** Repeating your goal to multiple people makes it more real and builds accountability.

### Pattern 5: Connecting Past, Present, and Future

**Structure:**
```
A: "I started [past difficulty]"
B: "Now [present achievement]"
C: "And I'm going to [future goal]"
```

**Example from text:**
```
"I started the year terrified of the bus ride.
Now in December, I'm committing to a full marathon.
I've changed a lot in a year.
Three months left. Let's do this."
```

### 5+ Practice Sentences Using These Patterns

1. **Announcement:** "I'm going to run a full marathon on March 2nd."
2. **Doubt response:** "I know it's difficult, but I've already trained for a half marathon."
3. **Confirmation:** "Yes, I've made up my mind. Absolutely."
4. **Support building:** "My teacher said he'd help me train, and my dad said he'd support me."
5. **Reflection:** "Six months ago I was scared of running 5km. Now I'm preparing for 42km."
6. **Commitment:** "Three months left, and I'm ready to do this."

### Alternative Structures for Similar Situations

**Announcing a big decision:**
- "I've decided to..."
- "I've made up my mind to..."
- "I'm going to..."
- "I'm committing to..."
- "I'm officially going to..."

**Responding to doubt:**
- "I know, but..." (acknowledges concern)
- "I understand, but..." (shows understanding)
- "Yeah, that's true, but..." (agrees with fact, differs on conclusion)
- "I hear you, but..." (validates concern, proceeds anyway)

### When to Use / When NOT to Use

✅ **Use "I know. But..."** when:
- Someone voices a legitimate concern
- You respect their opinion
- You want to show maturity in your thinking
- You want to maintain the relationship while disagreeing

❌ **Don't use "I know. But..."** when:
- You're dismissing someone's real safety concerns
- You're being stubborn without good reason
- Someone is giving expert advice you should follow
- You need to show more deference/respect

## PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

### Characteristic Features of Teen Speech in This Entry

#### 1. **Simple, Direct Sentences**
Instead of complex structures, teens often use short, punchy sentences:

```
"I've decided.
I'm going to run a full marathon.
42.195km."

NOT: "I have made the decision to run a full marathon, which is 42.195 kilometers in distance."
```

**Why:** Reflects internal thinking, creates rhythm, shows emotion through punctuation

#### 2. **One-Word Responses**
```
"What?!"
"Yeah."
"I know."
"Yeah. It was."
"So why?"
"Because I want to try it."
"Thanks."
```

**Why:** Shows comfort with conversation partner, confident communication, natural speech flow

#### 3. **Filler Words and Thinking Pauses**
While not explicit in the written text, implied by the diary format:

- "..." (ellipsis showing pause or thought)
- "Minseo stared at me for a while." (implies thinking, contemplation)
- Fragment sentences like "His eyes went wide." (immediate observation)

**In conversation, add:**
- "like" (filler, connects ideas)
- "you know?" (seeks agreement)
- "I mean" (clarification)
- "umm" / "uh" (thinking pause)

**Example with fillers:**
```
"So, like, I'm going to run a full marathon, you know?"
"Yeah, umm, I'm just like, super determined right now, I guess?"
```

#### 4. **Incomplete/Fragmented Sentences**
```
"This morning." (not "This morning I was running")
"At school." (not "At school, I told Minseo")
"After school." (not "After school, I had a conversation")
"Evening." (not "In the evening, I told my family")
```

**Why:** Diary style, free-form thinking, sets scene efficiently

#### 5. **Repetition for Emphasis**
```
"I know. But I want to try it."
(repeated after: "I know. But I want to try it.")
```

The same response repeated shows:
- Determination
- No room for negotiation
- Clear thinking
- Familiarity with conversation partner

#### 6. **Question-Form Statements**
```
"So why?" (Really = "Tell me why")
"You've made up your mind?" (Really = "You've definitely decided?")
"What?!" (Really = "Did you really just say that?")
```

These aren't requests for information but expressions of emotion disguised as questions.

#### 7. **Casual Profanity/Intensity Markers**
```
"You're actually crazy."
```

The word "crazy" here is:
- Complimentary, not insulting
- Casual, teen speech
- Shows intimacy between friends
- Equivalent to: "You're bold," "You're fearless," "You're insane (in a good way)"

#### 8. **Contrastive "But"**
```
"But you said the half marathon was hard?"
"But I want to try it."
"But it's cool."
"But I started the year terrified..."
```

"But" introduces:
- Contrast with previous statement
- Reason for proceeding anyway
- Acceptance after doubt

This is heavily used in the text and marks a key speech pattern.

### Converting Formal to Casual Teen Speech

| Formal | Teen Speech | Level |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| "I have made a decision." | "I've decided." | More casual |
| "I am going to participate in a full marathon." | "I'm going to run a full marathon." | More direct |
| "That is quite bold." | "That's actually crazy." | More honest |
| "I acknowledge your concern, but..." | "I know, but..." | More connected |
| "May I inquire as to why?" | "So why?" | More natural |

### Practice: Adding Teen Speech Features

**Original (formal):**
```
"I have decided that I will participate in a full marathon event."
```

**Teen version with fillers:**
```
"So, like, I've decided? I'm going to run a full marathon."
```

**Adding casual affirmation:**
```
"Yeah, I'm going to run a full marathon."
```

**With doubt acknowledgment:**
```
"I know it's crazy, but I'm going to run a full marathon."
```

### Common Mistakes with Casual Speech

❌ **Incorrect:** "I'm being decided about running a marathon."
✅ **Correct:** "I've decided to run a marathon."

❌ **Incorrect:** "What is the meaning of your statement?!" (Too formal for "What?!")
✅ **Correct:** "What?!" or "Really?!"

❌ **Incorrect:** "You possess a quality of craziness." (Too formal for "You're crazy")
✅ **Correct:** "You're crazy." or "You're actually crazy."

### Common Mistakes with Sentence Starters

❌ **Incorrect:** "I've decided to run, but a full marathon seems hard."
✅ **Correct:** "I've decided to run a full marathon, but it seems hard." (or: "...but I'm scared.")

❌ **Incorrect:** "The half marathon was hard, yet it was."
✅ **Correct:** "The half marathon was hard, but I did it." or "The half marathon was hard, yet I'm doing another one."

❌ **Incorrect:** "If that's what you want to do, Doyun, but I'll support you."
✅ **Correct:** "If that's what you want to do, Doyun, I'll support you." (remove "but")

## PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

### Practice Dialogue 1: Convincing Someone of Your Decision

**Scenario:** Your friend thinks your plan is crazy. Practice explaining your reasoning.

```
Friend: "Wait, you're doing WHAT? A full marathon?!"

You: "Yeah, I've decided to run the Jeju Full Marathon on March 2nd."

Friend: "But you told me the half marathon was so hard!"

You: "I know. It was really difficult. But I want to try the full distance now."

Friend: "That's insane. Why would you do that?"

You: "Because I want to challenge myself. I started this year scared of running 5km. Now I'm going to run 42km. I've changed a lot."

Friend: "Okay, you're actually crazy. But... it's cool."

You: "Thanks. I'm scared too, honestly. But I'm committed to this."

Friend: "Well, if that's what you want to do, I'll support you."

You: "That means a lot. Three months left to train!"
```

### Practice Dialogue 2: Discussing Your Training Plan with a Coach/Teacher

**Scenario:** Your teacher is creating a training plan for your marathon.

```
Teacher: "So you've decided to run a full marathon?"

You: "Yes, sir. The Jeju Full Marathon on March 2nd."

Teacher: "That's a big jump from your half marathon. You made up your mind?"

You: "Yes, absolutely. I want to do this."

Teacher: "Going from 21km to 42km takes serious preparation. We'll need to build your base fitness over winter."

You: "Okay, I understand. What's the plan?"

Teacher: "Starting in January, we'll really increase your distance. I'll make you a detailed training plan."

You: "Thank you, sir. I'm ready to work hard."

Teacher: "Good. Let's prepare together. You've shown amazing growth this year."

You: "That gives me confidence. I'm scared, but I know you'll help me."

Teacher: "You've got this."
```

### Practice Dialogue 3: Family Dinner Conversation

**Scenario:** You announce your marathon goal to your family over dinner.

```
You: "So... I've decided to run a full marathon."

Dad: "A full one? 42 kilometers? Are you sure?"

You: "Yes, I've made up my mind. The Jeju Full Marathon on March 2nd."

Mom: (looking concerned) "You struggled so much with the half marathon, honey..."

You: "I know, Mom. I remember how hard it was. But I want to try it."

Dad: "Why? Why push yourself that hard?"

You: "Because I want to see what I can do. I started the year terrified of just running to school. Now I'm training for a marathon. I've changed a lot."

Mom: "I worry about you getting hurt..."

You: "My teacher is helping me with a proper training plan. And I have three months to prepare."

Dad: "If that's what you want to do, Doyun, I'll support you. We'll support you."

You: "Thank you, Dad. Thank you, Mom. That means everything."

Mom: "Just promise to listen to your body."

You: "I will, I promise."
```

### Practice Dialogue 4: Processing Your Fear Privately with a Trusted Friend

**Scenario:** Late night conversation with close friend about your real fears.

```
You: "Hey, can I talk about the marathon?"

Friend: "Sure, what's up?"

You: "I'm scared, honestly. Like, really scared."

Friend: "Why did you decide to do it then?"

You: "Because even though I'm scared, I want to try it. Does that make sense?"

Friend: "Yeah, actually it does. That's brave."

You: "It doesn't feel brave. It feels crazy."

Friend: "That's what makes it brave. You're scared AND you're doing it."

You: "What if I can't finish?"

Friend: "What if you do? You've already come so far this year."

You: "Yeah... I have changed a lot. I guess I can do this."

Friend: "You've got this. And I'll be cheering for you."

You: "Thanks. That helps."
```

### Discussion Questions (5+)
