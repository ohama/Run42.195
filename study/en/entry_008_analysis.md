# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Weather & Morning Expressions

### "Clear skies. A bit windy."

**Original Korean:** "맑음. 바람 조금 부는 날."

**Analysis:**
- 일기 형식의 간결한 날씨 표현
- Fragment style (불완전 문장)로 일기 분위기 연출

**Weather Description Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example | Korean |
|---------|---------|--------|
| [Weather]. A bit [condition]. | "Clear skies. A bit windy." | 맑음. 바람 조금. |
| [Weather]. Pretty [condition]. | "Sunny. Pretty warm." | 화창함. 꽤 따뜻함. |
| [Weather]. Kind of [condition]. | "Cloudy. Kind of cold." | 흐림. 좀 추움. |

**Variations by Weather:**

| English | Korean | Formality |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| "Clear skies." | 맑은 하늘 | Neutral |
| "Not a cloud in the sky." | 하늘에 구름 한 점 없음 | Descriptive |
| "Beautiful day." | 좋은 날 | Casual |
| "Perfect weather." | 완벽한 날씨 | Enthusiastic |

**Wind Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "A bit windy" | ★☆☆☆☆ | 바람이 조금 분다 |
| "Breezy" | ★★☆☆☆ | 선선하다 |
| "Windy" | ★★★☆☆ | 바람이 분다 |
| "Very windy" | ★★★★☆ | 바람이 많이 분다 |
| "The wind is howling" | ★★★★★ | 바람이 윙윙 분다 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: How's the weather today?
B: Clear skies. A bit windy, though.
A: Perfect for a walk then.
B: Exactly what I was thinking.
```

### "I checked the weather forecast last night."

**Original Korean:** "어젯밤에 일기예보를 확인했다."

**Grammar Point:** Simple past for completed actions

**Weather-related Vocabulary:**

| English | Korean | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| weather forecast | 일기예보 | "Did you check the weather forecast?" |
| check the weather | 날씨를 확인하다 | "Let me check the weather real quick." |
| weather app | 날씨 앱 | "My weather app says it'll rain." |
| forecast says | 예보에 따르면 | "The forecast says sunny tomorrow." |

**Common Phrases:**
```
"What's the weather like tomorrow?"
"Is it supposed to rain?"
"The forecast says..."
"According to the weather..."
"It's calling for rain." (예보상 비 온대)
```

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: Are you going to walk tomorrow?
B: I'm not sure. I need to check the weather forecast.
A: I heard it might rain.
B: Yeah, that's what I'm worried about.
```

### "The sun was actually shining."

**Original Korean:** "해가 진짜 빛나고 있었다."

**Grammar Point:** "Actually" for pleasant surprise

**"Actually" Nuances:**

| Usage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 기대와 다르게 좋음 | 의외로/진짜로 | "The sun was actually shining." |
| 정정 | 사실은 | "Actually, I can't come." |
| 강조 | 정말로 | "I'm actually excited." |
| 예상 반전 | 알고 보니 | "It actually worked!" |

**Expressing Pleasant Surprise:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "actually" | 진짜로/의외로 | 기대보다 좋을 때 |
| "to my surprise" | 놀랍게도 | 더 격식체 |
| "believe it or not" | 믿거나 말거나 | 강조할 때 |
| "sure enough" | 역시나 | 예상대로일 때 |

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "The sun was actually shine." (동사 형태 오류)
✅ "The sun was actually shining."

## 1.2 Greeting & Short Conversation Expressions

### "Good morning."

**Original Korean:** "안녕하세요."

**Analysis:** 간단하지만 중요한 인사말. 한국어의 "안녕하세요"보다 시간대 특정적.

**Time-based Greetings:**

| Time | Greeting | Response |
|------|----------|----------|
| 아침 (~12pm) | "Good morning." | "Good morning." / "Morning." |
| 점심 (12pm~6pm) | "Good afternoon." | "Good afternoon." |
| 저녁 (6pm~) | "Good evening." | "Good evening." |
| 밤/헤어질 때 | "Good night." | "Good night." |

**Casual vs. Formal:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Morning." | "Good morning." | "Good morning, sir/ma'am." |
| "Hey." | "Hello." | "Hello. How are you?" |
| "What's up?" | "Hi, how are you?" | "Good day." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Doyun: Good morning.
Grandfather: Oh, student. Walking again?
Doyun: Yes.
Grandfather: Keep it up. Walking is good for you.
```

### "Walking is good for you."

**Original Korean:** "걷기는 몸에 좋아."

**Grammar Point:** [Gerund] + is good for you

**Health Advice Pattern:**

| Activity | Sentence |
|----------|----------|
| Walking | "Walking is good for you." |
| Sleeping well | "Sleeping well is good for you." |
| Drinking water | "Drinking water is good for you." |
| Exercise | "Exercise is good for you." |

**Variations:**

| Pattern | Example | Nuance |
|---------|---------|--------|

| "X is great for you." | "Running is great for your heart." | 강조 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```

```

### "You really do like walking, huh."

**Original Korean:** "너 진짜 걷는 거 좋아하는구나."

**Grammar Point:** "really do" for emphasis + "huh" tag question

**Analysis:**
- "really do" = 강조 ("진짜")
- "huh" = 확인/동의 구하는 태그 ("~구나", "~지")

**"Really do" Emphasis Pattern:**

| Without emphasis | With emphasis |
|------------------|---------------|
| "You like walking." | "You really do like walking." |
| "She works hard." | "She really does work hard." |
| "I need this." | "I really do need this." |

**Tag Questions for Confirmation:**

| Tag | Nuance | Example |
|-----|--------|---------|
| "huh" | 캐주얼, 확인 | "Nice day, huh?" |
| "right?" | 동의 구함 | "You're coming, right?" |
| "yeah?" | 매우 캐주얼 | "Pretty cool, yeah?" |
| "don't you think?" | 의견 구함 | "It's good, don't you think?" |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: You walked again today?
B: Yeah.
A: You really do like walking, huh.
B: I don't know if I like it... but I don't hate it.
A: Better than the bus?
B: Way better.
```

---

## 1.3 Family Dinner Conversation

### "How have you been getting to school lately?"

**Original Korean:** "요즘 학교 어떻게 다녀?"

**Grammar Point:** Present perfect continuous for recent habits

**Structure:** How have you been + [gerund]?

**Similar Questions:**

| Question | Context | Korean |
|----------|---------|--------|
| "How have you been getting to school?" | 통학 방법 | 학교 어떻게 다녀? |
| "How have you been sleeping?" | 수면 상태 | 요즘 잠은 잘 자? |
| "How have you been feeling?" | 건강/기분 | 요즘 기분 어때? |
| "How have you been eating?" | 식습관 | 요즘 밥은 잘 먹어? |

**Variations:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "How you getting to school?" | "How have you been getting to school?" | "May I ask how you've been commuting?" |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Mom: Doyun, how have you been getting to school lately?

Doyun: ... Sometimes I walk.

```

### "Sometimes I walk."

**Original Korean:** "가끔 걸어요."

**Frequency Expressions:**

| Expression | Frequency | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "always" | 100% | 항상 |
| "usually" | 80-90% | 보통 |
| "often" | 60-70% | 자주 |
| "sometimes" | 30-50% | 가끔 |
| "rarely" | 10-20% | 드물게 |
| "never" | 0% | 절대 안 |

**Context Note:**
이 장면에서 "Sometimes"는 사실을 약화시키는 표현. 실제로는 4번 걸었지만, 적게 들리게 하려고 "sometimes"라고 함.

**Hedging with "Sometimes":**
```
"Sometimes I eat out." (실제로 자주 먹지만 숨김)
"I sometimes stay up late." (매일 그러지만 약화)
"Sometimes I skip breakfast." (자주 건너뛰지만 약화)
```

### "I heard from your aunt."

**Original Korean:** "이모한테 들었어."

**Grammar Point:** "I heard from [person]" = ~한테서 들었다

**Information Source Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Context |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I heard from your aunt." | 이모한테서 들었어 | 직접 연락 |
| "I heard about you." | 네 얘기 들었어 | 소문/간접 |
| "I heard that you..." | 네가 ~했다고 들었어 | 구체적 정보 |
| "Someone told me..." | 누가 말해줬는데... | 출처 모호 |

**Similar Expressions:**

| English | Korean | Nuance |
|---------|--------|--------|
| "I heard from X" | X한테서 들었다 | 직접 연락 받음 |
| "X told me" | X가 말해줬다 | 직접 정보 전달 |
| "According to X" | X에 따르면 | 격식체 |
| "X mentioned that" | X가 언급했다 | 부수적 정보 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Mom: I heard from your aunt.
Doyun: ... (silence)
Mom: She said you've been walking in the mornings. Multiple times.
Doyun: ...
Mom: How many times have you walked?
```

### "I thought it was just once in a while."

**Original Korean:** "가끔인 줄 알았는데."

**Grammar Point:** "I thought" for incorrect assumption

**"I thought" Patterns:**

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| "I thought it was X." | X인 줄 알았다 | "I thought it was just once." |
| "I thought you were X." | 네가 X인 줄 알았다 | "I thought you were busy." |
| "I thought we were X." | 우리가 X인 줄 알았다 | "I thought we were friends." |

**Expressing Surprise at New Information:**

| Expression | Intensity | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "I thought..." | ★★☆ | ~인 줄 알았는데 |
| "I had no idea..." | ★★★ | 전혀 몰랐어 |
| "You're telling me..." | ★★★★ | ~라고? (놀람) |
| "Wait, what?" | ★★★★★ | 뭐라고? |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: I've walked to school four times.
B: Four times? I thought it was just once in a while.
A: I took the bus when it rained.
B: Obviously. You can't walk in the rain.
```

### "Obviously."

**Original Korean:** "당연히."

**Usage:** 너무 당연한 말에 대한 반응

**"Obviously" Variations:**

| Expression | Tone | Korean |
|------------|------|--------|
| "Obviously." | 약간 짜증 | 당연하지 |
| "Of course." | 중립 | 물론이지 |
| "Duh." | 캐주얼/짜증 | 당연한 거지 (slang) |
| "Clearly." | 격식 | 분명히 |
| "Naturally." | 격식 | 자연스럽게 |

**Context:**
엄마가 "Obviously"라고 한 것은 약간의 짜증이나 "그건 당연한 거지"라는 뉘앙스.

**Example Sentences:**
```
"You can't walk in the rain." → "Obviously."
"You need to eat to live." → "Obviously."
"She's mad because you forgot her birthday." → "Obviously."
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Exhaustion & Tiredness

### "It'll wear you out."

**Original Korean:** "지칠 거야."

**Vocabulary Deep Dive:**

**"Wear out" /wɛr aʊt/**
- 원래 의미: 닳게 하다 (옷, 신발)
- 비유적 의미: 지치게 하다, 기진맥진하게 하다

**Tiredness Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "I'm a bit tired." | ★☆☆☆☆ | 좀 피곤해 |
| "I'm tired." | ★★☆☆☆ | 피곤해 |
| "I'm pretty tired." | ★★★☆☆ | 꽤 피곤해 |
| "I'm exhausted." | ★★★★☆ | 지쳤어 |
| "I'm worn out." | ★★★★☆ | 기진맥진해 |
| "I'm dead tired." | ★★★★★ | 완전 지쳤어 |
| "I'm running on empty." | ★★★★★ | 힘이 하나도 안 남았어 |

**"Wear out" Usage:**

| Subject | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Activity wears you out | "Walking every day will wear you out." | 매일 걷기가 널 지치게 할 거야 |
| I'm worn out | "I'm completely worn out." | 완전히 지쳤어 |
| Wear oneself out | "Don't wear yourself out." | 너무 무리하지 마 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Mom: Walking for over an hour is too much. It'll wear you out.
Doyun: It is tiring, but I'm okay.
Mom: You say you're okay, but I still worry.
```

---

### "It is tiring, but I'm okay."

**Original Korean:** "힘들긴 한데 괜찮아요."

**Grammar Point:** Concession + but + reassurance

**Structure:** It is [negative], but I'm [positive].

**Similar Concession Patterns:**

| Admission | Reassurance | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| "It is tiring" | "I'm okay" | "It is tiring, but I'm okay." |
| "It is hard" | "I can handle it" | "It is hard, but I can handle it." |
| "It is far" | "I don't mind" | "It is far, but I don't mind." |
| "It is cold" | "I'm fine" | "It is cold, but I'm fine." |

**Alternative Structures:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "Sure, X, but Y." | "Sure, it's tiring, but I'm okay." |
| "X, but that doesn't mean Y." | "It's tiring, but that doesn't mean I can't do it." |
| "X, true, but Y." | "Tiring, true, but worth it." |

### "The packed bus. The smell. The nausea. That suffocating feeling."

**Original Korean:** "꽉 찬 버스. 냄새. 메스꺼움. 그 숨 막히는 느낌."

**Literary Device:** Fragment listing for emotional impact

**Why This Works:**
- 완전한 문장 대신 명사구 나열
- 독자가 직접 경험하는 것 같은 효과
- 불쾌감을 더 강하게 전달
- 내면의 생각 흐름 표현

**Similar Fragment Patterns:**

| Context | Fragments |
|---------|-----------|
| 버스 | "The packed bus. The smell. The nausea." |
| 시험 | "The questions. The pressure. That blank feeling." |
| 첫 날 | "New faces. Strange halls. That lost feeling." |

**Suffocation Expressions:**

| Expression | Intensity | Korean |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| "stuffy" | ★★☆ | 답답한 |
| "stifling" | ★★★ | 숨막히는 |
| "suffocating" | ★★★★ | 질식할 것 같은 |
| "I can't breathe" | ★★★★★ | 숨을 못 쉬겠어 |

## 2.3 General Physical State

### "Since I started walking, all of that is gone."

**Original Korean:** "걷기 시작한 이후로 그런 것들이 다 사라졌다."

**Grammar Point:** "Since I started X" for cause-effect

**Pattern:** Since I started [gerund], [result]

**Examples:**

| Activity | Result | Full Sentence |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| walking | no more nausea | "Since I started walking, I don't feel sick anymore." |
| exercising | more energy | "Since I started exercising, I have more energy." |
| reading | better vocabulary | "Since I started reading more, my vocabulary improved." |
| meditating | less stress | "Since I started meditating, I feel less stressed." |

**"All of that" Reference:**
```
Previous: "The packed bus. The smell. The nausea. That suffocating feeling."
→ "Since I started walking, all of that is gone."
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING SPACES/SITUATIONS (공간/상황 묘사)

## 3.1 Describing a Tense Atmosphere

### "The rest of dinner felt heavy."

**Original Korean:** "나머지 저녁 식사는 무거운 분위기였다."

**Vocabulary:** "Heavy" as emotional weight

**"Heavy" Meanings:**

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 분위기 | 무거운/불편한 | "The conversation felt heavy." |
| 감정 | 무거운/슬픈 | "My heart felt heavy." |
| 주제 | 무거운/심각한 | "That's a heavy topic." |
| 물리적 | 무거운 | "This bag is heavy." |

**Tense Atmosphere Expressions:**

| Expression | Korean | Intensity |

| "I can't stop." | 불가능 (의지와 상관없이) |

### "Even if Mom says no."

**Original Korean:** "엄마가 안 된다고 해도."

**Grammar Point:** "Even if" for hypothetical opposition

**"Even if" Patterns:**

| Situation | Full Sentence |
|-----------|---------------|
| 엄마 반대 | "Even if Mom says no, I want to walk." |
| 비 옴 | "Even if it rains, I'll walk." |
| 힘듦 | "Even if it's hard, I won't give up." |

**Variations:**

| Expression | Formality |
|------------|-----------|
| "Even if..." | Neutral |
| "Even though..." | 사실 기반 (이미 일어남) |
| "No matter what..." | 강한 결심 |
| "Regardless of..." | 격식체 |

**Important Distinction:**

| Expression | When to Use |
|------------|-------------|
| "Even if" | 가정 (아직 안 일어남) |
| "Even though" | 사실 (이미 일어남) |

**Examples:**
```
"Even if Mom says no..." (아직 안 말함)
"Even though Mom said no..." (이미 말함)
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Parent Asking About Daily Routine

```
Mom: How have you been getting to school lately?
You: Uh... sometimes I walk.
Mom: Sometimes? How often is sometimes?
You: Maybe three or four times this week.
Mom: That's almost every day! Why didn't you tell me?
You: I didn't think it was a big deal.
Mom: Walking for over an hour in the dark? That's a big deal.
You: I'm always careful.
Mom: Being careful doesn't mean nothing will happen.
You: ...
Mom: I'm worried about you.
You: I know, Mom. But I really like walking.
```

### Scenario 2: Defending Your Choice to a Friend

```
Friend: Your mom found out you've been walking?
You: Yeah. My aunt saw me and told her.
Friend: Uh oh. Was she mad?
You: Not mad exactly. More like... worried.
Friend: What did she say?
You: That it's too tiring, too dark, too dangerous.
Friend: Is she right?
You: I mean... kind of? But I still want to walk.
Friend: What are you going to do?
You: I don't know. I need to figure out what to say to her.
```

### Scenario 3: Internal Dialogue (Thinking Aloud)

```
"Why do I like walking so much?"
"Because the bus makes me sick? Yes."
"Because the morning air is nice? That too."
"Because I like meeting the grandfather? Also true."
"But there's something more..."
"I can't put it into words."
"How do I explain this to Mom?"
"I already said I'll be careful."
"I already said I'm fine."
"What else can I say?"
"I don't know."
"I'll think about it Monday."
```
