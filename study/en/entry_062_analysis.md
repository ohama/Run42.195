# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Post-Achievement Reactions

### "That's insane. That's seriously amazing."

**Original Korean:** "진짜? 대박이다. 정말 대단해."

**Analysis:**
- Two-part reaction: initial disbelief + genuine admiration
- "That's insane" conveys impressive achievement (not negative)
- "That's seriously amazing" reinforces with stronger emphasis

**Variations by Intensity:**

| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "That's nice." | ★☆☆ | 약한 칭찬 |
| "That's really good." | ★★☆ | 일반적 칭찬 |
| "That's amazing." | ★★★ | 감탄적 칭찬 |
| "That's insane." | ★★★★ | 대단함 강조 |
| "That's seriously amazing." | ★★★★★ | 최고의 칭찬 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: I finished a 10km run.
B: What? That's insane! That's seriously amazing!
A: Thanks, I've been training for months.
B: It shows. You must be so proud.
```

**Similar Congratulatory Phrases:**
- "Congratulations!"
- "That's incredible!"
- "I'm so impressed."
- "Well done!"
- "You crushed it!"

---

### "Congratulations on finishing the marathon."

**Original Korean:** "마라톤 완주 축하합니다."

**Grammar Point:** Formal congratulation structure

**Structure:** Congratulations on + [noun/gerund]

**Examples with "Congratulations on":**

| Achievement | Full Sentence |
|-------------|---------------|
| finishing marathon | "Congratulations on finishing the marathon." |
| getting the job | "Congratulations on getting the job." |
| graduation | "Congratulations on your graduation." |
| promotion | "Congratulations on your promotion." |
| new baby | "Congratulations on the new baby." |

**Formality Levels:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Nice one!" | "Congratulations!" | "Please accept my congratulations." |
| "You did it!" | "Congrats on finishing." | "Congratulations on your achievement." |
| "Way to go!" | "That's impressive." | "I extend my congratulations." |

**School Context:**
- "That's impressive." (선생님 톤)
- "I'm rooting for you." (응원 표현)

---

### "Are you going to keep running?"

**Original Korean:** "계속 달릴 거야?"

**Grammar Point:** Future intention question with "going to"

**Structure:** Are you going to + 동사원형?

**Similar Future Questions:**

| Question | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "Are you going to keep running?" | 앞으로 계속할 거냐 |
| "Will you run again?" | 다시 할 거냐 |
| "What's next?" | 다음 목표가 뭐냐 |
| "Do you have a next goal?" | 다음 목표 있냐 |
| "Are you going to do it again?" | 또 할 거냐 |

**Responding to Future Plans:**

| Response | Tone |
|----------|------|
| "Yes, I'm going to." | 확실함 |
| "I think so." | 약간 불확실 |
| "Maybe, I'm not sure yet." | 미정 |
| "Probably not." | 부정적 |
| "Definitely not." | 명확한 거절 |

## 1.2 Goal-Setting Conversation

### "So, what's your next goal?"

**Original Korean:** "그다음 목표가 뭐야?"

**Analysis:** Casual, friendly inquiry about future plans

**Similar Goal-Asking Questions:**

| Question | Formality | Context |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| "What's your next goal?" | Casual | 친구들 사이 |
| "Do you have a next goal?" | Standard | 일반적 |

| "What's the plan?" | Casual | 향후 계획 |
| "What's next?" | Very casual | 자연스러운 질문 |

**Answering About Goals:**

| Goal Size | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Small goal | "I want to run 10km regularly." |
| Medium goal | "A half marathon, I think." |
| Big goal | "Eventually, a full marathon." |

### "A half marathon. 21km."

**Original Korean:** "하프 마라톤. 21킬로미터."

**Analysis:** Fragment structure - two facts stated separately for emphasis

**Why Use Fragments:**
- 목표를 명확하게 강조
- 자신감 있는 톤
- 정보를 단호하게 전달

**Related Goal Statements:**

| Goal | Fragment Structure |
|------|-------------------|
| Marathon | "A full marathon. 42.195km." |
| Half marathon | "A half. 21km." |
| 5K run | "A 5K race. 5 kilometers." |
| Long distance | "Long distance running." |

---

### "Can I do it?"

**Original Korean:** "할 수 있을까?"

**Grammar Point:** Ability/possibility question

**Structure:** Can I + 동사원형?

**Contexts for This Question:**

| Doubt Type | Example |
|------------|---------|
| 능력 의심 | "Can I do it?" (할 수 있을까?) |
| 체력 확신 없음 | "Can I make it?" (버틸 수 있을까?) |
| 충분함 의심 | "Is that enough time?" (충분할까?) |
| 가능성 불확실 | "Will I be ready?" (준비 됐을까?) |

**Reassuring Responses:**

| Response | Tone |
|----------|------|
| "Of course you can." | 확신 |
| "Why not? You can do it." | 응원 |
| "You've got this." | 격려 |
| "Absolutely. You're ready." | 강한 동의 |
| "I believe in you." | 신뢰 표현 |

---

### "Could you help me train again, coach?"

**Original Korean:** "코치님, 다시 훈련을 도와줄 수 있어요?"

**Grammar Point:** Polite request for help

**Structure:** Could you + 동사원형 + again?

**Polite Request Levels:**

| Politeness | Structure | Example |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Casual | "Will you help me?" | 친근함 |
| Standard | "Can you help me?" | 표준 |
| Polite | "Could you help me?" | 공손함 |
| Very polite | "Would you be willing to help me?" | 매우 공손 |

**"Again" for Repeated Action:**
- "Help me again" = 다시 도와주다
- "Practice again" = 다시 연습하다
- "Try again" = 다시 시도하다

## 1.3 Reflective Expressions

### "It still doesn't feel real."

**Original Korean:** "아직도 현실 같지 않아."

**Grammar Point:** Present perfect continuous for ongoing state

**Uses:** 놀라운 경험의 비현실성

| Situation | Sentence |
|-----------|----------|
| 졸업 후 | "Graduating still doesn't feel real." |
| 합격 후 | "Getting in doesn't feel real yet." |
| 결혼 후 | "Being married still doesn't feel real." |
| 큰 성취 후 | "Finishing the marathon doesn't feel real." |

**Variations:**
- "It feels surreal." (초현실적이다)
- "It feels like a dream." (꿈 같다)
- "I can't believe it happened." (일어났다는 게 못 믿겠다)
- "It hasn't sunk in yet." (아직 실감이 안 난다)

---

### "I touched the medal. It was cold and solid."

**Original Korean:** "메달을 만졌다. 차갑고 단단했다."

**Analysis:** Sensory verification - 현실을 감각으로 확인

**Tactile Sensation Vocabulary:**

| Texture | Korean | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| cold | 차갑다 | "It was cold and solid." |
| warm | 따뜻하다 | "The sun was warm on my face." |
| smooth | 매끈하다 | "The surface was smooth." |
| rough | 거칠다 | "The texture was rough." |
| hard/solid | 단단하다 | "The medal was cold and solid." |
| soft | 부드럽다 | "The pillow was soft." |
| heavy | 무겁다 | "It was heavier than expected." |
| light | 가볍다 | "The jacket was light." |

**Pattern: Sensory Detail + Emotion:**
```
"I touched the medal. It was [sensory detail]."
= 현실을 감각으로 확인하는 표현
```

# PART 3: DESCRIBING ACHIEVEMENTS & DISTANCES (성취와 거리 묘사)

## 3.1 Achievement Descriptions

### "48 minutes 47 seconds."

**Original Korean:** "48분 47초."

**Analysis:** Specific achievement statement - facts over emotion

**Time Format in English:**

| Format | Example | How to Say |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Hours:Minutes | 2:15:30 | "Two fifteen thirty" / "Two hours, fifteen minutes, thirty seconds" |
| Minutes:Seconds | 48:47 | "Forty-eight forty-seven" / "Forty-eight minutes, forty-seven seconds" |
| Spoken casual | 48:47 | "Forty-eight forty-seven" |
| Spoken formal | 48:47 | "Forty-eight minutes and forty-seven seconds" |

---

### "Wait, you did it in under 50 minutes?"

**Original Korean:** "뭐, 50분 안에 했다고?"

**Grammar Point:** "In under" = ~이하로, ~미만으로

**"Under" Usage for Comparison:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "in under 50 minutes" | 50분 미만으로 |
| "in under an hour" | 1시간 미만으로 |
| "under budget" | 예산 이하로 |
| "under pressure" | 압박 속에서 |

**Time Achievement Expressions:**

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| "in [time]" | "I finished in 48 minutes." |
| "in under [time]" | "I did it in under 50 minutes." |
| "[time] flat" | "Fifty minutes flat." (정확히) |
| "in just [time]" | "In just 48 minutes!" |
| "[time] or less" | "Under 50 minutes or less." |

## 3.2 Distance Comparisons

### "I ran 10km, so 7km seems short now."

**Original Korean:** "10킬로미터를 뛰었으니까, 7킬로미터가 짧게 느껴져."

**Grammar Point:** Comparison based on recent achievement

**Structure:** [Recent achievement], so [shorter distance] + feels short now

**Distance Perspective Shift:**

| Before | After Longer Distance | Effect |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| 7km felt long | Ran 10km | 7km feels short |
| 5km seemed far | Ran 21km | 5km feels easy |
| 1km was tough | Ran marathon | 1km is nothing |

**Relative Distance Language:**

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| "seems short" | 짧아 보인다 |
| "feels easier" | 더 쉬워 보인다 |
| "is nothing compared to" | ~에 비하면 아무것도 아니다 |
| "pales in comparison" | 비교가 안 된다 |

---

## 3.3 Progress Markers

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "That's [intensity] [adjective]."

**Structure:** Emotional reaction using "That's" + degree + adjective

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "That's insane." | 미쳤다 |
| "That's seriously amazing." | 진짜 대단하다 |
| "That's incredible." | 대박이다 |
| "That's impressive." | 인상적이다 |

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"That's amazing."
"That's seriously impressive."
"That's absolutely incredible."
"That's completely insane."
```

---

## Pattern 2: "I ran X, so Y seems short now."

**Structure:** [Recent achievement], so [smaller task] seems [easier/shorter]

| Example |
|---------|
| "I ran 10km, so 7km seems short now." |
| "I finished the marathon, so 5km is nothing." |
| "I climbed that mountain, so this hill seems easy." |

**This Pattern Shows:**
- 성취감
- 능력 향상
- 관점의 변화

---

## Pattern 3: "What's your next goal?"

**Structure:** Open-ended goal inquiry

**Follow-up Responses:**

| Response Type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Ambitious | "A half marathon. 21km." |
| Cautious | "Maybe another 10k race." |
| Vague | "I'm still figuring it out." |
| Long-term | "Eventually, a full marathon." |

---

## Pattern 4: "Can I do it?"

**Structure:** Self-questioning about ability/readiness

| Context | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Before event | 할 수 있을까? |
| After setback | 다시 해낼 수 있을까? |
| Before bigger challenge | 더 큰 것도 할 수 있을까? |

**Reassurance Pattern:**

```
A: "Can I do it?"
B: "Why not? You can do it."
   / "Of course you can."
   / "You've got this."
```

---

## Pattern 5: "First time [action] since [time period]."

**Structure:** Significant marker of return to activity

| Example |
|---------|

| Request |
|---------|
| "Could you help me train again, coach?" |
| "Could you help me study again, teacher?" |
| "Could you guide me again, mentor?" |

---

## Pattern 7: "Maybe one day I'll... ...?"

**Structure:** Dreaming about distant future possibility

**Uses:** 먼 미래의 가능성을 생각하기

| Example |
|---------|

# PART 7: TEEN/CASUAL SPEECH FEATURES (구어체 특징)

## 7.1 Fragment Sentences for Emphasis

**Why Use Fragments:**
- 사실을 강조
- 에너지 표현
- 명확한 목표 설정
- 생각의 흐름 표현

| Fragment | Effect | Full Sentence |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| "48 minutes 47 seconds." | 시간 강조 | "I finished in 48 minutes and 47 seconds." |
| "A half marathon. 21km." | 목표 강조 | "My next goal is a half marathon, which is 21km." |
| "5 days since the race ended." | 시간 강조 | "Five days have passed since the race ended." |

---

## 7.2 Rhetorical Questions

**For Reflection:**

| Question | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| "Did I really run it?" | 성취를 믿을 수 없음 |
| "Can I do it?" | 다음 도전 위해 자문 |
| "Could I run a full marathon someday?" | 큰 꿈에 대한 의문 |

**Rhetorical vs. Real Questions:**
```
Rhetorical: "Did I really do that?" (답 기대 안 함)
Real: "Did I really do that?" (네/아니오 기대함)
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Congratulating a Friend on Achievement

```
A: I can't believe you finished! That's insane!
B: Thanks! It was really tough.
A: That's seriously amazing. What was your time?
B: 48 minutes and 47 seconds.
A: Wait, you did it in under 50 minutes? You're crazy!
B: Yeah, I'm pretty happy about it.
A: You should be! Are you going to keep running?
B: Yeah, I want to try even longer distances.
A: Really? That's awesome. What's your next goal?
B: A half marathon. 21km.
A: That's twice what you just did!
B: I know, but I think I can do it.
A: Of course you can. I'm rooting for you!
```

---

### Scenario 2: Coach and Student Returning Equipment

```
Coach: Hey, thanks for returning the watch. Did it help?
Student: Yeah, it really helped me keep the right pace.
Coach: Good. I heard you finished in 48:47. That's incredible!
Student: Thank you, coach. It was thanks to you.
Coach: No, that was all your hard work.
Student: So, do you have a next goal?
Coach: A half marathon. 21km.
Student: Can you do it?
Student: Why not? You just proved you can.
Coach: Could you help me train again, coach?
Coach: Of course. Let's make a training plan together.
Student: Thank you so much!
Coach: You're welcome. Let's do this.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Reflecting on Achievement with a Friend

```
A: So how are you feeling after the race?
B: Honestly? It still doesn't feel real.
A: I get that. You actually finished a marathon!
B: I know! I keep touching the medal just to make sure.
A: [laughs] That's funny. How does it feel?
B: Cold and solid. Very real, actually.
A: And now you're already thinking about your next goal?
B: Yeah, a half marathon. 21km.
A: That's a big jump.
B: Well, I ran 10km, so 21km should be doable.
A: Have you trained for that before?
B: Not yet, but my coach and I are making a plan.
A: You're crazy, but I love it. I'm rooting for you!
B: Thanks. Maybe one day I'll even run a full marathon.
A: Why not? You're already doing impossible things.
```
