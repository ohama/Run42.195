# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Running & Athletic Context

### "Scale back your training."

**Original Korean:** "훈련을 줄여야 해."

**Analysis:**
- 운동 선수들 사이의 일반적인 표현
- Coach의 조언/지시 형태
- 중요: "cut back", "reduce", "scale back" 모두 같은 의미

**Variations by Context:**

| Expression | Intensity | When to Use |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| "Take it easy." | ★☆☆ | 캐주얼한 조언 |
| "Reduce your workouts." | ★★☆ | 중립적 지시 |
| "Scale back your training." | ★★☆ | 전문적 지시 |
| "Cut back on exercise." | ★★☆ | 의료 조언 |
| "Pull back now." | ★★★ | 긴급/중요 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
Coach: You're looking tired. Scale back your training this week.
You: But I feel strong. Can't I keep pushing?
Coach: Trust me. Your body needs rest to perform well.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "Cut down your training" (틀린 표현)
✅ "Cut back on your training" 또는 "Scale back your training"

**Related Athletic Expressions:**

## 1.2 Race & Competition Vocabulary

### "One week left."

**Original Korean:** "남은 시간이 일주일이다."

**Countdown Expressions:**

| Time | Expression | Korean |
|------|------------|--------|
| 7 days | "One week left" 또는 "D-7" | 일주일 남았다 |
| 3 days | "Three days left" 또는 "D-3" | 사흘 남았다 |
| 1 day | "Tomorrow" 또는 "D-1" | 내일 |
| 0 days | "Race day" 또는 "D-Day" | 경기 당일 |

**D- Notation:**
- "D"는 "Day"를 의미하며, 중요한 행사까지의 날 수
- 원래 군사 용어 (D-Day = 상륙 작전일)
- 현대에는 스포츠, 기업, 이벤트에서 광범위하게 사용

**Sentence Variations:**
```
"One week left until the race."
"Seven days until race day."
"The race is next Saturday."
"In seven days, I'll be running."
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "One week left until the race."

**Structure:** Time marker + "left until" / "left before" + event

**Countdown Patterns:**

| Format | Example |
|--------|---------|
| "One week left." | 1주 남음 (간단) |
| "One week left until the race." | 1주 남음 (명확) |
| "The race is in one week." | 1주 후 경주 |
| "Seven days until race day." | 경주일까지 7일 |

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"Three months left until graduation."
"Two weeks left in the semester."
"One day left before my birthday."
```

---

## Pattern 2: "Back in March, I was getting carsick. Now I'm running a marathon."

**Structure:** Time reference + contrast marker + contrasting situations

**Contrast Structures:**

| Marker | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| "Back in..." | 과거 시점 | "Back in March, I was nervous." |
| "Now..." | 현재 | "Now, I'm confident." |
| "Then..." | 그때 | "Then, I was scared." |
| "These days..." | 요즘 | "These days, I'm focused." |

**Full Pattern with Contrast:**
```
"Back in March, I was getting carsick on the bus.
Now I'm running in a marathon.
I can't believe it."
```

**Reflection Phrases:**
- "What a change." (얼마나 많은 변화)
- "I've come a long way." (많이 성장했어)
- "Who would have thought?" (누가 생각했겠어)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Conversation with Coach About Taper

```
Coach: One week before the race. How are you feeling?
You: Nervous but excited. I've trained really hard.
Coach: Good. That's why we're scaling back your training now.
You: Really? I feel like I should keep pushing.
Coach: Trust me. Your body needs rest to be in peak condition.
You: Okay, I understand. How much should I train?
Coach: Just easy runs. 5km at most. Let your body recover.
You: Got it. I want to finish strong.
Coach: You've done the work. Now rest and you'll be fine.
```

## 10.4 Multiple Choice Practice

### Question 1
**"I'll give you more mandarins after the race."**

What is the grandfather implying?
- A) The harvest will continue after October 5th
- B) He's congratulating the narrator in advance for finishing
- C) Mandarins will be ready after one week
- D) He wants to watch the race

**Answer: B** - The grandfather is offering a reward/celebration gift, implying confidence in the narrator's success.

---

### Question 2
**"Scale back your training. Your body needs rest so you'll be in peak condition on race day."**

The coach is describing which training phase?
- A) Building phase
- B) Maintenance phase
- C) Taper phase
- D) Recovery phase

**Answer: C** - This is the taper phase, reducing intensity before a race.

---

### Question 3
**"I don't want to let them down. Actually, I don't want to let myself down."**

What does "actually" reveal about the narrator's priority?
- A) Friends are more important than family
- B) Personal goals matter more than others' expectations
- C) Self-discipline is the real motivation
- D) The narrator is selfish

**Answer: B** or **C** - The shift from external motivation (others' expectations) to internal motivation (personal goals) reveals maturity.

## 10.5 Discussion Questions

1. **Comparing then and now:** "Back in March, I was getting carsick. Now I'm running a marathon." What major changes have you experienced in a year? How did you grow?

2. **Taper mentality:** Why is it difficult to scale back training right before a big event, even when you know you should? What strategies help you manage this?

3. **External vs. internal motivation:** The narrator says, "I don't want to let them down. Actually, I don't want to let myself down." Which is more important to you—others' expectations or your own standards?

4. **Support systems:** How important is having people "rooting for you"? Who are the people in your support system, and how do they help?

5. **Pre-event rituals:** Many athletes have specific routines before competition (packing, eating certain foods, visualization). What's your pre-event ritual for something important?

6. **Goal-setting:** The narrator has a specific goal: "Under 50 minutes." How specific are your goals? Do exact numbers help or create pressure?

7. **Grandpa's wisdom:** Why do you think Grandpa's brief, simple responses ("Good. Do well.") felt meaningful to the narrator?

8. **The packing checklist:** Making a list before a big event (race, interview, trip) seems simple, but why does it reduce anxiety? Have you experienced this?
