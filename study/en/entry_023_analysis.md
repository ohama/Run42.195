# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Success and Failure

### "I made it on Thursday, so I figured today would be fine too."

**Original Korean:** "목요일에 성공했으니까 오늘도 괜찮을 거라고 생각했어"

**Analysis:**
- 과거 성공 경험을 기반으로 미래를 예측
- 자신감과 나이브한 기대 동시에 표현
- 경험 부족으로 인한 순진한 가정

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "I did it Thursday, so today should be easy." | "I made it on Thursday, so I figured today would be fine too." | "Given my success on Thursday, I anticipated similar results today." |
| "I succeeded once, so..." | "If I could do it Thursday, why not today?" | "The precedent established on Thursday suggested feasibility." |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: You finished your presentation already?
B: Yeah, I did one last week, so I figured this one would be easy too.
A: How'd it go?
B: Not as well as I expected. Public speaking's harder than I thought.
```

**Related Expressions:**
- "I thought I had it figured out." (해결됐다고 생각했어)
- "I assumed I could handle it." (할 수 있을 거라고 가정했어)
- "I was overconfident." (자신감이 넘쳤어)

---

### "It wasn't."

**Original Korean:** "아니었다"

**Nuance:** Stark, blunt contradiction; reality check

**This Simple Response Conveys:**
- 예상과 현실의 괴리
- 절망감/혼란
- 직설적인 다짐
- Narrative punch

**Variations for Different Tones:**
| Expression | Tone | Context |
|------------|------|---------|
| "It wasn't." | 직설적, 냉정 | 현실 직면 |
| "Turns out it wasn't." | 약간 유머 | 신경 덜 쓰는 듯 |
| "Apparently not." | 약간 자조 | 자신의 실수 인정 |
| "But I was wrong." | 반성적 | 자기 잘못 인정 |
| "Unfortunately not." | 공식적 | 격식체 설명 |

**English Storytelling Technique:**
Fragment sentences ("It wasn't.") create rhythm and emphasize disappointment. This is very characteristic of teen/young adult voice.

---

### "I failed. Failed to run the full 7km."

**Original Korean:** "실패했다. 7km를 풀로 뛰지 못했다."

**Repetition for Emphasis:**
- "I failed" (주제 제시)
- "Failed to run the full 7km" (구체적 내용)
- 중복으로 실패의 무게감 증가

**Failure Expressions Spectrum:**
| Expression | Intensity | Self-Blame |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| "I didn't make it." | ★☆☆ | 중립 |
| "I couldn't do it." | ★★☆ | 중립 |
| "I failed." | ★★★ | 자책 |
| "I gave up." | ★★★ | 약간의 자책 |
| "I completely messed up." | ★★★★ | 강한 자책 |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: How'd the marathon training go?
B: I failed. I only made it 4km before my legs gave out.
A: That's not failure. You've run 7km before!
B: I know, but I couldn't do it today.
```

## 1.3 Casual Conversational Responses

### "That happens."

**Original Korean:** "그런 일은 있는 거야"

**Meaning:**
- 흔한 일이다
- 그것이 정상이다
- 위로와 정규화

**When Minseo says this:**
- Doyun은 이 간단한 말로 위로받음
- 혼자가 아니라는 느낌
- 실패가 비정상이 아님을 깨달음

**Similar Reassuring Phrases:**
| Expression | Context |
|------------|---------|
| "That happens." | 일반적 위로 |
| "It happens to everyone." | 모두에게 일어남 |
| "No big deal." | 큰일 아니야 |
| "These things happen." | 이런 건 있는 거야 |
| "Don't sweat it." | 신경 쓰지 마 (informal) |
| "You can't always get it right." | 항상 잘 되지 않지 |

**Dialogue Practice:**
```
A: I totally bombed that interview.
B: That happens. You can try again.
A: But they probably hated me.
B: Don't think like that. Everyone has bad interviews.
A: Really?
B: Yeah. It's just part of the process.
```

---

**Original Korean:** "매번 다 하진 못해"

**Implication:**
- 일관성은 불가능
- 좋은 날 나쁜 날 모두 있음
- 이것이 인간

**Variations:**
| Expression | Nuance |
|------------|--------|
| "You can't do it every time." | 현실적 조언 |
| "You can't expect to succeed every time." | 더 명시적 |
| "It's impossible to do it perfectly every time." | 더 이상적 |
| "Nobody bats a thousand." | 누구도 항상 성공 못 함 (야구 용어) |
| "You're bound to fail sometimes." | 실패는 피할 수 없음 |

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS & ATHLETIC EXPRESSIONS (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running and Fatigue

### "My legs stopped."

**Original Korean:** "다리가 멎었다"

**Physical Sensation:**
- 의지와 무관하게 신체가 멈춤
- 통제 불가능한 피로
- 좌절감

**Variations for Body Giving Out:**
| Expression | Intensity | Context |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "My legs felt tired." | ★☆☆ | 단순 피로 |
| "My legs got heavy." | ★★☆ | 무거워짐 |
| "My legs stopped." | ★★★ | 완전히 멎음 |
| "My legs gave out." | ★★★ | 무너짐 |
| "I hit the wall." | ★★★★ | 극도의 피로 (runner's term) |
| "I couldn't go on." | ★★★★ | 더 이상 못함 |

**Related Athletic Expressions:**
- "My lungs were burning." (폐가 타는 듯)
- "My muscles were screaming." (근육이 비명을 지르는 듯)
- "I was completely gassed." (완전히 기진맥진)
- "I hit my limit." (한계 도달)

---

**Original Korean:** "속도를 늦췄지만 계속 갔어"

**Running Vocabulary:**
| Term | Meaning | Korean |
|------|---------|--------|
| pace | 속도/리듬 | 페이스 |
| slow down | 속도 줄이기 | 속도 늦추기 |
| keep going | 계속 나아가기 | 계속 가기 |
| push through | 밀어붙이기 | 버티기 |
| second wind | 새로운 힘 | 제2의 바람 |
| hit the wall | 극도의 피로 | 극한점 도달 |

**Pace Expressions:**
```

```

### "Ran 300 meters, walked 200."

**Original Korean:** "300미터 뛰었다가 200미터 걸었다"

**Breaking Down the Distance:**
- 구체적인 수치로 노력과 실패 모두 표현
- 포기하지 않았음을 보여줌
- 현실적 혼합 노력

**Similar Pacing Statements:**
```
"I ran for 5 minutes, walked for 2."
"I did a mile of running and half a mile of walking."
"I alternated between running and walking."
"I mixed running with walking intervals."
"I wasn't able to run the whole way."
```

**Original Korean:** "흐렸다. 습했다."

**Fragment Style for Impact:**
- 각 단어가 별개의 문장
- 진입장벽/부정적 조건 강조

|---------|--------|--------|

| 더움 | 빨리 지침 | "The heat exhausted me quickly." |
| 추움 | 근육 경직 | "The cold made my muscles stiff." |


# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: Hypothetical Doubt

**Structure:** "Did X or was it Y or was it Z?"

**From Text:**
```
"The weather? Was it the humidity?
My condition? Was I feeling off?
Fatigue? Still not recovered from Thursday?"
```

**This Pattern Expresses:**
- Multiple possible causes
- Uncertainty about the real reason
- Confusion and analysis

**Practice with Other Topics:**
```
"Was I too nervous? Did I not study enough? Was the test just harder?"
"Did I say something wrong? Was I not friendly enough? Did they not like me?"
"Was the food cold? Did I overcook it? Was the recipe wrong?"
```

**Variations:**
```
"I don't know. Maybe it was the weather?"
"Could it have been...?"
"I wonder if it was..."
"The problem might have been..."
```

---

## Pattern 2: Contradicting Expectations

**Structure:** "I thought [expectation]. But [reality]."

**From Text:**
```
"I thought since I succeeded Thursday, I'd keep succeeding.
But I didn't."
```

**This Pattern Expresses:**
- Naive assumptions
- Reality check
- Learning moment

**Practice Sentences:**
```
"I thought I was ready. But I wasn't."
"I thought it would be easy. But it was hard."
"I thought I had it figured out. But I didn't."
"I thought I'd never do it again. But maybe I will."
```

---

## Pattern 3: Self-Reassurance

**Structure:** "I tell myself [reframe]."

**From Text:**
```
"I succeeded once on Thursday. That's what matters.
If you can do it once, you can do it again."
```

**This Pattern Expresses:**
- Cognitive reframing
- Growth mindset
- Hope despite setback

**Practice with Your Own Examples:**
```
"I made one sale. That means I can make another."
"I finished one book. I can write another one."
"I had one good day. Good days can happen again."
"I lost 5 pounds. I can lose more."
```

---

## Pattern 4: Normalizing Failure

**Structure:** "Failure is [adjective]. Everyone [does same thing]."

**From Text:**
```
"Failure is normal."
"Everyone fails. Even pro athletes have bad days."
```

**This Pattern Expresses:**
- Universalizing struggle
- Reducing shame
- Perspective shift

**Examples in Different Contexts:**
```
"Mistakes are normal. Everyone makes them."
"Fear is natural. Everyone gets scared sometimes."
"Doubt is part of the process. Even experts question themselves."
"Struggling is okay. Nobody gets it right the first time."
```

---

## Pattern 5: "It's Okay to..."

**Structure:** "It's okay to [verb]. [Reason why]."

**From Text:**
```
"It's okay to fail. I can always try again."
```

**This Pattern Expresses:**
- Self-compassion
- Permission-giving
- Growth mentality

**Examples:**
```
"It's okay to ask for help. Nobody knows everything."
"It's okay to take a break. Rest is important."
"It's okay to change your mind. New information matters."
"It's okay to be tired. You've been working hard."
```

# PART 6: EXPRESSING EMOTIONS & GROWTH (감정 표현 & 성장)

## 6.3 Acceptance and Moving Forward

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE (연습)

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Sharing Failure with a Friend

```
A: How'd your run go this morning?
B: Not great, honestly. I failed. Couldn't make it the full 7km.
A: Again? But you did it last week.
B: I know. That's what made it worse. I thought I'd do it again.
A: What happened?
B: My legs just gave out at 4km. I had to walk the rest.
A: How do you feel about it?
B: Initially, I was really disappointed. But my friend Minseo said something that helped.
A: What?
B: That you can't do it every time. There are just good days and bad days.
A: That's true. Do you think you'll try again?
B: Yeah, next Thursday. It's okay to fail. I can always try again.
```

---

### Scenario 2: Parent Offering Support

```
A: Mom, I walked partway during the 7km.
B: During the run? What happened?
A: At 4km my legs just stopped. I was so frustrated.
B: Of course. You can't run 7km every single time.
A: Really? Even professional runners have bad days?
B: Absolutely. Even pros struggle. It's totally normal.
A: It bothered me that I couldn't do it today.
B: But you did it before. That means you can do it again.
A: You really think so?
B: I know so. Just try again next time. Don't push too hard.
A: Okay, thanks Mom.
B: You're doing great. One step at a time.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Reflecting on Growth with a Coach/Mentor

```
A: I wanted to talk about my run on Monday.
B: The one where you had to stop early?
A: Yeah. I felt like such a failure.
B: Why? You were running 7km just days before.
A: Because I thought once you can do something, you can always do it.
B: Is that what you believe now?
A: No. I learned that wasn't true. Some days are harder than others.
B: That's called experience. That's how you grow.
A: I read online that even pro athletes have bad days.
B: Exactly. Failure is normal. What matters is not giving up.
A: So I should try again?
B: Absolutely. Succeeding once means you can succeed again.
A: Thank you. I needed to hear that.
```
