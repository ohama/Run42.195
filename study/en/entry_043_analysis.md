# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Morning Routine Expressions

### "Wake up at 5. Leave by 5:15. Home by 6:30. Shower. Breakfast."

**Original Korean:** "5시에 깨어나다. 5시 15분에 출발. 6시 30분에 집에 도착. 샤워. 아침밥."

**Analysis:**
- Fragment sentences for rhythm and routine emphasis
- Lists convey efficiency and habit formation
- Creates diary-like, stream-of-consciousness effect

**Variations by Formality:**
| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "Wake up at 5. Leave by 5:15." | "I wake up at 5 and leave by 5:15." | "My morning routine commences at 5 AM." |
| "Home by 6:30." | "I'm back home by 6:30." | "I arrive home approximately at 6:30 AM." |
| "Shower. Breakfast." | "Then I shower and eat breakfast." | "Subsequently, I shower and consume breakfast." |

**Grammar Point:** Fragment sentences (불완전한 문장)
- Used for emphasis and natural speech rhythm
- Conveys habit and routine efficiency
- Creates urgency and immediacy

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: What's your summer routine like?
B: Pretty simple. Wake up at 5. Run. Shower. Breakfast. Done.
A: Every day?
B: Yeah, even weekends. It's my routine now.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I wake up at 5 and I leave by 5:15 and I shower and I have breakfast." (too formal, loses rhythm)
✅ "Wake up at 5. Leave by 5:15. Shower. Breakfast." (natural diary style)

### "My morning running routine has settled in."

**Original Korean:** "아침 달리기 루틴이 정착되었다"

**"Settle in" Meaning:** become established as habit

**Structure:** Subject + has + settled in / has settled into + noun

**Examples with "settle in":**
| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| "My routine has settled in." | 루틴이 정착되었다 |
| "The new schedule has settled in." | 새로운 일정에 적응했다 |
| "Summer life has finally settled in." | 여름 생활이 정착되었다 |
| "The team has settled into their roles." | 팀이 역할에 적응했다 |

**Related Expressions:**
- "I've gotten into a routine." (루틴을 만들었다)
- "My habit is fully formed now." (습관이 완전히 형성됐어)
- "I've established a pattern." (패턴을 확립했다)

### "That's my summer break morning routine."

**Original Korean:** "그게 내 여름방학 아침 루틴이야"

**"That's my..." Pattern:** Personal definition/summary

**Structure:** That's + my + [time period/situation] + [noun/noun phrase]

**Examples:**
| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| "That's my summer routine." | 그게 내 여름 루틴이야 |
| "That's my daily commute." | 그게 내 일일 통근길이야 |
| "That's my typical Tuesday." | 그게 내 전형적인 화요일이야 |
| "That's my perfect morning." | 그게 내 이상적인 아침이야 |

## 1.2 Observations & Reactions

### "It's August."

**Original Korean:** "8월이다"

**Analysis:** Single-word sentence for impact and seasonal transition

**When to Use Single-Word Sentences:**
- Seasonal transitions
- Emotional realization
- Time passage awareness
- Shock or surprise

**Examples in Context:**
```
"It's August." (시간이 흘렀다는 깨달음)
"It's summer." (계절 표현)
"It's beautiful." (감탄)
```

---

### "Clear skies. The sunflowers have bloomed."

**Original Korean:** "맑은 하늘. 해바라기가 피었다"

**Fragment Structure for Scene Setting:**
- Creates visual snapshot
- Conveys diary observation
- Emphasizes what narrator notices

**Scene-Setting with Fragments:**
| Fragment | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| "Clear skies." | 날씨 상태 강조 |
| "The sunflowers have bloomed." | 계절 변화 인식 |
| "Yellow everywhere." | 색상 임팩트 |
| "Cicadas singing." | 음향 효과 |

---

### "Not the way to school—I go the opposite direction."

**Original Korean:** "학교로 가는 길이 아니라 그 반대 방향으로 간다"

**Dash Usage:** Clarification/Correction

**When Dashes Are Used:**
- After stating something wrong
- To add unexpected information
- To emphasize a point

**Examples:**
```
"I run north—away from school."
"Same route—but backward."
"Different path—better scenery."
```

---

### "They're pretty."

**Original Korean:** "예쁘다"

**Asterisks for Internal Thoughts:**
- Indicates personal reflection
- Shows narrator's emotional response
- Creates intimacy with reader

**When to Use Asterisks for Thoughts:**
```
*I love this moment.*
*This is what I live for.*
*This is peace.*
```

## 1.3 Casual Reactions to Experiences

### "This isn't bad either."

**Original Korean:** "이것도 나쁘지 않다"

**Grammar Point:** "isn't bad" as positive understatement

**Meaning Spectrum:**
| Expression | Meaning | Enthusiasm |
|------------|---------|------------|
| "It's terrible." | 끔찍해 | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| "It's not great." | 별로야 | ★★☆☆☆ |
| "It's okay." | 괜찮아 | ★★★☆☆ |
| "It's nice." | 좋아 | ★★★★☆ |
| "It's not bad either." | 꽤 좋아 (under상황 억제) | ★★★★☆ |
| "It's wonderful." | 멋져 | ★★★★★ |

**Context in Entry:**
The narrator realizes that listening to cicadas instead of music is actually enjoyable—a moment of discovering beauty in natural sounds.

**Variations:**
- "I could get used to this." (이거 좋을 수도)
- "Actually, this is kind of nice." (실은 꽤 좋네)
- "I didn't expect to like it." (좋아할 줄 몰랐는데)

---

### "I should stop by and say hello sometime."

**Original Korean:** "언젠가 들러서 인사해야겠다"

**"Should" for Future Intention:**
- Softer than "must" or "have to"
- Indicates resolve but not urgency
- Suggests vague future timing

**"Stop by" Phrasal Verb:**
- Meaning: 잠깐 들르다 (brief visit)
- Casual and friendly
- Often used for unexpected/informal visits

**Examples:**
```
"I should stop by Grandpa's field next week."
"Let me stop by your house this weekend."
"I'll stop by when I have time."
```

# PART 2: PHYSICAL SENSATIONS & RUNNING (신체 감각 표현)

## 2.1 Running Sensations

### "Sweat poured off me like rain."

**Original Korean:** "땀이 빗처럼 쏟아졌다"

**Simile:** "like rain"
- Vivid comparison
- Emphasizes quantity/intensity
- Sensory imagery

**Intensity Spectrum for Sweating:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "I was a little sweaty." | ★☆☆☆☆ | 약간 땀났어 |
| "I was sweating." | ★★☆☆☆ | 땀을 흘렸어 |
| "Sweat dripped down me." | ★★★☆☆ | 땀이 흘러내렸어 |
| "Sweat poured off me." | ★★★★☆ | 땀이 쏟아졌어 |
| "Sweat poured off me like rain." | ★★★★★ | 빗처럼 땀이 쏟아졌어 |
| "I was drenched in sweat." | ★★★★★ | 땀에 흠뻑 젖었어 |

**Similar Vivid Comparisons:**
- "Sweat ran down like waterfalls."
- "I was soaking wet."
- "Sweat completely drenched me."

---

### "I felt refreshed."

**Original Korean:** "상쾌했다"

**Post-Exercise Feelings:**

| Expression | Timing | Meaning |
|------------|--------|---------|
| "I'm exhausted." | immediately after | 지쳤어 |
| "I'm out of breath." | right after | 숨이 차 |
| "I'm tired but good." | few minutes later | 피곤하지만 좋아 |
| "I felt refreshed." | after shower | 상쾌해 |
| "I feel energized." | later | 활기 있어 |

**"Refreshed" Usage:**
- After physical activity + shower
- Mental and physical renewal
- Positive energy boost

**Antonyms:**
- exhausted (지친)
- drained (기진맥진한)
- worn out (피곤한)

---

**Original Korean:** "이미 뭔가 해낸 것 같은 기분이었다"

**"Accomplish" + "Something":**

**Grammar Point:** "It felt like..." for emotional impression

**Examples:**

# PART 3: DESCRIBING NATURE & SEASONAL CHANGES (자연 묘사)

## 3.1 Flora Observations

### "Sunflowers had bloomed. Someone must have planted them by the roadside."

**Original Korean:** "해바라기가 피었다. 누군가 길가에 심은 것 같았다"

**"Bloom" Vocabulary:**

| Stage | Verb | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 싹 나다 | sprout | 새싹 |
| 자라다 | grow | 성장 |
| 꽃피다 | bloom / flower | 꽃이 핀다 |
| 만개 | be in full bloom | 만개 상태 |
| 시들다 | wilt | 시들기 시작 |
| 떨어지다 | fall / drop | 꽃잎 떨어짐 |

**Flower Descriptions:**
- Yellow sunflowers = 노란 해바라기
- Big ones = 큰 것들
- They were lifting their heads = 고개를 들다

### "They were lifting their heads toward the sun. Just like their name suggests."

**Original Korean:** "고개를 해에 향해 들고 있었다. 이름 그대로다"

**Personification:** Giving human qualities to sunflowers

**"Just like their name suggests":**
- Sunflower = flower that follows the sun
- Practical observation + metaphor
- Shows understanding of nature

**Personification Examples:**
```
"The trees were dancing in the wind."
"The flowers were reaching for the sky."
"The mountains were watching over the valley."
"The river was singing through the valley."
```

---

### "Yellow sunflowers. About five big ones."

**Original Korean:** "노란 해바라기. 큰 것 다섯 개쯤"

**Fragment Description:**
- Immediate sensory detail
- Creates visual scene
- Mimics diary entry style

## 3.2 Fauna & Insect Sounds

### "The cicadas were deafening."

**Original Korean:** "매미 소리가 귀에 쩌렁쩌렁했다"

**"Deafening" = extremely loud**

**Loudness Spectrum:**

| Expression | Decibels | Context |
|------------|----------|---------|
| "I couldn't hear myself." | 90+ dB | Very loud |
| "It was deafening." | 110+ dB | Extremely loud |
| "It was loud." | 70-90 dB | Normal loud |
| "It was noisy." | 60-70 dB | Background noise |
| "It was quiet." | 40-60 dB | Peaceful |
| "It was silent." | 0-30 dB | Complete silence |

**Sound Verbs for Cicadas:**
- buzzing (윙윙거리다)
- chirping (울다)
- singing (울다)
- humming (윙윙거리다)

---

### "Meem-meem-meem-meem."

**Original Korean:** "매미 우는 소리 onomatopoeia"

**Onomatopoeia (의성어):**
- Represents actual sound
- Helps non-native speakers hear the sound
- Adds authenticity to nature description

**Common Insect Sounds:**
| Insect | Sound | Representation |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Cicada | High-pitched buzz | "meem-meem" / "bzzzzzz" |
| Cricket | Chirp | "chirp-chirp" |
| Bee | Buzz | "bzzzz" |
| Frog | Croak | "ribbit-ribbit" |

---

### "It was barely dawn and they were already singing."

**Original Korean:** "아직 새벽인데 이미 울고 있었다"

**"Barely dawn":**
- Just starting to get light
- Very early morning
- Before most people wake up

**Time Expressions for Early Morning:**

| Expression | Time | Darkness |
|------------|------|----------|
| "Barely dawn" | 5:30 | Still mostly dark |
| "Early morning" | 6:00 | Getting light |
| "First light" | 6:15 | Sky orange/yellow |
| "Sunrise" | 6:30 | Sun appears |
| "Morning" | 7:00 | Full daylight |

---

### "It felt like every tree had cicadas clinging to it."

**Original Korean:** "모든 나무마다 매미가 붙어있는 것 같았다"

**Hyperbole for Effect:**
- Exaggeration for emphasis
- Conveys overwhelming number/sound
- Natural way to express abundance

**Examples of Hyperbole:**
```
"There were a million people." (엄청 많았다)
"I've told you a million times." (계속 말했어)
"I'm so tired I could sleep for a week." (엄청 피곤해)
```

---

### "The sound was so loud that instead of music, I listened to the cicadas as I ran."

**Original Korean:** "소리가 너무 커서 음악 대신 매미 소리를 들었다"

**"Instead of" Pattern:**
- Substitution/choice
- Shows preference or necessity
- Compares two options

**Examples:**
```
"Instead of running at night, I run in the morning."
"Instead of earphones, I listened to nature."
"Instead of driving, I took the bus."
```

# PART 4: PHRASAL VERBS & ACTION VERBS (구동사)

## 4.1 Morning/Exercise Phrasal Verbs

### "Wake up at 5"

**Phrasal Verb: Wake up** (깨다/일어나다)

**Wake up variations:**
| Expression | Meaning | Usage |
|------------|---------|-------|
| "Wake up" | 깨다 | 수동적 (자동으로 깬다) |
| "Wake up at 5" | 5시에 깨다 | 시간 지정 |
| "Wake up early" | 일찍 일어나다 | 일반적 |
| "Wake up on your own" | 알아서 깨다 | 자발적 |

---

### "Leave by 5:15"

**Phrasal Verb: Leave** (떠나다/나가다)

**"Leave by [time]":**
- Departure deadline
- Must depart before this time
- Different from "leave at" (정확히 ~시에 나가다)

**Examples:**
```
"I leave by 5:15 every morning." (5시 15분까지는 나가야 해)
"We need to leave by noon." (정오까지는 떠나야 해)
"Leave by sunset." (해지기 전에 나가)
```

---

### "Home by 6:30"

**Phrasal Verb: Home** (집에 가다/귀가하다)

**Note:** "Home" is an adverb/noun, not typically a phrasal verb
- "be home by [time]" = [시간]까지 집에 있다
- "get home by [time]" = [시간]까지 집에 도착하다

**Examples:**
```
"I'm home by 6:30." (6시 30분까지 집에 있다)
"I get home at 6:30." (6시 30분에 집에 도착한다)
"We'll be home before dinner." (저녁 전에 집에 도착할 거야)
```

---

### "Ran under the streetlights"

**Verb + Preposition: "Run under"** (...아래로 달리다)

**Directional Prepositions with "Run":**
| Preposition | Direction | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| through | 통과해서 | "run through the park" |
| along | 따라 | "run along the road" |
| under | 아래 | "run under the lights" |
| past | 지나서 | "run past the house" |
| toward | 향해 | "run toward the sun" |
| around | 주위를 | "run around the loop" |

---

### "Stopped running for a moment"

**Phrasal Verb: Stop (+ -ing)** (멈추다)

**Usage:**
- "Stop running" = 달리기를 멈추다
- Different from "stop to run" (멈춰서 달리기 시작하다)

**Examples:**
```
"I stopped running to look at the flowers." (꽃을 보기 위해 멈쳤다)
"Stop talking." (말 멈춰)
"I stopped to take a photo." (사진 찍으려고 멈쳤다)
```

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Third day of summer break..."

**Structure:** Ordinal number + time period + [observation/fact]

**Uses:** Marking progression, routine formation

**Examples:**
```
"First day of summer: I'm excited."
"Third week of school: I'm settled in."
"Tenth time running: It's getting easier."
"Hundredth morning: My routine is solid."
```

---

## Pattern 2: "My route's changed, so I don't run through [place] anymore, but I could see it from a distance."

**Structure:** Cause + effect + unexpected detail

**Grammar:** Main clause + adverbial clause + contrast

**Examples:**
```
"My life changed, so I'm not sad anymore, but I still remember."
"The weather got better, so I don't need my jacket, but it's still in my bag."
"I moved, so I don't see my friends often, but we text all the time."
```

---

## Pattern 3: "I should stop by and say hello sometime."

**Structure:** "Should + [action] + sometime"**

**Meaning:** Vague future intention

**Examples:**
```
"I should call my grandmother sometime."
"I should visit that café sometime."
"I should try that new trail sometime."
```

---

## Pattern 4: "[Object] + Subject + [verb]"

**Structure:** Reordering for emphasis/observation

**From Text:** "Sunflowers had bloomed."
- Expected: "The sunflowers had bloomed."
- Stated: "Sunflowers had bloomed."
- Effect: General observation vs. specific flowers

**Examples:**
```
"Flowers were everywhere." (vs. "The flowers were everywhere.")
"Cicadas were singing." (vs. "The cicadas were singing.")
"People were laughing." (vs. "The people were laughing.")
```

---

## Pattern 5: "[Observation]. [Observation]. [Observation]."

**Structure:** Fragment sentences for scene-setting

**Uses:**
- Diary entry style
- Building visual scene
- Rapid impressions

**Examples:**
```
"Blue sky. Green grass. Warm sun." (아름다운 공원)
"Clear water. Rocky beach. Cool breeze." (상쾌한 해변)
"Crowded street. Bright lights. Loud voices." (분주한 도시)
```

---

## Pattern 6: "Since I started [activity], I've [result]"

**Structure:** Cause-and-effect showing transformation

**Examples:**
```
"Since I started running, I've become aware of the seasons."
"Since I started studying, I've improved my grades."
"Since I started waking up early, I've felt healthier."
```

---

## Pattern 7: "In [season], [observation]. In [season], [observation]."

**Structure:** Seasonal comparison list

**Uses:** Showing awareness of yearly cycle

**From Text:**
```
"In spring, rapeseed flowers and cherry blossoms.
In summer, hydrangeas and sunflowers."
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Talking About Summer Routine

```
A: What are you doing for summer break?
B: I've gotten into running. Wake up at 5, run 8km, home by 6:30.
A: Every day? That's intense!
B: Yeah, but I actually like it. The mornings are beautiful.
A: What's so special about them?
B: The sunflowers, the cicadas, the sunrise. Things I never noticed before.
A: That sounds peaceful. I'm usually sleeping at that time!
B: You should try it sometime. It's pretty nice.
```

---

### Scenario 2: Discussing Plans with a Friend

```
A: Hey, what are you up to today?
B: Nothing lol, it's too hot so I'm just staying home.
A: I ran 8km this morning.
B: You're insane lol. I still can't wake up before 7.
A: Let's run Saturday. Evening.
B: Okay, sounds good. What time?
A: Around 5? Before it gets too hot.
B: Sure, I'm in.
```

---

### Scenario 3: Noticing Seasonal Changes

```
A: Have you noticed it's getting warmer?
B: Yeah, definitely. The flowers are blooming everywhere.
A: What flowers?
B: Sunflowers! They're pretty. And the cicadas are so loud.
A: Cicadas? In the city?
B: Yeah, you'd be surprised. When you're running in the morning, you notice things.
A: Like what?
B: Like how the sky changes color. Orange, then yellow, then blue.
```

## 10.4 Discussion Questions
