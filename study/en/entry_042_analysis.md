# PART 1: EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (일상 표현)

## 1.1 Planning & Goal-Setting Expressions

### "I made a training plan for the break."

**Original Korean:** "방학 동안 훈련 계획을 짰어"

**Analysis:**
- 미래 계획을 명확하게 표현하는 표현
- 더 일반적인 표현: "I came up with a training plan" / "I created a training plan"
- 보다 캐주얼: "I planned out my training for break"

**Variations by Formality:**

| Casual | Standard | Formal |
|--------|----------|--------|
| "I planned out my training" | "I made a training plan" | "I have formulated a training schedule" |
| "I've got a plan" | "I created a plan" | "I have developed a strategic plan" |
| "I'm gonna work out a lot" | "I'm planning to run a lot" | "I intend to engage in consistent training" |

**Practice Dialogue:**
```
A: So what are you doing over the break?
B: I made a training plan for the summer.
A: Really? What does it involve?
B: Running six days a week, gradually building up to 10 kilometers.
A: That's ambitious! Do you think you can stick to it?
B: I'm going to try. I've been building up to this all year.
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ "I made a training plan for break." (약간 어색)
✅ "I made a training plan for the break." (더 자연스러움)

### "I want to push it to 10km by the end of summer."

**Original Korean:** "방학이 끝나기 전에 10km까지 올리고 싶어"

**Grammar Point:** Push + object + to + target

**Structure:**
```
Push + [something] + to + [target]
```

**More Examples:**

| Sentence | Domain |
|----------|--------|
| "I want to push my sales to $100k" | 비즈니스 |
| "I'm pushing myself to run faster" | 운동 |
| "We're pushing the deadline to next month" | 일정 |
| "Can we push the meeting to 3pm?" | 회의 |

**Alternative Structures:**
- "I want to increase it to 10km by the end of summer."
- "My goal is to get up to 10km by late August."
- "I'm aiming for 10km before summer ends."

**Related Fitness Expressions:**
- "build up to" (조금씩 늘려가다): "I built up to 5km over three months."
- "work up to" (노력해서 얻다): "I'm working up to a marathon."
- "improve from" (에서 향상되다): "I've improved from 5km to 8km."

**Original Korean:** "넌 미쳤어 lol"

## 1.3 Expressing Confidence

### "I can do this too. I've already come 8km."

**Original Korean:** "나도 할 수 있을 것 같아. 이미 8km까지 왔는데"

**"I can do this" Pattern:** 자신감 표현

**Confidence Spectrum:**

| Expression | Certainty | Tone |
|------------|-----------|------|
| "I might be able to do it." | 30% | Uncertain |
| "I think I can do it." | 50% | Hopeful |
| "I can do this." | 80% | Confident |
| "I've got this." | 85% | Very confident |
| "I can definitely do it." | 90% | Assured |
| "No question, I can do it." | 100% | Certain |

**Building Confidence Pattern:**
```
[Negative thought] → [Evidence of progress] → [Renewed confidence]
Example: "I worried I couldn't do it. But I've already run 8km. So I can do this."
```

**Supporting Statements:**
- "Just a little more." (조금만 더)
- "I've come this far." (여기까지 왔잖아)
- "If I could do that, I can do this." (그걸 할 수 있으면 이것도 할 수 있어)
- "One step at a time." (한 걸음씩)

## 2.2 Heat & Temperature Expressions

### "Clear skies. Scorching hot."

**Original Korean:** "맑은 하늘. 해가 엄청 뜨거워"

**Fragment Writing for Impact:** 완전한 문장 대신 명사만 사용

**Why This Works in English:**
- 시각적 강임팩트 (visual impact)
- 빠른 리듬감 (quick pace)
- 직접적인 표현 (immediacy)
- 감정 전달 (emotional punch)

**Temperature Expressions Spectrum:**

| Expression | Intensity | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| "It's warm." | ★☆☆ | 따뜻하다 |
| "It's hot." | ★★☆ | 덥다 |
| "It's very hot." | ★★★ | 매우 덥다 |
| "It's scorching." | ★★★★ | 살이 타는 듯 덥다 |
| "It's scorching hot." | ★★★★★ | 엄청 뜨겁다 |
| "The sun is beating down." | ★★★★★ | 햇볕이 내리쬐다 |

**Weather Fragment Examples:**
```
"Sunny day." (맑은 날씨)
"Clear skies." (맑은 하늘)
"Burning heat." (뜨거운 열)
"Intense sun." (강한 햇빛)
"Sweltering afternoon." (지글지글 끓는 오후)
```

**Complete vs. Fragment Writing:**

| Fragment | Complete Sentence |
|----------|-------------------|
| "Clear skies. Scorching hot." | "The skies were clear and the sun was scorching hot." |
| "No school. No need to run." | "There was no school, and I didn't need to run to get there." |
| "8km. 48 minutes." | "I ran 8 kilometers in 48 minutes." |

# PART 3: DESCRIBING RUNNING ACHIEVEMENT & GOALS (달리기 성취와 목표 묘사)

## 3.1 Expressing Numerical Achievement

### "8km. 48 minutes."

**Original Korean:** "8km. 48분"

**Running Metrics & Pace Calculation:**

| Term | Korean | How to Calculate |
|------|--------|------------------|
| "distance" | 거리 | 달린 km 수 |
| "time" | 시간 | 걸린 시간 |
| "pace" | 페이스 | 거리 ÷ 시간 |
| "speed" | 속도 | km/h로 표현 |

**Pace Calculation Examples:**
```
8km in 48 minutes = 10 minutes per kilometer (10min/km)
= 48 minutes ÷ 8 km = 6 km/h
```

**How to Express Running Performance:**

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| "I ran [distance] in [time]" | "I ran 8km in 48 minutes." |
| "My pace was [time] per kilometer" | "My pace was 10 minutes per kilometer." |
| "I covered [distance] in [time]" | "I covered 8 kilometers in 48 minutes." |
| "I did [distance] at a [pace] pace" | "I did 8km at a 10min/km pace." |

**Common Running Metrics in English:**
- "5K run" (5km 달리기) - 약 30분
- "10K run" (10km 달리기) - 약 50-60분
- "half marathon" (하프 마라톤) - 21.195km
- "marathon" (마라톤) - 42.195km
- "ultramarathon" (울트라 마라톤) - 50km 이상

# PART 5: CONVERSATIONAL PATTERNS (회화 패턴)

## Pattern 1: "Running every day. Six days a week."

**Structure:** [statement]. [Clarification/detail].

**Fragment Pattern for Emphasis:**
```
Broad claim → Specific detail → Reality check
"Running every day" → "Six days a week" (수정/명확화)
```

**More Examples:**

| Broad | Detail | Full |
|-------|--------|------|
| "Exercising daily" | "Six days a week" | "I'm exercising daily—well, six days a week." |
| "Working all the time" | "Twelve hours a day" | "I work all the time. Twelve hours a day." |
| "Always eating healthy" | "Trying to, anyway" | "Always eating healthy. Trying to, anyway." |

**Contradictions That Clarify:**
```
Pattern: [Claim] [Contradiction/Reality]
"Running nonstop" "Well, I take one day off"
"Never stopping" "Except Sundays"
"Training intensely" "But with rest days"
```

---

## Pattern 2: "It's going to be tough. In this summer heat."

**Structure:** [Prediction statement]. [Reason/context]

**Expressing Difficulty with Context:**

| Statement | Context | Full |
|-----------|---------|------|
| "It's going to be tough" | "In summer heat" | "It's going to be tough. In this summer heat." |
| "It will be challenging" | "With work" | "It will be challenging. With work every day." |
| "This is hard" | "At my age" | "This is hard. At my age." |

**"Going to be" Pattern:**

| Structure | Meaning | Tense |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| "It's going to be tough" | 앞으로 힘들 것 | near future |
| "It will be tough" | 힘들 것이다 | future prediction |
| "It'll be tough" | 힘들 거야 (casual) | future |
| "It's going to get tough" | 더 힘들어질 것 | escalating future |

---

## Pattern 3: "What will I be doing three months from now? 10km? Or more?"

**Structure:** [Rhetorical question] + [Possible answers]?

**Self-Directed Questioning Pattern:**

| Question | Answer Options | Use |
|----------|-----------------|-----|
| "What will I be doing?" | "X? Or Y?" | 미래 예측 |
| "Where will I be?" | "Here? Or somewhere else?" | 미래 위치 |
| "How far can I get?" | "10km? 15km? More?" | 목표 설정 |

**Progressive Speculation:**
```
Direct question → Multiple possibilities → Open ending
"What will I be doing in three months?"
"10km? Or more?"
(희망과 불확실성을 모두 표현)
```

**Similar Patterns:**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Escalation | "Will I run 10km? 15km? Maybe 20km?" |
| Negation | "Will I still be running? Or will I quit?" |
| Time progression | "Now? Later? Eventually?" |
| Possibility | "Could it happen? Will it?" |

## Pattern 4: "In March, I missed the bus and started walking. Even 1km was hard. Now I run 8km."

**Structure:** [Past difficulty] → [Current achievement] (contrast for motivation)

**Progress Narrative:**

| Time | Distance | Difficulty |
|------|----------|------------|
| March (beginning) | 1km (walking) | 매우 힘듦 |
| Now (current) | 8km (running) | 괜찮음 |
| Goal (future) | 10km (running) | 가능할 것 |

**Personal Progress Stories - English Structure:**

```
Past: "In March, I couldn't even walk 1km."
Present: "Now I run 8km."
Future: "Maybe soon I'll run 10km."
```

**Emphasis Technique - "Even":**

| With "Even" | Meaning | Impact |
|------------|---------|--------|
| "Even 1km was hard" | 1km도 힘들었어 | 과거의 약함 강조 |
| "I can even run 8km now" | 이제 8km도 달릴 수 있어 | 발전 강조 |
| "Even a marathon someday" | 언젠가 마라톤도 | 야망 강조 |

## 7.1 Fragment Sentences for Impact

### "No school. No need to run the 7km to get there. But I run anyway. Because I want to."

**Why Fragments Work:**

| Fragment Type | Effect | Example |
|---------------|--------|---------|
| Single noun | Emphasis | "Heat" / "Exhaustion" |
| Incomplete clause | Speed | "No school" |
| "Because" clause | Motivation | "Because I want to" |
| Negation | Contrast | "But I run anyway" |

**Fragment Patterns:**

| Pattern | Korean | English |
|---------|--------|---------|
| "X. Y." | 둘 다 없어. 둘 다 필요 없어. | "No school. No need to run." |
| "X. But Y." | 없지만 어쨌든 해. | "But I run anyway." |
| "Because X." | 왜냐하면 ~하고 싶으니까. | "Because I want to." |

**Stylistic Technique - "But Anyway":**
```
Typical expectation: "No school, so I could rest."
Contrasting reality: "But I run anyway."
(Reversal of expected behavior)
```

# PART 10: EXTENDED PRACTICE

## 10.1 Dialogue Practice

### Scenario 1: Discussing Summer Training Plans

```
A: What are you doing over the summer break?
B: I made a training plan for running.
A: Really? What's your goal?
B: I want to push my distance to 10 kilometers by the end of break.
A: That's ambitious! How far can you run now?
B: 8 kilometers. I ran it this morning in 48 minutes.
A: In this heat? That's crazy!
B: Yeah, but I want to try. It's tough, but if I keep it up, I can get to 10.
A: How often do you run?
B: Six days a week. I take one day off for recovery.
A: Your dedication is impressive. Stick with it!
B: Thanks for the encouragement. That really helps.
```

### Scenario 2: Sharing Progress with a Friend

```
A: Hey, I heard you're training hard for a marathon.
B: Not quite a marathon yet, but I'm working toward it.
A: What's your current distance?
B: 8 kilometers. I started at just 1 kilometer back in March.
A: That's huge progress! From 1 to 8 in a few months.
B: I know, right? Even 1km was hard back then. Now I want to reach 10.
A: By when?
B: Hopefully by the end of summer. And eventually... a full marathon.
A: 42 kilometers? That's impressive.
B: Yeah, still far. So far. But someday.
A: I believe you can do it. You've already done so well.
B: Thanks! Reading success stories online made me feel confident too.
```

### Scenario 3: Morning Motivation

```
A: You're going running at 5:15 in the morning?
B: Yeah, I wake up at 5, and leave at 5:15. Six days a week.
A: That's dedication! Don't you want to sleep in during break?
B: I could, but I want to accomplish my goal first.
A: What goal?
B: Run 10 kilometers nonstop by the end of summer.
A: Is it hard?
B: Yeah, honestly, it's going to be tough. In this summer heat.
A: So why keep doing it?
B: Because I want to try. And I've already come this far.
A: That's a good mindset. I admire that.
B: Thanks. Honestly, I'm a little nervous some days, but the progress keeps me going.
```
