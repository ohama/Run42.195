# A Jeju Boy's Marathon Journey

A 14-year-old boy transforms from a motion-sick bus commuter to a full marathon finisher over one year.

## About This Story

Doyun Kim is a first-year middle school student living in Jeju Island, Korea. Every day, he battles motion sickness on a packed 40-minute bus ride to school. One day, he misses the bus and has to walk—and discovers something unexpected.

Through 104 diary entries spanning March 2024 to March 2025, follow Doyun's journey from walking to running, from his first 5K to his first 10K race, from a half marathon to the ultimate challenge: 42.195 kilometers.

## Format

- **Genre**: Young Adult Coming-of-Age (Diary Format)
- **Length**: 104 entries (~108,000 words)
- **Timeline**: March 2024 – March 2025
- **POV**: First person (Doyun Kim)

## Main Characters

- **Doyun Kim** (14) - An ordinary student who hated exercise
- **Minseo Kang** - Doyun's friend who becomes his running partner
- **Grandfather Ko** - A mandarin farmer, quiet supporter
- **Mom** - Worried but supportive mother
- **Mr. Park** - PE teacher, marathon advisor

## Chapter Guide

### March - Beginning (Entries 1-8)
First day of middle school—Doyun battles motion sickness on a packed bus. One day he misses it and has no choice but to walk. To his surprise, it feels refreshing. He meets Grandfather at the tangerine orchard for the first time and decides to keep walking despite his mom's worries.

### April - Building Habits (Entries 9-17)
Walking twice a week becomes routine. He notices birdsong and wind—sounds he never heard on the bus. Grandfather gives him tangerines. In PE class, long-distance running feels easier than before. Even Mom notices: "Your complexion looks better."

### May - Starting to Run (Entries 18-25)
He oversleeps and runs part of the way—and feels a strange thrill. "Running was this good?" He tries running the full 7km but fails. School sneakers have their limits. Grandfather encourages him: "You've been running hard lately. Good job."

### June - Running for Real (Entries 26-33)
He finally buys his first running shoes. His feet feel like they're flying. He runs in the rain during monsoon season and completes his first non-stop 5km. The PE teacher takes interest. Minseo asks, "Want to run together?"

### July - Trials (Entries 34-42)
Heat wave and typhoon. Days without running make his body restless. "Why do I run?" His first slump arrives. He tries taking the bus again but only feels suffocated. He starts running again. Summer break means he can run every day.

### August - Growth (Entries 43-50)
Morning runs every day during break. 7km becomes easy. He challenges 10km and succeeds. He discovers the joy of running with Minseo. He hears about an October marathon and signs up for the 10km division. Second semester begins. Friends say, "You've changed."

### September - Goals (Entries 51-59)
D-30 to the race. Mr. Park teaches him training methods: long runs, intervals, tempo runs. Structured training begins. He enjoys the perfect running weather of autumn. Dad shows interest for the first time. A week before the race, he starts tapering.

### October - First Race (Entries 60-68)
Nervous and excited, he stands at the 10km starting line. **48 minutes 47 seconds**—first race complete. The emotion of crossing the finish line. He receives his finisher's medal and sets a new goal: the half marathon, 21km. Training continues at 15km, 17km.

### November - Half Marathon (Entries 69-76)
Countdown from D-20. Minseo and family promise to cheer. A nervous race week. Finally, half marathon day. At 18km, he wants to quit but keeps going. **21km, 2 hours 24 minutes 38 seconds complete.** And a new dream emerges: the full marathon.

### December - New Resolution (Entries 77-85)
He declares his goal: 42.195km. Mr. Park creates a 12-week training plan. Winter running begins. White breath in the cold air, 15km, 17km long runs. The last run of 2024 with Minseo, reflecting on the year.

### January - Full Marathon Prep (Entries 86-94)
First run of the new year. Now a tenth grader, he seriously increases distance. 20km, 25km, and **30km long run success**. He wanted to quit at 27km but finished. Final 25km long run complete, then tapering begins.

### February - Tapering (Entries 95-102)
Countdown to race day. Reducing distance, letting the body rest. Rapeseed flowers start blooming. He remembers one year ago—motion-sick me is now preparing for a full marathon. Race week, final run. Mr. Park says: "When you finish, your life will change."

### March - The Finish Line (Entries 103-104)
The day before the race, heart pounding. A year of preparation. Finally, the 42.195km starting line. The wall at 30km, the agony at 37km. "Should I stop?" He thought it a thousand times but never stopped. One step, one step. And then—the finish line. **4 hours 42 minutes 18 seconds, full marathon complete.** In spring with rapeseed flowers in full bloom, the boy who got motion-sick became a marathoner.
