# Things I Never Could Have Seen from the Bus

## A Short Story for Grammar Units 25–27
### *When/If* clauses, *Can/Could/Be able to*, *Could have done*

## Part 1: March — The Boy Who Couldn't 🚌
*Grammar focus: couldn't (과거 일반 불능 — Unit 26)*

March 2024. My name is Doyun, and there are a lot of things I can't do.

**I can't breathe** on the crowded bus. **I can't move my legs** when the bus lurches. **I can barely stand it.**

In elementary school, **I couldn't even run three laps** around the track. **I couldn't breathe** during PE class. The athletic kids felt like people from another world. **I couldn't even imagine** being like them.

This morning on the bus, **I could see people crammed inside, standing wall to wall.** Minseo said it like a joke, but **I could tell he was worried** about me.

Then I missed the bus. And I walked. All 7 kilometers.

That evening, I sat in my room and thought:

"Mom, **can I walk to school sometimes?**" (허락)

She looked worried. "It's 7 kilometers."

"I know. But **I can do it.** I did it today."

> 📝 **can't / couldn't** (Unit 26):
> - **can't** = 현재 못하는 것: "I can't breathe"
> - **couldn't** = 과거 일반적으로 못했던 것: "I couldn't even run three laps"
> - **could** + 감각동사 = 과거 지각: "I could see people" / "I could tell he was worried"
> - **can** + 허락: "Can I walk to school?"

## Part 2: April — When I Walk, Everything Changes 🌸
*Grammar focus: When/If + 현재시제 (Unit 25) + can (현재 능력 — Unit 26)*

Walking became my new routine. And I discovered something:

**When I walk, I can hear the birds.** (걸으면 새소리가 들려)

**When I pass the tangerine orchards, I can smell the blossoms.** (감귤밭을 지나면 꽃향기가 나)

**When the wind blows, I can feel the season changing.** (바람이 불면 계절이 바뀌는 게 느껴져)

None of this was possible on the bus.

One morning, Grandfather Yeongcheol was tending his tangerine trees. He handed me a tangerine.

"**When you pass by tomorrow, I'll have more for you,**" he said. (내일 지나가면 더 줄게)

"Thank you, Grandfather. **If it rains, I'll still come.**" (비가 와도 올게)

He smiled. "Good. **As soon as the rain stops, the air smells even better.**" (비 그치자마자 공기가 더 좋아져)

I walked in the rain the next week. **I couldn't have done this in March.** Something had changed.

> 📝 **When + 현재시제, will** (Unit 25):
> - "When I walk, I can hear..." — when절에 **현재시제** (will 안 씀!)
> - "When you pass by tomorrow, I'll have more" — when + 현재, 주절에 will
> - "If it rains, I'll still come" — if + 현재시제 (비가 올지 **불확실**)
> - "As soon as the rain stops, the air smells better" — as soon as + 현재
>
> 📝 **When vs If** (Unit 25):
> - **When** I pass the orchards (매일 지나감 — 확실)
> - **If** it rains (비가 올지 — 불확실)

## Part 3: May — I Couldn't Have Imagined This 🏃
*Grammar focus: couldn't have done (과거 불가능 — Unit 27) + was able to (특정 성취 — Unit 26)*

One morning, I overslept. I had to run part of the way. And something clicked.

Running felt... good?

"**I never could have imagined this before.**" (예전에는 상상도 못했을 거야)

In March, during the long-distance run, **I couldn't even finish three laps without walking.** But today, I ran 3 kilometers without stopping. Not fast. But I didn't stop.

PE class changed too. The teacher timed us. When she read my result, she looked surprised.

"Doyun, you've improved. **If you keep this up, you'll get even faster.**" (이대로 계속하면 더 빨라질 거야)

"Really?" I said.

"**When the sports day comes, you should enter the race.**" (운동회가 오면 경주에 나가봐)

I went home and told Minseo: "**I was able to run 3km today without stopping.**" (오늘 3km를 멈추지 않고 달릴 수 있었어)

Not *I could run.* That would mean I had the general ability. No — this was a **specific achievement**. Today. This morning. For the first time.

> 📝 **couldn't have imagined** (Unit 27):
> - "I never could have imagined this" — 과거에 **절대 불가능했던** 일
> - 도윤이가 성장을 깨달을 때마다 반복하는 핵심 패턴
>
> 📝 **was able to vs could** (Unit 26):
> - "I was able to run 3km" — **특정 상황**에서 해낸 것 → was able to
> - "I couldn't run 3 laps" — **일반적으로** 못했던 것 → couldn't
> - ⚠️ 부정문(couldn't)은 항상 사용 가능, 긍정 특정 성취에만 was able to

## Part 4: Summer — You Could Try Running with Me ☀️
*Grammar focus: could (제안/가능성 — Unit 27) + will be able to (미래 능력 — Unit 26)*

By summer, I had my first running shoes. Running became real.

Minseo watched me one afternoon. "You're fast now."

"**You could try running with me sometime,**" I said. (같이 달려볼 수도 있어 — 제안)

"Me? I don't know..."

"**You could come if I run slower.**" (내가 천천히 달리면 같이 올 수 있어)

"**I think I could do it if we did it together,**" he said. (같이 하면 할 수 있을 것 같아)

We ran together the next morning. 5 kilometers. Minseo was gasping.

"How... do you... do this?"

"Six months ago, **I couldn't even run 7km.** Now **I can run 10km and still have energy left.**"

Coach Park noticed me too. "**With your level, you could definitely finish a 10km race.**" (네 실력이면 10km 대회 충분히 완주할 수 있어)

"Really? A race?"

"There's one in October. **If you sign up now, you'll have two months to prepare.** **After you finish your first race, you'll see everything differently.**" (지금 신청하면 두 달 준비할 수 있어. 첫 대회를 끝내면 모든 게 달라 보일 거야)

He paused. "**Before the race starts, I'll teach you how to pace yourself.** **Until you cross the finish line, don't look at your watch.**" (대회 시작 전에 페이스 조절법 알려줄게. 결승선 넘을 때까지 시계 보지 마)

I thought about it. Then: "**When I finish, I'll call my mom.**" Not *if*. **When.** I was going to finish.

But Grandfather Yeongcheol said it differently: "Run a lot while you're young. **You won't be able to later.**" (젊을 때 많이 뛰어. 나중에는 못 뛸 거야)

> 📝 **could** (현재/미래 제안 — Unit 27):
> - "You could try running with me" — 부드러운 **제안**
> - "You could come if I run slower" — **가능성**
> - "You could definitely finish" — **현재 능력에 대한 확신**
>
> 📝 **will be able to** (미래 능력 — Unit 26):
> - "You won't be able to later" — 미래에 **못할 것**
>
> 📝 **시간 접속사 + 현재시제** (Unit 25):
> - "If you sign up now" / "After you finish" / "Before the race starts"
> - "Until you cross the finish line" — 모두 **현재시제** (will 아님!)

## Part 5: August — The Heat and the What-Ifs 🌡️
*Grammar focus: could have done (과거 위험/후회 — Unit 27) + haven't been able to (현재완료 — Unit 26)*

August was brutal. 35 degrees. I pushed through 12 kilometers in the scorching heat.

Bad idea.

My vision blurred. I sat on the curb, dizzy.

"**If I'd pushed myself any harder, I could've gotten heatstroke.**" (더 무리했으면 열사병에 걸렸을 수도 있어)

"**I could have collapsed on the road.**" (길에서 쓰러졌을 수도 있어)

"**I could have been seriously hurt.**" (크게 다쳤을 수도 있어)

Mom was not happy. "You need to rest. **You haven't been able to sleep well lately,** have you?" (요즘 계속 잠을 잘 못 자고 있지?)

She was right. **I hadn't been able to train properly all week** because of the heat. (이번 주 내내 제대로 훈련을 못하고 있었어)

Coach texted: "Rest tomorrow. **After you've recovered, we'll start again.** **If your body tells you to stop, listen.**" (회복하고 나서 다시 시작하자. 몸이 멈추라 하면 들어)

That night, I thought about what **could have** happened. And what **could** happen if I was smarter about it.

> 📝 **could have done** (과거 위험 — Unit 27):
> - "I could've gotten heatstroke" — **실제로 일어나지 않았지만** 가능했던 위험
> - "I could have collapsed" — 같은 패턴
> - "I could have been hurt" — 수동태 + could have
>
> 📝 **haven't been able to** (현재완료 — Unit 26):
> - "haven't been able to sleep well lately" — **요즘 계속** 못하고 있는 상태
> - "hadn't been able to train all week" — **그 주 내내** 못하고 있던 상태
> - ≠ couldn't (과거 한 시점, 끝남) / ≠ can't (현재 순간만)
>
> 📝 **After + 현재완료** (Unit 25):
> - "After you've recovered, we'll start" — **완료된 후에** → when/after + 현재완료

## Part 6: October — The Race 🏅
*Grammar focus: when/if/until/as soon as + 현재시제 총정리 (Unit 25) + was able to (특정 성취 — Unit 26)*

October 3rd. Race day.

The night before, I couldn't sleep. I wrote down everything Coach told me:

- **When the gun goes off, don't sprint.** (총이 울리면 전력질주하지 마)
- **If you feel pain before 5km, slow down.** (5km 전에 통증 느끼면 속도 줄여)
- **Before you reach the halfway point, stay behind the pace group.** (중간 지점 전에 페이스 그룹 뒤에 있어)
- **After you pass 7km, you can push a little.** (7km 지나면 조금 밀어도 돼)
- **Until you see the finish line, don't look at your watch.** (결승선 보일 때까지 시계 보지 마)
- **As soon as you cross the line, walk. Don't stop suddenly.** (넘자마자 걸어. 갑자기 멈추지 마)
- **By the time you finish, your legs will be shaking. That's normal.** (끝낼 때쯤이면 다리가 떨릴 거야. 정상이야)

At 7km, my legs burned. **If I walk now, it'll be hard to start running again.** (지금 걸으면 다시 달리기 힘들어질 거야)

I kept going.

**When I saw the finish line, my eyes got blurry.** Not from exhaustion. From tears.

**48 minutes 47 seconds. I was able to finish.**

Not *I could finish.* I **was able to** finish — this one specific race, this one specific morning, after months of work.

**I was able to do something I couldn't have imagined six months ago.**

> 📝 **시간/조건 접속사 총정리** (Unit 25):
> - When the gun **goes** off — when + 현재
> - If you **feel** pain — if + 현재
> - Before you **reach** — before + 현재
> - After you **pass** — after + 현재
> - Until you **see** — until + 현재
> - As soon as you **cross** — as soon as + 현재
> - By the time you **finish** — by the time + 현재
> - ⚠️ 전부 **will이 아닌 현재시제!**
>
> 📝 **was able to** (특정 성취 — Unit 26):
> - "I was able to finish" — 그 대회에서 해낸 **한 번의 성취**
> - ≠ "I could finish" (일반적으로 끝낼 수 있었다 — 부자연스러움)

## Part 7: Looking Back — The Road Not Taken 🍊
*Grammar focus: could have done vs would have done vs couldn't have done 총정리 (Unit 27)*

After the race, I walked the 7-kilometer road home slowly.

I passed the tangerine orchards. The air smelled sweet. The ocean sparkled.

And I thought about a different version of myself. The one who never missed that bus.

**"If I'd kept taking the bus every day, this never would have happened."**
(매일 버스를 탔으면 이런 일은 절대 안 일어났을 거야 — 확실한 결과)

**"I never would have met Grandfather Yeongcheol."**
(할아버지를 만나지 못했을 거야 — 확실)

**"I never would have gotten those tangerines."**
(감귤도 못 받았을 거야 — 확실)

**"I could have taken the bus that morning. But I chose to walk."**
(그 아침에 버스를 탈 수도 있었어. 하지만 걷기로 했어 — 하지 않은 선택)

**"I could have given up when it got hard. But I didn't."**
(힘들 때 포기할 수도 있었어. 하지만 안 했어 — 하지 않은 선택)

**"Things I never could have seen from the bus."**
(버스에서는 절대 볼 수 없었을 것들 — 과거 불가능)

Cherry blossoms in spring. Morning dew on leaves. The wind by my ears. Rice fields turning golden. The grandfather's smile.

**"A tangerine I received while walking in the morning. Something I never could have had on the bus."**
(아침에 걸으면서 받은 감귤. 버스에서는 절대 가질 수 없었을 것)

I looked at the road ahead. **When spring comes again, I'll run a full marathon.** But for now—

**"I couldn't have imagined this a year ago."**

And maybe that's the point.

> 📝 **could have vs would have vs couldn't have** (Unit 27):
>
> | 표현 | 의미 | 예문 |
> |------|------|------|
> | **would have** | 확실한 결과 (~했을 거야) | "This never **would have** happened" |
> | **could have** | 가능했지만 안 한 선택 | "I **could have** taken the bus" |
> | **couldn't have** | 절대 불가능했던 것 | "I **couldn't have** imagined this" |
> | **never could have** | 더 강한 불가능 (감탄) | "Things I **never could have** seen" |
>
> 📝 **When + 현재** (Unit 25):
> - "When spring comes, I'll run" — 봄은 **확실히** 오므로 when

## Quick Decision Guide (빠른 판단 가이드)

### Unit 25: 시간/조건 부사절

```
When / If / Before / After / Until / As soon as 뒤에 → 현재시제!

❌ When I will arrive → ✅ When I arrive
❌ If it will rain    → ✅ If it rains

When = 확실히 일어날 일  (When spring comes...)
If   = 불확실한 조건     (If it rains...)
```

### Unit 26: Can의 시제별 선택

```
지금 할 수 있다         → can         "I can run 10km."
지금 못한다             → can't       "I can't breathe."
예전에 (일반적으로) 못했다 → couldn't    "I couldn't run 3 laps."
그때 특정 상황에서 해냈다  → was able to "I was able to finish the race."
앞으로 할 수 있을 것이다  → will be able to "You'll be able to handle 17km."
요즘/이번 주 계속 못하고 있다 → haven't been able to "I haven't been able to sleep."
```

### Unit 27: Could vs Could have

```
지금/미래에 가능하다      → could        "You could try running."
과거에 가능했지만 안 했다  → could have   "I could have taken the bus."
과거에 불가능했다         → couldn't have "I couldn't have imagined this."
과거에 위험했을 수도 있다  → could have   "I could've gotten heatstroke."
과거에 확실히 ~했을 것이다 → would have   "This never would have happened."
```
