# Thursday, October 17, 2024

Clear skies, scent of tangerines.

---

Morning.

Running to school, I noticed the tangerine orchard had turned orange.

*Wow, they're really ripe now.*

When I first passed by in March, they were still green.

Green all through summer.

Now they're orange. It's tangerine harvest season.

---

Grandfather Yeongcheol was in the field.

He was working on the harvest.

"Grandfather! Hello!"

I stopped to greet him.

"Oh, Doyun! I heard you did well in the race?"

"Yes! I finished it. 48 minutes and 47 seconds."

Grandfather smiled.

"Well done. That's impressive."

"Thank you."

He handed me a few tangerines.

"Here, try these. They turned out really good this year."

---

I peeled the skin and ate one.

Sweet and just a little sour.

"This is delicious!"

"Good. Take plenty with you."

I put the tangerines in my pocket.

"Thank you so much, Grandfather."

"When's your next race?"

"November. 21 kilometers."

His eyes widened.

"21 kilometers? That's twice the 10 kilometers."

"Yeah. I'm going to train hard for it."

"Good. Do your best. I'm rooting for you."

---

I ate tangerines while running to school.

They were sweet.

Grandfather's tangerines.

He's been supporting me all the way from March until now.

He doesn't say much, but he's warm-hearted.

---

Lunchtime.

I shared the tangerines with Minseo.

"I got these from my grandfather."

"Which grandfather?"

"The one whose orchard I run past. The guy I see when I'm running."

"Oh, that one? The one who always waves?"

"Yeah."

Minseo ate a tangerine.

"Whoa, so sweet. This is really good."

"Right? He said they turned out well this year."

---

After school.

I gave a tangerine to my teacher.

"Teacher, my grandfather gave me these. Please have one."

"Thanks. This is the grandfather you meet on your way to school?"

"Yes. I first met him back in March."

"That's a nice connection."

My teacher ate the tangerine.

"Mmm, tasty. These tangerines are really good this year."

---

On the way home.

Looking out the bus window, I watched the tangerine orchard.

It was painted orange.

*In March, it was all green.*

The seasons have changed.

I've changed too.

---

In my room, writing this entry.

The tangerines ripened.

The field turned orange.

I got tangerines from Grandfather.

"You did well in the race? Good job."

I remember when I first met him back in March.

"Where are you headed?" "To school."

Back then, I was walking.

Now I'm running.

Grandfather has watched all of that change.

I want to do well in the next race too.

21 kilometers.

I want to tell Grandfather the good news.
