# Monday, September 16, 2024

Clear skies. End of Chuseok break.

---

The Chuseok holiday is over.

I didn't get much running in over the three days—just some light jogging while visiting relatives. Back to training starting today.

---

Morning long run. 12 km.

My legs felt heavy after the break. The first 3 km were tough.

But I kept going. By 5 km, my body was warmed up.

Finished the 12 km. 1 hour 10 minutes.

Slower than usual, but that's okay. I'll get my rhythm back.

---

Evening.

We all had dinner together as a family.

Dad suddenly asked me something.

"Doyun, you're running in a marathon?"

I was shocked. Dad had never shown interest in my running before.

"Yes. A 10 km race in October."

"10 km? How long does that take?"

"About 55 minutes right now. My goal is to get under 50 minutes."

Dad nodded.

"Doesn't it hurt?"

"It was hard at first, but it's okay now."

---

Mom said something.

"Doyun started running back in March. It's been almost six months now."

"Six months? It's been that long?"

Dad looked surprised.

"You didn't know?"

"I've been busy... with work. There's been a lot of business trips lately."

Dad is always busy with work. Lots of business trips, coming home late most nights.

I guess that's why he didn't know much about my running.

---

Dad asked me.

"But why did you suddenly start running?"

"I hated the bus."

"The bus?"

"I get really bad motion sickness. So I started walking instead, and then one day I tried running and... I liked it."

Dad laughed.

"From bus sickness to marathons. That's interesting."

"I think so too."

---

My little sister jumped in.

"Oppa, you're so different these days. You're running every morning."

"Really?"

"Yeah. I want to come with you, but you run too fast."

"You could come if I run slower."

"Really? I want to run too!"

"Let's go together this weekend."

My sister got excited.

---

Dad said something.

"Can I come cheer you on at the race?"

"Huh?"

"The race is in October, right? I want to come cheer you on."

My chest felt tight.

*Dad is coming to cheer me on at my race?*

"Yes, please come. I'd really like that."

"Got it. Tell me the date."

"October 5th."

Dad made a note in his phone.

---

Now I'm in my room writing this entry.

Today, Dad showed interest in my running.

*"Doyun, you're running in a marathon?"*

It was the first time. The first time Dad ever asked me about my training.

Dad is busy. Work, business trips, late nights at the office.

So I thought he didn't know what I was doing.

But today he asked.

And he said he'd come cheer me on at the race.

Honestly, I was happy.

Mom's been worrying and cheering me on from the start. Grandpa too. My teacher. Minseul.

But Dad... I thought he didn't care.

Today I realized something. It's not that he didn't care. He just didn't know.

Now he knows.

On race day, I'll see Dad at the finish line.

I need to work harder.

D-19.
