# Thursday, May 16, 2024

Clear skies. The early morning air was crisp.

---

Today I tried running the whole way for the first time.

7km. From home to school. Every step.

Some people might call that crazy. But I wanted to try it.

---

I woke up at 5:50.

Thirty minutes earlier than usual. Since I had no idea how long it would take to run the whole way, I wanted to leave with plenty of time.

I ate breakfast, drank some water, and left the house at 6:20.

Mom asked, "Why are you leaving so early today?"

"I'm going to run. The whole way."

"The whole way? All 7km?"

"Yeah."

Mom's eyes went wide.

"Are you crazy? You'll exhaust yourself."

"I'll try. If it gets too hard, I'll walk part of it."

"..."

Mom let out a sigh. But she didn't try to stop me.

"Be careful. Don't push yourself too hard."

"Okay."

---

I started running.

First kilometer. Getting out of the village. A familiar road. I'd run it many times before.

My breathing got a little heavier. But I was okay.

2km. The tangerine orchard road.

Grandpa Yeongcheol was out in his field. When he saw me running by, he waved. I waved back. Without stopping.

3km.

My legs started feeling heavy. My breathing got harder.

*This is tough.*

But I didn't want to stop.

---

The crisis came at 4km.

Suddenly my legs went stiff. My breath caught in my throat. My pace slowed down.

*Should I walk?*

The thought crossed my mind. Just a short walk would help me recover.

But.

*No, just a little more.*

I gritted my teeth. Slowed down even more. Almost to the pace of a fast walk. But I kept my feet in a running motion.

50m. 100m. 200m.

I pushed forward, little by little.

---

5km.

Strangely, I started feeling okay again.

At 4km I thought I was dying, but once I passed 5km, my body felt lighter. Breathing got easier too.

*What is this?*

I looked it up later. Apparently it's called a "second wind." That moment when you're struggling and suddenly feel fine.

I didn't know that then. It just felt amazing.

6km.

The school came into view. I pushed with everything I had left.

7km.

I passed through the school gate.

---

I checked my watch.

7:05. It took 45 minutes.

Walking takes an hour and ten minutes. Running took 45 minutes. Twenty-five minutes faster.

I collapsed onto a bench, gasping for air. Sweat poured off me like rain. My legs were trembling.

*I did it.*

I ran 7km. Without stopping.

---

I sat there for a while, then drank some water.

My heart calmed down. My breathing returned to normal.

Then I started feeling good. Runner's high.

But stronger than usual. Maybe because I'd run 7km. My whole body tingled.

*I never could have imagined this before.*

Running 7km. When I started middle school, even 1km was hard.

---

I told Minseo.

"Hey, I ran 7km to get here today."

"What? The whole way?"

"Yeah."

"That's insane. Seriously insane."

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Wasn't it hard?"

"It was hard. I thought I was going to die in the middle. But I did it."

"Wow..."

Minseo looked me up and down.

"Dude, are you gonna run a marathon or something?"

"A marathon?"

"Yeah. Someone who can run 7km could run a marathon too, right?"

A marathon. 42.195km. Six times 7km.

*No way.*

But Minseo's words stuck in my head.

---

Writing in my diary now.

Today I ran 7km. From home to school. The entire distance.

It took 45 minutes. I thought I was going to die in the middle. At 4km my legs went stiff and I couldn't breathe.

But I did it. I didn't stop.

Minseo asked me, "Are you gonna run a marathon or something?"

A marathon. 42.195km.

Honestly, I can't imagine it. 7km was hard enough, and a marathon is 42km.

But back in March, even 1km was hard. Back then, 7km was unimaginable.

Now I run 7km.

So maybe someday, 42km?

I don't know. But it doesn't seem completely impossible.

For now, let me keep practicing 7km. Once I'm used to it, I'll try going farther.

Slowly. Little by little.
