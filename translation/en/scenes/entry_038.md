# Monday, July 15, 2024

Clear skies. That crisp freshness after the rain.

---

The typhoon passed.

By Friday afternoon, the skies had cleared. Saturday was completely sunny.

So I ran.

---

Friday afternoon.

The moment the typhoon passed, I went outside.

Four days. Four days since I'd run.

I put on my running shoes and stepped out the door. The air felt different. Clean, after the rain. Cool.

I started running.

---

The first kilometer felt strange.

My legs were awkward. Like my body had forgotten the rhythm.

But after the second kilometer, it came back. My body remembered. The feeling of running.

A sense of freedom washed over me.

Like all the pent-up energy from being trapped for four days had finally burst out. My legs moved with excitement. My heart pounded. Sweat dripped.

*Ah, this feeling.*

The feeling of running. Feeling it again after four days.

---

I stopped after 5km.

Breathing hard, I looked up at the sky. Blue sky. Not a single cloud.

It was like the typhoon had washed the sky clean.

*This is nice.*

Not being able to run for four days was hard. But that made today even better.

---

Saturday, I ran with Minseo again.

Second time since last week.

We met at the park at 7 a.m.

"Dude, I was itching to move for four days."

"Same. I wanted to run."

Minseo said it like he meant it.

This time we ran 2km. Walked once in the middle.

Minseo seemed less tired than last time.

"Hey, am I getting better?"

"Yeah. You look better than before."

"Really?"

Minseo grinned.

---

This morning.

I left at 5:15 and ran the full 7km route.

44 minutes. A solid time.

Even after four days off, my performance hadn't dropped. If anything, my body felt lighter. Thanks to the rest, maybe.

---

After arriving at school, I thought about it.

What they say about rest being part of training.

I think it's true. Four days of rest, and my condition was actually better.

Running every day isn't the only answer. You have to rest when you need to, so your body can recover.

---

At lunch, Minseo said something.

"Hey, I felt really good after running Saturday."

"Yeah. Me too."

"Not being able to run because of the typhoon made it even better when we finally did."

"Exactly. It was like that."

Minseo smiled.

"I'm gonna keep at it. Running."

"Seriously?"

"Yeah. I'm gonna buy running shoes."

Minseo showed me his phone. A search page for running shoes.

"Which ones are good?"

"Hmm... for beginners, something like this would be fine."

We picked out shoes together. Minseo said he'd go with white.

---

Writing in my diary.

The typhoon passed.

I ran after four days. It was liberating.

I learned that rest is part of training too. Four days off, and my condition was actually better.

Minseo said he's buying running shoes. Said he'll keep running.

I have a real training partner now. For real.

Back in March, I started walking alone. Now I have a friend to run with.

This is good. Really good.

Summer vacation is coming up.

I need to run a lot during the break.
