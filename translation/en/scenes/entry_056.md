# Thursday, September 19, 2024

Overcast. Strong wind.

---

After school today.

Coach Park called me over.

"Doyun, come here for a second."

I sat down on a bench in the gym.

"How's the race prep going?"

"Pretty well, I think."

"What's your 10km time right now?"

"Around 53 minutes. I've shaved off a bit."

"Good. But I want to teach you something today."

---

Coach Park said, "You know about pacing?"

"Yeah. Like controlling your speed."

"Right. And that's the most important thing in a race."

"Why?"

"If you run too fast at the beginning, you'll burn out toward the end and can't finish strong."

I nodded.

"On the flip side, if you go too slow at first, you won't get a good time."

"So how am I supposed to do it?"

---

Coach Park pulled out his phone.

"If you want to run 10km in 50 minutes, that's a 5-minute pace per kilometer."

"Five minutes per kilometer..."

"For the first 1-2km, start a little slower—about 5 minutes 10 seconds."

"Why?"

"Your body's still warming up. If you get excited and run too fast at the start, you'll suffer later."

"Oh..."

"Kilometers 3 through 7, hold steady. Keep that 5-minute pace."

"Got it."

"For the last 8-10km, use everything you've got left. You can push down to 4 minutes 50 seconds."

---

I wrote it all down.

**Pacing Strategy**
- 1-2km: 5 min 10 sec (easy start)
- 3-7km: 5 min 00 sec (steady state)
- 8-10km: 4 min 50 sec (sprint finish)

"Coach, can I practice this?"

"Of course. Try it tomorrow during your tempo run."

"Yes!"

---

Coach Park smiled.

"And one more thing. Keep your eye on your watch."

"A watch?"

"You have a running watch?"

"No. I just use a phone app..."

Coach Park thought for a moment, then said, "Wait."

He pulled something out of his bag.

It was a wristwatch.

"Here."

"What is it?"

"A running watch. I used it back in the day. You can borrow it."

"Really?"

"Give it back after the race."

---

I put it on.

I pressed a button and the screen came to life.

Pace, distance, heart rate all displayed.

"Whoa, that's insane."

"With that on your wrist, it's way easier to control your pace."

"Thank you so much!"

Coach Park laughed.

"Work hard. You can do this."

---

On the way home.

I couldn't stop staring at the running watch on the bus.

I played around with all the buttons.

It saves records, shows split times by section, everything.

*Tomorrow I need to run with this on.*

---

Writing this at home.

Today I learned about pacing from Coach Park.

You can't start out running too fast.

Easy for the first 1-2km, steady for 3-7km, then sprint the last 8-10km.

And Coach Park lent me a running watch.

Wearing it on my wrist makes me feel like a real runner.

Tomorrow during the tempo run, I'll try the pacing strategy.

Five minutes per kilometer.

I need to get my body used to this pace.

On race day, I won't get excited and run too fast.

Calm. Steady. The plan.

D-16.
