# Monday, April 15, 2024

Clear skies. The breeze felt nice.

---

It hit me while walking today.

Walking has become natural.

When I first started in early March, it was hard. My legs hurt, I was out of breath, and all I could think was *When will I get there?*

But now it's different.

I just walk. I don't even think about whether it's hard or not. Left foot, right foot, left foot, right foot. It moves automatically.

When did this happen?

---

I noticed it from the moment I left the house.

Before, I used to sigh at the front door. *I have to walk for over an hour.* That kind of thought.

But today, I just opened the door and walked out. Without thinking. Like it was the most natural thing.

Tied my shoes, put on my bag, started walking.

*Nice weather today.*

That was the only thought in my head.

---

I looked around while walking.

The canola flowers were starting to fade. Yellow petals scattered on the ground. But the green leaves had grown thicker instead.

The tangerine groves had changed too. Most of the white blossoms had fallen, and tiny green fruits were starting to form. Future tangerines.

Spring is passing.

But it's strange. I started walking when spring began, and now spring is already leaving. Time moves fast.

---

The old man was there.

"Good morning!"

I greeted him loudly. Mom told me last time—always greet your elders properly.

"Oh, kid. Walking again today?"

"Yes."

"Good for you. You've gotten used to it by now, haven't you?"

"Yes, a lot."

The old man nodded.

"That's a good thing. Nothing stays hard once you get used to it. The beginning is always the hardest part."

"..."

I thought about that. He's right. The beginning was hard. But now I'm used to it.

"What's your name, kid?"

He asked suddenly.

"Kim Doyun."

"Doyun. That's a nice name."

"What's your name, sir?"

"Me? Go Yeongcheol."

Grandfather Go Yeongcheol. Now I know his name.

"Thank you, sir. The tangerines you gave me last time were really good."

"Yeah? Glad you enjoyed them."

The old man smiled. I smiled too.

---

After I got to school, I thought about it.

Now I know his name. Grandfather Go Yeongcheol. The man who works in the tangerine grove.

We greet each other every morning, and sometimes he gives me tangerines.

*This is a connection.*

A connection that wouldn't exist if I still took the bus.

A connection that only happened because I started walking.

It's strange. I used to hate exercise, and now I walk over an hour twice a week. And it's not even hard. I actually like it.

Have I really changed?

---

I asked Minseo.

"Hey, do you think I've changed?"

"What?"

"Me. Do I seem different from before?"

Minseo looked me up and down.

"I don't know... maybe you've lost weight?"

"Weight?"

"Yeah. Your face looks slimmer. And your legs look more toned."

I looked down at my legs. Hard to tell. But my pants do feel a bit looser.

"From walking?"

"Obviously. If you walk for over an hour twice a week, you're gonna lose weight."

That's what Minseo said. I guess so. I've lost weight.

"But why? Why are you suddenly asking if you've changed?"

"No reason. It's just... walking feels natural now. I couldn't have imagined this before."

"Oh, that. Yeah, you've definitely changed."

Minseo nodded.

"The old you would never have walked. You hated exercise."

"Yeah."

"But now you're doing it on your own. That's a change for sure."

---

Writing in my diary now.

Walking has become natural.

I started a month and a half ago. Because I missed the bus and had no choice.

But now it's just normal. Mondays and Thursdays are walking days. Leave at 6:40. Arrive at 7:50.

Apparently I've lost weight. That's what Minseo said. My face looks slimmer.

I looked in the mirror. Can't really tell. But I do feel lighter somehow.

Walking is changing me.

And I like it.

Grandfather Go Yeongcheol.

I learned his name today. Next time I see him, I should use it.

"Good morning, Grandfather Yeongcheol!"

Like that.
