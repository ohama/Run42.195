# March 2, 2025 - Sunday

Clear skies. Spring.

---

The full marathon race day.

42.195km.

---

5:00 AM.

My eyes opened.

Before the alarm.

My heart was pounding like crazy.

---

5:30 AM.

One banana. A little bread.

Ate light.

Drank some water too.

---

6:00 AM.

Headed to the stadium with my family.

It was quiet in the car.

Dad said, "Nervous?"

"Yeah."

"You can do it. I'll be waiting at the finish line."

"Thanks."

---

6:30 AM. We arrived at the stadium.

People everywhere.

More than the half-marathon.

Thousands of runners.

---

Minseo's family was there.

"Doyun! You made it!"

Minseo ran over to me.

"There you are."

"Of course I'm here."

Minseo's mom said, "Run well. We'll be cheering you on."

"Thank you."

---

I pinned on my bib number.

3827.

Same number as the half-marathon.

---

I stretched.

Loosened up my body.

My legs felt light.

Peak condition.

---

7:50 AM.

Moved to the starting line.

My heart felt like it would explode.

I looked around.

Thousands of runners.

All of us standing here with the same goal.

42.195km.

---

8:00 AM.

The signal.

We started running.

---

**0-10km**

The first 5km slow.

Like my coach taught me.

Can't run fast just because I'm excited.

Pace: 6:30 per kilometer.

---

The scenery passed by.

Rape blossoms in full bloom.

Yellow fields.

Spring has arrived.

---

10km checkpoint.

1 hour 5 minutes.

Right on schedule.

Feeling good.

---

**10-20km**

Holding the pace.

6:30 per kilometer.

---

15km.

Getting close to the half-marathon distance.

Still plenty of energy left.

---

20km.

2 hours 10 minutes.

---

I spotted Minseo.

"Doyun! Fighting!"

I waved back.

Got a boost of energy.

---

21km.

The half-marathon distance.

2 hours 15 minutes.

---

*Halfway done here.*

21 more kilometers to go.

---

**20-30km**

This is where it gets real.

My coach told me this.

"The 20-30km section is crucial."

---

25km.

2 hours 42 minutes.

My legs started feeling heavier.

But it's okay.

I expected this.

---

28km.

Legs getting heavier.

Pace slowed down.

7-minute range.

It's fine. Right on plan.

---

30km.

3 hours 18 minutes.

---

*The wall starts here.*

My coach told me.

"Once you hit 30km, the wall comes."

---

**30-35km**

31km.

My legs weren't listening anymore.

Heavy.

Like lead weights.

---

32km.

My breathing got rough.

Pace slowed more.

8-minute range.

---

33km.

*Should I quit?*

The thought flashed through my head.

---

No.

I've come this far.

I've trained for a year.

I can't give up.

---

34km.

One step.

One step.

Forward.

---

35km.

3 hours 55 minutes.

*Just 7 more kilometers. Just 7.*

---

**35-40km**

It was hell.

My legs wouldn't move.

Every step was agony.

---

36km.

I wanted to walk.

*Should I just walk a little?*

---

No.

If I walk, it'll be hard to run again.

Just run slow.

---

37km.

Pace in the 9-minute range.

It's okay.

I'm still moving forward.

---

38km.

My coach's words came back to me.

"You just have to not give up. Even slow, just keep moving forward."

---

39km.

3 kilometers left.

*Just 3km. Just 3.*

---

40km.

4 hours 28 minutes.

*2 more kilometers.*

---

**40-42.195km**

The final 2 kilometers.

My legs felt like they wouldn't move anymore.

But they moved.

---

41km.

The finish line came into view.

I heard the crowd cheering.

---

41.5km.

I could see my family.

"Doyun! Almost there!"

My dad's voice.

---

42km.

*Just 195 meters more.*

---

I squeezed out the last bit of strength.

I ran.

Toward the finish line.

---

42.195km.

Finish.

4 hours 42 minutes 18 seconds.

---

I stopped.

My knees buckled.

I collapsed right there.

---

I gasped for breath.

My whole body was shaking.

---

But I was smiling.

Tears came.

*I did it.*

---

I got my finisher's medal.

So much bigger and heavier than the half-marathon medal.

I hung it around my neck.

---

Dad came running.

"Doyun!"

He hugged me.

Dad was crying too.

"You did it. You did it, son."

"Dad... it was so hard."

"I know. I know. But you did it."

---

Mom came over.

She was crying too.

"I'm so proud. My son."

My little sister said, "Oppa, you're insane!"

---

Minseo came over.

"You did it! You finished!"

Minseo's eyes were red too.

"I saw you at the 20km mark. You looked exhausted, but you kept going."

"It was brutal. After 30km, I felt like I was dying."

"But you didn't die. You finished."

"Yeah."

---

I texted my coach.

"Coach, I finished. 4 hours 42 minutes 18 seconds."

The reply came right away.

"Congratulations. That's really amazing. The kid who got carsick on the bus a year ago just completed a full marathon."

Tears came again.

---

On the way home.

In the car, I touched my medal.

I felt its weight.

The weight of one year.

The weight of every single step.

---

I came to my room and I'm writing this entry.

42.195km.

Finished.

4 hours 42 minutes 18 seconds.

---

I did it.

One year ago, today.

I got carsick on the bus.

Walking 7 kilometers was hard.

---

Now.

I completed 42.195 kilometers.

---

After 30km, I felt like I was dying.

I thought about quitting a thousand times.

But I didn't stop.

One step.

One step.

Forward.

---

And I crossed the finish line.

---

My coach told me this.

"Once you finish, your life will change."

I think he's right.

Completing 42 kilometers means.

You can do anything.

---

I hung my finisher's medal on my wall.

Next to my 10km medal and half-marathon medal.

Three medals.

One year's record.

---

What's next?

I don't know yet.

Let me just rest today.

Today, I'll just celebrate.

---

42.195km.

Finished.

I did it.

---

The rape blossoms are in full bloom.

Spring has come.

A year ago this spring, I got carsick on the bus.

Now, I'm a marathoner.

---

The Jeju Boy's Marathon Challenge.

One year's record.

The End.
