# Thursday, April 11, 2024

Cloudy. No rain, though.

---

The grandfather gave me tangerines today.

He'd given me one before. Early April. Just a small one back then, but today he handed me a whole bag.

"Here, take these with you."

"What? This many?"

"That's not many. Just a few. Share them with your friends at school."

"Thank you..."

They were heavy. Dropped the bag in my backpack and felt the weight. But it felt good.

---

I ended up talking with the grandfather a bit while taking the tangerines.

"Grandfather, do you come out to the orchard every day?"

"Pretty much. Come out in the morning, putter around, and suddenly it's lunchtime."

"Doesn't it get tiring?"

"Sure it does. But hey, it's what I've done my whole life."

He smiled. Wrinkled face. Rough hands. Sun-darkened skin.

"Why do you walk, anyway?"

"Huh?"

"Wouldn't the bus be easier? Why bother walking?"

I thought for a moment. What should I say?

"I get motion sickness on the bus. That's why I started walking."

"Motion sickness? Ah, I see."

"But it's not just about the motion sickness anymore."

"Then what?"

"I just... started liking walking. I don't really know why."

The grandfather nodded.

"That's how it goes. That's a good thing. Don't need a reason for something good to be good."

---

When I got to school, I opened the bag.

Six tangerines inside. Small ones. Thin skin, deep orange color.

I gave some to Minseo.

"Hey, eat these."

"What? Tangerines?"

"Yeah. The grandfather gave them to me."

"Seriously? The tangerine orchard grandfather?"

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Dude, you guys are really close now. Getting tangerines and everything."

"I don't know if we're close. We just say hi when I pass by."

"That's being close. You don't give tangerines to strangers."

Minseo peeled one as he talked. I peeled one too and took a bite.

Sweet. Sweet and tangy. Juice spread through my mouth.

"Man, these are good."

"Yeah."

"Nothing beats Jeju tangerines."

Minseo gave a thumbs up. I smiled too.

---

While eating the tangerines, I got to thinking.

Why did the grandfather give me tangerines?

I'm just some random student. We only say hi for a moment each morning.

*Maybe he just wanted to give them. That's it.*

That's probably it. Kindness without reason. For the grandfather, that must just be natural.

Have I ever done something like that for someone?

Giving something for no reason. Just because I wanted to give it.

I don't know. Probably not.

---

I took the bus home.

Sat by the window and watched outside. Tangerine orchards flashed by. The spot where I talked with the grandfather this morning.

He's probably working in that orchard right now. Thinning the blossoms, checking on the trees.

*I want to do something for him too.*

The thought just came to me. Felt bad only receiving tangerines.

What could I even do?

What does the grandfather need?

I don't know. But next time I see him, I should ask.

---

I showed Mom the tangerines when I got home.

"Where'd you get these?"

"A grandfather gave them to me. The tangerine orchard grandfather."

"Tangerine orchard grandfather?"

Mom tilted her head. Oh right, I never really told her the details.

"When I walk to school, I pass by a tangerine orchard. There's a grandfather who works there. I say hi every morning."

"Oh, really?"

"Yeah. He gave me tangerines today. Told me to share them with friends at school."

Mom picked one up.

"What a kind man."

"Yeah."

"Make sure you greet him properly. It's important to show respect to your elders."

"I know."

Mom peeled one and ate it. Then she smiled.

"These are delicious. Nothing beats Jeju tangerines."

Exactly what Minseo said. I smiled too.

---

Writing in my diary now.

The grandfather gave me tangerines.

Walking to school this morning, stopped to say hi, and he handed me a whole bag.

It feels like something special.

If I'd kept taking the bus every day, this never would've happened. I never would've met the grandfather. Never would've gotten tangerines.

A connection that came from walking.

That feels kind of nice.

Next time I see the grandfather, I want to do something for him. I need to think about what I can do.

For now, I'll just keep greeting him well. Loud and cheerful.

"Good morning!"

Like that.
