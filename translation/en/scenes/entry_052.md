# Thursday, September 5, 2024

Overcast. Looks like rain coming.

---

T-30.

Exactly one month left until the race.

---

This morning I ran to school again.

While running, I kept thinking.

*What can I actually accomplish in a month?*

I can run 10km now. Tried it a few times during summer break. Takes about 55 minutes.

But a race is different. Running with other people. The nerves that come with it. Having to manage your pace.

---

Arrived at school.

Changed into my PE clothes and sat on the bench.

Searched on my phone.

"How to train for a 10km marathon"

A bunch of articles came up.

- Run 3-4 times a week
- Interval training
- Long runs (slow, long distance)
- Importance of rest

I nodded while reading.

*I need to do this more systematically.*

Until now I just ran whenever. Did whatever I felt like. Wanted to go far, went far. Wanted to go fast, went fast.

Seems like I can't do that anymore.

---

After school.

Met Coach Park Jaehyun at the gym.

"Coach, I have a question."

"Yeah, what is it?"

"How should I prepare for the 10km race?"

Coach smiled.

"You're taking this seriously. Good. Sit down."

I sat on the bench.

---

Coach said,

"First, what's your 10km time right now?"

"About 55 minutes."

"That's decent. With a month, you could get under 50 minutes."

"Really?"

"Yeah. But you can't overdo it. Injury is the scariest thing."

I nodded.

"How many days a week are you running?"

"Almost every day."

Coach shook his head.

"Can't do every day. You need rest days. Your muscles need time to recover."

"Oh..."

"4-5 days a week is enough. The rest, just light walking or stretching."

---

Coach continued.

"You've got to mix up your training types."

"How do you mean?"

"There are three kinds. First, long runs. Running slow over a long distance. Good for building endurance."

"I got it."

"Second, intervals. Running fast, then slow, then repeating. Good for building speed."

"Intervals..."

"Third, tempo runs. Running at race pace steadily. Real-world practice."

I took notes.

Long runs. Intervals. Tempo runs.

"Coach, how should I organize this?"

Coach thought for a moment, then said,

"Monday long run, Wednesday intervals, Friday tempo run. The rest, light jogging or rest. Try it like that."

"Got it!"

---

On the way home.

Organized my training plan on the bus.

**September Training Plan**

| Day | Workout |
|------|------|
| Mon | Long run (10-12km, slow) |
| Tue | Rest or easy jog |
| Wed | Intervals (fast-slow repetition) |
| Thu | Morning run to school 7km |
| Fri | Tempo run (race pace) |
| Sat | Run with Minseow |
| Sun | Rest |

This should work.

Systematic.

Feels like the first time I'm actually "training" for something.

---

Evening.

Mom asked me,

"How's the race prep going?"

"Good. Got some advice from Coach today."

"What'd he say?"

"To train systematically. Not just running every day, but different types of training."

Mom nodded.

"Right, don't push too hard. Don't get hurt."

"Okay."

---

I'm in my room writing this.

T-30.

One month left.

Today I learned about training methods from Coach.

Long runs, intervals, tempo runs.

Words I'd never heard before.

Until now I just ran. No thinking. Just whatever I felt like.

That was good too. I got to know the joy of running.

But a race is different.

There's a goal. Complete 10km. If possible, under 50 minutes.

I need to prepare systematically for that goal.

I'm nervous.

The guy who got carsick in March is now planning training schedules before a marathon race.

That's kind of wild.

Let me work hard for this month.

T-30.
