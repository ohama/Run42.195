# Thursday, March 14, 2024

Clear skies. It smells like spring.

---

I'm still thinking about walking to school on Monday.

Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday. Three days of taking the bus. Back to normal. Packed bus, motion sickness, churning stomach.

But something's strange.

Before, I just accepted it as the way things were. Now it feels... harder.

Maybe because I know what walking felt like.

---

This morning, my stomach churned on the bus again.

I couldn't open the window, so I felt suffocated. Someone's shoulder kept touching my face. Smells mixing together. I closed my eyes and pulled out one of the peppermints Minseo gave me.

*Monday wasn't like this.*

That thought kept coming back.

When I walked, my stomach didn't churn. The air was fresh. I could go at my own pace. Nobody bumped into me.

It was hard, though. My legs ached. It took an hour and ten minutes.

But still... it was better than the bus.

---

At lunch, Minseo asked me.

"Hey, wasn't it really tough walking on Monday?"

"It was. My legs hurt."

"So why do you look like you want to do it again?"

I paused. Did Minseo read my expression?

"... What are you talking about."

"You made a face when we were talking about the bus. But when you mentioned walking, your expression totally changed."

Minseo's observant like that. Kind of scary sometimes.

"It's just... my stomach didn't churn when I walked. It was better than the bus."

"But it takes over an hour."

"Yeah."

"Isn't that too long?"

"It is."

"Then why do you want to walk again?"

I couldn't answer. Because I don't know either.

I just wanted to walk. That's all.

---

After class, I looked out at the sports field.

Some kids were playing soccer. Running around, shouting, sweating.

*Doesn't that tire them out?*

That's what I used to think. Why run around like that? It's just exhausting.

But today I saw it differently.

Those kids are running because they want to. It's tiring, but they want to run, so they run.

Is that what this is for me?

I want to walk, so I want to walk?

Even if it takes an hour and ten minutes?

... I don't know.

---

On the bus home, I thought about it.

The afternoon bus is fine. Fewer people, and I can sit. But the morning bus is the problem.

*What if I just walk in the morning?*

The thought crossed my mind.

Walk to school in the morning, take the bus home. That would be 1 hour 10 minutes + 40 minutes = 1 hour 50 minutes. Round trip by bus is 1 hour 20 minutes, so 30 minutes more.

Thirty minutes.

If I spend 30 extra minutes, I don't have to get motion sick in the morning.

*That might... be worth it?*

But there's a problem. To walk for an hour and ten minutes, I'd have to leave around 6:30. An hour earlier than now.

6:30. Right around sunrise.

Would it be dark? Would it be dangerous?

And do I even have the stamina to walk over an hour every day?

I only walked once on Monday, but on Tuesday morning my calves were killing me. My legs were shaking going down the stairs.

*Every day would be too much.*

Right. Every day is too much.

But... what about sometimes?

What about once a week?

---

At dinner, I asked Mom.

"Mom, can I walk to school sometimes?"

Mom looked surprised.

"Walk? All the way there?"

"Yeah."

"How long does it take?"

"About an hour and ten minutes."

"What? An hour and ten?"

Mom's eyes went wide.

"The motion sickness on the bus is really bad. When I walk, I don't feel nauseous."

"But it's so far. Isn't it hard?"

"It is hard... but it's better than riding the bus."

Mom looked at me for a while.

"Really?"

"Yeah."

"..."

Mom ate a spoonful of rice, thinking, then said.

"If it's just sometimes... sure, go ahead. But don't push yourself. If it gets hard, just take the bus."

"Okay."

"And you have to eat breakfast before you go. Otherwise you'll collapse."

"Got it."

Mom said yes.

She said I can walk sometimes.

---

Writing this diary, I'm thinking.

Maybe I'll try walking next Monday.

Monday is the hardest bus day because it comes after two days off. If I walk that day, it might be better.

I need to wake up at 6:30. I should set multiple alarms.

I have to eat breakfast too. Otherwise I'll run out of energy halfway.

I should lighten my bag too. My shoulder hurt on Monday.

Lots to think about.

But strangely, I'm excited.

Walking instead of taking the bus. Why does that excite me?

I don't know. I just want to walk.

I'll be waiting for Monday.
