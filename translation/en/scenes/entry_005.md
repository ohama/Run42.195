# Monday, March 18, 2024

Clear skies. The cherry blossoms are starting to bloom.

---

Today I walked to school on purpose.

I didn't miss the bus. It was a choice. My choice to walk.

My alarm went off at 6:20. I set three of them, so I definitely woke up. Got out of bed in the dim room, washed up, ate breakfast, and left the house at 6:40.

Mom followed me to the front door.

"You're really walking?"

"Yeah."

"Be careful. Watch out for cars. If it gets too hard, catch a bus halfway."

"I know."

"Enjoy your lunch."

Mom watched me with a worried look on her face. I waved and walked out the door.

---

Jeju at 6:40 was still dusky.

The sky was just starting to brighten. The eastern edge was shifting from violet to pale pink. The air was cold. Late March, but the mornings were still chilly.

I took a deep breath.

Cold air flowed into my lungs. Refreshing. Nothing like that stuffy air on the bus.

*This is nice.*

The thought came without me realizing it.

I started walking.

---

It felt different from last time when I missed the bus.

Back then, I had no choice but to walk. I was rushing because I couldn't be late. Annoyed the whole time.

But today was different.

This was my choice. Waking up at 6:20, eating breakfast, walking right now. I decided all of it.

That difference was bigger than I expected.

My mind was at ease. I wasn't in a rush. I just had to walk. At my own pace.

Left, right, left, right.

The steady sound of my footsteps reached my ears.

---

Once I left the village, the tangerine orchards appeared.

The same road I walked before. But today it looked different somehow.

Sunlight filtered through the tangerine trees. Dew on the leaves sparkled in the morning light. It was early, so nobody was around. The road felt like it was mine alone.

And the cherry blossoms.

A few cherry trees lined the roadside. Last week they were still buds, but today they were starting to bloom. Pale pink petals clung to the tips of the branches.

*Pretty.*

I never would have seen this from the bus. Just a blur passing by. But walking, I could see it. Slowly, closely.

I stopped in front of a cherry tree.

Looked up at the flowers. Soft pink. Petals glowing in the morning sun.

I felt strangely good.

---

Around forty minutes in, my legs started to ache.

I expected this. They hurt around this time last time too.

But today felt less tiring.

I thought about why. Probably a mindset thing. Last time I kept thinking, *When will I get there? What if I'm late?* But today, I just thought, *I just need to keep walking.* That made it easier.

And I think my stamina improved a little. I was less out of breath than last time. Just slightly.

*Did my stamina actually increase in one week?*

Probably not. My body just remembered. Like, *Oh, I can handle this much.*

---

7:50.

I could see the school.

One hour and ten minutes. Same as last time. But it felt different. Last time I was gasping for air, but today I just thought, *Oh, I'm here.*

I passed through the front gate.

School starts at 8:30, so I was forty minutes early. Plenty of time.

I sat on a bench. Drank some water. Breathed in the morning air while looking at the school building.

*This is so much better.*

Better than arriving barely alive after getting carsick on the bus. Walking for an hour and arriving relaxed.

It takes more time. It's tiring too. But it feels so much better.

---

When I walked into the classroom, Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Hey, why are you here so early?"

"I walked."

"Again? Seriously?"

Minseo asked with a shocked look.

"Yeah. On purpose this time."

"On purpose?"

"I didn't miss the bus. I just wanted to walk, so I walked."

Minseo had this blank expression for a moment.

"...Dude, you're actually crazy."

"Why?"

"You know it takes over an hour, and you walked on purpose?"

"Yeah."

"You're crazy, seriously."

Minseo shook his head. But the way he said it wasn't mean. More like... amazed?

"But why though? Is the motion sickness that bad?"

"It is bad. But that's not the only reason."

"Then what?"

I thought about it. Why did I walk? Because I hate motion sickness? That's true. But it's not just that.

"...I guess I like walking? I'm not really sure."

Minseo snorted a laugh.

"You like walking? Weren't you the kid who hated exercise?"

"That's true, but..."

"Oh man, you've changed."

Minseo said it teasingly. I laughed too.

Have I changed? Maybe I have.

---

I'm writing this diary, thinking about it.

Today was good. Walking was good.

I saw the cherry blossoms starting to bloom. I saw the morning dew. I saw the colors of the dawn sky. Things I never could have seen from the bus.

My legs are a bit sore. I'll probably have muscle pain again tomorrow.

But that's okay.

I'll walk again next Monday.

Actually, should I walk on Friday too?

I think I could do it twice a week. Monday and Friday.

Should I gradually increase it?

I don't know yet. For now, I'll take it slow.

Today was a good day.
