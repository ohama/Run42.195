# Monday, April 8, 2024

Clear skies. The canola flowers were fragrant.

---

Monday. Walking day.

It's become a routine now. Mondays and Thursdays, twice a week. The other days, I take the bus.

Mom set the rule. Twice a week max. No more than that, she said.

At first, I was a little frustrated. I wanted to walk more. But now I think she was right. Walking only on Mondays and Thursdays makes me actually look forward to those days.

Today was like that.

Starting Sunday night, I was already excited. Waking up tomorrow morning, walking, listening to the birds.

---

The alarm went off at 6:20.

I used to hate that sound. Didn't want to open my eyes. *Just five more minutes. Just ten more.*

But lately it's different. Monday mornings, I want to get up fast. Because I get to walk.

I washed up, ate breakfast, and left the house at 6:40.

"Be careful."

"Yeah."

Mom's gotten used to it too. At first, she'd follow me to the door and say all kinds of things. Now it's just a quick goodbye.

---

The canola flower scent was especially strong today.

Every time the wind blew, that sweet smell reached my nose. If I closed my eyes, I could picture waves of yellow.

I thought about it while walking. *Could I smell this on the bus?*

No way. Bus windows don't open. And even if they did, exhaust fumes or the smell of the person next to me would hit first.

This fragrance—you have to walk to smell it.

---

I met a grandfather at the tangerine orchard.

"Good morning."

"Oh, hello there."

He smiled and nodded. He was holding small pruning shears.

"What are you working on?"

"If too many flowers bloom, it's no good. Gotta thin them out."

"The flowers?"

"Yeah. If every single one becomes fruit, the tree gets exhausted. Need to remove some."

I looked at the tangerine tree. White flowers covered every branch. No wonder the scent was so strong.

"That must be hard work."

"It is. But it has to be done."

The grandfather said that. Then he looked at me.

"Same for you, right? Walking is hard?"

"...Yeah, a little."

"But you do it because you have to?"

"..."

I thought about it for a moment.

"I'm not sure if I *have* to. I just want to."

"Then that's even better."

The grandfather smiled.

"Doing something because you want to beats doing it because you have to. Keep at it."

---

On the way to school, I kept thinking about what he said.

*Doing it because I want to.*

I walk because I want to walk. Not because Mom makes me. Not because a teacher told me to. I walk because I like it.

Is that something special?

I don't know. But the grandfather said it was good, so it must be.

---

At lunch, Minseo asked me.

"Hey, do you seriously walk every Monday and Thursday?"

"Yeah."

"That's impressive, honestly. I can barely drag myself to the bus."

"You get used to it."

"You actually get used to it?"

"Yeah. It was hard at first, but now walking just feels... normal."

Minseo tilted his head.

"Normal?"

"Yeah. When I wake up Monday morning, I think, 'Today's a walking day.' It's not some big deal. Just routine."

"Oh... routine, huh."

Minseo looked like he was thinking about something.

"Maybe I should start a routine too. Like exercising or something."

"That'd be cool."

"But I don't know what to do."

"Try walking. With me."

When I said that, Minseo waved his hands.

"No way. An hour is way too long. I'll stick with the bus."

I laughed. That was fine. Walking is my routine.

---

I'm home now, writing in my diary.

Today was my fourth Monday walk. Combined with Thursdays, my eighth walk total.

It's a routine now.

Monday, Thursday. Leave at 6:40. Arrive at 7:50. One hour and ten minutes of walking.

It's hard, but it's okay. The good stuff outweighs the hard stuff.

The canola flower fragrance.
The birdsong.
The grandfather.
That feeling of freshness when I arrive.

Things you don't get on the bus.

I'm already looking forward to Thursday.

I wonder if the grandfather finished thinning the tangerine blossoms.
