# Thursday, April 25, 2024

Rain. A light drizzle.

---

It rained today.

Back in March when it rained, I took the bus. I thought there was no way I could walk. Walking over an hour with an umbrella seemed impossible.

But today was different.

I woke up and looked out the window. Rain was falling. A soft spring drizzle.

*What should I do?*

Should I take the bus? I hesitated for a second.

But I didn't want to. I didn't want to take the bus. Today was Thursday. A walking day.

---

I grabbed my umbrella and headed out.

Mom asked at the door.

"You're walking in the rain?"

"Yeah."

"Really? With an umbrella?"

"Yeah. It's just a drizzle. Should be fine."

Mom looked at me for a moment.

"... Be careful. It's slippery."

"Okay."

She let me go. Or rather, she didn't stop me.

---

I walked through the rain.

At first, it felt kind of strange. Holding an umbrella narrowed my field of vision. The sound of raindrops hitting the umbrella. Pitter-patter.

I watched my step. Walked around the puddles.

But.

It wasn't bad.

---

After about ten minutes, I got used to it.

The sound of the rain was actually nice. A steady rhythm. Raindrops falling on my umbrella. Pitter-patter-patter.

The air felt different. More humid and cool than usual. Like the dust had settled. Clean.

The rapeseed flowers were wet from the rain. Water droplets clung to the yellow petals. They sparkled.

*Pretty.*

A view I'd never have seen from the bus.

---

I was surprised when I reached the tangerine orchard.

Grandpa Yeongcheol was there.

Even in the rain, he was out in the field. Wearing a raincoat and rubber boots. Walking between the trees.

"Good morning, Grandpa!"

"Oh, Doyun. You walked in the rain?"

"Yes."

"That's impressive."

Grandpa smiled.

"Do you work even when it rains?"

"When it rains, there's work to do. Gotta check how the water drains."

"Ah..."

"Trees need water, but too much is bad. I'm checking on that."

Grandpa explained. So there's work to do even on rainy days.

"That must be hard."

"Well, I've been doing it my whole life. Isn't it the same with your walking?"

"Huh?"

"You want to walk even when it rains. That means you've come to like it."

---

I got to school.

My shoes were a bit wet. My socks were damp. But I felt good.

I sat on the bench and thought.

I walked even though it rained. Something I couldn't have done in March.

I've changed.

Before, I thought I had to take the bus when it rained. Obviously. But not anymore. I can walk in the rain. As long as I have an umbrella.

Of course, not in a downpour. But a drizzle? That's fine.

---

At lunch, Minseo asked.

"Hey, did you walk again today?"

"Yeah."

"In the rain?"

"Yeah. With an umbrella."

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"You're crazy, seriously. Who walks when it's raining?"

"I didn't want to take the bus."

"Why?"

"Just... I wanted to walk."

Minseo shook his head.

"Dude, you actually like walking now."

"I think so."

I admitted it. I walked even in the rain. That means I really do like it.

---

I took the bus home.

The rain had gotten heavier. Walking with an umbrella would've been tough.

I sat on the bus and looked out the window. Rain streaked past.

*Good thing I walked this morning.*

That's what I thought. I was glad I'd walked to school instead of taking the bus.

Getting wet was okay. Damp socks were okay. Because I liked walking.

---

Writing in my diary now.

I walked in the rain today.

A first. Walking on a rainy day.

It felt strange at first. Walking with an umbrella. Having to watch my step.

But I adapted quickly. I liked the sound of the rain. I liked the air. The rain-soaked scenery was beautiful.

Grandpa Yeongcheol said, "You want to walk even when it rains. That means you've come to like it."

I think he's right.

I've come to like walking. Enough to want to walk even when it rains.

It's strange. A month and a half ago, I never could have imagined this.

Next time it rains, I'll walk again.

As long as it's not a downpour.
