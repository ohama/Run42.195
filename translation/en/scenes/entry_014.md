# Thursday, April 18, 2024

Cloudy. There was fog.

---

Fog this morning.

When I stepped outside, the whole world looked hazy. I could barely see what was ahead. It was kind of magical.

I walked through the fog.

My footsteps sounded louder than usual. Other sounds seemed muffled, swallowed by the mist. Even the bird calls came from somewhere far away.

*I'd never feel this on the bus.*

That thought crossed my mind.

---

While walking, I started thinking about time.

Forty minutes by bus.
One hour and ten minutes on foot.
A thirty-minute difference.

I used to think those thirty minutes mattered. I'd think about what I could do with them. Sleep a little longer. Eat breakfast slowly.

But now, I'm not so sure.

Thirty minutes doesn't feel like a waste anymore.

---

Why doesn't it feel like a waste?

I thought about it while walking.

Forty minutes on the bus is just... forty minutes. Time spent sitting and enduring. Fighting the nausea, the smell, the stuffiness. Waiting for it to end.

But walking for seventy minutes is different.

I hear birds. See flowers. Feel the wind. Meet Grandpa Yeongcheol.

I don't hate that time. I actually like it.

Forty minutes I hate versus seventy minutes I enjoy.

Which is better?

The answer's obvious.

---

I ran into Grandpa Yeongcheol at the tangerine grove.

"Grandpa Yeongcheol, good morning!"

I called him by name. He smiled.

"Oh, Doyun. Foggy today, huh?"

"Yeah. It's so cool walking through it."

"Be careful. Cars can't see you."

"I will, I'll be careful."

He nodded. Then he pointed at one of the tangerine trees.

"Take a look. See the little ones?"

Tiny green buds hung from the branches. About the size of a fingernail.

"These turn into tangerines?"

"Yep. They'll grow by fall. That's when we pick them."

"That takes a long time."

"That's how it goes. Fruit takes time."

That's what he said. I stared at the tiny buds.

*Time.*

Maybe I'm like that too. I started walking and something's changing in me, but maybe it just takes time.

---

I got to school and sat on the bench.

The fog was starting to lift. Sunlight filtered through, soft and hazy.

*Seventy minutes.*

That's how long I walked today. But it didn't feel wasted.

I wondered why again.

Probably because I actually like doing it.

Time spent on things you like doesn't feel wasted. Like how gaming never feels like wasted time. Or watching YouTube.

Walking is the same. I like it, so it doesn't feel like a waste.

---

At lunch, I asked Minseo.

"Hey, do you ever feel like time is wasted?"

"Random much? What kind of time?"

"Just... time. Like when you're doing something and you think, this is such a waste."

Minseo thought about it.

"Hmm... math class?"

I laughed. Right. For Minseo, math class is wasted time.

"Why? What about you?"

"I used to feel that way about riding the bus."

"The bus?"

"Yeah. Forty minutes on the bus felt like such a waste. Like I was throwing time away."

"And now?"

"Now I walk. An hour and ten minutes. But it doesn't feel wasted."

Minseo tilted his head.

"It takes longer but doesn't feel wasted?"

"Yeah. Because I actually enjoy that time."

"..."

Minseo seemed to think it over, then nodded.

"Oh, I get it. If you like something, time doesn't feel wasted?"

"Yeah. I think that's it."

"Wow, that's deep."

Minseo laughed. I don't know if it's deep. It's just what I felt.

---

I'm writing in my diary.

Time well spent.

One hour and ten minutes. Thirty minutes longer than the bus. But it doesn't feel wasted.

During that time, I see good things. Feel things. Experience things.

Seventy minutes I enjoy beats forty minutes I hate.

It's not about the amount of time—it's about the quality. How you spend it. That's what matters.

Today I walked through fog. It was magical.

Grandpa Yeongcheol showed me the tangerine buds. He said they ripen in fall. That it takes time.

I'll probably take time too.

I don't know what I'll become.

But I'll take it slow.
