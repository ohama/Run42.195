# Sunday, November 24, 2024

Clear. Cold.

---

Half marathon race day.

21.0975km.

---

5 a.m.

My eyes opened.

Before the alarm even went off.

My heart was pounding.

---

6 a.m.

One banana. A little bread.

Kept it light.

Drank water too.

---

Dad drove me to the sports complex.

"Nervous?"

"Yeah."

"You got this. I'll be waiting at the finish line."

"Thanks, Dad."

---

Arrived at the stadium. 7 a.m.

So many people.

Way more than the 10km race.

---

Minseo was there.

"Doyun! You made it!"

Minseo waved at me.

"Yeah, I'm here."

"Of course. I came to cheer you on."

Minseo's mom said something.

"Run well. We'll be cheering for you."

"Thank you."

---

Pinned on my bib.

Number 3827.

Started stretching.

Loosened up my body.

---

7:50 a.m.

Headed to the starting line.

My heart was going crazy.

I looked around.

Hundreds of runners.

Everyone looked nervous.

*At least I'm not the only one.*

---

8 a.m.

The starting signal.

I started running.

---

First 5km nice and easy.

Like Coach said.

Getting excited and running too fast—that's how you blow up.

6:30 pace.

---

The scenery passed by me.

The coastal road.

I could see the ocean.

Heard the waves.

*It's beautiful.*

---

5km checkpoint.

33 minutes.

Right on schedule.

Feeling good.

---

10km.

1 hour 5 minutes.

Same as the 10km race.

But it doesn't end here.

I've still got half left.

---

At the 10km mark.

I spotted Minseo's group.

His little sister was there too.

"Oppa! You got this!"

"Doyun! You're doing great!"

I waved back.

Got a boost of energy.

---

12km.

My legs were starting to feel heavier.

But that's okay.

I expected it.

---

15km.

1 hour 37 minutes.

Now I'm in unknown territory.

I've trained up to 17km, but never gone past that.

---

My legs are heavy.

My breathing's ragged.

Pace is slowing down.

From 6:30 to 7:00 per kilometer.

It's fine. Just keep moving slowly.

---

17km.

1 hour 58 minutes.

I've been here before.

Now it's really new.

---

18km.

My legs weren't listening anymore.

*Should I just stop?*

The thought flashed through my head.

---

No.

I came this far.

Eight months of training.

I can't give up now.

---

19km.

One step.

One step.

Keep going.

---

20km.

2 hours 18 minutes.

*One more kilometer. Just one more.*

---

The finish line came into view.

I heard the crowd cheering.

---

I gave it everything I had left.

I ran.

Toward the finish line.

---

21.0975km.

Finished.

2 hours 24 minutes 38 seconds.

---

I stopped.

Gasping for breath.

My legs were shaking.

But I was smiling.

*I did it.*

---

They gave me the finisher's medal.

Bigger and heavier than the 10km medal.

I hung it around my neck.

---

Dad came running over.

"Doyun! You did it!"

He pulled me into a hug.

"I was watching you. That last stretch looked brutal."

"It was. Really was."

"But you finished."

I felt tears coming.

---

Mom showed up too.

"You did great. I'm so proud."

My little sister said something.

"Oppa, that was so cool!"

---

Minseo's group came over.

"Yo! You finished!"

Minseo grabbed my hand.

"We saw you at the 10km mark. You looked like you were struggling, but you powered through."

"Yeah. After 18km, it got really hard."

"But you finished. That's amazing."

---

On the drive home.

In the car, I kept touching the medal.

Feeling its weight.

Eight months of weight.

---

Back in my room, writing this now.

Half marathon finished.

21.0975km.

2 hours 24 minutes 38 seconds.

It was hard.

After 17km, my legs weren't working right.

At 18km, I wanted to quit.

*Should I just stop?* That thought hit me.

But I didn't.

One step.

One step.

I kept moving forward.

And crossed the finish line.

I did it.

The me from March who got carsick on the bus.

The me who couldn't even walk 7km.

That me just ran 21km.

Eight months.

A lot happened.

Ran in the rain.

Ran in the heat.

Hit a slump.

But I didn't give up.

That's how I got here.

The finisher's medal feels heavy.

Good heavy.

What's next?

I don't know yet.

For now, let me just rest.

For now, let me just be happy about this.

21km finished.

I did it.
