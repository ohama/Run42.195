# Monday, September 23, 2024

Clear skies, autumn weather.

---

Today's the day I run the full race distance.

10km full course tempo run.

I'm going to test my pace strategy.

---

5 a.m.

Strapped on my running watch and headed out.

Deep breath.

*Take it slow at the start.*

---

1km.

Checked the watch. 5:15.

Good. Started nice and slow like planned.

My body's not warmed up yet. My legs feel stiff.

But I held back. Fought the urge to speed up.

2km.

5:12. Total time: 10:27.

Good. This is still warm-up pace.

---

3km is where I picked up the pace.

5:02. Almost perfect.

4km. 5:00.

5km. 4:58.

Watching the watch while running helps me control my speed.

*Gotta remember this feeling.*

I need to train my body to understand what 5 minutes per km feels like.

---

6km.

Halfway there.

My breathing's getting harder. But it's still manageable.

5:03. Slowed down just a bit.

*Focus.*

7km. 5:01. Back on pace.

---

8km.

Here's where I kick it in.

Time to dig deep.

4:52.

My legs are getting heavy. My breathing's getting rough.

But I didn't stop.

9km. 4:48.

A little more. A little more.

---

10km.

The final stretch.

All-out sprint.

My legs are screaming. My lungs feel like they're about to burst.

But I kept going.

Eyes forward.

The finish line—well, the utility pole I'd marked—came into view.

I squeezed out the last bit of energy.

---

I stopped.

Checked the watch.

49:32.

*I broke 50 minutes!*

I laughed while catching my breath.

For the first time, I'd run 10km in under 50 minutes.

---

I checked my split times.

| Segment | Pace |
|---------|------|
| 1km | 5:15 |
| 2km | 5:12 |
| 3km | 5:02 |
| 4km | 5:00 |
| 5km | 4:58 |
| 6km | 5:03 |
| 7km | 5:01 |
| 8km | 4:52 |
| 9km | 4:48 |
| 10km | 4:21 |

I ran the final km in 4:21.

My kick worked perfectly.

---

Arrived at school.

As I was cooling down, I thought about my teacher.

It's because of the running watch. Watching the timer while running helped me control my pace.

*Thank you, teacher.*

The words came out naturally.

---

After school.

I reported to my teacher.

"Teacher! I did 10km in 49:32 today!"

My teacher smiled.

"Oh, you broke 50 minutes. That's impressive."

"Watching the watch while running really helped me control my pace."

"Exactly. If you can do it in practice, you can do it in the race."

"Yes!"

"But you might run too fast at the start because of race day nerves. Be careful."

"Okay. I'll start slow."

---

I'm home now writing this diary.

Today: 10km in 49:32.

I broke the 50-minute barrier for the first time.

My pace strategy worked.

Start slow, hold steady in the middle, kick hard at the end.

If I run like this on race day, I can do it.

I feel confident now.

I can do this.

D-12.
