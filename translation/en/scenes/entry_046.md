# Monday, August 12, 2024

Clear skies, but cooler in the evening.

---

On Saturday, Minseo and I went running together. Something special.

He suggested it to celebrate my 10km milestone.

"Hey, want to try going further than usual?"

---

We met at the park at 7 PM.

Right when the sun was setting. The sky was shifting from orange to purple.

Minseo had brought a water bottle and a towel.

"Wow, look at you being prepared."

"Of course. We're gonna be out there a while."

---

We set 5km as our goal.

For Minseo, that's a long distance. He usually does 3km.

"You good with that?"

"Yeah. Let's do it."

Minseo nodded.

---

We started running together.

I matched his pace. It was a bit slower than my usual speed, but it felt right.

It was different from running alone.

*Someone's there beside you. You're both heading the same direction.*

It felt reassuring.

---

At the 2km mark.

Minseo started breathing hard.

"You okay?"

"Yeah... just... a bit longer..."

"Go slow. No rush."

I eased up the pace. Minseo kept up.

---

3km.

Minseo looked like he was really struggling. But he didn't stop.

"Want to walk for a bit?"

"No... just... a little more..."

Minseo gritted his teeth. I knew that expression. The face of someone who doesn't want to quit.

---

4km.

Minseo suddenly said something.

"Hey, this is weird."

"What is?"

"It's hard, but... I feel good."

I laughed. *Runner's high.*

"That's runner's high. It's when your brain releases good chemicals."

"Really? Is that what this is?"

"Yeah."

Minseo laughed too. Struggling and smiling at the same time.

---

5km.

We made it.

Minseo collapsed, breathing heavily.

"Man... that was tough..."

"You did great. You just ran 5km."

"Really?"

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Did I actually run 5km?"

"You did."

Minseo smiled. Face drenched in sweat.

"No way. Five kilometers..."

---

On the way back, we walked and talked.

"Running with someone is better than running alone," Minseo said.

"Why?"

"Because you're there. When I wanted to quit, I could keep going."

"..."

"If I'd been alone, I would've walked at 3km. But because you were there, I could keep running."

His words hit me. *Really hit me.*

It was the same for me. Running with Minseo felt different. Better.

---

Writing this now.

Saturday. Minseo and I ran 5km together.

His first time completing 5km.

Running alone and running with someone are not the same thing.

When you run alone, you set your own pace. You're free.

When you run with someone, you have to adjust. But there's something solid about it.

When you want to quit, having someone beside you makes you push through.

That's what happened to Minseo. I think it happened to me too.

Running with a partner.

It matters.

Thanks, Minseo.
