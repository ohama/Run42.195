# Monday, May 6, 2024

Clear skies. The day after Children's Day holiday.

---

I overslept today.

Maybe because I stayed up late last night for the Children's Day holiday. I didn't hear my alarm and woke up at 6:50.

*Oh no.*

Thirty minutes later than usual. I need to leave at 6:40 to get there by 7:50.

If I leave now, I'll arrive at 8:20. Class starts at 8:30. That's cutting it close.

---

I washed up fast, scarfed down breakfast, and left at 7:05.

Twenty-five minutes behind schedule.

Walking won't cut it. What do I do?

Bus? If I head to the bus stop now, I can catch the 7:20 bus. That gets me there around 8:00.

Or.

*Should I try running?*

I remembered what Minseo said. That once I got used to walking, I should try running.

---

I started running.

The stretch out of the village. About 1km. Usually takes ten minutes on foot.

I moved my feet fast. Jogged. Ran.

My heart started pounding. My breathing grew heavy.

*This is hard.*

By the 500m mark, I was already struggling. My legs ached. I was out of breath.

But I didn't stop.

---

After running 1km, I walked.

Gasping for air. Heaving.

I checked my watch. 7:12. Seven minutes. Three minutes faster than usual.

*Three minutes.*

Doesn't seem like much, but it's three minutes. Running saved me three minutes.

I started walking again. Catching my breath.

---

I reached the tangerine orchard path.

Still breathing hard. But something felt strange.

I felt good.

It was exhausting, I was out of breath, so why did I feel good?

Something that came after running. Refreshed? A rush? I don't know what to call it.

But I definitely felt good.

---

Grandpa Yeongcheol wasn't in the field. Maybe it was still too early.

I just passed by.

I ran once more in the middle.

The stretch after the tangerine orchard, before entering the next village. About 500m. This time it was a bit easier. Compared to before.

---

I arrived at school.

8:15. I left twenty-five minutes late, but only arrived fifteen minutes late.

I saved ten minutes. Thanks to running.

I sat on a bench catching my breath. I was sweating. More than usual.

*I actually ran.*

I ran. Me, who only ever walked, actually ran.

About 1.5km. Out of the whole 7km route.

Not much. But I ran.

---

When I walked into the classroom, Minseo asked.

"Hey, why are you sweating?"

"I ran."

"You ran? While walking to school?"

"Yeah. I overslept."

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Oh, you finally ran?"

"Because of what you said. To try running."

"I said that? Oh right, I did. But you actually ran?"

"Yeah. About 1.5km."

"No way."

Minseo gave me a thumbs up.

"How was it?"

"Hard. Out of breath."

"And?"

"But..."

I thought for a moment.

"I felt good."

"You felt good?"

"Yeah. Feeling good even though it's hard, isn't that weird?"

Minseo nodded.

"Nah, that's runner's high, isn't it?"

"Runner's high?"

"When you run and feel good. Something gets released in your brain."

"..."

Runner's high. First time hearing that.

---

I looked it up at lunch.

Runner's high.

Apparently when you run, your brain releases endorphins. That's what makes you feel good.

*So that's what it was.*

The feeling I had after running this morning. That exhausted-but-good sensation.

Runner's high.

---

I thought about it on the bus ride home.

I tried running today. For the first time.

It was hard. I was out of breath. My legs hurt.

But I felt good. They call it runner's high.

I want to feel it again.

Maybe I'll try running again next time?

---

Writing this diary now.

Today I ran.

I ran because I overslept and had no choice. About 1.5km. Maybe 20% of the total distance.

It was hard. Out of breath, legs aching.

But I felt good. They call it runner's high. Running releases good chemicals in your brain.

It's weird. Feeling good even though it's hard.

But it really was good.

I should try running again. Even when I don't oversleep.

I want to run because I want to.

Walking started because I had no choice too. I missed the bus.

Running started because I had no choice. I overslept.

But I like them both.

It's strange. Me, who used to hate exercise.

But if it feels good, well.

Let's keep going.
