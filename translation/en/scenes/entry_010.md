# Thursday, April 4, 2024

Clear skies. Heard a lot of birds today.

---

Today I tried opening my ears while I walked.

Usually I just walk. Eyes forward, feet moving, breathing. But today was different. I focused on sounds.

The first thing I heard was my own footsteps.

Tap, tap, tap.

My sneakers hitting the asphalt. Steady and rhythmic. Left foot, right foot, left foot, right foot. The sound of my own steps.

*This is my sound.*

On the bus, I had no sound. Engine noise, other people's voices, phone notifications. All sounds that belonged to someone else. But now I can hear my footsteps.

---

Once I left the village, I started hearing birds.

Chirp chirp. Tweet tweet. Trill.

I don't know what kind of birds they were. But different kinds were singing all around me. From between the tangerine trees, from the power lines, from somewhere.

*Were birds always singing this much?*

I never knew before. You can't hear them on the bus. Only after I started walking did I realize. That mornings are full of birdsong.

I stopped and listened.

Closed my eyes. Opened my ears.

Birdsong. Wind. A car passing in the distance. My own breathing.

I could hear everything.

---

On the tangerine orchard road, the wind sounded different.

Whoosh.

The sound of tangerine leaves rustling. A soft breeze brushing against the foliage. A sound you don't hear in the city.

I walked slowly.

Wind grazed my face. My hair lifted. I could hear the wind right by my ears.

*Ah, this feels nice.*

You don't get this on the bus. Even with the windows open, the breeze doesn't reach you. Stuck between people, all you hear is the smell of sweat and heavy breathing.

Walking is different.

---

Grandpa wasn't there today.

The orchard was empty. He must have finished picking tangerines on Monday. I felt a little disappointed.

But instead of Grandpa, I heard another sound.

Bees.

The tangerine trees must be starting to bloom. Bees were buzzing all around. Small yellow ones. Flying between the flowers, making their sound.

Buzz. Buzzzz.

Spring is here. The bees are out.

You can tell the season by sound. That was kind of amazing.

---

I sat on a bench when I got to school.

The schoolyard was loud. Kids yelling, teachers calling, soccer balls bouncing. All kinds of sounds mixed together.

But strangely, it didn't bother me.

The sounds inside the bus were suffocating. People breathing, phone alerts, engine rumble. All mixed together, it gave me a headache.

But the schoolyard was different. Sound spreading through open space. No feeling of being trapped.

*Maybe it's the space.*

The bus is cramped. Sound gets trapped. But outside is wide open. Sound spreads out.

All the sounds I hear while walking spread out. Maybe that's why it feels good.

---

I asked Minseo.

"Hey, do you hear birds in the morning?"

"Birds?"

"Yeah. On your way to school."

"I don't know... not really? I take the bus."

Minseo tilted his head. Right. You can't hear them on the bus.

"Why? What about birds?"

"Nothing. It's just, when you walk, you hear them. Birds."

"Oh, that sounds nice. I just wear earbuds and listen to music on the bus."

That's what Minseo said. Music. I used to do that too. Earbuds in on the bus, listening to music.

But now I walk without earbuds. Mom told me not to wear them. At first I thought I'd be bored, but actually it's better. Instead of music, I hear birdsong.

---

I'm writing this diary now that I'm home.

Today I focused on sounds while walking.

My footsteps.
Birdsong.
Wind.
Bees.

All things I couldn't hear on the bus.

The world changed after I started walking. I see more, I hear more. Things I used to pass by without noticing now catch my eyes and ears.

I think this is what's good about walking.

Things you miss when you rush past. Things you can catch when you walk slowly.

Tomorrow is Friday, so I have to take the bus. Earbuds in, music on.

But I'm already looking forward to Monday.

I want to hear the birds again.
