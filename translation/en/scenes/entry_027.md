# Thursday, June 6, 2024

Rain. The rainy season has begun.

---

I woke up this morning to rain.

Not a drizzle. Real rain. Raindrops streaked down the window.

I checked the forecast. "Rainy season begins. Rain expected all week."

*Ugh...*

I wanted to run.

---

I took the bus.

It was raining too hard. I had no choice. Can't exactly run with an umbrella.

The bus was humid. Everyone's clothes smelled damp. The windows fogged up completely.

*So stuffy.*

This used to be normal. Now I can barely stand it.

---

I got to school and sat on a bench.

The rain kept falling. The field was covered in puddles.

*I want to run.*

The thought wouldn't leave my head. My body was restless. My legs wanted to move.

But it was raining. Nothing I could do.

---

Minseo asked me.

"Hey, did you take the bus today?"

"Yeah. Because of the rain."

"Makes sense. Can't run when it rains."

"Yeah..."

I must have sounded pretty down. Minseo laughed.

"Dude, you're this depressed over skipping one day?"

"I'm not depressed... I just want to run."

"You've really become a runner, haven't you."

---

At lunch, I looked out the window.

Still raining. No sign of stopping.

*They said rain all week.*

A whole week without running? The thought felt suffocating.

Back in March, when it rained, I just thought, *whatever, I'll take the bus.* But now it's different.

Running has become normal. Not running feels like something's missing.

---

I looked it up.

"Running in the rain."

A bunch of articles came up.

"You can run in the rain."
"Just wear a waterproof jacket."
"It's fine if your running shoes get wet. Just dry them out."

*Oh, so you can run in the rain.*

But they said not in heavy rain. Too slippery, too dangerous.

Light rain is okay, though.

---

On the bus ride home, I thought about it.

*What if it rains again tomorrow?*

I checked the forecast again. Tomorrow, rain. Day after, rain. Rain until early next week.

This is how the rainy season works. About two weeks of nonstop rain.

Two weeks without running?

*No, there has to be a way.*

I can run when the rain lightens up. Or find a waterproof jacket.

---

When I got home, I asked Mom.

"Mom, do we have a waterproof jacket?"

"A waterproof jacket? Why?"

"I want to run when it rains."

Mom looked at me.

"You're going to run in the rain?"

"I looked it up and it said you can run in the rain. If you wear a waterproof jacket."

"..."

Mom sighed.

"You really have fallen in love with running, haven't you."

"Yeah."

"We don't have a waterproof jacket. But only go out when the rain is light. Not in heavy rain."

"Got it."

---

Writing in my diary.

The rainy season has begun.

It rained today, so I took the bus. Felt suffocating. I wanted to run.

Looked it up and apparently you can run in the rain. With a waterproof jacket. As long as it's not pouring.

We don't have a waterproof jacket, but maybe I'll try running when the rain eases up.

It's rainy season, but it can't be pouring nonstop for two whole weeks. There'll be days with light rain.

I'll try running then.

I want to run.

Even if it's raining.
