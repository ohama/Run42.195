# Thursday, May 9, 2024

Clear skies. The wind was cool.

---

Today I ran on purpose.

I didn't oversleep. I woke up at the usual time. 6:20 a.m.

But I wanted to run.

That feeling from Monday. Runner's high. I wanted to feel it again.

---

I left the house at 6:40.

Same time as always. But today, I planned to run.

I started jogging once I left the village.

Slowly at first. Just a little faster than a brisk walk.

Then I gradually picked up the pace.

---

Running felt different.

When I walk, the scenery passes slowly. But when I run, it's fast. Things fly by in front of my eyes.

The wind brushed my face. My hair whipped around. My heart pounded.

*This feeling.*

It was that same feeling from Monday.

---

After running 1 km, I walked.

I was gasping for breath. Huff, huff.

It was hard, but it felt good. Just like Monday.

*So this really is runner's high.*

What I'd read online was true. Running makes you feel good. Something gets released in your brain.

---

I ran into Grandpa Yeongcheol at the tangerine orchard.

He spotted me and his eyes went wide.

"Doyun, did you run here today?"

"Yeah. A little."

"You're sweating a lot."

"Because I ran."

Grandpa nodded.

"That's good. You should run a lot while you're young."

"Really?"

"Yeah. When you get old, you want to run but you can't."

Grandpa smiled. I smiled too.

"Isn't it hard?"

"It is. But it feels good."

"Oh, yeah? Then it's right for you."

---

I ran about 2 km to school.

2 km out of the total 7 km. I walked the rest.

I checked the time. 7:42. Eight minutes earlier than usual.

Running is faster. Obvious, I know, but experiencing it for myself felt different.

Eight minutes.

I arrived eight minutes early. That means I could sleep eight more minutes. Or eat breakfast with eight more minutes to spare.

*Running has a lot of perks.*

---

I sat on a bench and drank some water.

I was sweating a lot. Way more than usual.

But it felt refreshing. Like something had drained out of my body. Cool.

*Running was this good?*

I really had no idea.

In elementary school, I hated running during PE. It was hard and I couldn't breathe. I just wanted it to end.

But now it's different. I run because I want to. Because I choose to run.

Being forced and wanting to are completely different.

---

I told Minseo about it.

"Hey, I ran again today."

"Again? Did you oversleep?"

"No. I wanted to."

Minseo paused.

"You wanted to?"

"Yeah."

"Man... you've really changed."

Minseo shook his head.

"The kid who hated exercise is running because he wants to."

"I can't believe it either."

"Is it the runner's high?"

"Yeah. I feel good after running. That's what I like."

Minseo thought for a moment, then asked.

"Maybe I should try it too?"

"What?"

"Running. Like you."

"Go for it. It's great."

Minseo laughed.

"But I can't run for over an hour. I'd die if I ran all the way to school."

"You don't have to run the whole thing from the start. I only ran 2 km. I walked the rest."

"Oh, really?"

"Yeah. You build it up little by little."

---

I'm writing my diary.

Running was this good?

I really had no idea.

In elementary school, I hated running. Because PE class forced me to.

But now it's different. I run because I want to. That's why it feels good.

Walking was the same way. At first, I walked because I missed the bus. But I kept doing it because I liked it.

Running is the same. At first, I ran because I overslept. But I'm running again because I like it.

Making my own choice changes everything.

Being forced and wanting to are completely different.

Grandpa Yeongcheol told me. Run a lot while you're young.

I think he's right. I should run while I still can.

Next Monday, I'll run again.

Even farther.
