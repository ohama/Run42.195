# Monday, September 9, 2024

Clear skies, cool breeze.

---

Autumn has come.

I felt it when I opened the door this morning.

The air is different. Not that humid, heavy summer air anymore. This is light, cool, refreshing.

---

Today's a long run day.

According to my training plan, I'm supposed to do 10km at an easy pace.

5 a.m. wake up. 5:20 a.m. takeoff.

---

I started running.

The moment my feet hit the ground, I knew.

*Today's going to be good.*

My body feels light. My legs, which felt heavy all summer long, feel like they could just fly.

---

I passed through the mandarin orchards.

The mandarins are still green. When autumn gets deeper, they'll turn orange.

A breeze came. So cool.

In summer, even when the wind blew, it was hot. It was just moving humid air around.

Now it's different. When the wind blows, it's actually cool. My sweat evaporates.

---

Grandfather Yeongcheol was working in his field.

"Grandfather! Good morning!"

"Oh, out running early?"

"Yeah, it's a long run. Slow and steady."

"Long run? What's that?"

"Oh, just running for a longer distance."

Grandfather laughed.

"Working hard, aren't you. When's that race again?"

"October 5th."

"Less than a month away now. You got this."

"Yes, sir!"

---

I kept running toward the school.

I passed my usual 7km school route and kept going. I looped around the park in front of school.

Total: 10km.

I checked my watch. 58 minutes.

Even though I ran slowly, almost an hour.

*That's fine. Long runs are about distance, not speed.*

My coach said so. Run at a pace where you can still talk. Your breathing shouldn't be ragged.

Today was exactly like that.

---

Arrived at school. 6:30 a.m.

Cooled down and washed up at the sink.

Looking in the mirror, my face is red. But a good kind of red.

*Autumn's the perfect time for running.*

In summer, I'd be completely wiped out after just 5km. Sweat would pour down like rain, and I'd be breathing hard.

Now I can run 10km and still have energy left.

Is it the season? Or am I getting stronger? Probably both.

---

During class.

I looked out the window. The sky is so high.

*That's an autumn sky right there.*

Summer skies were packed with puffy clouds. Heavy, humid, like rain could come any second.

Autumn skies are different. High, clear, endless.

I should look at that sky while I'm running.

---

Lunch time.

Minseo asked me.

"You're running Saturday, right?"

"Yeah."

"Where to?"

"What about the coastal road? We can see the ocean while we run."

"Oh, I like that. How many kilometers?"

"Around 5km?"

"Cool. I still can't do 10km yet."

"That's fine. You'll get there eventually."

Minseo laughed.

"Dude, you sound just like the coach."

---

After school.

I thought about running home, but I took the bus instead.

I already did 10km this morning, so I need to rest in the afternoon. My coach said recovery is part of training too.

Looking out the bus window.

I could see rice fields. The rice is turning golden, ripening.

*It's harvest season.*

The seasons are changing.

When I first walked this road back in March, it was covered in yellow canola flowers.

Now the rice is ripening, and soon the mandarins will turn orange.

---

Home now, writing this entry.

Autumn has come.

This morning, the moment I opened the door, I knew.

The air was different. Light, cool, refreshing.

I ran 10km in 58 minutes.

A time I couldn't have imagined back in summer.

My body felt light. My legs felt like they could fly.

So this is autumn.

They say it's the best season for running, and man, they're right.

26 days till the race.

I'm going to keep this form and train hard.

Running under an autumn sky feels really, really good.
