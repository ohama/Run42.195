# Thursday, September 12, 2024

Clear skies, morning fog.

---

This morning.

I ran to school and the fog was thick. Really thick.

Couldn't see more than ten meters ahead. Everything was blurry.

At first, it freaked me out a little. *What if a car doesn't see me?*

But there's barely any traffic at this hour. It's only 5:30 a.m.

So I just ran slower.

---

Past the mandarin groves and up the hill.

There's a field of silver grass at the top.

In the fog, the grass swayed like it was dancing.

*Wow.*

I stopped.

The silver grass flowers were blooming white. In the fog, they looked almost dreamlike.

A year ago, I could only see this view through the bus window.

Back then, I had no idea it could look this beautiful when you got close.

---

I paused and took a photo.

Pulled out my phone. *Click.*

Silver grass in the fog. That blue light just before the sun rises.

*I should send this to Minseo.*

I like seeing views like this while running.

Things you'd never see from inside a bus.

---

I started running again.

Coming down the hill, cosmos flowers were everywhere.

Pink, white, purple.

The autumn flowers were just starting to bloom.

Back in March, there were rapeseed flowers. Yellow waves.

April was cherry blossoms. Petals drifting down.

Summer had sunflowers.

Now there's silver grass and cosmos.

Different views every season as I run.

That's another reason why I love this.

---

Got to school.

Cooled down and sent the photo to Minseo.

[Photo: Silver grass in the fog]

"Where is this?"

"My route to school. Up on the hill."

"No way. There's a place like that there?"

"You can't see it from the bus."

"lol are you just advertising running now"

"No, seriously, it's actually beautiful"

"Yeah, true. Let's go there on Saturday."

"k"

---

During class.

I looked out the window. The fog had cleared and the sky was bright blue.

I was thinking about the silver grass in the morning fog.

*The camera doesn't catch all of it.*

You have to see it in person. The air, the smell, the feeling.

It's even better when you're running through it. Heart pounding, breathing hard, and seeing the landscape in between.

It's completely different from sitting on a bus watching it go by.

---

Lunchtime.

Minseo brought it up.

"Hey, that photo you sent was seriously beautiful."

"Right? Even prettier when there's fog."

"You've totally become a photographer lately."

"Nah. I just see stuff when I'm running."

"I'm jealous. I want to see things like that."

"I told you—let's go together on Saturday."

"Yeah, let's go see the silver grass."

---

After school.

I rode the bus home and looked out the window.

The route I ran this morning passed by.

I could see the silver grass field. From the bus, it just looked like a field.

Totally different from how it looked in the fog this morning.

*Yeah, you gotta actually go there and experience it.*

---

Writing this at home now.

This morning, I saw the silver grass in the fog.

The flowers were blooming white. Dreamlike in the mist.

A year ago, it was just a view through a bus window.

Back then, it was just scenery passing by.

It's different now.

I run right through it. I stop and take photos.

I breathe the air, smell it, feel the wind.

Ever since I started running, the world looks different.

Same streets, but completely different somehow.

I'm going to the silver grass field with Minseo on Saturday.

I think it'll be better seeing it together.

D-23.
