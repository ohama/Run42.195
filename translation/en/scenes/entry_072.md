# Thursday, November 14, 2024

Overcast. Chilly.

---

Ten days left.

The nerves are kicking in.

---

Morning.

Walking to school, I couldn't stop thinking about it.

*Can I actually run 21km?*

I've run 17km before.

But 21km? That's a first.

---

School.

I couldn't focus in class.

My mind kept drifting back to the race.

---

Lunchtime.

Minseo asked, "Hey, what's up? You look out of it."

"Oh, just thinking about the race."

"You nervous?"

"Yeah. A little."

Minseo laughed.

"You were like this before the 10km race too."

"It's different this time. 21km is really long."

"I know. But you can do it."

---

"Why are you so sure?"

"Because the guy who was dreading the bus back in March is running 17km now. That's proof."

"..."

"You've kept going this far. You can do it this time too."

"Thanks."

---

After school.

I ran into my coach.

"Coach, I'm nervous."

"Of course you are. It's your first half marathon."

"Can I really finish 21km?"

My coach said, "You ran 17km, didn't you?"

"Yeah."

"How did you feel when you hit the 15km mark?"

"It was hard. My legs weren't cooperating."

"But you kept going."

"Yeah."

"There's your answer. You pushed through even when it was hard. It'll be the same for 21km."

---

"The last 4km is going to be the toughest."

"I know."

"But as long as you don't give up then, it's fine. You can keep moving forward, even slowly."

"Okay."

"You can do this. You've done the training."

---

Evening.

I sat in my room thinking.

What Minseo said.

What my coach said.

Everyone says I can do it.

But I'm the one freaking out.

---

I'm in my room now, writing this.

Ten days left.

I'm nervous.

21km scares me.

But when I think about it—

Back in March, even walking 7km felt impossible.

Now I'm running 17km.

I was scared then too, but I did it.

I can do this too.

Actually, I'm going to do it.

In ten days.

21km.
