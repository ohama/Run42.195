# Thursday, August 8, 2024

Clear skies, at least the dawn was cool.

---

Today I challenged myself to run 10km.

And I did it.

---

I woke up at 5 a.m.

I was nervous. Today was the day—10km.

I'd mapped out the route yesterday. My usual 8km loop, plus one extra lap around the park on the way back. That should be about 10km.

I drank plenty of water and took off at 5:15.

---

The first 5km felt okay.

I ran like I always do. Familiar route. Familiar pace.

My breathing got heavy, but I could handle it.

*Five kilometers. Halfway there.*

---

From 6km on, things got harder.

My legs started feeling heavier. My breathing got rougher.

*6km. This is where I usually feel it on my 8km route.*

But this time, it's different. Beyond 8km is territory I've never run before. An unknown distance.

---

8km.

I've been here before. But today isn't the end here.

I could see the park entrance. One lap around the park is 2km.

*Let's do this.*

I headed in.

---

8.5km.

My legs felt stiff. My feet were heavy.

*This is tough.*

I remembered May, when I ran 7km for the first time. How I thought I was going to die at 4km.

It's similar now. Once I hit that 8km mark, my body sends out distress signals.

*But I made it through that too, didn't I?*

I clenched my teeth.

---

9km.

Halfway around the park. 1km left.

My breath came up to my throat. My legs were shaking.

*Just 1km. One more kilometer.*

I slowed down. More of a fast walk at this point. But my feet kept moving in a running motion.

I didn't stop.

---

9.5km.

I could see the exit. Almost there.

I pushed with everything I had left. I picked up the pace a bit.

My legs screamed. I ignored them.

---

10km.

I checked my watch.

58 minutes.

I stopped.

---

I was gasping for breath. Panting hard.

I put my hands on my knees. Sweat poured off me like rain. My legs were still shaking.

But I was smiling.

*I did it.*

10km. For the first time.

In March, even 1km felt impossible. In May, I thought I'd die after 7km.

Now I've run 10km.

---

On the walk home, I kept thinking about it.

*10km.*

That's not even a quarter of a marathon. Just 10 out of 42.195km.

But for me, it's huge.

Five months ago, I couldn't have imagined it.

---

When I got home, I told Mom.

"Mom, I ran 10km."

"10km? Really?"

Mom's eyes went wide.

"Yeah. It took 58 minutes."

"Wow... that's amazing."

She hugged me. Out of nowhere.

"My Doyun is amazing."

"Mom..."

I was embarrassed. But I was happy.

---

I texted Minseo.

*hey i just ran 10km*

*WHAT???? SERIOUSLY????*

*yeah 58 minutes*

*YOU'RE INSANE!!!!! NO WAY!!!!*

Minseo sent me like ten amazed emoji.

*yo im celebrating saturday with you*

*what do u want me to buy u*

*lol nah its cool. just run together ok?*

---

Writing this now.

10km complete.

58 minutes. It was hard. My legs got stiff after 8km and I could barely breathe.

But I didn't stop. I kept going all the way.

I did it.

It was my summer goal. A non-stop 10km run. Achieved.

What's the next goal?

Run 10km faster? Or a longer distance?

I don't know. Today I'm just going to celebrate.

10km.

I ran 10km.

I want to tell the version of me from March:

*You're going to run 10km in August.*

He'd never believe it.

I wouldn't have believed it either.
