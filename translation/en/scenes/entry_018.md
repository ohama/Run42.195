# Thursday, May 2, 2024

Clear skies. The citrus blossom scent was strong.

---

May is here.

I can feel the season changing. The morning air is different now. It was chilly back in March, but now it's warm. I haven't worn my padded jacket in a while.

When I stepped outside today, something smelled different.

A sweet smell. Flowers. It hit my nose.

*Citrus blossoms.*

---

The citrus flowers are in full bloom.

White flowers started opening in April, and now the whole orchard is white. The fragrance is thick. So sweet.

I took a deep breath as I walked.

The sweet scent filled my lungs. It made me feel good.

*So this is what May in Jeju is like.*

I didn't know this last year. In elementary school, I took the bus. Never noticed the citrus blossom scent.

I started walking, and now I notice.

---

Grandpa Yeongcheol was in the orchard.

"Good morning, Grandpa! The citrus flowers are beautiful."

"Oh, Doyun. The blooms turned out well this year."

"The fragrance is so strong."

"Really? I smell it every day, so I can't tell anymore."

He smiled.

"You stop noticing when you smell it every day?"

"That's right. Once you get used to something, you don't feel it anymore. Seeing you reminds me."

"Oh..."

*Do I have things like that too? Things I've gotten used to and stopped noticing?*

---

The walk to school has become too familiar.

When I first started walking, I kept thinking, *When will I get there?* But now I just walk. No particular thoughts. On autopilot.

Is that good or bad?

I think it's good. It's not hard anymore.

But I also feel like I'm missing something. That excitement from when I first started walking—it's gone.

---

I sat on the bench and thought.

Walking has become routine. Normal.

What's next?

Should I just keep walking like this? Twice a week, an hour and ten minutes each time.

It feels like enough, but also like something's missing.

I don't know.

---

At lunch, Minseo asked me.

"Hey, how long are you gonna keep walking?"

"How long?"

"Yeah. You gonna keep doing it?"

"I don't know... never thought about it."

I really hadn't. Never thought about how long I'd keep walking.

"Until graduation? Even in high school?"

"No idea. I don't even know which high school I'll go to."

"That's true."

Then Minseo said something.

"But hey, you've gotten pretty good at running too. I saw you in PE."

"Huh?"

"During the long-distance run, you actually ran the whole thing. You used to stop and walk halfway."

"Yeah, I think it's because of all the walking."

"So why not try running? Instead of just walking."

Minseo asked out of nowhere.

"Running?"

"Yeah. If walking's gotten too easy, maybe try running."

---

Running.

After lunch, I couldn't stop thinking about what he said.

He's telling me to run because walking got too easy?

Run for an hour and ten minutes?

Impossible. Absolutely impossible. Walking was hard enough at first. Running would be way harder.

But.

I ran six laps in PE class. Without stopping to walk.

That used to be impossible.

Now I can do it.

So what about my commute?

Maybe just part of it?

---

I'm writing in my diary now.

May is here. The citrus blossom scent is strong.

Walking has become too routine. Grandpa said the same thing. Once you get used to something, you stop noticing.

Minseo asked me. Want to try running?

I never thought about running. I thought walking was enough.

But Minseo has a point. If walking's become routine, maybe it's time to think about the next step.

Running.

Running the whole 7 km would be impossible. But just part of it?

I don't know.

But it seems worth thinking about.

Maybe next time I'm short on time, I'll try running.

Just a little bit.
