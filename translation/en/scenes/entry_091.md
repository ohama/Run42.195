# Monday, January 20, 2025

Clear skies. Cold, but clear.

---

Today's long run goal: 30km.

My longest distance ever.

---

4:30 AM.

Time to go.

30km takes over three hours.

I left early.

---

Started running in the darkness.

Turned on my headlamp.

---

10km.

1 hour 3 minutes.

The sun came up.

I switched off the light.

20km.

2 hours 8 minutes.

My legs were getting heavy.

---

25km.

2 hours 42 minutes.

I've done this distance before last week.

Now I'm in uncharted territory.

---

26km.

My legs stopped listening.

27km.

*Should I quit?*

The thought flashed through my mind.

No.

Just 3 more kilometers.

---

28km.

My pace slowed.

8-minute pace.

That's okay. Just keep moving forward.

29km.

*One more km.*

30km.

I stopped.

3 hours 18 minutes.

---

*I did it.*

I was gasping for breath.

My legs were shaking.

But I did it.

30km.

71% of a full marathon.

---

I walked home slowly.

Mom was worried.

"Are you okay? Your face is so pale."

"I'm fine. It was tough."

---

Evening.

I texted my coach.

"Coach, I ran 30km."

"Great work. How was it?"

"It was hard. At 27km I wanted to give up."

"But you didn't."

"No."

"That's what matters. Not giving up."

---

I came to my room and wrote this down.

30km complete.

3 hours 18 minutes.

My longest distance ever.

It was brutal.

At 27km I really wanted to stop.

But I didn't.

I went 3km further.

A full marathon still needs 12km more from here.

Can I do it?

I can.

Because I just did 30km today.

41 days until the race.
