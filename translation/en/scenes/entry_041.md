# Thursday, July 25, 2024

Clear skies. Hot, but manageable.

---

I'm out of the slump.

I felt it for sure. This morning.

---

The alarm went off at five.

I opened my eyes. *I should run.* That was my first thought.

Not like Monday, when I thought *maybe I'll take the bus.*

Washed up, ate breakfast, and headed out at 5:15.

---

When I started running, my body felt light.

That heaviness from Thursday was gone.

My legs moved smoothly. My heart pounded. Sweat poured.

*Ah, this feeling.*

The feeling that I love running. It came back.

---

I ran the full 7 km.

Didn't walk once. Ran the whole way.

43 minutes. A decent time.

I'd been worried my pace would drop because of the slump. It didn't.

---

I got to school and sat down on a bench.

Wiping off sweat, I thought about it.

Why did the slump happen?

Tired? The heat? Lost motivation?

Probably all of the above.

But the important thing is I got through it.

Taking the bus showed me. I need to run.

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"Hey, I escaped the slump."

"Oh, really? How?"

"Taking the bus made me realize. Running's still better."

Minseo laughed.

"Ah, that's why you took the bus Monday."

"Yeah. Wanted to compare."

"And the verdict?"

"Running wins. By a lot."

Minseo nodded.

"I feel lazy sometimes too. On Saturdays when I go to practice."

"Really?"

"Yeah. But I feel great after running, right? Thinking about that gets me going."

---

Slumps.

They say everyone goes through them.

I looked it up. Hitting a slump when you're exercising is totally normal.

What matters is not quitting completely.

It's okay to take a break. It's okay to take the bus. Just don't give up for good.

You just have to start again.

---

I thought of Old Man Yeongcheol.

I didn't see him Monday since I took the bus. Today I did.

"Good morning, sir!"

"Oh, Doyun. Haven't seen you in a few days."

"I took the bus on Monday."

"The bus? Why?"

"...I was in a bit of a slump."

He looked at me.

"A slump?"

"Yeah. When you don't feel like running."

He nodded slowly.

"Happens to everyone. Whatever you do, there'll be times you don't want to. What matters is getting back to it."

"Right."

"Good for you. You're running again."

---

I'm writing in my diary.

I broke out of the slump.

It was a rough week. Didn't want to run. *Why am I even doing this?* Thoughts like that.

But it passed.

Taking the bus made me realize. Running really is better.

Slumps happen to everyone. What matters is not quitting completely.

It's okay to take a break. You just start again.

Old Man Yeongcheol said the same thing. Everyone goes through it. What matters is getting back to it.

Summer break started.

Now I can run every day.

Time to give it my all.
