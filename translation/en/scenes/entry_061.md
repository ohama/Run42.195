# Monday, October 7, 2024

Clear skies. Autumn morning.

---

Saturday.

October 5th.

I ran my first race.

---

I woke up at 5 AM.

I didn't get much sleep, but somehow I wasn't tired. Must be adrenaline.

I showered, put on my running gear, and ate a banana.

---

6 AM.

The whole family got in the car together.

Dad was driving.

"Nervous?"

"Yeah."

"You'll be fine. Just enjoy it."

Mom chimed in.

"Drink plenty of water. There'll be water stations along the way."

My little sister said, "Fighting, oppa! I'll wait for you at the finish line!"

---

7 AM.

Arrived at Jeju Sports Complex.

There were so many people.

Just in the 10km category, they said there were over 500 runners.

I got my race bib. Number 347.

I pinned it on my chest and back.

---

7:30 AM.

Minseo showed up.

"Yo! Doyun!"

He was waving.

"Did you sleep?"

"Nah. Barely."

"I'm nervous just watching you."

"ㅋㅋ Thanks for coming."

"Of course I came. I'll see you at the finish line!"

---

7:50 AM.

We moved to the starting line.

Hundreds of runners were gathered there.

My heart was pounding like crazy.

My hands were shaking.

I checked my running watch. Fully charged. GPS connected.

*Start slow. Keep the pace.*

---

8 AM.

The starting signal went off.

I started running.

---

1 km.

Running through crowds of people. All packed together.

I checked my watch. 5:18.

Right on pace. Slow start as planned.

My body wasn't warmed up yet. That's okay.

---

2 km.

The crowd started to thin out.

I could finally run at my own pace.

5:10. Total time: 10:28.

Good. Still warming up.

---

3 km.

I picked up the pace.

5:02.

My breathing got a bit harder. But still manageable.

I could see the coastal road. The ocean was sparkling.

---

4 km.

5:00.

Perfect. I need to keep this pace.

Some guy next to me said, "Are you a student? That's impressive."

"Thanks."

I answered short, breathing hard.

---

5 km.

Halfway there.

I checked my watch. 25:12.

Right on schedule.

There was a water station. I drank a cup of water.

*Halfway done. You can do this.*

---

6 km.

My legs were starting to feel heavier.

5:05. A little slower.

*Focus.*

I got my pace back on track.

---

7 km.

5:01.

I ran while looking at the ocean.

*Beautiful.*

I couldn't believe I had the energy to notice the scenery at a race. Six months ago, I never could have imagined this.

---

8 km.

Time to kick it in.

I started using everything I had left.

4:55.

My legs started screaming. My breathing got rough.

But I didn't stop.

---

9 km.

4:50.

It's hard. Really hard.

*Just a little bit more. Just a little bit more.*

The sports complex was getting closer.

---

9.5 km.

I could see the complex ahead.

I could see the finish line.

I heard people cheering.

---

Last 500 meters.

All out sprint.

My legs felt like they weren't moving. But they were.

My lungs felt like they were going to burst. But I kept breathing.

Eyes forward.

Finish line.

Finish line.

Finish line.

---

I crossed the finish line.

I checked my watch.

48:47.

---

I stopped.

I was gasping for breath.

I put my hands on my knees.

*I did it.*

Tears came out.

I don't know why I was crying. I just was.

---

"Doyun!"

I heard Minseo's voice.

I looked up.

He came running over.

"Dude! You did it! That was insane!"

My family came from behind.

Mom was crying.

"Doyun, you did it. You really did it."

Dad patted my shoulder.

"I'm proud of you, son."

My little sister hugged me.

"Oppa's the best!"

---

I got my finisher's medal.

I put it around my neck.

It felt heavy. But it was a good kind of heavy.

---

I'm writing this diary entry tonight.

Saturday. I ran my first race.

10 km.

48:47.

I finished.

When I crossed the finish line, tears came.

I'm not sure why.

Happy? Relieved? Proud?

Probably all of it.

Seven months ago, I was the kid getting carsick on the bus.

Seven months.

From walking to finishing a 10km race.

My family all came. Minseo too.

We all celebrated together at the finish line.

I got my finisher's medal.

I put it on my desk.

Every time I look at it, I'll remember.

This feeling.

My first race.

My first finish.

10 km.

48:47.

I did it.
