# Thursday, October 10, 2024

Clear skies, crisp air.

---

Five days since the race ended.

It still doesn't feel real.

---

This morning.

The finisher's medal sits on my desk.

Every time I wake up, it's the first thing I see.

*Did I really run it?*

I touched the medal. It was cold and solid.

It's real.

---

I ran to school again today.

First time running since the race.

I took three days off to let my muscles recover.

My legs felt light.

7km felt so much easier than before.

*I ran 10km, so 7km seems short now.*

---

At school.

When I walked into the classroom, everyone clapped.

"Hey, you finished the marathon!"

"That's insane. That's seriously amazing."

"What was your time?"

"48 minutes 47 seconds."

"Wait, you did it in under 50 minutes?"

Minseo laughed from beside me.

"He was really fast. I saw it."

My face got hot.

---

My homeroom teacher said something.

"Kim Doyun, congratulations on finishing the marathon. That's impressive."

"Thank you, sir."

"Are you going to keep running?"

I thought for a moment.

"Yes, sir. I want to try even longer distances."

My teacher smiled.

"Good. I'm rooting for you."

---

Lunch period.

Minseo asked, "So, what's your next goal?"

"A half marathon."

"A half? How many kilometers is that?"

"21km."

Minseo's eyes widened.

"You're crazy. That's twice 10km."

"Yeah. There's a race in November."

"Are you seriously going to do it?"

"I'm thinking about it."

---

After school.

I went to find Coach Park.

"Coach, I'm returning your running watch."

"Thanks for using it. Did it help?"

"Yeah. It really helped me keep the right pace."

Coach smiled.

"48 minutes 47 seconds, right? That's incredible."

"Thank you, coach. It was thanks to you."

"No, that was all your hard work."

---

Coach asked, "Do you have a next goal?"

"A half marathon. 21km."

Coach nodded.

"Good. There's a race in November, right?"

"Yeah. Can I do it?"

"Why not? You can do it."

"Could you help me train again, coach?"

"Of course. Let's make a training plan together."

"Thank you!"

---

On the way home.

I looked out the bus window.

*21km.*

Double 10km.

Can I do it?

Six months ago, I couldn't even run 7km.

But now I'm running 10km in under 50 minutes.

So I should be able to do 21km, right?

---

I'm in my room writing this.

I looked at the finisher's medal again.

*Could I run a full marathon someday?*

The thought suddenly came to me.

A full marathon. 42.195km.

That distance is unimaginable right now.

But three months ago, I couldn't imagine 7km either.

And now I've run 10km.

So maybe one day I'll run 42.195km...?

That's still a long way off.

First, let me focus on the half marathon.

21km.

November goal.

A new challenge is beginning.
