# Thursday, June 13, 2024

Cloudy. No rain though.

---

Today I ran 5km without stopping for the first time.

Non-stop. 5km.

---

My original plan was just to run like usual.

Run parts of the 7km, walk when it gets hard. That kind of thing.

But today was different.

---

From the moment I started running, my body felt light.

I don't know why. Did I sleep well last night? Was I just in good shape?

Anyway, my legs were moving smoothly. I wasn't as out of breath. My legs didn't hurt.

1km. Fine.
2km. Still fine.
3km. A little tough, but manageable.

*I think I can keep going today.*

---

4km.

Normally this is where I start walking. 4km was my limit.

But today was different. I was breathing hard, but I didn't want to stop.

I kept running.

4.5km. Still okay.

*Let's push for 5km.*

---

5km.

I checked my watch. 28 minutes.

I stopped. Caught my breath.

*I did it.*

I ran 5km without stopping. For the first time ever.

---

My chest swelled with emotion.

My breathing was ragged. My heart was pounding. My legs were shaking.

But I felt incredible. Like I was going to lose my mind—in the best way.

*5km non-stop.*

When I first walked to school in March after missing the bus, even 1km was hard.

When I first tried running in April, I was gasping after just 500 meters.

When I attempted the full 7km route in May, I had to start walking at 4km.

But now I run 5km non-stop.

---

I walked the remaining 2km.

Caught my breath while walking slowly. Passed by the tangerine orchards. Grandpa Yeongcheol wasn't there.

While walking, I kept thinking.

*5km.*

Maybe it's not that impressive. Marathon runners do 42km.

But for me, it's huge. I couldn't have imagined this three months ago.

---

When I got to school, I sat on a bench.

Wiping off my sweat, I checked my watch. 28 minutes for 5km. 5 minutes 36 seconds per kilometer.

Not a bad pace. Still slow, but pretty decent for a beginner.

*Can I get faster?*

Probably. Once my endurance improves.

---

I told Minseo.

"Hey, I ran 5km non-stop today."

"Non-stop? Without stopping?"

"Yeah."

"Whoa. Seriously?"

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Dude, that's insane. Isn't 5km super far?"

"About 5 and a half minutes per kilometer. Took me 28 minutes."

"Wait... you ran for 28 minutes straight without resting?"

"Yeah."

Minseo shook his head.

"That's crazy. I can barely do 1km."

---

Over lunch, I thought about it.

5km non-stop.

What's the next goal?

7km non-stop? Is that even possible?

Or run 5km faster?

I want to try both.

---

Writing in my diary now.

Today I ran 5km without stopping.

28 minutes. Non-stop.

It was my first time. My chest swelled with pride.

Three months ago, even 1km was hard. Now I run 5km.

Growth. Definitely.

What's next? 7km non-stop? Or 5km under 25 minutes?

I want to try both. Slowly. Step by step.

Today I feel proud. Genuinely proud.

I can't believe I ran 5km non-stop.

I want to tell March-me about this.

*You're going to run 5km non-stop in three months.*

He wouldn't believe it. I wouldn't have believed it either.

But I did it.

Let's keep going.
