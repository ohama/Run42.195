# Monday, June 17, 2024

Clear sky. Hot.

---

This happened during PE class today.

The PE teacher called my name.

"Kim Doyun."

"Yes?"

"Have you been running a lot lately?"

---

My heart skipped a beat.

That feeling of being caught. I felt embarrassed, though I'm not sure why.

"... Yes, a little."

"A little? Looks like a lot to me."

The teacher smiled.

"I've noticed in PE class—your running has improved a lot. You've gotten faster in the 100 meters, and you're doing well in the endurance runs too."

"..."

"Have you been practicing on your own?"

---

I told the truth.

"I walk to school... actually, I run."

"To school? Where do you live?"

"In the Jocheon area."

"From there to here?"

The teacher's eyes went wide.

"How far is that?"

"7 kilometers."

"You run 7 kilometers?"

"Yes. About two or three times a week."

The teacher looked at me in silence for a moment.

---

"Impressive."

That's what the teacher said.

"7 kilometers is quite a distance. And you run it every week?"

"Yes."

"Since when?"

"I started walking in March, then started running in May."

"So it's been three months."

The teacher nodded.

"That explains why your stamina has improved so much. You're doing great."

"Thank you..."

I felt embarrassed. Being praised like that.

---

The teacher asked one more thing.

"Have you ever thought about joining the track team?"

"The track team?"

"Yeah. Our school's track team. We need long-distance runners."

I hesitated.

"... I just run as a hobby."

"I know. I'm not forcing you. Just let me know if you're interested."

"Okay..."

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"Hey, the PE teacher asked me to join the track team."

"What? Seriously?"

"Yeah. They said they need long-distance runners."

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Dude, that's huge. You got scouted?"

"I wouldn't call it scouted..."

"Isn't it basically the same? The teacher came to you first."

Minseo said excitedly.

"Are you gonna do it?"

"I don't know. I need to think about it."

---

I thought about it during class.

The track team.

Running for the school. Competing in meets.

Honestly, I'd never thought about it. I just run because I like it.

What would change if I joined the track team?

There'd be training. Every day. It'd be tough.

I'd have to compete in meets. I'd get nervous.

*Is that what I want?*

I'm not sure.

---

When I got home, I told Mom.

"Mom, the PE teacher asked me to join the track team."

"The track team? You?"

"Yeah."

Mom looked surprised.

"My Doyun, on the track team?"

"The teacher said my running has improved. They need long-distance runners."

"Wow..."

Mom smiled.

"The kid who used to hate exercise, getting invited to join the track team."

"I don't know if I'll do it or not."

"Take your time thinking about it. Do it if you want to."

---

I'm writing in my diary now.

The PE teacher asked me to join the track team.

I was surprised. Embarrassed too.

I just run because I like it. I'd never thought about joining the track team.

What would change if I joined?

Training, meets, a team.

Right now, I run alone. At my own pace, whenever I want.

If I joined the track team, that would change.

Is that good? Is that bad?

I don't know.

I need to think about it.

But it felt good that the teacher recognized me. That my running has improved. That I'm doing well.

It all started when I missed the bus in March.

Now I've been invited to join the track team.

Strange. Surprising.

But good.
