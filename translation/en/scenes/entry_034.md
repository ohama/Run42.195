# Monday, July 1, 2024

Clear skies. The heat wave begins.

---

July is here.

And so is the heat wave.

This morning at 5:30, it was already 27 degrees. It's dawn and it's still hot.

---

The moment I started running, I was sweating.

Less than a kilometer in, my back was soaked. My breathing felt heavier than usual.

The air was sticky. Must be the humidity.

*So this is what July feels like.*

Definitely different from June.

---

I had to stop at 3km.

My head was spinning. My body felt overheated. Warning signs.

I walked. Caught my breath.

Drank water. My bottle emptied fast.

*Better watch out for heatstroke.*

I remembered what I'd looked up before. Tips for running in summer. Don't overdo it.

Today felt like overdoing it.

---

I walked the remaining 4km.

Even walking was hot. The sun was scorching. I stuck to the shade.

By the time I reached school, I was completely drained.

I slumped onto a bench. Wanted more water, but my bottle was empty.

---

I went to the nurse's office.

Got some water and sat in front of the air conditioner.

The nurse asked me, "What's wrong? Your face is all red."

"I was running... it's so hot."

"You ran in this weather?"

"Yeah. But I stopped at 3km."

She nodded.

"Good call. You can't push yourself in weather like this. Heatstroke is dangerous."

"Yeah..."

"Drink plenty of water. Rest up today."

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"Dude, I could only run 3km today. Too hot."

"Really? Is it that bad?"

"Yeah. It was 27 degrees at dawn."

"Whoa... so can we still run on Saturday?"

Saturday. The day we planned to run together.

"... Should be fine if we go really early. Like 6?"

"Six? That's rough..."

"Then evening. After sunset."

Minseo thought about it.

"Evening's better. How about 7?"

"Yeah, let's do that."

---

On the bus home, I searched it up.

'Running in heat wave'

- Absolutely no running at midday
- Run at dawn or evening
- Drink water frequently
- Take breaks
- Stop immediately if you feel unwell

What I did today was right. Stopping at 3km.

If I'd pushed myself, I could've gotten heatstroke.

---

When I got home, I told Mom.

"Mom, I could only run 3km today."

"Why?"

"Too hot. I got dizzy."

Mom looked worried.

"Be careful. Heatstroke is dangerous. They talk about it on the news every day."

"I know."

"Don't overdo it. If you can't run, just rest."

"Okay."

Mom pulled out some watermelon from the fridge.

"Eat this. You need to hydrate."

---

Writing in my diary now.

July heat wave.

I only ran 3km today. It was 27 degrees at dawn. Way too hot.

I need to be careful about heatstroke. Can't push too hard.

But I don't want to stop running.

I need to find a way. A way to keep running even in this hot summer.

Run earlier at dawn. Run in the evening. Drink lots of water. Take breaks.

Saturday, Minseo and I are running in the evening. At 7.

It's our first time running together. Hope it won't be too hot.

July. Feels like this will be a month of trials.

But I have to get through it.

Once summer passes, autumn will come.
