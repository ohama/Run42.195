# Monday, October 14, 2024

Cloudy with occasional sun.

---

Half marathon. 21km.

I've set a new goal.

---

After school.

I worked out a training plan with my teacher.

"You've got about a month until the November race."

"Yeah."

"If you're jumping from 10km to 21km, you need to increase the distance gradually."

I took notes.

"Add to your long run once a week. This week 12km, next week 14km, the week after 16km..."

"Got it."

"You push too hard, you risk injury. Don't rush it, no matter what."

"I understand."

---

**Half Marathon Training Plan (4 weeks)**

| Week | Long Run | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| Week 1 | 12km | Building foundation |
| Week 2 | 14km | Getting used to distance |
| Week 3 | 17km | Extending range |
| Week 4 | 12km | Adjusting condition |

My teacher explained it.

"The week before the race, cut back the distance. You need to let your body rest."

"Yeah. We did that with the 10km race too."

"Exactly. Same principle."

---

On the bus ride home.

I was thinking about it.

*21km.*

That's more than double 10km.

Honestly? I'm scared.

Even with 10km, the last 2km was brutal. What about 21km?

---

Evening.

I told my family.

"I'm going to do a half marathon."

Mom asked, "A half? What's that?"

"21km. Half of a full marathon."

Dad was surprised.

"21km? That's like double 10km."

"Yeah. There's a race in November."

Mom looked worried.

"Isn't that too much for you?"

"No. My teacher made a training plan for me. I'm going to prepare gradually."

Dad said, "If Doyun says he's doing it, he can do it. You finished 10km, didn't you?"

"Thanks, Dad."

---

My little sister asked, "Oppa, how many hours is 21km?"

"Like two hours?"

"Whoa. You're running for two hours straight?"

"Yeah."

"That's insane..."

I laughed.

"I used to think the same thing."

---

I'm in my room now, writing this.

I set a new goal.

Half marathon. 21km.

I'm scared. Honestly.

10km was already hard, and now 21km?

But I want to try.

Back in March, even walking 7km was tough.

But now I can run 10km in under fifty minutes.

So shouldn't I be able to do 21km too?

My teacher made a training plan.

Four weeks. Slowly building up the distance.

No pushing it.

I can do this.

Actually, I'm going to try.

A new challenge.

Half marathon. 21km.

November goal.
