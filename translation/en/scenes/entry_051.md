# Monday, September 2, 2024

Clear skies, cool breeze.

---

First day of second semester.

Back to school after a week. Opening ceremony.

---

I ran to school again today. Up at 5, left at 5:20.

After running every day during break, 7km felt easy. Didn't even take 40 minutes.

I passed through the tangerine orchards. Yeongcheol's grandfather was working in his field. I waved at him.

"Heading to school?"

"Yes! Second semester starts today!"

"Work hard."

He laughed.

---

Arrived at school. 6:20.

The morning sun was hitting the courtyard. It was definitely cooler than August because it was September now.

I wiped the sweat off and washed my face at the sink.

I looked in the mirror.

*I've changed.*

Compared to who I was in March, I'm completely different.

My face looks different. My pale skin is healthy now.

My eyes are different too. The tired, motion-sick look is gone. My eyes are clear now.

---

I walked into the classroom.

Around 7. Nobody else was there yet.

I sat by the window and looked outside. The athletic field was visible.

*I'll be running there during PE class.*

Back then, long-distance running was torture. I'd get out of breath, my legs would feel heavy, and I'd always finish near the back.

It's different now. I actually look forward to PE.

---

Around 8, friends started trickling in.

"Hey, have a good break?"

"Yeah."

Minseo came in.

"Oh, Doyun! You ran here?"

"Yep."

"I heard you ran every day during break. That's seriously amazing."

"You ran with me on Saturdays."

"I only did 3km. You did 10km."

Minseo whistled.

---

Opening ceremony.

We gathered in the auditorium. Principal's speech.

The friend sitting next to me whispered.

"Hey, something's different about you."

"What?"

"I don't know. Did you get taller? Or something..."

"I've been exercising some."

"Oh, running? I heard. You're doing a marathon?"

"10km."

"No way."

---

We went back to the classroom.

My homeroom teacher spoke.

"Second semester is starting. Everyone had a good break?"

Responses came from all around.

The teacher looked at me.

"Kim Doyun, I heard you were training hard for a marathon during break?"

"Yes."

"Your PE teacher told me. Said you were really dedicated."

The whole class looked at me.

My face got hot.

---

Lunch period.

As I was heading to eat, some kids asked me.

"Hey, when's your marathon?"

"Early October."

"Are you nervous?"

"A little."

"We'll cheer for you."

It was unexpected.

Honestly, I was the kind of kid nobody really noticed in class. Not athletic, quiet.

But since I started running, something changed.

---

PE class.

Coach Park Jaehyun said.

"Alright, first PE of the semester. Do five laps around the field. Easy pace."

Five laps. 2km.

If it were the old me, it would've been rough.

I started running.

Steady. Controlling my breathing.

Lap one. Lap two.

Looking around, some kids were already struggling. Guess they didn't exercise much over break.

Lap three. Lap four.

I was fine. My heart was racing, but it wasn't hard.

Lap five.

I finished with the front group.

The coach nodded.

"Kim Doyun, you really kept up with your training."

---

Heading home.

I took the bus today. Bus ride home.

I looked out the bus window. The tangerine orchards passed by.

*One month until the race.*

October 5th. 10km.

D-33.

I was nervous. Excited.

---

I'm writing this at home.

First day of second semester.

I've changed. Really changed.

Friends said it.

"Something's different about you."

"Did you grow?"

"Your face looks different."

Six months ago. March.

There was me, suffering from motion sickness on the bus.

Pale face. Tired eyes. No stamina, no confidence.

It's different now.

I run 7km every day. 10km is no problem.

My skin looks healthier. My eyes are brighter. And most of all, I'm confident now.

Running changed me.

One month until the race.

I'll train harder.

10km. I'll finish it for sure.
