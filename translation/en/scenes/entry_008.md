# Thursday, March 28, 2024

Clear skies. A bit windy.

---

I walked to school today.

It rained on Monday so I couldn't walk then. That's why I really wanted to walk today. I checked the weather forecast last night and it said no rain. When I woke up and looked out the window, the sun was actually shining.

I left the house at 6:30.

---

When I passed by the tangerine orchard, the old man was there.

The same grandfather I met last Thursday. Today he was doing something among the tangerine trees again.

"Good morning."

I said hello first. The grandfather looked up.

"Oh, student. Walking again?"

"Yes."

"Today too?"

"Yes."

The grandfather nodded.

"Keep it up. Walking is good for you."

Then he went back to tending his trees.

It was a short conversation. But it made me feel good. "Walking is good for you." That's what he said.

---

At school, I talked to Minseo.

"Hey, you walked again today?"

"Yeah."

"You really do like walking, huh."

Minseo laughed. I cracked a smile too.

Do I like it? I'm not sure. But I don't hate it. It's way better than the bus.

---

The problem came at dinner.

I was home eating when Mom suddenly asked.

"Doyun, how have you been getting to school lately?"

"... What?"

"School. Are you taking the bus?"

I hesitated. Mom's expression was strange. Like she already knew something.

"... Sometimes I walk."

"Sometimes?"

"Yeah."

Mom set her chopsticks down.

"I heard from your aunt."

Ah. My aunt.

Aunt's house is about halfway between our place and school. She must have seen me.

"She said you've been walking in the mornings. Not just once, either. Multiple times."

"..."

"How many times have you walked?"

I counted on my fingers. The day I missed the bus and had to walk. The day I walked on purpose. The day I met the grandfather. And today.

"Four times...?"

"Four times?"

Mom's eyes went wide.

"I thought it was just once in a while. You've walked four times?"

"I took the bus when it rained."

"Obviously. You can't walk in the rain."

Mom let out a sigh.

---

"Doyun."

Mom looked at me.

"I understand that you want to walk. I know how bad your motion sickness gets. But..."

"But?"

"Walking for over an hour is too much. If you do that every morning, it'll wear you out."

"It is tiring, but I'm okay."

"You say you're okay, but I still worry."

Mom's voice rose a little.

"You're walking alone when it's still dark out. You pass roads with lots of cars. What if something happens?"

"I'm careful."

"Being careful doesn't mean nothing will happen."

That's what Mom said. I had nothing to say back.

---

The rest of dinner felt heavy.

Mom didn't say anything more. Neither did I. Just the sound of chopsticks.

After eating, I came back to my room.

I felt like she didn't understand me.

I want to walk. Walking is better than taking the bus. What's wrong with that?

I was frustrated. But Mom isn't wrong either. Walking for over an hour is hard. It is dark in the morning. And I do have to watch out for cars.

I know Mom is worried.

But I want to walk.

---

I lay there for a while, thinking.

What should I say to Mom?

"I like walking." I already said that. But it doesn't seem like enough. Why do I like walking? Why do I want to walk even though it takes over an hour?

I don't really know myself.

Because I hate getting carsick? True.

Because the air is nice? That too.

Because I like meeting the grandfather? Also true.

But it's not just those things. There's something more, but I can't put it into words.

---

I'm writing this diary and thinking.

Of course Mom worries. I'd probably worry too if I were a parent. A kid walking over an hour when it's still dark outside. You can't help but worry.

But I don't want to stop.

If I stop, I have to take the bus again. The packed bus. The smell. The nausea. That suffocating feeling.

Since I started walking, all of that is gone. My mornings feel refreshing. I don't hate the trip to school anymore.

I don't want to give that up.

How do I explain it to Mom?

"I'll be careful." I already said that.

"I'm fine." Said that too.

What else can I say to make her understand?

I don't know.

Tomorrow is Friday, so I have to take the bus anyway. I'll think about it again after the weekend, on Monday.

But I still want to walk on Monday.

Even if Mom says no.
