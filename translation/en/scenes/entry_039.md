# Thursday, July 18, 2024

Clear skies. Hot.

---

A strange thought hit me today.

*Why do I run?*

---

When I woke up this morning, my body felt heavy.

I was tired. Stayed up late last night.

The alarm went off at 5:00. I turned it off and went back to sleep. It went off again at 5:30. Turned it off again.

I finally got up at 6:00.

---

I debated whether to go run or not.

Tired. Body feels heavy. It's hot.

*Taking the bus would be so much easier.*

That thought crossed my mind.

The bus. The packed bus. The nausea.

...But if I took the bus, I could sleep thirty more minutes.

*Maybe I'll just take the bus today.*

---

I ended up going out to run.

But my heart wasn't in it.

The whole time I ran, I kept thinking, *Why am I running?*

In March, I started walking because of bus sickness. In May, I overslept and started running.

That's how I got here.

But why do I keep going?

---

Around 3 km in, I stopped.

I was out of breath, sure, but more than that—my motivation just died.

*I don't know.*

I don't know why I run.

At first, it was because of motion sickness. But now I don't have to take the bus. It's summer break.

So why am I still running?

---

I walked the rest of the way to school.

The remaining 4 km. I thought about it as I walked.

*Because I like running?*

True. I do like it. But on days like today, it's just hard.

*Because it's making me healthier?*

That's true too. But is that reason enough?

I don't know.

---

I couldn't stop thinking about it at school either.

Why do I run? Why do I do something that's so hard?

Wouldn't it be easier to just live comfortably? Take the bus, sit in the air conditioning.

*Wouldn't that make me happier?*

---

I didn't say anything to Minseo.

At lunch, Minseo told me he bought running shoes.

"Hey, I got running shoes! White ones."

"Oh, nice."

"Let's wear them and run together on Saturday."

"Yeah."

Minseo was excited. I played along.

But something felt heavy inside.

---

On the bus home, I thought about it more.

*Am I in a slump?*

I've heard about that. When you're exercising and suddenly lose all motivation.

When you start asking yourself, *Why am I even doing this?*

Maybe that's what's happening to me.

---

Writing this diary now.

Today was strange.

The thought kept coming: *Why do I run?*

I was tired this morning. Didn't want to run. Kept thinking how much easier the bus would be.

I ran anyway. But I stopped at 3 km and walked the rest.

I think I'm in a slump.

Four months of walking and running, and suddenly my motivation is just... gone.

Why do I do this? What's the point of making myself suffer?

I don't know.

What about tomorrow? What if I feel like this tomorrow too?

For now, I should just sleep.

I'm tired.
