# Monday, May 20, 2024

Cloudy. Humid.

---

Today I tried to run the full 7km again.

I made it on Thursday, so I figured today would be fine too.

It wasn't.

---

The start was okay.

I left the house at 6:20. Same time as Thursday. I thought my condition was fine.

I started running.

1km. Fine.
2km. Fine.
3km. Getting tough.

And then 4km.

---

At 4km, my legs stopped.

Thursday was tough at 4km too. But I pushed through. Slowed my pace and kept going.

Today I couldn't. My legs wouldn't move.

I walked.

*Damn...*

I failed. Failed to run the full 7km.

---

I walked the remaining 3km.

Ran a bit, walked a bit. Ran 300 meters, walked 200.

When I got to school, it was 7:25. Took me an hour and five minutes.

The same distance that took 45 minutes on Thursday. Twenty minutes longer today.

---

I sat on a bench and thought.

*Why couldn't I do it?*

I did it on Thursday. Why not today?

The weather? Was it the humidity?
My condition? Was I feeling off?
Fatigue? Still not recovered from running 7km on Thursday?

I don't know. Probably all of the above.

---

I didn't tell Minseo.

At lunch, Minseo asked.

"Hey, did you run the 7km again today?"

"No."

"Why not?"

"...I walked partway."

"Oh, really?"

Minseo said it like it was nothing.

"That happens. You can't do it every time."

"..."

Minseo made it sound like no big deal. But I felt uneasy about it.

I thought since I succeeded Thursday, I'd keep succeeding. But I didn't.

---

During class, I stared out the window.

*Doing it once doesn't mean you can do it forever.*

It's obvious when you say it. But experiencing it firsthand felt different.

Just because you're good at running in PE doesn't mean you nail it every time. There are good days and bad days.

Today was a bad day.

*It's okay.*

That's what I tried to tell myself.

I succeeded once on Thursday. That's what matters. If you can do it once, you can do it again.

I just have to try again next time.

---

On the bus home, I searched online.

"Overcoming running failure"

A bunch of posts came up. Running community stuff.

"Failure is normal."
"There are good days and bad days."
"What matters is not giving up."

Reading them made me feel a bit better.

Everyone fails. Even pro athletes have bad days.

I'm not the only one.

---

When I got home, I told Mom.

"Mom, I walked partway today."

"During the 7km?"

"Yeah. At 4km my legs just stopped."

Mom nodded.

"Of course. You can't run 7km every single time."

"Really?"

"Yeah. Even pros have bad days. It's totally normal that you couldn't do it."

That's what Mom said. Similar to what Minseo said.

"Just try again next time."

"Okay."

"Don't push too hard. Listen to your body."

---

Writing this diary now.

I failed today.

The full 7km run. My legs gave out at 4km.

I made it on Thursday but not today. It bothered me.

But Minseo, Mom, and all those posts online said the same thing.

"Failure is normal."
"There are good days and bad days."
"Just try again next time."

I think they're right.

Succeeding once doesn't mean you'll succeed forever. Sometimes you fail.

What matters is not giving up.

I'll try again next Thursday.

It's okay to fail. I can always try again.

I still have a long way to go. But that's okay.

One step at a time.
