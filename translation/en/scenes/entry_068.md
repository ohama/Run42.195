# Thursday, October 31st, 2024

Clear skies. Halloween.

---

I ran 17km today.

My last long run before the race.

---

5 in the morning.

I took off.

Today I went all the way to the end of the coastal road and came back.

8.5km out, then the turnaround.

---

1km, 2km, 3km...

My body was warming up nicely.

5km.

I still felt good.

---

7km.

My usual distance to school.

No problem.

---

8.5km.

The turnaround.

I turned around.

*Here's where it really starts.*

---

10km.

My breathing got a bit rough.

12km.

My legs started to feel heavy.

15km.

This is hard.

But I didn't stop.

---

16km.

*Just a little more. One more kilometer.*

17km.

I stopped.

1 hour 42 minutes.

---

*I did it.*

I was standing there, breathing hard.

17km.

Only 4km left until 21km.

---

I got home and showered.

My legs were shaking.

But I felt proud.

---

School.

During lunch, I told Minseo about it.

"I ran 17km this morning."

"Seriously? That's crazy."

"It was tough. Once I got past 15km, it was really hard."

"But you did it."

"Yeah. It was my last long run before the race."

---

Minseo asked me.

"When's the race?"

"November 24th."

"So you've got like three weeks left."

"Yeah. Now I need to cut back on distance and work on my condition."

"Got it. I'll cheer you on."

---

After school.

I reported to my coach.

"Coach, I ran 17km today."

"Oh, nice work. Was it hard?"

"Yeah. Once I got past 15km, it was really tough."

"Of course. That's uncharted territory for you."

"But I did it."

My coach smiled.

"You'll be able to do it in the race too. You just need to run 4 more kilometers."

---

"Next week, start cutting back the distance."

"Yes, sir. I'll stick to the plan."

"You need to let your body rest. Condition is everything."

"I understand."

---

Evening.

I told my family about it.

"I ran 17km today."

Dad was impressed.

"17km? That's amazing."

"It was hard. But I made it."

Mom said, "Don't push too hard. You can't get injured before the race."

"I know. I'm cutting back on distance from now on."

---

I'm in my room writing this now.

I ran 17km today.

My last long run before the race.

1 hour 42 minutes.

It was hard.

Once I got past 15km, my legs stopped listening to me.

But I kept running to the end.

Only 4km left until 21km.

Someone said those last 4km would be the hardest.

I know that.

But I'm going to do it anyway.

In three weeks.

November 24th.

The half marathon.

I'm ready.
