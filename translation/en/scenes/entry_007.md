# Monday, March 25, 2024

Rain. It's been falling since morning.

---

I was going to walk today, but it rained.

As soon as I opened my eyes, I looked at the window. I could hear the rain. When I pulled back the curtain, everything outside was gray. It was coming down pretty hard.

*Ah.*

I sighed.

I was going to walk and see that grandfather. The one I met on Thursday. I wanted to see him again.

When it rains, I can't walk. Holding an umbrella for an hour and ten minutes isn't realistic. My clothes would get soaked, my bag would get soaked, my textbooks would get soaked.

I had to take the bus.

---

I walked to the bus stop with my umbrella.

Minseo was already there.

"Hey, it's raining today. You were gonna walk, right?"

"Yeah..."

"That sucks."

Minseo said that after looking at my face. Do I really show it that much?

"Can't help it when it rains."

"Yeah. Let's take the bus."

The bus came. Monday morning. Packed, of course.

I folded my umbrella and climbed on. Water dripped from the wet umbrella. The floor was slippery. I got wedged between people.

The smell.

That rainy day smell. Wet clothes, damp air, sweat. All of it mixed together, making it worse. The windows were shut too. Because of the rain.

My stomach started churning.

*Ah, here we go again.*

I closed my eyes. Pulled out a peppermint candy and popped it in my mouth. The ones Minseo gave me. I still had a few left.

The mint spread across my tongue. It helped a little. But the nausea didn't completely go away.

---

At school, I sat on a bench.

It was raining, so I sat under the covered area. I could hear the rain falling. Cold air touched my face.

I took a deep breath.

The breath I couldn't take on the bus. Now I could. The air was fresh. It smelled like rain. Like earth too.

*I wanted to walk.*

That thought kept coming back.

If it hadn't rained, I'd be passing through the tangerine orchards right now. I might have seen the grandfather. Could have said hello: "Good morning, I'm heading to school."

But it rained.

Nothing I can do about it. I can't control the weather.

---

During class, I looked out the window.

The rain kept falling. Puddles formed on the field. No one went outside.

*I wish it would stop.*

That's what I thought.

Before, when it rained, I'd just think "oh, it's raining." Neither good nor bad.

But now it's different.

When it rains, I can't walk. And I hate that.

When did this happen? When did walking become this important to me?

---

At lunch, I talked with Minseo while we ate.

"Hey, you looked rough on the bus earlier."

"Yeah, felt nauseous."

"So walking really is better for you?"

"... Yeah."

I nodded, and Minseo laughed.

"Dude, you really started liking walking."

"I don't know if I like it. Maybe I just hate the bus."

"Isn't that the same thing?"

Minseo said. I thought about it.

Is it the same? Is hating the bus the same as liking to walk?

I'm not sure. But I felt it today on the bus. That I wanted to walk. That walking is better than the bus.

That much was certain.

---

The ride home was on the bus too.

Still raining. Fewer people in the afternoon, but the air inside the bus was still stuffy.

Rain-soaked scenery flashed past the window.

I could see the tangerine orchards. Wet from the rain, the leaves glistened. The grandfather didn't seem to be there. He probably didn't come out to the field because of the rain.

*I wonder if it'll stop tomorrow.*

I should check the forecast.

---

I'm home now, writing my diary.

Today was a disappointing day because I couldn't walk.

Taking the bus made it all come back. The nausea, the smell, the suffocation. Things I'd forgotten.

Since I started walking, I've had fewer bus days. I guess that's why I forgot. How hard the bus really was.

Today reminded me.

I want to walk.

Even if it rains, even if it's hard, I still want to walk.

What is this? Why do I want to walk so much?

I still don't know.

But if it's not raining tomorrow, I'm walking.

I want to see the grandfather too.

Checked the forecast. Tomorrow it'll be cloudy, clearing up in the afternoon.

It might not rain in the morning.

I hope I can walk tomorrow.
