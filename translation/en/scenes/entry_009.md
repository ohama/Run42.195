# Monday, April 1, 2024

Clear skies, warm. Real spring at last.

---

April is here.

I barely talked to Mom over the weekend. Not since that conversation on Thursday night. At meals, we only said what was necessary. It was awkward.

Sunday evening, Mom spoke first.

"Doyun."

"Yeah."

"Are you walking tomorrow?"

I paused. I didn't know how to answer.

"... I want to walk."

"..."

Mom sighed. But it wasn't an angry sigh.

"Okay. But promise me."

"Promise what?"

"Eat breakfast before you go. Always stop at crosswalks and look both ways. Don't walk with earphones in."

"... Okay."

"And twice a week max. No more than that. You can't push yourself too hard."

That's what she said. I nodded.

"Got it. I promise."

Mom looked at me. Her eyes were worried. But at the same time, there was something else in them... something like acceptance.

---

This morning.

I left the house at 6:30. Just like I promised, I ate breakfast and chewed every bite. Mom came to the front door.

"Be careful."

"Okay."

"If it gets too hard, catch a bus partway."

"I will."

I opened the door and stepped out.

---

April mornings are different.

In March, it was still chilly. I had to wear my padded jacket. But today, a light windbreaker was enough. Even when the wind blew, it wasn't cold.

And the scenery had changed.

Rapeseed flowers.

They'd started blooming in March, but now they were in full bloom. Both sides of the road were yellow. Like waves of gold.

*Beautiful.*

I stopped without thinking. I wished I had my phone to take a picture. But Mom told me not to bring earphones, so I left my phone at home too.

I just took it all in with my eyes.

Yellow rapeseed flowers. Blue sky. Morning sunlight.

---

I passed the tangerine orchard.

The old man was there.

"Good morning."

"Hey there, student. It's April now."

The old man looked up at the sky as he spoke.

"Yes. It's gotten warm."

"Sure has. Good weather for walking."

The old man smiled. I smiled too.

"Are you working again today?"

"Yeah. Harvesting the late tangerines. They're called mangam. You pick them late."

"Oh..."

"If you get thirsty on your way, have one."

He pulled a small tangerine from his pocket and handed it to me. An orange tangerine. Small, but the color was deep.

"Thank you."

"Go on. You'll be late for school."

---

I ate the tangerine on my way to school.

When I peeled it, a sweet smell rose up. I popped a segment into my mouth. Sweet. Tangy and sweet. The juice spread across my tongue.

*So good.*

A tangerine from the old man. A tangerine I received while walking in the morning. Something I never could have had on the bus. I can eat it because I'm walking.

That thought put me in a good mood.

---

Arrived at school at 7:50.

Today was easier. I think it's because the weather was warm. I wasn't as out of breath, and my legs didn't hurt as much.

I sat on the bench and drank some water. I was a bit sweaty, but I felt refreshed.

*Ah, so this is spring.*

I started walking in March, and it's already been a month. It all began because I missed the bus and had no choice. Now I'm walking on purpose.

Strange. A month ago, I never would have imagined this.

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"The old man gave me a tangerine today."

"Really? The tangerine orchard grandpa?"

"Yeah."

"Dude, you're totally close with him now."

"I don't know if we're close. I just say hi whenever I pass by."

"That's being close."

That's what Minseo said. Is it?

"So what did your mom say?"

"About what?"

"About you walking. You said she was worried."

I told him what happened yesterday. How Mom said I could walk twice a week.

"Oh, that's good. Your mom's being understanding."

"I don't know if she understands. I think she just... allowed it."

"Isn't that the same thing?"

Minseo said. I thought about it.

Understanding and allowing. Are they the same? Does Mom understand why I want to walk? Or is she just letting me because I want to?

I don't know. But I'm grateful she gave me permission.

---

I'm home now, writing in my diary.

April is here. Spring has arrived.

I felt it while walking today. Walking has gotten a little easier. In early March, an hour and ten minutes felt too long. Now, it feels... manageable.

Have I gotten used to it?

Or has my stamina improved?

I'm not sure. But it's not bad.

Mom said twice a week. Monday and Thursday. I'll stick to that and keep walking.

I want to see the old man again.

I should walk as much as I can before the rapeseed flowers wilt.

Spring doesn't last long.
