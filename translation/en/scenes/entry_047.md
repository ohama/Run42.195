# Thursday, August 15, 2024

Clear skies. Liberation Day.

---

It's Liberation Day.

It's a holiday, but I ran anyway. Running every day has become a habit at this point.

Left at 5:15 AM. 10km route.

---

Something strange crossed my mind while I was running.

*Summer's almost over.*

Mid-August. Summer break is on its last legs. Two weeks until school starts again.

I looked around as I ran.

The sunflowers were starting to droop. Their heads bowed down. Petals falling.

Even the cicada sounds seemed quieter than before. The mornings were getting a bit cooler.

---

I passed by the mandarin orange groves.

I spotted Grandfather Yeongcheol in the distance, working in his field.

I got closer and said hi.

"Hello, Grandfather!"

"Oh, hey Doyun. Out running again?"

"Yeah. Doing 10km today."

"10km? You've come a long way."

Grandfather laughed.

---

"How are your mandarins doing, Grandfather?"

"They're starting to grow now. Once fall gets here, they'll start changing color."

He pointed at the trees. Small green mandarins hung thick on the branches.

"We harvest around November. I'll bring you some then."

"Thank you."

"Keep working hard. Running in fall is the best."

---

Running gave me time to feel the seasons changing.

When I first started back in March, it was chilly. Rapeseed flowers were just starting to bloom.

April came with cherry blossoms. Things warmed up.

May had mandarin blossoms. That sweet smell.

June brought hydrangeas and the rainy season.

July was brutal heat. Sunflowers everywhere.

August was hot too. But now it's fading away.

Next comes fall.

---

Finished the 10km in 56 minutes.

Two minutes faster than last time.

I showered when I got home and just lay there thinking.

*What happens when summer break ends?*

Can I keep running while I'm going to school?

What if I just run in the morning before heading to school?

I need to figure that out.

---

We had lunch together in the afternoon.

Dad was home because of Liberation Day.

He asked me something.

"So you're running these days?"

"Yeah."

"How much are you running?"

"10km."

Dad's eyes got huge.

"10km? That's impressive."

"..."

It was the first time he'd shown interest in my running. It felt weird somehow.

"You're doing great," he said. Short and simple.

---

Writing this diary entry now.

Ran 10km on Liberation Day morning.

Summer's ending. The sunflowers are drooping, and the mornings are getting cooler.

Grandfather Yeongcheol said fall's the best time to run.

I'm actually looking forward to it.

Two weeks left of summer break.

But once school starts, how am I gonna keep running? Early morning jogs before school?

I'll have to think about it.

Dad said I'm doing great.

It was the first time he's actually shown interest in my running.

Feels like he finally acknowledged it. Like maybe he actually notices.
