# Monday, April 29, 2024

Clear skies. The sunlight was dazzling.

---

Mom said something strange this morning.

"Doyun, your face looks healthier these days."

"Huh?"

"Your complexion is brighter. It used to look kind of dull."

I looked in the mirror. I couldn't really tell. Looked the same to me.

"Really?"

"Yeah. Maybe it's because you've been walking every morning."

That's what Mom said. And then she smiled.

---

Mom smiled.

When I first told her I was walking to school, she worried. Said she heard about it from Auntie. That it was dangerous. That it would be too hard.

But now she's smiling. Saying my complexion looks better.

*Does this mean she's accepted it?*

That's how it felt.

---

I thought about it on my way to school.

Part of me wishes Mom had understood from the start. When I first began walking. Cheering me on instead of worrying.

But thinking about it now, I understand her side too.

Your son suddenly says he's going to walk over an hour to school. Leaving at the crack of dawn. Of course she'd worry.

If I were a parent, I'd probably worry too.

---

I ran into Mr. Youngchul at the tangerine orchard.

"Good morning, sir!"

"Oh, Doyun. Nice weather today."

"Yes. The sun's really bright."

"Better start wearing a hat. Summer's coming."

He looked up at the sky. The sun was already high.

"Sir, my mom said my complexion looks better."

"Oh? Did she?"

"Yeah. She thinks it's from walking."

"That's right. Walking outside in the sunshine makes your body healthier."

He nodded.

"I've worked outdoors my whole life, so nothing ever hurts. Hardly ever catch a cold."

"Really?"

"Yep. Moving around outside is the best medicine."

---

I got to school and sat on a bench.

The sunlight was warm. Late April. Almost May. Spring is ending.

It's been nearly two months since I started walking. Since that first time in early March when I missed the bus.

What's changed in two months?

My stamina improved. I got better at distance running. Lost some weight. And apparently my complexion looks better.

And Mom started to understand.

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"My mom said my face looks healthier today."

"Oh, really? You got her approval."

"Yeah. She thinks it's from walking."

"That's great. It must be nice having your mom understand."

Minseo said.

"She was worried at first. Said it was dangerous. Too hard."

"And now?"

"Now it's just 'be careful.' Feels more like support than worry?"

"Oh, that's progress."

Minseo laughed. I laughed too.

It is progress. For Mom and for me.

---

I talked to Mom this evening.

"Mom, is it okay if I keep walking?"

"Of course. Why are you suddenly asking?"

"Just... you worried at first. How do you feel now?"

Mom thought for a moment.

"I still worry sometimes. You leave when it's dark out. And there's traffic."

"..."

"But it seems good for you. Your face looks better, your stamina's up."

Mom said.

"And you like it. Walking."

"Yeah."

"It's good to do what you like. I know that much."

Mom smiled. I smiled too.

*She understands.*

Maybe not completely. She might still worry. But she's trying to understand. I could feel it.

---

Writing this diary now.

Mom started to understand.

She said my complexion looks better. That it must be from walking.

At first she only worried. Now she's telling me to do what I like.

It took time. Almost two months.

But it happened. Mom recognized it.

That makes me happy.

Mr. Youngchul said moving around outside is the best medicine.

I think he's right. Two months of walking and my body's gotten better.

May is coming. I should walk even harder.

No, I'm just glad I get to walk.

Just happy.
