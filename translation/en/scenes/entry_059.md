# Monday, September 30, 2024

Clear skies, crisp morning.

---

D-5.

Five days left.

---

Light run today. Just 5km. Nice and easy.

Coach told me:

"Don't push it this week. If your body gets too tired, you won't have anything left for race day."

So short. And slow. That's it.

---

While I ran, I played the race course in my head.

I'm at the starting line.

The gun goes off.

I run.

1km, 2km, 3km...

My breathing gets harder.

5km, 6km, 7km...

My legs start to feel heavy.

8km, 9km...

I dig deep for the last stretch.

10km.

Finish line.

---

Just imagining it made my heart race.

*I can do this.*

Six months of training.

Started with walking. Got all the way here.

---

School.

Couldn't focus in class.

The race kept creeping back into my mind.

*Don't go out too fast at the start.*

*Keep the pace. Five minutes per km.*

*Sprint it at the end.*

I ran through it all in my head over and over.

---

Lunch.

Minseo asked, "You nervous?"

"Yeah. A little."

"You'll be fine. You're gonna kill it."

"Thanks."

"I'll come early Saturday. Can I see you before the start?"

"Yeah. We're taking off at 8, so come around 7:30."

"Got it. I'll be cheering my lungs out!"

---

After school.

Coach called me over.

"Doyun, you ready?"

"Yeah. I'm nervous though."

"Being nervous is normal. Actually, that's a good thing."

"Good thing?"

"Nervous means you're focused. If you're too comfortable, that's when you mess up."

I nodded.

"You wearing your running watch?"

"Yeah. I have it on every day."

"Wear it Saturday too. Watch your pace while you run."

"Yes, sir!"

---

Evening.

My family talked about the race.

Dad asked, "What time do we need to leave Saturday?"

"Around 6. We gotta be there by 7:30."

"Got it. Let's all go together Saturday morning."

Mom said, "What snacks should I pack? Banana? Chocolate?"

"Banana. I want to eat something before I start."

My little sister jumped in: "I'm coming too! I'm gonna cheer for my big brother!"

"Yeah, let's all go together."

---

I'm in my room now, writing this.

D-5.

Five days left.

I'm nervous. But like Coach said, that's good. It means I'm sharp.

I'm ready.

My body feels good.

Saturday. I'm ready to run to that finish line.

My whole family's coming.

Dad, Mom, my sister.

Minseo too.

They're all coming to cheer me on.

I don't want to let them down.

Actually, I don't want to let *myself* down.

Six months of hard work.

Gotta finish this race.

D-5.
