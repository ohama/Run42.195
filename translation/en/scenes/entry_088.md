# Thursday, January 9, 2025

Clear skies, below freezing.

---

Today's long run goal: 25km.

Taking on 25km for the first time.

---

5 a.m.

I head out.

Still dark.

Stars above.

---

Today I'm going all the way down the coastal road and back.

Turn around at 12.5km.

---

5km.

My body's warmed up now.

10km.

The sun came up.

15km.

This part I know.

---

17km.

My legs started getting heavy.

20km.

This is tough.

But I didn't stop.

---

22km.

*Three kilometers left.*

I've broken the half-marathon distance.

This is new territory.

---

24km.

My legs weren't listening anymore.

My pace dropped.

*It's okay. Just keep moving forward.*

25km.

I stopped.

2 hours 42 minutes.

---

*I did it.*

25km for the first time.

It was hard.

Really hard after 22km.

But I ran all the way.

---

I walked back home slowly.

My legs were shaking.

---

Evening.

I reported to Coach.

"Coach, I ran 25km."

"Nice work. How was it?"

"It was tough. After 22km especially."

"That's the wall. In a full marathon, there's a bigger wall past 30km."

"Yes."

"But you got over it today. You can get over it next time too."

---

Back in my room writing this.

25km complete.

For the first time, I've gone beyond the half-marathon distance.

It was difficult.

But I made it.

17km left until 42km.

Next goal: 30km.

I can do this.
