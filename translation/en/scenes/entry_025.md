# Monday, May 27, 2024

Clear skies. The morning breeze was cool.

---

I bought running shoes this weekend.

Mom and I went to a sporting goods store. The running shoe section had so many options. I had no idea which ones to pick.

A salesman helped us out.

"First pair?"

"Yes."

"Then these should work. Good for beginners, nice cushioning."

They were blue running shoes. When I tried them on, they felt soft. Like my feet were floating on clouds.

"I'll take these."

55,000 won. I paid 28,000 from my allowance, Mom covered the other 27,000.

My first running shoes.

---

Today I ran in my new shoes.

Completely different.

My feet didn't hurt. The impact felt absorbed. My toes didn't rub against anything. The shoes fit perfectly.

*So this is what running shoes are like.*

I felt stupid for running in regular sneakers before. The difference was night and day.

---

I was feeling good on my run when I passed Grandfather Yeongcheol at the tangerine orchard.

I planned to wave and keep going without stopping.

But he called out.

"Doyun! Hold on!"

I stopped. Catching my breath, I walked over to him.

"Yes, Grandfather?"

"I see you running by all the time these days."

"Yes. I started running."

"New shoes too, I see."

He looked down at my feet. Blue running shoes.

"Yes. Bought them this weekend. Running shoes."

"Oh, running shoes. Getting serious, aren't you?"

He smiled.

---

"Doyun."

Grandfather spoke.

"I used to run when I was young."

"Really? You too?"

"Yeah. Ran a lot in the army. Back then, running was just part of the job."

"Part of the job?"

"Being a soldier. Running was training."

He gazed into the distance as he spoke.

"But running felt good. Hard, sure. But good. That's what I liked about it."

"Same for me. It's hard, but it feels good."

"That's it. That's what running tastes like."

Grandfather nodded.

---

"You're doing well, Doyun."

Grandfather said.

"When I first saw you back in March, you were just walking. Now you're running by like this."

"Yeah. I started walking, then ended up running."

"That's a good thing. Run a lot while you're young. You won't be able to later."

He patted his own legs.

"These legs, they ran when I was young. Now even walking's hard."

"..."

"But the memories of running back then, they're still here. That's the good part."

He smiled. Wrinkled face. But his eyes sparkled.

"Go on. You'll be late for school."

"Okay. Thank you, Grandfather."

---

I started running again.

His words kept echoing in my head.

*Run a lot while you're young. You won't be able to later.*

He's right. Run while I can.

*You're doing well, Doyun.*

That's what he said. It was embarrassing but also made me happy.

---

I arrived at school.

7:40 AM. Ran 7km in 50 minutes. The running shoes made it so much easier.

I sat on a bench and caught my breath.

*You're doing well.*

His words kept coming back to me.

When I first walked by in March, Grandfather said "Keep at it." Just keep going.

And now he says "You're doing well."

It felt like being recognized.

---

I told Minseo about it.

"Hey, that grandfather said I'm doing well."

"The tangerine orchard guy?"

"Yeah. Said I've been running past all the time. Said I'm doing well."

"Nice. Must feel good to get encouragement."

"Yeah. A bit embarrassing, but I'm happy."

Minseo grinned.

"Dude, you're totally a runner now."

"A runner?"

"Yeah. Someone who runs. A runner."

Runner.

Am I really a runner?

---

Writing in my diary now.

Today I ran in my new shoes. Completely different. My feet were comfortable and the impact was absorbed.

And I got encouragement from Grandfather Yeongcheol.

*I see you running by all the time. You're doing well.*

I was embarrassed. But happy.

He said he used to run when he was young too. In the army. Hard but felt good.

Same for me. Hard but feels good.

*Run a lot while you're young. You won't be able to later.*

He's right. Run while I can.

Minseo called me a runner. Someone who runs.

Am I really a runner?

It all started when I missed the bus in March. And now I've become a runner.

Strange. But good.

May is ending. June is coming.

Let's keep running.
