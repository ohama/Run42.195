# Monday, June 10, 2024

Rain, but just a drizzle.

---

I woke up and looked out the window.

It was raining. But not hard like Thursday. Just a soft, gentle sprinkle.

*I can run in this.*

I made up my mind.

---

Mom asked.

"It's raining again today?"

"It's just a drizzle. I can run."

"Really?"

"Yeah. I looked it up, and light rain is fine."

Mom looked out the window. Stared for a while, then nodded.

"Alright, this should be okay. But if it picks up, stop right away."

"Got it."

---

I headed out.

The rain touched my face. Damp. But cool.

I started running.

---

This was my first time running in the rain.

It felt strange. My whole body was getting wet. Hair stuck to my forehead. Clothes grew heavy.

But.

It was refreshing.

June had been getting hot, but the rain made it cool. Even when I sweated, the rain washed it away.

---

Around the 2km mark, a strange feeling came over me.

I felt free.

I'm running in the rain. Other people are walking with umbrellas or waiting for the bus. But I'm running.

Not because someone told me to. Because I wanted to.

*So this is what freedom feels like.*

Getting soaked is fine. Being uncomfortable is fine. Because I'm doing what I want.

---

I passed by the tangerine groves.

Grandpa Yeongcheol wasn't there because of the rain. Guess he doesn't work in the fields on rainy days.

The tangerine trees were dripping wet. Water droplets fell from the leaves. The green looked even deeper.

*Beautiful.*

A rainy day landscape. A different kind of beauty from sunny days.

---

After running 5km, I walked.

I caught my breath and looked up at the sky. Raindrops fell through the gaps in the gray clouds.

The rain touched my face. I closed my eyes. Just stood there.

*This feels good.*

Strangely good. I was soaked and damp, but I felt good.

---

I arrived at school.

7:50. Took 50 minutes. The rain slowed me down a bit.

I was completely drenched. Water dripped from my hair. My clothes clung to my body.

I went to the nurse's office and borrowed a towel. Dried my hair, dried my face.

Changed into my gym clothes. Hung the wet clothes in my locker.

---

Minseo's eyes went wide.

"Dude, did you run in the rain?"

"Yeah. Ran here."

"You're crazy, seriously. In this rain?"

"It was just a drizzle. It was fine."

"What if you catch a cold?"

"I'm fine. Actually, it was refreshing."

Minseo shook his head. But his eyes were sparkling.

"So how was it? Running in the rain?"

"... It felt freeing."

"Freeing?"

"Yeah. It's hard to explain. It just felt good."

---

At lunch, I touched my wet clothes. Still damp.

*Gotta wash these when I get home.*

But no regrets. Running in the rain today, I don't regret it.

It was a new experience. Running while getting soaked. First time.

And it felt good.

---

By the time school let out, the rain had mostly stopped.

I took the bus. The damp clothes were uncomfortable, but it was fine.

I looked out the window and thought.

*I can run even when it rains.*

I learned that today. By actually doing it. As long as it's not pouring.

I can run even during monsoon season.

---

I'm writing in my diary.

I ran in the rain today.

First time. Got completely soaked. Damp and heavy.

But it felt good. Refreshing. Freeing.

Not many people run when it rains. Most walk with umbrellas or take the bus.

I ran. Because I wanted to.

That's what freedom is, I think. Doing what you want to do.

I can run even in monsoon season.

Even if it rains, I run.

And I like that.
