# Monday, May 13, 2024

Clear skies. The sun was scorching.

---

This happened in PE class today.

We ran the 100-meter dash. Prep for the fitness test. The teacher had us race in pairs.

My partner was Park Junhyuk. Athletic kid. On the soccer team.

I figured I'd lose, obviously. I'm bad at running. Always near the bottom.

But.

---

The whistle blew.

I ran. Full speed.

My legs pumped fast. Wind rushed past my face. My heart pounded.

The finish line came into view.

I ran. All the way to the end.

---

The result.

Second place.

Junhyuk came first. But the gap wasn't big. About 0.5 seconds?

The teacher called out the times.

"Park Junhyuk, 14.2 seconds. Kim Doyun, 14.7 seconds."

14.7 seconds.

Not bad. Actually, pretty good. Back in elementary school, my time was around 17 seconds.

I'd shaved off more than 2 seconds.

---

After PE, Minseo came running over.

"Dude, did you see what just happened?"

"See what?"

"The 100-meter. You were almost as fast as Junhyuk."

"I mean... I lost, though?"

"Still! Junhyuk's on the soccer team. Being 0.5 seconds behind him is insane."

Minseo was all excited.

"You've been getting way better at running lately. I saw you in PE. You've seriously improved."

"You think?"

"Yeah. You used to always come in last. Now you're in the middle. No, like, upper half."

---

I thought about it during lunch.

Minseo was right. I've gotten better at running.

In March, during the long-distance run, I couldn't even finish three laps without walking. In April, I ran six. Now I could probably do even more.

Same with the 100-meter. Went from 17-something seconds to 14-something.

*It's from the walking and running.*

Definitely. Nothing else has changed.

---

During afternoon classes, I looked out the window.

I could see kids playing soccer on the field.

*Could I run like that too?*

Before, I couldn't even imagine it. The athletic kids felt like people from another world.

But now it's different.

I run too. Twice a week to school. Walking and running.

*I've become someone who exercises.*

Weird. Me, who used to hate exercise.

---

On the bus home, Minseo asked me something.

"Hey, what do you think about when you're running in PE?"

"What do I think about?"

"Yeah. When you're running."

I thought about it.

"Just... wanting to get there fast? To the finish line."

"That's it?"

"Yeah. I don't think about anything else. I just run."

Minseo nodded.

"When I run, all I can think is 'this is so hard, this is so hard.'"

"I used to be like that too."

"But now?"

"Now... I just like running. The good feeling is bigger than the hard part."

Minseo looked at me for a moment.

"You've really changed."

"Have I?"

"Yeah. Completely."

---

I'm writing this in my diary now.

Minseo said, "You've been getting way better at running lately."

I ran the 100-meter in PE. 14.7 seconds. Just 0.5 seconds behind Junhyuk.

My old time was around 17 seconds, so I've cut more than 2 seconds.

It's from the walking and running.

Minseo also said, "You've really changed."

I think I have changed. Me, who used to hate exercise, has gotten good at running.

It all started when I missed the bus in March. That was two months ago already.

I've changed a lot in two months.

What will happen from here?

Will I get faster? Will I be able to run farther?

I don't know. But I'm excited to find out.

Tomorrow I'll walk to school, and Friday I'll try running.

Let's keep increasing the distance, little by little.
