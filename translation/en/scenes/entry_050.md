# Monday, August 26, 2024

Clear skies. First day back at school.

---

Second semester has started.

Summer break is over.

---

This morning.

Woke up at 5 AM. Like always.

Decided to run to school. 7km. All the way.

Left at 5:15. Should arrive by 6:25. Classes start at 8:30. Two hours to spare.

---

I started running.

The familiar route. The same one I walked for the first time back in March.

That day took me 1 hour 10 minutes. Now it takes 40 minutes. Running makes all the difference.

Passed through the tangerine groves.

---

Grandpa Yeongcheol was there.

"Grandpa! Good morning!"

I didn't stop, just waved as I ran past. He waved back.

*I'll tell him about the race registration later.*

---

Arrived at school. 6:30 AM.

Two hours early.

Cooled off, washed up in the bathroom, changed into my gym clothes.

Sat on a bench and drank some water.

*I've changed.*

I thought back to the first time I came to school in March.

I barely made it there, fighting nausea the whole way on that crowded bus. Miserable mood.

Now I ran here. 7km. Feeling great.

---

Walked into the classroom.

Friends started arriving one by one.

Minseo came in and spotted me.

"Yo, you ran here?"

"Yeah."

"Of course you did lol. I took the bus."

"Let's run together on Saturday."

"Sure."

---

Some kids looked at me.

"Hey, Kim Doyun's different."

"Different how?"

"I don't know. Just looks different somehow."

---

PE class.

The teacher said, "Kim Doyun, I heard you registered for the marathon?"

"Yes, sir."

"That's great. Work hard preparing."

"I will, sir."

The other kids started buzzing.

"A marathon? Doyun's running a marathon?"

"Guess he's entering a race."

---

At lunch, several kids asked me about it.

"Hey, you're really doing a marathon?"

"Yeah. 10km."

"No way. Isn't 10km super far?"

"Pretty far."

"How long have you been running?"

"Since March."

They looked shocked.

"A marathon in six months?"

"Well, a real marathon is 42km. I'm doing 10km."

"Still, that's impressive."

---

It felt like I was finally getting recognition.

The kid who used to suck at sports is entering a marathon race.

It was strange, but it made me happy.

---

Headed home.

Thought about running but took the bus instead. Bus for the ride home.

Sat in my seat and watched out the window.

The tangerine groves passed by. The same route I ran this morning.

*Second semester will be like this.*

Run in the morning for school, take the bus home.

This is going to be my new routine.

---

Writing this diary entry now.

First day of second semester.

Ran to school. 7km. 40 minutes.

Same route I walked for the first time in March. That took 1 hour 10 minutes back then.

I've changed. Definitely.

Friends said it. Said I look different.

What's changed?

Physical fitness? Appearance? Confidence?

Probably all of it.

Six months of walking and running.

That's what changed me.

About a month and a half until the race.

I'll keep preparing while managing second semester.

Complete the 10km.

I really want to do this.
