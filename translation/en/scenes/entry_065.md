# Monday, October 21, 2024

Clear skies. Crisp autumn day.

---

Today's long run goal: 15km.

Going beyond 10km for the first time. A real distance challenge.

---

5 a.m.

Take off.

Not toward school today. I'm heading toward the coastal road instead.

Out 7.5km, back 7.5km. That's 15km total.

---

Started slow.

1km, 2km, 3km...

My body warmed up nicely.

5km.

This distance feels familiar by now.

---

7km.

The usual school commute distance.

Still feeling good.

---

7.5km.

The turnaround point.

I pivot.

*Now it really begins.*

The stretch beyond 10km.

A distance I've never run before.

---

8km.

Still okay.

9km.

My legs are starting to feel heavy.

10km.

My breathing gets rougher.

*Into uncharted territory from here.*

---

11km.

That feeling from the race—running 10km—it's coming back now.

Heavy legs. Short of breath.

But I don't stop.

12km.

*You can do this. Just a little more.*

13km.

It's hard. Really hard.

My pace is slowing down.

That's fine. Long runs aren't about speed. They're about distance.

14km.

Almost there.

*Just a bit more. Just a bit more.*

---

15km.

I stop.

Check my watch. 1 hour 28 minutes.

*I did it.*

---

I'm breathing hard, but I'm smiling.

First time I've gone beyond 10km.

15km.

That was brutal. After 11km, it got really brutal.

But I did it.

---

Walk slowly back home.

My legs are shaking.

But I feel amazing.

---

I get to school late.

Today it's not a run to school—it's arriving after a long run.

Minseow asks when I walk into the classroom.

"Hey, what's up with being late?"

"Oh, I did a long run first."

"How many km?"

"15km."

Minseow's eyes go wide.

"15km?! That's insane."

"It was brutal. Once I got past 11km, it was really hard."

"But you did it. That's amazing."

---

Back in my room now, writing this.

I ran 15km today.

First time going past 10km.

It was hard.

11km, 12km, 13km...

The further I went, the heavier my legs got.

I thought, *Should I just stop?*

But I didn't.

I kept going until 15km.

1 hour 28 minutes.

Slow pace, but that's okay.

Long runs aren't about speed. They're about distance.

6km left until 21km.

Next week I'm going for 17km.

I can do this.
