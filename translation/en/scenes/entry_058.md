# Thursday, September 26, 2024

Clear skies. Cool morning.

---

D-7.

One week left.

---

Starting today, it's taper time.

My coach explained it. You can't push yourself hard the week before a race.

"Scale back your training. Your body needs rest so you'll be in peak condition on race day."

So today I just did an easy 5km.

---

I thought about it while running slow.

*One week until the race.*

I was nervous. Excited.

Back in March, I was getting carsick on the bus. Now I'm running in a marathon.

I can't believe it.

---

I passed the mandarin orchards.

Grandpa Yeongcheol was there.

"Grandpa! Hello!"

"Oh, running early today. When's your race?"

"Next Saturday. October 5th."

"Good. Do well."

"Yes!"

---

Grandpa handed me a mandarin.

"Try this. Just picked it."

I peeled the skin and ate a section.

Sweet. Still greenish but already sweet.

"It's delicious."

"Had a good harvest this year. I'll give you more after the race."

"Thank you!"

---

Arrived at school.

Minseo asked in the classroom.

"Hey, one week left until the race?"

"Yeah."

"Nervous?"

"A little."

"You've got this. You trained so hard."

"Thanks."

Minseo grinned.

"I'm coming to cheer you on. It's Saturday, right?"

"Yeah. October 5th. We're leaving at 8 a.m."

"Early. But I'll be there."

---

Lunch break.

A few classmates asked.

"Doyun, you're running in the race? You nervous?"

"A bit."

"It's 10km, right? That's awesome."

"I've been training hard."

"We'll cheer for you!"

A few guys from my class said they'd come.

I felt grateful.

---

Evening.

Mom asked.

"Did you pack what you need for the race?"

"Not yet."

"What do you need?"

I thought about it.

- Running shoes
- Running clothes
- Running watch (coach's)
- Water
- Banana
- Race bib (they give it the day of)

"I'll pack tomorrow."

"Getting nervous?"

"A little."

Mom laughed.

"You're going to do great. Mom's coming to cheer."

"Dad's coming too."

"I know. Your dad's really looking forward to it."

---

I'm in my room writing this.

D-7.

One week left.

I'm nervous. Excited.

I've trained hard.

Long runs. Intervals. Tempo runs.

I planned my pace strategy too.

I've cut my time down to 49 minutes 32 seconds.

I'm ready.

Now I just need to stay in good shape.

Rest this week, eat well, sleep well.

On race day, I'll line up feeling strong.

Grandpa, Mom, Dad, Minseo, my coach, my friends.

They're all rooting for me.

I don't want to let them down.

Actually, I don't want to let myself down.

I'll finish. I know I will.

Under 50 minutes.

D-7.
