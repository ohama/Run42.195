# Thursday, October 3rd, 2024

Clear skies. National Foundation Day.

---

T-2 days.

The race is the day after tomorrow.

School's closed today for the holiday.

---

Morning.

Just ran a light 3km.

More like a warm-up, really.

While I was running, I kept thinking about it.

*The day after tomorrow, I'll be running 10km.*

I was nervous.

---

Lunch.

Mom asked, "What do you want to eat tomorrow?"

"Pasta. I need to load up on carbs."

"Okay. I'll make pasta for dinner."

My coach said before a race you should eat a lot of carbohydrates. For energy storage.

---

Afternoon.

I checked everything I need.

**Race Day Checklist**
- [x] Running shoes (cleaned)
- [x] Running clothes (short sleeves, shorts)
- [x] Running watch (charged)
- [x] Water bottle
- [x] 2 bananas
- [x] Towel
- [x] Change of clothes
- [ ] Race bib (picking up on race day)

All set.

---

Evening.

I packed my bag and sat in my room.

Pulled up the race course on my phone again.

Jeju Sports Complex start → Coastal road → back to the complex.

Mostly flat. Almost no hills.

*I can do this. I can.*

---

10 PM.

I got into bed.

I need to sleep early. I have to wake up before dawn the day after tomorrow.

But I can't fall asleep.

Even with my eyes shut, I keep thinking about the race.

Imagining myself at the starting line.

Running.

Crossing the finish line.

---

Mom opened the door.

"Still awake?"

"I can't sleep."

"You're nervous, huh?"

"Yeah."

She sat down next to my bed.

"It's okay. Being nervous is normal."

"..."

"You worked hard. From March until now. I've seen it all."

"..."

"Whether you finish or not, I'm proud of you."

My eyes started to burn.

"Thanks."

"Get some rest now. Take it easy tomorrow, and give it your best the day after."

"Okay."

---

After Mom left.

I stared at the ceiling.

*Finish or not finish. She's proud of me anyway.*

Her words stuck with me.

But I want to finish.

I've been running for six months.

I started with bus sickness and made it this far.

I really want to cross that finish line.

---

I didn't fall asleep until after midnight.

---

Writing this the next morning.

Last night I couldn't sleep.

I was nervous.

Mom came in and told me something.

"Whether you finish or not, I'm proud of you."

That meant a lot.

But I'm going to finish.

Tomorrow.

10km.

To the finish line.

T-2 days.

Actually, T-1 now.
