# Thursday, March 21, 2024

Clear skies. The tangerine orchard smelled like morning.

---

I walked to school again today.

This is my second time since Monday. I was planning to walk last Friday, but it rained, so I couldn't. I walked today instead.

I left the house at 6:35. The sky was getting brighter. The air was fresh.

Walking is becoming familiar. An hour and ten minutes still doesn't feel short, but it's less tiring than the first time.

---

I was passing by the tangerine orchard.

Someone was standing up ahead.

An old man.

He was checking the trees in the middle of the orchard. Wearing a worn jacket and rubber boots. His back was slightly hunched. He looked to be in his seventies.

I was going to just walk past. I hesitated about whether to say hello. He was a stranger, after all.

But the old man saw me first.

"Oh, student."

He spoke to me. His voice had a hint of Jeju dialect.

"...Yes?"

"Where are you going?"

"School."

"School? Walking?"

The old man's eyes widened.

"Yes."

"Where's your school?"

"Jeju Middle School."

"That place? That's quite a ways from here."

"It takes about an hour and ten minutes."

The old man paused. Then he looked at me again.

"You're walking there?"

"Yes."

"..."

He thought about something for a moment, then nodded.

"Gyeong hae. Take care."

"Yes, goodbye."

I bowed my head and continued walking.

It was a strange conversation. But it didn't feel bad.

---

I kept thinking about that old man on my way to school.

Was he the owner of the tangerine orchard? He was checking the trees early in the morning.

'Where are you going?' 'School.' 'Walking?'

A simple exchange. But somehow... it felt warm.

That elderly man was working in his tangerine orchard early in the morning. I was walking early in the morning too. Something about it felt similar.

Strange. He was a complete stranger.

---

I arrived at school. 7:52. Plenty of time again.

Sitting on the bench drinking water, I thought about that old man.

'Gyeong hae.'

In Jeju dialect, it means "do that" or "go ahead." That's what the old man said at the end.

'Gyeong hae. Take care.'

It felt like... encouragement.

He seemed surprised that I walked to school. But he didn't criticize me. Just said "go ahead."

Was he approving of me? Or was it just something he said?

I don't know. But it felt good.

---

At lunch, I told Minseo.

"I met an old man at the tangerine orchard today."

"An old man? Who?"

"I don't know. I think he's the owner of the orchard. He asked me where I was going."

"What did he say?"

"When I said I was going to school, he was surprised I was walking. But he said 'gyeong hae.'"

"Gyeong hae?"

"Yeah. Like, go ahead and do that."

Minseo chuckled.

"Sounds like the old man was cheering you on."

"You think?"

"Of course. What's he gonna say to a kid who walks over an hour to school? He probably thought you were impressive."

Minseo said. I thought about it.

'Impressive?'

I'm just walking. Walking because I hate the bus.

Is that impressive?

I'm not sure.

---

I took the bus home.

I sat on the bus and looked out the window. The road I walked this morning flashed by.

I could see the tangerine orchard too. Where that old man was standing this morning.

No one was there now. Just rows of tangerine trees.

'I wonder if he'll be there tomorrow.'

That thought crossed my mind. I don't know why, but I wanted to see him again.

---

I'm home now, writing in my diary.

I keep thinking about that old man today.

I don't know his name. I don't know what kind of person he is. Just someone who works in the tangerine orchard.

But he said 'gyeong hae.' I can't stop thinking about those words.

Walking a distance where taking the bus is normal. Adults might think it's weird. They might ask why I'm doing something so hard.

But that old man didn't say that.

Just 'gyeong hae.'

It felt like... being acknowledged.

That what I'm doing isn't strange. That it's okay to do this.

It was just one phrase, but it gave me more strength than I expected.

Next time I walk, if I meet that old man, I should say hello.

'Good morning, I'm heading to school.'

Like that.

I'm looking forward to next Monday.

Will I get to see the old man again?
