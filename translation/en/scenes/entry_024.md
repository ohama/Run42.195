# Thursday, May 23, 2024

Clear skies. Starting to get hot.

---

I realized something while running today.

My sneakers don't fit right.

---

I attempted the full 7km again today.

Since I failed on Monday, I had to try again.

The result... half success?

I ran 5km and walked the remaining 2km. Not as good as Thursday (when I made the full 7km), but better than Monday (when I gave up at 4km).

That part is fine. The problem is something else.

---

Around the 3km mark, my feet started hurting.

Not the soles—my toes and the top of my foot. A rubbing sensation.

At 5km, I finally stopped. I took off my shoe.

There was blood on my sock.

*What the...*

The side of my toe was scraped raw. From the friction.

---

I walked the rest of the way to school.

I went to the nurse's office. The nurse looked at my foot and said:

"Did this happen while running?"

"Yes."

"Seems like your shoes don't fit properly."

"Huh?"

"Are those running shoes?"

I looked at my shoes. Just regular sneakers, like indoor school shoes. The pair I bought last year.

"They're just sneakers."

"Not running shoes, then. If you're running a lot, you should wear proper running shoes."

---

Running shoes.

So those are a separate thing. Shoes specifically for running.

I didn't know that. I thought all sneakers were the same.

I put a bandage on my foot and went to class.

---

I told Minseo.

"Dude, I scraped up my foot."

"How?"

"Running. My shoes don't fit right."

"Oh, sneaker problem?"

"Yeah. The nurse said I should get running shoes."

Minseo nodded.

"That's right. There are shoes made specifically for running. They're comfortable and absorb shock."

"Really?"

"Yeah. My dad runs. He says running shoes are super important."

I didn't know Minseo's dad was a runner.

"Are running shoes expensive?"

"Um... the good ones are. But there are cheaper ones too."

---

I looked them up during class.

Running shoes.

The prices were all over the place. Some were 50,000 won, others were 200,000 won.

I don't get much allowance. Even 50,000 won feels like a lot.

*Should I ask Mom?*

---

When I got home, I talked to Mom.

"Mom, can I get running shoes?"

"Running shoes?"

"Yeah. Shoes made for running."

Mom looked at me.

"Why? You have sneakers."

"When I run in those, my feet get scraped up."

I showed her the bandage on my foot.

"Oh my, that's a bad scrape."

"They say if I want to run a lot, I need running shoes."

Mom thought about it for a moment.

"How much are they?"

"The cheap ones are around 50,000 won..."

"50,000 won?"

Mom frowned. She seemed to think it was expensive.

"..."

"If you pay half from your allowance, I'll cover the other half."

"Really?"

"Yes. But you actually need them, right? You're not going to run once or twice and never wear them?"

"No, I'm going to keep running."

"Promise me."

"I promise."

Mom nodded.

"Then let's go buy them together this weekend."

---

Writing in my diary now.

Today my foot got scraped.

Because my sneakers don't fit. Apparently you need running shoes if you want to run.

I talked to Mom. We agreed—half from my allowance, half from her.

We're going shopping this weekend.

Running shoes.

Shoes made specifically for running.

I didn't know those existed. I only found out because I started running.

Gear matters, I guess. Even though it seems like you're just running with your body, shoes make a difference.

What will it be like wearing new running shoes? Will I run better?

I'm excited.

I wish the weekend would come sooner.
